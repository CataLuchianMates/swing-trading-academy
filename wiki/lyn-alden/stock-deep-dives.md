# Lyn Alden: Stock Deep Dives

This page compiles Lyn's detailed analysis of individual companies across multiple reports.

---

## Exxon Mobil (XOM)

**Sector**: Large-cap integrated energy producer

**Thesis**: One of the world's lowest-breakeven oil producers with a fortress balance sheet. Survived the 2015/2016 oil crash and 2020 Covid lockdowns without requiring government support. Pays a growing dividend that has increased for 25+ consecutive years. Management has become more capital-disciplined since 2020.

**Current status (Jan 2026)**: Trading near highs with "somewhat richly priced" valuation. Lyn maintains existing positions but was not adding new capital at prevailing prices. Held through Hormuz crisis as energy tail risk hedge.

---

## Chevron (CVX)

**Sector**: Large-cap integrated energy producer

**Thesis**: Similar to XOM with additional upside from Venezuela. CVX was uniquely authorized to operate in Venezuela under sanctions. When the US captured Maduro in January 2026, Lyn noted CVX was "already allowed to operate in Venezuela" and thus would benefit disproportionately from any increase in Venezuelan oil production over the subsequent years. Venezuela has largest oil reserves globally but heavy/expensive crude, so ramp-up will be gradual.

**Current status**: Held alongside XOM. Similar richly-priced-but-holding posture.

## US Bancorp (USB)

**Sector**: Super-regional banking

**Full deep dive**: May 11, 2025 premium report dedicated entirely to USB.

**Key facts**:
- 5th largest US bank
- 13 million retail clients
- $500B AUM (asset/wealth management is a significant fee revenue stream)
- 2,200+ branch locations
- Long history of conservative underwriting through 2008 and 2023 bank crises

**Valuation context (Dec 2025)**: Trading below historical average P/E, with above-average dividend yield. Lyn compared it favorably to JPMorgan (USB at under 12x vs JPM at 16x at the time).

**SLR tailwind**: June 2025 SLR reform reduces capital requirements for holding Treasuries, expands USB's ability to grow its balance sheet and potentially increases the return on equity.

**Private credit exposure**: Approximately 6-12% of total assets in NDFI lending, which Lyn considers manageable given banks' first-loss protections and the scale of capital buffers.

**Thesis summary**: Lyn holds USB as a core "fairly priced risk" position — banks are one of the pockets where she consistently finds value in an overpriced US equity market. Maintained through the 2025 tariff correction and through the Hormuz crisis.

## Truist Financial (TFC)

**Sector**: Super-regional banking

**Context**: TFC is the result of the 2019 merger of BB&T and SunTrust. Integration took several years but Lyn views the combined entity as stronger. She highlighted TFC's private credit exposure ($19.5B = 3.6% of $535B assets) as manageable, noting even a 50% default on that exposure would be a moderate capital dent rather than an existential risk.

**Current status**: Trading below historical average P/E, maintained alongside USB and PNC as the super-regional bank trio.

## Enterprise Products Partners (EPD)

**Sector**: Midstream energy infrastructure (MLP)

**Thesis**: The gold standard of midstream infrastructure. Volume-driven business model (moves crude oil, natural gas, NGLs, refined products, stores hydrocarbons) rather than price-driven, giving it more predictable cash flows than producers. ~7% distribution yield with 25 consecutive years of distribution growth — the longest streak in the MLP space.

**Structural advantage**: Partnership structure (K-1 tax form) creates tax efficiency for domestic investors in taxable accounts. The K-1 complexity limits institutional ownership, which is part of why the yield stays elevated.

**Current status (as of May 2026)**: Core income holding across multiple Lyn portfolios. Held through Hormuz crisis — energy infrastructure volumes were disrupted but US domestic pipeline volumes were less affected than Middle Eastern production.

## Valaris (VAL) — Complete Cycle 2023-2026

**Sector**: Offshore drilling (oilfield services)

**Entry**: Tiny position October 2023
**Thesis**: World's largest offshore drilling fleet, held at below-replacement-cost book values. Emerged from 2020 bankruptcy with clean balance sheet. Day rates rising sharply. Offshore drilling in structural undersupply as shale matures and offshore becomes the primary growth vector for global oil production.

**The rough years**: The stock had "an extremely rough year" in 2024 and through mid-2025. Lyn maintained and re-examined the thesis, noting in September 2025 that VAL had not made a new low since April, suggesting a bottom was forming.

**September 2025 bottom call**: Lyn recommended "topping up" positions in SLB, HAL, and VAL as they showed signs of bottoming. Within five months, VAL was up 90%.

**February 2026 exit**: Transocean (RIG) announced acquisition of Valaris in an all-stock deal. VAL surged to ATH. Lyn sold entire position: "Valaris has reached my target upside potential and I no longer view it as having asymmetric risk/return aspects." Replaced with ADBE in portfolios.

**Lesson**: Low-conviction, high-volatility position that required patience through a deep drawdown before delivering. Position sizing was appropriately small throughout.

## Copa Holdings (CPA)

**Sector**: Latin American aviation (Panama)

**Thesis**: Initiated July 2025 as a "small Other Holdings position" based on:
- Single-digit P/E at initiation
- $900M+ cash equivalents, net debt ~1x net income
- 106-aircraft all-Boeing-737 fleet (operational simplicity = lower costs)
- Panama hub: geographically optimal for North/South America connectivity
- Strong dividend yield

She expanded to a 2% Newsletter Portfolio dividend-stock-pie position in February 2026 after CPA broke out to the upside. Lyn is typically not a fan of airline stocks (prefers airports/travel agencies for moats) but Copa's unusual combination of cheap valuation, balance sheet strength, and geographic positioning justified an exception.

**Caveat**: "One does not bet the farm on a Latin American airline stock." Geopolitical and operational risks warrant conservative sizing.

## Alphabet/Google (GOOGL)

**Sector**: Hyperscaler / internet advertising

**Entry**: 2022, approximately $120/share
**Thesis**: AI concerns about Google Search were "overblown on an investable time horizon." Google was both an AI player in its own right (Gemini, DeepMind) and had YouTube as a secular growth asset. Traded at discount to MSFT and META despite being comparable franchise.

**Exit**: November/December 2025, approximately $300/share. "Taken to neutral after the thesis played out." After a 150%+ gain, Lyn shifted from bullish to neutral — not bearish.

**Post-exit performance**: GOOGL subsequently underperformed the S&P 500 from that point through at least Q1 2026, consistent with her neutral call and the broader hyperscaler FCF concerns she raised in November 2025.

## Adobe (ADBE)

**Sector**: Creative software (SaaS disruption play)

**Original entry**: January 2019, ~$230/share
**Price history through holding**: $230 → $650 → $250 → $630 → $260 (Feb 2026 approximate)

**Current thesis (2026)**: Value play rather than value trap. At ~11-12x forward earnings with a fortress balance sheet, creative software market still growing overall (AI makes design more accessible to more people), Adobe's professional market dominance remains intact, Firefly AI integration showing strong engagement metrics.

**Bear risks**: Canva competing from the entry-level upward; Figma threatening UI/UX segment (Adobe's attempted $20B acquisition blocked by regulators); general per-seat pricing pressure from AI productivity gains.

**Lyn's action**: Gradually adding, not yet pounding the table. Watching for technical bottom formation and CEO succession clarity.

## PayPal (PYPL)

**Sector**: Digital payments

**Initiated**: No Limits portfolio, approximately November 2025
**Valuation at initiation**: $1.4B net debt vs $4.9B income = 0.3x net debt/income, 15x earnings
**Catalysts**: Solid Q3 2025 earnings, OpenAI partnership announced, initiated first-ever dividend

**Current view (March 2026)**: Potential deep value, potential acquisition target. "As a $40B company, it's a potential acquisition target in the years ahead, for a company that can unlock more value from its massive userbase." Watching for technical bottom.

## HDFC Bank (HDB)

**Sector**: Indian banking

**Context**: India's largest private bank. Long-term structural growth play on Indian financial sector penetration (rising household debt capacity, 1.4B+ population, fast-growing middle class).

**Headwind (2023-2025)**: Post-merger integration with parent HDFC Ltd slowed growth metrics for 18-24 months.

**Action (April 13, 2026)**: Added to existing position during Hormuz crisis sell-off when HDB reached near-record low valuation (both earnings multiple and price-to-book). Lyn expects growth acceleration as Year 3 of integration approaches completion.

## DBS Group (DBSDY)

**Sector**: Singapore banking (Southeast Asian exposure)

**Thesis**: "Emerging market correlation with a developed market jurisdiction." Singapore provides strong regulatory framework while DBS's ASEAN business exposure provides growth characteristics similar to EM.

**Valuation**: Has historically traded at reasonable multiples vs its growth rate. Included as a geographic diversifier.

## Itaú Unibanco (ITUB)

**Sector**: Brazilian banking

**Entry**: 2020 Covid lows
**Returns as of December 2025**: 81% YTD in dollar terms; 5-year total return ~179% including dividends

**Thesis validation**: Brazil proved resilient to Trump's 50% tariff threat (letter sent July 2025) because US trade is only ~2% of Brazilian GDP, and commodity exports are easily redirected. The bank's conservative management, capital discipline, and Brazilian economic resilience resulted in exceptional returns even in a year of tariff risk.

**Current view (May 2026)**: Still potentially undervalued on absolute basis, though not the "table-pounding buy" it was at Covid lows. Lyn continues holding for what she views as a multi-year EM upcycle thesis.

## Komatsu (KMTUY)

**Sector**: Japanese heavy equipment (EM proxy)

**Thesis**: "Emerging markets correlation with a developed market jurisdiction." Komatsu's revenue is driven by global construction and mining equipment demand — both heavily weighted toward EM physical infrastructure buildout. Lyn used it as a way to get EM exposure with Japanese legal/regulatory backing.

## Micron Technology (MU)

**Sector**: Memory semiconductors

**Cash-secured put (April 13, 2026)**: Sold June 18 $380 puts with MU at $420. Generates premium income; if assigned, cost basis ~$350.

**Thesis**: Three companies make almost all the world's RAM (Micron, SK Hynix, Samsung). AI agents require substantially more working memory than chatbots, creating a new structural demand driver. "We've broken through the soft ceiling on RAM demand."

**May 2026 outcome**: MU broke out to fresh new highs, along with SK Hynix and Samsung. Short puts expired profitable and the stock outperformed expectations.
