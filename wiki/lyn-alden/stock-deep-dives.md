## Enterprise Products Partners (EPD)

**Deep Dive: January 2024**

EPD is the largest midstream MLP in the US, operating ~50,000 miles of pipeline plus storage, fractionation, and Gulf Coast export terminals. Lyn has held it continuously since her first coverage in April 2022 and considers it the single most reliable risk-adjusted income investment in her portfolio.

**Thesis pillars:**
- Fee-based revenue (pipeline tolls) insulated from commodity price swings
- 25+ consecutive years of distribution growth through GFC, 2015 oil bust, and COVID
- 20-year average debt duration locked at low rates — the opposite risk profile of banks
- Valuation at October 2022 was at P/CF levels not seen since 2009-2011

**January 2024 re-confirmation:** The deep dive confirmed EPD was still yielding 7.5%, distributable cash flow coverage was 1.8x the distribution (conservative payout ratio), and balance sheet leverage was declining. The thesis remained intact at higher prices than her initial entry.

**Why she prefers EPD to bonds in fiscal dominance era:** Bonds lose real purchasing power over time when inflation exceeds the coupon. EPD's distributions grow over time, tracking the nominal volume of hydrocarbons moving through the system. In an inflationary or stagflationary environment, EPD preserves purchasing power while paying income; nominal bonds do not.

## Simon Property Group (SPG)

**Deep Dive: August 2022**

SPG is the largest US mall REIT by market cap and the dominant operator of Class A mall properties. Lyn's August 2022 deep dive came when SPG was trading at multi-year lows after COVID closed malls and killed brick-and-mortar retail sentiment.

**Thesis:**
- Class A malls (Lyn's analogy: destination entertainment centers) are fundamentally different from Class B/C malls that are dying
- SPG's specific tenants (luxury, restaurants, entertainment, fitness) drive foot traffic that online retailers cannot replicate
- SPG was proactively acquiring distressed retailers (J.Crew, Aeropostale, Brooks Brothers) and reviving them as portfolio companies — a genius asymmetric bet that converted tenant bankruptcies into ownership equity
- Net operating income per square foot was recovering quickly, but the stock price had not recovered

**Valuation at entry:** SPG was yielding above 6% at trough prices — unusually high for a premier real estate franchise.

## Truist Financial (TFC)

**Deep Dive: January 2023**

TFC is the product of the 2019 BB&T/SunTrust merger, creating the 8th largest US bank with deep franchise in the Southeast. Lyn wrote the formal deep dive in January 2023 as she was building the barbell framework.

**Thesis:**
- BB&T/SunTrust merger created $2-3 billion/year in cost synergies, but the integration costs were front-loaded into 2019-2022, depressing near-term earnings and making the stock appear more expensive than it was on normalized earnings
- Southeast US franchise: Charlotte, Atlanta, and the broader Sunbelt are the fastest-growing US demographics
- Super-regional status: diversified enough to be resilient, small enough to trade at value multiples
- 4-5% dividend yield paying investors to wait through integration

**2023-2025 experience:** TFC navigated the SVB crisis without significant distress. Earnings were below consensus (trade war headwinds), but capital position strengthened. By 2025, Lyn's view was "buy on continued weakness — this buying opportunity looks a lot like spring 2023."

## US Bancorp (USB)

**Deep Dive: May 2025**

USB is the 5th largest US bank with $500+ billion in assets, 13 million retail clients, 1.4 million business clients, 500k wealth clients, 45k corporate/institutional clients. Processing $900+ billion in payment volume annually through its merchant acquiring bank function. 2,200+ branches in the western two-thirds of the US.

**Thesis:**
- Super-regional with exceptional deposit diversification (no unusual uninsured deposit concentration that caused SVB's vulnerability)
- Lost ~30% of tangible book value to unrealized securities losses in 2022-2023, recovered fully within two years — confirming management quality
- Analysts projecting strong earnings growth over 2025-2027
- Trading at value multiples with 4-5% dividend yield
- Incoming rate steepening (post-Warsh Fed) would expand net interest margins

**Key comparison to JPM:** Lyn prefers USB's valuation (reasonable P/TBV) over JPM (significant premium). USB's lower premium means more upside from mean-reversion.

## HDFC Bank (HDB)

**Deep Dive: December 2024**

HDB is India's largest private bank — the banking franchise for 1.4 billion people in the fastest-growing major economy. Lyn published a formal deep dive in December 2024.

**Thesis:**
- India's demographic profile (median age ~28 years) means decades of runway for banking penetration growth
- HDFC Bank's three-decade history of conservative lending with historically low NPA ratios
- Pre-merger (HDFC Bank + HDFC Ltd completed 2023) created the largest privately-held bank in Asia by assets
- Growing middle class means household credit demand (mortgages, auto loans, consumer loans) is in early innings
- Premium valuation vs peers justified by quality and growth profile

**Entry timing:** Lyn added formally in April 2026 after the Hormuz-driven pullback in Indian markets. The currency risk was partially offset by the structural EM rotation thesis.

## Realty Income (O)

**Deep Dive: March 2024**

O is the world's largest publicly traded net-lease REIT. Net-lease means tenants pay property taxes, insurance, and maintenance directly (triple-net leases), making O's income stream unusually predictable. Over 15,400 properties across 49 US states and 7 European countries.

**Thesis:**
- 30+ years of consecutive monthly dividend increases (the "Monthly Dividend Company")
- ~12,000 commercial tenants with weighted average lease term of 9+ years — extreme income visibility
- Investment-grade balance sheet (rated Baa1/BBB+)
- By early 2024, yielding 5-6% — attractive relative to historical averages
- Geographic and tenant diversification protects against sector-specific downturns

**Difference from SPG:** O is diversified net-lease (convenience stores, drug stores, dollar stores, theaters, gyms, industrial) vs SPG's Class A mall concentration. O is the steady compounder; SPG was the deep value recovery play.

## British American Tobacco (BTI)

**Analysis: Throughout 2022-2026**

BTI is one of Lyn's persistent "boring income" holdings. She describes tobacco companies as the archetype of value investing in the fiscal dominance era: legally addictive products with inelastic demand, cash-flow-generating franchises that pay shareholders consistently, and priced at single-digit P/E multiples because ESG mandates drive institutional selling.

**Thesis:**
- BTI yielding 6.5% at time of primary analysis (December 2022)
- Declining smoking volumes offset by pricing power (tobacco companies raise prices faster than volumes decline)
- Transition to alternatives (vaping, heated tobacco) provides potential volume recovery path
- ADR structure gives US investors easy access to a UK-domiciled company trading at far cheaper multiples than US consumer staples

**Why BTI over MO (Altria):** BTI has international diversification; Altria is pure US tobacco with concentrated regulatory risk from FDA menthol bans and similar.

## Bristol-Myers Squibb (BMY)

**Analysis: December 2022 and ongoing**

BMY is a pharmaceutical company trading at deep value multiples due to patent cliff concerns — key blockbuster drugs face generic competition in 2025-2028. Lyn's view: the market was pricing in too severe a revenue decline, and the pipeline of next-generation drugs (cancer immunology, cardiovascular) was undervalued.

**Thesis:**
- 2.7% yield raised 5.7% annually — dividend growth even as stock languished
- P/E of ~8-10x (unusually cheap for a company with this-scale pharmaceutical operations)
- Patent cliff was known and already priced in; pipeline success would provide upside that wasn't priced in
- Fortress-level balance sheet (large pharma companies generate enormous free cash flow)

## Fiserv (FI)

**Analysis: January 2024**

Fiserv (ticker changed from FISV to FI) provides fintech infrastructure — payment processing, core banking systems, and merchant acquiring. Lyn cited it in her January 2024 EPD deep dive as an example of an equity with "variable revenue but mostly fixed costs" — the ideal profile in a slow-motion crack-up boom/inflationary environment.

FI has rising nominal revenue (as payment volumes grow with nominal GDP) while its long-term debt was locked in at low rates. As inflation gradually inflates away the real value of its fixed debt while growing its revenue base, margins expand.

## Bank OZK (OZK)

**Deep Dive: May 2024**

Bank OZK is a specialty construction lender based in Arkansas. Lyn published a deep dive in May 2024 when the stock was near tangible book value.

**Thesis:** 
- Construction lending is short-duration (typically 18-36 month project timelines) and secured by the underlying project value — different risk profile from permanent CRE loans
- OZK's "Real Estate Specialties Group" has specialized expertise in large construction projects, providing superior underwriting vs generalist banks
- Historical NPA ratios extremely low despite heavy CRE concentration
- At tangible book value, the market was pricing in significant expected losses; Lyn viewed this as pessimistic

She noted this in the K-shaped economy context: upper-class residential construction (OZK's market) was still proceeding; the distress was in office and Class B/C retail.

## Komatsu (KMTUF)
