# Lyn Alden — Macro History 2022–2026

A chronological narrative of how Lyn read the macro environment from February 2022 through May 2026 — what she anticipated, what surprised her, and how each turning point shaped her portfolio positioning.

---

## Early 2022: Inflation Confirmed, Fed Behind the Curve

Entering 2022, Lyn had already been arguing since 2020 that the combination of massive fiscal stimulus and Fed accommodation would produce a genuine inflation wave — not the temporary post-GFC disinflation of the 2010s. By early 2022, that call was vindicated: CPI had reached 7-8% and would eventually peak at 9.1% in June 2022. The Fed, which had been dismissing inflation as "transitory," was dramatically behind.

In her February 2022 article on Twilio and other growth tech, Lyn was already positioned away from high-multiple growth stocks and toward value — energy, pipelines, banks, and EM. Her February portfolio reflected that: EPD, ET, XOM, PBR, HDB, ITUB, plus some defensive healthcare names. Growth tech (ROKU, TWLO) was still in the portfolio but flagged as expensive.

Ukraine's invasion of Russia in March 2022 complicated the inflation picture by spiking energy prices further, but Lyn's pre-existing energy positioning meant she benefited rather than scrambled.

## Mid-2022: Rate Hike Cycle Accelerates, Dollar Peaks

The Fed hiked aggressively throughout mid-2022 — ultimately taking the fed funds rate from 0% to 5.25% in just 15 months, the fastest cycle in 40 years. The dollar index surged to a 20-year high of ~114 by September-October 2022, crushing global liquidity and creating distress across emerging markets and commodity markets.

Lyn sold growth tech positions (ROKU, TWLO) in December 2022 and at various points throughout the year pruned positions she'd held since the 2019-2020 accumulation period. In October 2022, she wrote about Treasury market illiquidity — a near-crisis in the world's most liquid bond market caused by the speed of the rate hike cycle. UK gilt crisis during this period served as a warning shot. She noted that the Treasury basis trade and the absence of QE created a "stretched rubber bands" dynamic that she expected would produce a significant tactical rally — which indeed happened from October 2022 through early 2023.

Precious metals bottomed in September-October 2022 in dollar terms, a call she made in her October 2022 gold market analysis and confirmed by November. She identified the dollar breaking its uptrend as the catalyst.

Bitcoin reached what she later confirmed as its cycle floor: below $16,000 in November 2022, which placed it below its Realized Price on-chain — the fifth time ever this had occurred, and historically a reliable accumulation signal.

## November 2022: FTX/Alameda Collapse — "Lehman Moment" for Crypto

The November 2022 collapse of FTX and Alameda Research was, in Lyn's framing, a "Lehman moment" for the crypto industry. Sam Bankman-Fried had been running Alameda as a prop trading firm with undisclosed risk, effectively backed by FTX customer funds. When this became public and a bank run ensued, FTX collapsed within 72 hours.

Lyn's analysis in the aftermath was clear: the contagion was primarily intra-crypto. The FTX collapse damaged centralized exchanges, yield platforms, and tokens interconnected with Alameda's books. Bitcoin itself was more insulated — the on-chain self-custody surge (40,000 new wallets with 1+ BTC opened) confirmed retail investors moving toward self-custody rather than trusting centralized platforms.

Meanwhile, also in November 2022, Brazil held its general election. Lula won over Bolsonaro in a close second-round vote. Markets initially sold off Brazil equities, but Lyn's analysis of ITUB and PBR was that their fundamentals were already priced at crisis levels (PBR at low single-digit P/E and a double-digit dividend yield), and the political noise was overriding fundamental value. She remained long both.

## December 2022: BOJ YCC Surprise, MSTR Added

On December 20, 2022, the Bank of Japan raised its yield curve control (YCC) ceiling from 0.25% to 0.50% — a surprise that roiled global bond markets. Lyn described it as the most significant signal of 2022's end: the last major yield-capping central bank was being forced to acknowledge inflation pressure.

In her December 2022 deep dive on US liquidity and Treasuries, she formalized the domestic liquidity formula (Fed BS − TGA − RRP) and showed its chart vs S&P 500. She also added MicroStrategy (MSTR) to her Newsletter Portfolio — a 10% allocation in the growth/speculation slice, noting it was effectively a leveraged Bitcoin vehicle trading near NAV. She paired this with SHY and T-bills as defensive positioning.

## January-February 2023: Debt Ceiling, Barbell Formalization

The debt ceiling standoff in January 2023 produced a counterintuitive pro-liquidity effect: with Treasury unable to issue new bonds, the TGA depleted and net liquidity flowed into the financial system. Lyn flagged this as the reason for the January risk rally.

In February 2023, Lyn published her formal barbell framework: Defense (BIL/SHY/cash, gold, EPD) and Offense (bitcoin, EM banks, energy, US super-regional banks). She formally added HDB (HDFC Bank India) to Other Holdings, analyzed ITUB's triple bottom structure, and introduced a detailed analysis of Truist Financial (TFC) as a value play in banking. She also noted the unusual stat: US banks were holding cash at 35% of assets — historically the highest level, making the system far more resilient than 2007.

## March 2023: SVB Crisis, Three Pillars Portfolio

Silicon Valley Bank collapsed in March 2023 — the second-largest bank failure in US history. The failure mechanism was specific: SVB had the highest ratio of uninsured deposits of any major bank (nearly all were business accounts above FDIC limits), combined with enormous unrealized losses on its Treasury portfolio from the rate hike cycle. A social-media-driven bank run in 48 hours killed it.

Lyn's immediate analysis was that the crisis, while significant, was not systemic in the 2008 sense. The aggregate US banking system had $2.35 trillion in equity capital. The $650 billion in unrealized losses (later circulated widely on social media) wiped out less than 30% of that capital, and for most banks would never be realized because they could hold to maturity. The specific kill shot required both components: unusual uninsured deposit concentration AND major unrealized losses — a combination that affected only a handful of banks.

The Fed's BTFP (Bank Term Funding Program) backstop was introduced, effectively guaranteeing that any bank could exchange its underwater Treasuries at par. This removed the liquidity threat.

Bitcoin rallied to $28,000 during the crisis — decoupling from traditional financials in a way that Lyn viewed as validating its "exit from the system" properties.

In her March 2023 article, Lyn formalized the Three Pillars Portfolio: (1) high-quality profitable equities with dividends, (2) commodity/hard money exposures (energy, gold, bitcoin), (3) cash/T-bills as a volatility buffer. This was the explicit template for all subsequent portfolio construction.

## May 2023: First AI Deep Dive

Lyn's first serious engagement with AI as an investment theme came in May 2023. Her framework was "10x accessibility" — AI does not primarily replace expert professionals but dramatically lowers the bar for non-experts to access expert-level capability. A graphic designer can now do in hours what required a full creative team. A junior programmer can write production-ready code. This expands total demand for the underlying tools even as it disrupts the labor market for human experts.

Her investment conclusion was contrarian relative to the NVDA mania: Nvidia had already priced in enormous expectations; TSM (Taiwan Semiconductor) offered better risk/reward as the foundational manufacturer. She also picked out Adobe (ADBE) as the AI beneficiary that hadn't run yet, adding it in May 2023. ADBE subsequently rose 44% by December 2023 before she began warning of overvaluation.

She also noted that AI + bitcoin could enable machine-to-machine payments at scale (Lightning network as the rails), a thesis she would develop further.

## August 2023: BOJ YCC Adjustment, $1.85T Treasury Borrowing

In July 2023, the BOJ announced a further adjustment to its YCC policy — making 0.50% a "soft" ceiling and 1.00% a hard ceiling. This was a significant step toward YCC removal, with major implications for Japanese capital flows: if Japanese bonds yielded more, the structural incentive to hold US Treasuries instead would diminish.

Lyn's August 2023 article on the BOJ's global impact was prescient. She added SMFG and MUFG (Japanese megabanks) to Other Holdings as a direct play on rising Japanese bank net interest margins. She shifted her Japan trading house positions (Itochu, Mitsui, Mitsubishi — bought in late 2020) from bullish to neutral after strong multi-year runs.

The other major development: Treasury Secretary Yellen announced $1.85 trillion in Treasury borrowing for H2 2023. This was a staggering figure. The key debate was the issuance composition — T-bills vs longer-term notes. T-bill heavy issuance would absorb money market funds that were sitting in the RRP and thus be liquidity-neutral; longer-term note issuance would compete with the private sector for savings and be liquidity-draining.

Yellen ultimately went T-bill heavy, a policy Lyn would later term "Activist Treasury Issuance" when formalized by the Miran/Roubini paper in August 2024.

## October 2023: TIPS Added, Hamas Attack, Oilfield Services Entries

In October 2023 (specifically following the Hamas attack on Israel on October 7), Lyn added to her real-asset positioning. Real yields had reached 2.36% on the 10-year TIPS — levels not seen since before the GFC, representing unusually high inflation-adjusted compensation on government paper. She purchased the TIP ETF and added STIP (short-term TIPS) as already-held.

She also added VAL (Valaris, offshore drilling) and TDW (Tidewater, offshore support vessels) as small positions. The oilfield services and offshore drilling thesis was that post-2020 supply destruction had been severe, dayrates were rising, and offshore drilling would be a key source of new oil supply as onshore shale matured. She sold URNM (uranium ETF) at this point, having held it through its run.

The October 2023 geopolitical escalation drove Lyn to re-examine BRICS+ dynamics and the trajectory of dollar use in global trade. She cited 83% bitcoin/global M2 correlation as evidence that liquidity — not geopolitics — remained the primary driver of bitcoin's price.

## Q4 2023–Q1 2024: Bitcoin ETF Era Begins

Bitcoin broke to $34,000 in October 2023 — a new cycle high at that point — on anticipation of spot ETF approvals. Lyn's analysis: the ETF would unlock institutional and retail flows that previously had no direct access. GBTC (Grayscale Bitcoin Trust), which she had purchased at a 30% NAV discount in 2022, would eventually be converted to an ETF and close the discount.

ETF approvals came in January 2024. The approval wave was immediately positive for price, as anticipated. Bitcoin began its move toward $73,000 by March-April 2024.

In March 2024, Lyn sold MSTR from her Top 12 list. MicroStrategy had risen to 2x NAV — meaning the market was paying $2 for every $1 of Bitcoin in the company. Her entry was near 1x NAV in December 2022. At 2x, the arbitrage closed and the risk profile shifted; she exited the position.

## Mid-2024: Asset Rotation Thesis Develops, BOJ Exit

By summer 2024, Lyn was developing the framework for an eventual US-to-EM asset rotation. The dollar had been persistently elevated, partly because the US fiscal deficit kept pushing capital flows inward. But this dynamic was unsustainable: US equities were at record valuation premiums vs the rest of the world (Shiller CAPE, price/book, etc.). Meanwhile, EM assets were pricing in significant distress that hadn't materialized.

The August 2024 Miran/Roubini paper introduced the term "Activist Treasury Issuance" — Treasury Secretary Yellen had been deliberately issuing more T-bills than longer-term notes to keep the RRP flowing into the system. This was equivalent to a ~100 basis point rate cut in terms of liquidity impact, Lyn noted.

The same month, the BOJ raised its policy rate to 0.25%, triggering the global yen carry trade unwind. The yen had been one of the primary funding currencies for global risk positions; as it strengthened, risk assets globally sold off. Unemployment rose to 4.3% in the July US data. Lyn's view: a mild recession was possible but not a 2008-style collapse. The fiscal deficit was too large to allow that outcome.

## Late 2024: Bitcoin Peaks Near $125,000, MSTR Gains

Bitcoin broke above $100,000 in November 2024, reaching approximately $125,000 in early 2026 before correcting. MSTR by November 2024 had accumulated over 330,000 bitcoin and had become the largest corporate holder in history. There was ongoing discussion of MSTR potentially being added to the S&P 500, which would force passive index funds to buy the stock.

Japan's political shift: PM Kishida was replaced and Sanae Takaichi was elected as the new Prime Minister in October 2025, bringing a $135 billion fiscal stimulus package (~$1 trillion US equivalent in GDP-adjusted terms). Lyn viewed this as structurally positive for Japanese domestic demand and for Japanese bank stocks, which would benefit from steeper yield curves.

The Fed's balance sheet began expanding again, shifting from QT to approximately $40 billion/month of balance sheet growth starting December 2025 (confirmed by Powell at the December 10, 2025 press conference). By the December–April period, the Fed averaged $41.5 billion/month.

## February–March 2026: Tariff Resolution, Iran Strikes, Hormuz Crisis

The Supreme Court ruled 6-3 against Trump's emergency tariff authority on February 20, 2026. Tariff ceiling was effectively set; the macro overhang from escalating tariffs began to fade. The ruling was a ~$300 billion annual fiscal policy swing.

Then on approximately February 28, 2026, the US and Israel conducted major strikes on Iran targeting regime change, including the killing of Supreme Leader Khamenei. Iran counterattacked against US military bases in the region. The Strait of Hormuz was announced closed; in practice, shipping volumes dropped sharply as insurers cancelled coverage. Three tankers were struck within days. Maersk suspended transit. Egypt, South Korea, and Slovenia began fuel rationing within weeks.

Lyn's immediate analysis: ~20% of global oil and LNG transit was affected. S&P 500 fell only 8% from its pre-crisis high by March 2026, confirming that US equities were not the safe haven in an inflationary supply shock. Gold surged to the $4,000+ range. Silver moved above $60. Bitcoin initially dropped to $60-70k before recovering.

She specifically noted that long-duration US Treasuries — the traditional crisis safe haven — were not acting as such, because the supply shock was inflationary. This was precisely the scenario her barbell was designed for.

## April–May 2026: Fiscal Re-acceleration, EM Breakout

By April 2026, with the Hormuz crisis six to eight weeks old and no ceasefire, the underlying fiscal dynamics reasserted themselves. The "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" of July 2025 had already kicked in — structurally extending and expanding fiscal transfers to the economy. Powell's term ended May 15, 2026. Kevin Warsh, the presumed successor, was in Senate confirmation. Markets anticipated a dovish shift regardless of Warsh's prior hawkish background, given Trump's explicit preference for low rates.

The SPX/gold ratio was rolling over on a long-term basis — Lyn interpreted this as the beginning of the next multi-year phase where real assets outperform financial assets in US markets, just as the 1966-1982 period saw gold and commodities dramatically outperform nominal equities.

By May 2026, the EM breakout was confirmed. The dollar had weakened from ~110 to ~98-100. EWZ (Brazil), INDA (India), and broader EM ETFs were outperforming US equities. Lyn described the dynamic as feeding on itself: dollar weakness eases EM debt burdens, which improves EM fundamentals, which attracts more capital, which weakens the dollar further — the same self-reinforcing mechanism that drove the 2003-2007 EM supercycle.

The US economy itself was showing "Japanification" characteristics: Q2 2026 GDP was solid, manufacturing PMI finally positive, but structural stagnation of the asset-heavy upper class vs the asset-light lower class was entrenched. Lyn expected this pattern to persist for years.
