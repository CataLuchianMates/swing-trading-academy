# Lyn Alden — Portfolio Construction

Lyn's portfolio construction evolved over the 2022-2026 period from a broad equity/dividend framework into a more explicitly structured barbell model, refined by the SVB crisis into the Three Pillars framework. This page documents those frameworks, the specific portfolio vehicles she uses, and how she applies rotation logic.

---

## The Value Decade Thesis (2022 Starting Point)

Entering 2022, Lyn was already positioned around the thesis that the 2020s would be a value decade — the inverse of the growth-dominated 2010s. Her October 2022 portfolio pruning article explicitly articulated this: "It took about 20-25 years for value to go from roughly matching the S&P 500 to significantly underperforming it, but the pendulum swings back."

Her value decade framework:
- Interest rates rising: Growth stock valuations compressed (long-duration cash flows discounted at higher rates)
- Commodity cycle turning up: Energy, materials, agriculture — all underfunded during the 2010s ESG-driven investment bust
- Geopolitical fragmentation: Deglobalization raises the cost of goods; the companies that produce physical things gain pricing power
- Fiscal dominance: Companies with pricing power protect margins better than high-multiple growth names

This thesis drove her October 2022 portfolio changes: sold BAC, EBAY, JPM, DHI, PHM (homebuilders had been a trade, now a sell), PINS. Added TFC, RTX (Raytheon defense), BLDR (Builder's FirstSource construction materials).

## The Barbell — Formalized February 2023

In February 2023, Lyn published "A Barbell Approach" — her most complete portfolio framework statement of the cycle. The barbell explicitly divides capital between two ends:

### Defense End

Assets with the lowest correlation to economic growth and financial system stress. Hold these regardless of what the offense end does.

- **BIL/SHY (T-bill ETFs):** Short-duration government paper earning near Fed funds rate. No duration risk. The "cash that beats short-term inflation" in a rising rate environment.
- **EPD:** Fee-based infrastructure with 25+ year distribution growth history. Not correlated with credit cycles. Real income that grows nominally over time.
- **Gold (PHYS):** Monetary debasement insurance. No counterparty risk. Negative correlation with real returns on government debt.
- **BTI (British American Tobacco):** Consumer staples with inelastic demand, 6.5%+ dividend yield, defensive characteristics.

### Offense End

Higher-volatility assets with significant upside in a liquidity expansion or currency debasement scenario. Size these for maximum position risk tolerance.

- **Bitcoin:** The highest-beta debasement asset; 83% correlated with global M2; benefits from fiscal dominance over the cycle
- **EM banks (HDB, ITUB):** Dollar cycle leverage; benefit from dollar weakening that Lyn expected to develop
- **US energy (XOM, CVX, SU):** Commodity cycle with energy underinvestment driving structural supply tightness
- **US super-regional banks (TFC, USB):** Value recovery play as rates normalized

### What's Missing (Intentionally)

Long-duration Treasuries, high-yield bonds, and investment-grade corporate bonds are deliberately absent. In a fiscal dominance era:
- Duration risk is not compensated — real yields go negative, or nominal yields keep rising to fund deficits
- Credit risk adds drawdown potential at the worst times (correlation with equities in crashes)
- The income they generate doesn't grow with inflation

The middle of the barbell — balanced funds, 60/40, standard bond allocations — is the explicit void in Lyn's portfolio.

## The Three Pillars — Formalized March 2023 (SVB Article)

Following the SVB crisis, Lyn refined the barbell into the Three Pillars:

**Pillar 1: High-Quality Profitable Equities with Dividends**
Definition: Profitable companies with strong balance sheets, consistent free cash flow, and dividend payment history. This includes EPD, ETF, BTI, BMY, ATO, USB, TFC, SPG, O, V, HDB, ITUB, PBR, XOM, CVX.

The requirement: they must be profitable NOW, not just in a projected future state. No pre-revenue companies. No companies burning cash for market share. Every position generates real free cash flow.

**Pillar 2: Commodity / Hard Money Exposures**
Bitcoin, gold (PHYS), SAND (gold royalties), GDX (gold miners, later exited), energy producers (XOM, CVX, SU, CNQ), oilfield services (VAL, TDW, SLB, HAL), TIPS (TIP, STIP), GUNR.

These assets are "hard" — either physically limited in supply or tied to the physical world. In a slow-motion currency debasement scenario, they protect real purchasing power.

**Pillar 3: Cash and Short-Term Instruments as Volatility Buffer**
BIL, SHY, T-bills directly, money market funds. This serves three purposes:
1. Earns real income during tightening cycles (4-5% short-term rates from 2022-2024)
2. Provides dry powder for opportunities when risk assets sell off
3. Reduces overall portfolio volatility without requiring long-duration bond risk

## Portfolio Vehicles

Lyn manages multiple distinct portfolios for her subscribers, each with a different risk profile:

**Newsletter Portfolio:** The primary reference portfolio for most subscribers. Diversified across all three pillars. Uses ETFs extensively alongside individual stocks. Specific allocation: a "growth/tech pie," a "commodity producer pie," a "defensive/income slice," and a "cash/T-bill buffer."

**Fortress Income:** A conservative portfolio focused on maximum yield/income stability. Heavy EPD, BTI, BMY, O, utilities. Minimal bitcoin or commodity speculation. Designed for retirees or income-dependent investors.

**ETF-Only Portfolio:** Built entirely from ETFs for investors who don't want to hold individual stocks. Uses AMLP (midstream), GUNR (natural resources), EWZ (Brazil), INDA (India), BIL/SHY (cash), PHYS (gold), VWO (EM broad).

**No Limits Portfolio:** The most aggressive portfolio. Holds everything in the Newsletter Portfolio plus individual small-cap plays (VAL, TDW, BLDR, ADBE at different times). More concentrated, higher volatility.

**Top 12 List:** A concentrated "best ideas" list of 12 positions across all categories. Changes the least of any portfolio. MSTR was on this list from December 2022 to March 2024.

**Other Holdings:** Lyn's personal discretionary portfolio. Includes smaller experimental positions. HDB was first added here before graduating to broader portfolio inclusion.

## Position Sizing Logic

Lyn's position sizing follows a few consistent principles:

**Quality to size correlation:** Higher confidence = larger position. EPD is typically 5-10% of the income slice. Bitcoin is sized for what can emotionally and financially be held through an 80% drawdown. Small speculative positions (VAL, TDW, SAND) are kept at 1-3%.

**No concentration above ~10%:** Even highest-conviction individual stocks are capped. Diversification is not just about return correlation — it's about protecting against unknown company-specific risks (regulatory action, management failure, fraud).

**Sector concentration caps:** She targets no more than 20-25% in any single sector. Energy was near this ceiling at peak cycle; banking + financials combined similarly.

**Cash as variable:** The cash/T-bill buffer floats between ~10% and ~30% based on market conditions. During 2022 when valuations were elevated across the board, she held more cash. During early 2023 crises (SVB), she deployed cash into TFC and USB. During Hormuz crisis 2026, she deployed cash into HDB and V.

## The October 2022 Portfolio Pruning

This article represents the clearest snapshot of Lyn applying value rotation in real time. Positions sold:

- **BAC and JPM:** Large bank megacaps; sold in favor of smaller, cheaper TFC/USB
- **EBAY:** Sold as e-commerce slowed; the pandemic surge in e-commerce was normalizing
- **DHI and PHM (homebuilders):** Rate-sensitive; housing market tightening with mortgage rates doubling
- **PINS (Pinterest):** Sold with ROKU/TWLO as high-multiple growth exits

Positions added:
- **TFC:** Super-regional bank at value multiples with SVB-crisis buffer
- **RTX (Raytheon):** Defense thesis — NATO spending acceleration post-Ukraine invasion was structural. Raytheon's missile and defense electronics were in strong demand
- **BLDR (Builder's FirstSource):** Construction materials supplier; Lyn's differentiated view was that while homebuilders (DHI/PHM) were rate-sensitive, BLDR was also exposed to renovation/remodel demand (less rate-sensitive) and the multi-family/apartment construction cycle that was still strong

This pruning also involved replacing BABA with BLDR in the aggressive list — Lyn moved from China single-stock risk to US domestic value.

## The August 2024 Rotation Framework

In August 2024, Lyn published a detailed "Potential Asset Rotation" deep dive. The framework laid out conditions for a major US-to-EM rotation that she viewed as the next multi-year macro trade:

**Rotation trigger signals:**
- Dollar index turning persistently lower (peaked at 114 in Sep 2022, tracking toward 98-100 by 2026)
- US valuations at record premium vs rest of world on any standard metric (Shiller CAPE, P/B, EV/EBITDA)
- EM current account positions improving (commodity producers and manufacturers benefiting from global industrial recovery)
- BOJ normalization reducing yen carry trade funding (repatriation to Japan, reducing US Treasury demand, mild dollar headwind)

**Her positioning response:** She did not make dramatic changes in August 2024 (mild recession risk she acknowledged was uncertain), but began tracking EM rotation signals more closely. The formal breakout wasn't confirmed until May 2026.

## Position Rotation Examples

**MSTR Trade (Dec 2022 → Mar 2024):**
Added MSTR to Newsletter Portfolio in December 2022 at ~NAV (bitcoin at ~$16,000). Thesis: leveraged bitcoin vehicle at a fair price. Sold March 2024 at 2x NAV when bitcoin reached ~$73,000. Premium exceeded rational value; discipline dictated an exit. Total return from entry to exit: approximately 400-500% (bitcoin roughly quadrupled plus the NAV premium doubled).

**GBTC Trade (2022 → 2024):**
Bought GBTC (Grayscale Bitcoin Trust) in 2022 when it was trading at a 30% discount to its Bitcoin NAV. The bet was that the NAV discount would eventually close — either through an ETF conversion or market normalization. When spot Bitcoin ETF was approved in January 2024 and GBTC converted to an ETF format, the NAV discount closed. Lyn exited the GBTC position at or near NAV, capturing both bitcoin's appreciation and the NAV discount closure.

**Japan Trading Houses (2020/2021 → 2023):**
Bought Itochu, Mitsui, Mitsubishi in late 2020/early 2021 inspired by Berkshire's position disclosure. Held through strong multi-year gains. Shifted from bullish to neutral in August 2023 as the BOJ YCC normalization began and valuations had normalized. Exited or reduced by 2023.

**VAL (Oct 2023 → Feb 2026):**
Added October 2023, endured roughly flat-to-negative performance for a year, re-confirmed thesis in September 2025 and topped up, then exited the full position in February 2026 when acquired by Transocean at a premium. Total return from initial entry to exit: substantial, particularly from the September 2025 re-entry.

## Equal-Weight vs Market-Weight Framework

In her February 2026 article, Lyn developed a key portfolio theme: the shift from market-weight to equal-weight S&P 500. The market-weight version (SPY, VOO) was massively dominated by mega-cap tech by 2025, with ~35% of the index in 7 stocks. The equal-weight version (RSP) spreads across all 500 equally.

Her thesis: AI's competitive dynamics are the opposite of the 2015-2023 network effect tech monopoly period. In 2015-2023, winner-take-all dynamics concentrated wealth at the mega-cap level (minimal capex, high margins, self-reinforcing data moats). In 2025 onward, AI requires massive capex, creates less sticky ecosystems (users switch easily), and the primary beneficiary is the consumer/user rather than the platform. This is a catalyst for equal-weight outperformance as value and mid-cap names close the gap.
