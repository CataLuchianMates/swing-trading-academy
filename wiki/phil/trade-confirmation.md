# Trade Confirmation — The Daily Signal

## Why Confirmation Exists

Phil describes confirmation as "the least important" topic in the curriculum — and then immediately explains why it is still essential. Without confirmation, swing trading has an approximate 60% win rate. With confirmation, that rises to approximately **80%**. That jump is enormous.

The analogy Phil uses is a car test drive. Before handing over the money, you test drive the car to verify it works. The test drive does not guarantee the car will never break down — it just gives you a much higher probability that it will perform. Confirmation is the test drive for a trade. You could enter without it, but why would you?

Confirmation does not apply to long-term investing. When investing, you are catching falling knives on assets with near-certain long-term upside — no confirmation needed, just buy the dip. But for swing trading, where you are timing entry and exit, confirmation is always used.

---

## Where Confirmation Fits in the Three-Step Process

Step 1 (Trend) and Step 2 (Structure) are both done on the **monthly timeframe**. Step 3 (Confirmation) is the only point where you leave the monthly and drop to the **daily timeframe**.

Here is the important conceptual nuance: although the overall strategy trades with the monthly uptrend, in practice you are catching **reversals on the daily timeframe**. Here is why:

- A monthly uptrend consists of alternating **daily uptrends** (the impulsive legs up) and **daily downtrends** (the pullbacks)
- Each monthly pullback to support appears, on the daily chart, as a downtrend — lower highs, lower lows
- You are entering at the precise moment that daily downtrend **shifts back to an uptrend**

This means Phil is technically a **continuation trader on the monthly** and a **reversal trader on the daily**. Both are true simultaneously.

## The Bullish Break of Structure (BBOS) — Step by Step

The confirmation signal is called a **Bullish Break of Structure (BBOS)**. It is the core mechanism and requires no indicators — just reading price action.

### What to Look For

When you drop to the daily chart after your monthly support alert triggers, you will see a downtrend: lower highs and lower lows as price pulled back to your monthly support zone. This is expected.

You are waiting for one specific event: **a break above the previous lower high on the daily chart.**

When price breaks above the most recent lower high, it creates a **higher high** — the first sign that the daily downtrend is ending. Combined with the monthly uptrend and price sitting at monthly support, this daily higher high is your signal to enter.

### The Precise Rules

1. Identify the most recent **lower high** on the daily chart
2. The level is defined by the **wick** (the highest point the candle reached, including the shadow) — not the body close
3. Wait for price to break above that wick level with **two consecutive daily candle closes** above it
4. A single candle wick poking above the level does not count — you need **closes**, not wicks, to confirm the break is real
5. Two candle closes above the level = your BBOS signal

Why two candle closes? One candle above the level can be a false breakout — a liquidity grab that immediately reverses. Two candles closing above means there is genuine buying interest sustaining price above the level. This single filter eliminates many false entries.

### The Multi-Candle Lower High Requirement

A valid lower high requires multiple candles clustering around the same price zone, not just a single-candle spike. A one-candle spike above previous price — even a large one — does not qualify as a meaningful lower high because it may represent a brief overextension rather than a genuine resistance zone.

From Phil's coaching session: "A strong lower high typically shows two or more candles clustering around the same price zone, a clear rejection of higher prices, and the level should be visible and distinct on the daily chart without zooming in excessively."

## Two Entry Methods

### Method 1: Breakout Entry

Enter as soon as the second daily candle closes above the previous lower high. This gives you an earlier entry and potentially a better price.

**Risk:** Higher chance of catching a false breakout if the second candle barely closes above the level. Best used when momentum is clearly strong and the breakout is clean.

### Method 2: Break and Retest (Preferred)

After the two-candle BBOS confirmation, wait for price to pull back toward the broken level. Previous resistance (the lower high) now acts as support. Enter when price bounces off this new support.

**Why this is preferred:**
- Cleaner stop loss placement — stop goes just below the retest level
- Better risk-to-reward — entry is closer to support, target is unchanged
- Higher probability — the bounce confirms the market genuinely respects the level

**Important:** If the retest never comes and price just keeps going, do not enter. You missed the trade. There is absolutely no harm in skipping. "Less is always more — if you don't have a good, maybe even perfect, setup, simply don't enter."

**Warning signal:** If price pulls back to the level and slices straight through without bouncing, the level has failed. Do not enter. Step back, wait for a new lower high to form, and repeat the process.

## Monthly Support and Daily Noise — The Critical Rule

This is one of the most important concepts in Phil's confirmation framework, and one that trips up many students:

**Monthly support is only invalidated by monthly candle closes below the level.**

If the daily chart shows price dropping below monthly support for 1, 2, 5, or even 10 candles — that is daily noise. You do not care. What happens on the daily timeframe stays on the daily timeframe. The support was identified on the monthly; it can only be invalidated on the monthly.

This means your stop loss (placed below monthly support with breathing room) will not be triggered by a brief daily pierce. Only a genuine monthly candle close below the level warrants closing the position.

## Volume Confirmation

Phil does not use volume as a primary indicator. His framework is price-action only — naked chart, no indicators. That said, volume is occasionally referenced in the live sessions as a secondary observation: a breakout accompanied by significant volume is stronger than a breakout on thin volume. But it is not a required condition for a valid BBOS.

---

## False Signals and How to Filter Them

The two main false signal filters in Phil's system:

**1. Two candle closes rule.** A single candle poking above the lower high wick is explicitly not confirmation. Require two daily candle closes above the level. This alone filters the majority of false breakouts.

**2. Retest entry.** By waiting for the retest of the broken level rather than entering on the immediate breakout, you add a second layer of confirmation. The retest shows the market respects the new support level, not just that it briefly exceeded it.

**If both methods fail** (two candles closed but no retest, and you entered on the breakout — then price reverses): your stop loss, placed below monthly support, protects you. The system has fail-safes at every layer.

## The Bearish Break of Structure (for Exit Timing)

Phil only takes long positions, so the bearish BOS is not used for entries. However, it is used as an **exit signal** when there is no resistance level above price.

When price is in open air (e.g., after breaking an all-time high with no clear resistance above), stay in the trade as long as the daily shows higher highs and higher lows. When the daily shifts to a downtrend — specifically, when price breaks **below the previous higher low** on the daily chart, creating a lower low — that is your exit signal. Take partials and begin closing the position.

## Quick Reference: BBOS Checklist

1. Monthly alert triggered — price is at monthly support
2. Drop to daily chart
3. Confirm daily is in a downtrend (lower highs, lower lows)
4. Identify the most recent lower high (use the wick, not the body)
5. Wait for two daily candle closes **above** that wick level
6. Option A: Enter on the second candle close
7. Option B (preferred): Wait for a retest of the broken level, enter on the bounce
8. If retest fails (price slices through) → no entry, reset and wait
