## How Marlin Manages the 2026 Portfolio

### Entry Protocol
- All new positions entered as **market-on-open orders** on the day announced
- **Equal weight** — every position starts at the same dollar amount
- No DCAing on initial entries (full position at once)
- New buys announced via email notification

### Position Sizing in 2026 Portfolio
- Originally: 37 positions at 2.7% each = 100% deployed
- After culling: ~16 positions at ~4-5% each
- When going risk-off: raise cash, reduce to 40% long
- When deploying aggressively (like April 8, 2026 ceasefire): deploy ~60% of capital at once

### Selling Rules
1. Stock enters Stage 3 or Stage 4 — exit (no exceptions)
2. "Laggard" test: if a stock cannot get out of its own way and the market does not view it as a leader, cut it (e.g., exited CRWV at -11%, exited AMPX at -24%)
3. Thesis break: fundamentals deteriorate (guidance cuts, collapsing margins)
4. Target reached: scale out in tranches, raise trailing stop on remainder

### Portfolio Dashboard
Marlin runs a live portfolio dashboard that updates every 15 minutes automatically. All current positions, entry prices, and % gains/losses are tracked in real-time.

## The Feb-March 2026 Selloff (How Marlin Navigated It)

This was a key test of the portfolio management system:
- Market action in Feb 2026 reminded Marlin more of 2022 than 2025
- Added **short positions** as hedges for longs (Jan 2027 puts or shorting stock directly)
- Shorts: QUBT, NNE, ACHR (speculative names that had already topped)
- Went to majority cash by late March 2026
- Rebuilt positions aggressively on April 8, 2026 ceasefire catalyst
- Result: "We sidestepped most of the -13% Nasdaq drawdown, preserved our YTD gains, and hopped right back in when the conditions changed"
