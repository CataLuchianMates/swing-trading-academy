"In my view, there is little doubt that we are currently in another equity market Bubble (the AI Bubble) in 2026. Here at Marlin Capital, we have embraced the AI Bubble since May, and this thesis has helped us to key in on some of the big winners of this supercycle before anyone else."

AI is the current version. The bull case: these bubbles often take 5+ years to form and run. We are still in the building phase.

## Historical Bubble Comparisons

Marlin frequently uses the **Dot Com Bubble** and **Covid Bubble** as comparators:

### How Bubbles Top
The pattern is consistent: **the most speculative stocks top first**, then weakness spreads, and the S&P 500 tops last.

- **Dot Com Bubble:** Pre-revenue SPACs/Pets.com peaked months before the S&P 500 topped in March 2000. By the time the SPX topped, 40%+ of SPX was already at 52-week lows.
- **Covid Bubble:** ZM peaked 14 months before SPX. ARKK peaked 11 months before. By January 2022 SPX top, 66% of Nasdaq was already in a bear market (-50% for 35% of NDX).

### 2025-2026 Parallel
The most speculative stocks (OKLO, IONQ, QUBT, NNE) began entering "climax top" patterns in October 2025. These are down 60%+ from highs. This follows the historical pattern: **the S&P 500 typically tops 8-14 months after the most speculative names begin cracking** → suggests potential SPX cycle top: Q4 2026 or Q1 2027.

## Reading Market Conditions

### Breadth is Everything
Marlin watches market breadth obsessively:
- Leaders breaking out across many sectors simultaneously = true bull market
- Only a few names catching a bid while most churn = low quality rally, be cautious
- 52-week highs list expanding = healthy market
- 52-week lows expanding while the index is still high = deteriorating breadth, danger signal

### Key Macro Inputs Marlin Tracks
- **10-year Treasury yields:** Rising yields = headwind for multiples. 4.59% 10Y = caution territory
- **Fed policy:** "Don't Fight the Fed" is a core principle. Fed easing = tailwind
- **EPS estimates:** Revisions matter more than current valuations. Estimates being revised UP while positioning is low = bullish divergence
- **Vol Control / Systematic flows:** Marlin tracks Nomura's Vol Control model for systematic buying windows (e.g., projected $114B of buying in Jul 2025 = 100% hit rate historically)
- **Goldman Momentum factor:** Surging >25% over 3 months = extreme momentum, historically followed by 1-3 months of consolidation

### The Valuation Framework
"Valuations do not matter on the way up, and they do not matter on the way down. During a Bull Market, valuations can always go higher. During a Bear Market, they can always go lower."

Markets swing from "overvalued" to "undervalued" like a pendulum — they do not stop at "fair value." Valuations tell you WHERE we are, not WHERE we are going.

## Presidential / Election Cycle

- Midterm years are worst of the 4-year presidential cycle (SPX up only 53%, avg +4.6%)
- Midterms under a 2nd term president slightly better (+8.8% avg)
- Historical pattern: markets bottom just before midterm elections (uncertainty clears)
- Last 2 midterm years (2022, 2018) = last 2 negative SPX years

## Headline vs. Chart Principle

**"It is not the news that matters. It is the market's reaction to the news."**

When scary macro headlines persist but leaders keep breaking out — the charts are right, not the headlines. During the Iran war in 2026:
- Trump's "an entire civilization will die tonight" threat → SPX closed green
- Failed peace talks → market started to shrug off Iran headlines
- "Headline Fatigue" = when markets stop reacting negatively to bad news, the underlying trend is bullish

"The scary Macro headlines are telling me one story right now. But the charts (and Micro) are telling me a completely different one."
