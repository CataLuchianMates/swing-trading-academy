# David Marlin's Trading System

## Core Philosophy

**Price action is the ultimate truth.** Charts tell the story that headlines obscure.

> "I follow the charts. I do not trade headlines. I trade charts. When the charts are telling me to buy, I buy. When they are telling me to sell, I sell."

**The hybrid framework:** Fundamentals for stock picking and idea generation. Technicals — exclusively — for entries and exits. *"To be very clear, my exit sales are based off technical analysis."* — Whirlwind, Feb 2026

> "My Big Picture approach is a combination strategy of Fundamentals + Technicals. I use primarily fundamental analysis for stock picking & idea generation, but solely technical analysis for exits." — Whirlwind, Feb 2026

**The stock is not the company.**

> "The stock is not the company. And the company is not the stock." — Jeff Bezos (quoted frequently by Marlin)

A great company can be a terrible stock to hold if the price action is deteriorating. William O'Neil built his legendary trading career around this principle: *there is no such thing as a good stock — only a stock that goes up.*

## The Three Portfolio System

Marlin runs three distinct strategies simultaneously. Each has a completely different timeframe, risk profile, and approach.

### Portfolio 1 — The Annual Portfolio (e.g., "2026 Portfolio")

**Timeframe:** ~1 year (launched each January, managed through year-end)

**Purpose:** Top picks for the year. Long-term fundamental conviction + technical Stage 2 confirmation. Not "set it and forget it" — each name is managed individually.

**Framework:** Every holding must satisfy both conditions:
1. Strong accelerating fundamentals (beat + raise quarters, strong book-to-bill, expanding margins)
2. Confirmed Stage 2 uptrend — Golden Cross in place, price above rising moving averages

**Sell rule (explicit):** Any stock believed to have entered (or be entering) its Stage 4 downtrend gets sold. *"The technicals should confirm the fundamental story. When the technicals are breaking down, I sell, regardless of how strong the fundamentals still look."*

**Position sizing:** Equal weight entries at ~4–5% per position. Diversification is critical in late-cycle markets. Running 37 names = ~2.7% per position; smaller portfolios = 4–5%.

**When to reduce:** Late-cycle or topping behavior → sell anything showing Stage 3/4 signs. Move to cash. Hold only confirmed leaders.

---

### Portfolio 2 — Active Trading Portfolio (Swing)

**Timeframe:** 1 day to ~3 months

**Purpose:** Capitalize on the sharpest momentum moves in the market. Disciplined risk management on every trade.

**Market environment dependency:**
- **"Easy Dollar" environment** (trending, bullish) → breakouts work, momentum runs big → size up, press winners, let positions run
- **"Hard Penny" environment** (choppy, treacherous) → breakouts fail, even A+ setups fail → go to cash, wait for better conditions

*"In the Stock Market, you cannot always be playing offense. Not every single day. Not every single week. Not every single month. Not even every single quarter."* — Wild Times, Feb 2026

> "Don't try to play the market all the time… Money cannot consistently be made trading every day or every week during the year." — Jesse Livermore (quoted by Marlin)

**When to be active:** April–October (historically the meat of bull trends). Be passive during pullback/chop phases.

---

### Portfolio 3 — SMID Cap Concentrated Portfolio

**Timeframe:** Multi-month concentrated bets (typically 6–12 months)

**Purpose:** 4–6 high-conviction small/mid-cap names in a single leading theme. Released when the right market environment and theme alignment is present — never released just to release one.

**Historical results:** 2024 Small Cap 6: +99%. 2025 SMID Cap 6: +64%. Both blew the SPX out of the water.

**Entry condition:** Must believe we are setting up for a period of SMID Cap outperformance. Thematic conviction + right market conditions required.

## Stage Analysis Framework

Marlin uses Stan Weinstein's Stage Analysis as his primary framework for managing positions:

| Stage | Condition | Action |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Stage 1 (Base/Accumulation)** | Stock is flat, basing | Wait. Do not buy yet. |
| **Stage 2 (Uptrend)** | Stock above rising moving averages | The ONLY stage to be long. |
| **Stage 3 (Topping)** | Losing momentum, churning at highs | Begin reducing or exiting. |
| **Stage 4 (Downtrend)** | Below falling moving averages | Exit immediately. No exceptions. |

> "I wouldn't call holding stocks through stage 4 downtrends/bear cycles 'conviction'. I would call it poor risk management." — Marlin

**Critical insight:** Stage transitions can happen in an instant. You must be ready to act. In a high-dispersion market, individual stocks can stay in Stage 2 while the index falls. But when the "final leaders" start breaking down, the high-dispersion environment is ending.

**Stage 4 expectations:** Expect 50–80%+ downside in all Stage 4 names. Speculative/high-beta names: -80%+ likely. Mega-cap secular leaders (NVDA, AAPL, META): -50–80% during their bear cycles (it happens every cycle, without exception).

## The EMA Framework

Marlin uses a **three-tier EMA stack** to define trend direction at both the index level and individual stock level. This framework was laid out explicitly in his foundational 2022 articles and is assumed knowledge in all subsequent writing.

### Trend Definition

| Indicator | Role |
|-----------|------|
| **21 EMA** | Short-term trend |
| **60 EMA** | Medium-term trend |
| **200 SMA** | Long-term trend |

**Bullish — Golden Cross (Stage 2 Confirmed):**
`Price > 21 EMA > 60 EMA > 200 SMA` — all three in alignment pointing up.

**Bearish — Death Cross (Stage 4):**
`Price < 21 EMA < 60 EMA < 200 SMA` — medium and long-term trend both pointing down. *"There were no death crosses in the Mag 7 during the August correction: 21 EMA < 60 EMA < 200 SMA."* — Do or Die Time, Mar 2025

> "The Bullish Golden Cross is a technical indicator that signals the downtrend has ended, and we have officially entered a technical uptrend on all three major time frames. In the prior 7 major bear markets dating back to 1973, there are no examples of an SPX golden cross resulting in new lows for the market." — Bear Market Bottom Checklist, Sep 2022

**Key rule:** In a bear market, counter-trend rallies do NOT flip the Golden Cross. The 60 EMA never crosses above the 200 SMA during a bear market bounce — only after the actual bottom.

### EMA Hierarchy for Intraday/Swing Levels (Strong Uptrends)

Once a stock is confirmed in Stage 2, Marlin uses shorter EMAs for entries and managing risk within the trend:

| EMA | Role in uptrend |
|-----|-----------------|
| **5 EMA** | Ultra-short term (hourly context) |
| **8 EMA** | Very short-term daily pullback support. A month-long ride of the 8 EMA = very strong uptrend. Breaking below 8 EMA for first time = watch for dip. |
| **15 EMA** | Primary pullback support in strong uptrends. Pullbacks here are buyable entries. |
| **21 EMA** | Secondary pullback support. Closing below 21 EMA = exit signal for active swing trades. |
| **60 EMA** | Deeper pullback support. Significant pullback to 60 EMA with a reversal candle (hammer) = buyable if stock is still a leader. |
| **200 SMA** | Critical market regime line. Reclaiming it = meaningful character shift. Losing it = danger. |

**Support hierarchy during a pullback:** 8 EMA → 21 EMA (~3.7% dip) → 60 EMA (~4.8% dip) → unfilled gap → 200 SMA. Each level is a potential entry for adding, not a reason to panic.

**Key signal:** *"What I love most about VIAV is that it never closed below its 21 EMA despite a -13% Nasdaq pullback."* — A stock refusing to close below its 21 EMA during an index pullback is a leading stock.

**Bollinger Bands:** Closing outside the upper BB with a spinning top candle = exhaustion/climax signal. When this appears across all major indices simultaneously, a pause/pullback is imminent.

## Entry Signals

### For the Annual Portfolio (Long-Term)
- Stock breaking out of a multi-month base on volume
- Fundamental catalyst (earnings beat + guidance raise) followed by constructive price action
- Golden Cross confirmed (Stage 2 entry)
- All positions entered at equal weight
- **Never chase extended stocks** — wait for pullbacks to 15 EMA or 21 EMA

### For the Active Trading Portfolio (Swing)
**Bread-and-butter setup: Episodic Pivots**
An earnings or news catalyst gap up followed by a tight consolidation (bull flag). This is Kristjan Kullamägi's term and Marlin's highest-conviction setup.

> "My bread and butter setup is the earnings/news catalyst gapper. Kristjan Kullamagi calls these 'Episodic Pivots' (and it's his favorite setup as well)." — The Playbook, Apr 2026

**Key setups:**
- **Episodic Pivots / Post-earnings gappers:** Day 1 catalyst gap + consolidation = high-probability setup. Buy on Day 2–3 (not Day 1 chasing).
- **Post-earnings consolidations:** Stock holds gains after blowout quarter → breakout from the consolidation
- **High tight bull flag:** Unfilled gap + tight consolidation above = one of the most powerful patterns
- **IPO base breakouts:** Recent IPOs breaking out of long IPO bases — where some of the market's largest gains have historically come from (Bill O'Neil principle)
- **Entry timing:** Gap down into support on a pullback is often the ideal entry, not chasing the initial move

**When not to enter:** In a "Hard Penny" (choppy) environment, skip even A+ setups. Wait for the market to confirm it's trending.

### For Short Positions
**Short setup:** Stage 4 downtrend confirmed + 3-day bounce to declining key EMA (21 EMA or 60 EMA acting as resistance).

> "My preferred method of shorting stocks is to enter on 3-day bounces to key moving averages (which should act as firm resistance). You do NOT want to be entering short on big red candles because that puts you at much higher risk of being caught in the dead cat bounce/short squeeze. Instead, you want to be entering short DURING these dead cat bounces, so that you can be covering your shorts on big red candles/gap downs." — Wild Times, Feb 2026 / Grinch Selloff, Dec 2025

**Optimal entry:** Day 3 of a bounce into declining 21 EMA = ideal short entry. Allows you to enter at resistance following a multi-day squeeze. The risk/reward is in your favor.

**Short target:** 50–80% decline for speculative names. Apply the Minervini 50-80 Rule.

## Exit Rules

### Annual Portfolio
- Exit when stock enters Stage 3 (topping behavior) or Stage 4 (downtrend beginning)
- No hard stop losses — exits based on technical character change
- Manage each name individually — not all stocks top at the same time in a late-cycle market
- The goal is to capture the majority of the move, not sell at the very top

> "How do you lock in your gains when you find yourself with a big winner? In my experience it is often best to sell a big winner while it is still advancing and looks strong. The common fear is 'what if it goes up more without me?' That may happen, but remember, your goal is not to sell at the very top price. That's almost impossible to do. Rather, you just want to participate in the majority of the move." — William O'Neil (quoted by Marlin)

### Active Trading Portfolio
- Scale out in **tranches (quarters)**: sell 1/4 at first target, 1/4 at second target, hold remaining 1/2 with trailing stop
- **Trailing stop:** Move stop up as stock advances (e.g., "trailing stop below $160 on the rest")
- **Exit trigger:** Stock closes below 21 EMA = exit signal for swing trades
- **Negative news = immediate exit candidate.** *"When you are trading a stock based off momentum, negative news is an expectation breaker. It completely changes said momentum, and therefore completely changes the entire idea of the trade."*
- **Cut losers fast:** Max loss typically -10–15% (in strong markets, up to -15% if target is 2x risk). Never let a loss grow past -15% on a swing trade.

> "If you can't stick to a strategy and let other strategies have their turn while your strategy is in limbo, you don't have a strategy... you have an addiction." — Mark Minervini (quoted by Marlin)

## Position Sizing

### Annual Portfolio
- Equal weight entries at ~4–5% per position
- Diversification is critical in late-cycle markets — do not concentrate heavily in 1–2 names
- Running a 37-name portfolio = ~2.7% per name; tighter portfolios = 4–5%

### Active Trading Portfolio — Equity Trades
- Max loss per trade: typically **-10–15%** (in strong markets can stretch to -15% with 2:1 reward-to-risk minimum)
- **The math:** Risk $1,000 on a $100k account with a $9.50 stop below entry:
  - $1,000 / $9.50 = 105 shares
  - 105 shares × $212 = $22,263 position size (22% of account, risking 1%)
- Adjust risk based on market environment and P&L: risk more on winning streaks, less on losing streaks

### Active Trading Portfolio — Options Trades
- **"Size for 0":** Always size options positions small enough that a total loss (–100%) has the same impact as a –5% stock trade
- Options are "lotto tickets" — when they fail, move on without emotion
- Use near-term calls for active trades (e.g., June calls), longer-term puts (Jan 2027) for shorts

## Risk Management Philosophy

> "It's not whether you're right or wrong that's important, but how much money you make when you're right and how much you lose when you're wrong." — Stanley Druckenmiller (quoted by Marlin)

**Core principle:** Making more when right than you lose when wrong. This is why avoiding big losses is emphasized above all.

**Loss psychology:** Taking a loss at -11% is manageable. A loss at -24% is already too late. *"Cut it at -11%. Don't let it become -24%."*

**Emotional discipline:** No emotions when cutting losers. *"If you are confident in your abilities, you should feel no emotion taking a loss."* A trader who takes losses without blinking trusts their ability to find the next winning trade.

**Track record reality:** Every great trader is wrong. A lot. The GOATs — Druckenmiller, Soros, PTJ, Minervini, Kullamägi — are often wrong. The difference is the size of wins vs. losses.

## The Minervini 50-80 Rule

Referenced repeatedly as a core risk management anchor:

> "When a secular leader makes a major top, there's a 50% chance it will drop 80% and an 80% chance it will drop 50%." — Mark Minervini

**Application:** Every secular bull market leader — NVDA, AAPL, GOOGL, META, Bitcoin — has suffered -50–80% drawdowns during bear cycles. Small/mid-cap speculative names typically decline -80%+. **Fundamental analysis does not matter in bear markets.** *"There is no escaping the selling."*

**Key insight:** The company is NOT the stock. A stock's fundamentals can still be improving while it declines -50% during a bear cycle. That's normal. Eventually the best fundamental names recover during the next bull cycle — after the decline.

## When to Go to Cash

Jesse Livermore principle: *"There's a time to go long, a time to go short, and a time to go fishing."*

**Triggers to reduce/go to cash:**
- Market enters choppy, low-volume, directionless phase
- No "fat pitches" visible in the market (shortage of A+ setups)
- Most leading stocks showing Stage 3/4 behavior
- Major indices closing below 60 EMA or 200 SMA

**Historical examples:**
- 100% cash in Active Trading Portfolio during Oct 2025–Mar 2026 choppy/low-volume period
- 60% cash in 2026 Portfolio during the Iran war selloff (Oct–Mar period)

**Rule:** When the market is choppy and not trending, the correct move is to wait, not force trades. *"The market does not reward you with any extra money for taking on a tougher challenge."*

> "There are times to make money. There are times not to lose money. And if you don't know the difference, you're going to get killed." — Stanley Druckenmiller

**Wait for -10% corrections** to deploy new capital, not -3% dips. Standard market corrections (-10%) occur in 63%+ of years. Don't buy the dip too early.

## Market Health Indicators

**What tells you the market is healthy (Bull phase):**
- Golden Cross on the daily SPX chart (21 EMA > 60 EMA > 200 SMA)
- Broad market breadth: high % of stocks above their 200-day MAs
- NYSE Net Highs outpacing New Lows
- Leaders holding their 21 EMA during index pullbacks
- Multiple breakouts confirming simultaneously ("risk on" across sectors)
- Small Caps participating (IWM breakout = broad bull confirmation)

**What tells you the market is deteriorating (late cycle/Bear incoming):**
- Speculative leaders forming Climax Tops and entering Stage 4
- Breadth deteriorating (more and more stocks below 200 MA)
- Hindenburg Omen signals clustering
- High SPX dispersion (stocks going in wildly different directions)
- Death crosses appearing in sector ETFs and leading stocks
- Former leaders failing to recover 21 EMA after pullbacks

**The "final leaders" signal:** When the last remaining leaders (the most-held, best-quality names) start breaking down — that signals the high-dispersion market is ending and a correlated selloff is beginning.

---

## What "Leaders" Look Like

Leaders are the stocks that confirm a new bull phase before the index does:

- Breaking out to new highs while the index is still recovering
- Not closing below key EMAs during an index pullback
- Making consecutive unfilled gaps after earnings (high tight bull flag)
- Volume confirmation on breakouts
- Book-to-bill ratios > 1.0 and accelerating in fundamental reports
- "Surfing" the 21 EMA (or 8 EMA) higher for weeks/months = ideal uptrend

When many leaders simultaneously break out, it signals broad "risk on" — not just a few names catching a bid.

**Leaders vs. laggards in pullbacks:** During a -9% NDX pullback, leaders may never even test their 60 EMA. Laggards will plunge through their 200 SMA. The spread between them is the signal.

## Bear Market Bottom Checklist

Marlin's 5-indicator checklist for confirming a genuine bear market bottom (from 2022 foundational article). In the prior 6 bear markets back to 1982, a minimum of 4/5 had triggered before the bottom.

1. **Fed Cutting Rates** — In the past 7 recessions dating back to 1971, equities never bottomed before the Fed began cutting rates
2. **Unemployment Spikes** — Has risen at least +2% in all official recessions (excluding 1987)
3. **2/10 Yield Curve Steepens** — In each of the last 6 bear markets, the curve had steepened before the market bottomed
4. **2Y Yield Peaks** — S&P 500 did not bottom in any of the prior 6 bear markets until the 2Y yield had peaked for the cycle
5. **US PMI Bottoms** — Manufacturing PMI improved vs. 12-month lows in 6 of the last 7 bear markets

**Additional indicator:** Equity Risk Premium rises 75 BPs before the market bottoms (seen in all 7 prior bear market bottoms).

**Additional rule:** Stocks have never bottomed before a recession officially starts. In recessionary bear markets, equities historically bottom when the recession is about halfway through.

> "Get a new hobby, get another dog, have another kid... Go study. Build your foundation for future success. So you can capitalize on the next opportunities when they come. When your edge is present." — Kristjan Kullamägi

**Core lessons:**
- Wait for the fat pitch (Buffett). Never force trades.
