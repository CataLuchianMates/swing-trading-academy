# David Marlin's Trading System

## Core Philosophy

**Price action is the ultimate truth.** Charts tell the story that headlines obscure. When countless stocks — particularly the strongest fundamental names — are all breaking out to new highs at the same time, Marlin is long. Full stop.

"I follow the charts. I do not trade headlines. I trade charts. When the charts are telling me to buy, I buy. When they are telling me to sell, I sell."

## Stage Analysis Framework

Marlin uses Stan Weinstein's Stage Analysis as his primary framework for managing positions in the long-term portfolio:

- **Stage 1 (Base/Accumulation):** Stock is flat, basing. Wait. Do not buy yet.
- **Stage 2 (Uptrend):** Stock is in an uptrend above rising moving averages. This is the ONLY stage to be long.
- **Stage 3 (Topping):** Stock is losing momentum, churning at highs. Begin reducing or exiting.
- **Stage 4 (Downtrend):** Stock is below falling moving averages. Exit. No exceptions.

"I wouldn't call holding stocks through stage 4 downtrends/bear cycles 'conviction'. I would call it poor risk management."

## Key Technical Levels

- **15 EMA (daily):** Primary support level during uptrends. Pullbacks to 15 EMA in an uptrend are buyable entries. If the index/stock loses the 15 EMA on a closing basis, caution is warranted.
- **21 EMA (daily):** Secondary support. If a stock closes below its 21 EMA, it's often an exit signal for active trades (e.g., exited CRDO when it closed below 21 EMA for the first time after a catalyst gap up).
- **Bollinger Bands:** Closing outside the upper Bollinger Band with a spinning top candle = exhaustion signal. When this happens across all major indices simultaneously, a pause/pullback is imminent.
- **200-day SMA:** Key level for the indices. Reclaiming the 200 SMA signals a meaningful shift.

## Entry Signals

### For the 2026 Portfolio (Long-Term)
- Stock breaking out of a multi-month base on volume
- Fundamental catalyst (earnings beat + guidance raise) followed by constructive price action
- All positions entered as market-on-open orders at equal weight
- Never chase extended stocks — wait for pullbacks to 15 EMA

### For the Active Trading Portfolio (Swing)
- **Episodic pivots:** Catalyst gap up + tight consolidation = high probability setup
- **Post-earnings consolidations:** Stock holds gains after a blowout quarter → breakout from the consolidation
- **High tight bull flag:** Unfilled gap + tight consolidation = one of the most powerful patterns
- Entry timing: gap down into support on a pullback is often the ideal entry, not chasing the initial move

## Exit Rules

### Long-Term Portfolio
- Exit when stock enters Stage 3/4 (signs of topping behavior or downtrend beginning)
- No hard stop losses — exits are based on technical character change
- Manage each name individually — not all stocks top at the same time in a late-cycle market

### Active Trading Portfolio
- Scale out in **tranches (quarters)**: sell 1/4 at first target, 1/4 at second target, hold remaining 1/2 with trailing stop
- Trailing stop: move stop up as stock advances (e.g., "trailing stop below $160 on the rest")
- Exit if stock closes below a key moving average (21 EMA for swing trades)
- Cut losers fast: if a stock "cannot get out of its own way" and the market does not view it as a leader, exit at a manageable loss (-11% is manageable, -24% is already too late)

## Position Sizing

### Long-Term Portfolio
- Equal weight entries at ~4-5% per position (started at 2.7% when running 37 names, 4-5% for smaller portfolios)
- Diversification is critical in a late-cycle market — do not concentrate heavily in 1-2 names

### Active Trading Portfolio
- Risk 1-3% of account per trade (typical: 1%)
- **The math:** If you risk $1,000 on a $100k account with a $9.50 stop below entry:
  - $1,000 / $9.50 = 105 shares
  - 105 shares × $212 = $22,263 position size (22% of account, risking 1%)
- Adjust risk based on market environment and your P&L (risk more on winning streaks, less on losing streaks)

## Options Approach

- **"Size for 0":** Always size options positions small enough that a total loss (-100%) has the same impact as a -5% stock trade
- Use near-term calls for active trades (e.g., June calls), longer-term puts (Jan 2027) for shorts
- Options are "lotto tickets" — when they fail, move on without emotion

## When to Go to Cash

Jesse Livermore principle: "There's a time to go long, a time to go short, and a time to go fishing."

- Marlin went 100% cash in the Active Trading Portfolio during the Oct 2025 – Mar 2026 choppy/low volume period
- He went to 60% cash in the 2026 Portfolio during the Iran war selloff (Oct–Mar period)
- When the market is choppy and not trending, the correct move is to wait, not force trades
- Goal: be very active during the meat of trends (April–October 2025), passive during pullback/chop phases

## What "Leaders" Look Like

Leaders are the stocks that confirm a new bull phase before the index does:
- Breaking out to new highs while the index is still recovering
- Not closing below key EMAs during an index pullback (e.g., VIAV never closed below 21 EMA during a -13% Nasdaq drawdown)
- Making consecutive unfilled gaps after earnings (high tight bull flag)
- Volume confirmation on breakouts
- Book-to-bill ratios > 1.0 and accelerating in fundamental reports

When many leaders simultaneously break out, it signals broad "risk on" — not just a few names catching a bid.
