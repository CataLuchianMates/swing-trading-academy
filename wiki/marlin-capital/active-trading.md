# Active Trading Portfolio — Marlin Capital

## Overview

The Active Trading Portfolio is Marlin's swing trading vehicle. Unlike the 2026 Portfolio (12+ month holds), active trades are catalyst-driven, typically held days to weeks, with entries and exits posted in real-time in the Marlin Capital Live Trading Room (Substack chat).

"I'm both an investor and a swing trader. That's why I have multiple different portfolios for the different strategies I employ. With that said, my biggest gains have come from buying and holding stocks (aka investing)."

## What Marlin Looks For in Active Trades

### The Setup Hierarchy (Best to Acceptable)

1. **Episodic Pivot (Highest Conviction):** A catalyst (earnings, deal announcement, guidance raise) causes a stock to gap up significantly. The key is what happens NEXT — if the stock holds the gap and consolidates tightly for a few days, it's setting up for the next leg.

2. **High Tight Bull Flag:** Stock gaps up (often on earnings), consolidates in a tight range (often 2-5% wide) for 1-3 weeks. Unfilled gaps below are particularly bullish. Breakout above the flag is the entry.

3. **Post-Earnings Consolidation Breakout:** After a blowout earnings report, stock consolidates. Once it breaks out of the consolidation range, that's the entry.

4. **Base Breakout:** Stock that has been building a multi-month or multi-year base (defined price range) breaks out on volume. The longer the base, the bigger the potential move.

### The Golden Formula
"Fundamentals + technicals. That is the golden formula at Marlin Capital."

The best active trade setups combine:
- A strong fundamental catalyst (massive earnings beat, huge deal, guidance raise)
- A clean technical setup (tight consolidation after the catalyst, breakout from a base)
- A clear price target derived from the technical setup

## Entry Logistics

- **Entry timing:** Market-on-open orders for portfolio buys
- **For "Top 5" monthly ideas:** These are active trading ideas tracked in the Live Trading Room
- **Scale in:** For very high conviction setups, Marlin occasionally adds to a winner as it confirms
- **Never chase:** If a stock is extended from its base/EMA, wait for the pullback. "I am not chasing early this week."

## Exit / Scaling Rules

Marlin scales out of active trades in **tranches** as targets are hit:

**CRDO Trade Example (2026):**
- Entered at $162.70
- Sold 1/4 at $171
- Sold 1/4 at $190
- Exited final 50% at $173.44 (closed below 21 EMA → sell signal)
- Adjusted overall gain: +8.8%

**Trailing Stop Management:**
- After selling initial tranches, move trailing stop up to protect remaining position
- "Trailing stop will remain below $160 on the rest" (TTMI example)
- Closing stop (end of day, not intraday) to avoid being shaken out by intraday volatility

**Cutting Losers:**
- If a trade doesn't work, exit at a manageable loss
- Never average down on a losing active trade
- "Our YSS trade failed and our calls are basically at 0 now. As I said when we entered this trade, I always size for 0 on all options trades... On to the next."

## Options in the Active Portfolio

- Options are used for asymmetric bets on highest conviction setups
- **"Size for 0" principle:** Size the position small enough that a -100% loss = same impact as a -5% stock trade
- Typical approach: near-dated calls (1-3 months out) on breakout setups
- Example: "I'm looking at the June calls" on YSS
- For shorts: Jan 2027 puts or shorting the stock directly

## When Active Trading Stops

The most critical skill: knowing when NOT to trade.

During Oct 2025 – March 2026, Marlin was 100% cash in the Active Portfolio. Reasons:
- Choppy, low-volume tape
- SPX flat for 2 months, QQQ down -3% over 2 months
- No setups meeting his criteria

"When this is the case, I have no problem simply waiting it out. My goal is to be very active during the meat of the trends (like April-October), and passive during the choppy/pullback phases (like October-[March])."
