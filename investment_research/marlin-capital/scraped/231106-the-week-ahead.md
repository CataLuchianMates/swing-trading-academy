# The Week Ahead

US stocks bounced big last week with the S&P 500 closing above its 21 and 60 EMAs for the first time since early September.

What was behind the big rally in stocks last week?

10 YR Yields and Short Squeeze Dynamics.

The rally was catalyzed by a substantial decline in 10-year yields.  The US 10 YR Treasury Yield declined from a high of 4.91% to a low of 4.49% by the end of the week.  This 5-day decline stands out as one of the largest in recent memory, acting as the key catalyst behind the market surge.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Cb6H!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe7e1bc84-e3cb-4ea0-8f52-8d1f8d788e53_1290x1052.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Cb6H!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe7e1bc84-e3cb-4ea0-8f52-8d1f8d788e53_1290x1052.jpeg)

In “The Key Story”, I discussed how the recent rise in 10 YR Yields was the main reason behind the market weakness we saw over the past couple of months.  However, instead of breaking out above 5%, 10 YR yields declined precipitously following last week’s Fed meeting.  This decline gave a “risk-on” signal to a market that has been closely watching every move in yields (and had already reached technically oversold status).

The other key factor behind last week’s rally was positioning.  Hedge Funds had been aggressively pressing shorts for 3 straight months during the recent market downturn.  In cumulative notional terms, the combined net selling from August to October across global equities was the 2nd largest over any 3-month period in the past 10 years (only Q1 ’22 was larger) and ranks in the 99th percentile.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VQub!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1b4033d0-f582-46c6-95e4-cbaa030a70ad_1218x538.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VQub!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1b4033d0-f582-46c6-95e4-cbaa030a70ad_1218x538.jpeg)

Data wise, we saw economic numbers soften over the past week.  US October ISM fell to 51.8 from 53.6, surprising to the downside versus estimates of 53.  This was followed by Friday’s labor/wages data, with a headline NFP miss and prior down revision of over 100k.  Unemployment also surprised to the upside at 3.9% versus estimates of 3.8%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8am2!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2774ec44-bb67-473f-92ec-e7990af5ca86_477x329.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8am2!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2774ec44-bb67-473f-92ec-e7990af5ca86_477x329.jpeg)

With last week’s number, the Unemployment Rate is now 50bps higher than its cycle low.  When the 3-month average rises 50bps from its cycle low, it will trigger the Sahm Rule recession indicator.  The Sahm Rule has triggered in/during the past 8 recessions dating back to 1960.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rpWY!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe6aed756-1c71-42ba-8f36-25fb0240a423_2048x1148.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rpWY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe6aed756-1c71-42ba-8f36-25fb0240a423_2048x1148.jpeg)

Earnings wise, we are now through the heaviest part of earning season with 80% (~450) of the S&P 500 having reported. 59% of companies have beat street estimates by >1SD. Only 8% of companies have missed estimates by >1SD.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vdRZ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F23d30154-decb-4d36-a4d6-9198657cc088_1177x744.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vdRZ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F23d30154-decb-4d36-a4d6-9198657cc088_1177x744.jpeg)

We have a quieter week in store for us ahead with earnings headlined by Uber, Rivian, Roblox, and Unity.
