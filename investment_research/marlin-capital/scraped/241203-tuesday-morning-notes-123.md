# Tuesday Morning Notes 12.3

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Quiet start to December yesterday with a more defensive tilt to the tape (Megacap 1 of the biggest leaders on the day). The 2/10 YR yield curve inverted for the 1st time since the Sept. Fed cut, causing some momentum unwinds across the board. Comments out from Waller caught some eyes late in the session: FED’S WALLER LEANING TOWARDS DEC RATE CUT, ABSENT DATA SURPRISE.

The consumer retail space stood out (GSXUMALL +3.4%), having 1 of its best sessions YTD as initial Black Friday numbers came in strong. Elsewhere, we saw some reversion of the Software > Semis outperformance with Semis finishing +2.71% vs Software +0.48%. On the Semis front, China export restrictions announcement was very well telegraphed and less severe than initially expected. The initial reaction was “buy the news/cover shorts on the news”.

Update from GS Prime Book - Hedge Funds have net sold US Tech stocks for 9 straight sessions. Friday’s net selling in the sector was driven almost entirely by short sales, and all subsectors (sans Tech Hardware) were net sold, led by Software & Semis. US Tech Long/Short ratio now stands at 1.73, near 5-year lows and in the 3rd percentile v the past 5 years.
