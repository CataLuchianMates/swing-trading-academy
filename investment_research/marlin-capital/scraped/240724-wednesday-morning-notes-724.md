# Wednesday Morning Notes 7.24

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Choppy price action for the SPX (-0.15%) and NDX (-0.35%) yesterday while the Russell 2000 outperformed once again (+1.11%).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Xr9K!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0f67bae1-24a0-4981-8278-250f2480de23_1195x886.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Xr9K!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0f67bae1-24a0-4981-8278-250f2480de23_1195x886.jpeg)

We saw asset managers largely sell earnings misses in cyclicals, specifically UPS, NXPI, and FCX.  There were some green shoots across regional banks, internet names (SPOT +11% on earnings, did not give day 1 earnings gapper entry I was looking for), software, and insurance.  Semis were weaker throughout the session following NXPI’s EPS print – Q2 results were largely in line but Q3 revenue guide (+4% QoQ) was below the street/some flagged continued softness in auto.

After the bell –

GOOGL: Headline beat, however Search & YouTube results were a touch light of heightened expectations.

Total revs $71.36B vs. consensus $70.7B

Google Ad Revs $64.62B vs. consensus $64.53B

YouTube Ad Revs $8.66B vs. consensus $8.95B

EPS $1.89 vs. consensus $1.84

Stock -4% this morning

TSLA: With gross margins seemingly about 200bps light, that falls short of what was likely needed for another leg higher in the stock.  This quarter does not seem like enough for TSLA after its recent 30% rally.  The big focus was going to be on profitability and how much f a hit they’d take to drive the delivery beat they (unexpectedly) pre-announced for Q2.  EBITDA was about 6% light versus the Street and FCF also came in light.  Neither is a total shock but is a reminder that they are struggling to drive upside to both deliveries and margins simultaneously.

Q2 EPS of $0.52 vs. consensus $0.61

EBITDA of $3.67 vs. consensus $3.90B

Revenue of $25.5B vs. consensus $24.4B (but deliveries were already pre-announced)

Q2 gross margins 18.0% vs. consensus 17.3%

Auto (ex credit) was 14.6% vs. consensus 16.6%

Stock -8% this morning
