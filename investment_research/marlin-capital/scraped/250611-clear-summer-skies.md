# Clear Summer Skies

The market is in rally mode so far this morning following the lighter than expected CPI data for May.

**May CPI:**

CPI MoM: 0.1% vs 0.2% exp.

CPI Core MoM: 0.1% vs 0.3% exp.

CPI YoY: 2.4% vs 2.4% exp.

CPI Core YoY: 2.8% vs 2.9% exp.

As mentioned yesterday, we are currently following the path of the 2018-19 market correction and rebound to a T (Powell’s “long way from neutral on rates” into the Christmas Eve ‘18 capitulation and Powell pivot in Jan ‘19).

On a timeframe basis, the 2018-19 overlay would suggest ~50 days out as sometime in early-mid August being the first real pullback, which is consistent with the more challenging seasonal period of the year (August/September).

Currently, we are in a very strong seasonal period of the year (June & July). 8 out of the last 9 June’s have finished positive for the NDX. For July, 17 out of the last 18 have finished positive, making it the strongest month of the year since 2008.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-K9C!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F965c8769-dbeb-4dc4-ad07-487db53ae388_1404x879.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-K9C!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F965c8769-dbeb-4dc4-ad07-487db53ae388_1404x879.png)

This bullish seasonality is further assisted by the open window for corporate buybacks (open window until mid-late July), which absolutely tilts money flows with Demand > Supply (literally shrinks the float with a consistent bid). Buybacks also simultaneously act as volatility killers, with repurchases tending to be most active into pullbacks/weakness and acting as shock absorbers when equities try to sell off. 2025 has been a record year for stock buybacks so far, and 90% of the SPX is currently in an open window for the next ~month.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!waW4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1d6f1e09-6c11-4fc6-9be7-b7956acf51dc_708x427.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!waW4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1d6f1e09-6c11-4fc6-9be7-b7956acf51dc_708x427.jpeg)

We also have the systematic bid projected as a bullish tailwind for stocks over the next 1-2 months, particularly among Vol Control Funds. The 3M rVol is still in a different world at 31.2 (87th %tile). We simply need more time to pass for the big vol shock days of April to roll out of the sample in the Target Vol space, but that time is coming here soon. If we continue this low volatility environment (~50bps daily move) , the April shock days will roll out and Vol Control Funds will be projected to buy somewhere between $62B over 1 month and $177B over the next 2 months.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Vn5J!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff069804f-d395-48e4-918a-6967011c17f4_1159x386.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Vn5J!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff069804f-d395-48e4-918a-6967011c17f4_1159x386.jpeg)

And again, all this comes when the vast majority of institutions remain underweight US equities (and overall positioning remains underweight). This fast and furious market rebound (+30% since April lows) has many on the sidelines/underpositioned waiting for “the eventual pullback”.

But as I would like to remind everyone, in order to have a significant market pullback, you first need the conditions for a pullback to be present (which were all present in late Jan-Feb, but are not now):

1. **Excess length/positioning/leverage**

   1. Total long positioning was at ATHs at the end of Jan ‘25, but is now currently still in “light/underweight” territory

      [![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Yywk!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F40486f2c-57cb-4610-9936-1f0b361ae670_1024x998.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Yywk!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F40486f2c-57cb-4610-9936-1f0b361ae670_1024x998.jpeg)
2. **Elements of speculative froth/euphoria in High Beta market segments**

   1. In my report [“Red Flags Emerge”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/red-flags-emerge) on 2/23, I noted some of the parabolic moves we had seen along with potential climax/blowoff tops

In my view, the elements of a market pullback are not currently in place here. Along with the mechanical flows from corporate buybacks and Vol Control Funds helping to subdue volatility and provide a consistent bid (coming at a time when institutions are underweight and summer liquidity is low/leans overwhelmingly bullish), the bar to be bearish here is pretty high. For these reasons, I remain in the bullish camp and see ATHs on the way .
