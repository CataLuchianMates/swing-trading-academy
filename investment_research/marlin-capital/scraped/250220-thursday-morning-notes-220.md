# Thursday Morning Notes 2.20

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Yesterday was a relatively quiet day with the Fed Minutes release catalyzing the action in both treasuries and equities. The confirmation that the Fed is on pause is not new news but the possibility of pausing or tapering QT lead to lower bond yields and a slight move higher in stocks.

FED: WANT FURTHER INFLATION PROGRESS BEFORE ADJUSTING RATES

FED: CAN HOLD POLICY AT RESTRICTIVE LEVEL IF ECONOMY STRONG

It’s liquidation day out there so far this morning in a lot of names, especially some retail trader favorites (PLTR -12%, HIMS -12%, TEM -12%, GRAB -10%, etc.)

I talked about the bullishness of retail a few times in the last couple weeks and this turned out to be a pretty big red flag for the market (top 3 largest retail buying days in history all came in the last 20 days). We certainly reached retail euphoria over the past week or 2 in crowded names like PLTR and HIMS. I also believe tomorrow’s monthly options expiration is playing a big factor in today’s liquidations as we have $615B of single stock options expiring tomorrow and we saw record retail call buying over the past 2 weeks.
