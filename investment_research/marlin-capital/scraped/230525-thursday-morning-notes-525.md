# Thursday Morning Notes 5.25

First off, wow, what an incredible and historic quarter for Nvidia.  Q2 Revenue guidance came in 40-50% above consensus estimates, making this one of the largest guidance raises I have ever seen in my stock market career.

This is a historic quarter in so many ways, as Nvidia has essentially pulled revenue levels that were projected 2-3 years in the future.  The AI revolution is here, coming faster than expected, and Nvidia is at the forefront of this generational technological movement.  Nvidia will likely be joining the $1T valuation club today, making it the 6thlargest company in the world.

I talked a bit about AI in my [podcast interview](https://youtu.be/c6w-fpCgD58) with Jonah Lupton last week.  This is a life-changing technology that will change everything about the way we live.  Similar to the dawn of the internet, it is difficult to fully comprehend all the ways in which AI will change society and the way we live over the next 5+ years.  However, one thing is for certain, and that is Nvidia is at the forefront of this revolution.

One more note on NVDA, as I have traded this stock many times over the past 5+ years (including at the beginning of this year for a paltry 15% gain) and also owned shares in my IRA since 2018.  The only way to capture 10x+ bagger returns like NVDA is to own it, not trade it.  You must share the long-term vision of the company and be willing to hold through massive drawdowns in the stock (which is not the same as the company).  This is why I designate long-term investing for my retirement account, as it allowed me to hold NVDA through multiple 70%+ drawdowns without feeling the need or desire to sell.

The flip side of this is, you will surely have 75%+ losses on certain investments as a long-term holder.  Not every investment will be a success, and this is part of the game we play as investors.  Investing and trading are 2 completely different games, and this is why I suggest my readers keep 2 separate accounts for these separate strategies.  For me, the long term investing account is a retirement account with money I will not need for many years, which allows me to remove any shortsightedness and emotion from the process.  In 2035, the problems plaguing us in 2023 will be largely irrelevant, but what will matter is the long term success of any company you own shares in.
