# Thursday Morning Notes 1.4

**Daily Market Commentary:**

US stocks slid lower again yesterday as traders remain apprehensive to start out the new year.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jToI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feed746e0-2eb4-4296-9fc0-0461c0175289_980x723.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jToI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feed746e0-2eb4-4296-9fc0-0461c0175289_980x723.jpeg)

Watch the 21 EMA level just below $4700 - this is the key support for the SPX.

Yesterday’s sell off had a different flavor than Tuesday’s, with Mega Cap Tech/high quality/safe havens hanging in while we saw broad based weakness across Short Momentum, Most Short Tech, and non-profitable tech.  The Russell 2000 underperformed the SPX/Nasdaq by ~2% yesterday.

The Nasdaq is now down nearly ~3% over the last 5 trading days (the worst stretch since late October).  This is just the 3rd time in history that the Nasdaq has started the year with back to back -1%+ declines (the other two were 1980 and 2005).  How did we finish those years? 1980 = +26%; 2005 = +3%.

10 YR yields touched 4% yesterday after Barkin (Fed Voter) stated that additional rate hikes remain on the table (though unlikely).  For a market that is pricing in 6 rate cuts for 2024 beginning in March, this was not welcome news.

While short books are benefitting from this move, we are not seeing significant short pressing from the hedge fund side.  GS Prime Book data shows that most of the non-profitable tech shorts were covered during the November/December squeeze, while the Mag 7 length remains in the 89th percentile versus history.
