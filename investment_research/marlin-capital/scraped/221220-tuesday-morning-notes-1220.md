# Tuesday Morning Notes 12.20

Technical Analysis

The indices kicked off the week with another red close, the 4th straight for the S&P 500. We are now well below all key moving averages on the daily chart.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xe_I!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1f901f44-ce1d-486d-845c-8ed0d4900370_1228x928.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xe_I!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1f901f44-ce1d-486d-845c-8ed0d4900370_1228x928.jpeg)

If the SPX can hold the $3800 support level, a bounce to $3900 is still in play (as I mentioned in “[Ghost of the Future](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/ghost-of-the-future)”). If this support level breaks, the next stop is likely $3740-$3750, which would constitute a full gap fill from November’s CPI day.
