# Wednesday Morning Notes 1.29

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The market had a nice bounce back day yesterday with a partial recovery in Tech (NVDA +9% yesterday, and “*only”* down ~8% this week).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TQku!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2a020f45-8a6c-4077-9398-20d7f4f2483d_1284x2115.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TQku!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2a020f45-8a6c-4077-9398-20d7f4f2483d_1284x2115.jpeg)

Time will tell if yesterday was just an oversold/technical bounce or if the market is ready to turn the page on DeepSeek. As I talked about yesterday, my view is that this selloff will prove to be overdone so long as companies continue to reaffirm/raise their 2025 guides. In my mind, this Earnings Season has become even more critical due to Monday’s DeepSeek selloff.

Initial data from Monday’s Tech rout shows heavy dip buying across the board:

* Biggest 1-Day inflows to QQQ since 2021
* Record retail investor inflows to NVDA with 88% of orders placed being buy orders
* Record inflows to leveraged ETFs

We have a huge day of potential catalysts ahead of us today. The focus will be on Mega Caps reporting post-close (MSFT, TSLA, META) alongisde the FOMC meeting this afternoon.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ywja!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9b7da0b9-f489-4159-9744-d4b7ca3307e5_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ywja!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9b7da0b9-f489-4159-9744-d4b7ca3307e5_1920x1080.jpeg)

For the FOMC, the market has fully priced in no changes at this meeting (99.6% probability according to Fed Funds Futures). More important will be Powell’s tone/the Fed’s openness to March rate cuts. Heading into today’s FOMC, the odds of a March rate cut sit at ~32%. In my view, this will be the most important number to watch coming out of today’s meeting.

After the Fed, we will get 3 massive earnings reports post-market close in MSFT, TSLA, and META. Needless to say, these reports will have a huge impact on the market. My view is that these earnings will prove to be a bigger catalyst than today’s FOMC, but obviously, there are many different headlines that will impact the market today so expect plenty of volatility this afternoon/post-market.
