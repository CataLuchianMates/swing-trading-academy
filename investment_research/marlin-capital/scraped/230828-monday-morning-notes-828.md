# Monday Morning Notes 8.28

The market narrative did not change dramatically last week.  Price action was choppy, sentiment was further challenged, and activity levels were muted (as expected for late summer trading).

Interesting note – through 19 trading sessions in August, the SPX has not garnered a single back to back gain.  This is the first month without two consecutive up days since August 2002, illustrating the lack of conviction on both sides.

Seasonality will become even more of a challenge for the SPX as we enter September, the worst month historically (by far) for US stocks.  The first half of September is the 2nd worst 2 week trading period dating back to 1928 (behind 2nd half of February).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IkB4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F79a68405-7795-4124-b173-b9f465863ed5_806x554.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IkB4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F79a68405-7795-4124-b173-b9f465863ed5_806x554.jpeg)

In Fed Chair Powell’s Jackson Hole speech on Friday, he noted that the FOMC will “proceed carefully” when deciding whether to hike or hold the policy rate constant at future meetings.  In my view, a hike in September is unlikely.  Powell was slightly more hawkish than his last press conference in July, saying that the committee is “attentive to signs that the economy may not be cooling as expected”.

With that said, the market rallied on Friday following Powell’s speech, finishing +0.67%.  We closed at $4405, right at the 60 EMA resistance level.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TTUL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff677ecc8-4dfa-4731-b699-9fb3ebeda1f5_1256x922.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TTUL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff677ecc8-4dfa-4731-b699-9fb3ebeda1f5_1256x922.jpeg)

$4330-$4350 has acted as strong support during this August pullback.  Now, we will see if the SPX can break some key resistance levels above.  $4410 and $4440-$4450 are the key resistance levels I’m watching above.

If the indices can start to gain more momentum and break resistance here, I will be more interested in some long trade opportunities.

CLS, COUR, RELY, VRT, UPWK, ROVR are the standout names from my earnings gapper watchlist.  These names could begin to provide big gains if the market pressure eases.
