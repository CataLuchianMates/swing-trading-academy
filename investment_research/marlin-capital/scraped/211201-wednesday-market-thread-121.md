**SNOW preview from BTIG - reports tonight** -

We Remain Highly Confident in Forecasts into FQ3'22 Print. The street estimate of $283.4MM / +91% y/y implies that SNOW adds $28.8MM in net new product revenue in FQ3'22 versus $40.8MM in FQ2'22. Given our positive checks, normal sequential trends, and strength of public cloud infrastructure vendors like AWS and MSFT, we think that assumption will prove conservative.

In an upside scenario, we think SNOW could post something closer to $295MM / +99% y/y in product revenue. Over the last few weeks, we spoke with six contacts (partners and customers) about broader demand and competitive trends in the cloud data analytics space in order to gain better insights on SNOW's business momentum ahead of FQ3'22 earnings on Wednesday 12/1 AMC. We highlight the unfiltered feedback from those discussions in this note.

In summary, we think demand trends remain strong and see a relatively stable competitive environment. As a result, we remain highly confident in our forecasts on SNOW for both 2H'F22 and FY'23

**5:13 EST: ARKK Technical Analysis**

ARKK, my proxy for growth stocks, closed at the low’s of the day.  Let’s take a look at the daily chart:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3Tb-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff0789023-16d1-498b-877f-613e00929f5e_1587x955.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3Tb-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff0789023-16d1-498b-877f-613e00929f5e_1587x955.png)

This is a decisive move lower for ARKK.  On a shorter time frame, it is a bear flag breakdown.  Now, ARKK must hold the May $97 lows.  If the ETF breaks the $97 lows, I believe the charts are suggesting a move to the $80 level.  That is another 19% downside, so I will remain short ARKK if this happens.  There will likely be other potential short opportunities that arise as well.  I likely will not begin buying in my IRA until ARKK reaches the low-$80 level, as I do not believe most growth stocks will bottom if ARKK continues moving lower.

This sell off, so far, is very similar to ARKK’s February-May correction.  Back then, ARKK fell from $159.7 to $97.2 (39%) in the span of just over 3 months.  The headline, at that time, was the rising 10Y.  The headline now is a more hawkish tone from Fed.  Institutions are selling growth stocks in a similar manner to the February sell off right now.

As I mentioned above, the February-May ARKK sell off brought a 39% correction.  Since that time, ARKK peaked at $132.  If we correct 39% from that level, that would bring us to $81.

In summary, ARKK has a final support level at $97.  There is the potential for a double bottom around that support area.  If it breaks, I believe we will see a waterfall of selling, similar to what happened in February.  I believe this move will take us to the low $80’s. Major caution is warranted right now.

The good news is, this will provide us with plenty of good buying opportunities for our Long Term portfolios.  The key will be preserving our capital and limiting the drawdown in our portfolios.  First, that means raising a lot of cash. I will also remain short ARKK, and look to potentially add other shorts, if we break $97 on volume.  Then, I will look to add to my long term stocks at key support levels.
