# Leaders Emerge

Today featured a good old fashioned “buy the dip” rally, with every SPX sector higher except Energy. AI stocks like NVDA (+3.6%) and AVGO (+3.2%) led the way once again.

It was a big bounceback day for the market, and suddenly, some strong post-earnings setups and leaders are emerging.

Earnings season is my hunting ground for active trades, and **Q1 Earnings delivered some MASSIVE winners for us**:

**HOOD** +114%

**APH** +40%

**SPOT** +15% and **DUOL** +5%

[My Top 4 Trade Ideas from Q1 Earnings](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-4-trade-ideas-from-q1-earnings?utm_source=publication-search)

The Q2 Earnings Season started out lackluster, but we are beginning to see some clear leadership start to emerge out of this market pullback.

**Here are my Top 4 standouts from Q2 Earnings so far:**

**APLD**

Applied Digital is 1 of my [top 6 Small and Mid Cap long term investments](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-smid-caps-with-massive-upside-potential). In my view, this stock is going way higher.

I’m not interested in trading it because I’m already invested here, but this is clearly emerging as 1 of the top standouts from Q2 earnings so far. Today was the stock’s highest close since April of 2022.

**WGS**

WGS is a stock we gained +207% on in the [original Small Cap 6.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-big-unwind?utm_source=publication-search)

It’s a name we know very well.

And it’s a name that’s now back on my radar after blowout earnings results.

GeneDx raised 2025 guidance by +11% at the midpoint and guided for +50% growth in exome & genome revenue (versus a forecast of +30%). They also raised gross margin guidance to 68% to 71%, up from previous guidance in the range of 66% to 68%.

The post-earnings price action is looking super bullish technically.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OzbZ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F95e42068-49e0-4ed9-a48a-e1e64e55c5b0_1132x652.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OzbZ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F95e42068-49e0-4ed9-a48a-e1e64e55c5b0_1132x652.png)

Remember [during Q1 earnings](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-4-trade-ideas-from-q1-earnings?utm_source=publication-search), when I said sometimes it can be even more bullish for a stock to sell off and get bought right back up after earnings.

> Sometimes, it is actually even more bullish to see a Day 1 earnings selloff that gets bought up quickly. This tells you that demand for the stock is very high (as we saw with APH), as the post-earnings dip does not last long and gets bought up right away…Again, my thesis here is that when the post-earnings dip in a leading stock gets immediately bought up (see APH), it means demand for the stock is extremely high. This is a common theme in leading stocks throughout history: dips don’t last long.
