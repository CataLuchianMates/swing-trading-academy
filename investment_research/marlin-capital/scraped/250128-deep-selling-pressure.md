# Deep Selling Pressure

US stocks were hammered yesterday with the global AI complex under tremendous pressure on competitions concerns from China’s DeepSeek (a 1-year old Chinese AI startup that has demonstrated AI models offering comparable performance to the best AI chatbots at a fraction of the cost). This matters because it calls into question the widespread belief that the future of AI will require ever increasing amounts of compute capacity, power, energy, chips, etc. to develop.

Prior to yesterday’s gap down, the market has rewarded both the companies spending on AI (AMZN, MSFT, GOOGL, etc.) and the picks and shovels plays (NVDA, AVGO, VRT, Semis, etc.), and a significant amount of market cap & length has been built up in each cohort. The DeepSeek story is shaking investor confidence in the entire AI ecosystem because the DeepSeek model calls into question the need for the same type of spending/investing moving forward.

Yesterday’s action brough us historic declines with the AI king, Nvidia, shedding ~$600B in market cap, by far the largest single day decline for any company in history.

This was NVDA’s first time below the 200dma in over 2 years. AI Semis were -12% and had its worst day since the March 2020 Covid Crash, Artificial Intelligence -9% (a 6 sigma move), Power Up America -14% (a 7 sigma move), Nuclear Energy -18%, AI Data Centers & Power -12%.

Rotational factors were at play with the Dow Jones finishing +289 pts (+0.65%), while the Nasdaq dropped -3.07%. 350 S&P names finished up on the day (Equal Weight +2bps) as Defensives outperformed +2.2%.

As mentioned yesterday, the market was in clear “Sell First, Ask Questions Later” mode with the injection of uncertainty into the AI infrastructure story. With MSFT, META and TSLA set to report in ~48 hrs, it feels reasonable to wait and see what these companies say and how “expected” these recent developments were and if it changes any of their capital allocation/spending plans and future outlook.

While tons of questions remain around the current reporting of DeepSeek (what chips were utilized, how many, what models were used, what data have the incremental improvements been trained on & overall input costs), this news is likely to act as an overhang on Tech & Energy until company management teams provide their own insight/perspectives during this Earnings Season. The good news is we are entering the meat of Earnings Season now and do not have to wait too long to find out.

My ultimate take is that we absolutely can NOT see AI companies begin to guide lower than expected/revise expectations lower (pulling guides would also be bearish). In my view, this would cause a significant wave of selling. On the other hand, if we see companies start to reaffirm their guides or raise expectations, this story will have been overblown and dips likely bought. In my mind, it all comes down to Earnings, Guidance, and CEO/CFO commentary now.

I moved to 100% cash in my Swing Trading account following yesterday’s gap down and I am in no hurry to do anything here. In many cases, the technical damage from bearish candles like yesterday takes weeks/months to set up again. Earnings Gappers will be my focus for new trades, but we will ideally need the general market to cooperate for these types of trades to work.
