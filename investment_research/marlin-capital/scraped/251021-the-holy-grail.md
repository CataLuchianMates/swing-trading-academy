Back to today’s news on GRAL, which is an incredible development for this exciting young AI company. Grail’s PATHFINDER 2 study results showed that their Galleri blood test boosts multi-cancer early detection (MCED) by over sevenfold. These findings detected 7.6 additional cancers per 1,000 participants versus 1.0 with standard screens alone. The PATHFINDER 2 study involved 35,500 participants, targeting individuals aged 50 and older who were not actively being evaluated or treated for cancer at the time.

This HUGE news for GRAL comes after last week’s news of a Samsung deal, which will help bring the Galleri test to Asia (starting in South Korea). Expansion in Asia was 1 of our growth theses on GRAL due to the Tencent backing.

GRAL is now trading at a $3.3B valuation. And yet, if this company is successful, that valuation will be looked at as a steal years from now.

Take a minute to comprehend the significance of what Grail is trying to achieve here. Right now, they are leading the Multi-Cancer Early Detection (MCED) space and leaving normal cancer tests in the dust (detecting 7x more cancers than normal tests). If Galleri continues to develop and improve, retaining its market leadership, GRAL could one day become 1 of the largest healthcare companies in the world.

Healthcare is 1 of the themes we have been most bullish on for AI (along with Data Centers and Robotics). It is an industry that I continue to believe will be transformed by AI in the coming years. I predict that we will see major AI-driven breakthroughs in healthcare/scientific discovery, and Grail will continue to be at the forefront of it all.

Another double from here (~$200) would not be crazy for GRAL. Remember, Ilumina acquired GRAL for an $8B valuation in 2021, but the buyout failed due to antitrust concerns. The FTC wanted Grail to be a standalone company, and I’m certainly happy with their decision! It gave us an opportunity to invest in 1 of Silicon Valley’s next great Tech companies.

In my [original SMID Cap 6 report](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-smid-caps-with-massive-upside-potential), I said that I see “a path back to an $8B valuation” (ILMM buyout valuation) and beyond for GRAL. That would be around ~$200 per share. Big picture, this is my new target from GRAL ($200).
