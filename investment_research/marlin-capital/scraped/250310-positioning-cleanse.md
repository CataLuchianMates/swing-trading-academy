# Positioning Cleanse

Last week was another eventful one for US stocks with the market continuing to sell off. Despite the Friday bounce (Powell : “the economy is fine”), the SPX closed -3.3% on the week and the NDX finished -3.8%.

The NDX is now down -4% on the year, its worst start to a year since 2022 (the parallels to 2022 just keep rolling in). For reference, the NDX drawdown was -35% in 2022 while we are currently at -10% here in 2025. With last week’s decline, the Nasdaq is now officially in correction territory (-10%+) while the S&P 500 enters this week ‘only’ -6.1% off all-time highs.

Thus far, 2025 has been a reversion of the last year + of price action. The Mag 7 names are struggling mightily YTD: TSLA -35%, AVGO -16%, NVDA -16%, AMZN -9%, GOOGL -8%, MSFT -7%, AAPL -6%, META (the only 1 in the green) +7%.

Talk about a reversion. Did anyone have this on their 2025 bingo card? The green line below is the Magnificent 7; the red line is European Banks (over the past 12 months).

China and Europe are the biggest outperformers so far in 2025 (Europe +10% YTD, China Tech +20%), while the United States is the clear laggard. Despite this, there is still a wide divergence in valuations between the US and Europe.

At the heart of the US underperformance is negative EPS revisions, a big positioning unwind, and economic policy uncertainty. Economy policy uncertainty is currently registering peak levels (outside of March 2020).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bk8a!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcf088466-4d30-4dc1-8d66-a9a5ca92b686_594x454.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bk8a!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcf088466-4d30-4dc1-8d66-a9a5ca92b686_594x454.jpeg)

Uncertainty is the enemy of financial markets. The Trump administration has been quite adamant that the trade balances need to be corrected and they are willing to endure some short term pain to get there. Somewhere between 27% and 40% of revenues for the S&P 500 companies come from outside the US. Current FY 2025 EPS consensus stands at $268.65 (+9% YoY), but JPM estimates this could drop to ~$252 if we incorporate first order tariff impacts. This is clearly weighing on US markets.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4pfK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F82d501fa-1921-4e8c-a129-074db8f3be74_576x823.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4pfK!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F82d501fa-1921-4e8c-a129-074db8f3be74_576x823.png)

The biggest thing for me, though, is the de-risking/de-leveraging that has taken place over the last month +. I have described this market sell off as a massive positioning unwind. At the end of January, overall long positioning was at ATHs (across Hedge Funds + Retail + Foreign Investors).

The good news is due to the volatility reset higher in recent weeks, we have seen a very necessary positioning cleanse. Overall long positioning is now underweight for the first time since early 2023.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wv_F!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F55087dda-07c1-4062-8ebc-f2a990cff9b8_1324x1176.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wv_F!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F55087dda-07c1-4062-8ebc-f2a990cff9b8_1324x1176.jpeg)

Of course, positioning can still go lower (as it is not light or extreme). But, this was the first step in the necessary positioning cleanse and a pre-requisite to stocks ultimately bottoming.

At the heart of the 2025 positioning cleanse are all the top trades of the last couple years (Mag 7, AI, Mega Cap, Momentum, Retail Favorites). These were all the most crowded trades heading into this year (while China and Europe were the most underweight longs).

Hedge Funds have de-risked the most aggressively (in fact, the most aggressive 2 week de-risking in their history).

While options activity from retail investors has waned over the past couple weeks, we have continued to see retail buying the dip during this drawdown (particularly in Nasdaq stocks).

To be clear, this is not typical behavior at market bottoms and there have been no signs of panic or capitulation to this point.

In “Do or Die Time”, I talked about how the indices and names like GOOGL and TSLA were sitting right at key long term support levels. For the SPX and QQQ, we broke below these key support levels before briefly recapturing them with Friday’s bounce. With this morning’s gap down, we are now testing last week’s lows.

These are now critical lows to hold for the indices. Again, this current sell off looks and feels different than any other we have experienced since this Bull Market began in October 2022. I continue to believe we have more downside to come before finding the ultimate bottom.

My Base Case remains a -10% correction in the S&P 500, which would take us to the $5500 region in the index. From there, I will be watching the status of EPS revisions and overall positioning (for signs of fear/capitulation) to help guide me into calling a market bottom.
