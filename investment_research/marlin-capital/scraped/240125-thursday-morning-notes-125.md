# Thursday Morning Notes 1.25

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Yesterday’s rally attempt ran out of steam into the bell with the S&P 500 finishing the day +0.08%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!c671!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fadcd3427-35b7-446e-a7da-e3eb00647d15_958x711.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!c671!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fadcd3427-35b7-446e-a7da-e3eb00647d15_958x711.jpeg)

Major indices started firmly in the green on solid earnings updated from tech bellwethers (NFLX +11% / ASML +9%).  Needless to say, breadth was very narrow yesterday compared to previous sessions with Mega Caps +1.6% supporting the broader indices (only 141 members of the S&P 500 finished green yesterday).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GHz4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F29e8599f-8493-4370-bb6d-c1602fd455f0_1168x602.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GHz4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F29e8599f-8493-4370-bb6d-c1602fd455f0_1168x602.jpeg)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yEfA!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8045279f-2a13-4377-af15-aab555d27c3b_1171x533.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yEfA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8045279f-2a13-4377-af15-aab555d27c3b_1171x533.jpeg)

Stocks reversed from highs as yields continue to creep higher (US 10 YR +4bps @ 4.18%) on stronger US PMIs.  The Russell 2000 got hit the hardest yesterday, finishing down -106bps.

After the bell, TSLA announced mixed results and is set for a gap down this morning on a gross margin miss and signal of weaker growth ahead (TSLA says 2024 volume growth may be “notably lower” than 2023).  Fundamentally, it is no surprise to see the stock gapping down this morning with this print.  It has traded down exactly -9-10% each of the last 3 EPS days.  The focus for most was the FY guide (weaker than expected).
