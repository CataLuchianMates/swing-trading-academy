Now let’s talk about the fundamental catalyst that put CRDO on my radar in the first place, because it is much bigger than many people realize. The acquisition of DustPhotonics is HUGE for the company.

As a result, CRDO is currently being re-rated as an Optics winner. 👇

Last Monday, Credo Technology (CRDO) announced the $750M acquisition of DustPhotonics, a Silicon Photonics company.

On the surface, the headline read like just another tech M&A deal. But when you dig into what this acquisition actually does for CRDO, the strategic implications are massive.

Here is the simple version. Before this deal, CRDO was a “point solution” provider in the AI interconnect space. They made the SerDes, DSP, and optical transceivers. Strong products, but only pieces of the full puzzle. With DustPhotonics, CRDO now owns the photonic integrated circuits (PICs) as well, which is the missing piece. That means CRDO can now offer hyperscalers a complete, vertically integrated AI interconnect solution spanning both the electrical and optical sides of the data center.

In an AI infrastructure market where every hyperscaler is racing to build out the next generation of AI clusters, that is exactly what customers are demanding. Faster interconnects, lower power, and a single supplier who can deliver the full stack. CRDO can now do that.

And the financial impact is real. Management guided that the combined optical business will generate over $500M in revenue in fiscal 2027 and be accretive to non-GAAP EPS in fiscal 2027. That kind of stated, quantified guidance for a deal this size is exactly what Wall Street tends to re-rate aggressively in a strong AI infrastructure tape.

In my view, the fundamental re-rating is underway for CRDO.

I am targeting ATHs at $213+ here.

In a “risk on” environment, these types of hot IPO breakout setups can fly (remember CRWV last year?).

I may decide to enter this trade via calls instead of shares. I covered the exact criteria for when I prefer options over shares in [“Small Caps Soar”.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/small-caps-soar)

In short, with a volatile stock that is likely to hunt stops, I prefer to enter calls and size for a -100% loss. That way, my risk is fully managed before I even enter the trade, and I do not have to worry about the intraday action. The trade either works, or it doesn’t. You are already sized for -100% loss, so you are not worrying about the huge intraday moves. Once you’re positioned, the idea will either work or fail, and the setup will make or lose money accordingly. But, the key is that you will be able to see the entire trade idea through ([buy the ticket, take the ride](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-4-rules-for-successful-swing-trading?utm_source=publication-search)).
