# Headwinds

Marlin Capital Readers,

The Nasdaq just finished lower for the 6th time in the last 7 weeks.

There are many different factors plaguing this market right now. And they are coming from multiple directions simultaneously.

Let’s walk through each one. 👇

**The Bull Case Is Eroding**

Coming into 2026, 1 of the biggest pillars of [the bull case](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-marlin-capital-2026-preview?utm_source=publication-search) was the Federal Reserve continuing to cut interest rates. Heading into the year, multiple cuts were expected.

That has changed dramatically.

The war in Iran has sent oil prices parabolic. When oil prices spike, inflation expectations follow. And when inflation expectations rise, the Fed’s hands are tied.

As of today, the Fed is now projected to cut just once this year.

I wouldn’t call the Fed cutting once a major market tailwind anymore. It is obviously not a headwind either, but the shift here is significant. What was a major tailwind coming into the year has now shifted to neutral. That is a meaningful change in the macro backdrop for stocks.

**The AI Labor Market Shakeup Is Accelerating**

I first covered this in [“Heading Into March”.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/heading-into-march?utm_source=publication-search) It started with Block (XYZ) announcing that they will be laying off ~40% of their workforce. The stock immediately rallied +24% on the news.

[At the time](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/heading-into-march?utm_source=publication-search), I said that Block’s positive stock reaction sends a very clear message to every other CEO in America. If you are struggling, cut your workforce significantly, attribute it to AI, and your stock will rally big. That is a powerful incentive.

Well, it didn’t take long.

On Friday, Reuters reported that Meta is planning sweeping layoffs that could affect 20% or more of the company. But Meta is far from alone.

Here are the total FAANG layoff tallies so far 👇

Meta: 20% layoffs (would be the largest single reduction in company history)

Amazon: ~30,000 gone (>10% of corporate workforce)

Microsoft: 13,000 gone

Read those numbers again. These are not small, struggling companies trimming fat. These are the largest and most profitable Tech companies on Earth, collectively cutting tens of thousands of white collar jobs.

Last Thursday, ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott said that unemployment for new college grads could “easily” reach the mid-30% range due to AI.

There is no going back now. AI has already begun to forever alter the job market, and this is likely just the beginning.

Notice how sentiment towards Artificial Intelligence has shifted dramatically in 2026. Last year (following the April ‘25 Liberation Day lows), AI was new and exciting. The possibilities were endless. It was going to change the world for the better.

Now? AI is going to destroy the job market, and with it, the consumer-based economy.

Whether or not that actually plays out remains to be seen. But the shift in sentiment is very real, and sentiment drives markets in the short term.

Polymarket recession odds for 2026 have soared to 32%. For Marlin Capital readers who were with us during last year’s Liberation Day selloff, you may remember that [the SPX bottomed at right around the same time Polymarket recession odds topped](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/day-3?utm_source=publication-search). This will once again be a key number to watch going forward.

But recession odds are not the only tool that helped us successfully navigate last year’s Liberation Day selloff. **Positioning data was even more important.**

**Total Positioning: Not at Contrarian Lows**

Overall positioning is falling fast as asset managers and systematic funds continue to unload. Asset Managers just dumped -$36B in S&P 500 futures in a single week. That is the largest liquidation in 10+ years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0Rs_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7dee56c2-391e-4f29-9239-3cc1a9744bbf_1230x1039.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0Rs_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7dee56c2-391e-4f29-9239-3cc1a9744bbf_1230x1039.png)

But despite this massive liquidation, asset managers are still ~71st %ile net long S&P futures.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KWTe!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8b1b9060-b17c-4583-b04a-c7e86d102618_1290x1106.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KWTe!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8b1b9060-b17c-4583-b04a-c7e86d102618_1290x1106.png)

In other words, there is still plenty of potential selling left if conditions continue to worsen. This is not the kind of washed-out positioning you typically see at meaningful market bottoms.

CTAs and systematic funds remain a headwind here as well. According to Goldman Prime, the systematic trading community has yet to meaningfully de-risk. In their estimation, Systematic Funds are currently long $195B of US equities (w/ CTAs currently long ~$36B).

On Friday, we closed below the medium-term CTA threshold (~$6,760). This means sell estimates will begin to grow quickly should we remain below that level this week.

Most importantly, overall positioning is still neutral right now. This is the same Goldman Positioning chart that helped us call the bottom last year (when it hit -2.5z, indicating severely depressed positioning). 👇

And right now, it is showing that total positioning is still neutral. It is not yet depressed (or anywhere close). It is not indicative of any type of widespread panic or contrarian lows. It is simply neutral.

When you look at what positioning looked like at the Liberation Day bottom last April (-2.5z!, completely washed out, max fear, everyone already out), we are simply not there yet. With that said, I will continue to closely monitor positioning data moving forward, as I believe it will once again help us determine when the market is truly washed out.

**Credit Conditions Are Deteriorating**

Credit conditions continue to deteriorate. My Credit Indicator (LQD/IEF ratio) is now trending meaningfully lower. Credit spreads are widening while Treasuries are outperforming Corporates, signaling rising stress in the Fixed Income market. This is a classic macro warning sign for potential equity market weakness ahead.

**The SPX Technical Picture**

The overall price action is not indicative of a market trying to bottom yet, in my view.

This is now a clear technical downtrend for the S&P 500, confirmed by the [moving averages crossing down bearish](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/rolling-over). In my view, the SPX is tracing out a classic rounded top pattern.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4AZI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa50b7baf-f56b-4797-b373-e9e6c40aa252_1566x846.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4AZI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa50b7baf-f56b-4797-b373-e9e6c40aa252_1566x846.png)

The first key support level I am watching is $6,450-6,500, which is the anchored VWAP from April 2025 Liberation Day lows.

Below that, $6,300 remains my base case target. That would represent a -10% correction from highs.

To be clear, I am not yet bullish right here. I still see more downside ahead. And stocks are getting much more correlated this month, which means if we do follow my base case path to $6,300, I would expect most all stocks to get hit hard (not just the stage 4’s).

I have been highlighting the rising risks for months now, dating all the way back to [“Risk Off” in October](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70?utm_source=publication-search). The warning signs I flagged back then (speculative leaders topping, breadth deteriorating, late-cycle behavior) have now materialized into something much bigger. And as I said in [“Rolling Over”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/rolling-over) on Thursday, the macro headwinds are getting louder and louder.

**The Big Picture**

This is a challenging market environment and the headwinds are real. The war in Iran has sent oil prices soaring. The Fed easing tailwind has evaporated. AI-driven job losses are accelerating and shifting sentiment from bullish excitement to genuine economic fear. Credit conditions are deteriorating. Financial conditions are tightening. Total positioning remains a headwind. And the overall price action is confirming all of it.

I have been warning about the late-cycle market risks [since October](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70?utm_source=publication-search), and I have been positioning accordingly. Right now, with >60% cash in the [2026 Portfolio](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/marlin-capital-live-portfolio-dashboards) and 100% cash in the [Multi-Year Investments Portfolio](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/marlin-capital-live-portfolio-dashboards), I am exactly where I want to be.

Right now, my plan is clear. I am watching $6,300 on the SPX as my level to begin deploying long-term capital. I am watching positioning data for signs of a true washout. And I am watching credit conditions for signs of stabilization. Until those signals arrive, I am focused on protecting capital.
