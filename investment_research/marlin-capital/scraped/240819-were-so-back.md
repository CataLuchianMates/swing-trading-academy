# We're So Back

US stocks recorded their best week of 2024 last week, led by a +5.5% surge in the Nasdaq.  The NDX, which is now up +12% since last Monday’s lows, exited the week on a 7 day winning streak (its best 7-day stretch in 2+ years).

The equities “volatility smash” that we’ve become accustomed to throughout this bull market is SO BACK.  The VIX dropped -27.3% last week, its most since November 2023.  The market’s fear index is now down -73% from last Monday’s peak panic spike.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Y7Dh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff4c83cd6-d5bb-4c22-aa2c-4f52a288c25d_1284x761.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Y7Dh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff4c83cd6-d5bb-4c22-aa2c-4f52a288c25d_1284x761.jpeg)

Bulls are now firmly back in control of the narrative.  In my view, this is all thanks to the resumption of a “Soft Landing” as the highest probability outcome yet again, thanks to the perpetually resilient US economy.  At peak VIX last Monday, the market had impulsively thrust itself into a “Hard Landing” bunker in panic fashion following [weak ISM and Unemployment numbers ~2 weeks ago](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/friday-morning-notes-82).

But now, after better jobless/continuing claims last week, alongside a strong retail sales print and more inflation downside surprises, we saw “Hard Landing” odds collapse back down just 9% by the end of the week.  Data from Nomura Quant Insights -

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bZMP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F410ab8ea-5a88-4de2-a633-8de93fcd263c_626x349.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bZMP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F410ab8ea-5a88-4de2-a633-8de93fcd263c_626x349.jpeg)

Institutional investors appear to agree with Nomura’s odds calculator.  The latest data from August shows 76% of Fund Managers surveyed by BofA are expecting a soft landing, while just 13% are expecting a hard landing.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!d6Hy!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7afa616f-74af-4d15-962b-40e14fa33efc_1195x643.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!d6Hy!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7afa616f-74af-4d15-962b-40e14fa33efc_1195x643.jpeg)

After the July jobs report released on August 2nd [triggered the dreaded “Sahm Rule”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/when-it-rains-it-pours), we hit peak “Hard Landing” probability.   The data released since that time (August 2nd), including retail sales and jobless claims last week, show no signs of a recession.  This market repricing has led to the +12% Nasdaq rally off last week’s intraday lows.

From a flow perspective, the case can certainly be made for this rally in US stocks to be sustained into the end of August.  CTAs have moved from massive sellers to net buyers globally and the Corporate buyback window is set to open.  CTAs are now projected to buy roughly $5B a day in the US over the next week.  Couple that $5B from CTAs with the $5B daily corporate bid, and you have $10B+ of non-discretionary/unemotional demand during one of the most illiquid weeks of the year.

The Hedge Fund community was also active last week, especially on Thursday.  Per Goldman’s Prime Book: “Amid better than expected US retail sales + earnings and the subsequent market rally, global equities saw their largest 1-day net buying since early January (+2.0 Z score 1yr)…Thursday’s $ short covering was the largest YTD.”

As I mentioned last week, Hedge Funds were big buyers into last Monday’s panic gap down.  They have largely continued since, helping to provide the bid for this +12% NDX rally off lows.  With that said, investor positioning is still lighter/cleaner today than it was when we were at these same levels back in late June/early July.  This could act as a tailwind into August end, as Hedge Funds continue re-risking, and CTAs + corporates provide a $10B+ per day bid into an illiquid/low volume end of summer market.

When the calendar flips to September, though, we will enter the weakest seasonal period of the year.  The average annualized SPX return for September (since 1928) is -13.5%, by far the worst month of the year.

8 of the last 10 September’s have ended in the red for the NDX.
