# Thursday Morning Notes 8.22

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Stocks were little changed yesterday with a small and slow, low liquidity, summer drift higher. The SPX finished +0.42% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jSqd!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faaa16cbc-8320-4dcc-a8a3-2efa7e88db9d_983x718.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jSqd!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faaa16cbc-8320-4dcc-a8a3-2efa7e88db9d_983x718.jpeg)

Underlying breadth improved with 401 S&P names finishing strength. Internal strength and a broadening out of this rally has been a feature of this V shaped recovery off lows. The SPX and NDX advance-decline lines have already broken out to new highs ahead of price. For me, this is a bullish leading indicator.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!a_cQ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6d9daa16-0555-49ce-9dc5-39f024ff525b_1224x823.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!a_cQ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6d9daa16-0555-49ce-9dc5-39f024ff525b_1224x823.jpeg)

Consumer discretionary outperformed yesterday, led by TGT up +10% on earnings and TJX +6%.

Macro data yesterday included the US Labor Department revising 12-month job growth down by a massive 818,000 jobs. In other words, the US economy actually created 818,000 LESS jobs than initially reported (that is 70k per month on average). This is the biggest negative revision since 2009.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yDQD!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F07c5925a-d203-468d-ac13-38e883e775c0_1284x746.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yDQD!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F07c5925a-d203-468d-ac13-38e883e775c0_1284x746.jpeg)

Elsewhere, the Fed Minutes reinforced the probability of a September rate cut. The noise now is on a 25 vs 50 bp cut, with 50 looking more likely after the negative job growth revisions. Powell will be speaking tomorrow at Jackson Hole, with many expecting him to open the door for a 50 bp cut.

Active Trading Strategy -

Took the loss on SG as it closed below my $33.5 closing stop.

Holding AXON, SN, SHOP, and SPOT into Thursday with stops in place.
