**To be very clear, my exit sales are based off technical analysis.**

My Big Picture approach is a combination strategy of Fundamentals + Technicals. I use primarily fundamental analysis for stock picking & idea generation, but solely technical analysis for exits.

Why?

Because Stage 4 downtrends exist.

All stocks have Bear Cycles. And during these Bear Cycles (Stage 4), individual stocks will typically decline 50-80%+.

Remember Mark Minervini’s “50-80 rule” I have talked about. The rule says:

> “When a secular leader makes a major top, there’s a 50% chance it will drop 80%, and an 80% chance it will drop 50%”
>
> - Mark Minervini

In my experience, Minervini’s rule is 100% true. And as I have learned, it is best to avoid holding stocks through 50-80% drawdowns.

During Stage 4 downtrends/Bear Cycles, even the best fundamental names get hit hard. You must never think that just because the company’s fundamentals are strong (or improving), the stock price cannot decline substantially. In fact, the stock performance may greatly diverge from the company performance (at certain points). Jeff Bezos highlighted this very important fact in a 2018 interview:

> “**The stock is not the company, and the company is not the stock.** And so, as AMZN stock dropped from $113 to $6 [during the dot com bust], the company was doing fine. We were continuing to grow our customers, our unit economics were continuing to improve, and so on. And so, I was always saying, we’re going to focus on the long term, we’re going to focus on our customers, we’re going to focus on the things we can control.”
>
> - Jeff Bezos

Many investors end up holding stocks through 80% drawdowns because they fall in love with the fundamentals. But, as you see in every new market cycle, many of this cycle’s darlings will **never** recover back to highs. Just like the 2020-21 Covid-QE Cycle (see ARKK, Cathie Wood, ZM, ROKU, FVRR, UPST, etc). If you traded through the 2020-21 Covid-QE Bubble, you probably remember Cathie Wood being compared to Warren Buffett. Her flagship ARKK fund never even came close to recovering back to 2021 highs during this massive AI Bull Cycle. This is an example of pure fundamental analysis (with very little risk management/technical analysis) leading to overall underperformance.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!UOLW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8dd4b6bb-49ac-470d-9b55-b7307af868a9_1266x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!UOLW!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8dd4b6bb-49ac-470d-9b55-b7307af868a9_1266x844.png)

This is why I believe in using a combination of fundamental analysis and technical analysis. I draw on my professional experiences as an Equity Research Analyst AND a former Prop Trader. As a Prop/Hedge Fund Trader, you are managing a lot of capital, so there are very strict risk parameters you must follow. My experiences taught me that risk management is the #1 priority for sustained success. Most importantly, risk management is the #1 priority for simply existing in this business.

This is why I firmly believe in using technical analysis to limit drawdowns/avoid holding stocks during their bear cycles. In my view, technical analysis is the **best** tool for effectively managing risk (making it a critical tool to use).

Here at Marlin Capital, we have translated this Fundamental + Technical approach into outperformance. The 2024 Small Cap 6 (+99% in ~7 months) and the 2025 SMID Cap 6 (+64% in ~7 months) are both successful examples of this combination approach in action.

But don’t just take it from me. Stanley Druckenmiller, Paul Tudor Jones, William O’Neil, Mark Minvervini, etc. are all examples of legendary traders/investors who also utilize some type of “fundamental + technical” approach.

**General Market Analysis**

Earlier, I mentioned that many stocks are currently in different stages. This is confirmed by the data.

We are in a split market right now. Many stocks are making new highs, while many others are making new lows. In normal market conditions, you will see one group dominate (new highs or new lows). When both extremes are expanding at the same time, it shows the market is deeply divided (and potentially fragile). This typically only happens near market tops.

The NYSE High-Low Logic Index just jumped above 1.82. Past jumps above 1.82 have have typically always resolved in more market downside. In fact, the average 12M max drawdown was -17% after this happened. Following these signals, the SPX fell 80% of the time 2 months later (not a good omen).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IZk_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F40ca267e-804d-48c3-b3e1-d428b429765b_2048x1802.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IZk_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F40ca267e-804d-48c3-b3e1-d428b429765b_2048x1802.jpeg)

We are also in a “low correlation” market right now. The S&P 500 is essentially flat over the past month, but the average stock inside the index has moved roughly ~11%. This spread ranks in the 99th %ile over the last 30 years (only exceeded during the dotcom bust and the 2008 GFC).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gVAl!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc82a579b-17f9-45db-ac3c-d074b000ca29_974x720.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gVAl!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc82a579b-17f9-45db-ac3c-d074b000ca29_974x720.jpeg)
