# Friday Morning Notes 9.22

Technical Update

The market staged an ugly sell off on Thursday with non-stop selling post-Fed meeting. The SPX closed -1.64% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QQ1t!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F90f48420-589c-4c41-8371-246c1e9fd259_971x727.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QQ1t!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F90f48420-589c-4c41-8371-246c1e9fd259_971x727.jpeg)

We briefly broke below August lows in the SPX and closed right at $4330 support. We are set for a small gap up this morning.

Key support levels to watch below include $4330, and $4200. Big picture, I am initially targeting a move to $4200 and the 200 day moving average for the S&P 500. With that said, we enter Friday very short term oversold after a day and a half of nonstop selling.
