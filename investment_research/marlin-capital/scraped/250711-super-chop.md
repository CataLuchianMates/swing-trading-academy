# Super Chop

Super choppy day in the market today with the SPX closing +0.27%, NDX -0.16%, and RTY +0.51%.

Goldman’s High Beta Momentum Basket (GSPRHIMO) finished the day -2.7% driven by both the long leg lower (-1%) and short leg higher (+1.7%). The momentum pair is now down -12% this month, its biggest drawdown since February.

It’s been a tricky start to July so far despite the SPX up +1.3% on the month. Underneath the surface, the action remains very choppy and mixed at best. Not an ideal environment for swing trading in my view, as we are seeing big fades and random rug pulls like in cybersecurity today, a former leading sector (CRWD -5%, NET -6%, RBRK -6%, ZS -6%, FTNT -7%, PANW -7%).

The thematic baskets with the lowest momentum exposure are having the strongest starts to July (along with Bitcoin sensitives w/ BTC breaking out to ATHs). High Beta 12M Losers/Laggards are up +11.1% so far on the month, the best performing basket. In many ways, July can be characterized as a rotation from leaders to laggards (so far).

Despite the July rotation to laggards, momentum dips are typically good entry points on a longer time frame.

One way to avoid a choppy market environment is to focus on “stocks in play”. This refers to stocks that are experiencing high trading volume and volatility due to an earnings/news catalyst. With earnings season starting up next week, there should be plenty of “stocks in play” for us to choose from.
