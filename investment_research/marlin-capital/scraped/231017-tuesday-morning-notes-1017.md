# Tuesday Morning Notes 10.17

Daily Market Commentary

The S&P 500 rallied to kick off the week on Monday, finishing the day +1.06%.  We closed right at the 21 EMA resistance level at $4370-$4380.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IGaH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8a0898e2-405d-4dc8-98a2-87609633185a_955x707.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IGaH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8a0898e2-405d-4dc8-98a2-87609633185a_955x707.jpeg)

The action yesterday had a short squeeze tone to it, with non-profitable tech, low profit r2k, and 12 month losers leading the charge.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!h0Zp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F93273eef-b246-4b84-ac39-f20f00fe3ef1_766x632.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!h0Zp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F93273eef-b246-4b84-ac39-f20f00fe3ef1_766x632.jpeg)

Monday’s and Thursday’s have been the strongest days for the S&P 500 this year.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!anIO!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc8ec7961-893b-412b-9b1e-670d5c0564e9_968x732.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!anIO!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc8ec7961-893b-412b-9b1e-670d5c0564e9_968x732.jpeg)

Meanwhile, the QQQ finished off Monday +1.13% and formed an inside day.  Key levels to watch for Tuesday – above $370.5 = bullish, below $366.5 = bearish.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4-B1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F74c9059f-9ad1-4812-afd2-2912748ebfbf_968x732.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4-B1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F74c9059f-9ad1-4812-afd2-2912748ebfbf_968x732.jpeg)

The VIX has not closed above 20 since May 24th, the longest streak since 2018.  However, some traders are betting on that streak coming to an end.  Last Friday, VIX call volume hit the 2nd highest level in history.  With that said, VIX put volume also hit the highest level in history on the same day, as not all traders are betting on a surge in volatility.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-dUj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe9ca4396-43ad-42ce-85ab-be8e20e95f78_910x540.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-dUj!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe9ca4396-43ad-42ce-85ab-be8e20e95f78_910x540.jpeg)

Futures are pointing to a gap down this morning as earnings season gets under way.  12% of the SPX market cap is slated to report this week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Y5nK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6b66a205-9ba5-4f2d-bfda-69b1f363b548_822x444.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Y5nK!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6b66a205-9ba5-4f2d-bfda-69b1f363b548_822x444.jpeg)
