The consistent storylines all year have been: Strength in the Magnificent 7 / Mega Cap Tech (NDX outperformed NDXE by +12% YTD) and narrow breadth (just 4 stocks account for >60% of NDX gains this year).  Despite the R2K Tech index +10% YTD, nearly 60% of the names in the index are actually down for the year.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!40Vs!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F913b420d-ba28-49df-a83e-615d78af2d33_1089x785.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!40Vs!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F913b420d-ba28-49df-a83e-615d78af2d33_1089x785.jpeg)

The Generative AI theme has remained hot in the 1st half with Goldman’s AI Basket +25% and >100% gains in NVDA, SMCI, and ARM.

As we enter the 2nd half of the year, it feels like investors remain confident in the names that are working (Mag 7 + AI Leaders).  Much has been made about Small Caps and other laggards starting to join the party (software like CRM, PANW, MDB; internet laggards, and even some left behind semis).

Consensus expects all 5 of the Mega Cap AI-related stocks to report a slowdown in sales growth into the 2H of the year (from +22% in Q1 to +14% in Q4).  In contrast, sales growth for the median S&P 500 stocks is projected to accelerate, albeit from a much slower rate (YoY growth of +2%, +3%, +5%, and +5%, respectively).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K9jF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F95c1bf50-7895-4b1a-a508-426efd1627c4_1129x515.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K9jF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F95c1bf50-7895-4b1a-a508-426efd1627c4_1129x515.jpeg)

The Mega Cap EPS growth premium is also projected to narrow, as the 31 percentage point gap is expected to compress to 8 percentage points in 2025 and 4pp in 2026.

All of this bodes well for some 1st half laggards performing better in the 2nd half of 2024.  My top laggard group to watch in the 2nd half is Software.  At the beginning of June, Software stocks were extremely cheap vs Semis (and versus its Macro variables).  This cheapness still exists even after the IGV Software Index rallied +11% in June.  If economic growth is slowing, falling rates will become a bigger narrative, and this backdrop should be a catalyst for secular growth + software trading better in the 2H.

One huge positive for the 2nd half of 2024 is (ironically?) the 1st half of 2024.  Success begets more success.

Since 1900, there have been 25 years when the S&P 500 total return exceeded +15% in the 1H (excluding 2024).  The 1st half of 2024 was the 21st best 1H since 1900.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jBws!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6567f71b-8d51-49bb-938e-a2d26dc491c0_975x942.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jBws!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6567f71b-8d51-49bb-938e-a2d26dc491c0_975x942.png)

The last time the 2nd half was down after being +15% in 1H was 1986.  It is not common to see a negative 2H after being up big to start the year, as FOMO starts to set in with investors chasing stocks higher.  This has only happened 7 times since 1900 (7/25; 28%).

Since 1900, if the S&P returns exceeds +15% in the 1H, the median return for 2H is +8.9% with a 72% hit rate.  This exceeds the +7.3% median return for all years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!S_Nq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F62eea871-2c58-4c45-bce4-73e439b158db_1165x760.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!S_Nq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F62eea871-2c58-4c45-bce4-73e439b158db_1165x760.jpeg)

2024 is also the 3rd best start to a presidential election year since 1900 (when William McKinley was president).  Only 1908 (William Howard Taft) and 1976 (Jimmy Carter) were off to a better start.  The 2nd half also remained strong in those years – 1908 rallied +20.9% and 1976 rallied +5% in the 2H.

The average return for November and December is +2.9% (since 1900).  During election years, that number grows to +3.4%.

Regardless of who wins the election, the election result is typically seen as a risk clearing event.  Election years exceed non-election years in the final 2 months of the year.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NvFy!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffa324e3e-4515-49a5-8252-152343b878b4_302x675.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NvFy!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffa324e3e-4515-49a5-8252-152343b878b4_302x675.png)

This week starts the best seasonal period of the year for US stocks.  Historically, the 1H of July is the best 2-week stretch on the calendar.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!a_jB!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0cde35f-edc6-4e2d-b4a4-b325cc15578d_988x658.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!a_jB!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0cde35f-edc6-4e2d-b4a4-b325cc15578d_988x658.jpeg)

Moreover, the NDX has finished positive for 16 straight July’s with an average return of +4.6%.  As mentioned, July’s returns are typically frontloaded (the 1st 2 weeks being much better).  After this positive trading window ends, Q2 earnings season will begin and the news/EPS cycle will take over.
