# Wednesday Morning Notes 5.24

Technical Update

The S&P 500 pulled back yesterday after failing to decisively break above the $4200 resistance level. The index closed -1.12% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!J0nJ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe11aef4d-feb2-4951-b384-e5ed6dcce29c_984x717.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!J0nJ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe11aef4d-feb2-4951-b384-e5ed6dcce29c_984x717.jpeg)

Technically, this setup remains healthy if 21 EMA support ($4130-$4140) can hold. If this level breaks, the next support level to watch below is $4090-$4100.

As I mentioned at the start of the week, a pullback was necessary to shake off short term overbought readings. With that said, the pull back has now occurred and bulls would like to see buyers start to step in at these key support levels.

Meanwhile, many of the names on my watchlist are failing at key support or failing to break out decisively. While it is too early to get bearish on the indices on a technical basis, the action in individual stocks remains less than ideal.
