# Friday Morning Notes 7.12

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Very interesting day for US stocks yesterday as market cap weighted equities dragged the SPX down -0.88% and NDX -2.2% lower, which now represents the biggest 1-day drawdown for the index of the year.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!V7E-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbea2cd59-e588-4d7e-a0ef-61a40881ae56_1213x887.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!V7E-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbea2cd59-e588-4d7e-a0ef-61a40881ae56_1213x887.jpeg)

All Mag 7 names finished > -2% lower on the day.  It has been 48 days since all the Mag 7 names closed red on the same day, the longest streak in >10 years.

For the SPX YTD, yesterday ended as the 9th worst day in terms of performance and the 10th best day in terms of breadth with 395/500 (79%!) finishing in the green. The NYSE had 3423 advancers vs 689 decliners (4x1) while the Nasdaq hit ~3x1 advancers to decliners.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!krvS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2d5e2a63-f8f2-422f-9cff-73ef2217094d_434x228.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!krvS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2d5e2a63-f8f2-422f-9cff-73ef2217094d_434x228.png)

The moves in Mega Cap felt huge yesterday, but its important to keep some context.

NDX -2.2%…back to where it was trading a week ago (7/5)

NVDA -5%...back to where it was trading on Monday (7/8)

Semis -4%...back to where it was trading a week ago (7/5)

AAPL-3%...back to where it was trading on Monday (7/8)

NDX/RTY -5%...top 3 biggest move in spread on record, but overall, back to where the spread was in mid-June (6/24) (which speaks to how much small caps have underperformed for a long time).

As mentioned, Small Caps outperformed Large Caps by 4.5%, a 6 sigma event and the 2nd biggest outperformance on record (trailing only the 10 sigma move on October 10th, 2008).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aqRz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F07fd820f-3710-4c58-a181-aa113e3ca4e7_1213x672.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aqRz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F07fd820f-3710-4c58-a181-aa113e3ca4e7_1213x672.png)

The IWM finished +3.59% on the day, and looks to finally be breaking higher out of this long consolidation period.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HnMo!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb53eac10-0a17-4926-bfe0-c323ae6c6ac9_970x724.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HnMo!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb53eac10-0a17-4926-bfe0-c323ae6c6ac9_970x724.jpeg)

CPI was clearly soft with Core MoM coming in at 6bps, 14bps below consensus and the slowest pace since way back in Jan ’21.  The market is now pricing in just over 2 rate cuts for 2024.

The big debate now is whether or not this is the start of a bigger rotational period (outside of Mega Cap, Mag 7, Semis, YTD winners) and into Small Caps, YTD losers, Biotech, S&P 493, etc.

Trading activity picked up yesterday but certainly not in a frenzied state like we have seen during previous CPI readings.  Total market volumes were trending +17% vs 20dma.  IWM call volume hit a new record high as traders chased the move higher + hedge funds hedging their small cap shorts with calls/call spreads.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XYfS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff17f85c6-472c-4cbf-ac12-18bb18695e4a_1163x666.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XYfS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff17f85c6-472c-4cbf-ac12-18bb18695e4a_1163x666.jpeg)

From where I’m standing, yesterday felt more like a knee jerk reaction than something more sustainable.  With that said, can this rotation out of YTD winners (Mag 7, Semis) and into YTD losers (Small Caps) last more than 1 day? Absolutely. The deciding factor will likely be this earnings season.

In my view, earnings will take center stage from here and the future performance of Mega Cap will rely heavily on their results to be released in the next ~2 weeks.  The expectations are high, and if we see disappointing results from the Mag 7, we could be in store for a more sustainable rotation out of these names.  In the meantime, some selling/consolidation in Mag 7 into their earnings reports would not be the worst thing in terms of the stock setups for post-earnings.

Updates on my trades –

NVO – Keeping my closing stop at $138 on my shares. No changes here.

MSFT – Sold near yesterday’s close for a +3% gain (closed below $455).  Position closed.

AVGO – Rotation out of semis yesterday hit AVGO, but I am still holding with a closing stop below $1630.

AMD – Pulled back along with semis yesterday, but the chart still looks good here.  Closing stop set below $170.
