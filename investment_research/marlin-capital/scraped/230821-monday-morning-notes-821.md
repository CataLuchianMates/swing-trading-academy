# Monday Morning Notes 8.21

Technical Update

The S&P 500 closed flat on Friday after breaking below key support late last week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2F8O!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F703f6f77-0c27-4378-9af7-c059516c2368_974x718.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2F8O!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F703f6f77-0c27-4378-9af7-c059516c2368_974x718.jpeg)

For all intents and purposes, the technical uptrend that began in late March following the regional banking crisis (and Fed intervention) has ended. A new downtrend has begun in the SPX daily chart.

Breadth has taken a beating. Less than 27% of SPX components are still trading above their respective 20-day moving averages, down from 80% just a month ago.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vO1g!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9d6d52a4-f272-4d0c-b81b-4d32a4690e05_1144x640.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vO1g!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9d6d52a4-f272-4d0c-b81b-4d32a4690e05_1144x640.jpeg)

With that said, we are heading into this week short term oversold. Currently, 14% of the SPX has an RSI under 30.

14% may not seem like a lot, but this is actually in the 94th percentile over the past decade.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!F17v!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F87c60b71-c733-4bda-8f41-e1bb882d7ce6_1144x640.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!F17v!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F87c60b71-c733-4bda-8f41-e1bb882d7ce6_1144x640.jpeg)

While some pockets of strength remain on my watchlist among the earnings gappers, many leaders are succumbing to the pressure of the general market. NVDA earnings this week will be a big catalysts for semiconductors and tech in general.

I will be exiting my small position in CLS at today’s open. The stock is acting quite strong relative to the general market, but I don’t see a favorable risk/reward with the tide changing and weak market seasonality in September (the weakest month of the year for the SPX, see below).

Big picture, I’m looking for a potential oversold bounce in the early part of this week and further downside in September.

Key resistance levels to watch above include $4400 and $4450-$4460.

Key support levels to watch below include $4350-$4360 and 4300. Big picture, I believe I move to $4200 and $4130-$4150 (the 200 SMA) will be in the cards for September.
