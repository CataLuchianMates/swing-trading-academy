**Watchlist:**

That does not mean new leaders aren’t emerging, as they clearly. With the S&P 500 hitting all time highs, my goal as a trader is to own the leading stocks. This means owning stocks that are at or near ATHs versus growth that remains down 40%+. I’m happy just owning AFRM there. All my positions have a shorter leash when accounting for the market environment.

Today, I’ll be watching MRVL, MU, DLTR, and RBLX.

**11:07 EST**

Stopped out of AFRM at $102 for a loss. Growth stocks are laggards right now and AFRM is in a clear downtrend. The call flow got me interested, but it needed to follow through today for me. Obviously, I have plenty of exposure to AFRM in my long term account, so there is no need to take a big risk here on a short term trade.

I will also be closing PSTG today at breakeven. I don’t like how it has been rejected twice at the $35 resistance area. PSTG and AFRM were lower conviction plays than AMD and CL, so I don’t mind closing my positions there.

If you’re looking to trade this market, I would continue to look elsewhere beyond growth tech. DLTR gave a decent entry today, but I did not take it.

I’m holding AMD and CL as these are 2 leaders that have seen nice call flow the past few days.
