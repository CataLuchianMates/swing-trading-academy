# Wednesday Afternoon Notes 5.28

Stocks began the holiday shortened week on high note yesterday with a gap up and steadily added to their gains as the session carried on.

The main catalyst was President Trump announcing that he will be delaying the implementation of his recommended 50% tariff rate for the EU until July 9 to allow more time to negotiate a deal.

Treasury yields also took a welcome step back, bolstered by reports that Japan is considering reducing its issuance of ultra-long bonds. The 10-yr note yield fell eight basis points to 4.43%, cutting below the closely watched 4.50% level, and the 30-yr bond yield dropped 10 basis points to 4.94%, moving below the closely watched 5.00% level.

US stocks continue to benefit from sidelined investors getting squeezed back into the action by a fear of missing out on further gains. Total positioning still sits in “light territory” heading into this week, though well off the April panic lows.

As I have covered in detail over the last month at Marlin Capital, systematic fund flows have been a major tailwind for stocks. That bullish tailwind is losing some of its luster here as Systematic positioning is now moderately net long. To be clear, systematic flows are still a slightly bullish tailwind here (albeit much smaller than they have been).

All eyes are on Nvidia’s earnings post-market close now. The market is flattish on low volume today heading into the report.

GS Prime Desk gives NVDA a 7/10 positioning score heading into the report. The stock is now +50% from April lows and now just ~10% below ATHs, though it has been fully range bound for the past year now as the revenue beats have normalized.

As I mentioned on Monday, NVDA’s report is a critical one for the general market and the AI optimism trade that has led the market rebound since April lows. The implied move for NVDA is +/- 6.6% by the options market.

No plans for me to do anything ahead of NVDA’s report today. Holding my current longs in my Active Portfolio with stops in place.
