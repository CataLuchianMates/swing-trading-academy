It’s hard to believe APLD hit $34 back in 2021 when Bitcoin Miners were going nuts (if you wanted to know where we currently are in relation to the 2021 Covid Bubble). Because now, Applied Digital is a legitimate AI Data Center business backed by Nvidia with a near-term path to $1.24B+ ARR and a future pipeline to $7B+ in ARR. This move (unlike the ‘21 Covid Bubble) is backed by real financials, and it hasn’t even gotten crazy yet. Crazy would be pricing in the full $7B ARR pipeline x 6 P/S = $42B+ valuation (which would take APLD to $120+).

But maybe that’s not so crazy…

Because Aligned Data Centers, a private AI data center business, was just bought by Blackrock’s Global Infrastructure Partners in a deal that values the company at $40B. It’s been reported that Aligned Data Centers is “poised to expand its capacity to over 5 GW”.

In comparison, Applied Digital’s CEO announced that the company currently has a 4 GW pipeline on their 10/9 earnings call.

And yet, Applied Digital is currently valued at $10.6B versus Aligned Data Centers buyout valuation of $40B (nearly 1/4th the valuation of Aligned).

**Aligned Data Centers:** $40B valuation at 5 GW = $8B per GW

**Applied Digital:** $10.6B valuation at 4 GW = $2.65B per GW

**At the same valuation as Aligned Data Centers ($8B per GW), APLD would be valued at $32B.** That would be a 3x from here, putting APLD above $100.

My first target for APLD on this 4+ year base breakout is $65+, which would be a $20B+ market cap. APLD continues to be 1 of my favorite growth stories in the market, and the stock has grown into my biggest position overall.

#### General Market Analysis

As I talked about in the beginning of tonight’s report, the overall market action has been mixed recently (since Friday’s selloff).

There has been a fair amount of dispersion underneath the hood with Non-Profitable Tech up +4% today and +18% in October.

In fact, Non-Profitable Tech has outperformed the SPX by +19% in October, which is the biggest monthly spread in 5+ years (since the 2020 GME/AMC Meme Stock Mania).

The bounceback since Friday has largely been a lower quality rally (most shorted + non-profitable tech outperforming big). In other pockets of the market, some weakness is beginning to emerge.

Former leading AI semi names like ALAB and CRDO are breaking below their 60 EMAs. NVDA, the AI king, is not participating in this post-Friday rebound. Momentum appears to be stalling in former leading names like META, HOOD, PLTR, RDDT, etc. Even very recent momentum leaders like OKLO, IONQ, UAMY, etc. appear to be stalling.

Sometimes, you see lower quality names outperform on the final leg of a rally. This means we could potentially be towards the end of our recent run-up and entering a consolidation/pullback period.

I remain bullish into year-end, but pullback/consolidation periods are a normal and healthy part of Bull Markets. In these choppy periods, I try not to overtrade because breakouts often fail and it becomes easy to lose money (within choppy bases). For new subscribers, I recommend reading [“September Lull”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/september-lull), where I cover an example of a winning stock (TSLA in 2020-21) and how there are opportune times to be aggressive vs passive (especially for how I like to trade).

Based on the overall market action, I do not see right now as a time to be aggressive on new trades. I’m keeping a small watchlist heading into tomorrow (and no surprise, the top names I’m seeing right now are mostly lower quality names fundamentally).
