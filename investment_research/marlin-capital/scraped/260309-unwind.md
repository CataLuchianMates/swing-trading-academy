# Unwind

Marlin Capital Readers,

Friday was an ugly close to a brutal week for US stocks.

The S&P 500 fell -1.3% on Friday and closed at $6,740, its lowest close of 2026. The index is now down -1.7% YTD and -3.4% off its all-time high.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!32Zf!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F80ddb838-c8bd-48f6-9aa4-354ca8afff03_1267x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!32Zf!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F80ddb838-c8bd-48f6-9aa4-354ca8afff03_1267x844.png)

On the week, the SPX lost -2%, its worst weekly performance in nearly 5 months.

It was a rough week on multiple fronts. The US and Israel launched strikes on Iran last weekend, and the fallout has been swift. The Strait of Hormuz is effectively closed, cutting off roughly ~20% of global oil supply. Oil surged +33% on the week (WTI hit $90+), its biggest weekly gain since oil futures trading began in 1983.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZCrI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5c4c832-e123-4f10-a4c9-3d7bd01d124c_2750x1568.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZCrI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5c4c832-e123-4f10-a4c9-3d7bd01d124c_2750x1568.png)

Rising oil prices mean rising inflation risk, which means the Fed is unlikely to cut rates as much (or as soon). Treasury yields climbed, with the 10 YR yield hitting 4.13%. The VIX spiked to 29.5. **Risk off action across the board.**

Then, Friday’s jobs report poured gasoline on the fire. Nonfarm payrolls fell -92,000 in February, a massive miss vs. expectations of +50,000. The unemployment rate rose to 4.4%.

This is the worst jobs print in years, and it is worth addressing the elephant in the room: AI. In [“Heading Into March”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/heading-into-march?utm_source=publication-search), I talked about Block cutting 40% of its workforce and CEO Jack Dorsey predicting that “within the next year, the majority of companies will reach the same conclusion and make similar structural changes”. Is Friday’s awful jobs number the first real sign of Dorsey’s prediction playing out? I said I would be watching this closely. Now, I am watching it even closer.

**But none of this “Risk Off” market action has caught us off guard here at Marlin Capital.**

We have been preparing for this type of market environment for months. Capital preservation mode is paying off for us.

With that said, here is how we are currently positioned heading into this week. 👇

We are currently 100% cash in 2 of our 3 **Marlin Capital Portfolios** (Trump Trades and Active Trading), and 43% cash with active short positions working in the other.

But most importantly, am I starting to buy the dip here? Or do I see more downside ahead?

Here’s where I think we might be headed next. 👇

**2026 Portfolio**

**Long Positions**

It was a tough week for our remaining longs in this portfolio. Many of the leading names we have been holding are suddenly showing signs of entering Stage 3 tops or Stage 4 downtrends. Up until late last week, our longs were still holding up well (and many even making new highs) while the market health deteriorated. As of Friday, that may no longer be the case. Stage transitions can happen in an instant, so you must be ready to act.

According to Nomura, S&P 500 dispersion is currently in the 97th percentile. In other words, individual stocks have been moving in completely different directions. Some going up, some going down, with very little correlation to each other. This is the “stock picker’s market” I have talked about, and it is the reason we were able to hold our leaders confidently while everything else was falling apart.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Hz8A!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe636aed1-c9ac-44e7-80c4-81470862a1cd_1155x975.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Hz8A!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe636aed1-c9ac-44e7-80c4-81470862a1cd_1155x975.jpeg)

But here is the problem. Late last week, it appears we may have suddenly entered a “sell everything” type of environment where nothing is spared. And we may have gotten our first real evidence of that shift on Friday.

According to Goldman Prime, hedge funds de-grossed Tech at a 98th percentile pace last week (largest since July 2024 and -1.8z). In other words, institutional investors heavily sold longs and covered shorts (unwinding risk).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DnFP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F63e441b4-be7a-45db-8722-9fa537cc6bfc_1050x790.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DnFP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F63e441b4-be7a-45db-8722-9fa537cc6bfc_1050x790.jpeg)

Friday was the textbook definition of a de-grossing event. The Nomura chart below tells the story perfectly. 👇

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!x3pc!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe929636b-0fe0-48bc-8f95-c4e8765854bb_782x590.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!x3pc!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe929636b-0fe0-48bc-8f95-c4e8765854bb_782x590.png)

On Friday, the market leaders (crowded longs) got hit the hardest while the laggards outperformed the most (driven by short covering). This is the textbook definition of a momentum unwind.

If we are now entering a correlated selloff, all names are likely to get pulled lower. And in my view, last week’s price action was the first real sign that this shift may be underway. The final leaders (Optics names like LITE, COHR, VIAV, AAOI, AXTI) appear to have potentially topped last week. Memory stocks began showing signs of topping a little earlier. When the final leaders start breaking down, that is a sign that the high dispersion market is ending (and a correction is likely coming). The environment where our leaders could hold up while everything else fell apart may be over.

With that said, all of our long positions are on the chopping block heading into this week.

**General Market Analysis**

Right now, the SPX is only -3.4% off ATHs. This does not even qualify as a mini correction (-5%) for the indices yet.

But it feels much worse than -3.4%. And that is because, underneath the surface, individual stocks are getting absolutely clobbered. Former 2025 leaders are down -30%, -40%, -50%, -60% ++ from their highs. We have had a [“Bear Market level” of individual stock blowups](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-clusters?utm_source=publication-search) over the past couple months. Moreover, we have now had 7 Hindenburg Omen’s in the last ~5 weeks ([a very bearish signal for future returns](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/mixed-market-a68?utm_source=publication-search)).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Diaz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4682cdf6-bfbc-42c4-a34c-52615addafe7_2048x1458.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Diaz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4682cdf6-bfbc-42c4-a34c-52615addafe7_2048x1458.jpeg)

As I always say, it’s a market of stocks. The indices do not even tell half the story right now.

In my view, this price action is very reminiscent of 2022. In late 2021/2022, individual stocks were getting destroyed long before the indices officially entered correction territory. The damage underneath the surface was far worse than the headline index numbers suggested. That is exactly what is happening right now.

**The Big Picture**

We have some key support levels looming below for the indices. I would expect to see a bounce there (SPX ~$6600). But after the bounce, if we roll over, I see another wave down to $6,300 for the SPX. That is another -6.5% downside from here, and would put us in official market correction territory (-10%).

As for individual names, you should expect to see more downside ahead if the market follows my correction path. It is unlikely any stocks will be spared if the indices are headed to $6,300. Even the strongest leaders will get hit, as the “High Dispersion” market is likely ending. There is no escaping the selling.

Remember the Mark Minervini 50-80 Rule.

> “When a secular leader makes a major top, there’s a 50% chance it will drop 80% and an 80% chance it will drop 50%”
>
> - Mark Minervini

In other words, you should expect 50-80%+ downside for **all** individual stocks in their Stage 4 downtrends.

I will begin looking to deploy my cash around the $6,300 SPX level (particularly in the Trump Trades/Multi-Year Investments Portfolio). Until then, I am being patient, preserving capital, and letting the market come to me.

As I have emphasized over the past few weeks, capital preservation IS the strategy right now.
