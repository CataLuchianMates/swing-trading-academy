# Thursday Morning Notes 8.15

Quiet day yesterday following the benign CPI print (core +17bps MoM) with the S&P 500 finishing +0.38% on the day.

Given that the key fear is now more about growth risk (versus inflation previously), we arguably have more sensitivity to the retail and claims numbers coming out this week. Starting with retail sales numbers released this morning, it appears bulls have dodged this obstacle:

Retail Sales 1.0%, Exp. 0.4%

Retail Sales ex auto 0.4%, Exp. 0.1%

We are set for a nice gap up this morning on the retail sales beat.

Flow wise, CTA estimates per this morning are no longer a headwind (this will remain noisy but as long as the SPX remains above the ST threshold of $5432 & MT threshold of $5264). GS estimates this group net sold $14B in S&P over the last week, but over the next week will be buyers across all scenarios.

Active trading strategy -

Holding AXON, SN, and SHOP with stops in place.

Top trade setup for today -

SG -

Bull flagging post earnings. A break above $35-$35.15 resistance could bring us to $40+.
