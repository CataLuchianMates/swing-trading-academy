# Tuesday Morning Notes 10.11

Technical Analysis

The market kicked off the week by breaking down and hitting new 52 week lows. There was a bit of a late afternoon bounce in the indices as they closed off lows of the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OzDb!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd1cba835-6bb3-4c7d-aaa3-bd40e378b35a_1246x941.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OzDb!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd1cba835-6bb3-4c7d-aaa3-bd40e378b35a_1246x941.jpeg)

The technical setup is the same story I have been discussing since Wave 3 down started. The structure remains highly bearish and points to more downside, while bounces should continue to come as we reach oversold levels.

Resistance levels ahead to watch on a bounce include $270, $273-$274, and $278-$281.

Yesterday’s low is the key support level to watch on the downside. If this level breaks, my target on the QQQ is $258-$260 before a larger bounce.
