# Monday Morning Notes 7.10

Technical Update

The S&P 500 formed an ugly looking candle on Friday with a later afternoon sell off into the weekend. The index closed -0.29% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SFhp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F881af583-8bcc-4771-ba69-6754ebeea97a_959x722.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SFhp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F881af583-8bcc-4771-ba69-6754ebeea97a_959x722.jpeg)

It should be noted that volume was light for the entirety of last week with holiday trading in full effect. With that said, we have a couple technical reasons for caution as we head into this week. As I mentioned in Friday’s morning notes, RSI has potentially made a lower high (not yet confirmed) for the SPX while MACD has now crossed over negative.

Lighter volume / lower liquidity trading often leads to more chop and directionless trading, as it takes less shares to change hands in order to create big swings. As long as we are trading between the 8 and 21 EMAs, I will watch and observe which way the market wants to go from here. I have no plans to add any positions until that point.

Key support levels I am watching include $4380-$4385 and $4355-$4360 (21 EMA).

Key resistance levels I am watching include $4410 (8 EMA), $4420, and $4440.
