**Q:** Do you consider selling some of your holdings? I sold portion of my LCID, GOOG and SOFI today mainly because I was on margin. I'm frustrated to see SOFI/ABNB/AFRM goes up and now almost back to where it starts. My SOFI's cost is ~15, regret not selling 50% when it was $24+. Was it still worth selling at price $16 in your opinion?

**David Marlin:** For companies that I’m bullish on long term, I don’t want to sell because of tax consequences.  For newer buys, I do consider selling.  If you’re on margin, absolutely as this is not the market to have leverage on.  I personally shift the money that I use in my Active portfolio to hedge my LT holdings, or in this case, get net short.

---

**Q:** That seems quite a distance to go on these charts in a straight line down without a black swan mar 20 covid crash like event. Do you expect a big bounce first to unwind some of the bearish bets and high IV on ARKK/QQQ?

**David Marlin:** ARKK topped out in February.  It’s consolidated since, but failed to make a new high.  Now, it is making lower lows and it looks ready for another leg lower.  We are already in a bear market if you ignore the window dressing that is the indices.  Many dominoes have already fallen (ZM, TDOC, ROKU, FVRR, PTON, etc.).  This is what I believe to be happening right now.  The question then, will be, what happens to the indices.  I don’t believe the indices will reach bear market territory (20% down) because I don’t believe this is the end of the secular bull market.  I believe this is the popping of the high valuation stock bubbles, triggered by the Fed getting hawkish.
