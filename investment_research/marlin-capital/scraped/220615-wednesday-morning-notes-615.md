# Wednesday Morning Notes 6.15

Federal Reserve Decision at 2 EST Today:

Marlin Capital Thoughts-

As some have correctly pointed out, in my opinion, a 50bp hike may be seen as a negative  development for the economy and the stock market because it shows the Fed is still behind the curve.  The market cannot begin to price in an end to this crisis when the Fed is not doing enough to put an end to it.

A 75bp hike is now the consensus and is what most are expecting heading into today’s decision.  This could potentially lead to an “event clearing squeeze” like we have seen on several past FOMC meeting days.

A 100bp hike is unlikely in my opinion.  If it happens, it could come as an initial shock to the market.  Once that shock wears off, though, I believe the market would feel more comfortable with the Fed’s ability to get a handle on inflation.  As of now, the Fed has a big credibility problem, and most investors do not believe the Central Bank is “on the ball” when it comes to the inflation crisis.
