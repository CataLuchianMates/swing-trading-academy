Here are today’s new buys. 👇

**LRCX**

Lam Research reported earnings Wednesday night, and the report was a blowout. Q3 revenue came in at a record $5.84B (up +24% YoY and the company’s 3rd consecutive record revenue quarter), with EPS exceeding the high end of the guided range. But the real story, as always, was the guidance. Lam guided next quarter revenue to $6.6B (plus or minus $400M), ~10% above Street consensus, with record EPS of $1.65 at the midpoint. Management also raised its full-year WFE (wafer fab equipment) outlook to $140B “with a bias to the upside,” setting up another strong year of industry growth in 2027. LRCX is the direct beneficiary of the same Memory Supercycle I have been talking about all year. HBM and advanced packaging demand are driving record equipment orders, and Lam one of the purest plays on that capex cycle. This is a fundamental + technical leader, and I want exposure.

**CRWV**

CoreWeave has been on an absolute tear following a series of massive deal announcements. In the last 2 weeks alone, the company has signed an expanded **$21B long-term AI cloud agreement with Meta** (through 2032), a new **$6B commitment from Jane Street** (who also took a $1B equity stake at $109/share), and a multi-year Anthropic deal to run Claude at production scale. The Meta deal alone extends CRWV’s already-massive contracted backlog. This is one of the purest AI infrastructure momentum plays on the tape, and 9 of the top 10 leading AI model providers run at least some portion of their workloads on CoreWeave’s platform. When you see this kind of deal flow, this kind of customer concentration, and this kind of price action, its best to pay attention. I am initiating a position today.

**AMPX**

Amprius is a battery leader that has delivered 4 consecutive quarter of triple-digit YoY revenue growth and its first quarter of positive EBITDA. Management guided 2026 revenue to at least $125M (up from $73M in 2025, +71% growth), with a projected net loss under $8M, compared to a $44M net loss last year. The silicon anode battery story is finally starting to convert fundamentals into scale. The company also just announced a $21M SiCore cylindrical cell order from a leading Chinese EV manufacturer, which is an important signal that their technology is moving beyond drones into the much larger EV end market. AMPX reports Q1 earnings on May 7th. I want to be positioned ahead of that report. Battery plays (EOSE, AMPX, SLDP) have been quietly strengthening here, and AMPX is the fundamental leader of the group.

**AMSC**

AMSC was one of my original [Small Cap 6 picks](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-small-caps-with-massive-upside?utm_source=publication-search) all the way back in 2024, and this chart is setting up again with a massive new catalyst behind it. On Monday, President Trump invoked Section 303 of the Defense Production Act on US grid infrastructure, formally declaring transformers, high-voltage transmission components, advanced conductors, power electronics, and substations as "dangerously limited" and essential to national defense. This is not a standard infrastructure bill. This is wartime-level mobilization. The federal government is now authorized to make direct purchases, financial commitments, and investments to rapidly expand domestic grid manufacturing, with normal procedural requirements waived. Price and time are no longer concerns. And AMSC is squarely in the crosshairs of this buildout, with exposure in 2 of the named categories (HTS wires as advanced conductors and power control electronics). On top of that, the company was already riding 2 major secular tailwinds before Monday (grid modernization / power resiliency and naval defense power systems), with 27% compounded annual sales growth over the last 5 years and revenue up +51% over the last 12 months. In my view, the remainder of 2026 will likely bring a wave of DOE contracts for names like this. For AMSC, this catalyst should add fuel. This is my way to play the Defense Production Act grid theme in the small cap space.
