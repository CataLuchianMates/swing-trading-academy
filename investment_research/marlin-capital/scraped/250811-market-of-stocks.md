# Market of Stocks

US equity indices advanced last week, rebounding from the prior week’s sell off. Tech led the way with the Nasdaq closing the week at record highs.

The catalyst? Earnings.

Q2 Earnings have been great across the board. 73% of S&P 500 companies have beaten EPS estimates this quarter (vs historical avg 59%) by an average of +7.1%. On the topline, 78% of companies have beaten revenue estimates (vs historical avg 59%), the highest breadth of revenue beats since Q2 2021.

However, despite the strong earnings season, overall participation in this rally is dwindling.

The Mag 7 was up +5% last week while the S&P 493 was only up +1%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fprL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd3a4a656-7fe0-4a5e-afd0-4aa30322441b_1085x703.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fprL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd3a4a656-7fe0-4a5e-afd0-4aa30322441b_1085x703.jpeg)

Less than half the stocks in the S&P 500 are currently above their 20-day moving average. While the S&P 500 now sits just below record highs, the percentage of members above their 50-day moving averages has declined to just 51%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MoIS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F00843dd1-6b69-412c-8f28-6f6a1b72e7b6_1190x997.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MoIS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F00843dd1-6b69-412c-8f28-6f6a1b72e7b6_1190x997.jpeg)

And despite the strong earnings season where most all companies are beating estimates soundly, we are seeing plenty of big downside reactions/landmines (even in recent/former leaders).

In fact, the average stock has actually had a negative earnings reaction this quarter, highlighting the bifurcated market we are in.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lLNa!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8316b4e1-cda8-4318-b08c-3ab494653322_850x699.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lLNa!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8316b4e1-cda8-4318-b08c-3ab494653322_850x699.jpeg)

As I talked about [on Thursday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/slugfest), there have been huge moves in both directions this earnings season.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Z7OV!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F30027f5a-0a6b-4339-a899-695c1b281f92_1173x956.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Z7OV!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F30027f5a-0a6b-4339-a899-695c1b281f92_1173x956.jpeg)

As I always say: “**It’s a market of stocks**”. You cannot judge the health of the entire market by the S&P 500 (especially in 2025 when 35% of the index is concentrated in 7 stocks).

Right now, we are seeing weakening participation across the board. This is a clear red flag for me, as it signals a market with deteriorating overall health. With that said, it does not necessarily mean we are heading for a market correction today or tomorrow.

In a market like this, there are basically 2 possibilities from here:

(1) the rest of the market eventually catches up after the SPX breaks out to new highs

(2) the leaders/generals eventually fall and join the rest of the market below their key moving averages (in my experience, this is typically the more likely outcome)

Topping is a process. In my experience, it is not unusual for a market topping process to begin with more speculative/higher beta stocks breaking down and breadth deteriorating. In market corrections, the leaders/generals are typically the last to fall.
