# The Week Ahead

On a technical basis, we head into the first trading week of October in a precarious position.  The market bounced to end last week, but remains firmly below the key 21 and 60 EMAs on the daily chart.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PtqN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff3c3926c-7c15-4587-ab66-9480c79e0d08_1009x754.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PtqN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff3c3926c-7c15-4587-ab66-9480c79e0d08_1009x754.jpeg)

Moreover, the 21 and 60 EMAs have given a bearish crossover, the first since March.  The weekly SPX chart looks a bit better despite making a lower low last week.  We are lodged firmly between the 21 and 60 week EMAs after an indecisive candle last week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SBrb!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fea32899c-6a6a-41ee-9fa0-1b28adf6d5c1_958x717.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SBrb!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fea32899c-6a6a-41ee-9fa0-1b28adf6d5c1_958x717.jpeg)

QQQ, which has outperformed YTD, has remained the stronger chart technically during this pullback.  However, the index is also firmly below the 21 and 60 EMAs on the daily chart and is heading for a bearish crossover (the first since March).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Dt4w!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9d7ba972-af97-4dd8-81a0-935d333b5ff4_958x727.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Dt4w!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9d7ba972-af97-4dd8-81a0-935d333b5ff4_958x727.jpeg)

The IWM index of small caps lagged during the YTD rally and is the first to break below its 200-day moving average.  This chart is much uglier and appears likely to test YTD lows.  Being that small caps are often seen as a good barometer of economic health and tend to outperform during the early parts of new bull markets, this should be a major concern for bulls.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!UHIe!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F98760741-34c9-455a-b94b-eb4be16891c0_962x713.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!UHIe!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F98760741-34c9-455a-b94b-eb4be16891c0_962x713.jpeg)

We head into the first trading week of October with a lot of uncertainty regarding the technical setups.  In my view, caution and patience are both very much warranted here until we see how the market reacts at key support and resistance levels.

Speaking of levels, the key resistance levels I am watching in the SPX include $4300, $4320-$4330, $4340-$4350, $4370, and $4400.

Key support levels I am watching below in the SPX include $4260-$4270, $4240-$4250, and $4200.
