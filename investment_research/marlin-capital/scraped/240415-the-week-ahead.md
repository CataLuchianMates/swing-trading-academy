# The Week Ahead

Last week was the worst one for the S&P 500 since last October (’23).  Inflation fears, geopolitical concerns, and a broad decline in US bank stocks on Friday led to a -1.6% weekly decline for the SPX.  All 4 major indices (SPY, QQQ, DJI, IWM) closed the week in the red.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xcQW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F87644a3a-878b-447b-83b1-1eece3e92b46_1176x674.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xcQW!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F87644a3a-878b-447b-83b1-1eece3e92b46_1176x674.jpeg)

Headlines concerning the ongoing conflict in the Middle East pressured the market on Friday while persistent inflation readings pushed long-term treasury yields higher.

On the move in rates – We are seeing a near historic low correlation between rates and stocks.  There has been almost no consistent pattern/correlation between rates and stocks over the last month +.  The US 10-YR yield has risen from 4.07% on March 7th to 4.6% on April 11th while the Nasdaq 100 has advanced 10 points during the same period.

The SPX finally caved last week and suffered its worst weekly loss since October (and 2nd week lower in a row).  The index closed at 1 month lows, while the median stock closed at 2 month lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6mdj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1a0b37db-c2fa-4146-9799-ed774fec64d0_1099x679.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6mdj!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1a0b37db-c2fa-4146-9799-ed774fec64d0_1099x679.jpeg)

The VIX closed at its highest level since last October (when the stock rally began) and briefly traded above 19.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wQzm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F323b3cec-fb70-4bde-a9cc-2937921d6042_1123x729.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wQzm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F323b3cec-fb70-4bde-a9cc-2937921d6042_1123x729.jpeg)

Technically, the SPX has now broken decisively below its 21 EMA and we are heading for a bearish 8/21 EMA cross unless we rally big today (which to me, signifies the end of the short term technical uptrend).  We have not touched the 60 EMA since this rally began last October.  I am looking for this test of $5060 support to occur at some point before the correction ends.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3F34!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffdd2f49d-a62a-4f85-b006-66acab0bf1cc_1209x897.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3F34!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffdd2f49d-a62a-4f85-b006-66acab0bf1cc_1209x897.jpeg)

The QQQ is also heading for a bearish 8/21 EMA cross on the daily, which would be the first to occur since last September.  Back then, the bearish 8/21 cross preceded a further ~7% decline in the QQQ.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!f9wV!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8c2a9f81-06fa-4141-829a-674b7005e639_1204x881.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!f9wV!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8c2a9f81-06fa-4141-829a-674b7005e639_1204x881.jpeg)

The QQQ has been incredibly choppy/rangebound for the past 7 weeks.  We had a sizable rally to the top of the range on Thursday, but that was followed by a gap down and selling all day on Friday on geopolitical headlines.  We are now trading towards the bottom of the 7 week range as we head into this week.  $433-$434 remains the key support level to watch below.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ec5n!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F60e0be76-1a09-4a94-997c-dd233e20ddc0_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ec5n!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F60e0be76-1a09-4a94-997c-dd233e20ddc0_1396x653.png)

Meanwhile, Small Caps have led this move to the downside as the IWM breakout above $210 proved to be a false signal.  The IWM is now trading below its 60 EMA for the first time since last November.  This is a bad sign for the SPX and QQQ.  Despite the Large Cap > Small Cap outperformance we had been seeing throughout the rally, Small Caps were always technically in an uptrend (albeit, a weaker one).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7TJF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3ecfeee3-5b07-4193-bacc-d818d84db4ef_1209x889.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7TJF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3ecfeee3-5b07-4193-bacc-d818d84db4ef_1209x889.jpeg)

Q1 Earnings will come in focus this week.  This is the first big week of the earnings season, headlined by ASML, NFLX, and ISRG.

No other planned moves for me early this week as we are in a rangebound and now headline driven market.  The market sold off Friday ahead of the escalating conflict in the Middle East, so this weekend’s headlines are not having the same initial effects many anticipated.  The geopolitical tensions add an additional layer of risk to the markets here as it is impossible to anticipate the headlines that may come out.

Overall, this is a time to manage risk tightly/closely and remain patient.  I am holding 2/3rd of my MU long (with a stop loss in the money) and my starter position in COIN into this week.  I am not willing to add any additional risk beyond these 2 smaller positions at this time.  My focus this week will be on potential earnings gapper setups with a loaded slate of companies reporting.
