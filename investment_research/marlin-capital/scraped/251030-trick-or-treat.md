# Trick or Treat

The past 24 hours have been a whirlwind for the market.

Below, I break it all down 👇👇

#### My FOMC Take

As expected, the Fed cut rates by 25 bps yesterday (lowering the target range to 3.75-4%), and announced that the balance sheet runoff would end at the beginning of December.

During his press conference, Fed Chair Jerome Powell struck a more hawkish tone. He emphasized that a December rate cut is not a foregone conclusion (“far from it”, he said). Powell acknowledged that there are “strongly different views” on the FOMC about how to proceed in December. In particular, he added “there’s a growing chorus now of feeling like maybe this is where we should at least wait a cycle”. He also acknowledged that some participants might see the lack of official data (due to the govt shutdown) as a reason NOT to cut in December. He said that some members see the absence of data and the greater uncertainty as a reason to slow down and leavy policy unchanged, comparing it to “driving in the fog…you slow down”.

Yet, despite Powell downplaying the certainty of a December rate cut, the market is calling his bluff. [Polymarket](https://polymarket.com/event/fed-decision-in-december) still shows 70% odds of a 25bps cut in December.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nj-P!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F09af1a4d-356e-495d-8c41-80d0a2db0c2d_1267x685.png)](https://polymarket.com/event/fed-decision-in-december)

This number is down from <85% before Powell’s presser yesterday, but the market still remains confident in a December rate cut. Fed Funds Futures currently show a similar December cut probability (~70%).

To summarize, no one is that moved by Powell’s hawkish commentary.

#### Big Tech Earnings

Mag 7 earnings kicked off for the group last night, and it was a mixed bag overall.

To summarize, Google announced a blowout report and increased spending. Their core business is doing fantastic (accelerating rev growth across all businesses), and search is not cracking despite competitive pressures + they are re-investing. Great report from GOOGL.

Microsoft was a slight disappointment because costs are increasing and that is eating into margins. Also, cloud growth (Azure) was in-line and the market has been conditioned to expect big beats there.

Meta was the big disappointment. The company missed big on EPS due to a -$15.9B one-time expense. They also announced that they are massively ramping their CapEx (more on that below). The issue here is if you want to give investors confidence on your HUGE spending, your core business must be firing on all cylinders. That was not the case for META in last night’s report. With that said, I view today’s ~10% drop as a bit of an overreaction. A lot of this selloff is likely due to the big headline EPS miss, which was due to a one-time, non-cash tax provision from the new US tax law. The stock might be in the penalty box for now, but I wouldn’t get too bearish on META just based off this report.

#### Big Tech CapEx Updates

Heading into the week, I said I would be most focused on the CapEx outlooks for these Big Tech companies, because the market is fully bought in to this theme (AI CapEx euphoria from the Mag 7).

Given the importance of Mag 7 spending for the entire AI trade, I’m highlighting all the big CapEx data points from last night. All 3 companies pointed to robust and growing investments, which is undeniably bullish for the entire AI trade.

**GOOGL** - raising FY25 CapEx guide:

“With the growth across our business and demand from cloud customers, we now expect 2025 capital expenditures to be in range of $91B-$93B” (note: prev guidance was for ~$85B).

**MSFT** - CapEx in quarter came in well above street estimates (no guidance)

FQ1 CapEx including leases came in at $34.9B, well above street estimates at $30B

**META** - raising FY25 CapEx guide and guiding to “significantly faster” expense growth in 2026:

“We currently expect 2025 capital expenditures, including principal payments on finance leases, to be in the range of $70-$72B, increased from our prior outlook of $66-$72B”

Together, these 3 companies spent about $78B on capital expenditures this quarter, up +89% from a year earlier. These numbers are continuing to grow, so there is no real reason to be bearish on the AI CapEx supply chain yet.

#### Trump-Xi Meeting

The Trump-Xi meeting was positive, but not much substance to talk about here beyond what the market had already expected. The US agreed to cut China’s tariff rate by 10% on Fentanyl progress. China agreed on soybean purchases and a 1yr pause on rare earth export restriction. Blackwell was not part of the deal nor was TikTok.

#### Broad Market: Bad Breadth

The SPX market rally of the last week has been characterized by some of the narrowest breadth you will ever see. Yesterday, 377/500 S&P names (~75%) finished lower.

On Tuesday, 398/500 S&P (~80%) names finished lower. This net decline of -294 (104 stocks up, 398 down) was the worst breadth day EVER for an S&P 500 “up day” (data dates back to 1990).

This has only happened 3 times before in history: twice during the Dot Com Bubble (~3 months before the March 2000 Nasdaq peak), and once in July 2015 (~1 month before the August 2015 market correction).

As you’ll notice with the dates in my table above and the dates in subutrade’s chart, **many of the Dot Com Bubble leaders had already topped by the time the this “negative breadth reading” occurred.** Qualcomm and Yahoo would go on to top a couple weeks after this “negative breadth reading” occurred (in Jan 2000).

The same thing happened in 2021-22 with SPACs, ZM, ARKK, etc. peaking many months before the S&P 500. **We had rapidly deteriorating breadth prior to the 2022 Bear Market.** I wrote about this way back in December 2021 in [“Wild Times in the Market”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/is-this-market-crazy-or-is-it-just?utm_source=publication-search). This report was written about 1 week before the SPX topped and we entered the 2022 Bear Market. Most stocks had already topped for the cycle at that point.

As I often say, it is a market of stocks.

Strong market breadth gave us great confidence that the SPX rally off April lows was legitimate. On [April 24th](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/day-3), I highlighted the Zweigh Breadth thrust and why it was a very positive omen for the market.

> Remember, it’s a market of stocks. When 96% of stocks are being bought, this is no doubt extremely bullish. The historical data confirms this as well, as strong breadth (especially the Zweig Breadth Thrust) has been an incredibly accurate indicator for future returns.

Weak market breadth works the same way.

In [“Risk Off”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70), I highlighted how topping is a process (not an event), and not all stocks peak at the same time. Please review this full report if you have not already. This is starting to become very relevant now that breadth is deteriorating. The generals always get shot last.

If weak breadth is not a big concern to you, than you simply have not studied market history. **For me, deteriorating breadth is greatly concerning and aligns with how previous market topping periods began.** Yes, I fully understand the Mag 7 Bull thesis and why these stocks can continue to advance while the rest of the market doesn’t.

My point is, you should look at every stock individually. Ignore the indices for a second. Every stock has its own Bull and Bear cycles. The concern for me is that many stocks may be starting to enter their Bear cycles here. What does this mean? See Mark Minervini’s 50-80 rule:

> “When a secular leader makes a major top, there’s a 50% chance it will drop 80% and an 80% chance it will drop 50%”
>
> - Mark Minervini

**I have found this 50-80 rule to be true in every cycle I’ve traded through.**

And eventually, I would expect the broad market weakness/bearishness to spill over into the Mag 7 (even if its just a routine market correction due to the Mag 7 “holding up” well).

**Summary**

As I mentioned, I am quite concerned by deteriorating market breadth. In late 2021, deteriorating market breadth helped us call the market top. We predicted a Bear Market in 2022, and that’s exactly what we got with the SPX falling -25% after our call. Many individual stocks and ARKK would go on to decline -70% from our call.

And now, I am saying that there are some big red flags emerging here in 2025. **My analysis of market history shows that every major market top since 2000 was characterized by deteriorating market breadth.** This is exactly what is occurring right now. Of course, it could always be different this time. But for me, this is not a time where I am looking to be aggressive personally.
