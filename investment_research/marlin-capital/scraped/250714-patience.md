For me, this drawdown presents a much more favorable setup in these types of growth names heading into their Q2 earnings reports. Had they continued rallying into EPS, I believe we were likely setting up for “sell the news” reactions across the board. Now, we have much cleaner setups, and that is good news for traders like us.

**Low Bar**

Overall, the bar is low for Q2 earnings. Consensus is forecasting S&P 500 EPS growth to decelerate to +4% YoY from +12% in Q1. Most of the anticipated deceleration in growth is accounted for by margins contracting.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!klr6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F231dab19-f8c6-4eba-ad8d-6c4b9b71ee8f_1188x805.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!klr6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F231dab19-f8c6-4eba-ad8d-6c4b9b71ee8f_1188x805.jpeg)

At a high level, the focus will be on the impact of tariff policies on margins, sales, and investment spending. The current expectation is for consumers to take the brunt of the tariff cost burden (GS economists think they will absorb ~70% of the cost), but could negatively impact margins.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ncv1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F59583105-e718-4e9b-8475-9881297b05ce_960x675.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ncv1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F59583105-e718-4e9b-8475-9881297b05ce_960x675.jpeg)

On a sequential basis, consensus is expecting margins to contract 50bps to 11.6% from 12.1%. Revisions to margins suggest some companies may not be able to fully offset the impact of tariffs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6azO!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F22f02b03-1dc3-470a-a6bf-63f9f8698f5e_1196x550.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6azO!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F22f02b03-1dc3-470a-a6bf-63f9f8698f5e_1196x550.jpeg)

**Implied Earnings Day moves are the lowest in 2 years:** Options expect earnings day moves to average +/- 4.7%. Investors are expecting much less volatility than last quarter when implied moves averaged +/- 7.1%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!D6St!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffb8a9dc6-1509-463a-9be6-b91f28327ad6_1211x539.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!D6St!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffb8a9dc6-1509-463a-9be6-b91f28327ad6_1211x539.jpeg)

Again, this is great news for traders like us.

**Systematic Buying Still A Tailwind**

Below is a chart of the 1 week change in SPX 3-month volatility. Last week saw the early April meltdown days drop out of this gauge, and as a result, the drop here was historic. Only 3 other episodes in history compete: June 2020 (3m after the March 2020 Covid Crash), Jan 1988 (3m after the Oct ‘87 crash), and Jan of 1930 (3m after the Great Crash of 1929).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!o1M7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc9e6108e-f136-43e4-a980-e43db0b6ab72_1204x646.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!o1M7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc9e6108e-f136-43e4-a980-e43db0b6ab72_1204x646.jpeg)
