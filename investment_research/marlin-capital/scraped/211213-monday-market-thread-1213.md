**Watchlist:**

None. There will be no trades for me ahead of the FOMC meeting. I personally believe we will see the Fed get more hawkish this week.

In 2018, Fed Chair Powell rattled the markets with his hawkish commentary. The 20% drop in the S&P 500 essentially forced Powell to change course, which is when the market started to reverse higher.

Now, with the S&P 500 at all time highs & inflation as front page news, the pressure on the Fed is coming from Main St instead of Wall St. In my opinion, the Fed is likely to see this as an opportunity to get more hawkish.

Any beginning of the week rally is likely to end up being sold at the end of this week in my opinion (much like last week). I will remain with my ARKK short hedge through the FOMC meeting, and potentially trim some long positions if we get strength at the beginning of this week. I would like to continue to build my cash position higher for what I see as better opportunities emerging in the coming weeks/months.

As I mentioned, the market has a number of challenges to contend with. Growth stocks have even more. When you discount growth at a higher rate, you get a lower valuation. Many valuations still look stretched to me in growth land, when accounting for 3 rate hikes in 2022. Buying opportunities will eventually come, but for me, we are not there yet.
