As I highlighted in [“On the Week”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/on-the-week) last Friday, I believe the 2 main factors driving this S&P 500 rally back above $6000 are:

(1) Positioning (re-leveraging, systematic, and forced buying)

(2) A more resilient economy than expected (Polymarket recession odds have dropped from ~68% post-Liberation Day to 26%).

On that 1st point, total positioning remains underweight/light US stocks here even after the ~30% NDX rally off lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6Fx4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8051e276-d51f-4e10-8ebf-f5079cfd3de0_1024x998.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6Fx4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8051e276-d51f-4e10-8ebf-f5079cfd3de0_1024x998.jpeg)

As I have covered in detail over the past month + , this is primarily due to low institutional equity exposure. Hedge Fund long exposure hit near ~15 year lows following Liberation Day, and even after some [big re-buying in May](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/wednesday-morning-notes-64), they have not caught up yet.

Discretionary investors have raised their long exposure to near neutral now (still slightly below, 36th percentile), but systematic strategies remain underweight US stocks (24th percentile).

The positioning backdrop remains favorable here (albeit less than it was in May), as current positioning still leaves plenty of room in the tank for re-risking.

Internally, the last ~2 weeks of chop in the indices are supportive of this big picture favorable backdrop. SPX and NDX members trading above their 200-day MAs are at ~30%. Typical levels of euphoria are at 80%. This suggests more room for stocks to run.
