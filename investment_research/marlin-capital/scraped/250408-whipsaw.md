# Whipsaw

The US stock market kicked off the week with one of its most volatile sessions in history yesterday. The S&P 500 gapped down -4.7% and traded to a low of $4835, officially hitting Bear Market territory for the first time since 2022 (-21% off ATHs).

Following the big gap down, we saw the index surge +3.4% at highs. This was the largest intraday move for the SPX since Covid times (reminder that March 2020 had multiple limit downs halts, while yesterday’s 851 bps swing came without a halt).

Markets traded another record amount of shares across all exchanges yesterday (that makes 2 in a row after breaking Friday’s record) at 28.8B. This is by far the largest 2-day trading volumes in the history of the US stock market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Opmy!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1d8cfb82-053e-444a-b9b9-f3877199f806_1290x801.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Opmy!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1d8cfb82-053e-444a-b9b9-f3877199f806_1290x801.jpeg)

The initial market bounce off morning lows was catalyzed by a fake headline report that NEC Director Kevin Hassett said President Trump is considering a 90 day pause on tariffs, except for China. The White House called the report fake news, and President Trump later indicated that the US will impose an additional 50% tariff on imports from China starting Wednesday (if China does not withdraw its 34% tariff on US imports).

Selling increased in response, but major indices remained well above their morning lows. The S&P 500 finished the day -0.23% and the QQQ eeked out a positive gain at +0.24%.

As I talked about on Sunday, we saw full on Hedge Fund capitulation last week. Thursday/Friday were the largest 2 day $ net selling by Hedge Funds on record.

Thursday/Friday also saw the largest 2 day decrease in net leverage by Hedge Funds EVER.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xqQr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbebdfe49-2d5f-4360-be3d-60e2469c7377_1288x868.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xqQr!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbebdfe49-2d5f-4360-be3d-60e2469c7377_1288x868.jpeg)

US Macro Products (Index + ETFs) saw the largest 2 day (Thurs/Fri) $ short selling on record.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!T8vw!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0617238a-9770-4c2d-a519-ede884bfff72_1290x829.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!T8vw!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0617238a-9770-4c2d-a519-ede884bfff72_1290x829.jpeg)

Following the “90 day pause” headline yesterday morning, we saw a flurry of short covering in Macro products (Index + ETFs), a possible sign of how squeezy things could get if we ever start to get some real positive headlines. At one point yesterday, ETFs were 42% of the tape (finished 39%) vs the YTD avg of 28%.

I talked on Sunday about how we were finally seeing real signs of capitulation and extreme fear. Total investor positioning (institutional + retail + foreign) reached -2.5 Z score at the end of last week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!E4nm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb4461dc-bfcb-47c2-b7bf-f819bf760911_866x755.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!E4nm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb4461dc-bfcb-47c2-b7bf-f819bf760911_866x755.jpeg)

We have very seldom reached lower total positioning levels than this, even during the 2022 Bear Market (slightly lower) and the December 2018 meltdown (bottomed at -2.6 Z score). Positioning wise, we are already below March 2020 Covid Crash lows and 2015 correction lows.

**APP Short Trade**

I came into Monday holding half of my original APP short trade.

I covered 40% of my APP short at the open at $200.80 and kept a 10% runner. Put simply, a -4% SPX gap down following the biggest 2 day market crash since March 2020 is not a time to press on shorts.

I later closed out the 10% runner after the 90 day tariff headline bounce when APP broke above Friday’s highs at $247.50.

Overall, this trade was a +21% adjusted gain from last Wednesday’s entry. Not bad!

**What’s Next?**

So what’s in store ahead? Again, the good news is that a ton of selling has already gotten done and we have reached the technical signs of capitulation that I look for. History says this is a time for long term investors to buy (best to layer in); with all buying/capitulation indicators ticking: 45+ VIX, -2.5z positioning, SPX in Bear Market, etc.

The h-sell breakdown has way overshot my technical target. The market has been more technically oversold just twice in the last decade (December 2018 lows, mid-2015). That’s the positive news, and it should be good for at least a multi-day technical oversold bounce off lows (if not more).

The Earnings risk remains the big one now. From JPM yesterday:

“We reduce FY25 SPX consensus by $29.20 given the impact from tariffs on Canada, China, and Mexico. If we include an additional reduction of $20 to include the impact from RoW, we reduce by ~$50. Current consensus for EPS is $268 avg.”

Looking at the matrix below, a consensus earnings reduction from $268 average to the $220-$240 region is the big risk now plaguing the market. Even at an 18x P/E (which would be the highest Bear Market bottom P/E ever), we would have downside to the lower $4k area in the SPX.

Of course, the earnings picture is highly dependent on tariffs and the duration they are implemented. I look at the current Bear Market as an event driven Bear (similar to March 2020) versus a structural Bear. We came into the year with ATH long positioning, which has now been washed out to capitulatory status (-2.5 z score).

We also came into the year expecting another great year on a fundamental basis, with consensus calling for +11% YoY EPS growth. These estimates have only been revised down slightly so far across the board (-2%), but again, the crashy type action in the market post-Liberation Day tells me the market is not in agreement there. Earnings are now the question mark and the risk, particularly if the tariffs push us into a recession.
