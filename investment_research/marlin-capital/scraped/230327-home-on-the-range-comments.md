# Home on the Range — David Marlin Q&A



---

## David Marlin Q&A (Comments)

**Q:** what is money market fund? is that if I deposite $100 into Fidelity, Robinhood, TD-Ameritrade those broker, the exchange $100 into money market fund?
In this case, why it is bubble? Will we not be able to get those money out if events like SVB happens?

**David Marlin:** A money market fund is a mutual fund that invests in short-term debt/cash securities such as overnight reserves and commercial paper.  You will have to check with your specific broker, but TD Ameritrade does not automatically sweep client funds into a money market fund (meaning you have to manually buy).  These funds are highly liquid so you should have no problem getting money out in any events.  The “bubble chart” simply speaks to how much cash there is on the sidelines rather than in the equity market.

---

**Q:** What are the main concerns with the unwinding of money market funds ? What will happen when folks will dump their treasuries, who will buy them ?

**David Marlin:** I don’t view the amount of assets in money market funds as concerning.  It is more concerning for the banking system as money market funds offer a higher yield than parking cash in a traditional savings account, so there is little reason to keep money with banks right now.  This is precisely why there are so many flows to money market funds and a big reason why the bank issue has arisen.  As it pertains to equities, the “bubble chart” simply speaks to how much cash there is on the sidelines rather than in the equity market, and speaks to the lack of risk taking right now.  This is potentially bullish for stocks as a rally could create FOMO with so much cash on the sidelines, with buyers being forced to chase stocks higher and bidding up prices even more.

**Q:** Do you still have the discord channel? I want to ask for the ZIM about its $6 dividend. It said the registration date is 3/24 and dividend is paid on 4/4. I'm wondering if I buy it now I won't be able to get the $6 dividend and I will suffer the drop of $6 after the dividend is removed? And those hold on 3/24 and sell it now can actually get the $6 dividend and may buy back at ($22-$6). Is that correct?

**David Marlin:** The ex-dividend date is usually set 1 business day before the record date.  To receive the dividend payment, you must own the stock before the ex-dividend date. If you buy the stock on the ex-dividend date or after, you will not receive the dividend.
