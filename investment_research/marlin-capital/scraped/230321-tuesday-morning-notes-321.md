# Tuesday Morning Notes 3.21

Technical Update

The S&P 500 rallied to kick off the week yesterday, closing +0.89% on the day. The index closed the day at $3951, 5 points above the 200 SMA.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!m3vX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faee4206b-cc2c-483f-8f89-ce05c14d8fa8_964x726.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!m3vX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faee4206b-cc2c-483f-8f89-ce05c14d8fa8_964x726.jpeg)

Technically, we appear to be setting up for a big move by the end of this week. Tomorrow’s FOMC meeting and press conference should provide the catalyst.

Yesterday’s action came on significantly lower volume than last week, and I would expect more of the same today as traders await tomorrow’s Fed meeting. I will not be placing any trades into tomorrow’s meeting. As I have discussed before, technicals matter much less in the face of market moving news events, and tomorrow’s FOMC meeting certainly qualifies as a critical event.
