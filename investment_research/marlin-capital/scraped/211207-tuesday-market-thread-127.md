As we can see, ARKK has not closed above its 8 EMA since November 8th. This bounce in ARKK is unconvincing until it can break that month long trend. If it does, I will close out my short hedge. The good news is, the 8 EMA is now below where I shorted, so I should walk away with a gain on this hedge no matter what.

Yesterday morning, I tweaked my long term holdings by selling FVRR and UPST. These are two stocks that round tripped to my original buy points. I did not want these once winning stocks to turn into my losers. I promptly covered portions of my BILI and NIO shorts to make up for my long term sells. I did not cover any of my ARKK short, as it is more correlated to my portfolio.

With these moves, I am now sitting at over 20% cash. Today, I expect to cover the rest of my BILI and NIO shorts. For BILI, it is set to gap up big and I want to keep the loss on the remainder of this short manageable. For NIO, I will take a small loss on the remainder of this short.

Excluding my cash position, I will be 50:50 Short:Long. I will look for a bounce to the 8 EMA in ARKK ($100 area) and rejection as a chance to add to my ARKK short and become net short again.

1:48 EST: ARKK Technical Analysis

A downtrend is characterized by a period of lower highs and lower lows. You can see this in ARKK’s daily chart below.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-F3p!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6ce9af39-88a6-4978-b905-dfce7b6526c7_1013x767.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-F3p!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6ce9af39-88a6-4978-b905-dfce7b6526c7_1013x767.png)

To break the downtrend, technically, ARKK will need to break its last lower high ($109). However, I am not willing to give my short hedge that much room.

As I’ve said, I will be closing out my ARKK short on a close above the 8 EMA (a close above $101 today). This is simply a rule based indicator and I must be disciplined to my rules. ARKK has not closed above its 8 EMA in a month, so I believe a close above could trigger a bigger bounce.

There are a number of potential scenarios on the table. Yesterday, ARKK made a low of $89, coming within $9 of my $80 price target. It is certainly one possibility that my price target is never fully reached.

Another possibility is that we end up re-testing (or breaking) yesterday’s lows. This is the most likely scenario to me. Both scenarios will also hinge on the CPI data on Friday and FOMC meeting Dec 14th and 15th. These are two market moving events over the next couple weeks. I will be closely monitoring both events.
