# Friday Morning Notes 3.17

Technical Update

The S&P 500 rallied big yesterday, breaking some key resistance levels in the process. The index closed above its 200 SMA ($3940) after spending 5 days below the level.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rTuM!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2e2d54bd-e2b0-422d-8ff8-86326168a74c_958x730.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rTuM!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2e2d54bd-e2b0-422d-8ff8-86326168a74c_958x730.jpeg)

The big question coming out of this week - Did last weekend’s Fed bailout backstop the stock market? It is now widely expected that the Fed will **not** raise interest rates at next week’s meeting, although there is still some debate about a possible 25 BP hike.

The dizzying move in the US 2Y seems to suggest the Fed is done hiking rates, while QT is now effectively over (see below). The market is certainly reacting as if the Fed’s dovish pivot has begun and will culminate next week with a pause in tightening. We will find out just how dovish the committee wants to get next week.

So, what should we make of this week’s rally following several of the largest bank failures in U.S. history? Is this just an oversold OpEx Week bounce, or something bigger? Full detailed analysis report coming this weekend.

Arkk and XLRE Short Updates

ARKK - this breakdown hit my first target point ($35) for cover before reversing along with the market on Monday. Tech has led the way this week. It came close, but did not hit my stop yesterday. Regardless, with the market now above 200 SMA and supported for now, I will be exiting the rest of this short position today at the open.

XLRE - this chart remains bearish as it forms a Bear flag under all key moving averages. With that said, I will be exiting this XLRE short position at today’s open. Again, with the market above the 200 SMA and supported for now, I see no reason to risk a losing trade here. Instead, I will use the opportunity to exit this position at breakeven and can always re-visit next week/later.
