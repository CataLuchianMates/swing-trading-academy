# The Week Ahead

The market pulled back last week (NDX down 4 of 5 days) with the selling accelerating into Friday.  For the QQQ, we closed below the 21 EMA for the 1st time in a month (right before the NVDA earnings gap shakeout).

On the 1 Hour chart, we closed Friday right at the $433-$434 support level I have highlighted.  If we break below this level decisively, we have an unfilled gap to $426 that I would look to be filled in the near term.

Big picture, on the weekly time frame, there is no big reason for concern here technically despite 2 red weeks in a row.  The trend remains firmly up and to the right.

The Semiconductor Index SOXX has lead this rally off lows, but is now down -8% in 5 sessions, underperforming the NDX by 600 bps during this stretch (one of the worst in 5+ years).  Looking to the chart, SOXX just registered its first close below the 21 EMA since January 5th.

In the Semi space, the news of this week is highlighted by Nvidia’s 4-day GTC developers event beginning today.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qejE!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4d86f23e-08f2-40e5-a9cb-11e3681441c1_432x568.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qejE!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4d86f23e-08f2-40e5-a9cb-11e3681441c1_432x568.png)

AI optimism across US equities remains high, but not yet at Dot Com Bubble or Post-Covid levels.  The Fwd P/E of the 10 largest TMT stocks currently equals 28x, which pales in comparison to the peak of the Dot Com Bubble (52x) and Covid Bubble (43x).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!O_5Y!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fae9f13f7-62d1-4902-89f6-208026596fd6_1168x806.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!O_5Y!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fae9f13f7-62d1-4902-89f6-208026596fd6_1168x806.png)

The other key event this week is the FOMC meeting concluding on Wednesday.  The Fed is widely expected to leave rates unchanged at this meeting, but commentary on future rate cuts (market pricing is down to 3 cuts in ’24 vs 6+ at the start of the year) will be critical for the market.

Another potential speed bump for the market is the corporate blackout window (no buybacks), which began last week and runs through 4/26.  27% of SPX components have entered their blackout window, a number that we will see continue to rise over the next 2 weeks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!iYg_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F785c73a5-2a93-419c-9686-e2ecaf7c6919_964x418.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!iYg_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F785c73a5-2a93-419c-9686-e2ecaf7c6919_964x418.jpeg)

In my view, the market appears to have settled into this zone of tactically nervous ahead of a big slate of news events this week.  This has resulted in investors not trying to do too much ahead of this week’s FOMC meeting.

Trading wise, I will **not** be looking to do much in the early part of this week ahead of Wednesday’s FOMC.  In my view, the Fed’s/Powell’s commentary on future rate cuts will be critical for determining the market direction from here.  This will be a particularly important meeting now that the major indices have pulled back to key support levels and are looking for a reason to either bounce back to highs or break down. Powell ordinarily leans dovish, but the risk remains that last week’s inflation numbers have forced his hand.  For me, patience is the name of the game here in the early part of this week (pre-FOMC).
