# Headline Roulette

Marlin Capital Readers,

Today was another day of fast and volatile headline-driven moves.

Goldman’s “AI at Risk” basket (GSTMAIR) traded -5% on the day, while Software underperformed Semis by -6.7% (top 5 worst day in the last 5 years).

But right now, as we have seen this week, it’s all about the headlines.

Yesterday, President Trump announced a 5 day pause on military strikes against Iranian energy infrastructure, contingent on progress in ongoing negotiations. The SPX rallied +1.2% on the news.

Today, the market gave back a good portion of Monday’s gains. Then, after the market closed, the SPX spiked nearly +1% on a report from Israel’s Channel 12 saying the US may be preparing a 15-item deal for a potential 1-month ceasefire. WTI crude fell back below $90 (it was ~$92 at the close).

This is headline roulette. Day-to-day moves are being driven by geopolitical news flow, not fundamentals (or even technicals). Just headlines. And for traders, that means there is no real edge to be had.

With that said, underneath all the headline noise, the leaders are shining bright this week. Small Caps have been the outperforming group this week (and basically all year). Beyond Small Caps, the Optical Connectivity theme I highlighted in [“Leaders Shine Bright”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/leaders-shine-bright?utm_source=publication-search) continues to be the leading theme in the market.

In past reports, I have talked about stacking layers of probability. You want to be positioned in the strongest names in the strongest groups, within the strongest themes. That framework has not changed.

There are a couple of names currently catching my eye right now. Both are Small/Mid Caps (the leading groups). And both are in the Optics theme (the leading theme). If we start to see geopolitical headlines improve and volatility drift lower here (a big if), these setups can work.

Here is what I am looking at. 👇

**Lightwave Logic (LWLG)**

Lightwave Logic is a development stage materials company that sits at the heart of the AI-driven photonics buildout. The company designs proprietary electro-optic polymer materials (branded Perkinamine) that convert electrical signals into optical signals for data center and telecom links. LWLG makes the materials that enable next-gen silicon photonics chips to move data faster and more efficiently inside AI data centers. Their polymers sit directly between the chips and the optical engines, enabling higher-bandwidth, lower-power co-packaged optics (CPO) and optical modules.

For years, Wall Street treated LWLG as a science project. Great potential, but just a business plan/science project. That all changed on March 11th, [when LWLG struck a massive deal with TSEM.](https://www.stocktitan.net/news/LWLG/lightwave-logic-and-tower-semiconductor-announce-development-9kv0qq89i8kk.html)

On 3/11, LWLG announced a company-changing agreement with Tower Semiconductor to integrate its electro-optic polymer modulator reference designs into Tower’s PH18 silicon photonics platform. This is a MASSIVE deal for LWLG. Tower’s PH18 is one of the most established silicon photonics foundry platforms in the world. Tower also recently announced a deal with NVIDIA to develop 1.6 terabits-per-second optical modules for AI data centers using this same platform.

By getting its Perkinamine polymers integrated directly into Tower’s process design kit (PDK), LWLG has essentially moved from the lab to the global assembly line. The collaboration targets modulators with 110GHz+ bandwidth, supports compact low-power 200G and 400G architectures, and includes multiple engineering tapeouts during 2026 for customer validation.

Most importantly, LWLG stock reacted to this major catalyst exactly the way you want to see. 👇

LWLG surged +42% on the day following the news (3/12), building on a +15% rally (earnings) from the prior session. Volume was (by far) the highest volume day in the stock’s history with >31M shares traded (>12x avg daily vol).

When a stock moves +40% on ATH volume following a massive fundamental catalyst, that is the market telling you something. For traders and investors, it’s best to pay attention.

Legendary trader Kristjan Kullamägi would call this LWLG candle (on 3/12) an “Episodic Pivot”. It’s 1 of the 3 main trading setups he used to help him turn $9k to $250M+.

An Episodic Pivot is a trading setup that occurs when a stock’s fundamental “story” changes significantly (because of some key catalyst), leading to a massive shift in price and volume.

Episodic pivots/Gappers are my #1 favorite trading setup, and where most of my biggest winners have come from. If there is 1 trading setup to take from me, it’s this.

**Here’s how it typically plays out:**

**The Catalyst:** It all starts with a HUGE, game changing, fundamental catalyst. The rally must be driven by a significant event, most commonly an earnings surprise, but also new partnerships, deals, order wins, FDA approvals, etc.

**The High Volume Rally:** Very important - the stock must then respond to the catalyst news with a BIG gap and rally on **massive volume**. The higher the volume, the better. An ATH volume day is the cream of the crop. Sometimes, you get multiple ATH volume days in a row (like APLD saw after the CRWV deal announcement; see below).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!S9V7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff6181a31-c9fd-4e0f-bcd8-3e5348b23d0e_1012x675.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!S9V7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff6181a31-c9fd-4e0f-bcd8-3e5348b23d0e_1012x675.png)

When a stock is up +40% on ATH volume (like LWLG) following a major catalyst, this is often times only the beginning of the big momentum move higher. Often times, this buying momentum carries over for weeks-months. Kullamagi calls this “post-earnings announcement drift” (but it doesn’t have to be earnings specifically, just a major fundamental catalyst).

To be clear, LWLG is a speculative small cap. They have no meaningful revenue yet. But, the Tower Semi deal is the kind of catalyst that can transform a story stock into a big winner (particularly when the Optics theme is as hot as it is). We have already seen it with AXTI, AAOI, and others in the Optics theme. Could LWLG be next?

**IPG Photonics Corporation (IPGP)**

IPG Photonics is a Mid Cap photonics name that plays the Optics theme from a different angle. IPGP designs, manufactures, and sells high-power fiber lasers, fiber amplifiers, and diode lasers, primarily for industrial materials processing (cutting, welding, marking, additive manufacturing). ~88% of IPGP’s revenue comes from industrial laser systems, with the rest split between communications, medical, and defense/aerospace applications.

Within the broader Optics stack, IPGP sits in the industrial laser segment (rather than the pure AI data center optical interconnect space). But the company leverages similar core photonics IP, and the defense angle here is real. IPGP recently landed a ~$10M Lockheed Martin order for CROSSBOW high-energy laser counter-UAS systems. That is a meaningful catalyst that keeps the defense-laser narrative alive alongside the core industrial recovery story.

The stock pulled back to its 60 EMA during the recent market weakness, where we saw dip buyers emerge. With today’s +5.5% rally, IPGP is now back above all key moving averages.
