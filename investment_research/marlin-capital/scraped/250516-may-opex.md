# May OpEx

The market continues to gravitate higher into today’s monthly options expiry, consolidating near highs. Looking at the technicals for the S&P 500, the index broke back above its last Fibonacci retracement level yesterday (the 76.4% retracement line). History suggests we are our way back to all-time highs.

Dating back to the 1956-57 Bear Market, there have been 15 distinct corrections with a peak to trough fall of 15% or more. On average, from the day the S&P 500 broke above the 76.4% retracement line, it took 7 months to make new ATHs. But this number is dragged up by the 1968-70, 1973-74, and 2008 Bear Markets that were associated with recessions. Those took 12, 46, and 23 months from such a breakout to make new highs. **On median, it took just 2.5 months from the 76.4% retracement breakout to ATHs (and excluding those 3 the average was 2.7).**

With that said, we remain short term extended/overbought on a tehcnical basis. More than 24% of Nasdaq 100 stocks are overbought (RSI >70). When this happened in the past (since Covid), it has led to a 1-week pullback 91% of the time (h/t subutrade).

Options Expiration today - Goldman Prime estimates that over $2.8T of notional options exposure will expire today including $1.2T of SPX options and $580B notional of single stock options. This options expiration will be the largest May expiration on record, with a notional value that is equal to 4.6% of the Russell 3000 market cap.
