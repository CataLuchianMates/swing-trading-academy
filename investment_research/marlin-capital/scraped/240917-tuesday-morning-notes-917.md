# Tuesday Morning Notes 9.17

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Sideways session to kick off the week yesterday with the SPX closing +0.13%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!myp4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F22641339-303d-46bd-bad3-7fc9a23bbf76_1203x889.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!myp4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F22641339-303d-46bd-bad3-7fc9a23bbf76_1203x889.jpeg)

The street is in clear “wait and see mode” ahead of the FOMC meeting Wednesday.  NY Empire Manufacturing flipped positive for the first time since November ’23 yesterday (+11.5 vs cons -4%).

Breadth improved under the surface with the S&P Equal Weight outperforming the SPX by 50bps.  The SPX/Mag 7 was weighed down by AAPL (-2.8% following negative sell side call) and NVDA (-2%, retracing a little of its +16% move last week).  After hours, Intel announced a partnership with Amazon \*IN MULTIYEAR FRAMEWORK FOR PRODUCTS, WAFERS, boosting Tech/semis a bit this morning.

“50 or 25” is pretty much all you read this morning.  I asked my connection at Goldman Rates Desk to run data on “futures pricing” vs “fed action” going back 15+ years.  As of today, we are pricing 42bps of cuts.  For hiking cycles, the Fed has typically delivered close to what is priced in going into the meeting and has never surprised the market since Bernanke.  For cutting cycles, the Fed has surprised on the dovish side during Crises (GFC/Covid) but has also underdelivered cuts versus expectations (i.e. Sep 2019).

SN – Still holding my final 1/3rd here.  Stop at $95.1.

CAVA – Pulled back to the 8 EMA but still above my stop level at $118.78.  I’m letting this trade play out.
