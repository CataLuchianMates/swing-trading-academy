# Friday Morning Notes 10.25

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Another choppy day for US equities yesterday (SPX +0.21%) ahead of what could be the SPX first weekly decline in 7 weeks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GPoh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc259fe48-6999-42b9-b9ce-c9c528ccf9e9_1187x884.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GPoh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc259fe48-6999-42b9-b9ce-c9c528ccf9e9_1187x884.jpeg)

TSLA was +22% following earnings, its best day in over 10 years. adding >$150B of market cap (more than GM & Ford combined).

Volumes have been drying up and it feels like today/Monday could be **very** quiet ahead of FAAMG earnings next week + the election in the coming weeks. Liquidity remains low overall.

Looking ahead, we get 41% of the SPX market cap reporting next week (busiest week of the Q3 season) with a focus on Mag 7 names along with payrolls on Friday. Things will surely be heating up from here after we get through this week.

Earnings movers after the bell:

DXM: -18%; US weakness overshadows OUS with a weak US rev guide ($702M vs $772M cons)

WDC: +12% on better than feared Q2 guide, boosting Semis + Tech this morning

NWL +18%, CNC +15%, DECK +13%
