# Thursday Morning Notes 12.21

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The S&P 500 finished -1.47% yesterday on an aggressive afternoon reversal.  12.8B shares traded across all US equity exchanges vs YTD daily avg of 10.9B shares.  The VIX rose +9% amid the biggest 1 day sell off in the SPX since September.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1xzm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3410cc3a-7199-4cbc-b100-1c792bea50eb_967x713.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1xzm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3410cc3a-7199-4cbc-b100-1c792bea50eb_967x713.jpeg)

The 30 Min chart tells the story of yesterday’s aggressive turn to the downside.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0qra!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6ab6cbbe-b8f1-4ee4-928d-73bdf1edec31_1361x628.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0qra!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6ab6cbbe-b8f1-4ee4-928d-73bdf1edec31_1361x628.png)

While we have seen passive selling over the past few days, it clearly is not enough to cause such a swift intraday reversal.  Looking at S&P 500 E-Mini’s, most volume started printing after 2:30 PM, even prior to the break of $4800 and the pickup in volume was real.  The average run rate over the 45 mins following the initial move was ~5x greater than earlier in the session.  There were a few headlines floating around as potential reasons for the selloff, most notably: “Graham: will draft pre-Taiwan invasion sanctions against China.”

The Russell 2000 Index of Small Caps finished -189 bps on the day, dragged lower by unprofitable tech (-475 bps on the session).  Hedge Funds did step in late in the day to cover some shorts around the edges with the consensus HF shorts bucket trading down -4%.

Speaking of Small Caps, TNA finished the session -5.95% on a big reversal candle.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3xvg!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7d8b32c3-a9ee-4e9b-adc2-6f1bfcdd975c_962x721.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3xvg!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7d8b32c3-a9ee-4e9b-adc2-6f1bfcdd975c_962x721.jpeg)

TNA closed at $37.30, just above my closing stop (of $37).  With yesterday’s extended Bearish Engulfing Reversal Candle after a big run, this is a sell signal for me on the final 20% portion of my TNA long.  We are set for a gap back above $38 this morning, where I will be closing out my TNA long (for ~+33% gains).

PATH also gave a technical warning sign yesterday with a break and close below its 8 EMA, the first since its earnings gap.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sWJl!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9b6c340c-444a-43c7-9d9e-436c891c9a62_978x732.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sWJl!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9b6c340c-444a-43c7-9d9e-436c891c9a62_978x732.jpeg)

We are set for a gap back above $25 in PATH this morning, where I will be closing out my long.  With yesterday’s reversals across the market and not a lot of profit cushion from my entries on PATH, I do not want to see a profitable trade turn red.  This is a risk reducing closing trade for me.
