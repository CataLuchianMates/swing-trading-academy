# Wednesday Morning Notes 10.25

Daily Market Commentary

The S&P 500 bounced back above its 200-day moving average yesterday, closing +0.73% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SLUy!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa7a6fa4a-e436-4d0c-ae19-2e0fe102e792_986x732.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SLUy!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa7a6fa4a-e436-4d0c-ae19-2e0fe102e792_986x732.jpeg)

Lower quality corners of the market squeezed following PMIs showing signs of positive growth alongside easing inflation.  China ADRs, Most short rolling, Liquid most short, and non-profitable tech led the way on Tuesday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gd8Z!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F26583106-a679-4bd6-b404-3d75ba9fd818_1232x1160.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gd8Z!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F26583106-a679-4bd6-b404-3d75ba9fd818_1232x1160.jpeg)

Tech (QQQ) finished +0.97% on the day, but the index remains below the 21 and 60 EMAs as we head into Wednesday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XlIZ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3412446d-50ce-4b02-85d3-ace0782ff934_980x729.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XlIZ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3412446d-50ce-4b02-85d3-ace0782ff934_980x729.jpeg)

Post close, the big earnings results of the day were mixed.

MSFT – Big EPS beat at $2.99 vs consensus $2.65.  Azure re-acceleration to 28% YoY vs 27% YoY consensus.  Stock +4% this morning as the market likes these results.

GOOGL – Operating income missed in quarter ($21.34B vs $21.44B consensus).  Ad revenue slightly better than expected, however magnitude of beat likely underwhelming.  Stock -6% this AM.

V – Focus is on stock buyback.  Authorized $25B buyback program.  Stock slightly red this morning.

TXN – Q4 revenue guidance well below expectations ($3.93B-$4.27B vs consensus $4.49B).  Stock -5% on guidance miss.

SNAP – Whippy action after initial pop following results.  Q3 revenue beat ($1.19B vs $1.11B consensus) / Q3 EBITDA beat (+40M vs consensus -64M) / $500M buyback authorization / Q4 guidance in-line.  Stock +4% this morning.
