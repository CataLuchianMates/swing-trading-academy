# The Turning Point

US stocks fell last week amid a continued rotation in market leadership.

On the week, the S&P 500 finished -1.9%, NDX -3.7%, and RTY +1.7%.  This was the largest 5-day outperformance for the Russell 2000 vs. the S&P 500 on record (dating back to 1985).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WfXa!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F182865d1-2d8d-4ea1-9d34-1d191e4be652_825x585.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WfXa!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F182865d1-2d8d-4ea1-9d34-1d191e4be652_825x585.png)

Overall, the week was filled with painful undwinds and violent moves lower in Semis / Mega Caps / AI winners.  SOXX, the largest Semiconductor ETF, saw its largest outflows in 2+ years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!73VP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa4620bce-b3d2-40eb-83ca-535c26184518_1121x374.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!73VP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa4620bce-b3d2-40eb-83ca-535c26184518_1121x374.png)

This was the theme of the week, as US equities saw their largest de-grossing since January 2021 (Meme Stock Mania), as Hedge Funds unwound risk for 7 straight days.  A good portion of the week’s selling was seen in funds/institutions reducing exposure to YTD AI winners.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EXIb!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd8c60507-b10f-4c26-bf5b-79697f6872df_988x569.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EXIb!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd8c60507-b10f-4c26-bf5b-79697f6872df_988x569.png)

10 of 11 sectors were net sold, led by Tech, Health Care, and Energy.  Consumer Staples (defensive sector) was the only sector that saw a modest increase in gross flows.

Meanwhile, Small Caps saw their 2nd largest weekly inflow EVER.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oq9T!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F71dd7cd0-16eb-47a9-8c8f-2476d982d25e_526x394.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oq9T!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F71dd7cd0-16eb-47a9-8c8f-2476d982d25e_526x394.jpeg)

Momentum winners and other consensus longs were heavily sold in favor of Small Caps, catalyzed by an underwhelming ASML earnings print, overbought technicals, tariff concerns, etc.  Prior to the recent Small Cap rally and Nasdaq pullback, the Russell 2000 reached an all-time low versus the Nasdaq.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZVNl!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1802d084-7b26-472a-82cb-6a8d08c23879_1171x880.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZVNl!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1802d084-7b26-472a-82cb-6a8d08c23879_1171x880.jpeg)

The Small Cap outperformance stalled a bit on Thursday and Friday after having regained most of the ground lost to the NDX since early May.  On a YTD basis, the RTY had underperformed the NDX by as much as -18.4%, and now stands at -8.5% after last week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XkJ1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5d8b0f9c-dadd-4bb7-8cad-f12efd60ef84_1171x785.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XkJ1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5d8b0f9c-dadd-4bb7-8cad-f12efd60ef84_1171x785.jpeg)

As mentioned, momentum winners (YTD winners) and consensus longs were heavily sold last week in favor of YTD losers.  The biggest outperformers on the week were: Regional Banks (+8%), Housing Exposure (+3.5%), Commercial Real Estate (+3.5%), Low Profit R2K (+2%), Liquid Most Short (+1.7%), and High Beta 12M Losers (+1.5%).  In other words, 2024’s biggest losers turned into last week’s biggest winners.  These were areas of the market with very institutional long exposure (or outright short exposure).

On the other hand, Mega Caps (which have led the market in 2024 and beyond) finished the week -5%, as we saw a violent de-risking in the year’s biggest winners/crowded longs (NVDA -10% on the week).  Goldman’s Momentum Index (GSP1MOMO) pulled back -5.7% on the week, the largest 5-day decline in 17 months.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TyMo!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd4f85810-e38c-497a-82a4-2d114c825fb5_1212x1189.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TyMo!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd4f85810-e38c-497a-82a4-2d114c825fb5_1212x1189.jpeg)

Analyzing 10 years worth of data looking at 5-day drawdown of >5% in Goldman’s Momentum Index, the median performance following such events is generally positive on a longer term basis.  1 year out, the average return is +9.9% with a hit rate of 85.7%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0Qks!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe1598079-63fe-483e-ab37-91d234d871ce_1094x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0Qks!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe1598079-63fe-483e-ab37-91d234d871ce_1094x1080.jpeg)

The question on everyone’s mind is: Are we nearing a turning point in the market? Small Caps have lagged for the duration of this bull run while Mega Caps have seen their market caps balloon to unprecedented levels.  Did last week mark a turning point for the market, or will it prove to just be another blip in this Mega Cap dominated bull run?

One positive for Small Caps is where we are in the Monetary Policy cycle.  Historically, Small Caps outperform when the Fed starts to ease.  The probability of the 1st Fed Rate cut in September is now sitting at 99%, so the easing cycle is nearing close.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NVeL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbf52e33b-f5c9-4fe4-b28f-ae514b8a97d8_1175x692.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NVeL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbf52e33b-f5c9-4fe4-b28f-ae514b8a97d8_1175x692.jpeg)

With that said, I continue to maintain that Q2 earnings season will be the determining factor in answering this question.  Last week’s price action only proves that institutional investors are (greatly) reducing their exposure to Mega Caps/Mag 7, YTD winners, and Crowded Longs ahead of their Q2 earnings reports.

In my view, this presents a much more favorable upside setup for these stocks post-earnings.  If these stocks had continued to melt up into their Q2 reports, earnings likely would have had a “sell the news” like theme to them.  Now, a nice upside surprise in earnings could create a violent re-risking event, as funds would be forced to chase the exposure they just reduced.

We will get more color on Q2 earnings this week, as 31% of the S&P 500 market cap is set to report.

Consensus expects +9% YoY EPS growth for the SPX in Q2, which would mark the strongest quarterly growth rate since Q4 2021.  This compares to ~0% growth over the past 3 quarters.  The bar is significantly higher this quarter, and this will likely mean that the magnitude of earnings beats will be diminished (smaller beats).
