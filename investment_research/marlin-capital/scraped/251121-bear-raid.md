# Bear Raid

#### Ugly Reversal Day for US Stocks

What started off as a promising day spiraled quickly for Bulls. The S&P 500 closed negative (-1.6%) after gapping up more than +1.4% at the open. This has only happened 2 other times in history before (4/7/20 & 4/8/25).

This is yet another sign (as if we needed more) that we are in a bearish market environment. In Bearish markets, good news does NOT matter.

The opposite is true as well. In Bullish markets, bad/negative news does not matter. It is quickly brushed off. There were plenty of examples of this from April - September during the incredible market rally.

In past reports, I have talked about how I like to think of price action as a battle between buyers and sellers/bulls and bears.

Right now, sellers are winning the battle. Bears are very much in control and have been for the last month. We first noted this in [“Risk Off” on October 19th](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zawI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F38cf7d38-9021-43ce-8fe2-7c27e88d6d85_1203x756.jpeg)](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70)

When Bears are in control, your playbook must change and change fast.

A Bull Market playbook will get you crushed in a Bearish environment.

So too will being overly reactive to every market move.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6EXc!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fec22ebc3-d75f-4111-abf6-2c79b32114f8_1201x552.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6EXc!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fec22ebc3-d75f-4111-abf6-2c79b32114f8_1201x552.jpeg)

When volatility is high (VIX >20), there are going to be huge and fast moves in both directions

This is what makes trading bearish environments so difficult. It’s not that the market just goes straight down (that would be easy to short). It’s that there is massive volatility both ways, huge dead-cat bounces to squeeze shorts and sucker in new longs, vicious rug pulls, huge gaps, etc.

Even in Bear Markets, your timing must be immaculate on shorts because you will get squeezed violently otherwise (like today’s gap up for those who came in short).

And if you are still buying longs on any type of strength, you will be slaughtered. I saw many Substack writers on my feed highlighting stocks like SNDK, ONDS, etc. as longs this week due to their impressive relative strength during this market pullback.

They both got pummeled today, down over -16% each.

There is no escaping the selling when the market gets bearish. No stock is immune. Line up and take your medicine.

Here at Marlin Capital, we are still on my [“risk off” signal given on 10/19](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70?utm_source=publication-search). I have been a boring trader since, but that is very much by design. When the market environment is not suited for my trading strategy, I simply sit and wait. This is something that I have learned from some of the trading GOATs, and has been reinforced by my personal experiences.

A lot of traders think that always being active/trading every day is the right move. I could NOT disagree more. This is a psychological hurdle for a lot of people. If you are a trader who is not trading every day, it might feel like you are being unproductive with your time. Our brains equate more activity with more productivity, and this is true for most jobs. But, trading is one of the very few professions where doing nothing is often the highest value activity.

Real-world data backs this up. The most comprehensive studies on retail traders (Taiwan dataset, Barber, Odean, etc.) show that the heaviest traders (in terms of # of trades) earn 10-12% less per year than lighter traders. Some of the top performing prop traders I have worked with were in cash/hedged 60-80% of the time.

But, don’t just take it from me. Take it from some of the actual trading GOATs:

> **Stanley Druckenmiller:**
>
> “There are times to make money. **There are times not to lose money.** And if you don’t know the difference, you’re going to get killed.”

> **Paul Tudor Jones:**
>

> **Jesse Livermore:**

> **Kristjan Kullamägi:**

>

>

> **Mark Minervini:**
>
> “If you can’t stick to a strategy and let other strategies have their turn while your strategy is in limbo, you don’t have a strategy... you have an addiction.”

Once you have been in the game long enough, it becomes crystal clear that there are times when the market feels “easy” to make money (May-September) and times when it feels extremely difficult (right now). As a trader, you do not score any extra points from the market Gods for playing on “All Madden Mode”, when you can just wait and play on “Rookie Mode” instead. The market does not reward you with any extra money for taking on a tougher challenge.

Some newer Marlin Capital readers have asked me if I ever short stocks and the answer is yes, I do. Those who have been with us for 6+ months will remember my [Top Trade Idea for Q2](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-trade-idea-for-q2-840?utm_source=publication-search) was an APP short in early April. We made +21% in less than a week on our APP short trade. Multi-year Marlin Capital readers will remember some of our big short trades from the 2022 Bear Market (HCP, MSTR, BILI, GTLB, etc.).

So my answer is yes, I will short stocks if the setup is there. However, as mentioned above, your timing must be immaculate on shorts. It is not nearly as easy as trading to the long side in a low volatility, bullish environment. This is why I am very selective on short trades.

My preferred method of shorting is to enter on bounces to key moving averages (which should act as firm resistance). Often, this best plays out as 3 straight green days (big bounce) into declining EMAs/resistance. This was the setup for our [APP short trade on 4/2](https://substack.com/chat/408010/post/3d65a58d-fce3-4f48-a8ed-ff9cc56f669e):

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FYYm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2ee986bf-982b-4390-9539-e6ef7976a990_1012x675.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FYYm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2ee986bf-982b-4390-9539-e6ef7976a990_1012x675.png)

In the APP chart above, notice the 3 day bounce into the declining 21 EMA (resistance). The ideal short entry is on Day 3, because that allows you to enter short at resistance (following a multi-day bounce) and puts the risk/reward in your favor. In my view, you do NOT want to be entering short on big red candles because that puts you at much higher risk of being caught in the eventual dead cat bounce/short squeeze. Instead, you want to be entering short **during** these dead cat bounces, so that you can be covering your shorts on big red candles/gap downs.

Remember, the market playbook has been flipped. In a bullish market, you can buy multi-day pullbacks to key moving averages (support). In a bearish market, you can short multi-day bounces to key moving averages (resistance).

#### My Big Picture Thoughts

[On Monday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/shaky-ground), I talked about how many former leading stocks are now establishing themselves below their 50-day moving averages for the first time since April. Bitcoin is establishing itself below its 200-day moving average.

And now, the S&P 500 and Nasdaq are following suit and establishing themselves below their 50-day moving averages (for the first time since April). Today’s bounce back above was forcefully rejected.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ab_n!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc633cc74-baf1-4043-b9f8-ee607e59cee0_1266x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ab_n!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc633cc74-baf1-4043-b9f8-ee607e59cee0_1266x844.png)

In my view, the major indices are likely setting up for a test of their 200-day moving averages. This would be ~$6200 for the SPX. Peak-to-trough, that would be a -10% drawdown for the SPX, putting us in official correction territory. Based off the recent price action, this is my base case on this pullback.

I wrote this to close out [Monday’s report,](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/shaky-ground) and it bears repeating tonight: Nothing we saw today was indicative of any type of bottoming action (in individual names or the indices). The market remains firmly in “risk off” mode heading into Friday.
