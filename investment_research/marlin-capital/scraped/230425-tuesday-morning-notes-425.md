# Tuesday Morning Notes 4.25

Technical Update

The choppy market action continued on Monday with the S&P 500 closing +0.09% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!t5k7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8715b388-8f9a-431a-8bf4-988df1d42d6f_1097x823.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!t5k7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8715b388-8f9a-431a-8bf4-988df1d42d6f_1097x823.jpeg)

If this type of market action (lack of action) has been putting you to sleep lately, you are surely not alone. Consider this - the SPX closed the first trading day of April at a price of $4124.  Yesterday (3+ weeks later), the SPX closed at $4137.  This is a 0.3% move in the index over 3+ weeks.

Moreover, the SPX high of April was $4169, while the low was $4069.  This means the market has traded in a 100 point range for almost an entire month.  In percentage terms, the market has traded in a narrow 2.4% range as we head into the final trading days of April.  This type of market action (lack of action) is designed to frustrate the impatient.
