Legendary trader Kristjan Kullamägi would call Tuesday’s candle in AEVA an “Episodic Pivot”. It’s 1 of the 3 main trading setups he used to help him turn $9k to $250M+.

An Episodic Pivot is a trading setup that occurs when a stock's fundamental “story” changes significantly (because of some key catalyst), leading to a massive shift in price and volume.

Episodic pivots/Gappers are my #1 favorite trading setup, and where most of my biggest winners have come from. If there is 1 trading setup to take from me, it’s this.

**Here’s how it typically plays out:**

**The Catalyst:** It all starts with a HUGE, game changing, fundamental catalyst. The rally must be driven by a significant event, most commonly an earnings surprise, but also new partnerships, deals, order wins, FDA approvals, etc.

**The High Volume Rally:** Very important - the stock must then respond to the catalyst news with a BIG gap and rally on **massive volume**. The higher the volume, the better. An ATH volume day is the cream of the crop. Sometimes, you get multiple ATH volume days in a row (like APLD saw after the CRWV deal announcement; see below).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tsg8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3f672410-807b-4650-9664-b44b80279c6d_1012x675.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tsg8!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3f672410-807b-4650-9664-b44b80279c6d_1012x675.png)

When a stock is up +34% on ATH volume (like AEVA) following a major catalyst, this is typically only the beginning of the big momentum move higher. Often times, this buying momentum carries over for weeks-months.

For AEVA, we may have been a year early on this name ([or ~7 months](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-smid-caps-with-massive-upside-potential?utm_source=publication-search)). Here’s how I’m playing this trade: [I bought](https://substack.com/chat/408010/post/3d65a58d-fce3-4f48-a8ed-ff9cc56f669e) the OSS Feb $10 calls at $0.65.

First off, when trading options, I always size for 0. Meaning my risk is -100% loss. I size accordingly (again, you are assuming -100% loss if it’s a losing trade, so you only risk the % of your account you are willing to lose on the trade).

Here’s my rationale on this OSS options trade:

OSS is a very volatile Small Cap that moves +/- 9% every day. It’s the type of stock that’s very difficult to trade with a stop loss. There is a high likelihood of being stopped out on a stock like OSS, no matter what happens in the end.

When I do not want to use a stop loss on a trade (because of high volatility and likelihood of being stopped out, even if the idea ends up working), I find it’s best to use call options. This allows you to fully see the trade idea through ([buy the ticket, take the ride](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-4-rules-for-successful-swing-trading?utm_source=publication-search)). This allows you to take the full ride. You are already sized for -100% loss, so you are not worrying about the huge intraday moves. Once you’re positioned, the idea will either work or fail, and the setup will make or lose money accordingly. But, the key is that you will be able to see the trade idea through (take the full ride).

We bought the Feb $10 calls at $0.65. On a winning trade (if the idea works), OSS could very easily see $12+. At OSS $12, the $10 calls would be worth at least $2 (in intrinsic value) + whatever time value, so the trade would be a 3x on our calls. We are risking -100% to make +200% +.

For these reasons, I’m playing this OSS trade idea via options.

In markets like the last couple months, it is important to remember that there will always be a better market ahead (eventually). I like to save my energy for that better market. And with the action to start off 2026, that “better market” looks like it has finally come.
