# Friday Morning Notes 10.11

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Quiet action in the major market indices yesterday following the slightly hotter than expected CPI reading.  The SPX finished -0.21% while the NDX closed -0.13%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YOjC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd0fd16ce-3995-474a-989c-1605752b3681_1200x885.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YOjC!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd0fd16ce-3995-474a-989c-1605752b3681_1200x885.jpeg)

It appears it was wishful thinking CPI would spark a fresh wave of trading activity.  Total US equity market volumes were down -16% vs their 20dma.  S&P 500 futures top of book liquidity ran in the 19th percentile over 1 year look back (liquidity is low).

We should see trading activity begin to pickup here with earnings season now kicking off.  With that said, the indices may continue in this a choppy period unless earnings are seen as overwhelmingly bullish or bearish.  Still, there should be plenty of trading opportunities in individual names provided the market is not breaking down.  [Earnings gappers](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-earnings-gapper-trade-setup?utm_source=publication-search) will be my focus over the next several weeks. Keep an eye on the daily morning notes for new trade ideas throughout earnings season.

The US election is under a month away, and history shows this October period can be choppy/down leading into close elections.  It is not typical to see the market rally into election day when the polls are close.
