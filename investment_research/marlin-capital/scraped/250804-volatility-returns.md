# Volatility Returns

US stocks had their worst week since early April last week with the **SPX -2.4%, NDX -2.2%,** and **RTY -4.2%**.

Volatility roared back into markets with the VIX finishing +22% on Friday and the S&P 500 finally snapping its streak of 19 consecutive days below a 0.8% daily move (with a -1.6% decline on Friday). Historical overlays said it was only a matter of time before the VIX awoke again.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DHyj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F19d51222-9800-4818-ac77-8cfbc75ce7ef_564x368.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DHyj!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F19d51222-9800-4818-ac77-8cfbc75ce7ef_564x368.jpeg)

The SPX also snapped its streak of 68 consecutive days above its 20-day moving average, its longest such streak since the late 90s **Dot Com Bubble**.

The catalyst?

**Friday’s disappointing jobs report.** The Labor Department reported that the US economy added only +73k jobs in July, well below consensus estimates of +115k. The readings for May and June were also revised down by a total of -258k.

The worse than expected jobs data sent September rate cut odds soaring. [Polymarket](https://poly.market/kuDz08S) odds of a 25bps cut in September have risen all the way to 71% after touching 43% on Wednesday (post-Powell FOMC speech).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!o48g!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1199f4c6-b5b1-4b82-8a3e-56e1f3ed6eea_1284x1594.jpeg)](https://poly.market/kuDz08S)

So, what now?

#### Routine Pullback or Major Market Top?

[Back in March](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/price-tells-all?utm_source=publication-search), I wrote:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JQM2!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa1fb0408-b61e-4316-a329-e7aa879c8ae3_1197x677.jpeg)](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/price-tells-all?utm_source=publication-search)

So, which one is this? Let’s start by looking at routine market pullbacks.

S&P 500 selloffs of -3% or more occur every 1.5 to 2 months, on average. It has been well over 3 months since we have had one (using SPX closing prices).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Joew!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F071fb453-7653-4fc5-a5ab-f31b96fbd5fd_1290x781.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Joew!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F071fb453-7653-4fc5-a5ab-f31b96fbd5fd_1290x781.jpeg)

Selloffs of -5% or more have historically occurred every 3 to 4 months, on average. With the last one occurring in April, we are approaching that average range now (and already beyond the median).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!x1An!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffc276cb0-47bd-401d-a4b8-cb2aaf2a3c5c_1290x849.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!x1An!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffc276cb0-47bd-401d-a4b8-cb2aaf2a3c5c_1290x849.jpeg)

As mentioned, prior to Friday, we were in the midst of a 97th percentile volatility crush streak AND the SPX’s longest streak above its 20-MA since 1999.

All this to say: **we were overdue for a market pullback.**

Now we’ve got one. **At this stage, I see no evidence that we’ve hit a major market peak/top for 2025.** Based off the price action so far, this appears to be a routine market pullback (and one that we were well overdue for), in my view.

For any new Marlin Capital readers looking for more context on what I consider to be “Bear Market” behavior/price action, I urge you to read my report [“Do or Die Time”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/do-or-die-time) (written on March 6th, 2025). Back then, we correctly identified the looming Bear Market due to the vast amounts of technical damage we were seeing across leading stocks and industry groups. Right now, we are not seeing anything close to that type of behavior.

Currently, we are only ~4 months into a new Bull Market that began in early April. A 4 month Bull Market would be the shortest in modern history, and the data does not support such a doomsday scenario.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K-Nm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5c7362b1-8943-4fb8-b5f1-19883f5060ee_1242x1484.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K-Nm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5c7362b1-8943-4fb8-b5f1-19883f5060ee_1242x1484.jpeg)

As you can see, the shortest Bull Market for the S&P 500 since 1960 was ~15 months (Dec ‘18 - Mar ‘20 Covid Crash).

Simply put, Bull Market Cycles typically always span multiple quarters (4+, throughout history).

That’s the big picture. The smaller picture is that we are still overdue for that -5% + market correction (at -2.9% now) and VIX spike (day 1 was Friday), and this points to the potential of more pain ahead. Moreover, we just entered the weakest seasonal part of the year for US stocks. Over the past 15 years, August and September are the 2 worst performing months for the S&P 500.

**Active Trading Portfolio**

Overall, I’m pleased with how we’ve managed a challenging past ~2 weeks in our Active Trading Portfolio.

We executed just 3 trades last week:

* **Sold MP for a +0.3% gain**
* **Exited HOOD for a +114% gain**
* **Exited ORCL for a +16% gain**

We are now 100% cash in our trading account for the first time since April.

While many traders likely chased new longs after Thursday’s MSFT + META induced gap up, we stayed disciplined. It’s never about the actual news, it’s how the market reacts to that news.

How did we know to pull back on entering new trades? We simply followed the money (OUR money). As I talked about in [“Negative Feedback Loop”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/negative-feedback-loop), the feedback on my trades tells me all I need to know.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!CCSk!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F38dd2a5f-b025-4996-9dee-33ea63b6b320_1198x618.jpeg)](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/negative-feedback-loop)

In the past couple weeks, we started to see breakouts failing (IREN, TMC, etc.), breadth deteriorating, and leadership thinning. Follow through was non-existent, stocks continued to roll over after our exits, and even the ideas I skipped started to fail.

When the market stops rewarding your strategy, it’s time to step back and focus on protecting your capital. No matter what your strategy is (swing trading, day trading, scalping, etc.), there will be times where the conditions are not in your favor. This doesn’t mean you chase the next shiny object.

> “If you can’t stick to a strategy and let other strategies have their turn while your strategy is in limbo, you don’t have a strategy…you have an addiction”
>
> - Legendary Trader Mark Minervini

As a swing trader, I thrive in certain market conditions. These conditions were present from late April to about ~2 weeks ago. They’re not present anymore. Knowing when to push the gas and when to ease off is one of the keys to outperforming.  **Know your strategy.**

Volatility has suddenly returned. Earnings gaps are fizzling, and excess returns on beats are historically weak. In fact, the median stock posting a positive Q2 earnings surprise has only outperformed the SPX by +55bps on the day following its report, well below the historical median of +101bps.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!n7QE!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F89c2d065-e913-48c2-bf4b-11937f1d533f_1012x820.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!n7QE!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F89c2d065-e913-48c2-bf4b-11937f1d533f_1012x820.jpeg)

For swing traders, this is now a “hard dollar” environment where it’s easy to lose money. Patience is key. In my view, most stocks need more time to consolidate their strong runs from April lows.

As I often say, it’s a market of stocks. Right now, individual stock setups are scarce. A month ago (and in ideal environments), the only issue is that there were too many setups and it was a challenge to narrow down my focus list. This is no longer the case here. For now, I’m in capital preservation mode.

We’ll be ready to pounce when the market is ready and the setups are present. Until then, less is more.
