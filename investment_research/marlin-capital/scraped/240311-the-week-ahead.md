# The Week Ahead

The big story to come out of last week was Friday’s reversal in the market (NVDA, Semis, etc.).  To start off, the QQQ gave an outside, bearish engulfing reversal candle on Friday on huge volume.  This was the highest volume for the QQQ since near the October lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sHrm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb73fde25-ccc8-4df1-afc1-4d53c76547a9_958x727.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sHrm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb73fde25-ccc8-4df1-afc1-4d53c76547a9_958x727.jpeg)

At one point during the week, NVDA closed 42% above its 50-day moving average, its highest close above its 50-day since June 2003 (it was a $2 stock at the time).  NVDA’s $2.3T market cap is now larger than ~30 other names in the SOX combined (think AMD + AVGO + QCOM + INTC + TSM, etc.).

NVDA, the undisputed market leader, ended up losing $250B in market cap on Friday afternoon (stock finished -5.5%) after briefly making new highs in the morning.  Technically, Friday’s bearish engulfing candle on huge volume (3rd highest in last 12 months, the other 2 were earnings) is a cautionary sign.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8TRa!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F02ab7503-e572-426a-9d8c-9721ebcb2a62_983x731.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8TRa!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F02ab7503-e572-426a-9d8c-9721ebcb2a62_983x731.jpeg)

4 of the past 8 sessions have been distribution on the Nasdaq (declines on increased volume).  This has coincided with decreasing net highs, reversal action in leaders (NVDA, semis, etc.), and breakdowns in others.  According to BofA, last week was the largest weekly outflow from Tech Funds on record.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NsJ3!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd2b0ddad-961a-4673-a7f9-853f8af2ac63_614x468.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NsJ3!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd2b0ddad-961a-4673-a7f9-853f8af2ac63_614x468.jpeg)

With all that said, we have more reasons for caution as we head into this week.  However, the indices remain in uptrends for now until key support levels begin to break.  For the SPX, the index heads into Monday still firmly above its 8 and 21 EMAs (nothing to be alarmed about in terms of technical damage yet).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kJdV!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F21e6d1cf-ddc5-482f-99ab-fd8db3da99d4_1195x887.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kJdV!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F21e6d1cf-ddc5-482f-99ab-fd8db3da99d4_1195x887.jpeg)

We have a big week ahead for Macro data points, as all eyes turn to CPI tomorrow and PPI on Thursday (key data points ahead of FOMC next week).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MPaV!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F82c5a27f-1dde-47d7-97e1-a44d9e23e008_1194x713.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MPaV!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F82c5a27f-1dde-47d7-97e1-a44d9e23e008_1194x713.jpeg)

A softer CPI number could restart the equity rally while a hotter number than expected could accelerate things to the downside.  This is my key catalyst to watch for the week ahead.

Elsewhere, we have a lighter slate of earnings ahead this week but still some potential gapper opportunities to watch (ASAN, ONON, PATH, ADBE, ULTA, SMAR).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ls2A!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fca3dc9da-7776-49c8-b666-b67cc6050bba_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ls2A!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fca3dc9da-7776-49c8-b666-b67cc6050bba_1920x1080.jpeg)

TNA – I’m still holding my long position here as we head into this week.  Friday’s reversal in the market pulled Small Caps along with it and TNA was unable to hold its breakout from early in the session.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GKYj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff154c2bd-4daf-48f6-b031-f888414b377a_987x726.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GKYj!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff154c2bd-4daf-48f6-b031-f888414b377a_987x726.jpeg)

With Friday’s reversal candle, I will be moving my stop loss to $39.48 (risk is now ~4.2% on this trade).  I am decreasing my risk here because of Friday’s reversals across the market and some warnings signs of a potential larger correction.

This is my only position heading into the week of 3/11.  have no plans to make any new trades on Monday ahead of Tuesday morning’s (3/12) CPI report.  As mentioned, I believe this is a huge data point for the market and will greatly impact the direction from here.  With the market looking more uncertain heading into this report, I see no reason to add additional risk ahead of time.  Instead, tomorrow’s CPI report should help to add some clarity to a market looking more and more uncertain.
