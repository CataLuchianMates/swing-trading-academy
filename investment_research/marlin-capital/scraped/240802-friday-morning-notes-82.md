# Friday Morning Notes 8.2

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Big equity sell off across the board yesterday with the S&P 500 finishing -1.37%, QQQ declining -2.42%, and RTY -3.5%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3Bj8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F653e0442-4b21-4d26-999c-1bbb31e21349_1204x885.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3Bj8!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F653e0442-4b21-4d26-999c-1bbb31e21349_1204x885.jpeg)

The catalyst for yesterday’s sell off was the release of recessionary-like ISM data at 10 AM EST, which ignited the sharp market reversal. Particularly alarming was the ISM jobs subindex, which plummeted to 43.4 from 49.3.  This is a severe drop and represents the weakest employment reading since June 2020, and is even lower than the 45.4 recorded in September 2008 (the month Lehman collapsed).  The only times we’ve seen this number lower over the last 25+ years was (1) The Dot Com Bubble Bust; and (2) the 2008 Great Financial Crisis.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qIlm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5fd1f99-f634-42a1-b8bf-778e2987b280_1284x765.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qIlm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5fd1f99-f634-42a1-b8bf-778e2987b280_1284x765.jpeg)

The playbook on how to trade stocks around economic data points in 2024 has officially flipped.  Previously, we were in a bad data = good for stocks regime, as it meant the market was looking ahead to (more + earlier) Fed rate cuts.  Now, the market is clearly anxious that the Fed is behind the curve on rate cuts, and that September will be too late/they will be too slow.

As I highlighted in “The Massive Week Ahead”, the beginning of Fed rate cuts is a crossroads moment for the market.  If the economy avoids a recession, stocks tend to perform quite well following Fed easing.  However, if a recession does occur, it is typical to see a Bear Market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tLhs!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F409dea19-38f3-484d-b1d5-9908073e2664_1178x650.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tLhs!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F409dea19-38f3-484d-b1d5-9908073e2664_1178x650.jpeg)

Yesterday’s action was a typical recessionary trade with a flight to safety/defensives.  Economically sensitive cyclicals underperformed Defensives by 429bps, one of the worst days in the last 16+ years.  The only other comparable days were during actual recessions (March 2020 Covid Crash and 2008 GFC).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fcbr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6ca9c621-8e50-409b-826d-8414ac43d2c5_1134x745.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fcbr!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6ca9c621-8e50-409b-826d-8414ac43d2c5_1134x745.jpeg)

Cyclicals (like Semis) were bid up on Wednesday following the FOMC with the consensus view that inflation was on the right track while economic growth/activity was holding together.  With the release of recessionary like ISM data yesterday, the consensus soft landing narrative that has been priced in throughout the last 12+ months is now in question.

Stat of the day – NVDA has trade up or down +/- 7% in each of the last 3 days.  These are $200B+ market cap swings on the daily…Nothing to see here.

Earnings post-close:

AMZN – AWS growth fine, but Op income worse in qtr and guide…Stock -9% this morning

AAPL – Slight headline beat w/ iPhones better ($39.3B vs cons $28.95B), but China revs were a bit light ($14.73B vs cons $15.26B)…Stock -1.5% this morning

INTC – Yikes! Missed on EPS & revs, suspending dividend…Stock -24% this morning

SNAP – Yikes! Missed in qtr and guided Q3 well below estimates…Stock -20% this morning

TEAM – Guiding Q3 well below estimates…Stock -12% this morning

NFP Report:

114k US jobs added in July vs estimates of 180K…Big miss.  June was revised lower as well (206K to 179K).

Unemployment rose to 4.3% from 4.1% (estimates were 4.1%).

Not a good report here for employment/jobs.  Today’s NFP data will do nothing to calm the market’s new recession fears.  Bad = Bad again, and we are set for a big gap down this morning for US stocks (SPX -1.5%; NDX -2.2%, RTY -3.6%).

Fed Funds futures are now pricing in a ~70% chance of a 50bps cut in September (it was 11% just 2 days ago).  But, the market does not care so much about that right now with recessionary fears taking hold of the narrative.
