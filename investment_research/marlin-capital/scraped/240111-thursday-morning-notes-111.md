# Thursday Morning Notes 1.11

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The S&P 500 closed +0.57% on Wednesday on the lightest volume day of 2024 so far, with most traders sitting on hands in the no fly zone ahead of CPI this morning (to be released at 8:30 AM EST).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6sIt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F79a3e014-ca3c-465c-b523-c1ce7df442c3_1204x894.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6sIt!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F79a3e014-ca3c-465c-b523-c1ce7df442c3_1204x894.jpeg)

With yesterday’s move, the SPX is officially in the green YTD while the Nasdaq is <20 bps away.  Yesterday’s market action included narrow breadth as the Magnificent 7 accounted for ~80% of the index move (META +3.7% on a handful of upgrades including GS and breakout above $360, NVDA +2.3% on continued upward momentum + MSFT +1.9% in tandem).

The bid for quality tech remains as the cautious cyclical micro data points are beginning to pile up so far this earnings season (Samsung, AEHR, MBLY, MCHP, EXTR).

All eyes on CPI this morning – the whisper number is for >0.3% Core MoM.

Assuming no large gaps in either direction following the CPI release at 8:30 EST, I like the way the indices are setting up technically despite some recent choppiness.  Small Caps have lagged behind during this week’s bounce, but the action in TNA looks constructive to me.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fJ4v!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcc654173-9ba2-46ab-a40e-e77fc77a9b66_1201x885.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fJ4v!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcc654173-9ba2-46ab-a40e-e77fc77a9b66_1201x885.jpeg)

TNA has carved out strong support around $34.50-$35.  In my view, this is setting up for a potentially bullish move back to recent highs ($41-$42).

I will be looking to get long TNA on a break above yesterday’s highs ($36.10).  My stop loss on this trade will be a closing stop below $35.
