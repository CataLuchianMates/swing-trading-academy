# Tuesday Morning Notes 1.30

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Monday was a true “calm before the storm” type trading session to kick off the week with trading volumes tracking down -16% vs 20d avg as investors sat on hands ahead of the heavy plate of Macro (FOMC and Payrolls) and Micro (32% of SPX mkt cap reports) slated for this week. The S&P 500 finished the day +0.76%, NDX +101bps, and the RTY led the way up 1.6%.

Stocks popped in the afternoon on the headline: “US Treasury quarterly financing estimates: to borrow $760B in Jan-Mar quarter vs $816B prior estimate”…I.E. - les bond supply = lower yields.

Top of the board yesterday:

Bitcoin Sensitive +4.5%

Non-Profitable Tech +3.9%

Most Short +3.6%

Bottom of the board yesterday:

China ADRs -2.3%

The market is clearly trying to widen breadth with 367 SPX names closing in the green yesterday. Small Caps have now outperformed 1% over the last week, which is the first outperformance for the group in 4 weeks. Short exposure in Small Caps is now sitting at multi-year highs.

Potential earnings gapper trades to watch today - SMCI, SANM
