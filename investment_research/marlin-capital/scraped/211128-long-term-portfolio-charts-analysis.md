# Long Term Portfolio: Charts Analysis 

The market is at an inflection point right now.  Despite the general market weakness we saw on Friday, growth stocks held up well and may be attempting to form a bottom.  I am keeping an open mind on what may come next.

First, let’s look at the QQQ.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!F3Op!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbd2c3f08-0b1a-4684-b73d-a87bf0df2df8_1198x911.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!F3Op!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbd2c3f08-0b1a-4684-b73d-a87bf0df2df8_1198x911.jpeg)

The QQQ is now below the 21 EMA and may be setting up a potential date with the 50 EMA.  If that happens, it would provide some good buying opportunities in my long term portfolio.

However, I am open to the possibility that this is simply a potential shakeout.  At this point, we are not even 5% off all time highs.  Correction territory starts at 10%, which is where I start to consider buying with a long term outlook.  We typically get 1-3 corrections per year, so I patiently wait for these opportunities.  If this is not one of them, I will **not** be adding to my long term positions.  Instead, I will shift the funds in my Active Portfolio to gain more long term exposure.

As we know, ARKK is a good proxy for growth stocks.  I am currently short ARKK to hedge my long term positions.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!M5rO!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa618fa7d-219e-4360-ae54-c22a0a760ebc_1221x920.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!M5rO!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa618fa7d-219e-4360-ae54-c22a0a760ebc_1221x920.jpeg)

ARKK is at an inflection point right now.  There are 2 distinct possibilities.  One is that a bottom was put in around the $103 level last week.  If this support level holds, and we get an upside crossover of the 4 and 8 EMAs, I will promptly remove my hedge.

The second scenario is that we are simply seeing an oversold bounce off the $103 level.  This would be confirmed by a break below this level in the coming days.  If this is the case, I believe we are setting up for a test of the May 2021 lows ($97).  Again, this scenario would provide us with some great opportunities to add to long term positions.

I am keeping an open mind on which scenario will play out.  In the first scenario, I will not be adding to long term positions and I will be removing by ARKK short hedge.  In the second scenario, I will be adding to many long term positions at the key support levels I mention below

Anyone who is bullish on the stocks mentioned below can consider adding at the key support levels I list, regardless if I choose to or not.  For my personal portfolio, I am more likely to add to my smaller positions than my biggest positions, unless there is a significant correction.  This is because I am happy with my position sizes at the top of my portfolio (AFRM, COIN, ENPH, NVDA, SHOP).  Again, if you are bullish on these top names and want to add to your positions, consider adding at the key support levels I listed.
