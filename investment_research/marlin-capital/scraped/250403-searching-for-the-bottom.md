# Searching For The Bottom 

**Where Will Markets Bottom?**

The Stock Market experienced a massive selloff today following President Trump’s announcement of sweeping tariffs on nearly all US trade partners.

The major indices suffered their worst days since the March 2020 Covid Crash. The S&P 500 fell -4.8%, while the Nasdaq plunged -6%.

US stocks are now in the process of being re-priced lower, reflecting heightened uncertainty and more pessimistic economic expectations.

**Impact of Tariffs and Earnings Pressure**

BofA ran a scenario analysis on the Liberation Day tariffs, focusing on (1) the cost increase from incremental tariffs, and (2) the foreign sales impact from retaliatory measures. Their data found that if trading partners respond with equal retaliatory force, reciprocal tariffs could hit current S&P 500 operating income by -32%. Just import cost inflation with no retaliation is estimated to be a -5% hit.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wfQL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb2dc6bc-54e5-4a34-8cc4-35ef72618780_1061x637.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wfQL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb2dc6bc-54e5-4a34-8cc4-35ef72618780_1061x637.jpeg)

BofA estimates that the Russell 2000 Index of Small Caps would see the biggest hit (due to thinner margins), a potential -22% decline in operating margin.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VtEC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6373aa91-b27b-4b44-ba85-ddc1450aa1dd_820x500.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VtEC!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6373aa91-b27b-4b44-ba85-ddc1450aa1dd_820x500.jpeg)

Mid Caps are estimated to see a bigger operating income hit than Large Caps (-11%), but smaller than Small Caps.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ddXU!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0b4c6b8-570c-4b59-9c24-5364ae19c647_859x548.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ddXU!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0b4c6b8-570c-4b59-9c24-5364ae19c647_859x548.jpeg)

Deteriorating operating margins are likely to weigh heavily on corporate earnings. Current data suggest S&P 500 earnings estimates remain overpriced, having only been revised downward by -2% so far YTD.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hHIF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb6315709-e3b4-453c-a88e-6b2b8c5f890e_840x586.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hHIF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb6315709-e3b4-453c-a88e-6b2b8c5f890e_840x586.jpeg)

Notably, [we have seen](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/price-tells-all) plenty of downward revisions in recent weeks, yet consensus still anticipates positive EPS growth in 2025. In fact, consensus is still projecting +11% YoY EPS growth in 2025. This is obviously a scenario that assumes no recession.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NobZ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fece3cc27-dc07-4556-a29b-774439cc13c0_868x588.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NobZ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fece3cc27-dc07-4556-a29b-774439cc13c0_868x588.jpeg)

Historically, the average recession leads to a significant decline in earnings (typically in the range of -15% +), yet almost none of this risk is currently priced in.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ewyX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0b43ca59-95ca-446a-9f0c-fc279ea98495_555x281.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ewyX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0b43ca59-95ca-446a-9f0c-fc279ea98495_555x281.jpeg)

Today’s market selloff underscores how quickly equities can get re-priced when a more pessimistic outlook emerges overnight. In my view, the equity market re-pricing is not quite finished yet.

**Recession Risks on the Rise**

Recession odds are climbing, according to live betting data from Polymarket. The live betting odds of a US recession in 2025 now stand at 50%.

Should a recession materialize, historical patterns suggest a median S&P 500 decline of -23%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Z7Mb!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F28f55450-b0cc-4e83-98b7-54e6f43e3a8d_643x382.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Z7Mb!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F28f55450-b0cc-4e83-98b7-54e6f43e3a8d_643x382.png)

10 out of the last 13 recessions (77%) were associated with a Bear Market in U.S. stocks.

Recessionary market selloffs also typically coincide with longer Bear cycles (in duration/time). The median recessionary correction lasts 282 trading days, while the current SPX correction just over 30 trading days old.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TmPL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F823d6e6b-a9e1-441a-8d29-c4bd17f83445_1284x668.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TmPL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F823d6e6b-a9e1-441a-8d29-c4bd17f83445_1284x668.png)

Again, history suggests that markets will face prolonged pressure if economic conditions deteriorate from here.

**No Signs of Capitulation Yet**

From a technical perspective, we are not yet seeing the capitulation selling typically associated with market bottoms. I have covered these [positioning dynamics](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-big-unwind) in detail at Marlin Capital since February.

New positioning data for March (just released today) paints a striking picture: Hedge Funds unloaded stocks at a record pace, while Retail Investors bought stocks at an equally unprecedented rate.

Starting with Hedge Funds, they have been unloading for awhile now. In March, Hedge Funds Net sold global equities at the fastest pace in 12+ years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!66Tr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fde164f38-0650-4298-9836-3c682df4c23e_632x375.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!66Tr!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fde164f38-0650-4298-9836-3c682df4c23e_632x375.jpeg)

All 11 GICS sectors were net sold by Hedge Funds in March.

For retail, I talked about the dip buying from this cohort throughout Q1. In March, retail traders net bought $39B of equities, the largest monthly inflow on record.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zk1q!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F78f143c9-461f-4636-9017-2d7f43130212_485x349.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zk1q!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F78f143c9-461f-4636-9017-2d7f43130212_485x349.png)

This was led by record monthly retail inflows to single stocks like NVDA, TSLA, AMD, and F.

Despite the record inflow, though, we saw retail demand start to wane by the end of the month. The strongest buying was observed at the beginning of March, finally flipping to moderate selling at the end of the month.

Overall, this type of retail dip buying behavior is reminiscent of Q1 2022. In Q1 2022, the first downside quarter after a roaring Bull Market was met with heavy retail dip buying.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!INVu!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff32af619-072b-464a-a2b4-88efb526fb23_1572x633.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!INVu!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff32af619-072b-464a-a2b4-88efb526fb23_1572x633.png)

This is also what we saw here in Q1 2025. In my view, this dynamic makes us vulnerable to future capitulation selling, potentially mirroring the sustained lows of 2022 (when retail investors finally turned sellers near the the bear market bottom).

As I pointed out in [“Rough Waters in Q1”,](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/rough-waters-in-q1) when you put it all together, overall market positioning has now moved into “light” territory. With that said, it remains above prior market bottoms/troughs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TFZ9!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd25011a1-2b67-47fe-8e14-7ef516aa9344_704x425.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TFZ9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd25011a1-2b67-47fe-8e14-7ef516aa9344_704x425.jpeg)

In [“Red Wednesday”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/red-wednesday), I talked about how following a major peak in JPM’s Tactical Positioning Monitor, we normally see 15%+ SPX declines. Following major peaks in the JPM TPM, SPX declines have historically been between -14-25% (ex. 07-08), with an average decline of -20% post TPM highs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mPLI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9e50f8ca-436b-4edc-b761-f1484bba5166_605x590.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mPLI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9e50f8ca-436b-4edc-b761-f1484bba5166_605x590.jpeg)

Overall, my positioning data suggests more downside is ahead for US stocks. A -14% SPX correction is the minimum for a major positioning cleanse ($5200 region).

Initial Target: $5200 – This remains my first downside target for the S&P 500.

Bearish Recession Scenario: $4600 – In a a recessionary scenario (with an earnings recession), the $4600 level could come into play. This aligns with historical median recession declines and historical Bear Market bottom valuations.

**Final Thoughts**

There is no historical playbook for these tariffs. Markets are navigating uncharted waters now. These tariffs are much more significant and widespread than Trump’s 2018 Trade War with China.

Recession probabilities are rising, earnings estimates are likely to remain under pressure, and capital flows have yet to show any signs of capitulation.

My first downside target remains $5200. In a more Bearish recessionary scenario, it is possible we can see $4600.

Patience will be key, as the market has yet to show any signs of bottoming. I will be continuing to monitor capitulation signals, recession indicators, and earnings revisions to help guide me in calling a big picture market bottom.
