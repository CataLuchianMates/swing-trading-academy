# Not So Fast 

* Mag 7 stocks are making a major statement today, pushing back against “the end of US exceptionalism” narrative.
* MSFT and META earnings last night are showing why it’s never a smart move to bet against US Big Tech.
* MSFT +9% today on big beat with Azure +35% YoY (vs +31-32% exp).
* META +5% on topline beat (unexpected) and raise on CapEx guide.
* NVDA +4%, responding to higher Meta CapEx guidance
* [2 weeks ago](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-patience-game), I talked about how the relative valuation of Big Tech vs the SPX was at 10-year lows, which felt very extreme

* So what happens? The scenario “nobody” wanted.
* Not only has Big Tech “held the line” on earnings (so far), but exceeded all expectations (which had been rapidly reset much lower over the last month +).
* Positioning and leverage were completely de-risked and underweight Mag 7 / US equities in general
* And suddenly, Mag 7 is awkwardly leading to the upside again following MSFT and META earnings last night
* In a rally that funds are extremely underweight for, but are now forced to buy some back higher
* We are starting to see some of the “hold your nose and forced to add back net exposure” phenomenon play out in equities
* I talked about this [on Monday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/repositioning-rally), particularly with mechanical buying/forced short covering from systematics/CTAs
* CTAs had begun buying from a -$26B net short position
