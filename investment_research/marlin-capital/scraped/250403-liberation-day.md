# Liberation Day

The S&P 500 and Nasdaq are sinking >3% this morning following the “Liberation Day” tariff news. The reciprocal tariff policy President Trump announced would impose a weighted average tariff rate of 18.3%, around 3pp higher than most estimates anticipated going in (with a minimum baseline tariff of 10% across the board).

US Futures tanked when Trump brought out the big board of reciprocal tariffs (chart below):

Those who were trading during Trump’s 1st term will remember the US Trade War with China, which weighed heavily on equities during periods of 2018-2019. This time around, it is a global Trade War.

Following the Liberation Day tariff news, the odds of a US recession in 2025 have risen to 46% (according to live betting market Polymarket).

As I have talked about, this is the key debate for markets now. Historically, the median recessionary S&P 500 correction is -23% and lasts 6+ months.

To make matters worse, we are also coming from a higher peak P/E ratio in 2025 (highest ever peak outside of the Dot Com Bubble), so I feel fairly confident in saying a recession would bring about -20% + declines during this cycle. My recessionary price target remains $4600.

On a technical basis, I talked on Tuesday about how the action was pretty standard action for an h sell breakdown. We briefly broke the h lows before squeezing for a couple days. The real breakdown typically comes on the 2nd move lower. With this morning’s gap down, we are now making new correction lows. My target on this breakdown remains $5200 for the SPX.

APP - I initiated my short near yesterday’s open at $276.50. We squeezed during the session, so there were plenty of opportunities to get short APP yesterday. I had a limit set to short more at $306 and buy July $300 puts at $50, but was never filled. I would not be looking to add more short on today’s huge gap down.
