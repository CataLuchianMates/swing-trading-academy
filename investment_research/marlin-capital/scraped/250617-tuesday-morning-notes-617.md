# Tuesday Morning Notes 6.17

US stocks kicked off the week on a high note yesterday (SPX +0.94%, NDX +1.42%, RTY +1.23%), recapturing all of Friday’s losses.

Headlines continue to be focused on Israel/Iran (Trump cut G7 trip short to return to Washington but denied suggestions it was to work on a ceasefire between Israel and Iran, instead saying his reason was “much bigger than that”.) Trump said he has not reached out to Iran for peace talks in any way shape or form but he wants a “real end” to the conflict. Axios reported the US was considering a meeting this week between US envoy Vance/Witkoff and Iran’s foreign minister to revive nuclear talks).

Not much action in the US stock market so far this morning with the indices slightly negative (-0.3%). The focus is now shifting to tomorrow’s FOMC decision, with the consensus that the Fed will leave rates unchanged (98% probability according to Polymarket).

Typically, it is common to see low volume index chop into Fed days and today’s action is no different so far. Even though the result of tomorrow’s meeting is known (no rate change), the FOMC statement, dot plot, and Powell’s commentary are all potential market moving catalysts. I expect to continue to see more directionless/choppy action in the market into tomorrow’s Fed decision at 2 PM EST.
