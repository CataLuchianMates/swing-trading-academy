# Thursday Morning Notes 3.23

Quick Fed Thoughts & Technical Update

The Fed delivered as expected yesterday, raising rates by 25 bps because of stronger inflation and labor market data.  This rate hike was a unanimous decision among the committee despite increased stress in the banking system.  Fed Chair Powell was relatively optimistic when discussing the banking sector, describing it as “sound and resilient”, while arguing that many banks have been able to handle the Fed’s “well-telegraphed” hikes so far.

With that said, increased financial stress and tighter credit conditions are likely to affect the Fed’s path moving forward.  Powell acknowledged this fact by saying what’s happening among banks is basically a substitute for another rate hike, and should help their fight against inflation.  Against this backdrop, the FOMC statement softened interest rate forward guidance from “ongoing increases to the target range will be appropriate” to “additional policy firming may be appropriate”.

The market responded well initially, rallying to daily highs during Powell’s press conference.  However, the indices closed very weak with a sell off in the last 30 minutes.  The SPX closed -1.65%.

The SPX closed at $3937, 3 points above the 200 SMA.  We also broke below the 21 and 60 EMAs during yesterday’s afternoon sell off.

Yesterday’s daily candle is an ugly bearish reversal candle.  With that said, we still closed above the 200 SMA and we are set for a gap up this morning.

On a technical basis, the close of this week will be critical.  Bulls are looking to recapture $3965 and $3977-$3980 with a rally of 200 SMA support.  Meanwhile, bears are looking to break down $3930 and $3900 decisively.  I will be waiting on the sidelines to see which way these key levels break before placing any new trades.  In individual stocks, I am seeing both bullish and bearish potential setups emerge, but one side is likely to fail depending on which direction the market moves.
