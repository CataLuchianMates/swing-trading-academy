# Wednesday Morning Notes 1.8

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Big risk off day yesterday with the SPX -1.11% and NDX -1.79%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KH0F!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F94d13632-d7b1-4b13-98ec-cb0a68acd5bf_955x715.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KH0F!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F94d13632-d7b1-4b13-98ec-cb0a68acd5bf_955x715.jpeg)

There was no real place to hide with Semis -2%, Software -2.5%, and Megacaps -2.6% all down in tandem. NVDA sold off -9% from pre-market highs (finished down -6%) on a sell the news reaction to Jensen’s CES keynote. This was an ugly reversal candle for the biggest and most important company in the world.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DYHt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff2809b69-ceb8-4ad8-aa77-42151152c186_1188x888.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DYHt!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff2809b69-ceb8-4ad8-aa77-42151152c186_1188x888.jpeg)

Data was hawkish yesterday (JOLTS and ISM) and 10 YR yields at 4.7% are clearly weighing on stocks. The US stock market is closed tomorrow (national day of mourning for Jimmy Carter) followed by NFP Friday, which now seems more like an uncomfortable setup. As a result, I’m not anticipating a ton of offense being played today into NFP.

Hedge Funds came into the year leaning net short after a weak end of December. We will see the HF data soon on yesterday’s sell off, but it is likely we saw funds press their shorts yesterday. Also signs of a retail de-risk in the market yesterday which is incredibly rare to see this early in the year. Higher rates and really poor market breadth appear to be the 2 main culprits.

Active Portfolio -

Plenty of reasons to take a more defensive stance now after yesterday’s action with the indices sloppy and leaders wavering (see NVDA). Not sure what happens from here but feedback in your own account is always the best indicator. I put on 3 longs on Monday looking for a move higher but yesterday’s reversal resulted in expectations breakers galore. Things can change quick on the market so the best way to handle it is to react and adapt.

RBRK - Stopped out for a -7.3% loss on this trade after yesterday’s reversal

AVGO - I’m putting in a hard stop at $225. Still a top pick for 2025 but if the market starts to break down here, it will be better to re-enter at $180-$200.

BE - Hard stop at $23.46. This will be a failed breakout if it breaks below.
