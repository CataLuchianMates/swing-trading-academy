# Weakness

We saw more risk off action in US stocks today despite the S&P 500 finishing flat.

The indices are masking A TON of weakness right now. As I always say, “it’s a market of stocks”.

In [“Risk Off”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70), I talked about how some leading AI stocks put in potential **Climax Top** patterns last week.

Quantum Computing stocks, probably the most speculative group out there (and therefore 1 of the best indictors of risk appetite), are all following through to the downside this week.

**IONQ**

This appears to be a classic topping formation to me.

Let’s start with the monthly chart. We still have 8 trading days left this month, but IONQ will need to pull off a miracle rally to avoid this monthly topping candle.

Let’s rewind back to 2020.

Zoom (ZM) was the hottest stock on Wall Street, with everyone saying that the company was going to change the way we worked forever. It rallied from $65 to $588 in ~8 months.

Then, it formed a shooting star topping candle on the monthly chart (while being outside its upper monthly bollinger).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tVjp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff9d98250-ea86-4834-8fcd-8b2ac6744f4e_1132x652.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tVjp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff9d98250-ea86-4834-8fcd-8b2ac6744f4e_1132x652.png)

This is a very similar chart to IONQ and many other hot stocks right now (RGTI, QBTS, OKLO, etc.).

After a huge upside move, a big shooting star candlestick is a reversal candle. It often signifies the end of the uptrend. It means that buyers initially pushed the price much higher (until exhaustion), but sellers finally took over and rejected the move. It means that sellers have taken over control for the first time.

Think of price action as a battle between buyers and sellers. Buyers were in control early in the battle, but sellers took over and won in the end.

The great William O’Neil, the Godfather of the **climax top pattern**, gives [several different signs for recognizing them](https://shop.investors.com/images/promotional/ClimaxTop_Booklet.pdf).

Let’s look at the current OKLO chart as an example. OKLO is a pre-revenue nuclear energy play that just went on a parabolic upside run.

O’Neil warns:

> “After already making a move up 18 or more weeks out of a first or second stage chart base, the stock suddenly accelerates. In many cases it will rise 25-50% in just one, two or three weeks.”
>
> - William O’Neil

OKLO did all this, accelerating (from an 20wk+ stage 2 base) and rising +64% in 2 weeks (and +129% in 4 weeks), before last week’s shooting star candle.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!majZ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faa05e9ae-c49b-4769-833f-3e517dc5a16b_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!majZ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faa05e9ae-c49b-4769-833f-3e517dc5a16b_1396x653.png)

Another climax top signal for O’Neil is when the stock breaks its upper trend channel:

> “On a weekly logarithmic chart you can draw an upper channel line over several months, connecting the highs of at least three points. When the stock breaks above this channel line, it is a sign the upward move is getting out of control”
>
> - William O’Neil

OKLO broke well above its weekly channel trend line and completely outside its upper bollinger band (for the 1st time in its history), a sign the upward move was getting out of control.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1oQu!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5efd5f3c-dfd1-4d89-bd5f-5843fc0e59ed_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1oQu!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5efd5f3c-dfd1-4d89-bd5f-5843fc0e59ed_1396x653.png)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9GYS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0d594be0-3b7b-4354-be2c-6f7ed774e3e3_1132x652.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9GYS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0d594be0-3b7b-4354-be2c-6f7ed774e3e3_1132x652.png)

In my view, stocks like OKLO have likely peaked for the cycle. And in the less likely (but still possible) case that they haven’t, we can probably say that the majority of the upside move is over. This is the most important thing to look at as an investor/trader. Again, wisdom from the great William O’Neil:

> “How do you lock in your gains when you find yourself with a big winner? In my experience it is often best to sell a big winner while it is still advancing and looks strong. **The common fear is “what if it goes up more without me?” That may happen, but remember, your goal is not to sell at the very top price. That’s almost impossible to do. Rather, you just want to participate in the majority of the move.”**
>
> - William O’Neil

Jesse Livermore had a similar philosophy. To paraphrase, he compared a bull/bear trend to a 9 inning baseball game. His goal was to participate in the middle innings of the trend (think 3rd - 7th inning), not the beginning/final innings of the trend. In other words, his goal was to participate in the majority of the move (like O’Neil), not the entire move. Minervini is the same. So is Druckenmiller.

Speaking of the great Mark Minervini, he has a rule he likes to call the 50-80 rule, which says:

> “When a secular leader makes a major top, there’s a 50% chance it will drop 80% and an 80% chance it will drop 50%”
>
> - Mark Minervini

**I have found this 50-80 rule to be true in every cycle I’ve traded through.**

Whether or not the stock **ever** recovers back to its old highs is completely dependent on the company and their success. Think back to the Dot Com Bubble. Most of the leaders from that cycle never recovered. But some did. AMZN dropped -94% during the Dot Com Bust, but ended up becoming 1 of the largest companies in the world. Over the long-term, it’s all about the company’s performance. Amazon’s founder Jeff Bezos talked about this in a 2018 interview:

> “**The stock is not the company, and the company is not the stock.** And so, as the stock price dropped from $113 to $6, the company was doing fine. We were continuing to grow our customers, our unit economics were continuing to improve, and so on. And so, I was always saying, we’re going to focus on the long term, we’re going to focus on our customers, we’re going to focus on the things we can control.”
>
> - Jeff Bezos

Remember that quote from Bezos: “**the stock is not the company, and the company is not the stock.**” Every individual stock is going to have a huge drawdown at some point (over -50% +, most over -80% +). Doesn’t matter how great the company is.

NVDA, MSFT, TSLA, AMZN, AAPL, META, AVGO, etc. have all had multiple -50% + drawdowns. Most of them have had -80% drawdowns before. No stock is immune to the Bear Cycle.

You could have been 100% correct about AMZN in 1999, but how many people held from $113 to $6? Unless you’re prepared to hold through -80% + drawdowns, “buy and hold” is not a strategy. The way I look at it, “buy and hold” is strictly a Bull Market strategy.

At Marlin Capital, “buy and hold” is not a strategy we employ indefinitely. As I said, it is strictly a Bull Market strategy. We look to exit everything when we believe the Bull Cycle has ended. We do not intend to hold positions through a Bear Cycle (even long-term positions).

**GLXY**

GLXY announced great earnings today, but the market reaction was not would you would hope for as a Bull.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4RMV!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F25e31f96-b605-489f-8fbb-5596422622d0_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4RMV!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F25e31f96-b605-489f-8fbb-5596422622d0_1396x653.png)

We tried a GLXY day 1 earnings trade today. GLXY is a name and theme (AI Data Centers) that I’m very bullish on fundamentally.

We traded GLXY for +16% adjusted in early October.

We re-entered GLXY today at $43.4 after a terrific earnings report, but the market environment is not quite the same as when we traded it in early October. AI Data Center stocks all got crushed today, which weighed on GLXY. There is potential risk that GLXY will catch down to its peers tomorrow. When the rest of the group is getting demolished, it’s hard to have any conviction on a post-earnings bull trend playing out for GLXY.

We took a -2.9% loss here towards the close. In this type of market (a weaker one), I’d rather keep all losses small and prioritize risk management. And if it’s not already clear, your position sizes should be much smaller than they were a month ago when everything was going straight up. Remember, [the feedback you’re getting on your trades should be a major factor for your position sizing](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/negative-feedback-loop?utm_source=publication-search). You should be trading your biggest when you’re on a winning streak, and your smallest when you’re on a losing streak.

**APLD**

The post-earnings action in APLD has been lackluster so far even after a tremendous earnings report.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vCey!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0b566549-fdd4-438d-a875-9d4c30df9444_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vCey!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0b566549-fdd4-438d-a875-9d4c30df9444_1396x653.png)

Perhaps the stock is exhausted after 2 straight big earnings gaps, but I am not seeing very many names emerge from Q3 earnings season in strong bull trends so far. The last 2 earnings periods saw bull moves everywhere. This time around (so far), gap ups are getting faded. It’s still early, but concerns are mounting for me.

Remember [after last quarter’s APLD earnings](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/big-tech-blowouts?utm_source=publication-search), when I said “I would not be surprised to see APLD go on a multi-month bull from here following this MAJOR catalyst?”

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PvWh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9242fc2c-27a3-4bf2-a046-39e45e7dfb56_1192x679.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PvWh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9242fc2c-27a3-4bf2-a046-39e45e7dfb56_1192x679.jpeg)

And it did, from $13 to $29+.

**That was an “Easy Dollar” environment.**

This is NOT. The character of the market (risk appetite, etc.) has noticeably changed. We flagged it last week, when we exited all our Active Portfolio positions and trimmed all of our SMID Cap 6 positions by 70%.

It is clear to me that we are no longer in a strong environment for new trades. It is a “risk off” environment, as I [talked about on Sunday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70).

When you’ve been in the game awhile, trading through multiple market cycles, you realize there are times where it is easy to make money and times where it’s hard to make money (and easy to lose money).

So, why waste mental capital trying to pick up pennies in a hard dollar environment?

Another easy dollar environment will come eventually. You should have no problem waiting until the odds are more in your favor. 3 weeks ago, we were in an easy dollar environment. We are NOT anymore. Would I like to be? Of course. But that’s not the reality right now. The Trump-China tweet has clearly changed the risk appetite of the market. When the character of the market flips back to “easy dollar”, I will be ready to strike.

Until then, smaller trades only for me (and fewer trades). It is hard to have high conviction on any trade in this type of environment. As I mentioned earlier, the earnings numbers have been good so far (same as the last 2 quarters), but the market reactions have simply not been.

Until that starts to change, I’m prioritizing risk management here.
