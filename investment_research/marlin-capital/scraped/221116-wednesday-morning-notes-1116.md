# Wednesday Morning Notes 11.16

Technical Analysis

The S&P 500 came within 50 pts of its 200 day moving average yesterday, but failed to close above the key $4000 level.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!W1vX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F44e288d5-ddd3-40e2-84fa-d07244d2529c_1073x822.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!W1vX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F44e288d5-ddd3-40e2-84fa-d07244d2529c_1073x822.jpeg)

Technically, the setup remains healthy at the moment. Key support levels to watch below include $3900 and the 21 and 60 EMA LEVELS ($3840-$3850). A downside move back below these key EMA support levels would signal a bearish reversal and a time to potentially think about adding shorts.

Until then, the setup remains healthy and the key resistance levels are within reach. For the S&P 500, the key resistance levels to watch ahead include the psychological $4000 level, $4040, and $4060-$4080.
