# Wild Times

Last week was a wild one for US stocks. The S&P 500 had its worst week since November, while the Nasdaq ‘only’ finished -2% after a huge bounce on Friday.

Let’s take a look at where we stand right now. 👇👇

The major market indices have been chopping around, stuck in the mud for ~3 months.

The boring Dow 30 stocks are breaking out. Consumer Staples, Homebuilders, Basic Materials, Industrials, and Energy stocks are leading the way.

At the same time, the exciting AI leaders of 2024-25 are breaking down.

It is a [split market.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/mixed-market-a68)

To demonstrate that point, 16% of S&P 500 stocks are currently trading at 52-week highs. On the other hand, >5% are sitting at 52-week lows.

This has only occurred 3 other times in history: July 1990, August 2015, and March 2025

All 3 times, the SPX saw a -10% + correction within the next ~2 months.

[![Image](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TW-c!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F868af59e-d1b3-4050-aaf4-f644b259cfa0_1456x1107.png "Image")](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TW-c!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F868af59e-d1b3-4050-aaf4-f644b259cfa0_1456x1107.png)

As I talked about in [“Mixed Market”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/mixed-market-a68), correlation among S&P 500 stocks has fallen sharply. In fact, the last time SPX correlations were this low was September 2000. The Dot Com Bubble peaked 6 months earlier, in March 2000.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!do1P!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9c2dab7f-80da-48f1-bc91-0ad222cc1059_2048x1325.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!do1P!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9c2dab7f-80da-48f1-bc91-0ad222cc1059_2048x1325.jpeg)

This all ties in to [my 2026 thesis to start the year.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-marlin-capital-2026-preview?utm_source=publication-search)

In the late innings of Bull Markets, it is typical to see stocks begin to drop out of the bull race (at their own pace). Individual stocks begin going their own ways, which is exactly what we have seen over the past 2+ months.

For me, this current **price action** is most similar to late 2021/early 2022.

I bolded “**price action**”, because the current Macro picture could not be any more different.

Back in 2022, the Fed was raising rates hyper-aggressively to combat 40+ year high inflation. Now, the Fed is cutting rates into a strong economy.

And historically, the S&P 500 does NOT peak when the Fed is cutting and there is no recession.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!90ch!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F55fe4fa4-0658-4469-965a-2e56d57d2d31_1215x847.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!90ch!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F55fe4fa4-0658-4469-965a-2e56d57d2d31_1215x847.jpeg)

This is what makes the current market setup so tricky. The Macro picture and overall price action are at odds with each other. But ultimately, as I talked about in [“Heading Into February”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/heading-into-february), I follow the price action above all else.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!RWkD!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5b9b20d6-7452-4d8c-8fd1-292fa3a840b1_725x382.png)](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/heading-into-february)

It all comes down to my Big Picture thesis about where we are in the market cycle and my big picture expectations for 2026.

[Coming into 2026](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-marlin-capital-2026-preview?utm_source=publication-search), it was always my plan to manage each position individually (so I’m sticking to that). This was my plan **because** of where I believe we are in the market cycle.

As I talked about in [my 2026 preview](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-marlin-capital-2026-preview?utm_source=publication-search), we are coming off 3 consecutive years of >16% gains for the SPX.

This has only happened TWICE before in history (dating back 100+ years).

Once was during the Dot Com Bubble, when the SPX posted 5 consecutive years of >16% gains from 1995-1999. The other was during the Covid Bubble (2019-2022), when the SPX peaked early in “year 4” (Jan 2022).

As I have highlighted all throughout this Bull cycle, the Dot Com Bubble and the Covid Bubble are the 2 best historical comparisons to our current market cycle.

**The Covid-QE Bubble**, and now

As I have written about in great detail, the Dot Com Bubble and Covid Bubble both peaked in a very similar manner. In fact, in both of our 2 historical comps, the topping process was largely the same.

Both times, [the most speculative/high beta leaders peaked first](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/heading-into-february).

These high beta names are critical to monitor because they are the **best** sign of excess liquidity during the bubble. During Bubbles, investors speculate wildly and the best “story names” go parabolic. So, when these leaders finally peak and start their Bear Phases, it tells us that market-wide risk appetite has also peaked.

From that point on, in both our historical comps (1999 & 2021), more and more stocks started to peak (at their own individual pace) over the next 12 months, until finally the SPX peaked.

My expectation for 2026 was similar price action, with more and more stocks peaking for the cycle and dropping out the bull race this year.

Why?

Because the most speculative leaders of this cycle [formed textbook climax top patterns back in October](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70?utm_source=publication-search) (2.5 months ago). Former leading spec names like OKLO, SMR, IONQ, RGTI, QBTS, JOBY, MP, OUST, SOUN, NVTS, GLXY, Bitcoin. etc. all peaked in October. They are now in Stage 4 downtrends.

And, as I pointed out above, this is late cycle Bull Market/early Bear Market behavior

So far in 2026, the market-wide price action has only served to confirm my big picture thesis. More leading names formed potential climax tops in January (including SLV, possibly SNDK, etc.). At the same time, many other names have showed signs of Stage 4 (Bear Phase) behavior.

This is why, last week, I made the decision to exit any positions that I believe to be showing any signs of topping/being in the Stage 4 bear phases. If I believe they have already peaked for the cycle and entered downtrends, why would I continue to hold them long?

Is it possible I was too hasty on closing out some of these names?

Yes, of course that is possible. No one has perfect market timing. No such thing exists.

But, that particularly worry sounds a lot like “what if it goes up more without me?” Aka FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out). Remember how William O’Neil views FOMO.

> “How do you lock in your gains when you find yourself with a big winner? In my experience it is often best to sell a big winner while it is still advancing and looks strong. **The common fear is “what if it goes up more without me?” That may happen, but remember, your goal is not to sell at the very top price. That’s almost impossible to do. Rather, you just want to participate in the majority of the move.”**
>
> - William O’Neil

If stocks like APLD, GRAL, SNDK, etc. go up more without me, then I will have made tremendous gains in those stocks. I participated in multibagger moves and made great returns on these names. In my view, it is very likely that I participated in the majority of these upside moves (and that is my main goal).

Now is not a time for FOMO. It is a time to play defense. My #1 priority is always risk management and protecting my gains.

**Active Trading Portfolio**

Some people are calling the current action a “market rotation” because Defensives, Consumer Staples, Basic Materials, etc. are breaking out.

But, in my view, you do not typically see former leaders/entire groups breaking down during market rotations. They may pull back heavily while others lead, but the action in these groups looks more like Stage 4 behavior to me. And this type of huge risk off liquidating (Stage 4) in former leaders does not happen during healthy market rotations. To me, this appears to be a more ominous picture than a simple “market rotation”. Eventually, it is typical to see the weakness spread throughout the market.

So, while I do see some potential longs on the menu (in defensive-type names), these would be low conviction plays at best due to the overall health of the market (which I assess as poor). Even in the leading names and sectors right now, I am not seeing any great long opportunities for early this week.

We could also have shorts on the menu this week.

My preferred method of shorting stocks is to enter on 3-day bounces to key moving averages (which should act as firm resistance). This was the setup for my [APP short trade on 4/2](https://substack.com/chat/408010/post/3d65a58d-fce3-4f48-a8ed-ff9cc56f669e)/, which was my [Top Trade Idea for Q2](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-trade-idea-for-q2-840?utm_source=publication-search) 2025. We made +21% in less than a week on our APP short trade. Multi-year Marlin Capital readers will also remember some of our big short trades from the 2022 Bear Market (HCP, MSTR, BILI, GTLB, etc.).

In the APP chart above, notice the big 3 day bounce into the declining 21 EMA (resistance). The ideal short entry is on Day 3, because that allows you to enter short at resistance (following a multi-day squeeze) and puts the risk/reward in your favor. In my view, you do NOT want to be entering short on big red candles because that puts you at much higher risk of being caught in the eventual dead cat bounce/short squeeze (like Friday). Instead, you want to be entering short **during** these dead cat bounces, so that you can be covering your shorts on big red candles/gap downs.

Your timing must be immaculate on shorts. It is not nearly as easy as trading to the long side in a low volatility, bullish environment. This is why I am very selective on short trades (and if I don’t get the right setup, I just don’t enter).

**Big Picture**

In my view, this is not a strong market environment overall, so I remain cautious here. Lots of warning signs have emerged for me over the last couple months.

**Most importantly, there are no fat pitches that I see in the market right now.**

For these key reasons, I am not looking to be aggressive heading into the week of 2/9. In my view, it is a time to play defense.

In the Stock Market, you can not always be playing offense. Not every single day. Not every single week. Not every single month. Not even every single quarter.

The market environment is constantly changing. In my view, it is best to wait until the market environment is favorable for your strategy. In the long run, this type of discipline will save you money and grow your confidence. This patience and discipline is something that I have learned from some of the trading GOATs, and has been reinforced by my personal experiences.

But, don’t just take it from me. Take it from some of the actual trading GOATs:

A lot of traders think that always being active/trading every day is the right move. I could NOT disagree more. This is a psychological hurdle for a lot of people. If you are a trader who is not constantly trading, it might feel like you are being unproductive with your time. Our brains equate more activity with more productivity, and this is true for most jobs. But, trading is one of the very few professions where doing nothing is often the highest value activity.

Real-world data backs this up. The most comprehensive studies on retail traders (Taiwan dataset, Barber, Odean, etc.) show that the heaviest traders (in terms of # of trades) earn 10-12% less per year than lighter traders. Some of the top performing prop traders I have worked with were in cash/hedged 60-80% of the time.

Once you have been in the game long enough, it becomes crystal clear that there are times when the market feels “easy” to make money (May-September 2025) and times when it feels extremely difficult (right now). As a trader, you do not score any extra points from the market Gods for playing on “All Madden Mode”, when you can just **wait** and play on “Rookie Mode” instead. The market does not reward you with any extra money for taking on a tougher challenge.

[As Buffett says](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/heading-into-february?utm_source=publication-search), wait for the fat pitch.
