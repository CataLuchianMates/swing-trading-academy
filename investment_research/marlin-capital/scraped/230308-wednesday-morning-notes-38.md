# Wednesday Morning Notes 3.8

Technical Update

Some big news out of Powell’s hawkish testimony yesterday as he began to prep the market for a 50 BP Hike in March. The market did not like this line from the Fed Chair - new data suggests “the ultimate level of rates is likely to be higher than previously anticipated".

Chief SGH Economist Tim Duy opined that Powell would not have entertained a 50 bps rate-rise in March “without intending to follow through with that outcome”.

“Only surprisingly weak data will prevent that outcome now.”

According to interest-rate futures tracked by CME Group, market-implied probabilities of a 50 bps increase at the March FOMC meeting before and after Powell's 10 am testimony went from around 30% to well over 50%.

The markets responded accordingly, with the 2Y yield breaking through 5% for the first time since 2007. The 2/10 yield curve inverted further and equities lost some key support.

For the S&P 500, this was a bearish follow through day technically after Monday’s shooting star reversal. The index broke and closed below the 21 and 60 EMAs.

Still, the index remains above the critical 200 SMA support level at $3940. It is not time to get overly bearish until we see a decisive break below the 200 day.

Powell continues his testimony today at 10 AM EST.

Key support levels below include $3970-$3975, $3940, $3925-$3930, and $3900.

Key resistance levels to watch above include $3995-$4000, $4030, $4050, $4075-$4080, and $4100.
