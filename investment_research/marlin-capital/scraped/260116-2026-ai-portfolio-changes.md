# 2026 AI Portfolio Changes

**Our [2026 AI Portfolio](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-picks-for-2026) is currently up +15% since inception <3 weeks ago**‼️

A year ago at this time, we made some adjustments to the 2025 AI Portfolio ([on 1/15](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/third-bounce?utm_source=publication-search)), and they worked out well for us. Often times, the first couple weeks of the year can be a microcosm for what the rest of the year might look like. This is because institutions are beginning to position themselves for 2026, so stocks that are in high demand early in the year often continue to outperform throughout the year (and vice versa).

With that said, we will be making some changes to the **2026 Top Picks Portfolio** at today’s close.

**Exiting NNE and QUBT shorts**

We gave it 2+ weeks, but I don’t see much reason to keep these short hedges on with Small Caps outperforming and breaking out. Big picture, my views have not changed, and I believe these stocks are likely to form lower highs after this bounce phase (ie, they have already peaked for the cycle). But, they are in their bounce phases right now and I can always re-enter these short hedges when needed. Right now, these short hedges are not needed, and I will be exiting.

**Exiting MDB and PATH**

Many have predicted AI Software to catch up for years now, but it’s just not happening. It was not my prediction for 2026 (as we have 12+ semi stocks in the portfolio but only 2 SaaS stocks), but I was still not expecting this level of continued underperformance from Software. The market has made its views clear to me. AI Semis »»» AI Software. Why should I argue with it?

**Exiting FSLR**

Solar energy is a beneficiary of the AI buildout, but solar stocks are not performing as you would hope to see. The market is more focused on battery plays and other alternative AI energy plays. Again, who am I to argue with the market? I will be replacing FSLR with a battery play (detailed below).

---

**Adding AEVA**

Like I said in [“Small Caps Soar”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/small-caps-soar), we may have been ~7 months early on AEVA. The new deal with NVDA is massive/company-altering. We bought AEVA around this price last June for the [SMID Cap 6](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-smid-caps-with-massive-upside-potential?utm_source=publication-search), so why would I not re-buy at the same price after a massive/company-altering deal? AEVA has become a 2026 hold for me.

**Adding AVAV and KTOS**

AI Security & Defense is a super hot industry right now (arguably the hottest on Wall Street) following President Trump’s announcement to increase military spending by +50% to $1.5 trillion annually. As I’ve detailed, I wouldn’t be buying these stocks here for a swing trade (too extended), but in this long-term portfolio where I am not looking to necessarily time things perfectly, I am comfortable buying here. Maybe they pull back here, maybe they don’t. But most importantly, I see these stocks finishing 2026 higher from here, and that makes them a buy for this portfolio.

**Adding CRWV**

Again, maybe not the best timing for this buy when looking at the shorter-picture, but I believe CRWV has bottomed and could see $150+ by the end of this year. Fundamentally, you could argue CRWV is cheap (when accounting for its hypergrowth) at $100. The bottom line is that I see CRWV likely finishing 2026 much higher than it is today, which makes it a buy for this portfolio.

**Adding EOSE**

I’m replacing FSLR with EOSE as the market is much more focused on battery plays than solar. EOSE was 1 of the last stocks not to make the cut for this portfolio, but that was a mistake (seeing as it’s up +33% YTD). I’m going to correct that mistake now by buying it here. In my view, EOSE can see $30+ this year. Strong fundamentals and a great technical setup.
