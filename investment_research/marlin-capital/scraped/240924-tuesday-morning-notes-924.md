# Tuesday Morning Notes 9.24

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Muted start to a quiet week of macro & micro outside of a handful of Fed speakers, Durable Goods (Thurs), and PCE (Fri).  The SPX finished +0.28% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gcwZ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcf2ea02a-f0a5-4ce1-adf8-93eefa2ccbe5_970x717.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gcwZ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcf2ea02a-f0a5-4ce1-adf8-93eefa2ccbe5_970x717.jpeg)

Total market volumes were -13% vs 20dma (on Friday 21B shares traded across all US exchanges, the most in 2024).  The SPX and NDX bounced around in positive territory but activity levels ran low and moves were mixed.

TSLA finished +4.5% and accounted for half of the NDX gain (deliveries + robotaxi events coming up).  Separately, China ADRs were bid (and are set for a continuation gap up this morning) following their stimulus announcement.  Per Goldman’s prime book, China equity exposure is currently hovering near 5 year lows.
