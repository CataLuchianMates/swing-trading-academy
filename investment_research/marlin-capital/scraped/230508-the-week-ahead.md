# The Week Ahead 

Technical Update

Last week’s action was a perfect example of the type of market we’ve seen dating back to last summer, but all wrapped up in 1 week.  Last week began with the fear of additional issues in the regional banking sector, igniting the possibility of a breakdown in the S&P 500.  The week ended with better than expected Q1 numbers from AAPL and other key names, as well as a better than expected jobs/unemployment report.  When all was said and done, at the end of the week, the S&P 500 finished relatively unchanged week over week.  More importantly, the rangebound market action in the indices continued while nothing significant changed on a technical basis.

For all the noise we’ve heard over the last several months, the S&P 500 remains right around the 50% retracement point of the move from the January 2022 highs to the October 2022 lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!R0Zh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5d675d5-5dbe-42b6-a191-bc5f5336e1ac_632x370.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!R0Zh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5d675d5-5dbe-42b6-a191-bc5f5336e1ac_632x370.jpeg)

With last week’s close, however, (as well as stronger than expected Q1 earnings from AAPL and falling unemployment) there is some optimism that we could break this range to the upside.

Market breadth continues to show a more unhealthy market under the surface with narrow leadership (mega cap tech accounting for a huge share of YTD index gains).  With that said, I am seeing more potential trade setups emerge in individual names (primarily large cap stocks).  I will be discussing these setups in more detail as the week unfolds.
