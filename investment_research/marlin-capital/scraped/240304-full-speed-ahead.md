# Full Speed Ahead

Another week that left little doubt about the global strength of this equity market run with the NDX logging a 2% weekly gain (its best week since mid-July), the S&P 500 notching its 15th record high this year, and major global indices now breaking out in unison (Japan & India new ATHs, Germany up 8 days in a row, etc.).

The Semis/Chip sector led last week’s rally yet again, with some eye popping moves last week in semis/hardware – DELL +37% on the week, NTAP +20%, AMD / WDC / MRVL +15%, STX / MU / CIEN +10%.  This shows me that the market is showing an increased willingness to explore new area of AI beneficiaries (outside of the obvious NVDA, MSFT, etc.).

The fundamental engines of this Tech rally remain intact (strong earnings revisions, continued margin expansion, and AI catalyst/upside).  Speaking of the AI catalyst, the first 310 trading days since the release of ChatGPT are remarkably similar to the market’s performance following the release of Netscape and AOL in the 90s.

To be sure, by Friday, the comparisons to 1999 started to pick up again investors exited the week feeling a bit marveled by the price action.  However, I continue to maintain that these types of comparisons are early at best (I covered this in more detail [last week](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bubble-talk)).

Stocks with EV/Sales ratios greater than 10x now account for 24% of US equity market cap vs 28% during 2021 and 35% during the late ‘90s Dot Com Bubble.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0t8k!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7a7a5f3b-5582-4128-95d8-c5040607b006_562x380.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0t8k!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7a7a5f3b-5582-4128-95d8-c5040607b006_562x380.jpeg)

The valuations of the 10 largest stocks today are well below the Dot Com Bubble peak: 10 largest stocks trade at a P/E of 29x now vs 47x at the peak of the Dot Com Bubble in 2000.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QNva!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcb413411-a147-43cd-aa09-ff4f40d34ff8_603x381.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QNva!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcb413411-a147-43cd-aa09-ff4f40d34ff8_603x381.jpeg)

As mentioned, the fundamental engines that have supported this rally remain in place (along with a more resilient economy than expected).  We are also seeing strong/increased participation in global markets (Japan, Germany, etc.) and in Bitcoin (one of the best indicators of where investor risk tolerance stands).

Moreover, we are starting to see Small Caps participate after a slow start to 2024.  The IWM just had its highest weekly close in 2 years (since March 2022).

Technically, Small Caps look great for continued upside from here.  We have had a number of false breakouts above $200 over the past couple years, but this is the strongest weekly close we have gotten above the key resistance level.

My strategy to play the breakout in Small Caps is to enter long TNA (3x small caps)  tomorrow on a break above $40.82, with a stop loss below $38.3 (-6.2% risk).
