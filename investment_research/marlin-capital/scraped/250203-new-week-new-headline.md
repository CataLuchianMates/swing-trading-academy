# New Week, New Headline

Already 1 month down in a year that has proven to be headline driven so far, with a new storyline to analyze each trading week. Last week was DeepSeek, 2 weeks ago was Stargate & Trump’s Inauguration, and this week is all about **Tariffs**.

Last week was one of the more intense weeks that I can remember in a long time. The NDX fell -1.4%, but we opened the week with -20%+ declines in a number of leading AI names (NVDA shed ~$600B in value on Monday alone). The -1.4% decline in the NDX masked much of the weakness (think NVDA, AVGO, VRT, ANET down -10-20% on the week). Looking back, the DeepSeek headline was clearly a “Sell First, Ask Questions Later” type of event, where most investors walked in Monday morning to see trillion dollar + market cap companies down -10%+ in the pre-market, leaving little time to react.

As the week progressed, we got a little more clarity and it seems we exited the week with investors generally agreeing that Monday’s DeepSeek bloodbath was a bit of an overreaction. There remains plenty of debate around what DeepSeek actually means (no change? accelerates AI adoption? impacts efficiency/CapEx?), but earnings from MSFT, META, and TSLA helped to calm fears that it would drastically alter these companies plans. I thought one of the best quotes of the week came from Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg during the company’s earnings call, discussing what impact DeepSeek could have on future CapEx:

> "It's probably too early to have a strong opinion on what this means for CapEx. We still believe heavy CapEx will be a long-term strategic advantage. Maybe we’ll learn otherwise at some point, but it’s way too early to make that call."
>
> Mark Zuckerberg

One lasting impact from the DeepSeek story, in my view, is that we are likely headed for a year of higher volatility in the AI theme as we navigate Year 3 of this AI capital cycle.

One of the other key themes we saw emerge in response to the DeepSeek storyline last week was Software > Semis outperformance. Monday registered as the biggest Software > Semis daily spread in the last 20+ years of data. With that said, Software exited the week in a bit of a tricky spot after mixed prints from NOW and MSFT. Both prints seemed to underscore a debate about how leading Software companies can transition to AI products/revenues, while continuing to execute on their core products (Azure core revs declerated ~4pts from last qtr amidst big upside in GenAI revs). Of course, we also got some solid earnings reports in TEAM and IBM, so there are reasons to be optimistic heading into a HUGE week of Software earnings (PLTR, BILL, FTNT, NET, and others set to report).

Now for the headline of this week: **Tariffs**

President Trump signed executive orders that impose tariffs on imports from Mexico, Canada, and China effective Feb 4th. The White House has conditioned removal of the tariffs on improvement in the immigration and fentanyl situations but has not provided specific goals.

Goldman Sachs estimates that a 25% tariff on imports from Canada and Mexico would raise core PCE prices by +0.7% and hit GDP by -0.4%. The potential rise on core PCE may just force the Fed’s hand (one that is already reluctant to cut rates any further).

Of course, anyone that remembers Trump’s first terms knows that a Trade War provides a ton of volatility for markets based off incoming headlines (tariffs raised, tariffs lowered, progress on trade, Trump tweets, etc.). The stock market in 2025 is more algo driven than ever, so expect incoming headlines to cause big jumps in the market before you can even think to react.

With this morning’s gap down, we are currently set to open below last Monday’s lows in the S&P 500. This is about ~3% off ATHs for the SPX. Like Kolanovic, I think we can very easily see a -5%+ decline in markets here after a big runup from the August correction. The market momentum over the last few months was predicated on a lack of negative newsflow/catalysts, but we’ve seemingly got a couple to contend with now. This is where stretched valuations can come more into play as momentum (potentially) turns negative.

Ultimately, I believe Trump’s tariffs are simply a negotiating tactic and the market being just ~3% off highs provides him with a buffer. A larger market correction may put more pressure on the administration, but we are not there yet. Needless to say, the market does not like Trade Wars and we saw a noticeable decline in Operating Margins during Trump’s first term trade war (this is where valuations come into play).

I come into Monday holding 1/4th of my CLS long after selling 75% on Friday at an average price of $129.35 (+11%). I typically like to hold a runner for a sustained move (25% in this case), but in hindsight, we are in a market where you really want to sell into strength. You should not be afraid to close out positions fully into strength in this market, which is how I will adjust in future trades.

My other long heading into this week is NFLX. I’ll be looking to take this position off for a tiny -1% loss this morning.

Bigger picture, I view a larger market correction due to tariffs as buyable as I do not expect these to be permanent (again, a negotiating tactic). In the meantime, we may see some short-term pain as investors re-think high valuations. At the very least, we are in a very headline driven market and one where I do not want to be very active in my swing trading portfolio (at least until the correction gets a bit deeper).
