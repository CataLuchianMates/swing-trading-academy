Entire sectors are breaking down, and weakness appears to be spreading.

This is very much a bipolar market setup right now.

As I have [written about extensively](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-marlin-capital-2026-preview), the later you get in a Bull Market (in the final innings specifically), the more stocks begin to follow their own paths.

This is **EXACTLY** what we are seeing 4.5 weeks into 2026.

Just to give you an idea how how mixed this market is: besides the Advance/Decline being around 50/50, we are seeing both new highs and new lows expand.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4HDo!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcc9f21c4-d3af-4821-bec7-ce4eb7fdeeb5_1219x576.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4HDo!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcc9f21c4-d3af-4821-bec7-ce4eb7fdeeb5_1219x576.jpeg)

Further, the Cboe 1-Month Implied Correlation Index (COR1M) just hit multi-year lows. The COR1M is a forward-looking indicator that measures how closely investors expect the top 50 stocks in the S&P 500 to move in tandem over the next month. Think of it as the “herd mentality” gauge for the stock market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tTsj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fda8c81a1-5d51-437d-9ec4-69e5ce2d3a4a_1214x578.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tTsj!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fda8c81a1-5d51-437d-9ec4-69e5ce2d3a4a_1214x578.jpeg)

In more bearish news, the S&P 500 just triggered its 2nd Hindenburg Omen in the last week.

The Hindenburg Omen is a technical indicator that signals big market weakness underneath the surface. It is triggered when a significant number of stocks hit both 52-week highs and 52-week lows simultaneously (indicating weak market breadth).

Historically, forward returns are extremely weak following these signals. The past 30 times this happened, the SPX fell 63% of the time over the next 6 months for an average return of -1.43%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-S4r!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5e197af-92a5-4772-aed3-a43142ea7f82_1496x1778.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-S4r!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5e197af-92a5-4772-aed3-a43142ea7f82_1496x1778.jpeg)

There are many bearish signs building in the market right now. My biggest concern, as I talked about on Sunday in [“Heading into February”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/heading-into-february), is that former speculative leaders like OKLO, IONQ, QUBT, NNE, ACHR, etc. appear to have entered their Stage 4 downtrends.

This is bad news because, historically, the speculatives are the first names to peak following a Bull cycle (6-14 months before the SPX).

Again, this is a very mixed market setup right now, which is expected during the latter stages of a Bull Market. This type of market action does not take us by surprise at Marlin Capital. In fact, this was [our exact thesis coming into 2026](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-marlin-capital-2026-preview), which is why I planned on managing each stock in the portfolio individually this year.

2025 was very much a market environment where you could simply “buy and hold” stocks.

I was not anticipating that to be the case in 2026. For me, “buy and hold” is strictly an early-mid cycle Bull Market strategy.

Here’s why 👇👇

Legendary trader Mark Minervini has a rule he likes to call the 50-80 rule. It says:

> “When a secular leader makes a major top, there’s a 50% chance it will drop 80% and an 80% chance it will drop 50%”
>
> - Mark Minervini

**I have found this 50-80 rule to be true in every cycle I’ve traded through.**

Whether or not the stock **ever** recovers back to its old highs is completely dependent on the company and their success. Think back to the Dot Com Bubble. Most of the leaders from that cycle **never** recovered. But some did. AMZN dropped -94% during the Dot Com Bust, but ended up becoming 1 of the largest companies in the world. Over the long-term, the stock price will follow the company’s performance. Amazon’s founder Jeff Bezos talked about this in a 2018 interview:

> “**The stock is not the company, and the company is not the stock.** And so, as the stock price dropped from $113 to $6, the company was doing fine. We were continuing to grow our customers, our unit economics were continuing to improve, and so on. And so, I was always saying, we’re going to focus on the long term, we’re going to focus on our customers, we’re going to focus on the things we can control.”
>
> - Jeff Bezos

Remember that quote from Bezos: “**the stock is not the company, and the company is not the stock.**” Every individual stock is going to have a huge drawdown at some point (over -50% +, most over -80% +). It does NOT matter how great the company is.

NVDA, MSFT, TSLA, AMZN, AAPL, META, AVGO, etc. have all had multiple -50% + drawdowns. Most of them have had -80% drawdowns before. No stock is immune to the Stage 4 Bear Cycle.

You could have been 100% correct about AMZN in 1999, but how many people held from $113 to $6? Unless you’re prepared to hold through -80% + drawdowns, “buy and hold” is not a strategy. The way I look at it, “buy and hold” is strictly an early-mid cycle Bull Market strategy.
