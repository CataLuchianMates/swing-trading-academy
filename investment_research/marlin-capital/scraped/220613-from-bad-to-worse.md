# From Bad to Worse

Inflation has still not peaked yet, and that means the worst case scenario is playing out for the economy and the stock market.

Friday was surely the worst case scenario playing out for the economy, the consumer, and the stock market.  Inflation unexpectedly accelerated to [fresh 40 year highs at 8.6%](https://www.wsj.com/articles/us-inflation-consumer-price-index-may-2022-11654810079), beating estimates that were calling for 8.3%.  This CPI print leaves no doubt that we are in a period of high and persistent inflation.  Inflation has not yet peaked here in June, and this simply means that the Federal Reserve must consider tightening even more aggressively than they already are.

This is a frightening thought because we are already in the fastest tightening year since 2008.

It is no coincidence, then, that equities are having their worst year since 2008. In real terms, US stocks are having their worst year since 1974.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OO7e!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F280e8ebb-2beb-4b8d-b55a-16a3b1fa5c8f_1141x611.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OO7e!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F280e8ebb-2beb-4b8d-b55a-16a3b1fa5c8f_1141x611.png)

To add fuel to the fire, we just began (what promises to be) the fastest quantitative tightening cycle ever.  As you can see in the diagram below, the few previous QT cycles we have seen paled in comparison to the Fed’s projected balance sheet reduction in ‘22-24.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DRyM!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1c08af49-7f46-41fa-b99d-46dab26cbfd6_1550x891.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DRyM!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1c08af49-7f46-41fa-b99d-46dab26cbfd6_1550x891.png)

Such aggressive tightening is sure to cause major problems for the economy and the stock market.  The further and further that inflation strays from the Fed’s 2% target rate, the more unrealistic a “soft landing” gets for the economy.  Of course, data shows that a “soft landing” for the economy is unrealistic for normal tightening cycles.  On average, 11 out of 14 Fed tightening cycles since WW2 have been followed by a recession within 2 years, implying a success rate of just 21%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HJdb!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F69381d40-8729-4b11-a7ee-ccec0226bad5_1183x757.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HJdb!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F69381d40-8729-4b11-a7ee-ccec0226bad5_1183x757.png)

It is not just recessions.  Federal Reserve tightening cycles have historically ended with major liquidation events.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HOAx!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F299129cb-2365-4aa6-86d2-89a3b8ab258e_697x484.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HOAx!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F299129cb-2365-4aa6-86d2-89a3b8ab258e_697x484.png)

Now, we are at the beginning of the fastest tightening cycle in recent memory, and the Fed must consider going even faster to control inflation.  This is another frightening thought because we are already starting to see cracks in the economy.

First off, it has been well documented that consumer sentiment is at its lowest level in 40 years. Now, it has reached its lowest level ever.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!N1Rm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8ba20225-fff0-4bf1-ae03-471195383fc6_732x347.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!N1Rm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8ba20225-fff0-4bf1-ae03-471195383fc6_732x347.png)

Data also shows that consumer credit growth has increased at its fastest pace in recent memory.  This is an indicator that the consumer is struggling with higher cost of living. As Soc Gen’s Albert Edwards points out, consumer credit growth typically booms as the economy is going into recession.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K9kf!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9982455b-31be-42c9-899a-66dc6cb7189a_490x220.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K9kf!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9982455b-31be-42c9-899a-66dc6cb7189a_490x220.png)

Secondly, we are starting to see cracks in the employment landscape.  Big tech companies including Meta, Tesla, Salesforce, Netflix, Coinbase, and Snap have recently announced hiring freezes or layoffs in the midst of cost-cutting pressure and rising inflation. Remember, we are only at the beginning of the tightening cycle, and this means things will get worse from here.

A great many companies and CEOs are beginning to warn about a deteriorating Macro environment, including Tesla’s Elon Musk and JP Morgan’s Jamie Dimon.  Elon Musk [said he had](https://www.cnn.com/2022/06/03/tech/elon-musk-tesla-jobs-economy/index.html) a “super bad feeling about the economy”, while Jamie Dimon [warned of an](https://www.cnbc.com/2022/06/01/jamie-dimon-says-brace-yourself-for-an-economic-hurricane-caused-by-the-fed-and-ukraine-war.html) “economic hurricane” coming. The list extends to CEOs/CFOs at Wells Fargo, Goldman Sachs, Target, Walmart, Snap, Restoration Hardware, Mariott, Best Buy, etc.

In general, CEO confidence has greatly deteriorated over the last year. The CEO Confidence Survey shows a drastic decline in confidence, and the indicator is now approaching levels only seen during the last 6 recessions.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2kXZ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F87e273a2-c860-40cf-96c1-6ac800baa5a3_1030x812.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2kXZ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F87e273a2-c860-40cf-96c1-6ac800baa5a3_1030x812.png)

So, what does this mean for stocks? As I discussed in detail in [“Nothing is Easy”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/nothing-is-easy?s=w), I do not believe a recession is currently priced into US stocks.  My target for the S&P 500 remains $2900-$3300 based on my analysis of historical “Bear Market Bottom” & Recession multiples.  This would take the S&P 500 to a 14x and below Forward P/E multiple, which is consistent with where recent Bear Markets/Recessions have bottomed.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0S7J!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9ab377e0-e612-4c80-97bd-733fb250b302_891x415.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0S7J!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9ab377e0-e612-4c80-97bd-733fb250b302_891x415.png)

This story ends with a recession and a major liquidation event.  I discussed my view on the recession part of the equation, but what about a major liquidation event?  There are some past examples in the BofA chart above, and the stars are aligning for another one to occur in 2022.

**Sentiment is so Bearish it’s…Bearish?**

It has been well documented how low investor sentiment currently is.  However, positioning has not yet caught down to meet sentiment.  This is a potentially dangerous combination.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GyKZ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0e1e19a1-1d03-4de7-b7df-8ccc3210c527_2048x1495.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GyKZ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0e1e19a1-1d03-4de7-b7df-8ccc3210c527_2048x1495.png)

As I discussed in [“Nothing is Easy”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/nothing-is-easy?s=w), there are many issues with using sentiment indicators to time bottoms.  Positioning has not yet met sentiment, so this is actually a very bearish setup.

Bank of America’s Michael Hartnett pointed out a similarly concerning signal in his most recent report.  In his report, he talks about how Bank of America’s sentiment indicator hit a 0 last week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!C64J!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3ee3f33d-f323-411c-a8d1-02f5b9b79a8d_1135x882.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!C64J!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3ee3f33d-f323-411c-a8d1-02f5b9b79a8d_1135x882.png)

Typically, such Bearish sentiment has been enough to turn markets.  When such bearish sentiment was not enough to turn markets, it has led to major liquidation events.  This includes the Dot Com Bust, Lehman Bust '08, US Debt Downgrade ’11, and China Devaluation ’15.

This Bear Market’s major liquidation event lies ahead.  As I have pointed out several times before, no major Bear Market has ended without at least 1 VIX spike above 45.

Summary Points

* We are in a period of high and persistent inflation. I expect the Fed to proceed with aggressive tightening until the economic data start to greatly deteriorate.  Monetary policy works with a lag, but the stock market looks 12+ months ahead.
* This is a very treacherous environment for equities.  I expect these conditions to persist until a recession is fully priced into markets.
* The Fed can only fix inflation via a recession (complete destruction of demand).
* The odds of a major liquidation event have greatly increased
