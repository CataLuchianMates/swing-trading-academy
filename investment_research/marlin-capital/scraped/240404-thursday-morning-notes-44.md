# Thursday Morning Notes 4.4

US Stocks bounced back and closed higher yesterday with the S&P 500 +0.11%, QQQ +0.22%, and IWM +0.66%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2t7t!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F55ae62e9-8f3c-4c64-b4cb-77c75dd1b138_1215x892.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2t7t!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F55ae62e9-8f3c-4c64-b4cb-77c75dd1b138_1215x892.jpeg)

Stocks liked the lighter ISM Services reading, which neutralized yields from pre-market highs.  Clearly, we are still in a bad economic data = good market regime.  Hotter than expected data over the last week sent yields higher and pressured equities (as the question of “will we see any rate cuts in 2024” emerged).

Despite reflationary price action across the board (Rates, Copper, Energy, Reflation Basket, etc.), Big Tech bounced back yesterday.  The QQQ closed just above its 21 EMA ($441) at $442.10 and just below its 8 EMA ($443).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!plD_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdfcb0150-2476-4a04-aadb-e1ec6cd983ff_977x717.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!plD_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdfcb0150-2476-4a04-aadb-e1ec6cd983ff_977x717.jpeg)

The QQQ hourly chart tells the picture of the type of market we have been in for the past month +.  Key support ($433-$434) has held on pullbacks, but we have been unable to break meaningfully higher through $445.   We remain in the same price congestion range that we entered in late February.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rSNU!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc42fb67c-c945-4ece-bfc2-4f093ff03490_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rSNU!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc42fb67c-c945-4ece-bfc2-4f093ff03490_1396x653.png)

COIN – Still holding long here.  No changes to position or stop (closing stop below $230).

MU – Big day yesterday as this stocks is emerging as a clear leader in the Semi space post-EPS.  We are approaching my first target of $130.   Bigger picture target = $150.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jSWH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff17a3762-ec3b-4f5d-bb12-19b1ea81dc08_1194x892.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jSWH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff17a3762-ec3b-4f5d-bb12-19b1ea81dc08_1194x892.jpeg)

We are set for a gap up this morning in the indices.  During this choppy/price congestion phase in the indices, gap up opens have been largely faded while gap downs have been bought.  I will not be looking to buy into today’s gap up as this has not been the right strategy for this market.
