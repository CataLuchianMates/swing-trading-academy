# The Generals Hold Court

The generals led the way higher to close out last week, with Mega Cap Tech accounting for the majority of Friday’s move up (+3.83%) following GOOGL and MSFT results.  META managed to close green on Friday after a -10% loss on Thursday following its Q1 results.

Hedge Funds net bought US equities for a 2nd straight week and at the fastest pace in ~5 months, driven by long buys and to a lesser extent short covers (7 to 1).  Aggregate US short flow decreased for the 1st time in 11 weeks.  GS Prime noted that Info Tech saw its largest notional net buying since December 2022.

We also saw strength in Chinese stocks, with China ADRs finishing as the 3rd best performing group (+3.18%) on Friday.  Hedge Funds have added net length in Chinese stocks since the start of the year, and the trend accelerated in April.

This morning, all eyes are on TSLA trading +12% in the pre-market after a successful Musk trip to China, where he reportedly cleared key hurdles to introduce its driver-assistance system to the world’s largest auto market.  China officials told Tesla that Beijing has tentatively approved the company’s plan to launch its “Full Self-Driving” or FSD, software feature in the country, people familiar with the matter say on Monday.  The US EV maker will deploy its autonomous services based on mapping and navigation functions provided by Chinese tech giant Baidu, the people said.  The partnership clears an important regulatory hurdle for Tesla to offer it driver assistance system there.

AAPL is also +2% this AM on an upgrade (Bernstein) + reports of new OpenAI features to be added in iOS 18 update.  The two companies have begun discussing terms of a possible agreement and how the OpenAI features would be integrated into Apple’s iOS 18.  The move marks a reopening of dialogue between the 2 companies.

This week, we get another large batch of Tech bellwether earnings with a focus on ON (today), AMZN (Tues), AMD (Tues), DASH (Wed), and AAPL (Thu).

While the Macro sledding remains treacherous (this next 2 week stretch – QRA, FOMC, NFPs, ISM, CPI – will be key in shaping the rates narrative into the summer months), the Micro story in Tech remains largely stable and consistent.  Exiting the 1st major week of Q1 results, the favorite names and subsectors have largely held court (excluding META).

One major highlight from last week was the snap back in Semis (NVDA +15% on the week), with the strength fueled by a step-function change in CapEx estimates across Mega Cap Tech companies (META, GOOGL, MSFT) as the AI race continues to heat up.  In fact, following updates this past week, MSFT, GOOGL, and META are set to spend a combined $140B+ on CapEX in 2024 (adding in AMZN & AAPL would take this # north of $200B).

There is not much reason to be bearish with Mega Caps performing the way they are thru Q1 results so far and the snap back in Semis on renewed market appetite for the AI theme.  The mini-correction into Q1 earnings season appears to have been a net positive for the stocks announcing as they enter their prints with a more favorable risk/reward (following the pull back).
