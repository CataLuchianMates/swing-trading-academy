# Thursday Morning Notes 3.13

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The market bounced yesterday (SPX +0.49%, NDX +1.13%, RTY unch) on cooler than expected CPI for February. Lower inflation was the clear catalyst for the oversold bounce, but is by no means a game changer for this market or the Macro picture in general. We are seeing prices come way down across the board and the US Truflation Index currently has its lowest reading in 4+ years (1.35%).

This is obviously good news (albeit expected), but the market is now more focused on economic growth. There are basically 2 Macro scenarios that could play out here:

Macro Scenario 1: Inflation is cooling alongside slowing/negative growth (recession), the Fed will start cutting rates

Macro Scenario 2: Growth is slowing but remaining positive (no recession), inflation moving lower, the Fed will stay on pause/start to cut rates slowly

As I talked about yesterday, the recession scenario would be much worse for stocks, as the typical recessionary market correction lasts 6+ months. JPM now assigns a 40% probability of recession in 2025. The Bond Market is not pricing in a recession right now, in my view. Recessionary cuts are more in the region of +200bps in a year vs 71bps at the time of this writing. This means if we do start to see some recessionary data points, the equity market very likely has more downside to come.

The price action in the stock market remains very weak despite yesterday’s bounce. We have already retraced a good portion of yesterday’s bounce this morning. I was expecting something more along the lines of a multi-day bounce just based off the extreme oversold conditions and the positive CPI catalyst (albeit, this is not a major catalyst as I have discussed, but still the last bit of good news we have seen in awhile). It is still possible we get that multi-day bounce here, but (right now) this looks more like bear flagging type action.

Underneath the hood, the NVDA [death cross](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/do-or-die-time) is inevitable now. Same with GOOGL. MSFT is already there. TSLA is now headed for a [death cross](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/do-or-die-time) as well. AAPL is breaking down now and looks headed for a death cross. That’s 5/7 Mag 7 names. META is clearly the strongest while AMZN is just below the 200 day.

None of the Mag 7 names had a bearish death cross since the Bull Market began in October 2022. Now, 5/7 are headed that way. The Tech Sector (XLK) is headed that way. The QQQ may be headed that way. Again, this is much more damage from a technical standpoint than you want to see during your standard market correction (like last August).
