# Wednesday Morning Notes 9.11

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The SPX rallied +0.45% yesterday in an uninspiring, low volume tape.

Tech led the way with the NDX finishing +0.9% on the day.  Stocks overall shrugged off some early weakness following negative bank updates at the Barclays conference (specifically from JPM and ALLY).

August CPI data released this morning showed inflation falling to 2.5% in August, in line with expectations for 2.5%.

Core CPI was unchanged at 3.2%, in line with expectations.  This marks the lowest annual inflation rate since March 2021.

Following today’s inflation data, 29bps of rate cuts are priced in for next week’s Fed meeting.  Fed funds futures are calling for a 25bp cut rather than a 50bp cut next week.

Stock futures are flattish to start the day today following CPI.  Technically, the setup remains questionable here on larger timeframes (daily/weekly).  I am not looking to add any long exposure just yet.

SN – Still holding long half my original SN long with a stop below $93.  My next target here is $100.  I will let the rest of this position play out.
