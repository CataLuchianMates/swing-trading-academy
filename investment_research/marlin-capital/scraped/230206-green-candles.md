# Green Candles

The Fed:

[Wednesday’s Fed meeting](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2023-02-01/fed-slows-rate-hikes-signals-further-increases-are-coming) went as well as it possibly could have for the market.  The Federal Reserve did not appear anxious about inflation (“the disinflation process has begun”).  In the press conference, Jerome Powell did not push back against implied rate cuts in H2 2023.  Moreover, the Fed Chair chose to offer zero push back against the recent easing of financial conditions we have seen, igniting a further squeeze in equities.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tkIB!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4b38eadb-7263-4130-9905-c6b1f7fb6c56_1267x1253.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tkIB!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4b38eadb-7263-4130-9905-c6b1f7fb6c56_1267x1253.png)

It is difficult to interpret the Fed’s message as anything but dovish coming out of this meeting.  The jobs data that came out [on Friday](https://www.cnbc.com/2023/02/03/jobs-report-january-2023-.html) showed unreal strength in the labor market, lending some credibility to the “Immaculate Disinflation/Soft Landing” scenario currently being priced by markets.

With the US and global growth story being significantly “less bad” than expected currently, investors (specifically institutional investors) have been forced to cover shorts and add longs/deploy cash, as the FCI regime has now been flipped on its head from “tighter” to “easier”.

Equity positioning is the clearest example of how funds were caught on the wrong side of this trade.  According to GS Prime Desk, Thursday’s (2/2) short covering was the largest since November 2015 (exceeding even the Jan 2021 meme frenzy) and ranks in the 99.8th percentile vs the past 10 years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YeAh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4ec76ed0-7d33-40ba-a7c9-9508db9d6345_442x369.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YeAh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4ec76ed0-7d33-40ba-a7c9-9508db9d6345_442x369.png)

Meanwhile, with the FCI regime being flipped on its head from “tighter” to “easier”, we are seeing a complete reverse of last year’s trade (where the most speculative stocks underwent massive multiple compression amid the FCI tightening regime of higher real rates).  So far this year, we are seeing the complete opposite, as financial conditions have eased and real rates have retreated.

* High retail sentiment +51% YTD versus -59% in 2022
* Most liquid short basket +37% versus -52% in 2022
* High beta 12M losers +34% versus -54% in 2022
* Non-profitable tech +33% versus -62% in 2022

In other words, last year’s biggest losers have been this year’s biggest winners (so far).

**Technicals**

Coming out of a massive news-filled week, the S&P 500 remains in very good technical condition.

On the weekly chart, the S&P 500 is firmly above both key moving averages (40 and 200) and has broken out above the $4100 resistance level.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Vglq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F68b00353-527c-4ddf-96fd-de108b643498_1031x651.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Vglq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F68b00353-527c-4ddf-96fd-de108b643498_1031x651.png)

On the daily chart, the S&P 500 has triggered a traditional bullish “golden cross”.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SRdu!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F04d54c82-a4a0-4eea-8d74-9ccebac08774_1033x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SRdu!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F04d54c82-a4a0-4eea-8d74-9ccebac08774_1033x653.png)

A traditional golden cross occurs when the 50-day moving average (MA) moves above the 200-day MA.  As the name suggests, this is a bullish technical signal.  This was the S&P 500’s 49th golden cross since 1928.  SPX returns are best 30, 65, and 195 days after the signal, with the index up 75% of the time on stronger than average returns.  The 260 day returns are slightly better than average, with an average return of 9.7% (10.7% median) versus the average 260-day SPX return of 7.8% (9.1% median).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!viSA!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa9befc24-032a-4e55-8ca5-563eaf592a2e_915x336.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!viSA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa9befc24-032a-4e55-8ca5-563eaf592a2e_915x336.png)

The traditional golden cross is even more bullish when the 200-day MA is declining, as it is currently (based on its 5-day slope).  Thursday marks the 20th time since 1928 that a golden cross has occurred with a declining 200-day MA.  The SPX boasts average 260 day returns of 14.1% under this scenario.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!L75p!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8fac802e-3241-4479-bcad-d8cca3a362cc_907x324.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!L75p!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8fac802e-3241-4479-bcad-d8cca3a362cc_907x324.png)

The market internals have also markedly improved during this rally.  This is evidenced by stronger market breadth, which is shown by the number of stocks above their 200-day MAs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ngIv!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe18c5101-516d-4cc7-896a-829aecb8c4fa_189x257.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ngIv!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe18c5101-516d-4cc7-896a-829aecb8c4fa_189x257.png)

As you can see, breadth has improved to levels not seen since early 2022 (when the indices topped).

Moreover, NYSE Net Highs are outpacing New Lows at the highest rate since November 2021 (before the indices topped).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8rIL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F41ccaecc-681d-48a1-9a0b-d2f682636247_188x248.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8rIL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F41ccaecc-681d-48a1-9a0b-d2f682636247_188x248.png)

As you can see, the market did not exemplify this much strength during any bear rally in 2022.

As I have noted in the past, it is a market of stocks, and one must look at the internals to gauge the overall health of the market.  The internals are telling us that the market is currently healthier and stronger than it was at any point during 2022.

Many of the all time greatest stock traders/speculators have stressed the importance of disciplined technical analysis. On Wall Street, the saying goes, “The trend is your friend”.

With that said, there are a couple different types of long setups in individual stocks that I am currently involved with/will consider getting involved with.
