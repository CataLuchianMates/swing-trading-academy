# Election Week

The S&P 500 declined -1.4% last week, finishing in the red for the 2nd consecutive week as investors continue to de-risk ahead of tomorrow’s US election.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qhdk!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbb098daa-8431-4f94-80f2-f1cde47940fb_1190x885.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qhdk!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbb098daa-8431-4f94-80f2-f1cde47940fb_1190x885.jpeg)

Harris saw an increase in polling numbers last week and the race very much remains a toss-up despite betting markets still showing more favorable Trump odds.  As I have written about over the past month, getting past the election is typically a risk-on event for markets.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uNu4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb9c5e7b-2412-4264-8b83-2861a68bd838_1284x739.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uNu4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb9c5e7b-2412-4264-8b83-2861a68bd838_1284x739.jpeg)

The reduction in uncertainty following the election has almost always been a positive for US equities, particularly when the race is as tight as this one.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Pdxs!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F89207d49-203c-4b5b-bdf0-bfd7b1106e73_893x562.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Pdxs!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F89207d49-203c-4b5b-bdf0-bfd7b1106e73_893x562.jpeg)

Given that positioning is light into tomorrow’s election, a likely reaction in equities would be a reduction of hedges/re-risking into the strong seasonality of the end of the year.  After the 2016 and 2020 elections, Financials and Tech were the 2 sectors that had the largest outperformance.  With the recent earnings prints from Tech and current Macro environment, I see that pattern continuing in 2024.

Speaking of earnings, 70% of the S&P 500 has reported so far.  68% of SPX companies have reported a positive EPS surprise and 57% of SPX companies have reported a positive revenue surprise.  On a YoY basis, the growth rates stand at +5.1% YoY for EPS and +5.2% YoY on revenue.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Fj0E!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8f3695c6-e6b8-4156-9193-e7fef0d7d842_1059x817.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Fj0E!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8f3695c6-e6b8-4156-9193-e7fef0d7d842_1059x817.jpeg)

We also have the FOMC decision on interest rates coming on Thursday, which seems anti-climactic given tomorrow’s election.  The Fed is on track to deliver a 25bps cut this week (~99% probability) and if the data stays in the same range, we are likely to see another 25bps cut in December.  Labor markets remain more important than inflation for the Fed and the weakness from Friday’s NFP did not spill over into a higher Unemployment Rate.

Earnings wise, we are through the Mega Cap Tech portion of the season (with the exception of NVDA), but there are still many growth names set to report this week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TDlW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F101f74d1-919d-479a-84b0-6b3dcdb458ee_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TDlW!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F101f74d1-919d-479a-84b0-6b3dcdb458ee_1920x1080.jpeg)

Earnings gappers will remain my focus for new trades this week.

I am heading into tomorrow’s election currently holding 1 partial long position in LRN (2/3rds) and 2 full positions in RBLX and TWLO.  On RBLX and TWLO, I will be looking to reduce these positions today as a way to reduce some risk into Election Day.  I am not looking to place any new long trades today, only risk reducing trades (RBLX and TWLO).
