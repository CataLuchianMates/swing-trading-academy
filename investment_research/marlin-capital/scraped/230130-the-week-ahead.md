# The Week Ahead

Last week, we saw stocks continue to explode higher ahead of the Fed this week.  A perfect storm of “soft landing” sentiment and big money positioning (offsides) has sparked this rally.

This remains an unloved rally, as the professional investor community has not chased.  Even the 2022 Bear Rallies saw a bigger spike in Hedge Fund Net L/S Positioning.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wAf3!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3d3d5e9a-8fdf-4d22-89ae-268a27f9a091_1024x629.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wAf3!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3d3d5e9a-8fdf-4d22-89ae-268a27f9a091_1024x629.jpeg)

The biggest losers of 2022 have flipped the script here in January ‘23.  So far in 2023, last year’s biggest losers are this year’s biggest gainers.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WHjK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd8a84e12-f3b0-4755-9ed7-cd14285bf4fe_604x1024.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WHjK!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd8a84e12-f3b0-4755-9ed7-cd14285bf4fe_604x1024.jpeg)

So far, earnings season (25% of SPX companies have reported) has also helped fuel the market rally.  The theme of this earnings season so far is “better than feared”.  Moreover, companies who miss on EPS have outperformed (the day after) on the largest pace on record.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!irha!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe44cea2c-d5f6-4205-b3a5-e5d2746a7313_1284x906.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!irha!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe44cea2c-d5f6-4205-b3a5-e5d2746a7313_1284x906.jpeg)

We have a big week of earnings ahead, particularly Thursday, where we will see AAPL, AMZN, and GOOGL each report.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7NSG!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F67931e56-1dbb-4212-94ac-20d21842cc63_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7NSG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F67931e56-1dbb-4212-94ac-20d21842cc63_1920x1080.jpeg)

The major news of the week is the FOMC meeting and decision on interest rates, which will be announced Wednesday.  The market is widely anticipating a 25 bp hike (96% probability) and hawkish tone from Powell, as financial conditions have eased considerably in recent weeks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TCgY!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd10be91b-43e2-493d-981f-da368291bb10_1024x768.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TCgY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd10be91b-43e2-493d-981f-da368291bb10_1024x768.jpeg)

This is problematic for the Fed, but it will be a tough line for Powell to walk.  It will be difficult to sound overly hawkish while *only* raising 25 bps.

Technically, this rally is different so far (both in the indices and many individual stocks).

On the weekly time frame, both the S&P 500 and QQQ closed above the 40 week.  For the QQQ, it is the first time this has happened during the Bear Market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qRws!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F851940a0-5027-404d-a2e9-c81eb073269b_956x717.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qRws!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F851940a0-5027-404d-a2e9-c81eb073269b_956x717.jpeg)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oCHU!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3205a51e-fda5-48cd-83db-60c0b34fa09c_1203x898.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oCHU!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3205a51e-fda5-48cd-83db-60c0b34fa09c_1203x898.jpeg)

On the daily time frame, the S&P 500 hit my $4100 target on Friday (Dec and Nov highs) before pulling back a bit before the close.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sHAP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F62e3ae40-ac60-4c22-b89e-85963339d4f4_1206x904.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sHAP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F62e3ae40-ac60-4c22-b89e-85963339d4f4_1206x904.jpeg)

This remains a bullish technical setup.  Notice the moving averages starting to curl up.  If the SPX can stay supported, we are not far away from the golden cross (21 EMA > 60 EMA > 200 SMA).  Once again, we have not seen such a cross in the SPX (or QQQ) during this Bear Market.

A pullback would be the healthiest thing here to shake off some of the short-term overbought conditions.  Key support levels to watch below include $4050, $4020-$4030, and $4000.  As long as we hold the key moving averages below on any pullback ($3963, $3957, and $3924), this setup still leans bullish technically.

Resistance wise, $4100 is the key level to watch above.  If we can break above this level, the market can start to make a run towards August highs ($4300).  This will of course also be dependent on the news flow this week.

NVDA and MELI are 2 leaders of this rally so far.  Both sported a [bullish inverse head and shoulders pattern that triggered last week](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-week-ahead-595).  Both stocks have also seen bullish golden crosses on the daily.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Gqsx!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fecb06511-c9ac-4a73-8bde-af6d085eff6f_1197x901.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Gqsx!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fecb06511-c9ac-4a73-8bde-af6d085eff6f_1197x901.jpeg)

In the short term, both stocks look a bit extended here after a big run last week.  For NVDA, my stop loss is now $185.  For MELI, my stop loss is $1100.  I am also holding CLH with a stop at $124.

As mentioned, a pullback into Wednesday’s Fed meeting (where key support levels hold) is my preferred setup this week.  This is a critical week in terms of news flow and technicals, so expect plenty of volatility.  I will be publishing a post-Fed meeting report towards the end of the week.
