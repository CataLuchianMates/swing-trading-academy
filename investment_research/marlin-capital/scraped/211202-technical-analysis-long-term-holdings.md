# Technical Analysis: Long Term Holdings

There is a lot of technical damage to ARKK and many growth stocks.

First, here is an interesting (and concerning) chart that has surfaced comparing ARKK’s parabolic post-Covid run to the Nasdaq Dot Com bubble.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gTLq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F04f21edb-3325-4c99-b50c-7c8a73a0d159_2048x1067.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gTLq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F04f21edb-3325-4c99-b50c-7c8a73a0d159_2048x1067.jpeg)

The similarities in the charts are eerily similar here.  I do believe that ARKK will have another leg lower here (potentially dramatic) as the charts suggest, but I’m not sure that weakness will carry over into 2023.  The market is much more efficient now than in the early 2000’s, meaning moves happen faster and over a shorter time frame in today’s market.

Now, let’s take a look at ARKK’s daily and weekly charts closer, starting with the daily below:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SeJO!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcfa090a1-0669-4e2c-a604-872c94771fe5_1015x765.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SeJO!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcfa090a1-0669-4e2c-a604-872c94771fe5_1015x765.png)

Pretty simple chart here.  ARKK is clearly in a downtrend.  It has not closed above its 8 EMA since November 8th, nearly a month ago.

If I am wrong about ARKK having another leg down, and the ETF double bottoms around this support area, I would first need to see ARKK close above its 8 EMA.  Until then, I will be treating every upwards move in the ETF as an oversold bounce.

As you can see, ARKK’s trend is very clearly defined with the 8 EMA.  There is no reason for me to go against it.

Now, let’s take a look at the weekly chart:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!olvi!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9573a811-9be2-48b5-b6f7-772710fca570_1589x957.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!olvi!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9573a811-9be2-48b5-b6f7-772710fca570_1589x957.png)

In technical analysis, this is a classic “h sell”.  Notice the pattern above.  The first price target on this move, from a technician’s standpoint, would be the $78 level.  A second price target, which I don’t believe will ultimately play out, would be $44.  Either way, the “h sell” setup suggests that the decline will accelerate in the coming weeks.

Things obviously look bad for ARKK, but what about my Long Term picks?  I go into more detail below with potential buy points.  Keep in mind I am expecting 15-20% of potential downside from today’s close in ARKK, so I am going to be extremely patient with any long term buys.
