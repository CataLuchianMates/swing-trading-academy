# The Key Story

The S&P 500 sold off -0.85% yesterday as long end rates continued their ascent higher. It was a volatile and whippy/choppy session and came on the highest volume in a month.  The VIX rose above 21, its highest level in 7 months. This ends a streak of 126 days below 20 for the VIX.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MDH2!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F04270201-7df8-48aa-a1c2-a48dd12986ca_1201x671.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MDH2!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F04270201-7df8-48aa-a1c2-a48dd12986ca_1201x671.jpeg)

Fed Chair Powell spoke yesterday, adding to the whippy price action in the market.  The key takeaway from Powell’s speech yesterday is that he is cautiously optimistic that policy is working and that the economy can sustain the higher rates.

Overall, while leaving the door open because of new and upcoming data, the message from Powell was fairly dovish on near term rates.  The hawkish caveats are about the need to hold rates higher for longer because it looks like the economy can sustain it just fine right now.

For the S&P 500, the technical setup is currently bearish. We have a potential h-sell setup forming on the index.  Key support is at $4200-$4230.   A decisive break below $4200 and the 200-day moving average would be very bearish and likely lead to a test of March lows ($3800-$4000), in my opinion.

Meanwhile, the Equal Weight S&P 500 (RSP) is now red for the year and also in the process of breaking down in a major way.  March lows at $136-$137 is the key support level to watch here. This is another potential h-sell setup.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lnys!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0170c7e5-a136-4e71-a81c-41e1d3b1f68e_984x726.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lnys!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0170c7e5-a136-4e71-a81c-41e1d3b1f68e_984x726.jpeg)

 The struggles of the Equal Weight S&P 500 (and small caps) show that without Mega Cap Tech, the market would be nearing its lows of 2023 (and in the red for 2023).  The Magnificent 7 (as coined by BofA) – Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Nvidia, Tesla, and Meta – have been carrying the rest of the market all year.

This is a problem because, as we saw with TSLA yesterday (-10% post-Q3 report), many of these Mega Cap stocks are priced near perfection heading into Q3 earnings.  In fact, these 7 stocks are mostly responsible for the S&P 500 PE currently being above median historical valuations.  Excluding the Magnificent 7, the other 493 stocks have a PE Ratio of 16x, **below** thelong term median of 16x.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LNSs!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F80c1f8e5-af39-4a97-9ad2-f54d95583288_1209x768.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LNSs!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F80c1f8e5-af39-4a97-9ad2-f54d95583288_1209x768.jpeg)

This is also a problem because recent history shows that the 2022-23 market rally off lows has not been broad enough to compare with previous new Bull Markets.  This would be the weakest performance in Year 1 of a Bull Market in 40+ years.  As you can see below, the other 6 instances (of new Bull Markets) were accompanied by a broad rally in Year 1, with the Equal Weight S&P 500 and Small Caps outperforming the SPX in all 6 cases.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lW0N!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5a78e0de-faa9-432e-afdb-a4855779a731_809x428.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lW0N!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5a78e0de-faa9-432e-afdb-a4855779a731_809x428.png)

The key story affecting markets right now is the recent rise in the 10Y Treasury yield.  The 10Y currently sits at 4.99% after rising to 5.0% in yesterday’s session.

The recent rise in the 10Y has been a major headwind for stocks.  As you can see below, $TNX broke out of its YTD range (above $41) in early August.  This is also the exact time when the S&P 500 and QQQ peaked for the year and began their downtrends.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1jz-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc7d8b7bc-b931-46e1-97f6-a84d0c9913ab_973x723.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1jz-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc7d8b7bc-b931-46e1-97f6-a84d0c9913ab_973x723.jpeg)

The question to consider now is: how much higher will 10Y yields go? A move above 5.0% would be a bearish sign for stocks (which are already nearing key support levels).

In my research, I have found that the 10Y Treasury yield tends to hit its cycle peak in the same region as the Federal Funds Rate peak.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pQX6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe4ee4c7b-178c-4072-acc9-2e0e34de6245_1132x477.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pQX6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe4ee4c7b-178c-4072-acc9-2e0e34de6245_1132x477.png)

This would suggest further potential upside in the 10Y to at least 5.25%.  If this happens, I would expect to see stocks continue their recent downtrends towards YTD lows (those that aren’t already there – IWM, RSP, etc.).
