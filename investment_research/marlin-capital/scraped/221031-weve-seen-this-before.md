# We've Seen This Before

**The Little Picture**

Another bear rally on the hopes of a Fed “pivot”.

In response to the newfound market narrative that the Fed wants to slow down the pace of rate hikes (following the [WSJ report](https://www.wsj.com/articles/fed-set-to-raise-rates-by-0-75-point-and-debate-size-of-future-hikes-11666356757)), we have seen a significant easing of financial conditions.  Consequently, financial conditions have relaxed at one of the fastest 5-day paces this century.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!CNdp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fba1620b4-04a9-412c-80f4-5514b12a7a1f_513x367.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!CNdp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fba1620b4-04a9-412c-80f4-5514b12a7a1f_513x367.png)

This has allowed risk assets to bounce, similar to the rally we saw in August on easing financial conditions (which [I wrote about here](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/late-summer)).  Recall that Fed Chair Powell ultimately put an end to that rally with his [Jackson Hole speech](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/speech/powell20220826a.htm).

As demonstrated in the chart above, we have had multiple equity rallies this year on the back of FCI easing.  Each time this has occurred, the Fed has pulled the strings back even tighter.  They will need to continue to do so this time, because inflation is at 8%, and nowhere near their 2% target range.

Former Fed President Bill Dudley echoed this sentiment in an interview last week:

> “If you want to crush inflation, you can’t back down all that much right now, and you can’t be a friend to the stock market…I would expect the Fed would ultimately beat back any major easing of FCI.”

And right on queue – Nick Timiraos has started to backtrack on Twitter.

> “The bottom line: while the Fed isn’t data-point dependent and the decision for next week of 75 basis points seems unlikely to change, another uncomfortably high ECI reading might argue for a somewhat higher terminal rate and could muddy the debate over slowing the pace in Dec.” - @NickTimiraos

In my view, the narrative of a Fed pivot is once again premature.  A move from 75 bps to 50 bps (if it even happens in December) would be a step down, not a pivot.  Recall that the [market historically bottoms when the Fed actually pivots](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-market-bottom-checklist) (starts cutting rates), not when the Fed is “only” raising by 50 bps.

The 2009-2021 reflex of “Fed pivot = stocks moon” is still kicking in, but the Fed has not pivoted, and they will need to make that clear to the market in order to achieve their goal (less we be faced with a bigger problem for the economy and the stock market – a sustained period of hyperinfation).  Financial conditions must remain unsupportive of a sustained market rally for the Fed to tame inflation. Each previous equity rally on FCI easing has failed because the Fed needed it to.

In my view, the market is setting up for another rug pull on a bout of Fed induced FCI tightening.  It could come as early as this Wednesday’s Fed meeting, where we will learn more about the Fed’s plans for December and beyond.

**The Big Picture**

Mega Cap Tech earnings were not good.

It is rare new Bull Markets start with the largest companies in the world performing so poorly and their stocks gapping down.  Prior to Friday’s bounce, mega cap tech earnings had their worst 2 day performance on record.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!RHk6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fabc925e1-1cdc-4dba-bc32-7b7f63b324ba_810x443.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!RHk6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fabc925e1-1cdc-4dba-bc32-7b7f63b324ba_810x443.png)

Apple [management said](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2022/10/apple-reports-fourth-quarter-results/) they expect Q1 revenue growth to decelerate YoY amid slowing demand for the new iPhone.  Microsoft [guided lower](https://www.cnbc.com/2022/10/26/microsoft-stock-sinks-more-than-6percent-on-weak-guidance.html) than consensus was expecting.  Perhaps most concerning was the YoY drops in operating margin each company saw this quarter:

AAPL: -0.9%

AMZN: -2.4%

GOOGL: -7.5%

META: -14.0%

MSFT: -1.7%

This is all occurring in a quarter where nominal US GDP ran at +11%.  What do these numbers look like in a slowdown?  Big tech companies cannot avoid such a Macro slowdown, as many investors are learning now.

Earnings will only get worse from here, yet analysts do NOT seem to be catching on.  In fact, analysts continue to project a solid year of 7% earnings growth in 2023.  The average recession sees a -15-20% decline in EPS instead, though Goldman Sachs reasons it may be closer to -10% in a period with elevated inflation.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EB8-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F740c1047-e05a-4419-aa36-6450eeed0acb_630x323.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EB8-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F740c1047-e05a-4419-aa36-6450eeed0acb_630x323.png)

As long as consensus is still calling for a solid year of earnings growth and profit margin expansion in 2023 (and 2024 & ’25), we can be assured there is no recession priced into the market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Nj6w!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0143c1e-2be7-4bf1-a9ee-3b2b001fb508_360x326.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Nj6w!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0143c1e-2be7-4bf1-a9ee-3b2b001fb508_360x326.png)

Big Tech valuations are already sitting at a premium multiple as it is (assuming there no drop in earnings). The 5 largest stocks in the index - AAPL, MSFT, AMZN, TSLA, and GOOGL - are trading at a Fwd P/E of 23x versus 15x for the other 495 stocks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!v5FE!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0f1c7d0e-e603-4c78-aa3f-3375168786ea_755x495.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!v5FE!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0f1c7d0e-e603-4c78-aa3f-3375168786ea_755x495.png)

Big Picture - Mega Cap Tech stocks are vulnerable to:

(1) further multiple compression on slowing growth; and

(2) future earnings cuts.

These earnings cuts would immediately increase the (already premium) multiple by nature, so these stocks are likely even more expensive than they look on the surface.

Weakness in big tech should ultimately drag the indices to new lows.  My base case target for the S&P 500 bottom remains the $2900-$3300 region.  I believe this will provide a great opportunity to deploy long term cash in retirement accounts for those who can afford to think long term.

However, I believe the risks of a more severe recession are also rising, and this could potentially open the door for greater declines in the market.  Jamie Dimon has coined this scenario as “[something worse than a recession](https://fortune.com/2022/08/16/jp-morgan-chase-jaime-dimon-warns-something-worse-than-recession-could-be-coming/)”.

In this scenario, my Bear Case, my S&P 500 bottom target is $2200-$2500.  I will continue to monitor and assess the probabilities of this scenario playing out, as it is in no way pre-determined what will happen from here.  There are many different ways things can play out over the next year or two, but I believe my “Bear Case” would provide the best long-term buying opportunity in stocks since early 2009.  In this case, equity prices would be near March 2020 Covid Crash lows, valuations would be severely depressed, and many investors will likely have given up on the stock market altogether.

Remember that Bear Markets provide tremendous long term buying opportunities for those that are patient enough to afford them. You do not have to time the exact bottom, but my goal is to time the bottom within 5-10% on these long term buys. Buying near bear market bottoms is how you set yourself up for early retirement.

**Strategy**

Technically, the S&P 500 has bounced off the 200 week EMA, a key long term support line that has only broken in Recessionary Bear Markets.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xdji!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F92fdee60-539a-4cb4-87a4-541545ac07bd_1327x637.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xdji!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F92fdee60-539a-4cb4-87a4-541545ac07bd_1327x637.png)

As mentioned above, I believe risks are elevated as we head into this Wednesday’s Fed meeting.  The internals are beginning to look overbought, as the S&P 500 is now 5% above its 20 day moving average.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!N495!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4bab87ed-636c-420e-9674-72c1fb4c6633_709x434.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!N495!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4bab87ed-636c-420e-9674-72c1fb4c6633_709x434.png)

The Fed will have the opportunity to squash the aforementioned large easing of financial conditions.  If they do not, and the market comes out of the week with a dovish interpretation of events, the technicals look supportive of a further bounce.

For the QQQ, a bounce to the top of the trend line (from the previous 2 Bear Rallies) would be another 5-7% higher from here ($295-$300).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0pnF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5918eaf9-9531-4610-91c1-b73a4c7ac01e_1365x651.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0pnF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5918eaf9-9531-4610-91c1-b73a4c7ac01e_1365x651.png)

I am open to both scenarios (a Fed induced rug pull, or further technical rally) coming into the week.  I will be waiting until after Wednesday’s Fed meeting to gauge the FOMC commentary, market reaction, and technicals before considering any trades in my Active Portfolio.
