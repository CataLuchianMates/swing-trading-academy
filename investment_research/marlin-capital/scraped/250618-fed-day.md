# Fed Day

**FOMC decision today - 2 PM EST. Press conference at 2:30 EST.**

There is a 99% chance of no rate cut at today’s meeting (according to Polymarket).

The Fed Funds Futures market is currently pricing in the 1st Fed rate cut in October ahead of today’s meeting.

The debate ahead of today’s meeting centers around the 2025 dot plot. Some expect it to rise (signaling just 1 rate cut this year), while other think it will stay flat. There is some hope that Powell will sound dovish in the press conference today given softer inflation and jobs. Others believe he will stay noncommittal due to tariff uncertainty (I lean towards the latter - noncommittal).

The market remains extremely choppy ahead of today’s Fed meeting. Geopolitical concerns remain at the forefront with BBC reporting yesterday that at least 30 US tanker aircrafts have been moved to Europe in the past 3 days to support bomber and fighter jet ops. So far, we have no confirmed reports of US involvement, but markets remain on high alert.

This uncertainty is certainly weighing on stocks, with the indices stuck in the mud here. Still, we have seen a flurry of headlines that one might expect to take the market lower, but we continue to bounce right back. Equities have begun pricing higher tail risk (SPX 1M normalized risk/reversal trading at higher end of 52wk range…spot VIX back at 21.6 despite 15d realized vol on SPX = 15).

US Stock Market Holiday tomorrow (market is closed).
