# Friday Market Notes 6.10

**Technical Analysis from Yesterday Post-Market:**

Today was anything but boring for the market. The indices broke down in a big way from their recent consolidation patterns. The SPY closed below its 8 and 21 EMA for the first time since May 24th. We saw a waterfall of selling in the indices this afternoon heading into CPI tomorrow.

There is still a possibility this could be a fakeout move if CPI comes in (much?) lower than expected tomorrow morning, but a lot of damage was done technically today. Many stocks are setting up for shorts after forming Bear flag patterns. Unless the market really likes the number tomorrow morning, this Bear Market rally is likely over.

**6/10 Morning Update (Post-CPI)**

Breaking: CPI hits fresh 40 year high

CPI for May came in at 8.6% (YoY) above expectations of 8.3%

Marlin Capital Thoughts:

Down goes the “inflation has peaked” argument. So, what does this mean for the stocks?

As discussed in “Nothing is Easy”, when equities rally, financial conditions ease.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ToX9!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9f99330a-4644-45d0-9e2c-a4c397ca1a0a_1024x846.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ToX9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9f99330a-4644-45d0-9e2c-a4c397ca1a0a_1024x846.jpeg)

This is not the Fed’s desired outcome.  I expect the Fed to put an end to any rally in equities until inflation is under control. With today’s CPI print showing accelerating inflation, we can safely say the Fed’s job is genuinely just beginning. The pipe dream of a Federal Reserve pivot in September is over. There is a long way to go in this process, and no way out for the Fed.

A Mike Wilson favorite -- EQUITY RISK PREMIUM

shows the earnings yield on the S&P 500 minus the interest rate on 10-year Treasury notes. The higher the risk premium, the more attractive stocks are relative to bonds
