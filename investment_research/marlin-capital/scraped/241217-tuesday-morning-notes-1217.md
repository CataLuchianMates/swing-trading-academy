My level of concern for this is not high at the moment, but it is something to keep an eye on. We may start to see a rotation into value and other sectors in the near future. With that said, we typically see a lot of end of the year type “window dressing” among fund managers. Example - underperforming fund managers will want to have stocks like TSLA/GOOGL/AVGO and other super-performers on their books at year end. You also have tax implications, where investors will typically not want to realize a gain 2 weeks before year end because they will owe taxes on it April 2025 (vs realizing the gain on Jan 1st and delaying the tax payment on it until April 2026). We also typically see some tax loss selling in losers/underperforming YTD stocks (realizing losses to offset gains and lower tax bills). These are all factors in why we often see winners keep running (and losers keep losing) into year end.
