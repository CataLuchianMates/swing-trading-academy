# Repositioning Rally

Big rally for US stocks last week with the SPX +4.6%, NDX +6.4%, and RTY +4.1%. The NDX is now positive (+1%) for the month of April. What a wild month it has been.

On Liberation Day (4/2), the S&P 500 closed at $5670. Following the market close on 4/2, we got our first look at the now infamous tariff board. 4 days later (4/7) the S&P 500 made its local low of $4835 (-21% off highs). Last week, we closed at $5525, recouping all but ~2% of the post-Liberation Day move.

So, how did we get here and what is the set up going forward?

I continue to point back to the historic de-risking we saw in US equities in February and March. We exited January at ATH total long positioning across institutional, retail, and foreign investors. Everyone, and I mean everyone, was “All In” US stocks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FP6y!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F01cb25d6-5080-4092-a98f-4d6dfa8336a3_1198x1075.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FP6y!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F01cb25d6-5080-4092-a98f-4d6dfa8336a3_1198x1075.jpeg)

From there, we saw some of the sharpest de-risking and PnL destruction within the Hedge Fund community that we have ever witnessed. I covered this historic HF selling in reports like [“The Big Unwind”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-big-unwind) and [“Positioning Cleanse”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/positioning-cleanse). This culminated with the largest HF net selling day ever on April 3rd (the day after Liberation Day).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!beMK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F302e68f1-aa06-4cc0-92f6-04e91ad020db_872x601.webp)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!beMK!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F302e68f1-aa06-4cc0-92f6-04e91ad020db_872x601.webp)

The de-risking was not limited to Hedge Funds, though. Foreign investors also dumped US stocks at the 2nd fastest pace since 2002.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NClT!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffbd1143c-a243-49d6-8fb7-5d4ad73dc9c3_1266x1010.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NClT!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffbd1143c-a243-49d6-8fb7-5d4ad73dc9c3_1266x1010.jpeg)

Total positioning reached -2.5z, near 15 year lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xFvp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3b530fa2-a109-4292-8355-c3168abe114e_1198x1075.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xFvp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3b530fa2-a109-4292-8355-c3168abe114e_1198x1075.jpeg)

This washout and -21% SPX drawdown (fortunately) set the stage for extreme underweight total positioning following Liberation Day. The next week, we got the 90-day tariff pause news.

The 90-day tariff pause news essentially removed the “worst case scenario” from the market (a sure fire recession - reached 67% odds on Polymarket). This forced a ton of short covering and repositioning (forced buying) back into US equites.

Now, Hedge Funds have been net buyers for 3 straight weeks. Last Wednesday was the 5th largest HF net buying day in the past year (+2.2 SDs), led by short covering and long buys in single stocks. Despite 3 straight weeks of net HF buying, positioning remains depressed, with US Fundamental long/short ratio still near 5 year lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!W9qB!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff2964469-5189-4413-82f8-1ca247f73d9d_456x378.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!W9qB!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff2964469-5189-4413-82f8-1ca247f73d9d_456x378.jpeg)

Total positioning has now risen from -2.5z (near 15yr lows) to -0.9 (just below neutral).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-w9m!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3dffa068-a26a-4c60-8e06-67bd382ae4f7_1198x1075.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-w9m!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3dffa068-a26a-4c60-8e06-67bd382ae4f7_1198x1075.jpeg)

However, the systematic trading community remains behind on this rally and could potentially be forced to chase (CTAs, etc). Overall systematic positioning is currently near 3 year lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vx72!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb7f660a2-70f1-4d86-82af-6e978eeda7b5_991x661.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vx72!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb7f660a2-70f1-4d86-82af-6e978eeda7b5_991x661.jpeg)

CTAs have just begun buying from a -$26B net short position.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sz1B!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8845940d-ecb3-4eee-bd07-08adf49705bd_945x651.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sz1B!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8845940d-ecb3-4eee-bd07-08adf49705bd_945x651.jpeg)

In [Day 3](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/day-3), I wrote this about the systematic trading community:

> Reminder that this cohort chases trends in equity markets. The “dumping” of the last few weeks is over, and sustained rallies will only extend demand.

This is how you get a “hated rally”, as total positioning shows participation was low and everyone was bearish at lows. The question now becomes: how much more repositioning (buying) is left? For me, given that Fundamental HF long/short ratio is in the 2nd percentile and systematic exposure remains net short, there is still some buying left to do.

The S&P 500 has now retraced close to 50% of the downside move from ATHs and is only down -2% from Liberation Day. The 61.8% retrace ($5700) aligns with the 200-day Moving Average, which is where I believe we are likely headed. From there, things could get cloudier based on a number of factors related to the economic growth slowdown.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uWEH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5700d67-58b2-4a34-9ea8-eacd4e9168b8_1027x715.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uWEH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5700d67-58b2-4a34-9ea8-eacd4e9168b8_1027x715.jpeg)

Bottom line - this repositioning rally likely isn’t over yet, in my view. We can see more systematic buying and more fund buying. I’m targeting the 200-day MA and 61.8% retrace level at $5700-$5750.

With that said, there will be no shortage of fundamental catalysts this week for the market to contend with. So far, 30% of the S&P 500 has reported Q1 results. Another 40% will report this week (by far the largest week of Q1 earnings).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ym3C!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff0dea8a8-e476-4fcd-989e-f4e339fe302f_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ym3C!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff0dea8a8-e476-4fcd-989e-f4e339fe302f_1920x1080.jpeg)

So far, earnings have been fine/good enough. 46% of companies have beaten earnings by >1 SD (only slightly below historic avg of 48%), while only 10% of companies have missed earnings by >1 SD (below historic avg of 14%). Given the overall Macro environment, beats are not being rewarded on T+1, with the median stock outperforming by only 50bps (vs 101bps historic avg), while misses have underperformed by 247bps (vs 206bps historic avg).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ww8v!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F336ba1f7-c861-4328-9640-624d96baf030_1131x794.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ww8v!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F336ba1f7-c861-4328-9640-624d96baf030_1131x794.jpeg)

Traders are still braced for the worst heading into these reports, which sets the bar very low. I talked last week about the lack of forward guidance by companies, which has largely continued. However, as I noted last week - during times like these, there is a premium for companies that are still issuing guidance. We saw that in some select names last week like APH, VRT, and PEGA.

For me, the theme from Q1 earnings has been: “good enough” to clear a very low bar. There is no better example of this theme than TSLA, which is now up +20% since last Tuesday’s report. The numbers were not impressive by any stretch, but the stock has rallied big anyway. While this is surprising to some, I talked last week about how stocks often bottom when news is at its worst (and top when news is at its best).

More importantly, as I highlighted last week, this a clear character change for this market (rallying on bad news). We are seeing the same type of character change in the indices, with negative trade headlines having less and less of an impact. As a trader, this tells me peak fear/uncertainty has likely already been discounted.

**Final Thoughts**

My view is that we likely have more gas left in the tank on this repositioning rally (driven by systematic buying and HF re-leveraging). I am ultimately targeting an SPX move to the 200d MA and 61.8% retracement level at $5700-$5800. The low bar for Q1 earnings also suggests this “hated rally” may have more room to run. 40% of the SPX reports this week, so stay tuned!
