# Is It Different?

To the surprise of many, the 2023 Stock and Bond portfolio (60% stocks/40% bonds) is off to its best start since 1987, up +4% YTD.  Since 1900, only 1938, 1987, and 1976 have gotten off to a better start.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_Liq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5c148ef-6fa6-4987-9dec-1041e96baaba_1150x455.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_Liq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5c148ef-6fa6-4987-9dec-1041e96baaba_1150x455.png)

This is following the 5th worst calendar year ever for the 60/40 portfolio.  Since 1900, only 1907, 1931, 1937, and 2008 fared worse than 2022.

Under the surface, hedge funds and institutional investors have been forced to cover outsized short positions.  In cumulative notional terms, last week’s short covering ranks in the 98th percentile vs. the past 5 years and is the largest since late October.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!puSY!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F68ff6efc-9f96-419c-bec1-659169cfcf8d_624x411.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!puSY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F68ff6efc-9f96-419c-bec1-659169cfcf8d_624x411.png)

We have seen this [type of action before](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/weve-seen-this-before) during this Bear Market.  Bear Markets typically cause max pain to both longs and shorts before all is said and done.  But, is this rally any different?

One of the key catalysts for this rally has been growing optimism over a “soft landing” for the US economy.  If there is indeed no recession, and the Fed successfully engineers a soft landing for the economy, it is more than probable that stocks have already bottomed.  The 2022 Bear Market is already deeper (in percentage terms) and has lasted longer than the typical non-recessionary Bear Market.

With that said, I remain in the “hard landing” camp.  If we do face a recession ahead, it is unlikely stocks have seen their lows for this cycle.

[Last week](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/were-a-long-way-from-kansas-now), I talked about the ISM Manufacturing Index and its importance as a leading economic indicator and a coincident indictor for stocks.  The chart below shows historical S&P 500 monthly returns in various ISM cycle phases.  The S&P 500 tends to perform the worst during the 50-to-trough phase of the ISM cycle.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mFzY!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa9141c80-bf2f-4cfa-8b8f-3991903f789d_412x356.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mFzY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa9141c80-bf2f-4cfa-8b8f-3991903f789d_412x356.png)

The ISM number just dropped below 50 in December (48.4), its weakest reading since May 2020 (when the economy was slammed shut by the COVID-19 pandemic).

The below chart predicts where ISM is headed given rising inventories and falling new orders.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!E7eL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdd8f81c9-72d8-4011-8225-8d38ea4ce1fc_629x415.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!E7eL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdd8f81c9-72d8-4011-8225-8d38ea4ce1fc_629x415.png)

If the overlay remains accurate, we will see ISM decline to the low 40s before hitting its trough.  This would likely mean more pain ahead for stocks.

This is an example of how stocks typically bottom after leading economic indicators (LEIs bottom), as I pointed out in “[Ghost of the Future](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/ghost-of-the-future)”.

**Technical Analysis**

Technically, this has been a big 2 week move for the market.  On the weekly time frame, the S&P 500 closed above its 40-week moving average.  This is near the spot at which the previous rallies have failed during the Bear Market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sPFG!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffa5590cc-dc8b-4176-bd95-e5919720a0f9_1031x651.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sPFG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffa5590cc-dc8b-4176-bd95-e5919720a0f9_1031x651.png)

The QQQ remains weaker on both time frames.  On the weekly, the QQQ is not yet at its 40 week MA.  It remains 3.9% below the level as we head into this week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Mi3S!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F92cf4016-15b5-4f5b-a87a-e383029c225b_1033x651.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Mi3S!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F92cf4016-15b5-4f5b-a87a-e383029c225b_1033x651.png)

On the daily time frame, the S&P 500 is now above its 200-day moving average.  Again, we are near the point at which the previous rallies have all failed.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3lCy!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb5effb26-7754-474f-9a5a-f5d77b1cf16e_1027x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3lCy!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb5effb26-7754-474f-9a5a-f5d77b1cf16e_1027x653.png)

In my view, the technical trend remains down until the 21 EMA and 60 EMA both cross over the 200 SMA.  As you can see above, this has not yet occurred during this rally.

In my technical analysis of the previous 7 Bear Markets since 1970, this has signaled that the bottom is in each time.  I discussed this during “[Bear Market Bottom Checklist](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-market-bottom-checklist)”.  Technically, this is where I believe the trend officially flips from “down” to up.

Internally, we are seeing some pockets of strength developing.  I noted the relative underperformance of growth and tech above, as value has taken the lead.

Growth leadership versus value appears to be broken following the 2021 peak.  The Dot Com Bust, which saw a similar Bubble peak in growth vs value, tells us that this rotation to value could still be in the early innings.  Back then, we saw several years of value outperformance before the pendulum swung back the other way again.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fFjY!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F29ea76f7-e7c2-49e0-b664-f54861702b43_1267x846.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fFjY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F29ea76f7-e7c2-49e0-b664-f54861702b43_1267x846.png)

Growth is bearish relative to the S&P 500, with an established downtrend following a double top breakdown in 2021.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LWnN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fea88abab-e71b-4992-bb1d-5430faea2ed5_1217x861.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LWnN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fea88abab-e71b-4992-bb1d-5430faea2ed5_1217x861.png)

As you can see, this phase of the economic cycle favors more defensive sectors and factors such as low volatility, high dividend yields, and strong balance sheets.

2 strong technical stocks I will highlight meeting those criteria include Clean Harbors Inc, (CLH) and Deere & Co (DE).

CLH is a waste management company with $2.43 in EPS last quarter.  Technically, the stock has a bull flag setup with a buy point above $122.  My stop loss would be below $117 (4.1% risk).
