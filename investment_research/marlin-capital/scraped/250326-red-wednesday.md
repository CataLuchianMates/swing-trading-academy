# Red Wednesday 

[On Monday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/big-picture-small-picture), I talked about my Big Picture and Small Picture views for the market.

For the Small Picture (my short term view), I was anticipating a market bounce this week based off underweight/oversold positioning. We got off to a great start on Monday with the S&P 500 rallying +1.8% and breaking back above the 200-day MA. This was followed up by a consolidation day on Tuesday.

Unfortunately, that upside momentum in the market was derailed today after 2 big news points came out. The S&P 500 broke back below the 200 SMA and closed -1.12%.

The first major news point today was a report from TD Cowen saying that Microsoft has cancelled new data center projects in the US and Europe that would have consumed 2 gigawatts of electricity.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!X2LM!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc6bde680-aeca-4733-8bb0-64ca3c6e400f_517x680.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!X2LM!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc6bde680-aeca-4733-8bb0-64ca3c6e400f_517x680.png)

The broader AI trade got hit hard on this news, as it now appears the AI hype cycle has likely already reached its peak. A Tech giant like Microsoft scaling back its investment in AI infrastructure is a very worrying sign for the Nasdaq, in my view.

The 2nd major news point today was Trump’s announcement of a 25% auto tariff on any cars made outside the US. It has truly been a game of ping pong trying to follow the constant back and forth tariff news, but today’s news certainly weighed on US stocks.

With the S&P 500 breaking back below the 200-day MA, I decided to close out the rest of my SPXL long for a +2.1% gain on this 1/2, as I do not want to take the risk of a profitable trade turning negative on me in a tough market.

[On Monday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/big-picture-small-picture), I covered my big picture market view, which is for a 2nd wave down in the SPX following this initial rebound. It now appears this 2nd wave down may be coming quicker than I anticipated after today’s negative newsflow.

Following an initial -9% + SPX selloff in <20 days (like we saw in 2025), it is typical to see the market attempt to stage a bounce/rebound.

Historically, we have seen the 1st bounce attempt fail, giving way to a 2nd wave down.

The 2nd wave down typically ranges between -3% and -15%, with an average of -10%.

A 2nd wave down of -10% would take us to the $5200 region in the S&P 500, which would be a -15% total drawdown from ATHs.

Anyone who has been reading Marlin Capital in 2025 knows how big of a factor I believe positioning has played in this selloff. This market correction has been a violent positioning undwind from ATH longs to now underweight US stocks in under 2 months. This is simply unprecedented.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6XzM!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2e1c7350-cd20-49e6-819a-36560a844137_1097x788.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6XzM!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2e1c7350-cd20-49e6-819a-36560a844137_1097x788.jpeg)

While this positioning cleanse is certainly welcome news for bulls, it is still unlikely to be the end of the positioning unwind. History says it is not. During Bear Cycles like 2022 and 2020, we saw overall positioning reach -1 Z score. We are not there yet in 2025.

Further, JPM’s Tactical Positioning Monitory (TPM) appears to have reached a major peak in 2025. After a major peak in JPM’s TPM, the S&P 500 has ultimately experienced larger peak-to-trough declines than we have seen so far.

Peak-to-trough declines have historically been between -14-25% (ex. 07-08), with an average decline of -20% post TPM highs. This would indicate another -10% + downside over the next ~6 months.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ajNm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3b716a06-a65c-4eb7-9bc5-3b6036e35554_605x590.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ajNm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3b716a06-a65c-4eb7-9bc5-3b6036e35554_605x590.jpeg)

Another bearish sign, in my view, is the buy the dip mentality still being seen from retail investors. This is not what you typically see at market bottoms. In fact, you typically see the opposite at market bottoms ([capitulation selling from retail](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/panic-selling)).

Retail investors have continued to buy the dip aggressively throughout this SPX correction. In fact, Retail has only been a net seller in 7 sessions for ALL of 2025.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JArI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1f710a5b-5631-4381-a2ee-feb0245c5c44_499x334.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JArI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1f710a5b-5631-4381-a2ee-feb0245c5c44_499x334.png)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lKUI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F04d4f0bf-24f0-4a79-adce-a69a5be509a8_385x278.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lKUI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F04d4f0bf-24f0-4a79-adce-a69a5be509a8_385x278.jpeg)

So far, the buy the dip behavior from retail is quite consistent with Q1 2022. It is not consistent with market bottoms throughout history. The market bottomed in Q4 2022 (October), as it took a large portion of the year for retail flows to finally flip negative.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qDDA!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffc22daf9-3dfd-494e-be69-251170ac75e6_1920x650.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qDDA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffc22daf9-3dfd-494e-be69-251170ac75e6_1920x650.jpeg)

Recession concerns have historically been correlated with retail flows. A large increase in searches for “recession” could be a precursor to eventual retail capitulation like we saw in the 2nd half of 2022. So far, retail behavior more so resembles Q1 2022.
