# Big Tech Stays Strong

On Friday, investors cheered the April jobs report, which showed the US labor market growing at a slower pace last month.  This is seen as a positive for investors, as it greatly increases the odds of the Fed cutting interest rates this year.

Combine this report with strong earnings late Thursday out of AAPL, which became the latest Big Tech company to boost its stock buyback plan, and all 3 major indexes finished the week in positive territory.

Both indices closed the week firmly above all key moving averages after making a potential higher low on Wednesday/Thursday.

The overarching narrative and outlook for Big Tech thru Q1 earnings so far remains a good one, though, I do think it is fair to acknowledge that as the group continues to progress higher, the legs to the bull case are narrowing a bit. Positioning is no longer the tailwind it once was (just a few weeks ago) and we are seeing signs of normalizing EPS revisions cycles in Q1 as the pendulum swings from ‘cost savings’ & ‘layoffs’ to ‘investing’ and ‘capex’ (i.e. MSFT, META, AAPL all saw flat/no EPS revision post Q1 prints).

With that said, the market has arguably digested this step-up in CapEx and the in-between macro backdrop we find ourselves in continues to put a bid in quality, liquid, self-funding assets with pricing power (hello big tech).  Big Tech is also accelerating topline growth at scale (AMZN AWS, META revs, MSFT azure, GOOGL YT/Search/GCP) and a growing moat around AI.  So yes, there is still a lot to like here from Q1 earnings.

If I had to try and rank investor sentiment and positioning in the group right now, I would say AMZN > MSFT > > GOOGL/AAPL > META.  Within this construct, I would note: investor conviction in AMZN feels very strong.  MSFT, at the moment, feels like a limited downside story (with bulls waiting for a breakout after 2 months of consolidation). META more bearishness post-print given tougher 2nd half comps & no clear AI case..GOOGL has an inflecting bull case (cost cutting), but carries more headline risk (DoJ + AI)…AAPL is turning into a popular under positioning and narrative trade.

Themes of Q1 earnings season so far:

1.      Public cloud trends are accelerating

2.      CapEx figures are very large ($150-$200B among the Big 5).  Mega Cap Tech (AMZN, MSFT, GOOGL, META) CapEx spending among these 4 is expected to nearly double from $100B in ’22 to ~$200B in ’25.

3.      Stock Buybacks are accelerating (GOOGL $70B buyback and AAPL $110B buyback).  This will shift from a headwind (stocks were primarily in their buyout blackout windows in April) to a tailwind for the market in May (see chart below).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5kxM!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F930bdbbb-ff5e-4d1f-8a41-00dc2e657c28_1032x444.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5kxM!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F930bdbbb-ff5e-4d1f-8a41-00dc2e657c28_1032x444.jpeg)

4.      EPS revisions are flattening out in places, putting a bit more pressure on multiples

5.      AI theme remains hot (though the market is seemingly gravitating towards faster payback like via cloud tailwinds versus very long term AI plays)

The YTD performance in Big Tech tells a pretty clear story with AAPL well behind the pack.  But, when you zoom out to pre-Covid levels, AMZN still stands out as the potential catch up candidate.  The YTD performance of this group is essentially an inverse of the total performance since 2020.  From the beginning of 2020, AMZN and META standout as the notable laggards vs MSFT / AAPL / and GOOGL in a tight band.

My big picture thoughts about each of the Mega Caps that have announced Q1 earnings so far –

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6Je5!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd55f7d75-9e91-4f69-b41e-92c24c2b42d8_694x190.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6Je5!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd55f7d75-9e91-4f69-b41e-92c24c2b42d8_694x190.png)

AAPL – While the math may not be overly compelling (PE Multiples vs EPS revisions), the story & narrative is quite compelling (positioning, seasonality, huge stock buybacks, and YTD laggard/catch-up trade).

META – The stock is now trading at 18-19x GAAP P/E ’25, but the big step-up revision from CapEx is behind us.  Tepid guidance but strong core product momentum.  On the bear side of things, it is a popular long with no notable catalyst.  Harder to own if the topline is modeled to decelerates from high 20’s YoY to low double-digits.

MSFT – A lot of overhangs are now priced into the stock.  Azure is re-accelerating, great catalyst path (MSFT build + AI product uptick), clear AI leadership position in Big Tech with a number of ways to monetize.

The lack of follow through post Q1 earnings in the name feels like it is primarily from stagnating FY25 EPS revisions (orange).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fV9W!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8cba62f7-904c-47db-8e71-56089fdea034_917x626.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fV9W!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8cba62f7-904c-47db-8e71-56089fdea034_917x626.png)

GOOGL – Improving AI narrative + inflecting cost cutting efforts.  Topline accelerating (Search, YT, GCP).  Inflecting cost/margin story (earlier in cost savings mode than peers).

On the bear side – lingering unkowns on the future of search (i.e. competition with AI Q&A chatbots like ChatGPT, Perplexity AI, etc.).  DoJ/regulatory concerns and tougher 2H comps.

AMZN – accelerating AWS growth (& margins) driven by: end of optimization + cloud migration + AI momentum.

The bear case – risk of a consumer slowdown.  Durability of op income beats.  CapEx ramping up “meaningfully” YoY in 2024.

Earnings expected in the week ahead won't include any members of the Mag 7 for the first time in 3 weeks.  With that said, we have a loaded slate ahead, which should provide some good opportunities for “Earnings Gapper” trade setups.  This will be my continued focus this week.
