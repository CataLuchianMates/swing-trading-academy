# The Marlin Capital 2026 Preview

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ANT3!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feb94efc7-02aa-4e05-aabd-181a236bfbba_1237x630.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ANT3!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feb94efc7-02aa-4e05-aabd-181a236bfbba_1237x630.png)

2025 was another terrific year for US stocks with the S&P 500 posting a +16.4% return, its 3rd consecutive year of >16% gains. This is rare territory for the US Stock Market. In fact, it’s happened just twice before in history (since 1928).

Once was during the Dot Com Bubble, when the SPX posted 5 consecutive years of >16% gains from 1995-1999.

The other was during the Covid Bubble (2019-2021).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0Ond!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F39204544-8c73-4b7f-8c05-4143b5789567_603x223.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0Ond!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F39204544-8c73-4b7f-8c05-4143b5789567_603x223.png)

In my view, there is little doubt that we are currently in another equity market Bubble (the AI Bubble) in 2026. Here at Marlin Capital, we have embraced the AI Bubble since May, and this thesis has helped us to key in on some of the big winners of this supercycle (**before anyone else**).

While there are many key differences between these 3 most recent equity market bubbles (the Dot Com Bubble, the Covid Bubble, and the AI Bubble), the most glaring similarity is in how the two previous bubbles topped.

During the topping of the Dot Com Bubble, the most speculative stocks who had nothing more than a business plan (i.e. Pets.com, eToys, etc.) peaked first.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!knuI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6cc67735-95bf-4291-98e0-7bb564620397_570x687.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!knuI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6cc67735-95bf-4291-98e0-7bb564620397_570x687.png)

From that point on, the weakness continued to spread. Individual names got taken out one by one. By the time the S&P 500 topped in March 2000, over 40% of the SPX was already at 52 week lows.

The same thing happened in 2021-22 with SPACs, ZM, ARKK, etc. peaking many months before the S&P 500.Pre-revenueSPACs (most now bankrupt) with nothing more than a business plan began peaking 11+ months before the SPX did. ZM peaked 14 months before the SPX. ARKK peaked 11 months before the SPX did.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kb1P!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa502cc0b-2226-4475-a2f1-a3999277c7be_1087x272.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kb1P!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa502cc0b-2226-4475-a2f1-a3999277c7be_1087x272.png)

By the time the S&P 500 topped in January 2022, 66% of the Nasdaq was already in a Bear Market (and 35% of the NDX was down over -50%). I wrote about this rapidly deteriorating breadth way back in December 2021 in [“Wild Times in the Market”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/is-this-market-crazy-or-is-it-just?utm_source=publication-search). This report was written about 1 week before the SPX topped and we entered the 2022 Bear Market. Most every stock had already topped for the cycle at that point.

**My research shows that Bubbles throughout history have peaked this way (with the most speculative groups/names topping first).**

And that brings me to one of the key risks we face heading into 2026: the most speculative stocks appear to have already topped for this cycle (starting in ~October).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fSEb!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F61f87413-3837-4ba0-9276-c92f5b48e8ee_538x688.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fSEb!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F61f87413-3837-4ba0-9276-c92f5b48e8ee_538x688.png)

I [wrote about this at length in October](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70?utm_source=publication-search), as speculative leaders like OKLO and IONQ were putting in classic “Climax Top” patterns. Now, these names are already down upwards of -60% from highs

If we were to follow the pattern of the 2 most recent equity market bubbles (the Dot Com Bubble & the Covid Bubble), the SPX eventually topped 8-14 months after the most speculative names began to crack. This would suggest a potential SPX cycle top in Q4 ‘26 or Q1 ‘27.

This would also be in-line with the typical recent Bull Cycles in terms of length (in months), as this would be around the ~20+ month mark of this Bull Market.

**The Bigger the Boom, the Bigger the Bust**

Generational technological advancements (like AI) have typically led to multi-year asset bubbles that have always ended in massive busts. Past examples include Railroad stocks in 19th century Britain, the Dow in the roaring ‘20s (with the invention of automobiles), Radios & Electronics, and the Internet in the late ‘90s. These bubbles form as investors celebrate the idea that technology will create a new paradigm.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!C-Iz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5100d63b-3a94-417b-bb31-f4159f554226_1177x460.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!C-Iz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5100d63b-3a94-417b-bb31-f4159f554226_1177x460.png)

As you can see in the diagram above, each Bubble took multiple years to build (like the AI Bubble has), and resulted in multi-year bust phases.

Historically, the bigger the boom, the bigger the bust.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QlPW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5089ce6-3d73-4b3b-9ab4-f0b2f06b7b98_442x472.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QlPW!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5089ce6-3d73-4b3b-9ab4-f0b2f06b7b98_442x472.png)

**Valuations**

Valuations are historically high heading into 2026 (>94th %ile for the SPX).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gXdm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F956769bc-2d07-47e5-af95-38a7d5d77073_678x526.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gXdm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F956769bc-2d07-47e5-af95-38a7d5d77073_678x526.png)

But, the same was true heading into 2025. All you have to do is rewind back to [my 2025 preview](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-roaring-20s-an-ai-revolution?utm_source=publication-search) and you will see that we began 2025 with a similar forward P/E to now, and that did not stop the SPX from returning +16%. In 2025, the forward P/E of the S&P 500 only rose by ~3%; the rest of the market’s +16% gain was attributable to earnings growth.

What [I wrote 1 year ago](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-roaring-20s-an-ai-revolution?utm_source=publication-search) holds true now (so if you are new to Marlin Capital, please [read this section](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-roaring-20s-an-ai-revolution?utm_source=publication-search)).

> “This is why I have said before, valuations do not matter on the way up, and they do not matter on the way down. During a Bull Market, valuations can always go higher. During a Bear Market, they can always go lower.”

Valuations do not matter on the way up or the way down. This is because markets and stocks gyrate from “overvalued” to “undervalued” like a pendulum. They do not stop at “fair value” and trade there infinitely. In fact, they do not trade at “fair value” at all.

Right now, we are in the “overvalued” phase of the pendulum swing (also known as the Bubble Phase). Optimism is high, earnings growth is strong, and profit margins are huge. There is no telling how high valuations can go or how “overvalued” stocks can get during this phase. Compared to the Dot Com Bubble, valuations could still get a whole lot sillier before the market peaks.

[![Exhibit 7](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!iDlM!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffd4dd231-d208-4f03-b37b-61ae07260f6a_1108x528.jpeg "Exhibit 7")](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!iDlM!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffd4dd231-d208-4f03-b37b-61ae07260f6a_1108x528.jpeg)

Or, we could top out tomorrow. This is to say that valuations are NOT a good predictor of what will come next. They tell you “where we currently are”, NOT “where we’re going”.

**The Fed**

One of the key tailwinds for equities heading into 2026 is that the Fed is currently in “easing mode”. The Fed Funds Futures market anticipates 2-3 interest rate cuts in 2026 (approximately 50-60 bps of total cuts are currently baked into the curve for the full calendar year).

But it’s not just rate cuts. Last month, the Fed announced that they will start buying $40B of Treasury Bills. I wrote about this in detail in [“QE Lite”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/qe-lite). While it’s not technically Quantitative Easing, I framed it as “QE Lite” (and it is undoubtedly a tailwind for equities).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xGsz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F78563da1-f659-424e-9905-696142dd2577_880x551.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xGsz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F78563da1-f659-424e-9905-696142dd2577_880x551.png)

If you subscribe to the expression “Don’t Fight the Fed”, the outlook for 2026 appears rosy.

**Macro**

2026 is a midterm election year. Historically, midterm years are the worst years of the presidential cycle, with the S&P 500 finishing higher just 53% of the time with an average gain of just +4.6%, while the other 3 years finished up 78% of the time, with an average gain of +11%. The silver lining for 2026 is that midterm years tend to be a little stronger under a 2nd term president (+8.8% avg return).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LY8q!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc27d7ad7-8a34-4746-b37f-1aea59a89ce5_993x449.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LY8q!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc27d7ad7-8a34-4746-b37f-1aea59a89ce5_993x449.jpeg)

The last 2 midterm election years (2022 and 2018) were the last 2 negative years for the S&P 500.

In my view, the primary driver of this weaker performance is that markets do not like uncertainty. Midterm years introduce a layer of political uncertainty and policy risk to the equation. This amplifies volatility, and as a result, midterm years tend to have a larger intra-year drawdown than usual (averaging a -17.5% drawdown).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!B8BX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff862696b-80a7-4dff-b5d6-c030277e3876_900x461.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!B8BX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff862696b-80a7-4dff-b5d6-c030277e3876_900x461.png)

The historical pattern is for markets to bottom just before midterms end in early November (unsurprisingly, once the uncertainty fully clears).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!isE6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F64de05a2-4fa8-4067-bb63-c478ce455380_900x531.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!isE6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F64de05a2-4fa8-4067-bb63-c478ce455380_900x531.png)

**The Labor Market**

As we turn into 2026, the softening in the labor market looms large. Historically, when the Unemployment Rate turns up from a low level and above 5%, it has eventually culminated in a recession.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_Oca!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F739db163-bcfa-482f-a319-226e15858cd9_1025x641.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_Oca!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F739db163-bcfa-482f-a319-226e15858cd9_1025x641.png)

This brings me to the Bull Case and the Bear Case for 2026. There are two sides to every story:

**The 2026 Bull Case**

* Don’t fight the Fed (the Fed is easing)
* Earnings are expected to grow at a strong +13% YoY rate
* There is no recession on the horizon
* The result: a 4th consecutive year of >16% gains (like the Dot Com Bubble)

**The 2026 Bear Case**

* The labor market is turning
* The most speculative stocks are topping
* Mid-term election years are weaker and more volatile
* The result: the SPX tops in 2026 and finishes the year negative

#### My Base Case

I have received many questions about a specific market headwind/tailwind heading into 2026. As you can see in this 2026 preview report, it’s all a balancing act. There are **so many** different factors at play at one time, making it impossible to zero in on just one headwind or tailwind. The Fed is easing, that’s bullish. BUT it’s a midterm election year, that’s bearish. BUT earnings growth is projected to be strong, that’s bullish. BUT the most speculative stocks have likely already topped, that’s bearish.

So, at the end of everything, where do I stand?

**My Base Case is for a +7-8% SPX return in 2026. My year-end price target for the SPX is $7300-7400.**

**It’s still all about AI in 2026.**

The market has been all about AI ever since the release of ChatGPT in November 2022. That will NOT change in 2026. AI is the dominant theme. AI is the dominant driver of everything. As I wrote back in May, we are in the midst of a generational technological advancement, the likes of which we have only seen a few times in history. It’s an exciting time to be an investor, because so many companies are playing a major part in this revolution. And for me, AI will continue to be my focus in 2026. It would be foolish to turn my attention elsewhere as we live through this exceptionally rare point in history.

But, as I have highlighted, I am expecting a more challenging and volatile year ahead in 2026.

In my view, we are now in the later stages of this Bull Market/Bubble. This tends to be a much more challenging part of the cycle to trade. As I have talked about before, topping is a long process, and not all stocks top at the same time. In my view, some stocks (mainly the most spec/high beta names) have already topped for this cycle. Going off historical patterns, this would mean we have 8-14 months (from October) left until the SPX tops for the cycle. If you look back at past cycles, the most spec/highest beta names began topping before the Fed was raising rates, before any recession surfaced, and before Financial Conditions were tightening. For this reason, as I talked about in my [Top 2026 Picks Report](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-picks-for-2026) last Sunday, I will be managing each name in the portfolio individually this year (because certain names will begin to drop out of the “Bull Race” this year).

I have received some questions about potentially positioning more defensively for 2026, given that I am anticipating a more challenging year. My answer is not yet (at this point in the cycle). Historically, bubbles often culminate with blowoff tops in select names that are viewed as the biggest winners. Personally, I believe it is important to try to milk this bubble for all its worth, because these types of technological leaps come once in a generation. We are living in a rare moment in time right now, and I intend to take full advantage. We have not seen anything like this since the dawn of the Internet in the 90s. When the music stops (and it will, eventually), there will be nowhere to hide (except in cash). If you look at the Dot Com Bubble as an example, it was strictly a Tech bubble. Yet, it did not matter for non-tech during the bust phase. There is no escaping the selling during a Bear Market (including defensive sectors), even if Tech is the sole reason for the Bear Market. This is why you must seize these types of rare opportunities when they come. This is exactly what we have done these past several years at Marlin Capital, and what we will continue to do in 2026. During times like these, decades happen in months.

At this point in the market cycle (late stage Bull Market), I believe that we are very much in a stock picker’s market for 2026. And I expect the biggest winners of this market to continue to come on the Micro side (think AI, AI, AI) versus the Macro side. So long as the economy remains stable, this should continue to be a Micro driven market (just like we’ve seen the past few years). [My top picks were released last Sunday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-picks-for-2026), and we entered each of them on Monday. As I talked about above, with the potential SPX cycle top coming this year, this portfolio will require much closer monitoring (on an individual basis) this year. So stay tuned for updates throughout 2026!

Overall, I am expecting another positive year for the SPX in 2026 (+7-8% base case), but it will come with more challenges and volatility. The biggest challenge, as mentioned, will be the fact that individual stocks tend to follow their own paths when we reach the latter stages of a Bull Market (meaning they begin topping at different times). And this makes trading and investing much more difficult. Everyone is a genius in a Bull Market, but many don’t keep those gains at the end. All and all, you should gear up for tougher sledding ahead in 2026.
