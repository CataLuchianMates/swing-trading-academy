# Thursday Morning Notes 3.20

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Yesterday’s FOMC meeting ended with little surprise, as the Fed left rates unchanged and indicated a consistent 2 cuts for 2025. The market was looking for a reason to rally and seems to have found it with Powell saying all the right things (“Michigan inflation expectations an outlier, bringing back the word “transitory” for any tariff related inflation, hard data still pretty solid, recession risk increased but still not high”).

I talked yesterday about my key reasons for turning bullish into month end. It all comes down to positioning, which has shifted from super bearish (throughout this correction) to now bullish (into March end). I expect to see some rebalancing back into US equities over the next week +.

I entered SPXL yesterday (3x S&P) for a long trade at $145.50 during Powell’s speech. I sold 1/3rd on the initial pop to $149.21 (+2.5%) to de-risk a bit on the rest of my trade.

Now, as long as the indices continue to act well, I am planning to hold the rest of my SPXL long position into next week. I am targeting a bounce to $154-$162.
