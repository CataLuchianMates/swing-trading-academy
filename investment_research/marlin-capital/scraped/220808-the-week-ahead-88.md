# The Week Ahead 8.8

Technical Analysis:

The market pulled back a bit on Friday following a much stronger than expected jobs report. Despite the morning gap down, the indices closed well off their lows of the day. On a technical basis, the QQQ setup remains constructively bullish heading into this week. **CPI data will be out Wednesday.**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6Aup!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8170cbda-f057-4130-95f1-a1a0b32c9fb9_1023x769.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6Aup!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8170cbda-f057-4130-95f1-a1a0b32c9fb9_1023x769.jpeg)

For the QQQ (Nasdaq proxy), which has been the strongest index, we still remain nicely above the key short (21 EMA) and medium term (60 EMA) moving averages. Today, we are set for a gap up in the indices to kick off this week.

Key Resistance levels to watch ahead include $324-$325 (Friday’s highs), and, if we can get above these levels, the all important 200 day MA ($342 and declining).

Key support levels to watch below include $318-$320 (Friday’s lows), $310-$312, and $304-$305. It would be ideal to hold Friday’s lows for this setup to remain constructively bullish.
