# The Week Ahead

Last week was a challenging one for US stocks as the SPX finished -3.1%, NDX -5.5%, and RTY -3.5%.  Technically, the major indices broke below key support levels and signaled an end to the uptrend that began last November.

Semis finished -9% on the week as both ASML and TSM signaled slowdowns in chips/auto segments.  NVDA, the undisputed leader of the AI boom, dropped -13% on the week for its worst stretch since 2020.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LqEb!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc55aa09f-da54-4136-aae0-d7f6124d897e_1151x667.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LqEb!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc55aa09f-da54-4136-aae0-d7f6124d897e_1151x667.jpeg)

Interest rate sensitive parts of the market have led to the downside on this correction and have pulled back the most from their recent highs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0nRI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8960583c-e9c4-4201-91c5-0f269bcc4991_1091x930.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0nRI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8960583c-e9c4-4201-91c5-0f269bcc4991_1091x930.jpeg)

A wave of Fed speakers took a hawkish stance with regards to rate cuts, and the consensus is now pricing in just ~2 cuts for 2024 versus the 5-6 that were being priced just a few short months ago.  In fact, rate hikes/0 cuts in 2024 are now seen as more likely than 3+ cuts.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fWSy!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fadab3a75-4b82-4d9f-a6cf-7357fcf3dc8e_1284x775.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fWSy!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fadab3a75-4b82-4d9f-a6cf-7357fcf3dc8e_1284x775.jpeg)

Middle East tensions also played a critical role on sentiment and shifted a “risk-off” market environment.  US 10 YR yields held 4.6% for the majority of the week, settling in at 4.62%  All of these factors led to a broader sell off across US equities and an unwind of the momentum/soft landing trade.

Sentiment and positioning ended the week a bit mixed across different timeframes.  On a short term basis, the market enters the week of 4/22 very oversold.

On the positioning side, systematic funds have been sitting max long almost all year.  This has exacerbated the recent sell off, as these funds are forced to aggressively take down their exposure levels once markets begin to hit downside trigger levels.  One of the main reasons we have not seen any major relief rally/follow through on bounces is because these funds are unloading supply into any and every pop.

As mentioned, equities breaking through key levels last week unleashed a wave of systematic selling from CTAs.  With that said, CTA equity length still sits in the ~90th percentile versus history, indicating that this cohort still has more de-risking to do.

We also saw a major wave of new institutional short flows last week.  Hedge Funds increased their short positions in US listed ETFs by the largest amount in 20+ months last week, according to GS Prime.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!m8rY!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F38129bd4-4a98-413a-9f9b-11defcc2a487_1284x1313.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!m8rY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F38129bd4-4a98-413a-9f9b-11defcc2a487_1284x1313.jpeg)

There is no question we have seen a major character change in the market with the major indices breaking through key support levels and no real dip buying to be seen.  With that said, we faced a perfect storm for a market correction as the indices had been grinding higher for months on end with little to no volatility.  Any bad news/rising inflationary pressures/rising 10 YR had been largely ignored until Middle Eastern tensions came to a boil.

While wars have always been a catalyst for market corrections in the past, history shows that these sell offs have typically provided buying opportunities for those patient enough to take advantage.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YZO6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F507e19e3-973c-4bb0-8186-724d1465bd6e_1245x1007.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YZO6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F507e19e3-973c-4bb0-8186-724d1465bd6e_1245x1007.jpeg)

Macro catalysts and geopolitical tensions are likely to take a back seat this week to a loaded slate of companies announcing Q1 earnings.  Tesla, Visa, Meta, Microsoft, and Google headline the list of companies reporting this week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XiTd!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6bf350ba-0492-4e19-8a4b-8f474bae13f4_1290x720.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XiTd!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6bf350ba-0492-4e19-8a4b-8f474bae13f4_1290x720.jpeg)

So far, Q1 earnings are not off to a hot start as NFLX showed that a simple beat/respectable quarter will likely not be enough in this environment (the stock sold off -5%+ following results last week).  The bar is high for Q1 results and investors are currently looking for any reason to unload risk.

I enter the week of 4/22 in all cash in my trading account as the technical breakdowns took me out of the last of my long positions last week.  Short setups could come into play for me in the coming weeks, but the best time to look short will be following a multi-day bounce into resistance and not at deeply oversold levels (like we currently find ourselves).

This is to say I have 0 plans to enter any new trades in the early part of this week.  Potential earnings gapper setups will be my top focus this week, but it must be noted that this will be a much more challenging environment for these types of setups with the general market now in a downtrend.  Heightened volatility creates increased opportunities for short-term/daytrades, but a much more challenging environment for swing trading.  In my view, this is a time to respect risk and focus on capital preservation.  Opportunities will be plentiful on the other side of this correction.
