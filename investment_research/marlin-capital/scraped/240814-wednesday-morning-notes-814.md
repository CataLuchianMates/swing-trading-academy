# Wednesday Morning Notes 8.14

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Stocks melted up yesterday on low volume following softer PPI (0.1% vs 0.2% MoM).  The SPX finished +1.68% while the NDX rallied +2.5% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cD_4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fda39c99a-b2a2-4eb9-a7c2-4b1878e47d8e_1194x892.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cD_4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fda39c99a-b2a2-4eb9-a7c2-4b1878e47d8e_1194x892.jpeg)

The NDX is now up +6.25% over the last 4 days, its best stretch in 15+ months.  This rally seems to be less about good news but more about a reversion in sentiment after last Monday’s washout/a lack of bad news on the Macro front.  Activity levels were quite low yesterday as investors were seemingly looking ahead to this morning’s CPI release.

Per Goldman’s Prime Book, US Tech gross trading flow has risen in 9 straight sessions, suggesting increasing risk appetite for institutional investors after the large de-grossing in July/early August.  By subsectors, Semis & Semi Equipment, and to a lesser extent Software, have seen the largest $ increase in gross flows MTD.

July CPI –

CPI YoY 2.9% vs 3% Expected

CPI Ex-food and Energy 3.2% vs 3.2%

CPI MoM 0.2% vs 0.2% Expected

CPI Ex-food and Energy 0.2% vs 0.2%

Another welcome sign for inflation confirming yesterday’s soft PPI number. We got the big rally on softer inflation yesterday, so it would not be surprising to see the market take a little rest here. With that said, the data flow this week has done nothing but confirm the market’s rally off last Monday’s washout lows.

My top trade setup for today –

SHOP – setting up nicely for a continued run post-earnings.  We have a potential gap fill to $77 if we can safely clear $70-$71 resistance.

Yesterday was an inside day for SHOP.
