Marlin Capital Readers,

Last week was a huge one for US stocks. The S&P 500 surged +3.5% for its best week since November. The Nasdaq ripped +4.5%. Both indices reclaimed their 200-day SMAs following the Iran ceasefire announcement on Tuesday night.

And it was an even bigger week for us here at Marlin Capital.

We made significant changes to the 2026 Portfolio [last week](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/2026-portfolio-update), redeploying capital into our highest conviction AI names. If you missed it, the full breakdown and strategy is in [“2026 Portfolio Update”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/2026-portfolio-update) and [“The Playbook”.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-playbook)

Our new buys are off to a hot start. We sidestepped most of the -13% Nasdaq drawdown, preserved our YTD gains, and hopped right back in when the conditions changed.

We are now up +16% YTD in the [2026 Portfolio](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/marlin-capital-live-portfolio-dashboards) heading into this week.

**This Weekend’s News**

But, as most of you have seen by now, this weekend’s newsflow brought some turbulence once again.

On Saturday, we learned that US-Iran peace talks in Islamabad collapsed after 21 hours of marathon negotiations. Vice President JD Vance said Iran refused to commit to giving up its nuclear ambitions.

Then, this morning, Trump announced that the US Navy would “effective immediately” begin blockading the Strait of Hormuz. Oil surged back above $100. Futures are indicating a gap down of -0.8% as of this final editing.

This is a significant new development. But, as I have said many times throughout this conflict, it is not the news that matters. It is the market’s reaction to the news.

As I talked about in [“Headline Fatigue”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/headline-fatigue) last Sunday, the market was already starting to shrug off the Iran headlines. We first saw it on Trump's [April 1st re-escalation speech](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/whiplash). The SPX closed green. Then we saw it again last Tuesday, when the SPX closed green despite Trump's “an entire civilization will die tonight” threat.

Regardless of how tomorrow opens, my conviction has not changed. I view this pullback as buyable. And the reason is simple. 👇

The scary Macro headlines are telling me one story right now. But the charts (and Micro) are telling me a completely different one.

How can I be bullish when the “peace talks” are seemingly falling apart? Because underneath the headline noise, the leaders in this market are doing something that you do not see in bearish markets. They are breaking out. All at once.

Forget the headlines for a second. Look at these charts. 👇

**Recap**

**There is nothing bearish about these charts.**

Notice how many times I said “x stock broke out to new highs last week”. That’s not bearish.

All 14 of our stocks are either breaking out or setting up for breakouts. This is not what you see heading into a bear market. This is what you see when institutional money is quietly accumulating the leaders while everyone else is distracted by headlines.

The charts are telling a completely different story than the news right now. And in my experience, the charts are usually right.

At the end of the day, I follow the charts. I do not trade headlines. I trade charts. When the charts are telling me to buy, I buy. When they are telling me to sell, I sell. And I live with the result. If this turns out to be a bull trap, it will have fooled me. And I’m 100% okay with that. Because when countless stocks (particularly the strongest fundamental names) are all breaking out to new highs at the same time, I am going to be long. Full stop.

**The Earnings Picture**

But here is what makes me even more confident. It is not just the charts. The fundamental earnings picture underneath is the strongest it has been all year.

S&P 500 EPS estimates are being revised upward at a pace we have not seen in over a decade.

Typically at this point in the year, consensus EPS estimates are being revised downward. Not this year. The fundamental earnings picture is accelerating.

Further, a divergence has opened up between projected earnings growth and equity positioning. Earnings are going one way. Positioning is going the other. Historically, that divergence resolves in favor of earnings.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!c5NI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb937d15e-3817-4f88-b420-33a42b39947b_1443x1084.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!c5NI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb937d15e-3817-4f88-b420-33a42b39947b_1443x1084.png)

We had an -18% SPX forward P/E contraction which began back in October. This is an outcome that typically coincides with a recession and/or an actively tightening Fed. We have neither right now.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!v-kC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb6b875f3-a2cf-4e14-aaa6-b378e8286640_1270x1080.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!v-kC!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb6b875f3-a2cf-4e14-aaa6-b378e8286640_1270x1080.png)

The combination of falling multiples with improving earnings growth is more indicative of a bull market correction than a bear market.

In a bear market, you typically see earnings estimates collapse alongside multiples. That is not what is happening here. Earnings are moving in the complete opposite direction, which helps explain why the damage has been contained to roughly -10% for the S&P 500 despite one of the most volatile headline environments in recent memory.

Outside of the Iran conflict, the micro picture remains incredibly strong. And in my view, the market is still in the process of moving past this conflict despite this weekend’s setback.
