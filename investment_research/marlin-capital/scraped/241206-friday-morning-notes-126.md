# Friday Morning Notes 12.6

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Stocks were little changed yesterday with the SPX closing -0.19% and NDX -0.31% as investors awaited this morning’s NFP report.

**US Jobs report (Nov.)**

NFP: 227k (exp. 200k, range 155-275k, prev. 12k, revised 36k)

Unemployment rate: 4.2% (exp. 4.2%, prev. 4.1%)

Earnings M/M: 0.4% (exp. 0.3%, prev. 0.4%)

Earnings Y/Y: 4.0% (exp. 3.9%, prev. 4.0%)

Nonfarm payrolls (+227k) came in above consensus of +215k and the 3-month average of +104k. This report appears to have hit the sweet spot for stocks. Firmly above consensus (+227k vs +215k) but not so strong that a rate cut is off the table (+275k+).

In fact, following this morning’s NFP, the odds of a December rate cut have risen to 91% (as it has cleared the risk of a “too strong” NFP report forcing the Fed to stay put). With this risk off the table, this morning’s jobs report has essentially locked in the upcoming December rate cut.

Stocks are advancing so far this morning following the report. As I mentioned, this jobs report appears to have hit the sweet spot for stocks and is in no way the bearish catalyst bears were praying for. As I talked about in “The Final Act of 2024”, absence a major bearish catalyst, I expect stocks to continue to drift higher into year end. My end of 2024 outlook remain bullish from here.
