# Risk Management Over Everything

### Study Your Losses

Here’s how I handle losing: 👇

**By becoming a good loss taker.**

True enlightenment as a trader comes from knowing that every trader is wrong. **A lot.**

This includes the best traders to ever live. Druckenmiller, Soros, PTJ, Minervini, Darvas, Tepper, Kullamagi, etc. are often wrong.

If you are in the business of trading stocks, you will be wrong too. Nobody will ever bat .1000 in this game.

But it’s **not** about batting .1000. It’s **not** about predicting the future perfectly.

It’s about **making more when than you are right**, **than you lose when you are wrong.** To quote Stanley Druckeniller:

> "It's not whether you’re right or wrong that's important, but how much money you make when you're right and how much you lose when you're wrong"
>
> - Stanley Druckenmiller

This is why many of the stock trading GOATs, like Mark Minervini and Bill O’Neil, all emphasize **avoiding big losses** as 1 of the keys to their success.

On the Prop Trading Floor, your max loss limit is typically set at a hard -10%. Many of the the trading GOATs have set a similar max loss limit on their own trades.

### OSCR trade

**The Sell Signal**

In “[Big Beautiful Breakout”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/big-beautiful-breakout?utm_source=publication-search), I talked about how negative news can absolutely kill a momentum trade.

> “When you are trading a stock based off momentum, negative news is an expectation breaker. It completely changes said momentum, and therefore completely changes the entire idea of the trade. This is why I am quick to exit a trade when a stock has negative news.”

**With OSCR, we had a waterfall of selling after hours (on 7/1) on news & volume.**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!w2w-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F37221b42-03bc-4c44-88de-2d4d6d4df2bb_1115x652.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!w2w-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F37221b42-03bc-4c44-88de-2d4d6d4df2bb_1115x652.jpeg)

Going into the OSCR trade, $18.80-$19 was my key support level to watch. We blew through that support level after hours on negative news (+ volume).

* Waterfall of selling after hours on news & volume 🚩
* Blowing through support after hours on news & volume 🚩

**My max loss limit on trades is typically in the -10-15% range.** In strong markets, I might stretch to -15% (as long my target is 2x1 on my risk, and many momentum stocks can hit 30%+ gains quickly).

After slicing through $19 support, OSCR was within my -10-15% max loss range. As the stock broke my final support level at $18.80 (again, on negative news and volume AH), I hit the sell button, **taking a -10.8% loss from my entry point.**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LqlS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffb79debb-f5d6-4a60-a5d5-830504516e4a_1284x180.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LqlS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffb79debb-f5d6-4a60-a5d5-830504516e4a_1284x180.jpeg)

Done.

Move on.

No emotions, just discipline.

Since my OSCR sale after hours (7/1) at $18.76, the stock has declined another -23%. It just closed this week at $14.38.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!d6jt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F65da92bd-b36a-45fe-b1a6-b9109e232378_1116x651.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!d6jt!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F65da92bd-b36a-45fe-b1a6-b9109e232378_1116x651.jpeg)

**Risk happens fast.** A trader can NOT control how and when risk happens. All they can control is how they react.

**If you are confident in your abilities, you should feel no emotion taking a loss.** This is a sentiment that Stanley Druckenmiller echoed in his interview with Jack Schwager for his book [“Market Wizards”](https://www.amazon.com/Market-Wizards-audiobook/dp/B00BYKKJPQ/ref=sr_1_3?dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.G6QgvAmPT54RZcaXJHcetgufyT4zGO7wsBXurWOWi_pDowS7Qx_KmbXfMoybfR8RwoipGRqE5NQwW5Bto2ppeAVZnquAySJy8QXxvfC20ul_S2LCI0cNwlcjmFCbp4WvTbvlTKRspGuuGOSfxDWW80SUDXWjco_UBRfUWbQl29YR3yUJ4_OiRn09BHbZqPMdoQTlQUPz26xpN4ChDdASvtXWMfih5uE08Y5oI_28JlQ.GTe2KlBzuQRcatRASaYTWCNVMFao1RJ_UwPDLjl08Xw&dib_tag=se&keywords=stanley+druckenmiller&qid=1752277946&sr=8-3) (a book I highly recommend reading for traders).

> "If you're extremely confident, taking a loss doesn't bother you…
>
> “Soros is the best loss taker I’ve ever seen. He doesn't care whether he wins or loses on a trade. If a trade doesn't work, he's confident enough about his ability to win on other trades that he can easily walk away from the position. There are a lot of shoes on the shelf; wear only the ones that fit."
>
> - Stanley Druckenmiller in his interview for “Market Wizards”

George Soros took losses without blinking because he trusted his ability to find winning trades. That confidence allowed him to move on quickly, and it’s a lesson every trader must internalize.

**Embrace Your Losses**

Michael Jordan famously said:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zr1X!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff4943963-baf2-4515-ab28-1cedbd01abc5_600x400.webp)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zr1X!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff4943963-baf2-4515-ab28-1cedbd01abc5_600x400.webp)

Losses are part of the journey. Failure is a part of life. Embrace it so that you can learn from it.

Taking losses well is a skill that sets great traders apart. Studying your losses is critical. Nicolas Darvas, another trading legend, kept a detailed journal to learn from his trades.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZmoX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fedc102d0-4812-4649-a782-b58b08165f91_1741x857.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZmoX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fedc102d0-4812-4649-a782-b58b08165f91_1741x857.jpeg)

Equally important, though, is finding peace with the outcome. You should be at peace with whatever happens in a trade knowing you did your research and followed your process. After that, whatever happens is out of your control.

Control what you can control. Do your homework. Follow your process. Then live with the result.

Trading is about discipline. Not perfection.
