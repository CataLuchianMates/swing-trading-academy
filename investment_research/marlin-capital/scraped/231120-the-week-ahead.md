# The Week Ahead

The S&P 500 finished slightly positive (+0.13%) to close out the week last Friday on a quieter/light volume session.  9.8B shares traded across all US equity exchanged vs YTD daily average of 10.9B share (and -14% vs 20d avg volume).

The technical setup for the SPX remains bullish as we head into this week. We have seen very little giveback after the sharp rally following softer than expected CPI.  Technically, this is a bullish sign as price consolidates/bull flags/allows the moving averages to catch up without pulling back sharply.

Top of the board last week: Regional Banks +10%, Momentum Losers +9%, Non-Profitable Tech +8%, Most Short +8%, High Beta +8%, HY Debt +8%, Low Profit R2k +7%.

Overall, GS Prime book was net sold for the first time in 4 weeks as gross trading flow decreased on the week, driven by both long and short sales (~2 to 1).  Single stocks and Macro Products were both net sold on the week, accounting for 52% and 48% of the notional net selling, respectively.

Hedge Funds sold US Consumer Staples at the fastest pace since April 2020.  Last week’s net selling in US staples ranks in the 99th percentile vs the past 5 years.

Info Tech saw the largest de-grossing activity (long sales + short covers) in nearly 9 months.  Notional long selling in Info Tech was the largest in 7 months and ranks in the 90th percentile vs the last 5 years, while short covering was the largest in 4 months and ranks in the 95th percentile vs the last 5 years.

Last week’s earnings calls gave us some insight into the health of the consumer/retail shopping.  While government retail sales beat for the 2nd straight month, corporate commentary gave a much more guarded tone.

WMT was the biggest case in point last week, speaking to a softer consumer than what they had presented 90 days ago, specifically highlighting a slowdown in spend they observed beginning in the 2H of October.

The bottom line from last week’s retail earnings is that things appear to be slowing, but it is not a sharp decline and we are still seeing some pockets of strength from the consumer/retail (restaurant strength in October, apparel/general merchandise bounce in November).

2 things working for the market for the next month/through year end.  (1) is seasonality.  For the SPX more broadly, seasonality is very bullish into year end.  The market has a tendency to continue rallying in December/following Thanksgiving when stocks are already up this much YTD.

(2) is corporate buybacks.  92% of corporates will be in the open window for buybacks by the end of this week.  GS estimates $5B worth of daily VWAP demand during this open window.  November and December are the best 2-month period for buyback executions of the year.
