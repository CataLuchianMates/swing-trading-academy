# Thursday Morning Notes 2.22

**Daily Market Commentary:**

It was a slow bleed lower for most of yesterday’s session, until a big reversal in the last 20 minutes for the SPX to finish green.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!c41E!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6955e512-f4d9-4b09-8065-7323c43e4043_1195x889.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!c41E!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6955e512-f4d9-4b09-8065-7323c43e4043_1195x889.jpeg)

NVDA earnings were the big story with the stock now up +11% this morning. Why? Strong GMs beat with a headline beat/guide above with the $22.1B in Jan and guide for $24B in April, which was in line with buyside expectations/whispers going into the print.

Maybe some will debate the ‘wow’ factor relative to elevated expectations (EPS beat by 12% in Jan qtr and guiding April revs +9% Q/Q), but there was clearly a fair bit of de-risking going into the print to the point where ‘beat and fade’ had arguably become the consensus view.  As such, the beat and fade reaction may have been pulled forward with the recent price action heading into the print.

Also, I think one can argue that a more steady beat and raise cadence is healthier for the stock (and the ongoing peak debate), if you can call beating by $2B in revs steady.  Said another way, the multiple arguably gets a boost from more revenue beats that feel sustainable than explosive “one off” quarters.

We are set for a big gap up open this morning across all indices.  While NVDA results/reaction so far is exactly what bulls wanted to see, I would like to see some backfilling of today’s gap/let the dust settle before jumping to add exposure.
