# Friday Morning Notes 10.14

Technical Analysis

The market staged a massive reversal yesterday following a hotter than expected CPI report for September. This is the latest example of the “event clearing squeeze” I have referenced throughout this year (CPI, FOMC, etc).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mnB0!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff32a8683-be77-41cc-b86a-4d286b5c829d_1284x1003.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mnB0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff32a8683-be77-41cc-b86a-4d286b5c829d_1284x1003.jpeg)

Yesterday’s bullish reversal candle is a positive sign for a potential short term bottom. In the chart above, I view yesterday’s candle as a similar reversal candle to 2/24/22 (see arrow above). In that instance, the reversal was followed by a 4 day rally that topped out at the 21 EMA, which was then followed by a (successful) re-test of lows and major Bear market rally.

I would look for a similar type 2-5 day rally this time around, followed by a re-test of lows. Whether or not we see a sustained Bear market rally following the re-test remains a big question mark, and traders should be open to all possibilities. So far, the 2022 Bear market has closely followed both the dot com bust and GFC.
