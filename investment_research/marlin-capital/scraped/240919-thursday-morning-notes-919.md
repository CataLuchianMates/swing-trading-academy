# Thursday Morning Notes 9.19

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The Fed delivered its first rate cut since 2020 yesterday by cutting 50bps.  44bps of cuts were priced in heading into the event, so the market essentially forced the Fed’s hand. The market initially popped, but faded the knee-jerk reaction higher to close -0.29% (SPX).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2vVv!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6eed98fa-a262-4fa9-861c-867cdb17da28_1200x882.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2vVv!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6eed98fa-a262-4fa9-861c-867cdb17da28_1200x882.jpeg)

Powell made it clear that this was a risk management decision, not one taken under duress or because the Fed thought it was behind the curve, but because inflation gives them confidence to make this decision.  The 50bp cut was ultimately the one that leaves them the best positioned to keep the labor market in a “strong place”.  We avoided the potential “policy mistake” fears that a 25bp move would’ve prompted, and sit positioned for new data to guide the ebb and flow of growth sentiment.

The post-meeting statement was updated to say that the FOMC was “strongly committed to supporting maximum employment” alongside the 2% inflation target and that the risks to the committee’s dual mandate goals were “roughly in balance”.  The median dot in the Summary of Economic Projections showed 50bps more cuts in 2024 to a Fed Funds Rate of 4.375%, 100bps of cuts in 2025 to 3.375%, and 50bps of cuts in 2026 to 2.875%.  This implies that 12 out of 19 participants projected 5 or 6 cuts over the next year.  The projections also showed a higher unemployment rate path and a somewhat lower core inflation path in 2024-2026.

As mentioned, the market faded into the bell, but we are set for a big gap up today with SPX futures +1.55% and NDX futures +2.13%.
