# Tuesday Morning Notes 11.8

Technical Analysis

The indices continued Friday’s bounce yesterday to kick off the week, with the SPX closing smack between its 21 and 60 Daily EMAs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Shc4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8ef5b424-d254-4f52-bea5-f5563c041a77_1241x940.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Shc4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8ef5b424-d254-4f52-bea5-f5563c041a77_1241x940.jpeg)

Closing above the 21 EMA is a good first step for bulls. The next key resistance level I’m watching is the 60 EMA ($3833). A close above $3833 would open the door for a larger rally, potentially up to the 200 day moving average ($4031). Keep in mind we have CPI data to be released Thursday and mid-terms this week as potential market moving catalysts.

The key support level on a move down remains the $3700 level. The Daily 21 EMA of $3775 and yesterday’s low of $3760 should also act as potential support levels if the market begins to pull back. If these levels break decisively, we will likely be setting for a test of the $3700 support level.
