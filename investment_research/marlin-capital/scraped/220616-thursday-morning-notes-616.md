# Thursday Morning Notes 6.16

Wednesday Post-Market Analysis:

The Federal Reserve hiked interest rates by 0.75% today, the biggest increase since 1994.  The market responded with a big rally after briefly turning negative following the decision.  This was not a surprise, as the market had priced in a 75bp hike coming into the day.  The Central Bank delivered, and the market once again rallied on Fed day.  This is an example of the “event clearing squeeze” I referenced in today’s morning notes. Throughout this year, we have had several rallies on Fed Day (after the Fed delivers what is expected) that failed the next day or a few days later.

It is also important to note that we were short term oversold on the technicals coming into the day.  We still are at this point, so this bounce can continue.  For the QQQ, a bounce to the 21 EMA at $297 is possible.  The first step is getting above today’s high (and the anchored VWAP) at $287.  Resistance levels to watch above are $287, $292, and $297. Another thing to keep in mind is that we have Opex this week, so the rest of this week will be very interesting to watch. Stock Market is closed next Monday for Juneteenth.
