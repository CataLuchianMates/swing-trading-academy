# Thursday Morning Notes 11.16

**Morning Market Commentary**

US stocks traded mostly sideways in a quiet session on Wednesday (SPX +0.16%).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!F_og!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F866c786b-7822-4080-b671-10622801e7d0_1188x900.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!F_og!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F866c786b-7822-4080-b671-10622801e7d0_1188x900.jpeg)

We saw the same flavor for lower quality/small caps with Liquid Shorts, High Beta, and Most Short pockets all up +2.5% yesterday, despite 10 YR yields inching 9bps higher (on the back of firmer retail sales/reversion from Tuesday’s aggressive decline).

Mega Caps finished at the bottom of the barrel yesterday with weakness coming from AMZN (-2.6%, worst day in ~3 weeks).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZSLO!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffc6102af-60ca-4793-bd39-8aac152d0152_1129x1123.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZSLO!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffc6102af-60ca-4793-bd39-8aac152d0152_1129x1123.jpeg)

Per GS Prime Book, Hedge Fund net exposure to Mega Cap tech is currently ranked in the 99th percentile (versus the 12th percentile at the beginning of 2023).  This speaks to the possibility of some continued rotation from leaders (Mega Cap Tech) to laggards (Small Caps, etc.), as the “Long Big Tech” trade has never been more crowded.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!A4MD!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fce9b0f29-c979-49ec-8a78-4cbbfc071ddf_1290x1091.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!A4MD!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fce9b0f29-c979-49ec-8a78-4cbbfc071ddf_1290x1091.jpeg)

It’s easy to see why this trade got so crowded - YTD, the magnificent 7 has accounted for 75% of the move in the Nasdaq.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2lIN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F448bacb6-dede-443a-a9d1-71cabac4999b_1150x716.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2lIN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F448bacb6-dede-443a-a9d1-71cabac4999b_1150x716.jpeg)

Retail sales numbers released yesterday came in slightly firmer than expected.  The headline number might have come in hotter, but the low read ended a 6 month streak of increases, suggesting consumer spending may be topping out.  Of the 15 retail sales categories, 9 witnessed decelerated in the YoY retail sales growth rates compared to last month.  Here’s how Target’s CEO put it in the company’s earnings call:

> “Consumers are still spending, but pressure like higher interest rates, increased credit card debt, and reduced savings have left them with less discretionary income, forcing them to make tradeoffs.”

In the options market, calls accounted for 57% of the tape yesterday (the highest level since early August).  Russell 2000 call volume remains at all time highs with 1.4M contracts traded.

After hours, CSCO and PANW reported mixed results with weaker guidance.

 CSCO – FQ2 sales guidance well below estimates ($12.7B vs $14.2B consensus) and cutting FY revenue guidance ($53.8-$55B vs pre $57-$59.2B)

PANW – guided Q2 billings below estimates ($2.34-$2.39B vs $2.43B consensus) and cutting FY billings guidance ($10.75B vs consensus of $10.95B).
