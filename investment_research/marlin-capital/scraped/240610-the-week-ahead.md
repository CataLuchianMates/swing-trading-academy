# The Week Ahead

US stocks rallied to new record highs last week, led by the S&P 500 (up +1.3%) and Nasdaq (up +2.4%).

Small and Mid Caps pulled back with R2K Futures declining -2.2% on the week.  Despite the S&P 500 setting new record highs on the week, we are still seeing net lows for the index.  The red bar at the bottom of the chart below displays more 52 week lows than highs (as of Friday’s close).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Omik!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6d0cbd31-665f-4c98-b2c4-52bd05e778b3_1826x1044.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Omik!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6d0cbd31-665f-4c98-b2c4-52bd05e778b3_1826x1044.jpeg)

This tells us that market breadth is currently quite narrow and only a few stocks continue to carry the indices.  The main contributor to the indices’ gains last week was NVDA, which rose 10% after the company unveiled its latest generation of AI chips named “Rubin”.  NVDA has risen 728% since the start of 2023 and its market cap has increased from $360B to $3T+ (passing AAPL as the 2nd largest company in the world).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!V9ed!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F60f4012e-4358-4477-b869-599de543211e_2048x1149.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!V9ed!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F60f4012e-4358-4477-b869-599de543211e_2048x1149.jpeg)

In total, NVDA is responsible for about 1/3rd of the S&P 500’s YTD gains.  This number rises to above 50% when you add in MSFT, META, and AMZN.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ssfM!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0f28fe24-ba65-464c-bbd1-d091e1944322_1025x970.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ssfM!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0f28fe24-ba65-464c-bbd1-d091e1944322_1025x970.jpeg)

Overall, Mag 7 stocks advanced for their 6th week in the last 7 and had their best week in the last 7.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZaUl!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F64e48f73-5e0d-44f1-bbd3-ee33c5da2d08_1203x743.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZaUl!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F64e48f73-5e0d-44f1-bbd3-ee33c5da2d08_1203x743.jpeg)

The question has been and continues to be, “How long can this Mega Cap outperformance continue?”  As I’ve touched on in past reports, the current market dynamics make it incredibly difficult to be bearish on Mag 7 stocks.

For example, Apple announced the largest stock buyback program in market history last quarter (a $110B stock buyback plan).  If you strip out the Mag 7 from the index, the average market cap for the S&P 493 is just $65B.  This means that Apple’s stock buyback program is 169% larger than the size of the average S&P 493 company.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!n3Bv!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa36ac7ca-5a4c-49a1-9eea-1dbcf2747b5d_590x395.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!n3Bv!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa36ac7ca-5a4c-49a1-9eea-1dbcf2747b5d_590x395.png)

Despite the narrow market breadth we have been observing recently, this year’s gains are actually broader than 2023.  More sectors, asset classes, and regions are participating in the upside in 2024 than was the case in 2023.  Many European indices are keeping pace with the US market, while 10 of the 11 SPX sectors are positive YTD (real estate is the only sector in the red).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!P_Xb!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3b93da8b-404e-4a0e-9f1d-cb79ecca8693_1284x861.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!P_Xb!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3b93da8b-404e-4a0e-9f1d-cb79ecca8693_1284x861.jpeg)

Small Caps continue to lag the broader indices.  Last week was the Nasdaq’s greatest outperformance over Small Caps in 7+ months, setting a new record high for Nasdaq outperformance over Small Caps.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!RVTC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5cafc74d-2b94-4582-ac53-0ca8c94ad9cb_1159x713.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!RVTC!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5cafc74d-2b94-4582-ac53-0ca8c94ad9cb_1159x713.jpeg)

History tells us that brighter days may be ahead for Small Caps with the Fed rate cutting cycle expected to formally begin this September.  Historically, Small Caps tend to outperform in the 6-12 months following the 1st rate cut.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Wjg9!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffa9a8978-adab-4026-8a8e-be87fbe6985f_585x314.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Wjg9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffa9a8978-adab-4026-8a8e-be87fbe6985f_585x314.jpeg)

Speaking of history, we are entering a seasonally choppy period of the calendar year.  Historically, mid-late June tends to be choppy/directionless for the Nasdaq (and SPX), as we typically see some end of Q2 rebalancing/housekeeping among Fund Managers, etc.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DV0n!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F43d1bacd-6a16-4f09-b94d-7d6a4738aba8_1102x750.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DV0n!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F43d1bacd-6a16-4f09-b94d-7d6a4738aba8_1102x750.jpeg)
