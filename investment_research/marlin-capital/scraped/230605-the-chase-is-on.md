# The Chase Is On

The market rally continued last week with the S&P 500 advancing +1.83%, Tech (QQQ) advancing 1.79%, and Small Caps (IWM) rallying +3.33%.

In line with this trend, we observed a substantial influx of capital into the Tech sector last week.  In fact, Tech stocks just saw their biggest weekly inflows on record (dating back to 2002).  This surge in demand suggests a clear chase for exposure in Tech.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EyXE!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F262e8734-3238-41aa-85ea-22bbe68cb7fc_614x423.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EyXE!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F262e8734-3238-41aa-85ea-22bbe68cb7fc_614x423.png)

Amid the sector’s sharp price outperformance of the last month, hedge funds have been moving into Tech at an accelerated pace.  Tech has been the most notionally net bought global sector on the GS Prime Book for 4 straight weeks, driven by short covers and to a lesser extent long buys (~1.5 to 1).  In cumulative notional terms, the recent streak of net buying in Tech stocks is the largest over any 4-week period since Feb ’21 and ranks in the 99th percentile vs. the past 10 years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!z8LR!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7c527dc7-b7a1-4fd6-85f9-ec540a0430d9_693x553.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!z8LR!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7c527dc7-b7a1-4fd6-85f9-ec540a0430d9_693x553.png)

Semiconductors were by far the most net bought subsector over this 4-week period, driven by long buys and short covers, accounting for over 80% of the net buying in the Tech sector.  Meanwhile, software stocks saw modest risk unwinds with long sales roughly offset by similar amounts of short covers.

Two trends I have talked much about over the [past few weeks](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/tug-of-war) include the strength of Mega Cap Tech and AI stocks. Mega Cap Tech giants have been leading the overall market higher, contributing to the vast majority of 2023’s gains.

The below chart puts this phenomenon into context.  The magnificent 7, as named by BofA - META, AMZN, AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL, TSLA, and NVDA are currently up +54% in 2023.  The sum of the other 493 companies are currently up +2% YTD.  Adding it all up, the S&P 500 is currently +12% YTD.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BqMH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5de8a107-d48a-4463-a9a9-13329262f843_1001x759.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BqMH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5de8a107-d48a-4463-a9a9-13329262f843_1001x759.png)

Meanwhile, the GS Basket of AI Related Stocks is currently boasting a YTD gain of +56%. As I discussed in [“Tug Of War”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/tug-of-war), Artificial Intelligence is the primary theme driving the market higher in 2023.

In my view, the AI theme is making the largest companies in the world even more dominant.  The barriers to entry in the AI sector are enormous when factoring in IP, capital intensity, and established industry relationships.  The investments that companies will need to make to be successful in AI are significant, as new entrants will be competing against the likes of Trillion Dollar tech behemoths like Microsoft, Google, Nvidia, etc.  There are only a handful of companies in the world that can afford such an investment.

In this way, it is reasonably similar to the Cloud, where Google, Microsoft, and Amazon have become the 3 most dominant players because they have the capital to afford competing in such a capital intensive industry.

As I noted above, the price performance of these Mega Cap tech companies YTD reflect this phenomenon.  With that said, the “Long Mega Cap Tech” trade has become the most crowded trade among Fund Managers surveyed by BofA.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vRM2!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F12b316c0-81d3-4e81-a97d-5c3bb2b66cbb_1148x436.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vRM2!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F12b316c0-81d3-4e81-a97d-5c3bb2b66cbb_1148x436.png)

When you follow the evolution of the “Most Crowded Trade” in BofA’s monthly survey, it is important to note that these consensus trades have largely underperformed after reaching that status.  This time could always be different, but I believe the Mega Cap Tech long trade is largely played out.

I noted above how Tech stocks just saw their largest weekly inflows on record (dating back to 2002).  The QQQ, specifically, saw its largest weekly inflow of 2023 last week and the 3rd largest over the last 2 years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Z1gN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F05dbe44d-931e-4091-86f7-595e811d2f57_849x783.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Z1gN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F05dbe44d-931e-4091-86f7-595e811d2f57_849x783.png)

As you can see in the chart above, we saw big spikes in QQQ inflows just before the all-time highs in late ’21, at the Aug ’22 Bear Market Rally highs before a -22% drawdown, and Nov ’22 (which preceded a -10% decline).

Small caps have experienced a much slower start to 2023, lagging behind large caps significantly.  To put this into perspective, the S&P 500 is up 12% YTD, the QQQ is up 34% YTD, Small Caps (IWM) are up 5% YTD, and Micro Caps (IWC) are up 0.5% YTD.

However, there were some encouraging signs of potential recovery on Friday as we witnessed funds flow into this sector.  We saw the largest 1 day outperformance of the iShares Russell 2000 ETF (IWM) compared to the Invesco QQQ Trust (QQQ) in over 2 years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Rqif!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F23f43f5f-a639-4b25-9f0c-9157639fe967_509x293.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Rqif!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F23f43f5f-a639-4b25-9f0c-9157639fe967_509x293.png)

Lastly, it is worth noting that the S&P 500 is currently sitting at YTD highs, while the VIX is currently sitting at post-Covid lows.  In fact, the current level of the VIX is lower than at any point during the post-Covid bull market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7Pub!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd15a819e-832e-4a96-a880-e0d6113fb56f_1273x692.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7Pub!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd15a819e-832e-4a96-a880-e0d6113fb56f_1273x692.png)

This reflects the absence of fear in the equity market and a lack of concern regarding an upcoming recession.  Yet, leading indicators are currently suggesting a recession is imminent within the next 12 months.

Since 1960, anything more than a -1% YoY decline in the US Conference Board’s Leading Economic Index (LEI) has led to a recession within a year.  We are currently at -6% YoY.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dud-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5563d99a-9ff1-4119-b4ab-dc29c0f24550_1155x687.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dud-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5563d99a-9ff1-4119-b4ab-dc29c0f24550_1155x687.png)

This is a chart to tuck away for later, as the market is not currently concerned or bothered.  However, as I noted above with flows and positioning, the “pain trade” is now becoming a recession and subsequent decline in the equity market in the future.  This is the opposite of the “pain trade” heading into 2023, where an impending recession, bearish sentiment, and underweight positioning was consensus.  The “pain trade” at this time was for markets to rally in the 1st half of 2023, which is exactly what has happened.

If the LEI chart above proves to be accurate once again, we are setting up for fireworks in the 2nd half of this year.  This is a bridge we will cross if/when it comes, as there is no reason fight a trending market.  With that, I will leave you with some words of wisdom from a fellow trend following investor/trader, the legendary Paul Tudor Jones:
