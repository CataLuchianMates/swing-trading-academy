# Tug Of War

In [past](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/light-at-the-end-of-the-tunnel) [reports](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-trend-is-your-friend), I have discussed how in Bear Markets, certain stocks tend to bottom before the broader market indices. This pattern has been witnessed throughout history, including during the Dot Com Bust.

However, the current cycle seems to be bucking this trend. Currently, the indices and Mega Cap stocks (the largest in the world) are showing signs of bottoming out before most other stocks. Various indicators, such as the lack of stocks outperforming the S&P 500 and breadth indicators support this observation.

S&P 500 market breadth is currently narrower than at any other point over the last 23 years (since late 1999/early 2000).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oHSk!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F905e8cf9-1883-42c4-93a0-cff87176e851_987x573.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oHSk!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F905e8cf9-1883-42c4-93a0-cff87176e851_987x573.png)

Moreover, we are currently seeing the biggest gap between the S&P 500 (market cap weighted) and equal-weighted S&P 500 since Dot Com Bubble (meaning most stocks are not participating in this rally).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!A369!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4ae8e3e4-63eb-4e05-b3e4-a8c156f14f81_1229x665.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!A369!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4ae8e3e4-63eb-4e05-b3e4-a8c156f14f81_1229x665.png)

When you dig even deeper into this dynamic, you can see that the core theme driving the market higher is Artificial Intelligence (AI).  Excluding AI-related stocks, the S&P 500 is currently flat YTD.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nORj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa0743391-daea-4850-99fc-16e9faa023f3_993x730.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nORj!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa0743391-daea-4850-99fc-16e9faa023f3_993x730.png)

Goldman Sach’s Basket of AI winners versus the equal-weighted SPX shows a parabolic move underway for AI stocks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!AiZ7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8dd4b34d-922b-4006-936f-4e59cc1e1cbb_633x374.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!AiZ7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8dd4b34d-922b-4006-936f-4e59cc1e1cbb_633x374.png)

This outperformance is unquestionably due to the staggering AI industry developments of the last year, headlined by the release of OpenAI’s viral chatbot ChatGPT in November.   
  
ChatGPT reached 100m monthly active users in just 2 months (in January) after launching in November, making it the fastest-growing consumer application in history.  For some context, it took TikTok 9 months after its launch to reach 100 million users and Instagram 2.5 years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BCng!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fde2a42ed-87a5-411a-98ac-f0aa1419498f_500x429.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BCng!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fde2a42ed-87a5-411a-98ac-f0aa1419498f_500x429.png)

While savvy tech insiders were already well aware of the major AI developments taking place under the surface, the release of ChatGPT opened Wall Street’s eyes to this phenomenon.  In my view, ChatGPT made AI “real”, allowing the average consumer a glimpse into how such technology will change our lives.   
  
Dario Perkins, head of TS Lombard’s Global Macro Desk, found that the widespread adoption of ChatGPT is likely to spark much faster innovation:

> “*ChatGPT gained 100 million users faster than any other application in history, and these fast adoption rates are not confined to individual users. Major corporations, such as Bain & Company, have entered into deals with OpenAI to use generative AI in their strategy consulting business, while companies like Expedia have integrated ChatGP T through plug-ins.*
>
> *The more exciting impact on living standards, however, is likely to come from the second of our productivity channels – the pace of technological innovation. Generative AI can significantly expedite the R&D process by automating complex tasks, analysing vast datasets and predicting potential outcomes. It has already been useful in biological research: DeepMind's AlphaFold predicted the 3-D structure of almost every known protein – a task that had been predicted to take decades of human labour (according to the journal Science, the most important scientific breakthrough of 2021).*
>
> *This, alongside other AI breakthroughs, has led Dr. David Baker from the Institute for Protein Design to estimate that the pace of innovation in his field is now 10 times higher than it was 18 months ago. If we see rapid increases in innovation across other areas, the impact on productivity could be transformative.”*

Personally, I am fully bought into the AI hype, as I have spent the last year helping develop [AI driven trading strategies for a quantitative hedge fund](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/trading-with-a-quant-fund).  I have observed firsthand how complex AI driven strategies are able to trade more effectively than humans (including me or any Wall Street/hedge fund trader I’ve met) over a shorter time frame.  Of course, this was by no means a new discovery by our team, as the largest hedge funds in the world solely rely on AI/algorithmic trading over a shorter time frame, and no longer employ any human traders to execute such strategies.  
  
Speaking of our AI trading, [Marlin Quant Portfolio Core Members](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/trading-with-a-quant-fund) have enjoyed a winning streak of 10 straight profitable trades between our 2 best strategies (with an additional 6 more entries holding unrealized gains heading into Tuesday).  This is our fund’s largest winning streak since we began live trading 1 year ago.

Despite revolutionary and life-changing technologies like AI, it is crucial to acknowledge that even the most groundbreaking innovations can experience asset bubbles. In fact, history has shown that the most exceptional technologies typically culminate in a bubble, fueled by the excitement and animal spirits of market participants.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PS6U!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc1a06fbd-2f33-4efa-b16a-afbfad4e17c6_778x431.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PS6U!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc1a06fbd-2f33-4efa-b16a-afbfad4e17c6_778x431.png)

If AI is the next bubble, it is only a “baby bubble” at this point, according to BofA.  Historically, the animal spirits of market participants have blown bubbles much bigger than the one we are currently seeing in AI stocks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4qFm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcd2ad0bc-77b1-425e-a37a-334ed33e57f1_1044x542.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4qFm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcd2ad0bc-77b1-425e-a37a-334ed33e57f1_1044x542.png)

Meanwhile, the ARKK bubble, which I have [discussed in detail since the launch of Marlin Capital](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/were-not-in-kansas-anymore), has fully burst, even blowing past my [original Bear Case](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/were-not-in-kansas-anymore) ($44 PT – 52% downside from [original report](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/were-not-in-kansas-anymore)) I gave in December 2021.

Most of these formerly leading stocks have fulfilled the Minervini 50/80 rule, having already declined by 80% or more:

> *“Once a secular leader puts in a long-term top, there is a 50% chance that it will decline by 80%—and an 80% chance it will decline by 50%.” - Mark Minervini*

Historical evidence (from the aftermath of the Dot Com Bust) suggests that the companies that can bounce back and become profitable over the long term (sustainably) will see their stocks fully recover eventually. I discussed this phenomenon in “[We’re A Long Way From Kansas Now](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/were-a-long-way-from-kansas-now)”.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yvWe!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb54400e2-f3f8-4a3a-95d5-a7495773ff5e_647x423.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yvWe!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb54400e2-f3f8-4a3a-95d5-a7495773ff5e_647x423.png)

With that said, the large majority of these stocks remain completely out of favor right now.  The recent strength in tech stocks is still not broad-based, with less than 60% of issues above their 200-day MA.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BAv4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb5ab4583-4f6a-477b-ab2a-22ffa6608c65_1218x859.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BAv4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb5ab4583-4f6a-477b-ab2a-22ffa6608c65_1218x859.png)

Small Caps and Dow 30 stocks are largely among those not participating in the current market trend.  Historically, we have never seen a sustainable Bull Market where only 1 major index, sector, or industry actively participates.  A rising tide lifts all boats.  For this uptrend to remain intact, it will be critical to see participation broaden out beyond mega caps and YTD winners (largely AI stocks).

With that said, the current market dynamic raises an interesting question: if a stock like NVDA, the 6th largest company in the world, has already reached its bottom, how likely is it for the indices to make new lows? In past market cycles, it was typically smaller and mid-cap companies that bottomed out before the indices with the generals getting hit last.  In this cycle, will we continue to see the opposite occur?

**My take:** In terms of active trading strategy, I advocate sticking with the large-cap tech AI theme until it shows signs of losing momentum. The market is currently driven by this theme, and the excitement surrounding it has the potential to propel it even further.

Monitoring the activity in the options market can provide valuable insights into where large bets are being placed in AI-related stocks. Some names to watch in this regard include MPWR and TER, as both stocks saw unusually high call buying activity towards the end of last week.  I will be continuing to post the largest options bets I see in the daily morning notes (as well as any setups in individual stocks that interest me), so be sure to keep a keen eye on this moving forward.

On the other hand, I would mostly avoid attempting to catch falling knives in stocks that have failed to catch a significant bid during the recent index rally.  For long-term investing, some of these out of favor names can provide good opportunities down here, provided the company is on path for future profitability.  I will be discussing some new long term ideas and top-down investment trends to watch in a report to be released over the next month.
