# Wednesday Morning Notes 10.11

**Daily Market Commentary**

US stocks rallied once again on Tuesday with the S&P 500 finishing the day +0.52%.  The index hit 60 EMA resistance at $4380 before retracing some of the intraday move and closing well off highs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!e2xP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe0940bd7-f4bf-4dbf-9302-d6a0134023b5_961x727.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!e2xP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe0940bd7-f4bf-4dbf-9302-d6a0134023b5_961x727.jpeg)

The QQQ remains the stronger index with price already above its 21 EMA resistance level.  The QQQ made a high of $371.28 yesterday before retracing back below $369 at the close yesterday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tCD_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa643cc3e-6617-4e97-b9da-53abdd4de8f7_967x721.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tCD_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa643cc3e-6617-4e97-b9da-53abdd4de8f7_967x721.jpeg)

$369-$370 and $372 remain key resistance levels to watch for the QQQ.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4wQq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6080167f-85c7-4c36-bb16-85aadb90a99f_1396x663.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4wQq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6080167f-85c7-4c36-bb16-85aadb90a99f_1396x663.png)

Dovish commentary from Fed officials + talks of a fresh round of China stimulus put downward pressure on rates as the 10 YR yield retreated -15 bps to 4.65%.  Yesterday’s move was among the 5 biggest daily declines in the 10 YR this year.

The market rallied out of the gates yesterday on Bostic’s comments “I don’t think we need to raise rates anymore.”  With that said, the rally was primarily of lower quality with Non-profitable tech +3.6%, China ADRs +3.6%, and Liquid most short +3.3% leading the pack.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!plXd!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc8eeb274-fc75-437d-8a7b-784ba5108e42_809x665.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!plXd!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc8eeb274-fc75-437d-8a7b-784ba5108e42_809x665.jpeg)

Positioning wise – after heavy selling in August that was driven almost entirely by hedge funds pressing shorts, the aggregate US equity long/short ratio is currently near 5 year lows (which ranks in the 2nd percentile).  Last Friday, cumulative short flow reached a fresh YTD high.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cNSP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3f16b7f5-2eff-45c9-93cd-1174993f214f_1235x490.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cNSP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3f16b7f5-2eff-45c9-93cd-1174993f214f_1235x490.jpeg)
