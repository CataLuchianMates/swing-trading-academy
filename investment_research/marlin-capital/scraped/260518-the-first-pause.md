# The First Pause

Marlin Capital Readers,

US stocks finished higher last week, but the way the week ended is the key thing I’m focused on.

The S&P 500 and Nasdaq both hit new ATHs on Thursday before Friday’s big selloff erased most of the week’s gains (SPX -1.2%, NDX -1.5%, RTY -2.4%).

The S&P 500 and Nasdaq finished the week up just +0.1%. The Russell 2000 finished -2.3%, snapping a 6 week winning streak.

After 7 straight green weeks on the NDX, Friday was the first real shakeout of this rally.

Let me explain what caused it, why it is healthy, and exactly what I am looking for next. 👇

#### What Caused the Pause

3 things converged last week, and the market finally took notice on Friday.

**1. The PPI bomb.**

April PPI came in at +1.4% MoM on Wednesday, the biggest monthly jump since March 2022 and nearly 3x the +0.5% estimate. YoY PPI hit +6%, the largest increase since December 2022. Core PPI (ex food and energy) jumped +1% MoM vs the +0.4% estimate. This came one day after a hot CPI print (+3.8% YoY). The Iran war and the resulting energy spike are the main drivers, but the inflation pressure is broadening beyond just energy.

**2. Yields ripped to new YTD highs.**

10Y Treasury yields surged 14 bps on Friday alone to 4.59%, its highest level since February 2025. The 30Y jumped 11 bps to 5.12%, a new YTD high. Rate hike odds for 2026 are now sitting near 50%, with a hike fully priced in by March 2027. The Bond Market is no longer buying the “rate cuts are coming” narrative anymore.

**3. The Warsh Handoff.**

Kevin Warsh officially takes over as Fed Chair tomorrow (Monday). He was confirmed on Wednesday in a 54-45 vote. He inherits a difficult setup with a +6% PPI print and a +3.8% CPI print last week. Warsh’s first FOMC meeting is June 16-17. There is some anxiety heading into that meeting. The market has been quietly leaning on Warsh being the “dove Trump wants” who will deliver rate cuts. But the inflation data from last week makes that an extremely difficult hand to play.

The combination of these 3 factors finally broke the vertical rally on Friday.

Semis got hit the hardest after their massive run, with MU -6.6%, AMD -5.7%, INTC -6.2%, and NVDA -4.4% (heading into earnings this week). The biggest winners of the past month (Semis) gave back the most. This is classic profit taking.

#### What I’m Watching For

In my view, the ideal scenario from here is 1-2 weeks of consolidation or a controlled pullback into key support levels. A few sideways digestion weeks would be incredibly constructive. So would an orderly pullback to the 15 EMA on the indices.

I am still bullish on the [big picture summer melt up thesis](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/summer-melt-up) I talked about last week. Nothing in last week’s price action changed that for me. Q1 earnings have been historically strong, the CapEx numbers from Big Tech are insane, and the AI Supercycle thesis remains fully intact (as evidenced by tremendous Q1 earnings).

What changed is the short-term setup. The weekly charts are pointing to some digestion and/or a pullback here. For me, that is a healthy, normal, and expected development. The vertical rally is finally taking its first breath.

Here is exactly what I am watching for this week, where I am looking to deploy capital, the 2 stocks at the top of my watchlist this week, and how I am thinking about NVDA earnings on Wednesday. 👇

#### 2 Stocks I Am Watching This Week

As I mentioned above, I am not looking to enter any new positions in the early part of this week. I believe the market needs to rest, and I want to let the dust settle before deploying more capital.

But below are the 2 names at the top of my watchlist on a greater market pullback (to the 15 EMA, ideally).

Both of these names have the kind of fundamental + technical setup I look for in leading stocks. The earnings reports were exceptional. The price action confirmed the fundamentals. Now all I’m waiting for is the right entry. 👇

**FPS**

FPS is an AI Power Infrastructure leader. The Q3 print last week was a blowout, and it is exactly the kind of report that puts a stock on my watchlist for the next leg of this AI bull run.

**Q3 was a record-breaking quarter across the board.**

Revenue of $378.7M grew +103% YoY, beating consensus by ~14%. Adjusted EBITDA of $85M grew +96% YoY with margins expanding +200 bps sequentially to 22.4%. Adjusted net income of $55M was up +132% YoY. But the real shockers were the bookings. $867M of bookings (+308% YoY), a 2.3x book-to-bill ratio despite record revenue, and a backlog of nearly $2B (+157% YoY).

**Then came the guidance raise.**

FY26 revenue guidance was raised to $1.35-1.39B, implying ~82% revenue growth. EBITDA growth implied at +86%. Net income growth implied at +128%. And the low end of the new guide is above the high end of the prior guide. Q4 EBITDA margins are guided to approach 25%. Management said backlog now gives visibility well into FY27 and beyond.

This is what I want to see from an AI infrastructure name. Demand is so strong that lead times are extending out to 12-15 months. Powertrain Solutions revenue (the higher-value integrated offering) was up +248% YoY and now represents 26% of sales.

The stock initially ripped higher post-earnings last week before pulling back -10% on Friday. But even after Friday’s pullback, the stock is still fairly extended here, and I am not chasing early this week.

**VPG**

VPG is a precision sensing and measurement play with quiet but powerful exposure to the AI infrastructure buildout and humanoid robotics. The Q1 print was a fundamental inflection, and the market treated it as such.

Q1 revenue of $84.4M grew +18% YoY and beat consensus by ~9%. Adjusted EPS of $0.07 vs consensus near breakeven. Gross margin expanded to 39%. But the headline number was bookings of $102.1M, up +26% sequentially, producing a 1.21x book-to-bill (the strongest since 2022). Sensor bookings specifically jumped +29% sequentially with a 1.36x book-to-bill, and Sensor backlog hit the highest level since Q1 2023.

Then came the new long-term model.Management raised its medium-term framework to 8-10% compounded annual organic growth over the next 3 years, with a target operating margin of 14.5-15.5% and EBITDA margin of 18.5-20.5%. Management explicitly tied the long-term model to advanced robotics and humanoid robots, modeling +50% annual growth from a 2025 humanoid revenue base. AI infrastructure, semiconductor equipment, defense, and humanoid robotics are all contributing to the bookings inflection.

This is VPG reframing itself from a slow industrial stock into a secular AI/robotics sensing story (and a leader at that). That kind of fundamental re-rating story is what creates the conditions for sustained momentum moves.

The stock exploded higher off the Q1 earnings print **on ATH volume**, rallying >30% in the 2+ days following. That is the kind of price action and volume that confirms the fundamental story is being repriced in real-time.

But like FPS, VPG is still extended here, and I am not chasing it into a weakening index setup.

#### The Big Picture

This is our first real market pause after a month + of vertical price action. So far, it is normal, healthy, and overdue.

Nothing about last week changes [my big picture summer melt up thesis](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/summer-melt-up). The fundamentals are still there. Q2 earnings have been historically strong. The CapEx numbers from the hyperscalers are not slowing down. The AI Supercycle is fully intact.

The macro noise (hot inflation, rising yields, Warsh’s handoff) is real, but it is also the kind of noise the market needs to digest at this point in the cycle. After 7 straight green weeks and a vertical rally off the late-March lows, a pause is nothing to panic about. More likely, this is the fuel for the next move.

To be clear, I am not getting bearish here. I am not panicking or running for cash. I am cutting my laggards, protecting some profits, mapping out my key support levels below, and getting ready to add more exposure into the right pullback.
