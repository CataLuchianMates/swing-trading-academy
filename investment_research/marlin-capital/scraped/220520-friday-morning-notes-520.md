# Friday Morning Notes 5.20

**Marlin Capital:**

Detailed report will be released this weekend.

---

Yesterday was a choppy day for US stocks, as I thought might be the case with monthly options expiration today. Put sweepers came in heavy on Wednesday expecting continuation to the downside, so it’s only natural we didn’t get any on Thursday. The technical setup remains highly bearish here for the indices. With that said, we are seeing some signs of extreme selling pressure that can lead to a bounce (elevated put/call, “9” reading on extreme fear, and below).

Interesting note - unless the Nasdaq can rally over 4% tomorrow, we are heading for our 7th consecutive down week. We have only seen 8 during the Dot Com Bust and GFC. Yes, we ARE currently in a Bear Market with many parallels to both of those times, as I have pointed out before. However, even in the worst Bear markets of this century, heading into the 8th week was not a time to press shorts for traders. I believe we are in need of a longer duration relief rally to reset some of the balance.
