# Broad Street Rally

The S&P 500 moved back towards record highs to end last week as investors cheered Fed Chair Powell’s Jackson Hole speech.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nTHi!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F81e68932-00f0-4ebf-ab6f-84361b876d01_1135x650.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nTHi!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F81e68932-00f0-4ebf-ab6f-84361b876d01_1135x650.jpeg)

The gains were broad based, with Small Caps outperforming Large Caps and the Equal Weighted S&P 500 index outpacing its Market Cap Weighted counterpart.

In traditional late August fashion, trading activity was light throughout the week (volumes tracked -12% vs 20dma on Fri).  On Friday, stocks jumped following the release of the text of Powell’s speech at Jackson Hole. Some of the key quotes the market reacted to below:

FED’S POWELL: “THE TIME HAS COME FOR POLICY TO ADJUST”

POWELL: “WE DON’T SEEK OR WELCOME FURTHER LABOR MARKET COOLING”

POWELL: “MY CONFIDENCE HAS GROWN THAT INFLATION IS ON PATH TO 2%”

POWELL: “TIMING, PACE OF CUTS TO DEPEND ON DATA, OUTLOOK, RISKS”

This was certainly a dovish tilt by the Fed Chair, as he implied that the time to cut rates has come and left the door open for a 50bps cut in September (instead of 25bps).  He emphasized the downside risks to the labor market rather than upside risks to inflation, making it clear what the Fed’s main priority is now.

5 of the 6 Jackson Hole speeches by Fed Chair Powell saw the SPX drop -7.5%, on average, in the next 3 months.  This is also in line with weak September seasonality.

With that said, this year’s Jackson Hole speech was much different than any of the previous 6 we saw from Powell.  The initial reaction on Friday was a +1.2% rally in the SPX/NDX and a +3.2% rally in the RTY.

Last week, I pointed out the internal strength we were seeing in the market with the Advance-Decline line breaking out to new highs ahead of the major indices.  This broad based strength continued last week, and in my view, is a sign of a very healthy market.  296 SPX stocks are on track to outperform the index in Q3, which would be the most since 2022.
