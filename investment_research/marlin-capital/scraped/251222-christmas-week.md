The week kicked off sharply lower, but reversed course towards the end driven by an encouraging CPI report as well as strong earnings from MU. Micron’s strong EPS report helped positively shift AI sentiment (the group was largely oversold heading into the report).

Last week’s action was nothing new. In fact, the market has been little changed for the last 2 months. With Friday’s close (at $6834), the SPX closed within 0.6% of where it closed on October 24th (2 months ago).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!D-8S!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcaf9db33-8490-442b-8f3f-e6bb5985b659_1566x846.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!D-8S!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcaf9db33-8490-442b-8f3f-e6bb5985b659_1566x846.png)

This is nothing unusual. Even in bull markets, you will have long consolidation periods and directionless chop. In [“September Lull”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/september-lull?utm_source=publication-search), I talked about how I like to handle these long consolidation periods as a trader.

Still, many individual stocks (particularly growthy/speculative names) have gotten clobbered over the last ~2-3 months. As I have been highlighting since October, this type of divergence (speculatives cracking while the indices hold up) is typically late stage Bull Market behavior. In past cycles, the most speculative names have topped 8-14 months before the SPX did. This happened during the Dot Com Bust and the 2022 Covid Bubble/Bust.

**Top Setups for this Week**

First off, some key details for this holiday-shortened week. The US Stock Market is opened for a half day on Wednesday and closed on Thursday:

Christmas Eve (Dec 24): Open, but closes at 1:00 PM EST.

Christmas Day (Dec 25): Closed.

Day After (Dec 26): Normal hours resume.

There are no major economic data or earnings releases this week.

The official “Santa Claus Rally” period begins this week (on Dec 24th). This refers to the last 5 trading days of the year + the first 2 days of January. Historically, the SPX has been up 77% of the time during this period (since 1950).
