# My Top 2 Earnings Setups 

The headline driven action continued for US stocks yesterday. The market initially gapped down big on tariff news, but later turned around on news of tariff delays.

The market rallied big when it was confirmed that the tariffs on Mexico would be delayed by a month, with a similar step then confirmed for Canada shortly after the US equity close. The SPX had been down as much as -2% early in the session, but bounced nicely after the Mexico tariff delay news hit and finished -0.76%.

While the indices are very headline driven right now, the good news is we’re seeing plenty of positive earnings reactions. So far through earnings season, 56% of companies reporting have beat street wide estimates by >1SD (higher than historical avg. of 49%), while 12% of companies have missed estimates by >1SD (slightly less than historical avg. of 13%).

Companies beating estimates by at least 1SD have outperformed the S&P 500 by +135bps on the trading session directly after reporting (vs historical avg. of +102 bps of outperformance). On the other hand, companies missing earnings estimates by >1SD have underperformed the SPX by -429bps (significantly worse than the historical avg. of -211bps).

My focus is always on companies that are beating earnings, specifically the ones raising guidance as well. Then, I look for the most positive Wall Street reactions to these earnings beats, which shows itself in volume and price. So far, we have a couple stocks that really stand out for me.

VIAV

This is my top earnings trade setup that I see emerging right now.  
  
All my key bullish boxes are checked here with the big beat and raise earnings catalyst along with the positive market reaction and huge volume patterns.  
  
Let’s start with the beat and raise quarter. Viavi Solutions delivered FQ2 revenue of $271M (+6.4% YoY, +13.7% QoQ), well above the consensus $260M estimate, while adjusted EPS of $0.13 also beat the $0.10 consensus. They also reported upside surprises in gross margins and operating margins in the quarter.

The company guided for $276M-$278M in FQ3 revenue, well above the consensus calling for $256M (+10% guidance raise). Adjusted EPS guidance of $0.1-$0.13 was also ahead of consensus ($0.09). The company also said it expects adjusted operating margin of 13-15% vs 12% consensus.   
  
**What really stands out for me here is the volume**. On Day 1 of the earnings gap, VIAV saw 17.6M shares traded (10.7x avg daily volume). This was the stock’s highest volume day in 8+ years…since June 2016. For context, the 2nd highest volume day in last 5 years was 11M shares traded (which was the only other day in the last 5 years with 10M+ shares traded).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uXdb!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc0a7c071-991e-408d-81d1-283dc920c746_1114x653.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uXdb!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc0a7c071-991e-408d-81d1-283dc920c746_1114x653.jpeg)

It was also the highest weekly volume in the last 7 years (only 1 other week in the last 5 years with 20M+ shares traded). We also saw big, unusual call buying in VIAV with 25.3x avg call volume on day 1 (9587 calls traded with 41% at ask).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xqjh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8b16297e-84d3-4b43-a883-aca86e78f51c_494x453.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xqjh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8b16297e-84d3-4b43-a883-aca86e78f51c_494x453.png)

As I said, we have price, volume, and fundamentals aligning here for VIAV. I’ll be looking to buy VIAV long here on a pullback.

IBM

Not an exciting stock but it has quietly performed really well and they announced a very good quarter last week. Friday was an inside day and we broke it higher yesterday. We actually didn’t even trade below Friday’s lows at all yesterday which is a good sign of relative strength as most stocks gapped well below Friday’s lows this morning.

Volume has been strong here all 3 days since earnings the relative strength is really standing out for me.
