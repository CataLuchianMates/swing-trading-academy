# On to Q4

The S&P 500 recorded its 41st record close of the year last week (on Thursday). We are now pacing for 53 ATH closes this year.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!U4yG!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F310c59b2-bb74-44e9-bb44-2b1f2831bdef_977x723.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!U4yG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F310c59b2-bb74-44e9-bb44-2b1f2831bdef_977x723.jpeg)

For context, the most ever record closes the SPX has made in a single year was 77 in 1995 (70 in 2021 was the 2nd most).

The market digested a barrage of positive news last week including monetary and fiscal stimulus announcements out of China, positive inflation data in Europe and US, resilient economic growth data in the US, and a great earnings report from chipmaker Micron.

Asia was the most net bought region in Goldman’s Prime Book last week and saw the largest net buying in over 10 years.  Last week’s net buying was driven by long buys and to a much lesser extent short covers (3.8 to 1).

Chinese equities saw the largest weekly net buying on record last week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!x6ZA!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fac7a7b26-b26a-453e-af0a-cf2bfceedca5_1170x701.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!x6ZA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fac7a7b26-b26a-453e-af0a-cf2bfceedca5_1170x701.jpeg)

Following the massive inflows last week, net allocation to Chinese equities is still in the 27th percentile versus the past 5 years (they were both at 5Y lows coming into the week).

Within the US markets, a rotation back into Tech continued last week with the Nasdaq outperforming on the week (+1% vs +0.6% SPX).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!czV1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F81b5fe1d-5060-4ef1-a381-c3a1770784ee_1194x695.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!czV1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F81b5fe1d-5060-4ef1-a381-c3a1770784ee_1194x695.jpeg)

The path of least resistance remains higher from here, in my view.  The Fed is now easing and the US economy has remained resilient, giving investors hope that a soft landing is on the way.

As I have pointed out in the past, historical returns are overwhelmingly positive after the Fed starts cutting so long as the economy can avoid a recession.  A soft landing is the consensus view among big money and market as a whole.  Unless we start to see some conflicting data to shake investors’ confidence, this should remain the prevailing view.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hH1C!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F50d911f9-01c9-4a48-86d5-3bc1e4536e93_725x532.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hH1C!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F50d911f9-01c9-4a48-86d5-3bc1e4536e93_725x532.jpeg)

The max pain trade in the market right now continues to be higher, in my view, led by cyclicals over defensives.  Many investors are currently positioned for a market selloff (underweight stocks) into the November election, and then are bracing for a potential move higher after we know for sure who the next president of the US will be.  In my view, the max pain trade would be for the SPX to make an earlier than expected run towards $6k in October as liquidity dries up into election day (11/5).
