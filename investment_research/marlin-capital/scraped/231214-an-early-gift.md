# An Early Gift

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Stocks rallied big yesterday on Fed’s dovish dot plot (2024 dot fell to 4.6% and 2025 fell to 3.6%).  On net, median of 3 cuts penciled in for 2024 (vs anywhere from 2-6 baked in), which certainly was taken as the green light the market has been looking for to add to rate cut bets and a weaker dollar in the near term.  10 YR yields retraced 18bps and are now sitting at 4.01%.

Small Caps, High Beta Momentum, High Volatility, and most shorted stocks were the star of the show yesterday.  Speaking of small caps, we telegraphed this catch up long trade for TNA 2 weeks ago. Yesterday’s +10.6% daily move was nothing short of incredible. The hourly chart pictured below tells the story.

For those who followed along on my TNA long trade, we are now up +30.4% (as of this morning’s writing) on the last 1/3rd portion.  I will be selling more of my TNA long at this morning’s open and will be keeping around 20-25% of my original long position.  I will be moving up my stop loss to a close below yesterday’s lows ($31.50), and will continue to let the final portion of this trade work while simultaneously raising my stops.  Congrats to those who followed along on this trade!

My other current long position is PATH, with 2 separate entries at $23.93 and $24.67.  The stock gave a shakeout move early in yesterday’s session but closed strong at daily highs.  We are set for a gap above $25 this morning.

My first target on this trade is $27-$30.  I will be moving my stop loss to a close below yesterday’s lows ($22.94).  A quick note on why I prefer using closing stops right now – in the current market environment with a depressed VIX, many of the intraday dips appear to be shakeouts and recover by the close (see PATH yesterday).  I feel more comfortable using closing stops in the current market environment.

Looking closer into yesterday’s epic post-FOMC market meltup, we can see plenty of evidence of a violent short squeeze.  Goldman’s basket of most popular Hedge Fund shorts lost -6% yesterday, its biggest 1-day loss since June 2001.
