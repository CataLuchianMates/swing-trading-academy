## David Marlin Q&A (Comments)

**Q:** Great analysis. Given higher beta tends to front run the S&P bottom, any insight into where these indexes have historically bottomed within the context of this analysis?l (e.g. recession start, when inflation peaks, etc)?

**David Marlin:** Thank you.  I think the best historical comparison for the specific carnage we have seen in higher beta is the Dot Com Bubble and Bust.  I believe certain higher beta stocks have started the process for bottoming in this cycle before the indices, as certain stocks did 12-24 months before the market bottomed back then.  I also now believe the major indices have 1-2 more waves down.  I would not expect higher beta stocks to completely avoid a major market drawdown, but simply, I believe some will not break their lows.  This is why I do not see any type of v-shaped bottom occurring in higher beta, but rather a long basing period.  The pressure on the greater market will weigh on all stocks, capping the potential upside of potential leaders.
