# The Week Ahead

The S&P 500 rallied once again last week, finishing off Friday (+0.59%) at its highest level since August.

This marks 5 straight green weeks in a row for the S&P 500.  The index has not completed 6 straight green weeks in a row this decade.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!95VB!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fafee0390-1f51-42d4-805a-d88fd2a38e71_1364x566.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!95VB!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fafee0390-1f51-42d4-805a-d88fd2a38e71_1364x566.png)

The big theme of last week was “the rest” > “the best”, with the other 493 stocks not in the Magnificent 7 (Mega Cap Tech) outperforming to the tune of ~350 bps.  We caught a piece of this reversion action with our Long TNA trade (3x Small Caps), [entered on Wednesday’s open](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/wednesday-morning-notes-1129) ($28.75).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-KtW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3ca80a2a-5786-4f67-8c5f-15e382b36308_1194x889.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-KtW!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3ca80a2a-5786-4f67-8c5f-15e382b36308_1194x889.jpeg)

We are now up +9% on this long trade.  My targets remain $33-$35 on this run, but I will be taking 1/3rd profits on today’s open.

The ongoing rotation/reversion out of Mega Cap Tech and into Small Caps/others appears to be a function of growing confidence in adding risk to “other stuff”.  As I have covered in detail throughout this year, the outperformance of the Magnificent 7/Mega Cap Tech has been unprecedented.  Other parts of the market finally taking the lead, particularly Small Caps, is a healthy and welcome sight.

Software is now up >20% over the last 25 sessions, one of the best stretches in history driven by ongoing relief on the rates side (US 10 YR 4.22%) + clear signs of fundamental stabilization during recent earnings (SNOW, ESTC, CRM).

FAAMG+ has now underperformed Non-Profitable Tech by 10% over the last 5 sessions, matching the worst 5-day stretch of the year.  Another 5-7% reversion in the spread would put it back into more normal territory over the past few years.

A couple things to be mindful of technically as we head into this week –

1.       The S&P 500 Equal Weighted Index is the most overbought it has been since the summer of 2020 (14d RSI = nearly 75, vs 14d RSI for the Mag 7 of 55).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!898h!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F79b7e12e-bc38-4ac5-a2c3-889c025ecf56_962x718.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!898h!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F79b7e12e-bc38-4ac5-a2c3-889c025ecf56_962x718.jpeg)

2.       We are coming off a period of historical easing.  US Financial Conditions eased ~90 bps in November, the largest monthly easing on record (dating back to 1982).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Nml1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff6d3d792-5be0-4fe6-b715-a3d4c013e67f_1140x681.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Nml1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff6d3d792-5be0-4fe6-b715-a3d4c013e67f_1140x681.jpeg)

November also saw the biggest monthly decline in 10 YR yields since the 2008 GFC (~70 bps retracement).

3.       Sentiment has followed price, as the AAII Bear/Bull Index now shows bearish sentiment is at its lowest level since January of 2018.
