# Wednesday Morning Notes 2.15 

Technical Analysis

The market staged a choppy session yesterday following slightly hotter than expected CPI for January. The S&P 500 closed flat on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3QpY!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0f0c43f-7616-4736-9b4d-708842a75d0d_899x714.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3QpY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0f0c43f-7616-4736-9b4d-708842a75d0d_899x714.jpeg)

No key resistance or support levels were broken in yesterday’s trade. The SPX made a high of $4159 on the day as the $4155-$4160 resistance level mentioned yesterday held up. The low of the day was $4095, as the key $4090-$4100 support level also held up.

We are still within the inside Week candle on the S&P 500 as we head into Wednesday. I will continue to stay patient until the market breaks in one direction. A break below $4060 is bearish. A break above $4176 is bullish.

Other key resistance levels ahead include $4155-$4160.

Key support levels below include $4090-$4100 and $4060.
