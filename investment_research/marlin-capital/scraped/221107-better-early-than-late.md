# Better Early Than Late

**Macro Update**

The Federal Reserve concluded its November meeting by raising its key interest rate by 75 bps to 4%.

In stark contrast to the “Fed Pivot” narrative that had been developing, Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell made his views abundantly clear: there is no pause, and there is certainly no pivot on the horizon.

“It is very premature, in my view, to think about or be talking about pausing our rate hikes. We have a ways to go.”

- Jerome Powell

With Powell’s speech, he put an end to the easing of financial conditions [I discussed last week](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/weve-seen-this-before).  As a result, the recent rally in risk assets flamed out, as financial conditions tightened and yields rose.  To get a taste of the market’s reaction to last week’s FOMC meeting, look no further than the US 2YR treasury yield, which climbed another 25+ bps last week.  This chart continues to tell you most everything one needs to know about prevailing Macro trends.

[Remember](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-market-bottom-checklist), the 2YR yield has peaked before each of the last 6 Bear Market bottoms.  In the prior 6 Bear Markets, the 2 YR yield [fell at least 50 bps](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-market-bottom-checklist) from its 6 month high before the equity market bottomed.  The 2 YR yield rising to new highs is an indicator of more downside to come in equities.

The other key story of the week was the ongoing tightness of the labor market.  The data that came in last week from the labor market gives the Fed the ammo it needs to continue hiking.  The JOLTS Report indicated that there is still an enormous amount of job openings at 10.7M, while Unemployment remains very low at 3.7%.  Payroll wages are still growing at 4.7% YoY, a number the Fed would need to see come down before thinking about pausing.

As I have [discussed in the past](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-market-bottom-checklist), labor market weakness is a prerequisite for a Fed pivot.  The Federal Reserve’s dual mandate outlines 2 key goals: to achieve (1) price stability, (2) and maximum employment.  Prices remain wildly unstable, while the labor market remains unusually strong.  The average Unemployment Rate when the Fed hikes for the last time is 5.7%, which would be a 54% spike higher from October’s number.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Dsm3!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F36940227-2e82-49a0-9eab-2426a1e08df7_1236x615.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Dsm3!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F36940227-2e82-49a0-9eab-2426a1e08df7_1236x615.png)

We saw a number of tech companies announce layoffs this week, including Meta, Stripe, Twitter, Lyft, Opendoor, etc.  Weakness in the labor market will begin to show up in the data in the coming months, but it has not yet to this point.  In my view, it is very premature to think about a Fed pivot until Unemployment spikes significantly.

Earnings are the key topic I keep covering in each report, because I do not believe the consensus 2023 estimates (+7% EPS growth) are achievable.  Analysts are always late to discount the effects of a recession on corporate earnings.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!u6US!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdf144428-dce0-499b-a7f0-6d15d175e772_1024x588.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!u6US!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdf144428-dce0-499b-a7f0-6d15d175e772_1024x588.png)

The point of this chart is - do not rely on sell side analyst estimates to be accurate (or close to accurate) with a recession on the horizon.  Few analysts want to be the lone wolf going against the popular crowd, but once consensus starts to flip, the EPS cuts will come quick. The forward indicators continue to suggest that consensus EPS estimates remain too high.  Morgan Stanley’s leading EPS indicator is pointing to negative EPS growth next year, while earnings revision breadth is already deeply negative.

**Technical Update**

The 200 week EMA is a long term support level of [great significance that I have discussed in the past](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-pendulum-swings-both-ways).  For the S&P 500, the 200 week EMA support was tested last week.  With Friday’s bounce, the support level held up (for now), and the index closed the week off its lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Oc8q!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F50e8c820-bfeb-4091-a657-eae5cbfde734_1031x651.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Oc8q!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F50e8c820-bfeb-4091-a657-eae5cbfde734_1031x651.png)

The 200 week EMA ($3685-$3700) will remain the key long term level to watch in the coming weeks.  In my view, a decisive break and weekly close below $3685 will begin the next wave down.  My target on the next wave down is $2900-$3300.

As we head into this week, keep an eye on the following key resistance and support levels.  I discussed the long term importance of the $3685-$3700 level above.  On the upside, look for resistance to come in the $3800-$3850 region.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7gE-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F91d644d9-c98b-4f01-8355-93ef071ee9ce_1365x649.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7gE-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F91d644d9-c98b-4f01-8355-93ef071ee9ce_1365x649.png)

In the short term, choppy action may continue as bulls and bears fight it out near the 200 week.  As a swing trader, I’ll happily sit on the sidelines during such market action and wait for superior opportunities to emerge.

The QQQ has been the weaker index this year. I have [discussed my view](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/weve-seen-this-before) that big tech is vulnerable to more multiple multiple compression and EPS cuts, which would point to that weakness continuing.

On a technical level, the QQQ has key support at the $260 level. A break below $260 is likely to begin the next wave down (and would also likely correlate with the SPX dropping below $3685, as discussed above). The short term resistance to watch on the QQQ is $268-$270.
