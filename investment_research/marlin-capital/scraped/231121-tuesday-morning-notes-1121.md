# Tuesday Morning Notes 11.21

**Morning Market Commentary**

The S&P 500 continued higher to kick off this holiday shortened week yesterday, finishing +0.74% on the day.  Trading volumes were light and flows were reflective of a quieter session ahead of Thanksgiving.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!CBCm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffad27b78-1e02-4d32-a75b-ffae00baf98a_977x723.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!CBCm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffad27b78-1e02-4d32-a75b-ffae00baf98a_977x723.jpeg)

With yesterday’s move, the NDX eclipsed new YTD highs on the 2-year anniversary of its ATH close (Nasdaq has not closed at all-time highs in 2 years).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-PvQ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9af9aafa-7f1e-422b-9585-33c3242130b2_959x711.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-PvQ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9af9aafa-7f1e-422b-9585-33c3242130b2_959x711.jpeg)

The action was led by Most Short +174bps, Non-Profitable Tech +160bps, and China Internet +343bps.

All eyes are on NVDA earnings post close today.  Investors are looking for another beat and raise quarter (questions on how big of a beat is needed with the stock at all-time highs going in).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WlIE!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F89e4b23f-26b0-4b8d-a345-5f5d3626b706_981x718.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WlIE!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F89e4b23f-26b0-4b8d-a345-5f5d3626b706_981x718.jpeg)

As a benchmark for magnitude, NVDA beat data center consensus revs by 10-25% in the last 2 quarters – this quarter, consensus sits at ~$12.7B for data center revenues.

Quick update on CTAs – GS no longer models them of buyers in S&P (although they are still buyers of global equities in most scenarios).  GS models that CTAs have net bought $40B in S&P over the past 5 trading sessions (and are now long $31.8B S&P).
