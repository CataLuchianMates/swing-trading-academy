# The Week Ahead

US stocks retreated on Friday with the S&P 500 finishing -0.5% on the day.  Technically, the index bounced off its 200 day moving average support level (~$4200) for a multi-day rally and has now run into resistance at the 60 EMA ($4380).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!glu1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd3bff2b9-d7a8-4aa6-a195-d0c08da84f33_948x708.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!glu1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd3bff2b9-d7a8-4aa6-a195-d0c08da84f33_948x708.jpeg)

So far, the SPX has been unable to break through its 60 EMA despite multiple attempts last week.  $4350 and $4370-$4380 are the key resistance levels to watch on the SPX as we head into the week of 10/16.

With all the moving parts last week (geopolitical conflict in the Middle East, warm CPI print, drop in consumer confidence, sharp moves higher in Crude & Gold, etc.), the QQQ grinded out a small weekly gain of +0.16%.  The market continues to treat big tech as a flight to safety / quality vehicle (for comparison, non-profitable tech was -3% on the week).

Technically, the QQQ rally stalled out at $374 last week before pulling back Thursday and Friday (Friday -1.26% daily close).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!d_l3!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5735c95a-8bad-42d0-8932-a94a6458a375_961x723.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!d_l3!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5735c95a-8bad-42d0-8932-a94a6458a375_961x723.jpeg)

With that said, the QQQ remains above its 21 and 60 EMA resistance levels as we head into the week of 10/16 (clearly painting a stronger technical picture than that of the SPX).

In a positive vein, Bank earnings got off to a strong start late last week with Citi, Wells Fargo, PNC, and JPM all delivering decent credit & capital.  JPM and WFC had respective 50 and 30 bps of capital accretion in the quarter vs flat expectation.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Mc0g!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F52df2259-c29b-4e1b-88f6-973c0cb9d5a2_1032x776.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Mc0g!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F52df2259-c29b-4e1b-88f6-973c0cb9d5a2_1032x776.jpeg)

Earnings have become the focus now with so much economic data now behind us.  We head into the week of 10/16 with a huge slate of earnings ahead – JNJ, ASML, MS, TSLA, NFLX, TSMC, ISRG, AXP, etc.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SA5l!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2ce7604f-9104-4805-bbed-fc0197244a34_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SA5l!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2ce7604f-9104-4805-bbed-fc0197244a34_1920x1080.jpeg)

Q2 is expected to have marked the trough in S&P 500 earnings with Q3 bringing some stabilization.  Big picture, GS is calling for 1% EPS growth this year, 5% EPS growth in 2024, and 5% EPS growth in 2025.  Given these estimates and the recent market correction, the backdrop heading into Q3 earnings season is more compelling than Q2.

Seasonally, things are looking up as we head into the 2nd half of October.  The historical path would suggest some more consolidation into November before a strong year-end rally.  November and December are 2 of the best months for the SPX historically.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!e3HG!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc65e649d-62b1-4be6-a4ae-da03bffa0851_1127x793.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!e3HG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc65e649d-62b1-4be6-a4ae-da03bffa0851_1127x793.jpeg)

Positioning – flows are inflecting towards the more positive side.  GS Prime Book suggests that the CTA community was carrying a record short position in global equity index futures.  Their recent selling appears to be done and the recent bounce to higher prices is bringing new buying demand.

On the discretionary side, GS Prime reported and increase in single stock shorts for 10 straight weeks.  Needless to say, fund flows have been a net negative for the market the past few months.  With that said, there are some potential catalysts for flows to become incrementally more positive from here (with the big one being earnings).
