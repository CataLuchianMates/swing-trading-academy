But the real story wasn’t the rally itself. It was what happened on Thursday.

On Wednesday night, I wrote [“Whiplash”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/whiplash) and told you to watch very closely how the market reacts to Trump’s re-escalation speech. I laid out 2 scenarios. 👇

**The Bull Case:** the S&P 500 does NOT make new lows on the re-escalation news. That would signal something very important about the underlying bid in this market, a possible change of character.

**The Bear Case:** we gap down hard and follow through to the downside, eventually taking out Tuesday’s lows.

On Thursday, we got our first positive sign. At least for now.

The SPX gapped down ~1% at the open following Trump’s speech. The QQQ gapped down -1.8%. Oil was surging above $104. The headlines were as bearish as they have been in weeks.

And the dip was bought.

The S&P 500 closed +0.1%. The QQQ closed +0.1%. Both indices erased their entire gap down losses and finished green on the day.

That is new and notable.

For the first time during this entire selloff, the market absorbed a major escalatory headline and refused to break lower. As I talked about in [“Whiplash”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/whiplash), in a headline-driven market, your job is not to predict the headlines. Your job is to stay nimble and react to the market’s reaction. In my view, Thursday’s reaction was the most constructive thing we have seen in months.

Does this mean we have bottomed? Not necessarily. We are still below the 200-day SMA on both the SPX and Nasdaq. We are still technically in a downtrend.

But the early signs are starting to improve. And here is why. 👇

**Revisiting My 2 Conditions**

In [“Capitulation Watch”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/capitulation-watch?utm_source=publication-search), I laid out the 2 things I am watching before deploying long-term capital.

1. Headlines start to meaningfully improve (particularly on Iran/oil, which is the primary driver of this selloff)
2. We reach signs of genuine capitulation selling (positioning reaching at least -1 standard deviation AND >40% of SPX stocks hitting oversold levels)

Let’s walk through where we stand on each. 👇

**Condition #1: Improving Newsflow**

Early last week, we got our first real signs of the market beginning to move past the Iran conflict. Trump announced a 5-day pause on military strikes against Iranian energy infrastructure. Oil prices dropped. Credit conditions started to stabilize. The SPX rallied +2.9% on Monday alone.

Then came Wednesday’s re-escalation speech. This was a golden opportunity for bears to take back control after a -1% gap down. But the market shrugged it off.

This is the key development. In my view, the market is starting to react less to the Iran headlines. And for traders like us, that is one of the most important things to watch in a headline-driven environment. When bad news stops producing bad price action, that tells you something is beginning to shift underneath the surface.

Is this confirmed? No, not yet. But the trend in headline reactivity is improving, and that is significant.

This weekend, Trump tweeted up a storm on Iran, including this doozy. 👇

Oil futures just crossed $115. And yet, as of this publishing, /ES and /NQ futures are flat (slightly red). A week ago, we likely would have seen futures down -2% on news like this.

In my mind, headline fatigue is clearly setting in. This is a bullish sign for the market.

**Condition #2: Signs of Capitulation**

We did not get full-blown capitulation selling like we saw during last April’s Liberation Day meltdown. But we did get something significant. 👇

According to Goldman Prime, hedge funds dumped stocks at the fastest pace in 13 years during March.

[![Image](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!miCZ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F31eb167f-06f0-4277-a047-753adf43bcc6_1226x522.jpeg "Image")](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!miCZ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F31eb167f-06f0-4277-a047-753adf43bcc6_1226x522.jpeg)

Faster than COVID.

Faster than the 2022 Bear Market.

Faster than Liberation Day.

Only March 2013 was worse.

That is a meaningful data point. Big money was aggressively de-risking throughout March, and that kind of massive selling is exactly what creates the conditions for a sustainable bottom (aka capitulation selling).

To be clear, total positioning did not reach the extreme levels we saw last April (when Goldman’s Positioning Indicator hit -2.5z). As I covered in [“Heading Into April”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/heading-into-april), the indicator moved to -0.9z (signaling underweight positioning). That is a meaningful move, but it is still not at the -1.5 to -2 standard deviation levels that have historically marked major SPX bottoms.

But here is the important context. Last April was a -20% SPX drawdown. This time around, if we bottomed on Tuesday, the SPX drawdown only hit -9.9%. That is not even an official -10% correction (though the Nasdaq did hit -13%). In other words, if we have indeed hit the bottom here, this was nothing more than a routine market correction that occurs in most years (63% of all years historically). A routine correction does not require the same level of capitulation as a -20% meltdown.

**The Dot Com Comparison**

I keep coming back to the Dot Com Bubble comparison because I believe it remains the best parallel to our current market cycle. We are currently in a generational technology supercycle that nothing in recent history comes close to (outside of the Internet boom).

As I talked about in [“Hard Penny”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/hard-penny?utm_source=publication-search), during the Dot Com Bubble, the first real selloff in 1999 (following the 1998 correction, which I have compared to Liberation Day) ended up being a bear trap. The SPX broke below its 200-day SMA but was bid back up to new highs. The bears who pressed their shorts got squeezed, and the market ripped to 1 final high before the real top came.

**The Big Picture**

Thursday was the most notable day of this entire selloff for me. For the first time, we got a major negative Iran headline and the market refused to break. And now, despite Trump’s Iran tweets all weekend, futures are barely flinching. This is a possible change of character.

In my view, things are starting to look up after a -13% Nasdaq and -9.9% SPX drawdown. The headline reactivity is fading. Hedge funds have already de-risked at the fastest pace in 13 years. And the Dot Com bear trap thesis remains in play.

But to be clear, the major indices are still below their 200-day SMAs and technically in a downtrend. A counter-trend bounce to the 200 SMA that fails would be textbook bear market behavior. So, until we break those key resistance levels, the market is still in no man’s land (and I will remain cautious).

The coming days will tell us a lot. Stay nimble.
