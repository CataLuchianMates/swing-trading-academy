# Wednesday Morning Notes 1.3

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Investors returned to the office for the new year with a bit of trading apathy as equities kicked off 2024 in the red.  The S&P 500 finished the day -0.57% (though still within ~2% of ATHs).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ricm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F119b2f38-a915-4174-927b-08ed434a98e1_945x711.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ricm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F119b2f38-a915-4174-927b-08ed434a98e1_945x711.jpeg)

There was a pronounced bid for defensives (cyclicals outperformed by 124 bps) and select 2023 laggards (Biotech +105 bps, Rate Sensitive Financials +88 bps, CRE +63 bps) vs some pretty ugly moves lower across 2023 winners (Software -3.9%, AI -2.9%, Non-Profitable Tech -2.9%, and Mag 7 -2%).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FYy2!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feb089d6c-75aa-43f2-92ad-e89575da3f66_1135x1374.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FYy2!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feb089d6c-75aa-43f2-92ad-e89575da3f66_1135x1374.jpeg)

On the downside, AAPL (-3.6%) had its worst day in over a year following the Barclays downgrade yesterday morning which sparked some Hedge Fund trims in Mega Cap Tech.

While tough to point to a singular reason for yesterday's sharp move lower, clearly there are a couple of factors at work (1) global yields inching higher (US 10 YR +6bps at 3.9%), (2) geopolitical uncertainty mounting, (3) calendar turning to 2024 (some profit taking pushed out into 2024 for tax reasons), and (4) overbought dynamics into year-end (see NDX RSI > 70 mid-last week).

In my view, patience is the right strategy here as the indices shake off last week’s overbought readings. This will allow more stocks to setup and potentially present better trading opportunities for us moving forward.
