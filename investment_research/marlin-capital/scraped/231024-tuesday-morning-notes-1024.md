# Tuesday Morning Notes 10.24

The S&P 500 finished lower by -0.17% on Monday.  10 YR yields retreated back to 4.84% after briefly pushing through 5.0% in the early morning, allowing stocks to catch a bid after the morning dump.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xN9k!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa5281702-9f66-4054-a4d5-1e34fac9c401_964x724.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xN9k!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa5281702-9f66-4054-a4d5-1e34fac9c401_964x724.jpeg)

Technically, the SPX remains below its 200 day moving average after breaching the level for the first time in 7 months last week. The $4235-$4250 region (200 SMA) acted as resistance yesterday and is a key level to watch moving forward.

There was some clear short covering action in equities (post HF Mgr Bill Ackman tweeting that he covered his treasury short), which eventually faded during the last hour of the trading day.  The QQQ finished slightly higher on the day (+0.3%), making its 16th consecutive positive Monday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MNCF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0d8cb3c8-681f-4e73-b36c-7799576d7a90_971x724.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MNCF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0d8cb3c8-681f-4e73-b36c-7799576d7a90_971x724.jpeg)

Monday’s price action will now take a back seat to what we will see later in the week (beginning today), with 39% of the S&P 500 set to report earnings.  Mega Cap tech will dictate the overall health of Q3 earnings and should act as a directional catalyst for the general market.  Microsoft and Google are set to report earnings after today’s market close.
