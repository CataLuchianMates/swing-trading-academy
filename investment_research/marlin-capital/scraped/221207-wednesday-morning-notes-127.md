# Wednesday Morning Notes 12.7

Technical Analysis

The SPX continued its slide yesterday, breaking the $4000 level and several key support levels in the process. On the daily, we saw the first bearish MACD cross since this rally began.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zlc0!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F33875f84-ffa9-409e-964a-b36c96292726_971x728.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zlc0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F33875f84-ffa9-409e-964a-b36c96292726_971x728.jpeg)

The key support level to watch now is the $3905 level. If price breaks below, we will have broken all my key moving averages to the downside (200, 21, and 60). This is the type of confirmation I am looking for before entering short.

After 3 straight red days, an oversold bounce is also possible. Look for resistance at $3960, $3980, and $4000.
