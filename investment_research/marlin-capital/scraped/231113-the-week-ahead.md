# The Week Ahead

The S&P 500 rose for a 2nd straight week last week and closed above $4400 for the first time since 9/20.

Value stocks once again underperformed growth stocks, pushing the outperformance divergence back to extreme levels.

Sentiment has rebounded intensely with the recent market rally.  According to the AAII sentiment survey, bulls are now at 43% (versus 24% last week) and Bears are now at 27% (versus 50% last week).

The 1-week positive change in the Bull-Bear spread is the largest since January 2009 (2 months before the GFC Market bottom).  Nothing like price to change sentiment.

Positioning – hedge funds have increased their short stock positions for 14 consecutive weeks, the longest streak recorded in GS Prime’s history.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ocn4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F46dd2e96-4b65-4abb-93c4-e1988ba652ca_1284x927.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ocn4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F46dd2e96-4b65-4abb-93c4-e1988ba652ca_1284x927.jpeg)

The target of these single stock shorts appear to be non-profitable tech/already highly shorted names.  Hedge Fund VIP longs versus Most Short just experienced one of its best weeks ever, indicating that funds continue to play what’s working (long Mega Cap/AI winners, short low quality/non-profitable tech).  Bottom line, crowded longs are outperforming while crowded shorts are underperforming.

We have a busy week ahead of Macro Data with CPI Tuesday, PPI/Retail Sales Wednesday, 20 Fed Speakers, and consumer retail earnings (HD Tues, TGT & TJX Weds, WMT & M Thurs).

My take – The data point I continue to hang my hat on is the labor market/unemployment rate.  While we saw a tick up in last month’s data, it is not yet a level to cause fear in the economy/stock market.

We will get a glimpse of the strength of the consumer this week with retail sales and earnings, but for now, the story is much the same as it has been throughout 2023.  That is, the economy remains resilient while smart money/big money mis-times the “big sell event”, thereby causing another major short squeeze in equities.  Positioning is as much a factor in these violent snapback rallies as anything, and once again “smart money” was positioned offsides for this one.

The bigger picture – The S&P 500 closed Friday less than 1% from the average closing price of the past 6 months ($4365). Plenty of pain has been dished out to both sides along the way, but not a lot of progress has been made by bulls or bears. Until the labor market deteriorates more meaningfully, we will likely have some stability in equities. With that said, I would be a seller (not a shorter) of stocks into September-August highs ($4500-$4550) based off my greater macro view.
