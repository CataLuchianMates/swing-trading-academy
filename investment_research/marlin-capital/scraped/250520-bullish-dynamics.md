# Bullish Dynamics

Stocks are taking a bit of a breather so far this week after moving +5% higher last week. The SPX and NDX finished slightly positive yesterday, reversing off morning lows after Moody’s stripped the US of its top credit rating.

Most notably, we saw big inflows from retail traders yesterday morning. According to JPM, retail traders aggressively bought the dip yesterday morning, purchasing $4.1B worth of US stocks by 12:130 PM. This marks the largest intraday retail inflow on record.

Meanwhile, as I talked about yesterday, institutional investors (both discretionary and systematic) remain underweight US stocks here. Systematic equity positioning (CTAs + Vol Control + Risk Parity) is currently in the 16th percentile versus history.

As I talked about yesterday, this is a bullish dynamic. Households remain buyers here. Institutions are underweight US stocks, and are therefore much more likely to be increasing exposure. We saw them begin to chase last week.

Remember, at its core, the stock market is an auction that fluctuates because of supply and demand. When demand for a stock (buyers wanting to purchase) is stronger than supply (sellers wanting to sell), the price goes up. When supply exceeds demand, the price goes down.

Right now, we are simply seeing more buying demand than selling demand (supply) across the board. We still have significant buying demand from retail/households, as mentioned above. In my view, we are also likely to see continued buying demand from institutions (as we saw last week), because they are still underweight (and have been for this whole rally). Putting it all together, I see these positioning dynamics as very bullish for the market. I remain bullish here.
