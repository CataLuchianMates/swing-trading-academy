## David Marlin Q&A (Comments)

**Q:** What metrics do you use to define if ARKK is in uptrend or downtrend?

**David Marlin:** Longer term, and in this case for ARKK, I will use the 40 week EMA.  Very first step for ARKK breaking this downtrend would be a weekly close above the 40w EMA.  Additionally, we know that stocks don’t move up or down in a straight line.  There are pullbacks, bounces, etc.  In a defined uptrend, a stock will pullback, but it should always pivot at higher lows than the previous pivot, and it should go on to make higher highs.  In a downtrend, the opposite is true.  A stock will bounce, but each pivot point should be lower than the last, meaning each bounce tops at a lower price before it goes on to make new lows. In this case, currently the most recent lower high for ARKK is at $126.
