# The Week Ahead

While US stocks have now finished higher in 12 of 13 weeks, last week was a bit of a listless one as the NDX did not register a daily move >55bps (+/-) as things took a bit of directional pause ahead of a big slate of earnings coming this week.

So far through Q4 earnings season, we have seen some concerns start to show (though we will get a much clearer picture this week with Mega Cap results).  Overall, Q4 EPS revision for the S&P 500 have declined -11% from 4 months ago.  Outside of the Magnificent 7, Q4 EPS revisions for the S&P 493 have declined -15%.

Overall, 123 SPX companies have reported Q4 results (30% of total market cap and 32% of total market cap is reporting this week).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tS2f!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdf7f0e7b-10a0-470f-b64c-3726ef527f4b_1248x1764.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tS2f!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdf7f0e7b-10a0-470f-b64c-3726ef527f4b_1248x1764.jpeg)

54% of companies reporting have beat street wide earnings estimated by 1 Std Dev (significantly higher than the historical average of 48%).  Only 10% of companies have missed estimates by 1 Std dev (less than historical average of 13%).  Companies beating estimates by 1 Std Dev have outperformed the SPX by +82bps on the trading session directly after reporting (vs historical avg of +98bps of outperformance).  Companies missing estimates by at least 1 Std dev have underperformed the SPX by -157bps (vs historical avg of -211bps of underperformance).

Some recent notables - TXN guided March revs -12% YoY (revs now >30% below peak with signs of Autos getting worse).  STMicro cut numbers well below the street due to a significant inventory correction in industrials.  In Materials/Chem, DuPont had its worst day in >15 years calling out continued industrials + China weakness.

With all this negativity out there at this point and US ISM Manufacturing Data now sitting below 50 for 14 consecutive months (and counting, the longest stretch since 2008), the dialogue has started to probe towards a potential 2nd half recovery (with perhaps some interplay from the election as well).

On AI – last week was upbeat with ServiceNow striking an optimistic tone on Generative AI.  NOW said that their Gen AI has “outsold any other new introduction we put into the marketplace” with management pointing to the Gen AI driving more C-level executive engagements & meetings (and because of that, driving faster time to close on deals) – i.e., NOW noted “if you’re improving productivity, 40%, 50%, it just sells itself”.  Furthering this point, IBM (their consulting book of Gen AI biz doubled Q/Q) and SAP (“Generative AI is the greatest opportunity since the rise of the cloud, especially for SAP) both touted the AI hype in their own companies.

Heading into the January FOMC meeting, the market is priced for a 50% chance of a rate cut at the next meeting in March.  The FOMC will likely aim to keep a March cut on the table without sending a decisive signal by removing the outdated hiking bias from its statement and noting that future policy changes will depend on incoming inflation and other data.

FAAMG set ups into earnings – recent history suggests the market’s reaction to these earnings is a coin flip.  For example, AMZN has traded lower on 9 of its last 13 EPS prints.  GOOGL has traded lower on 5 of 7 prints.  META has traded lower on 6 of 10.  MSFT has traded lower on 3 of 5. TSLA has traded lower on 5 of 6 prints (including last week’s).  Since January 2021, the returns for MAGMA are nearly identical if you included the earnings reactions or not.  For example, if you held FAAMG since Jan ’21, you would be up 56pts on the positions.  Yet, if you “sat out” each earnings print, you would still be up ~51pts on the positions.

Positioning – AMZN (GS Prime has positioning at 9/10) > MSFT (9/10) > META (8.5/10) > GOOGL (7.5/10) > AAPL (7/10)

AAPL – sentiment skews more cautious with some debate about ongoing concerns given China macro, offset by upshoots from Vision Pro, AI optimism, and potentially accelerating services growth (price increases).

META – positioning skews more long.  Investors expect a clean beat (Q4 revs guide $37-40B) and a March guide at least inline for Q1 revs.  All about AI + capex/opex trajectory + Temu/China E-commerce.

MSFT – Investors expect a nice beat on Azure in Dec qtr (vs guide 26-27% YoY).  Focus on Office Co-Pilot adoption trends, 1H FY Azure outlook and EPS momentum/revisions to support the stock given PE multiple has now expanded to >10yr highs

GOOGL – easily the most debated FAAMG stock with investors expecting a clean topline (acceleration in Search + YouTube) coupled with much more debate on risks to Q4 Op Income.  Lots of moving parts in 2024 including regulation, AI positioning, cost cutting, CFO transition, etc.

AMZN – consensus long into this week’s print.  This print feels like the least controversial in years as bulls are looking for signs of a AWS acceleration into Q1 and continue EBIT revisions higher.
