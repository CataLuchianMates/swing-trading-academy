# Wednesday Morning Notes 11.29

**Daily Market Commentary:**

US stocks staged another choppy session on Tuesday with the S&P 500 finishing +0.1% on the day.  We are now just over 10% off the October lows, but relatively flat for the last 4 sessions.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!71__!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3d11a461-1e30-4ba9-a60e-162c9eff537d_1200x892.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!71__!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3d11a461-1e30-4ba9-a60e-162c9eff537d_1200x892.jpeg)

Technically, some consolidation/rest in the indices is healthy and much needed here.  With the indices resting, we saw some squeezy action in the lower quality pockets of the market – non-profitable tech +2.9%, liquid most short +2.3%, and memes 2.1% led the way yesterday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hRfq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7305b0d1-903d-454e-b6cb-a0972ac2fd2d_1172x622.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hRfq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7305b0d1-903d-454e-b6cb-a0972ac2fd2d_1172x622.jpeg)

With the indices stalling a bit over the past 4 sessions, we are hearing more conversations about whether the recent move in equities had run its course (NDX RSI almost in overbought territory), especially against a “higher for longer” environment and lofty valuations.

Fed Speaker Waller was optimistic in his comments yesterday, giving credence to the market’s assumptions around 2024 rate cuts and removing (at least for now) the higher for longer overhang.  During his commentary, Waller noted he has been encouraged “by what we have learned in the past few weeks – something appears to be giving, and it’s the pace of the economy”.  Waller went on to say that there “could still be a shock that throws a soft landing off course, but that cannot be forecasted.  Absent a shock there is no reason why the Fed cannot pull off a soft landing”.

The equity markets are way ahead of Waller on these comments, with 4 rate cuts being priced in to 2024 along with the consensus “soft landing” view for the global economy.  In the Fixed Income market, the 2YR Treasury rallied 52 bps in the last month, which is one of the strongest monthly moves in the last decade.  10 YR yields, on the other hand, are now at 4.33% (2 month lows).

Positioning – the chart below shows how crowded the long NDX (winners) vs short R2K (losers) trade is right now within the fundamental L/S hedge fund community.  The current 44% YTD performance gap between NDX and R2K is on pace to set records.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mG8h!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa2cdcefc-6575-4070-99a9-137806fd789a_713x579.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mG8h!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa2cdcefc-6575-4070-99a9-137806fd789a_713x579.jpeg)

In my view, this performance gap will be hard to sustain into year end and we are likely to see some reversion/outperformance from R2K names.

Long Trade Idea: We have a potential catch up trade brewing in TNA (3x small cap ETF) with a bullish looking flag/consolidation setup after the recent rally.  A decisive break above $29 sets us up nicely for a continuation move higher to the $33-$35 region.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xbcu!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F41d628d1-7fa1-4ba2-878b-2499d8a5af36_1188x885.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xbcu!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F41d628d1-7fa1-4ba2-878b-2499d8a5af36_1188x885.jpeg)

I will be entering TNA long today for this catch up trade, with a closing stop below recent flag lows ($27.5).
