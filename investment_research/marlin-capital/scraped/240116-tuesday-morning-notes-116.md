# Tuesday Morning Notes 1.16

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The choppy market action to begin 2024 continued last Friday with the S&P 500 closing +0.08% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!asrb!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5cd8a51d-ed94-4bd9-9077-8c29e0edf0d4_965x726.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!asrb!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5cd8a51d-ed94-4bd9-9077-8c29e0edf0d4_965x726.jpeg)

Here’s my take on the price action so far in 2024:

The market entered the year very overbought after a furious year-end rally to end 2023 (specifically in lower quality/highly shorted names).  Now, the indices are having some indigestion concerning the path of the 2024 Fed easing cycle.  Fed Funds futures have gyrated between pricing in a 25bps cut in March and pricing one out.  This all came alongside a drumbeat of negativity within the sell side analyst community towards certain areas of Mega Cap Tech (specifically AAPL).  Over the last week, the market finally found its footing with NVDA and AI stocks leading the way (just as they did last year).

We are set for a small gap down in the indices to kick off this holiday shortened week.  For the SPX, key support levels to watch include $4760, $4720-$4730, and $4700.

Strategy wise, I believe patience is the right play here for me.  Earnings season kicks off this week with a slate of bank stocks earnings coming out (GS, PNC, SCHW, etc.).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!01cP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F73c5ccb1-2849-4fcb-a17d-4033db0310c2_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!01cP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F73c5ccb1-2849-4fcb-a17d-4033db0310c2_1920x1080.jpeg)

Earnings gapper setups will be my focus for the next few weeks as these tend to be some of my favorite low risk/high reward trade setups.  For newer subscribers, please read “[Earnings Gapper Trade Setup](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-earnings-gapper-trade-setup)” to learn more about my day 1 entry strategy on these setups.  I will be calling out my favorites on the daily morning notes as they come along.
