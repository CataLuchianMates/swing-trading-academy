# Tuesday Morning Notes 1.23

**Daily Market Commentary:**

US Stocks extended last week’s rally with a reshuffle at the top of the board yesterday with the lowest quality sleeves leading the way higher: Non-Profitable Tech +3.6% (-9.6% YTD), Most Short +3% (-8.3% YTD), and Low Profit R2K +2.8% (-5.5% YTD).  These pockets each have plenty of room to keep squeezing if the SPX/QQQ starts to rest/consolidate here.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6v3W!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa8f88863-ddb1-49b7-9097-ac050db7d55e_970x724.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6v3W!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa8f88863-ddb1-49b7-9097-ac050db7d55e_970x724.jpeg)

Retail community engagement was also extremely elevated yesterday.  Reminder – we will also see corporate repurchase demand coming back online after this week (buybacks).  The pulse of yesterday among funds was to cover shorts (aka reduce gross exposure which is currently sitting near 5 year highs, according to GS PB) and take some profits in Mega Cap Tech ahead of NFLX and TSLA earnings this week.

Friday was the 1st time in history that the S&P 500 made a fresh ATH while the Russell 2000 is still in a bear marker (>20% off highs).  Yesterday, we saw the R2K outperform (+2.1% vs SPX +22bps / Nasdaq +9 bps).  It feels like Small Caps have real room to run as Nasdaq had outperformed R2K 10 consecutive trading sessions.

What happened following the 3 previous largest Russell 2000 drawdowns when the S&P 500 was at a record high? Both indices would rally higher over the next year with the R2K outperforming and joining the S&P 500 at all time highs.

1)      April 7, 1999 (-19.2% R2K drawdown): SPX gained +14.3% over the next year while R2K gained +36.5%

2)      February 13, 1991 (-13.5% R2k drawdown): SPX gained +12.1% over the next year while R2K gained +35.5%

3)      January 21, 1985 (-13.3% R2k drawdown): SPX gained +17.4% over the next year while R2K gained +18.2%

This will continue to be a heavily micro focused week ahead with 24% of the S&P 500 reporting this week + the EPS bar is set low.  NFLX, ISRG, and TXN headline the group of companies reporting after today’s closing bell.
