# Clearing Event

US stocks are rallying so far this morning following President Trump’s announcement of a ceasefire agreement between Israel and Iran (though both sides appear to have violated the ceasefire already). Markets do not seem particularly worried that this will lead to an escalation of the conflict, however, and the early action appears to clearly be signaling the worst is in the rearview mirror.

Equities and Oil are both confirming this view. Crude oil sold off -8% yesterday along with Energy stocks (-2.5%). Oil is seeing additional weakness this morning, falling towards $66/bbl. The bottom line is that markets are signaling Iranian oil supply will not be disrupted.

Yesterday, the market rallied following Iran’s counterattack (which was telegraphed to the US). This was seen as a benign retaliation by Iran and markets clearly viewed this as a clearing event/de-escalatory strike (more of a symbolic retaliation). The NYT reported that Iran coordinated the attacks on US bases with Qatari officials in order to minimize casualties. Trump confirmed this, saying that “no Americans were harmed, and hardly any damage was done. 14 missiles were fired - 13 were knocked down, and 1 was “set free" because it was heading in a nonthreatening direction.”

Overall, this is probably the best case scenario for US stocks and the price action is confirming this. As far as the US stock market is concerned, the worst of this conflict is very likely in the rearview mirror.

The big Micro news of the day yesterday was Tesla, which accounted for ~25% of the NDX’s +1.06% gain on the session with the stock rallying +8%. The TSLA rally was due to the Robotaxi launch day in Austin. Elon Musk confirmed that customers were charged a $4.20 flat fee per ride.
