Last week’s Small Cap outperformance event was catalyzed by Thursday’s CPI release, which showed headline prices falling 0.1% in June.  In the wake of the CPI report, Small Caps outperformed Large Caps by over 300bps.  The Russell 2000 finished up over 3% on Thursday while the S&P 500 finished negative on the day.  The only other day that happened was back on 10/10/2008.

The Fed Funds Futures market is now pricing in >90% probability of a rate cut in September, which is seen as a big tailwind for Small Caps moving forward.

Indeed, as I have highlighted in past reports, Small Caps tend to outperform in the 6-12 months after the first Fed rate cut.  This is not totally surprising, as Small Caps are the most rate sensitive area of the market and stand to benefit the most from falling interest rates.

Q2 earnings are underway.  From my perspective, the bar feels quite high with many Tech names arguably approaching “already priced in” territory (meaning EPS/Rev beats are already priced in ahead of the prints).

Q1 earnings were pretty solid across the board and set a high bar for this quarter.  Moreover, many Tech stocks are up meaningfully from Q1 prints (NDX up +20% since the late April lows) and growth rates are set to decelerate into the 2H in many places.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!o1qj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbffc5c8f-33f4-4a82-a93d-fe1e7f83c3de_1112x375.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!o1qj!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbffc5c8f-33f4-4a82-a93d-fe1e7f83c3de_1112x375.jpeg)

On the flipside, latest positioning data from GS Prime Book points to some recent selling/de-risking across the Tech sector, which will help the setup into Q2 prints.

While we are still 2 weeks out from Mega Cap Tech earnings, this earnings season will be of utmost importance for the market theme heading forward.  Was last week the beginning of a larger/sustainable rotation out of Mega Cap and into Small Caps? Q2 earnings will have a lot to say about that.

The below chart from Goldman is worth flagging, showing the somewhat checkered history of T+1performance across Big Tech stocks following their earnings reports.  Said another way, Mega Cap tech earnings have basically been a coin flip over the past 9 quarters with no stock above a 56% hit rate.
