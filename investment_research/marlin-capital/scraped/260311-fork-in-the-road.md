Marlin Capital Readers,

**So, the market bounced at key support. Now what?**

As I laid out in [Sunday’s report](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/unwind), the 200-day SMA was the logical spot for the market to bounce. That is exactly what happened for /ES and /NQ.

But now comes the real test. 👇

**The Price Battle**

In past reports, I have talked about how I like to think of price action as a battle between buyers and sellers (bulls and bears). I first introduced this concept back in [“Weakness”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/weakness) in October, and it has never been more relevant than right now.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IaKw!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F428dd2da-f6fc-478f-bde8-36124f664aff_2816x1536.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IaKw!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F428dd2da-f6fc-478f-bde8-36124f664aff_2816x1536.png)

For the last 5 months, the indices have been locked in an epic tug of war battle. Bulls push it up, and bears push it back down. The range holds. Over and over again. Neither side has been able to land the knockout punch.

On Sunday night, it looked like the bears were finally about to take control. Futures were plunging. The 200-day SMA was being broken, and panic was starting to set in (with Oil going parabolic). I saw investors on X predicting a “Limit Down” day on Monday. If there was ever a moment for the sellers to break this market wide open, that was it.

But they failed.

Buyers stepped in aggressively, starting overnight Sunday and continuing throughout Monday’s session. The dip was bought. The indices reversed. And by the close, Monday was a solidly green day. Bears had every opportunity to break this market down yesterday, but they could not do it.

In my view, this is a big win for bulls. And it adds real weight to the “Bear Trap” thesis I laid out in [“Hard Penny.”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/hard-penny?utm_source=publication-search)

Right now, sentiment is still very bearish. But stocks just refuse to break down. /ES hit -6.5% off highs on Sunday night. /NQ hit -9.1%. Short interest remains elevated. Put volume remains high. And yet, every dip continues to get bought.

**The Fork in the Road**

This brings us to the most important moment of 2026 so far. 👇

We are at a fork in the road.

The indices just bounced at their 200-day SMAs. That was essentially the last line of defense. It held (for now).

**Nasdaq Futures (/NQ)**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!i04Q!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faa5f5d39-5773-425c-b5af-aeaa104a7470_1267x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!i04Q!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faa5f5d39-5773-425c-b5af-aeaa104a7470_1267x844.png)

Think about what happened on Sunday night. The market got everyone bearish and made it look like we were breaking the 5 month range to the downside. Futures plunged to the 200-day SMA. Panic set in. Bears got excited and we heard talks of a “Black Monday” coming.

And then, the dip was bought (aggressively I might add). That is textbook Bear Trap behavior. Make it look like the big breakdown is coming, flush, and then reverse violently. As I pointed out in [“Hard Penny”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/hard-penny?utm_source=publication-search), this is exactly how the first major market pullbacks played out during both the Dot Com and Covid Bubbles.

On the other hand, I am certainly not ruling out more downside from here. As [I talked about on Sunday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/unwind), it is typical to see a -10% SPX correction in most years. It is particularly common in the first half of Midterm election years, occurring in 7 of the last 10.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!e0te!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbd8f4504-80a1-4efd-98f1-454a548e828e_702x827.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!e0te!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbd8f4504-80a1-4efd-98f1-454a548e828e_702x827.jpeg)

If this market bounce fades and we roll over, -10% down for the SPX (~$6300) is my next downside target.

This is the fork in the road. The way I see it, we have 2 clear scenarios from here:

**The Bear Case Scenario**

This market bounce fades. We roll over and sell down to an official -10% SPX correction (~$6300). The 200-day SMA bounce was just a dead cat bounce, and bears ultimately win this 5 month battle.

The Bear Case Scenario would be a real reset for the market. But, the good news is that it could provide us with a nice outlook for the rest of the year.

**The Bull Case Scenario**

The market bottomed on Sunday night. Oil and the VIX topped on Sunday/Monday. Bulls defended the 200-day SMA (the last line of defense). The Bear Trap thesis plays out exactly as it did during the Dot Com and Covid Bubbles, and the market bottomed on Sunday night.

The fork 👇

So, which scenario plays out? And what am I watching to find out? 👇

**The Bull Case Scenario**

Here is what I am looking to see for the Bull Case scenario.

Ideally, the indices would flag and consolidate here as volatility continues drifting lower.

No more big rug pulls or VIX spikes. Just sideways chop while the market digests Monday’s bullish reversal move. From there, I will be looking for a break back above the 21 EMA and 60 EMA resistance levels (~$6,875 on /ES).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!I-US!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5bedbb2-b797-46b7-888e-80885a09f8a3_1267x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!I-US!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5bedbb2-b797-46b7-888e-80885a09f8a3_1267x844.png)

If we reclaim and establish back above those moving averages by the end of this week, that is a very strong signal that the Bear Trap is playing out and the 200-day SMA (Sunday night) was the low.

**The Bear Case Scenario**

If the indices fail here and start rolling over by week-end, that tells me that this bounce was just a dead cat bounce, and the -10% correction is in play.

In that case, I will be looking to add some long-term investments once we reach official market correction territory (~$6300). I am in the process of preparing my wishlist for this scenario.

**Final Thoughts**

So far, the market bounced off logical support levels. And just like that, we are right back to where we were last week (back in the range). Now comes the real test.

We are at a fork in the road here. So how am I playing it?

I am letting the price action guide me here, and avoiding getting too fixated on one scenario playing out over another. There is no magic 8 ball to tell you where we are heading next. A professional trader prepares for every likely scenario, and never gambles.

One of the biggest advantages a trader has over a gambler is that you do not have to play every hand. You can sit on the sidelines, watch the cards get dealt, and choose which hands you want to play. In other words, you can wait for the market to show its hand before placing your bet. Mark Minervini put it perfectly in “Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard”:

> “The Achilles’ heel of most gamblers and speculators is the desire to play every hand. You have the luxury of staying on the sidelines, free of charge. See the cards, free of charge. Exploit this. Unlike poker.”

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GLdO!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe02b56bf-a8d3-4d28-8e70-7cd1046bb033_2816x1536.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GLdO!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe02b56bf-a8d3-4d28-8e70-7cd1046bb033_2816x1536.png)

That is exactly what I am doing right now. As I highlighted above, the market bounced off logical support. Both scenarios are very much still on the table here.

My strategy here is to wait until the market shows its hand and gives me more evidence that 1 of our 2 scenarios is beginning to play out. As William O’Neil says, the goal is not to time the exact bottom (or the exact top). The goal is to participate in the majority of the move.

**This is how I trade.**

And it worked out pretty well for us in 2025. During last year's Liberation Day correction, I waited several weeks after the April lows to begin buying (as Marlin Capital readers who have been with us since last spring know). The result is that we had a **MASSIVE** Q2 and Q3. Why? Because we participated in the majority of the move, not because we timed any exact bottoms.

If the market indeed bottomed Sunday night and our Bull Case scenario is playing out (another wave higher from here), this sustained uptrend is likely just beginning. There will be plenty of opportunities ahead in the coming weeks and months, so there is no reason to jump in right away.

I’m staying ready for both scenarios, and letting the market show its hand before getting involved.
