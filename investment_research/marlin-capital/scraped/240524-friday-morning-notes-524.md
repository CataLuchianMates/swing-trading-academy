# Friday Morning Notes 5.24

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Big reversal day in the market yesterday with the S&P 500 closing -0.74% after gapping up on the backs of NVDA earnings.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4uQN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc4941fd7-4395-43f9-9f07-417df40201c1_978x714.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4uQN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc4941fd7-4395-43f9-9f07-417df40201c1_978x714.jpeg)

The flavor of the day was to sell everything to buy NVDA, which closed up +9% and added another ~$275B in market cap (roughly the equivalent of the entire market caps of CRM, NFLX, ABBV, BAC, and AMD).

There was no great explanation for the fade other than ‘sell the news’ into a long weekend + big market catalysts are now behind us.  Breadth was awful with only 54 members of the S&P 500 in the green (87% downside day).  Both S&P and NDX Equal-Weights underperformed their respective indices yesterday by ~70bps.  HF VIP vs Most Short gained +296bps.  Though the pace of risk taking by HFs has slowed notably in May, gross leverage remains near multi-year highs and net leverage is now well above historical averages (but not yet extreme).
