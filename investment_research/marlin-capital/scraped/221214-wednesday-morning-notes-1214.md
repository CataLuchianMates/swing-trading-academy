Technical Analysis

Yesterday’s post-CPI gap up was mostly erased with an intraday reversal, as the indices were unable to hold most of their pre-market gains. Technically, the SPX gave a false range breakout on the 30 minute chart.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FSxh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe77ecb72-be32-4d61-8d4a-45c13394bff1_1396x669.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FSxh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe77ecb72-be32-4d61-8d4a-45c13394bff1_1396x669.png)

As I have discussed in the past, every move must be questioned in a Bear Market. Yesterday is a prime example of the type of fakeout/false breakout moves commonly seen in Bear Markets.

In any case, it is not a bearish event to see inflation coming in cooler than expected. Now with CPI behind us, we have the FOMC meeting today and options expiration Friday as we battle through a volatile week of news. Keep in mind, as we have seen in previous FOMC days, the initial move on Fed day is often a fakeout move.

Technically, we are back in the month long range for the indices. I continue to believe the “real” break of this range will be followed by a large move in the indices. The key is to remain patient until there is sufficient evidence that the “real” range break has begun.
