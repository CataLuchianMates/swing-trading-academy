# Jackson Hole Jitters

It has been a volatile week for US stocks as we head into Powell’s highly anticipated Jackson Hole speech tomorrow. The focus remains squarely on the prospect of a September rate cut, which has been the dominant driver of this week’s market action .

In my [weekly preview](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-4-stars-of-q2-earnings), I flagged this Sept. rate cut uncertainty as the biggest risk to our current uptrend. That concern has played out ahead of time, with rate cut odds falling in anticipation of Powell’s speech tomorrow.

At the start of the week, the probability of a September cut stood at ~75% ([per Polymarke](https://poly.market/kuDz08S)t). It has since dropped to ~63% (due to some investor anxiety over what Powell will say).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vxlo!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F44390a09-a702-408b-8381-6ab94a2e9aea_1284x1615.jpeg)](https://poly.market/kuDz08S)

Here’s what I’ll be watching closest tomorrow:

It’s all about Sept. rate cut odds.

If Powell’s speech pushes Sept. rate cut odds back above 80%, I expect a strong rally, with Small Caps/rate sensitive names leading the charge.

On the other hand, if Powell sounds more hawkish and Sept. rate cut odds fall below 50%, it could trigger more selling pressure and a break below Wednesday’s lows.

At Wednesday’s lows, the QQQ saw a -4.1% drawdown from ATHs (SPX had a very minor -2.4% pullback). Momentum stocks were hit the hardest, with a -15% pullback overall and a ~10% decline over 5 trading days.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cN5C!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F953a070e-062e-4287-8e53-80b258ecde2a_1107x773.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cN5C!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F953a070e-062e-4287-8e53-80b258ecde2a_1107x773.jpeg)

Technically speaking, this is a potentially concerning setup for the QQQ with 2 consecutive closes below its 21 EMA for the first time since April .

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ep7v!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9618affa-8e4c-4c41-942c-3117493c27e9_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ep7v!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9618affa-8e4c-4c41-942c-3117493c27e9_1396x653.png)

The key QQQ level to watch tomorrow is Wednesday’s lows ($558-59). A break below these lows after Powell’s speech could open up the floodgates for more selling. In this bearish scenario, I believe we would likely test August 1st lows at $551.

A break lower tomorrow would also signal the first bearish 8/21 EMA crossover since the market bottomed in April.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Mh_y!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4923aaeb-9061-4c00-8e62-075f8ce2576d_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Mh_y!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4923aaeb-9061-4c00-8e62-075f8ce2576d_1396x653.png)

For me, I am avoiding getting overly bullish or bearish ahead of Powell’s speech tomorrow. The technicals are suggesting a break lower is very possible tomorrow, but that could all change in an instant. It is still my belief that the Fed will likely cut rates in September, which (if signaled tomorrow by Powell), should provide a strong tailwind for stocks.

In a bullish scenario (dovish Powell speech + rising rate cut odds >80% + strong market reaction), I will look to re-enter ALAB in my Active Portfolio. ALAB remains 1 of [my top 4](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-4-stars-of-q2-earnings) and longer-term, my target is still $230+.
