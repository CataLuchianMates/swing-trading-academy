# June 2024 Market Report

Last week was a very challenging 4-day week as the NDX pulled back -1.5% (vs R2K flat) and SPX finished -0.6% after Friday’s turnaround.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!v8Po!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F34f1734a-cd56-41ad-a788-df3e5c5c730b_1215x892.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!v8Po!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F34f1734a-cd56-41ad-a788-df3e5c5c730b_1215x892.jpeg)

The pullback was led by a number of outsized earnings drawdowns (CRM worst day in 20+ yrs, DELL worst day ever, PATH worst day ever, MRVL biggest day of underperformance in 9 yrs, MDB 2nd worst day ever).

Friday saw a big reversal into the close with the SPX bouncing 165bps off the lows, thanks to month-end dynamics at play and asset managers using the last 30 minutes as a liquidity grab to load up on tech.  For the majority of Friday’s session, stocks were deep in the red with broad selling pressure (led by earnings related drawdowns mentioned above) + a big miss & recessionary-like reading for Chicago PMIs (which overshadowed a benign PCE reading; recall last PCE the NDX jumped +1.7%).

Overall, it was a tough 4-day week with big pain in Software stocks (CRM, MDB, PATH, etc.) and some pockets of AI (DELL, MRVL).  The Macro data got a bit more friendly for future rate cuts, causing more of a squeeze in highly shorted names (potential for more with GME gapping up +70% after Roaring Kitty revealed a $200M long position in GME).

As we enter the first trading day of June, it is important to note that a good first 5 months of the year tend to lead to more strong returns through year-end.

Since 1950, there have been 21 episodes where the S&P 500 was up more than +10% at the end of May.  Out of these, the only 2 instances (~10%) when the SPX was down the rest of the year were 1986 (-0.1%) and 1987 (-13%).  The SPX was up in the other 19/21 instances (~90% of the time).

The median return for the last 7 months of the year (6/1-12/31) since 1950 is +5.4%.  During these 21 episodes, the median 7 month return increases to +8.1%.
