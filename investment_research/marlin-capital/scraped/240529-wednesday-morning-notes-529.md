# Wednesday Morning Notes 5.29

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Quiet start to the holiday shortened week yesterday with the S&P 500 finishing +0.02%.

NVDA continued to lead Tech while 369 S&P names traded lower as yields backed up (US 10 YR +7bps @ 4.54%) following a hotter consumer confidence survey hawkish comments from Fed member Kashkari, and a slightly weaker 5y and 2y bond actions.  NDX and SPX Equal Weights again underperformed their cap weighted indices by 100bps and 720bps respectively.

NVDA finished +7% on no real incremental news flow, driving further $ concentration into Semis and AI infrastructure plays (DELL +3.7%, now +40% over the last month, reports 5/30).

Elsewhere, software extended losses from last week despite light positioning and negative sentiment in the space, particularly from WDAY, SNOW, and INTU earnings last week (CRM and OKTA up next to report this week).

ONON – Took half profits on this Day 1 Earnings gap (inside 15 minute) entry +19%.  Holding the other half with stop at $37.44.

Holding ONON, LSPD, VITL, SG, ENVX, MU, and DESP into Wednesday’s session.
