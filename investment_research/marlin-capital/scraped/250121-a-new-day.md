# A New Day

The S&P 500 rallied +2.9% last week, the index’s best performing week since November 8th, and now sits just -1.4% below its all-time-highs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OE9W!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9c1c66dd-670d-4b61-aa96-4f47f85fb4ba_1187x882.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OE9W!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9c1c66dd-670d-4b61-aa96-4f47f85fb4ba_1187x882.jpeg)

The equity rally was primarily driven by lighter than expected Core CPI, which triggered the much needed move lower in bond yields. The US 10Y yield fell -18bps to 4.6% after Core CPI surprised to the downside. As I talked about last week, rising yields were weighing heavily on the market (particularly small caps), so the downbeat inflation # allowed investors to breathe a collective sigh of relief.

Q4 Earnings kicked off with solid results across large cap Banks, with both revenue and credit metrics remaining healthy. Conference calls from banks earnings highlighted the healthy US consumer and benign credit conditions. In addition, TSMC earnings last week helped set a bullish narrative for Semis and AI demand.

Big picture, the US economy remains healthy and stable in the midst of a Federal Reserve cutting cycle. Historically, this backdrop has been extremely bullish for stocks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3K-4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdc9d54bd-b957-4664-85c1-cd7edea4e403_1001x690.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3K-4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdc9d54bd-b957-4664-85c1-cd7edea4e403_1001x690.jpeg)

Over the holiday weekend, Trump’s inauguration took center stage. The initial WSJ article on no immediate tariff implementation was a relief for global equities. It was later offset last night by headlines on a possible 25% Mexico/Canada tariff beginning Feb 1st.

Q4 Earnings continue this week with a number of big names set to report (NFLX, IBKR, MMM, PG, JNJ, AXP).
