# Crash Up

**Marlin Capital Readers,**

The Nasdaq finished perfect last week, extending its winning streak to 13 consecutive green days.

The S&P 500 is back at all-time highs. Breakouts are everywhere. The [lockout rally](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/lockout-rally) is in full swing.

And yet, despite the market ripping back to ATHs, one thing has quietly flown under the radar.

The current P/E multiple on the SPX is actually lower than where it was back in January. Let that sink in.

The market is slightly higher (+2%), but the P/E multiple is still lower. That means only 1 thing. Earnings have been doing all the heavy lifting. 👇

**This Week’s Earnings Watchlist**

Earnings season is my favorite time of the quarter to trade, and it is not particularly close.

For me, earnings are the #1 source of new momentum trades in the market. When a company reports blowout numbers and raises guidance aggressively, the stock doesn’t just move for a day or two. It often launches into a sustained uptrend that can last weeks or months. In a strong market, the momentum from an A+ earnings report often carries a stock for the entire quarter (until its next EPS report). We saw this many times with our winners last year (APLD, NBIS, LITE, BE, etc).

This is the setup Kristjan Kullamägi coined as the “Episodic Pivot”. I broke down the full setup and criteria in [“Headline Roulette”.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/headline-roulette?utm_source=publication-search) If you have not read it, start there. It is one of the most important setups I trade. For new subscribers, I also recommend reading the [“Earnings Gapper Trade Setup”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-earnings-gapper-trade-setup?utm_source=publication-search), where I cover my criteria for Day 1 earnings entries and a few historical examples.

There is also another bullish post-earnings trade setup I added to my arsenal last year. That is the “market leader gap down”.

Sometimes, the most bullish thing a leading stock can do is gap DOWN on earnings (after announcing good numbers), particularly when that dip gets bought up immediately. This is a huge tell. It tells you that demand for the stock is very high (as we saw in our HOOD, APH, SPOT, etc. trades last year), as the post-earnings dip does not last long and gets bought up right away. This is a common theme in leading stocks throughout history: dips don’t last long.

Some of our biggest winners from last year started with a post-earnings gap down (in a leader) that got immediately bought up aggressively. HOOD was our top active portfolio trade of 2025 (>100%) on that exact setup.

If we are truly back in a “risk on” market here (and the price action suggests we are), this earnings season should produce a ton of opportunities. I foresee plenty of post-earnings trade setups emerging over the next ~4-6 weeks.

That is exactly why I am keeping dry powder on hand here, rather than chasing more names after a vertical rally. I want to be ready to strike on A+ earnings setup names.
