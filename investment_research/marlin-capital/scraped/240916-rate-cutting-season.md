# Rate Cutting Season

We’re coming off a strong week for markets with the SPX +3.0%, QQQ +4.9%, and Mag 7 +8.1%.  Tech had its best week of the year with the NDX closing up +6%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HDsr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd04895b0-cb99-465c-8637-7084f9522ecd_1192x895.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HDsr!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd04895b0-cb99-465c-8637-7084f9522ecd_1192x895.jpeg)

The upside move last week was driven by a combination of reversion (week prior = NDX down -6%, its worst week of the year), better corporate newsflow (ORCL earnings, GS TMT Conference, AI headlines, etc.) and some “good” Macro datapoints (mainly CPI).

Sentiment/enthusiasm for the GenAI theme received a jolt last week, as key execs across a number of conferences and interviews sounded upbeat.  Price action was certainly supportive of the theme, with NVDA, ANET, VRT, ARM, and AVGO up 15-20% on the week.

The GenAI theme has long been the leading group of this Bull Market, but has faltered since the QQQ topped in July.  I have written in detail about other sectors/industries taking the lead since that time (mostly defensive themes), as the AI trade has become more 2-sided.  Even within Tech (which has underperformed since July), we have seen underperformance in Semis versus Software.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5faM!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F547a918c-b772-4547-8d6b-50200920016c_607x407.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5faM!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F547a918c-b772-4547-8d6b-50200920016c_607x407.jpeg)

During this period of AI underperformance, earnings estimates for the group have continued to rise.  Relative to YTD earnings trends, AI stocks are now the cheapest they have been in awhile.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OHi_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F188bed59-c5d5-4646-bd07-e077f8837f0c_466x389.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OHi_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F188bed59-c5d5-4646-bd07-e077f8837f0c_466x389.jpeg)

The Broad AI basket has beat earnings by an average of 8%, which is 3pp higher than the S&P 500.  Moreover, valuations in the space have normalized while estimates have continues to increase.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JcOu!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fec1fc8b1-8e26-44d2-ae21-207860996cf5_821x618.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JcOu!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fec1fc8b1-8e26-44d2-ae21-207860996cf5_821x618.jpeg)

Overall, the AI space is expected to roughly double net income in the next 12 months vs the last 12.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rsch!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa4545fb3-9466-4c40-adfa-54772fc246d9_574x256.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rsch!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa4545fb3-9466-4c40-adfa-54772fc246d9_574x256.jpeg)

In terms of datapoints from last week’s conferences, the highlights came from MSFT’s CEO (“we are demonstrably not at the point of diminishing marginal returns on how capable these AI systems can get”) and NVDA’s CEO (on Blackwell: “the demand on it is so great. And everybody wants to be first, and everybody wants to be most”).

Flow wise, Tech saw continued buying throughout the week in the Hedge Fund community.  Positioning remains lighter than when we topped in July, but the Mag 7 was net bought (nearly +2z) over the past 5 days while Semis saw <1z of net buying.  Overall, global semis positioning is in the ~40th percentile on a 12 month lookback, as HF net exposure to the group has fallen 50% from mid-June highs.  Hedge Funds used last week to do some re-risking in Tech/Semis, but head into this week’s FOMC meeting still relatively light.

This week’s Fed Meeting (concluding Wednesday) is a huge one for the market with the committee expected to cut rates for the first time since March 2020.  While there is still some debate on a 25 vs 50bp cut, I am sticking with by 25bp call.  Powell has never surprised the markets before, and I would expect to see some loud whispers on a bigger cut coming if they are about to cut by 50bps.  Perhaps there is a way to deliver 25bps with a dovish message on forward such that it will manage to not disappoint markets, and I believe this is the line Powell will try to walk.  I expect Powell to emphasize the downside risks to the labor market, with the US Labor Market Surprise Index at its cycle lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VrzX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8a0b096d-faf0-4844-9747-a7db45c5f061_1270x758.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VrzX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8a0b096d-faf0-4844-9747-a7db45c5f061_1270x758.jpeg)

Overall, equities are back to the high end after last week’s rally while the market is left with a lot of uncertainty about how the Fed will act/what Powell will say this week.  I am seeing a number of solid stock setups technically, particularly in earnings leaders, but the risk of a post-FOMC selloff is certainly there (especially if we continue rallying Monday/Tuesday).
