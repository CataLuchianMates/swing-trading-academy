**Marlin Capital Thoughts:**

In previous reports, I have said “there is no escaping the selling.”  The warning bells are ringing here.  This is a treacherous setup for equities (over the next year or so), especially stocks with high valuations.

We are heading into a super fast tightening cycle that may solve the demand part of the inflation conundrum, but not the supply problems.  We will also be embarking on a faster quantitative tightening cycle than we had in 2018. History teaches us this is a major headwind for the equity markets.

There is no soft landing from here for the economy, in my opinion.  Soft landing’s, in general, are a myth. When the Fed embarks on a tightening cycle, it typically only ends one way.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!--LK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F355313f1-ebb9-4ab9-a877-24ad9eabc0de_941x679.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!--LK!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F355313f1-ebb9-4ab9-a877-24ad9eabc0de_941x679.jpeg)

The equity market will likely need to price in a recession by the end of this year (the fixed market already is).  Be careful of any rally, as stocks will likely need to go much lower before this cycle ends, and a recession hits. Like I have said in the past, it can come now or later, but there is no escaping the eventual selling that will take place over the next year or so.
