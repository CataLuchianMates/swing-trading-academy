## David Marlin Q&A (Comments)

**Q:** People who work with legit AI companies know that revenue per employee goes up, as less manual labor is needed. But take a look at $INOD revenue/employee data and tell me what you see. Can this be a "AI hype" type company that is doing fake-AI work but pretending it's real by hiring lots of cheap manual labor to clean the data? What gives you confidence that Innodata is legit or has grown into a legit company from whatever it is they were before? Thanks!

**David Marlin:** Hi Krzysztof, 

Question - where are you getting your data? By my calculations, Innodata’s revenue per employee is increasing fairly significantly as they grow revenues (+136% YoY in Q3).

Revenue per Employee:

- 2021:
  - Revenue: $69.8 million
  - Employees: 4,900
  - Revenue per Employee: $69.8M / 4,900 ≈ $14,245

- 2023:
  - Revenue: $86.78 million
  - Employees: 4,200
  - Revenue per Employee: $86.78M / 4,200 ≈ $20,662

- 2024:
  - Revenue: $147 million (estimated using Q3 growth rate for Q4)
  - Employees: 4,300
  - Revenue per Employee: $147M / 4,300 ≈ $34,186
