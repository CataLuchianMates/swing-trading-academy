# Choppy Waters

Last week was another turbulent one for US stocks, this time with the market closing higher following Wednesday’s announcement of a 90-day tariff pause (excluding China), On the week: SPX +5.7%, NDX +7.4%, RTY +1.6%

A circus of trade/tariff headlines came through this weekend. Much different to last weekend, this weekend’s overall newsflow felt much more skewed to the upside.

Markets are a wild ride right now, but I will continue to zero in on what matters most. On Friday, I highlighted the 2 charts/data points I’m focusing on the closest right now:

(1) Total Positioning; and

(2) The VIX

The market’s fear gauge, the VIX, spiked to a high of 60 last Monday before starting to roll over. Wednesday’s face ripping market rally catalyzed the largest 1-day decline ever for the VIX (-36.4%).

We are by no means out of the woods yet, but the panic levels from last Monday are easing. This tells me the market is starting to exhale. The VIX is set to open below Friday’s (and Thursday’s) lows this morning.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Fc05!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0d926162-2cb3-4e53-bcde-66b9d88dedb8_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Fc05!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0d926162-2cb3-4e53-bcde-66b9d88dedb8_1396x653.png)

The next step will be a sustained move below 30. As I talked about on Friday, I continue to believe the VIX likely peaked/topped last Monday, which would mean we are past the point of peak fear/volatility (which, in turn, allows the market bottoming process to begin).

Positioning backs this up. In [“Retracement Day”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/retracement-day), I highlighted the fact that Goldman’s Total Positioning Indicator is near 15 year lows at -2.5z:

> “We are currently sitting at -2.5 standard deviations below the 15-year norm. -2.5z total positioning is in the region where every other Bear Market/Market Correction since 2010 has bottomed. Positioning has never gone much lower than this in the last decade and a half (the absolute lowest we’ve gone was -3z in Oct ‘11, which marked that correction bottom).”

Following last week’s repositioning (mechanical) and short covering rally, total positioning has now moved up from -2.5z at the beginning of last week to -1.7z at the end of the week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bgTf!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F14036308-92bc-49b3-be98-4c554359cf1a_1290x1134.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bgTf!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F14036308-92bc-49b3-be98-4c554359cf1a_1290x1134.jpeg)

On [Thursday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bottomed-out), I theorized that total positioning may have bottomed last week at -2.5z. As I talked about, -2.5z is a capitulatory number (near 15-year positioning lows). Wednesday’s tariff pause headline **forced** some inflows back into the market (both mechanical buying and short covering). In [“Retracement Day”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/retracement-day), I highlighted the massive short covering bid we saw from Hedge Funds following the tariff pause news: “Yesterday’s notional US short covering was the largest since August 2015 and the 4th largest in the past 15 years (+6.3 Z score).”

In [“Retracement Day”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/retracement-day), I wrote:

> “If (and a big if) my thesis that positioning has potentially bottomed and the VIX has (hopefully) peaked is correct, I still lean towards the idea that the market low is probably in.”

This continues to be my view here. I believe the VIX is forming a topping pattern and a sustained move below 30 will confirm this. As for total positioning, I believe Wednesday’s 90-day tariff pause headline **forced** buyers back into the market (and we are now at -1.7z from -2.5z). I do not see positioning needing to be washed out further below -2.5z, as Wednesday’s news brings some form of stability to markets. It shows there is indeed a “Trump Put” if things get too bleak. Jamie Dimon and other heavyweights made it clear to the Trump administration: a global trade war would tank the economy and send us into a deep recession. They’re listening, even if it’s messy.

Along these lines, Fox Business Correspondent Charles Gasparino tweeted this weekend:

> “As one major GOP political adviser just told me “Trump is finally bending to reality” that if you want to fix global trade with a sledge hammer the cure can be worse than the disease”

This is a welcome change from the administration’s views a week ago, and has helped to stabilize markets a bit. The ‘Trump Put” exists, even if we needed a -21% SPX selloff in <2 months (and potential Bond Market blowup) to get there.

Again, if the VIX has topped and total positioning has bottomed (which is my current view), this is what allows the bottoming process to begin. I continue to believe we are past the point of peak fear/uncertainty and past the point of capitulatory positioning lows (-2.5z).

Combine that with Polymarket odds dialing back on recession bets (down to ~50% from 67% a week ago), and we have a setup where markets can start stabilizing.

In the short term, last week removed the extreme Bear Case from markets (the $220 EPS scenario I talked about in [“Recession Roulette”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/recession-roulette)), as the Trump administration showed some sign of relent after SPX traded in Bear Market territory (-20% +) and backend bonds went bidless. This is what allowed the VIX to peak on Monday and positioning to (potentially) bottom.

The question is: should this positive newsflow continue to happen, where can the broader market go from here?

When the market mistakenly thought “Liberation Day” was just a 10% universal tariff, SPX futures rallied to 5773 in the post market (before falling off a cliff when Trump unveiled the big board). From here, it feels difficult to rally through that level in the short-term, considering we have had further escalation with US/China and have experienced turmoil in equities and bonds that likely have left some structural damage in the system (1000 point peak-to-trough selloff in SPX and 50bp selloff in 30y bonds in a week).

The underlying economic data clearly shows that things are slowing and earnings estimates have fallen. The trading community has revised down 2025 EPS to 250 (+3% YoY), and the most optimistic 2026 EPS is in the mid 270s. This means we are currently trading above a ~20x fwd P/E, an 85th + percentile valuation throughout history. The Fed can only cut rates once the hard data allows them to, and we are not at that point yet.

With that said, I believe more positive newsflow could take us back to 5650-5700 in the SPX. From there, it is difficult to imagine much more upside (absent significant progress on trade, including China). All of this leads me to believe we will be stuck in a 5000-5700 trading range for the next few months.

In my view, we have likely started the market bottoming process. With that said, I do not see any V-shaped bottom this time. A choppier/U-shaped bottom is not unusual for market correction bottoms throughout history. In fact, this type of choppy bottom (U-shaped) is more common than not. The V-shaped bottoms of 2018 and 2020 were outliers due to Fed pivots/massive stimulus.

Here are some examples of choppy bottoming patterns we have seen for the S&P 500 in the last 15 years.

2015 was a very choppy bottom, as we actually re-tested (and broke through) August 2015 lows in Q1 2016.

As you can see, market bottoms are often a long process. In recent times, we have become spoiled with V-shapped bottoms (but as mentioned, the circumstances of the 2018 and 2020 V-shaped bottoms were different with the Fed pivoting).

In my view, the market bottoming process has started here in 2025 (VIX cooling, positioning stabilizing, etc.). But with tariff headlines still flying, my Base Case is for a more rangebound market for the next few months. This headline uncertainty is bad for markets, which is part of the reason I believe we are headed for a choppier bottoming process this time.

Overall, I believe we are past the point of peak fear (VIX), peak tariff uncertainty, peak recessionary fears (see Polymarket odds) and peak capitulatory positioning lows (-2.5z). This is what allows the market to begin a bottoming process.

The tariff circus is still in town, but the panicky “sky is falling” sentiment from last Monday has faded (as the 90-day tariff pause news removed the extreme Bear Case scenario). With that said, I do not expect a clean, V-shaped recovery. In my view, the market bottoming process is underway, but I am expecting this to be a choppy one (like 2015, 2011, and 2010 examples shown above). Worsening economic data and headline noise will keep things bumpy.

With that said, if my view is correct and last week established a floor in the market, we will be entering a stock picker’s market (particularly with earnings season beginning). With the general market pressure easing, stocks will be able to go their separate ways based off their respective earnings results. Companies unaffected by tariffs, who are still able to raise guidance despite the overarching global trade uncertainty, will be poised to outperform. This earnings season should highlight those companies, and I expect the market to reward those stocks handsomely. These types of [Earnings Gapper](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-earnings-gapper-trade-setup?utm_source=publication-search) trades will be my focus for Active Trading for the next month +.
