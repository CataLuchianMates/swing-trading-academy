Active Portfolio:

Short ARKK

Netflix kicked off Big Tech earnings season with an ugly print & guide. Interestingly, Nasdaq futures are now shaking off the Netflix miss after the initial reaction.

Based off this reaction, I believe we are unlikely to see a significant break (on the index level) in either direction in the coming days. In my view, we are likely to see more rangebound/choppy price action as individual company earnings take center stage. As we can see with NFLX, the market still has no tolerance for weak results, even in supposed “value traps” (down a lot, but still not cheap).

In my view, it is likely that many individual stocks begin to head their separate ways based off earnings. For the general market, this potentially means a lot of choppy action that is driven by headline earnings results. However, earnings season often provides some of the best trading opportunities, so it’s important to keep an open mind and stay focused.

As for my ARKK short, I will monitor the price action today, and decide on whether to hold or close out the position. As mentioned, many of the individual components are set to announce earnings over the coming weeks, so we are likely to see even more volatility in the ETF.
