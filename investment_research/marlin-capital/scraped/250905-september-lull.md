# September Lull

The S&P 500 closed out a choppy week today. The QQQ has been trading in a ~4% range for over a month.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cptS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F78c3f343-7aba-402f-97a8-1907daa21874_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cptS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F78c3f343-7aba-402f-97a8-1907daa21874_1396x653.png)

Today’s jobs number was worse than expected. The US economy added just 22k jobs in August, far below expectations of 75k.

The June number was also revised down to -13k jobs, making it the first negative month since December 2020.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ix2i!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faa0d9a2f-42f0-4d4d-b4fb-c91e128c3c20_765x557.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ix2i!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faa0d9a2f-42f0-4d4d-b4fb-c91e128c3c20_765x557.png)

Following this report, the market is now pricing in 3 rate cuts+ by January, with the first coming this month.

The weak Jobs Report was initially seen as a positive for the market (more rate cuts priced in), but the S&P 500 closed lower after testing the top of its recent price range.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6niC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F93f7a498-d6d5-46ee-bb06-4a8fd3e90cde_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6niC!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F93f7a498-d6d5-46ee-bb06-4a8fd3e90cde_1396x653.png)

Big picture, this type of choppy price action is normal and healthy for the market, especially after the type of V-shaped recovery rally we’ve had.

My research shows the most similar V-shaped recovery rallies to this one also began to falter around the 4-5 month mark. 👇👇

In the 2020 Covid recovery, we had 5 green months off lows before the first down month. Months 6 and 7 were both negative.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!M-RH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F21e604c8-0fcb-4e55-8973-520471ce7eba_967x559.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!M-RH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F21e604c8-0fcb-4e55-8973-520471ce7eba_967x559.png)

In the 2018-19 recovery, we had 4 green months off lows before the first down month.

Here in 2025, we have had 4 green months off April lows (+ we nearly finished positive in April / lows were in early April & we rallied the rest of the month).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7Abz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8c59869e-24ca-4765-9aaf-68ef8a3d5b6b_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7Abz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8c59869e-24ca-4765-9aaf-68ef8a3d5b6b_1396x653.png)

September would be a 5th green month. This is about the time where previous V-shaped recoveries first began to falter and had their first down months.

Yet, they all ended up breaking meaningfully higher a couple months later. These Month 5 and 6 pullbacks were simply consolidation phases. I am confident that this AI-driven Bull Market will end up heading the same way.

As I highlighted in [“Heading into July”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/heading-into-july?utm_source=publication-search), the shortest Bull Market since 1960 was ~15 months (Dec ‘18 - Mar ‘20 Covid Crash).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!iKTt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6fde7e54-5d3a-4f84-8210-45aec9e8e1cb_750x758.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!iKTt!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6fde7e54-5d3a-4f84-8210-45aec9e8e1cb_750x758.png)

We are still just 5 months into this one.

Big picture, I remain bullish and expect the market + most names to eventually break higher from these consolidation zones.

Shorter-term, we have the perfect storm for weakness/chop right now:

(1) 4-5 months off lows, which is historically where similar v-shaped recoveries first began to falter

(2) September is the worst month for the market

(3) Flows are not supportive with Systematic Positioning in the 90th percentile versus history

Most importantly, the price action is weak right now with many names chopping violently/basing.

As a trader, I firmly believe there are times to get aggressive and times to conservative. This is not a time where I want to be aggressive.

In a typical year, the stock market might offer you 1-3 times a year to make big money. My goal as a trader is to capitalize on these periods and not lose too much money in between.

Legendary trader Dan Zanger says it best in a 2005 interview below.

> “I really have found that the market may have 2 big moves during the year. They may last 6-8-10-12 weeks, and other than that, they just exhaust themselves after that. The rest of the time, the market is just consolidating the gains.”

This hold true even in Bull Markets.

The 2020-2021 TSLA chart is a perfect example of this:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!r845!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9e5ae2b3-e47f-4445-a15a-70750e6c8734_1224x754.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!r845!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9e5ae2b3-e47f-4445-a15a-70750e6c8734_1224x754.png)

I’ve marked up the chart above to help demonstrate what I mean.

In 2021, there were only 2 instances to get aggressive in TSLA stock. The key is fully participating in these moves, and avoiding the consolidation/choppiness phases. Consolidation phases are healthy for a stock long term, but difficult to trade. Many traders underperform because they overtrade the stock, and participate in the choppy A-B-C-D etc. moves that are marked above.

The Tesla chart above is just one example of how winning stocks move. A typical winning stock will have 2-4 major advance phases over the course of the year, with the rest of the time being consolidation.

As a trader, my strategy is to be aggressive during the run ups and avoid getting chopped up in the consolidation/down moves.

In my view, many leading stocks are currently in their consolidation phases right now. It can be a solid time for long term investors to accumulate positions, but it is a very difficult time for swing trading with stop losses. It is very easy to get chopped up/lose money in these basing phases, because stocks often break lower before breaking higher. Stop losses are hunted within these bases.

This can lead to a trader becoming frustrated. By the time the real move comes, you don’t trust it enough/are too frustrated from the last loss to re-buy. I have experienced it all. And due to my experiences, my strategy is to drastically reduce my trading activity during these periods. That’s what I did this week.
