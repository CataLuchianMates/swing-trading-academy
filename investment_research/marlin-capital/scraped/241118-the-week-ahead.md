# The Week Ahead

Last week was a tough one for the market with the Nasdaq finishing all 5 days in the red. This was the first time this has happened in ~5 years and is 1 of just 36 scoreless weeks for the NDX in the last 10 years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gHHo!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F93f66bb1-6bb9-49e0-be9b-23416da046fc_1190x689.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gHHo!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F93f66bb1-6bb9-49e0-be9b-23416da046fc_1190x689.jpeg)

While investors entered last week confident in a year end melt up (seasonals, technicals, policy optimism, positioning were all aligning), it sure felt like we exited the week more confused by all the moving parts as it relates to possibly policy implementations (government spending, tariffs, HHS) and Macro uncertainty (Rates, stronger USD).

To be fair, the NDX is still up +2.5% so far in the month of November (and +2% if we take out TSLA’s contribution). With that said, it was certainly more of a challenging week across Tech with names like META, AVGO, TSM, and COHR all down -6-14% last week.

The selling accelerated in the indices on Friday with the SPX finishing -1.28% and QQQ -2.38%. GS Prime Book noted an uptick in selling pressure in Tech last week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0mve!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcd745c66-60a9-49d5-9f3e-09f86844d9bc_1259x1099.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0mve!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcd745c66-60a9-49d5-9f3e-09f86844d9bc_1259x1099.jpeg)

This comes after the chase for long exposure we saw post-election, where we saw the strongest retail investor buying flow since March 2022 (~137 weeks ago).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!x07p!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb352c98e-23cd-4309-afa7-40cf27a44686_938x380.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!x07p!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb352c98e-23cd-4309-afa7-40cf27a44686_938x380.jpeg)

As such, it will be telling to see if investors take a further step back this week (8 sessions before Thanksgiving) or if we see this 1st post-election dip bought up.

My view remains a bullish one for US equities into year-end. I believe we are likely to see this dip bought up and that the SPX has not seen its 2024 highs yet. With that said, all eyes now shift to NVDA earnings after the close on Wednesday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5ydm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1ebb6f9a-baf9-4036-a1f4-2e15094a410b_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5ydm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1ebb6f9a-baf9-4036-a1f4-2e15094a410b_1920x1080.jpeg)

NVDA is the most dominant stock in the market, driving 20% of the S&P 500’s return over the past year, and is expected to drive nearly 25% of the SPX EPS growth in Q3.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!a3wR!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9de075c4-2ab8-46e5-9021-88045ac063c1_1133x730.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!a3wR!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9de075c4-2ab8-46e5-9021-88045ac063c1_1133x730.jpeg)

With the market taking a much needed breather last week, NVDA Q3 earnings have become even more critical in dictating the market’s next move. In my view, we may see the market chop around ahead of NVDA’s earnings on Wednesday. The options market is currently pricing more broad market risk around NVDA earnings than the next NFP & CPI and as much as the December FOMC.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FbGW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5fadff1b-c69e-4b6e-9ee1-9e1ff7348b58_1035x822.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FbGW!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5fadff1b-c69e-4b6e-9ee1-9e1ff7348b58_1035x822.jpeg)

To summarize, NVDA earnings Wednesday is seen as the most important event/catalyst remaining in 2024 for the S&P 500. I am not seeing a clear reason to take on more active risk ahead of the event, especially with the QQQ bouncing back from last week’s sell off so far this morning. I am holding my 3 long positions into this week as well as my [Trump trades](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/activate-the-trump-trades) in my long term account (TSLA, COIN, [Top 6 Small Caps](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-small-caps-with-massive-upside)).
