# Fed Day

Yesterday was another quiet day for US stocks with the SPX finishing -0.77%. Volumes were once again extremely light (-23% vs 20dma) as investors take a breather ahead of today’s FOMC meeting.

The Fed is widely expected to keep rates unchanged today as the FOMC awaits greater clarity on the evolution of the economy in the face of elevated policy uncertainty.

The official odds from Polymarket are currently at 98% for “No Change” today.

I also expect Fed Chair Powell to signal that the Fed is in no rush to adjust rates at today’s meeting. FOMC participants have made it clear that they view the current policy stance as well positioned to deal with the risks to inflation (rising near term inflation expectations) and employment sides of their mandate.

For the Fed decision in June, Polymarket odds are currently sitting at 72% for “No Change” and 25% for a “25 bps decrease”.

My Base Case projection is for the FOMC to cut rates 2-3 times this year, starting with a 25bps cut in July. This will allow the Fed’s policy stance to remain restrictive enough so that inflation ultimately returns to target over time, but it would avoid having overly restrictive policy as the economy slows and labor market deteriorates.

The market is inline with my Base Case, currently implying ~3 rate cuts by year end with a 25 bps cut coming in July (~61% implied probability).
