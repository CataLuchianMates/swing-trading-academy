# Thursday Morning Notes 9.1

Technical Analysis

Yesterday’s attempted bounce in US stocks ran out of steam towards the close, and the QQQ finished the day at lows. For the QQQ, yesterday qualified as an “inside day”.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xCJN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0bf5394-f414-4cce-922f-cca97c406c7b_872x654.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xCJN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0bf5394-f414-4cce-922f-cca97c406c7b_872x654.jpeg)

Today, we are set for an immediate gap down below yesterday’s lows and the $298 support level. There are some short term oversold signals flashing, so it would be unsurprising to see some type of bounce today. With that said, the technical setup remains an ugly picture as we head into today’s session breaking key support. The next support levels I am watching include the $288-$290 region, which is my target level on this breakdown for QQQ. If the indices begin to bounce, look for past support ($298-$300) to turn into resistance. Bulls will want to retake $298 as quick as possible. Higher potential resistance levels include $304-$305 and $307.
