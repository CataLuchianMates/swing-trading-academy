# Wednesday Morning Notes 9.28

Technical Analysis

Yesterday’s morning gap up could not hold up as the QQQ ran into previous resistance around $280. The market bounced at the low of the range and previous support, $272.

The selling pressure remains very strong on every bounce. We may be setting up for more of a consolidation/Bear flag than a sustained bounce. The QQQ is now trading in a range that began with Friday’s gap down, between $270-$280.

Key support is $270-$272.

Key resistance is $278-$280.

It appeared like we were set for a big gap down this morning after [news of Apple’s production cut due to weaker demand](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2022-09-28/apple-iphone-14-sales-not-strong-enough-to-trigger-production-boost), but stocks have pared their losses in the pre-market. Keep in mind that we have had 6 straight negative days at the index level, so we are looking at an extreme short term oversold reading.

MKM Research Note:

As of yesterday's close, the real interest rate on the five-year TIPS security (a market-determined rate) has surged nearly 400 bps from levels that prevailed late last year, the largest rise over a similar timeframe in the history of the TIPS market dating back to the late 1990s. The surge has been precipitated by a dramatic re-pricing of terminal Fed policy rate expectations and the most rapid seven-month rise in policy rates in more than a generation. In the nomenclature of Hicksian ISLM analysis, what we have here is a leftward shift in the LM curve. In short, a monetary shock. - MKM's Darda
