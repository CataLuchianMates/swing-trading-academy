# Thursday Morning Notes 7.11

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The S&P 500 closed above $5600 for the 1st time in its history yesterday, finishing off the day +1.02%.

All broader indices melted higher as the best 2 seasonal weeks of the calendar year (the 1st 2 weeks of July) are showing why they’ve earned that title.

Recently, we have been seeing the indices rally the day before a light CPI number.  This happened again here in July.

No surprises for CPI to come in lighter than expected after yesterday’s rally.  It has been a huge 2 week move for the SPX and NDX, so it is healthy to see the early outperformance this morning coming from small caps.  Following this morning’s CPI data, Fed funds futures are now pricing in >86% chance of a September rate cut.

Updates on my trades –

NVO – Letting this trade play out with my shares.  Closing stop below $138.  Let’s see if it can start to turn up back towards $145.

MSFT – No changes here.  Closing stop set at $455.

AVGO – Why sell when Pelosi is buying?

AMD – Big day for AMD yesterday.  Hit my first target of $187 and pulled back.  I took 1/3rd profits and am holding the rest with a closing stop below $170.  Next target = $200.  Then, we can see a test of ATHs at $227.

Big picture, a breakout to new ATHs (above $227) should ultimately take us to $300+ in this name (perhaps by the end of 2025).  This is definitely a name you want to have some exposure to in the IRA/long term account.

SGH and AEHR – Didn’t give the type of day 1 earnings gapper entries I was looking for yesterday.  AEHR had an inside hour setup but was >12% risk to LOD which is not a favorable risk/reward for me.  I am looking for <10% risk on these trades.  No positions here, but we will see if these can start to set up on the daily chart.
