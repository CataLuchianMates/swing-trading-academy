# Bull On The Loose

Last week, the Fed officially provided the market with a planned rate cutting cycle beginning in 2024, and as a result, the consensus ‘24 forecast now calls for 3 consecutive 25 bp cuts in March, May, and June.

The resulting buying pressure across US stocks was powerful, and has lead to some rare occurrences in the technical landscape.

At the index level, the S&P 500 is at its most overbought level (RSI >70) since 2020, and nearing its most overbought levels in a decade.

Under the hood, 46% of SPX members have a 14d RSI >70, the most in 30+ years, and 2nd most of all time (behind only 1 data point in 1991).

Following last week’s Fed pivot, Hedge Funds unwound risk at a staggering pace, leading to the squeezy action we saw in highly shorted names. Thursday’s (12/14) risk unwind (from Hedge Funds) was the largest 1-day de-grossing activity since January, which ranks in the 99.6th percentile on a 5-year lookback (-3.17 Z Score), driven by short covers and to a lesser extent long sales (1.4 to 1).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7LCR!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff82d950d-b39c-408a-aea3-22a7e053e04d_606x498.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7LCR!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff82d950d-b39c-408a-aea3-22a7e053e04d_606x498.jpeg)

Last week’s equity rally was also indicative of a large chase for long exposure via call options.  On Thursday (12/14), 42M call options traded across all exchanges, the most for any day on record.

Speaking of small caps, we have seen a large reversion in performance over the last several weeks with Small Caps finally shedding their laggard status and joining in on the rally.  As I have covered throughout this year, Small Caps have underperformed mightily in 2023.  However, the recent breakout in small caps has led to a catch up in performance, of which we have captured to the tune of 30%+ gains in 2 weeks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!eTow!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F76b28b9e-14fa-4e12-80bb-140006067196_1188x891.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!eTow!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F76b28b9e-14fa-4e12-80bb-140006067196_1188x891.jpeg)

I am now holding 20% of my original TNA long heading into the week of 12/18.  In this situation, we had  a parabolic 2 day move in Small Caps followed by a small pullback on Friday.

This is where one must determine for themselves how much profits they are willing to give back on this trade.  Technically, TNA could easily pull back to its 8 EMA (currently $34.52) in the context of a healthy/bullish up-trend.  This would be a ~12% correction from Thursday’s highs.

Personally, on the final portion of a trade like this (which has already exceeded my high end target), I prefer to hold a small piece as a runner and allow the stock to continue to play out until my trailing stop is hit.  So long as the bullish trend continues, I will continue to hold a small piece (20%) of my original long.  With that said, one must be prepared to give back 10-15% profits in a case like this if they are continuing to hold a runner.  There is no wrong answer on how to manage a trade in this position (i.e. close the long completely or hold a small runner), so it must be determined based off one’s own risk tolerance/trading style. My stop loss on the final portion of my TNA long will be a close below the 8 EMA at $34.50.

Historically, the December through February period typically favors Small Caps over Large Caps.  If this pattern holds true, we may be in for more continued outperformance from Small Caps into 2024.

As we head into 2024, the key equity debate will be concerning the Soft vs Hard Landing narrative.  From an equity markets perspective, the Soft Landing narrative is being priced in with the S&P 500 currently near all-time highs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8hBk!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffb3830cb-40c4-4f30-a1fd-4dea1a8fad2e_1154x963.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8hBk!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffb3830cb-40c4-4f30-a1fd-4dea1a8fad2e_1154x963.jpeg)

During the past 8 Fed cutting cycles since 1984, we can see a wide distribution of outcomes for US stocks depending on whether or not the economy falls into Recession following the cutting cycle.  Equities have historically struggled when a Recession occurs shortly after the first Fed cut.  On the other hand, stocks have shown the ability to perform well when the Fed cuts and the economy avoids Recession.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5suQ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F61d3af42-ee8f-476a-9be9-42fb47f9829e_1245x759.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5suQ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F61d3af42-ee8f-476a-9be9-42fb47f9829e_1245x759.jpeg)

Hard vs Soft Landing will be the key question for markets heading into the New Year, with 2024 set to be the 1st time since 2020 that cuts outnumber hikes.

**The Week of 12.18:**

Markets enter the week of 12/18 severely overbought on multiple technical indicators.  There is no reason to fight the up-trend here. Last week’s action should be seen as very bullish following the Fed’s pivot and adjusted dot plot.

With that said, I believe patience is the best strategy for the early part of this week.  With markets extended and severely overbought, the risk/reward of opening new long positions here is skewed to the downside.  In my view, the ideal course of action in the indices to begin this week would be some consolidation/pullback as the key moving averages catch up to price.

I am currently holding long TNA (20% of original position) and long PATH (full position).  PATH closed out last week with an inside day candle and continues to act constructively after breaking through recent highs at $25.
