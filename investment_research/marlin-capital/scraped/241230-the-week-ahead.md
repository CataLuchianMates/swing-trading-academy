# The Week Ahead

The mid-week Christmas holiday led to a quiet week for US stocks last week. At first, we saw markets rebound from the post-December FOMC selloff with Tuesday’s half-day seeing one of the broadest SPX gains all year (458/500 names trading higher). The “Santa Claus Rally” faded on Friday, however, with the S&P 500 finishing -1.1% and QQQ -1.3%.

Despite the quiet/low volume trading sessions, last week brought a few Macro data points. While initial jobless claims came in below consensus (219k vs 223k consensus), continuing claims notably hit the highest weekly level since November 2021 (1,910k vs 1,884 cons and 1,874 prev). Fed Chair Powell has maintained that the labor market is in “solid shape” despite some modest cooling. This will continue to be one of the main areas to watch for investors in 2025.

Many of the other Macro data points released last week also surprised to the downside. The Conference Board reported that its index of US consumer confidence fell in December to 104.7 from 112.8 in November. This was a surprising number for most investors as this print marked a sharp contrast to the post-election animal spirits that have pulled this Bull Market higher.

For the 4th time in the past 6 months, new orders for durable goods declined, falling -1.1% vs. consensus expectations for a +0.2% rise. New home sales in November also came in below consensus expectations.

With all that said, there are plenty of reasons to remain bullish here, beginning with solid earnings. As we round out Q3 earnings, the SPX has now notched its 4th consecutive quarter of beating expectations with 75% of SPX names exceeding EPS estimates. Q3 EPS grew an impressive +7.5% YoY (vs. +3% cons) while 8/11 sectors delivered positive YoY growth). Utilities (+22% YoY), Tech (+21% YoY), and Healthcare (+13% YoY) led the way.

As we wrap up the final 2 days of 2024, it is worth nothing that the SPX dramatically outpaced nearly all Wall Street estimates this year.

Most 2025 predictions have another solid year for stocks next year. I will be releasing my full 2025 preview report in the coming days. Stay tuned!

This week is likely to be another quiet one with the Bond market opened for a shortened trading day on New Year’s Eve (but a full day for the stock market). The stock market will be closed on Wednesday, January 1st for New Year’s Day.

For me, these are not the types of days/weeks where I want to be active in my trading (I prefer higher volumes and stronger liquidity). My trailing stops were all taken out during the post-December FOMC market selloff and I did not open any new positions last week. I am heading into this week 100% cash in my active portfolio (still holding the Trump trades and Small Cap 6 in IRA, which are long term holds).

I am not looking to open any new positions at this time. Early this Monday morning, we are seeing a continuation of Friday’s selloff with the SPX and QQQ gapping down between -1.3% and -1.7%.

With that said, I am not reading too much into this current end of year market selloff. As it stands, the SPX is set to finish 2024 with +25% gains while the Nasdaq is pacing for a +30% year.

Some end of year profit taking is nothing to overreact about, in my view. It is typical to see some profit taking in early January in some of the previous year’s biggest winners as investors look to delay their tax payments to the next year. This is a known fact, however, and we seem to be seeing some frontrunning selling ahead of January in Mag 7 names, AVGO, etc.

There will be plenty of opportunities ahead in 2025. Whatever the new year may bring, I am beyond fortunate for all my readers and those who have supported me on this journey at Marlin Capital. Wishing you all a happy, healthy, and successful 2025!
