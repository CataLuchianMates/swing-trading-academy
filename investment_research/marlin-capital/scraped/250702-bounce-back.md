# Bounce Back

Before getting into today’s newsletter, I would like to share a general note about my philosophy.

If you **trade and invest** like me, I think it is important to separate your differing strategies. For me, this means maintaining distinct portfolios (literally separate accounts) for each separate strategy.

There is a big difference between **trading a stock** and **investing in a company**. It is important not to conflate the two, as this can cloud judgment and erode discipline.

For me, investing in a company is simply buying and owning shares in a business. I evaluate the business on a quarterly basis, focusing on fundamentals and long-term growth. Over the long-term, a stock’s performance will mirror the performance of the business.

Trading, on the other hand, is tactical. My active trades have defined risk (not always a hard stop necessarily, but a max % loss you are willing to lose on the trade). For me, it is typically under -12% risk, and never exceeding more than -20% risk. I am always targeting a 2x1 reward-to-risk ratio, ideally higher.

I consider myself both a **trader and investor**, and I cover both approaches/strategies in detail here at Marlin Capital.

Not the way we usually see July begin. It’s still unclear what really drove the move, but you do not always need to have a clear catalyst for violent rotation days. If you traded through the 2020-21 post-Covid Bull Market, you probably recall some of those abrupt shifts out of the WFH winners (software, ARKK, etc.) and into laggards (cruiselines, airlines, hotels, etc.). The good news is that these momentum de-grossings are typically short-lived, and do not usually last more than a couple days (throughout the last 10 years).

Over the past decade, 1W, 1M, 6M, and 1Y returns after the worst 1 day Momo drawdowns are all above average with >70% hit rates.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Bqe0!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff02b9f67-3046-49e8-a58a-9b08698d9438_1290x1563.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Bqe0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff02b9f67-3046-49e8-a58a-9b08698d9438_1290x1563.jpeg)

I have no reason to expect anything different this time around.

Yesterday’s biggest winners included Homebuilders (+4.2%) and Regional Banks (+3.5%). These are not the types of stocks that will lead a strong, sustained Bull Market over the long term. The fact that yesterday was a rotation day rather than an outright selloff (Dow finished >1%) is an even more bullish signal for the broader market. Rotations like these are healthy part of Bull markets and strong uptrends.

We saw momentum, high beta, and tech bounce back some today. My big picture view remains bullish with a $7300+ target for the SPX (big picture).
