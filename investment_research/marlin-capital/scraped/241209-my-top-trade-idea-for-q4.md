# My Top Trade Idea for Q4

The post-election market rally continued last week with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq finishing at fresh record highs.

Friday’s NFP report hit the sweet spot for stocks (not too strong, not too light) and the QQQ responded with a +0.89% rally to close out the week.

Since the November 6th election, we have seen record inflows to US equities AND Cryptos. Over the last month, US equities have seen a record $141B in inflows, the largest monthly inflows EVER (by far).

We have also seen record Crypto Fund inflows, which is a bullish sign for investor risk appetite/speculation, and shows how people are buying risk assets in response to Trump’s victory.

With 16 trading days left in 2024, the question becomes: How much more gas is left in the tank?

My view remains the same: I am looking for stocks to continue to melt up into Inauguration Day (1/20/25). There are a few reasons for my bullish outlook for the next month+.

(1) Strong Seasonals.

The end of the year and the beginning of the year are notoriously bullish times for the market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ybip!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F85d6708b-5fb6-4760-b525-f773ad44d58d_496x304.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ybip!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F85d6708b-5fb6-4760-b525-f773ad44d58d_496x304.jpeg)

We are currently following the path of a post-election melt up into Inauguration Day, which is not an uncommon occurrence.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!AqjY!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa562f490-4f73-4fe9-a5ab-f15b80702d81_1284x1206.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!AqjY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa562f490-4f73-4fe9-a5ab-f15b80702d81_1284x1206.jpeg)

(2) Money Flows.

This is the why: Why are the end/beginning of calendar years notoriously bullish times for the market? Money flows. This is the time of year when the **most** institutional capital is deployed: mechanical/passive End of year/Beginning of year money flows.

The passive/mechanical flows will become even more bullish when the calendar flips to January. This is a phenomenon known as the January Effect. The January Effect is when the largest capital in the world gets deployed into the US equity market during the first few weeks of the year.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!c_o1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe1e81a51-b56c-44d0-89a1-c29299189532_354x210.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!c_o1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe1e81a51-b56c-44d0-89a1-c29299189532_354x210.jpeg)

(3.) Low Liquidity & Volatility

Liquidity is low right now and the SPX is not moving much intraday. In fact, the average daily trading band for the SPX is currently its lowest in years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vG34!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F43068e6e-26a5-4268-b49f-dda09e3782d4_860x616.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vG34!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F43068e6e-26a5-4268-b49f-dda09e3782d4_860x616.jpeg)

Liquidity will continue to dry up as we approach Christmas and New Year’s. When liquidity is low, it is very common for stocks to simply “drift up” and continue to follow the prevailing trend (which is currently up). This is where the saying “Never short a dull market” comes from.

Look for stocks to continue to drift higher into year end.

Big picture - I am expecting this market rally to persist until mid-January. The inauguration is 1/20. I am bullish until at least the first 2 weeks of January. I don’t expect any major pullback until then. We may then start to pull in from there with the inauguration approaching/liquidity coming back/flow dynamic running its course.

**Snowflake (SNOW)**

I have been long SNOW since Day 1 of its earnings gap. Zooming out, I believe this upside rally in SNOW is still very much in the early stages.

First off, SNOW is a major beneficiary of AI adoption. The success of Gen AI models are dependent on the data that the models are trained on, increasing the importance of data strategy for both structured and unstructured data. Databases and platforms will be critical to organization of proprietary data to train LLMs.

This is where Snowflake comes in. The management team spoke about AI adoption in the company’s Q3 report, which helped ignite this whole rally:

> “Our AI adoption continues to be strong. As of the end of Q3, we have over 1,000 deployed use cases, which you can think of as individual projects we manage with our customers. Of our AI and ML products in-productions deployment, more than 3,200 accounts are now using our AI and ML features. Equally exciting is the momentum that our latest data engineering features are seeing.”

Prior to its Q3 earnings gap up, SNOW stock had essentially been in a downtrend since its IPO. This all changed after an outstanding quarter where SNOW announced EPS of 20 cents vs. 15 cents expected and revenue **of** $942 million (+28% YoY) vs. $897 million expected (+5% beat).

Snowflake also raised guidance, calling for $3.43 billion in fiscal 2025 product revenue, implying +29% YoY growth. That’s up +2% from the $3.36 billion forecast management gave [three months ago](https://investors.snowflake.com/news/news-details/2024/Snowflake-Reports-Financial-Results-for-the-Second-Quarter-of-Fiscal-2025/default.aspx).

The company’s outlook also included an adjusted operating margin of 5%, up from the 3% guidance in August.

In response to these results, the stock gapped up +20% and finished Day 1 of its earnings gap +33% on its **highest volume day EVER**.
