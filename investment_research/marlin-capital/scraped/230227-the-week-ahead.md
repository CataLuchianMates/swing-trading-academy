# The Week Ahead

US stocks are coming off their worst week of 2023.  The Fed's preferred measure of inflation is the Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) index, which tracks the prices of goods and services purchased by consumers.  January Core PCE re-accelerated to 4.7% YoY versus expectations of 4.3%, leading to the stock market selloff we saw on Friday.

In all, January’s inflation data suggests the underlying trend may have stopped moderating.  As a result, the odds of a 50 BP hike for March have risen to over 30% (from near 0), and the hope of rate cuts in the 2H are quickly dissolving.

The US 2Y yield reacted accordingly, breaking out to new highs (4.8%) for the cycle.  As I have discussed in [previous newsletters,](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-market-bottom-checklist) a rising 2Y yield is typically a sign of future rate hike expectations.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XqyC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F15a229c7-0e62-4a2c-9963-5fd46fa83f04_752x481.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XqyC!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F15a229c7-0e62-4a2c-9963-5fd46fa83f04_752x481.png)

Furthermore, [historical data shows](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-market-bottom-checklist) that in the previous 6 Bear Markets, the S&P 500 did not bottom until the 2-year yield had peaked for the cycle.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SHUr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F40d24d62-9b34-42b3-aeb7-e50944f20fcf_739x511.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SHUr!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F40d24d62-9b34-42b3-aeb7-e50944f20fcf_739x511.png)

Technically, the S&P 500 pulled into some key levels last week.  The index remains slightly above its 40-wk MA after a test of the support level on Friday ($3940).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Oi2O!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F77c4757e-cd99-4ed0-96a4-dce1e7a6ce56_1035x651.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Oi2O!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F77c4757e-cd99-4ed0-96a4-dce1e7a6ce56_1035x651.png)

The daily chart shows a similar key support level in the 200-day moving average ($3940).  The index managed to bounce after testing its 200-day MA on Friday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gyF6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F85d355d3-1afa-45fd-acd3-0e6453418bf4_1031x649.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gyF6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F85d355d3-1afa-45fd-acd3-0e6453418bf4_1031x649.png)

On both time frames, $3940 is the key downside level to watch as we enter this week.

We are set for a gap up to kick off the week today, with futures implying an open near $4000 in the SPX.  Key resistance levels to watch above include $3997-$4000 (60 EMA), $4020, and $4050-$4060 (21 EMA).
