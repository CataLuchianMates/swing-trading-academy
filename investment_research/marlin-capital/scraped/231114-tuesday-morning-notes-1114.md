The market continues to be in rally mode following this morning’s lower than expected CPI report.  Risk appetite remains present under the hood with the path of least resistance still being higher.

As [mentioned yesterday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-week-ahead-0e5), CTAs remained net short heading into this week.  I would expect this market squeeze to continue until all CTA shorts are unwound and they flip net long.  We will continue to monitor these positioning dynamics, as it has played a critical role in many/all of this year’s violent snapback rallies.

Following this morning’s lower than expected CPI report. 10 YR Treasury yields fell sharply to below 4.5%.  As I mentioned in [“The Key Story”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-key-story-19e), 10 YR yields were (in my view) the main headwind plaguing the market in the correction from late July highs. With yields on the decline, this pressure has been relieved, allowing stocks to rally so far in November.
