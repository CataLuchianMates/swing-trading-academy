# Tuesday Morning Notes 3.25

**Daily Market Commentary:**

We saw a strong rally to kick off the week yesterday (SPX +1.76%, NDX +2.16%) following reports over the weekend that Trump’s April 2nd reciprocal tariff announcement will be “narrower than initially planned, with some countries exempt”.

Cleaner/underweight positioning had the table set for a squeeze in US stocks this week, and that is exactly what we saw yesterday. Momentum, Most Short, Memes, and High Beta Cyclicals all finished Monday up > +4%.

The S&P 500 recaptured its 200-day MA ($5752) for the 1st time since March 7th. It was a low volume day overall (3rd lowest volume session of the year with 13.4B shares trading).

As I talked about yesterday, I am looking for a rally here to the 100-day MA in the S&P 500 ($5900-$5930). I entered long SPXL (3x S&P) for a trade last week and trimmed a bit more yesterday (17%) at $152.30 for a +4.7% gain. I am now holding half my original SPXL long, targeting $5900-$5930 (100 day MA).

The flow of funds remains bullish for the rest of this week with big pension buying (+40B Q-end rebal) and CTA turning to buyers in all scenarios (with convexity to the upside in a big up tape ~>$5900 level).
