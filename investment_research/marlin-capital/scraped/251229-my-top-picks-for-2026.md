#### **Data Centers**

[In 2025](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-on?utm_source=publication-search), I talked about why Data Center Infrastructure was my #1 AI theme to own.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Gr5w!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6f69dded-0e59-47bc-9ad4-51861dcbd552_1196x690.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Gr5w!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6f69dded-0e59-47bc-9ad4-51861dcbd552_1196x690.jpeg)

It remains at/near the top of my list heading into 2026.

What makes Data Centers so compelling is that demand is **contracted**, visible, and supply-constrained. We saw several massive, company transforming deals announced in 2025 (for companies like NBIS, APLD, etc.). I expect this theme to continue in 2026 as AI CapEx continues to grow.

As the physical backbone of the AI revolution, Data Center Infrastructure is one of the very first places hyperscalers are choosing to invest (as they should). Every AI model, inference workload, and real-time application ultimately runs through a data center.

We had great success investing/trading in the Data Center theme in 2025 (via +118% gains in APLD & +112% gains in NBIS). So, I see no reason to switch up my strategy for 2026. 2025 was a year of record CapEx in Big Tech, and I believe the Data Center companies that already secured mega deals are more likely to secure future deals in 2026 (rather than new Data Center industry entrants with no major deals/partnerships). After all, the companies with existing deals already have the partnerships/relationships and have shown the ability to effectively negotiate with large Hyperscalers. For that reason, my favorite way to play the Data Center theme is by owning the Big 3 (NBIS, APLD, and IREN). These 3 have the best existing deals in the industry along with more capacity for future deals.

#### **AI Hardware**

AI Hardware remains the engine of the entire ecosystem. These are the companies designing and manufacturing the chips, memory, and semiconductor equipment that make AI computation possible.

Without continued advances in compute, memory, and manufacturing equipment, AI progress simply stalls. The AI hardware layer is characterized by high barriers to entry, long lead times, and tremendous pricing power (all qualities that should support continued margin expansion and EPS growth). In 2026, I expect continued strong demand for leading-edge AI accelerators, high-bandwidth memory (HBM), and the wafer fab equipment needed to manufacture these advanced chips.

#### **My Top Picks:**

The AI King. There is no universe in which NVDA is not making a top AI picks portfolio.

#### **AI Energy & Power**

The most underrated constraint of the AI revolution is the sheer amount of power that it consumes. Training a single large language model can consume as much electricity as hundreds of homes use in a year. Meanwhile, massive data centers consume the same amount of electricity as small cities.

The energy intensity of AI is driving unprecedented demand for clean, reliable, and scalable power generation. Solar, nuclear, and other power sources are being rapidly deployed to meet this demand. But it goes beyond just power generation. AI also requires power management, storage, and delivery at unprecedented scale and precision.

For these reason, I expect AI Energy + Power to be a major theme in 2026. The companies that can deliver energy and power solutions will be essential partners in the AI revolution

#### **My Top Picks:**

**FSLR**

FSLR is the leading US solar company benefiting from the AI data center power buildout. The stock is also looking to break out of a 17+ year base.

**TLN**

TLN is a pure-play power producer with nuclear assets and is securing direct data center power deals (including a deal with AWS for for a data center in PA), positioning it as a leader in AI energy.

**CEG**

CEG is the largest nuclear operator in the US. They are directly fueling AI data centers through partnerships with MSFT and others, and are ideally positioned for more massive power-purchase agreements with AI hyperscalers in 2026.

**GEV**

GEV provides gas turbines, grid solutions, and electrification equipment critical for powering the next generation of AI infrastructure. The company just raised its guidance for 2026

**VICR**

VICR designs best-in-class power modules for high-performance computing and AI data centers.

**FLNC**

FLNC is a leader in battery energy storage systems (BESS), essential for smoothing out the power demand spikes of AI clusters. Energy storage becomes critical as AI loads stress the grid.

**POWL**

POWL manufactures electrical distribution and power management equipment essential for supporting the massive power infrastructure needs of data centers.

**MPWR**

MPWR is the king of power management chips. Their ability to integrate a whole power system onto a single chip is a massive efficiency win for AI servers.

#### **AI Healthcare**

AI Healthcare is where AI moves from answering questions and helping kids cheat on their homework to saving lives. AI Tech visionaries likes Jensen Huang, Elon Musk, and Sam Altman have all predicted massive healthcare breakthroughs due to AI in the next 10+ years.

By using AI to parse through billions of genetic data points, companies are shortening the drug discovery cycle and enabling “liquid biopsies” that can detect cancer before symptoms even appear. As models improve and datasets expand, AI will increasingly drive diagnostics, drug discovery, and personalized medicine. AI in Healthcare is still very early-stage, but this industry offers massive opportunities for companies that emerge as market leaders.

#### **My Top Picks:**

**GRAL**

One of our biggest winners of 2025 (+111%), GRAL remains a top pick for me in 2026. GRAL is leveraging AI and machine learning for early cancer detection through its Galleri multi-cancer blood test. They are the clear leader in this industry.

**NTRA**

NTRA is a leader in cell-free DNA testing that leverages AI to identify cancer recurrence at the molecular level, far earlier than traditional imaging.

#### **Robotics & Physical AI**

Robotics represents the physical manifestation of AI. This is where the “Brain” (AI) meets the Body” (Robot).

The combination of improved AI, better sensors, and more capable hardware is finally making robotics economically viable. While still early, I predict that 2026 will be a year where autonomous systems move from pilot programs to real-world deployment across manufacturing, logistics, transportation, services, etc.

The test and measurement equipment providers, semiconductor test companies, and industrial automation leaders are all positioned to benefit as robotics scales. This is very much still an emerging theme, but the long-term TAM here is ENORMOUS. Eventually, robots will handle all repetitive, dangerous, and precision tasks across every industry.

#### **My Top Picks:**

**TER**

TER manufactures automated test equipment for semiconductors and electronics, which is essential for the chips and sensors that power robotic systems.
