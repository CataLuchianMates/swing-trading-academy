# Tuesday Morning Notes 1.10

Technical Update

Yesterday’s big move in the S&P 500 reversed after hitting the $3950 level. The index closed below $3900 at $3892, back in the consolidation range.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YOVf!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F03548ade-8159-4b19-9088-5a4e5808956b_908x682.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YOVf!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F03548ade-8159-4b19-9088-5a4e5808956b_908x682.jpeg)

Technically, the shooting star reversal candle suggests more immediate downside is possible. We are set for a small gap down this morning. Support levels below include $3870-$3880, $3840-$3845, and the key Bear flag low $3800 level.

On the upside, the first resistance level to watch is once again $3900. If the SPX can clear $3900, further resistance levels to watch include $3920, $3950-$3960 (yesterday’s highs), and $3980-$4000.
