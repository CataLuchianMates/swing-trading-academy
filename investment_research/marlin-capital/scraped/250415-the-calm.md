# The Calm

It was a calm start to the week yesterday following a fairly positive weekend of tariff related newsflow. The SPX finished +0.79, NDX +0.57%, and RTY +1.14%.

Volumes have lightened up considerably, with 17.8B total shares traded across all US equity exchanges vs the YTD daily avg of 16.5B. Last Wednesday, Monday, and the previous Friday were the 3 highest volume days in the history of the US stock market.

The VIX finished -18%, and in my view, continues to form its topping pattern on the daily and hourly charts.

The VIX spiked to a high of 60 last Monday before declining ~50% in the last week to ~31. Historically, VIX spikes above 45 are a capitulation signal. Since 1997, there have been 11 VIX spikes above 45. 10 out of 11 times, the S&P 500 was higher 4 months later by an average of +6.41% and a median of +5.55%. The only exception was the 2008 Financial Crisis.

As I discussed yesterday, a topping VIX allows the market to stabilize and begin the bottoming process. Yesterday, I highlighted some choppy market bottoming patterns over the last ~15 years (2015, 2011, 2010), as this is my Base Case for 2025.

In 2015 and 2011, we actually briefly broke through the initial market lows in the S&P 500. Both times, the VIX diverged and did not make new highs despite the market making new lows months after the bottoming process began.

As you can see above, the undercuts of initial lows were shallow and there was no major downside remaining. If we were to break below last Monday’s SPX lows here in 2025, I believe we would see a divergence with the VIX (not making new highs while SPX makes new lows), and the undercut would be quite shallow (like 2011 and 2015). Overall, I believe the bottoming process has begun, but I continue to expect a choppier bottom here.

Looking at the current drawdown of stocks more broadly also pains a picture that suggest the market correction is well advanced and on par with the 2022 Bear Market (which took 10+ months to play out).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!iqJN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F768bb21b-1ef4-4ae3-82a2-377eea4c889c_829x1454.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!iqJN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F768bb21b-1ef4-4ae3-82a2-377eea4c889c_829x1454.jpeg)

As you can see above, the average SPX stock declined -34% in the 2022 Bear Market versus -29% here in 2025. The average Tech stock declined -40% in 2022 versus -37% here in 2025. Remember, the 2022 Bear Market also coincided with a shallow economic recession (2 consecutive qtrs of neg GDP) and a shallow earnings recession.

Overall, my view remains that the market bottoming process has begun here in 2025. I mentioned the VIX topping pattern above, which has allowed the market to stabilize since last Monday. The other key factor I have been covering is positioning. By any measure you use, positioning hit capitulatory levels last week. Deutsche Bank’s data has current equity positioning in the 2nd percentile since 2010.

I’ve covered Goldman’s Positioning Measure in detail, which essentially hit 15-year lows last week. Again, I view this as a clear sign of capitulation.

The focus will now shift to Earnings Season, where expectations have been revised down -6% YTD for the S&P 500.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aoq6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0f757967-15af-471a-80f6-6e1e2e343329_918x600.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aoq6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0f757967-15af-471a-80f6-6e1e2e343329_918x600.jpeg)

The key indication of this Earnings Season will be lower revisions of company guidance for the year, or any pause in investments/projects. Q1 had limited impact from tariffs or other policy changes and my expectation is the remainder of the year will face growth challenges.

As I mentioned yesterday, this is why companies that are able to buck the trend and reaffirm or even raise guidance will clearly separate themselves. These companies will be my active trading focus for the next month +.

Overall, I see the S&P 500 as most likely rangebound between $5000-5700 for the time being, as Trump “bending the knee” on tariffs put a likely floor in stocks and removed the extreme “Bear Case” scenario stocks had begun to price in. However, I also see a clear ceiling based off valuation (20x + Fwd P/E) and stagnating earnings growth (+3% YoY FY EPS growth projected).

I continue to believe we are in a “Hard Dollar” Environment (as coined by Mark Minervini), meaning this is not a time for swing/position traders to be aggressive. With that said, I do believe this Earnings Season will bring about some solid trading opportunities in select individual names, and this will be my focus for the next month +.
