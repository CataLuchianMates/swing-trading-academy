# The Week Ahead

Earnings season is in full swing, with 65% of the S&P 500 market cap having now reported Q2 results.  This week, we will get another 15% of the SPX market cap reporting, highlighted by Apple and Amazon on 8/3 post-market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EgJ1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F454a77c9-2ba7-41c1-80ac-9247eb60ecfd_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EgJ1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F454a77c9-2ba7-41c1-80ac-9247eb60ecfd_1920x1080.jpeg)

So far, earnings have been better than feared with 54% of companies beating by at least 1 standard deviation (vs historical avg of 48%).  14% of companies have missed consensus estimates by at least 1 standard deviation (vs historical average of 13%).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!F310!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc388f1b0-2417-464b-a1c4-39c8891ca224_731x531.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!F310!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc388f1b0-2417-464b-a1c4-39c8891ca224_731x531.jpeg)

On the other hand, companies that are beating estimates by >1 Std dev are only outperforming the SPX by 22 bps on the trading session directly following earnings.  Typically, beats outperform the SPX by over 100 bps.  The few companies that are missing estimates are only underperforming the SPX by -62 bp versus the historical average of -211 bps (however those missing sales are underperforming the market by -199 bps).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3p1h!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff0d42da6-2e7e-4507-a896-2fa8dc4b3b1e_1033x746.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3p1h!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff0d42da6-2e7e-4507-a896-2fa8dc4b3b1e_1033x746.jpeg)

The S&P 500 is currently once pace for a -7.3% YoY earnings decline for Q2, which would be the largest such decline since Q2 2020 (-31.6%).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cmNT!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F910bc543-3bec-4c5b-b40d-5adba48cfcc8_917x670.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cmNT!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F910bc543-3bec-4c5b-b40d-5adba48cfcc8_917x670.png)

With that said, my active trading focus remains on earnings gappers as we head into this week.  These stocks often present favorable trading setups, as I highlighted in [“The Earnings Gapper Trade Setup”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-earnings-gapper-trade-setup).  A few new stocks caught my attention late last week.

**COUR**

COUR reported on Thursday after market close, raising Q3 and FY guidance.  The company cited AI as a potential opportunity with more and more people seeking new ways to learn about the subject.

COUR stock presented a long opportunity on the 5 min chart on Friday in the first 30 minutes following its gap up.  I was not watching the stock at the time, but this setup fulfilled my [“Earnings Gapper Trade Setup”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-earnings-gapper-trade-setup).

The stock gave double inside 5 min candles with a break to the upside (above $14.54) on the 4th candle of the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2xFW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9406010b-a1af-4035-b8c1-f92906256417_1107x651.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2xFW!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9406010b-a1af-4035-b8c1-f92906256417_1107x651.png)

The proper long entry here was at $14.55 with a stop below $13.82 for -5.1% risk.

Now, I will be watching for the stock to setup on the daily chart after missing the day 1 entry.  Should we see a healthy pullback this week, I will be looking for an entry.  The weekly chart leaves plenty of room for upside into the $20-$25 range with the stock appearing to break out of its stage 1 base last week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9RY3!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5e5b918-4bed-4f41-bc6d-d74c8af0bd2c_1107x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9RY3!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5e5b918-4bed-4f41-bc6d-d74c8af0bd2c_1107x653.png)

**CLS**

CLS also emerged as a stock to watch after releasing earnings late last week.  The stock sold off on Day 1 (Thursday) after a 15%+ pre-market gap up, but bounced back in a big way on Friday (day 2).  The volume patterns are hard to ignore here, with the stock seeing its largest weekly volume in 15+ years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-BbC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F45142c3d-8d92-4407-94ff-1544472769d0_1105x651.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-BbC!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F45142c3d-8d92-4407-94ff-1544472769d0_1105x651.png)

The surge in volume was accompanied by some unusual activity in the options market on Friday, with traders making a couple large bets in the Dec ’23 $20 calls.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7GCq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd8e4b1c6-f49d-4b05-a579-d331d6171c92_458x199.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7GCq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd8e4b1c6-f49d-4b05-a579-d331d6171c92_458x199.jpeg)

I will be stalking CLS for a pullback early this week after a big move to close last week.  Some rest/consolidation would be healthy here before the next move up.

**ROKU**

ROKU has emerged as one of the early stars of earnings season with a +30% move higher on Friday.  ROKU did not present a great opportunity for entry on the 5 minute chart on day 1 (Friday), as the stock immediately surged higher after its gap up.  This is another way we can see earnings gappers move, as they do not always present ideal setups (like COUR).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!w8vp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8a92682d-f339-4746-b838-394d0cd49a8f_1107x651.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!w8vp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8a92682d-f339-4746-b838-394d0cd49a8f_1107x651.png)

In these cases, I prefer to wait and let them set up on the daily chart rather than enter on day 1 where the risk is outsized.  I am typically looking for a specific type of entry technically on day 1 earnings gapper entries (highlighted in [“The Earnings Gapper Trade Setup”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-earnings-gapper-trade-setup)), as this system has proven successful for me in my career.

With that said, a Day 1 entry is not a requirement to profit on earnings catalysts.  On the weekly chart, ROKU appears well set up for a move towards $130.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HFRf!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F590d87b3-0eab-471c-bf5a-51cbe153cd42_1109x651.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HFRf!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F590d87b3-0eab-471c-bf5a-51cbe153cd42_1109x651.png)

These are my top 3 stocks to watch as we head into this week.  With a full slate of earnings ahead, we are likely to see more potential setups emerge this week.  As always, keep an eye on the daily morning notes for daily updates.

**General Market Thoughts:**

Stay open to anything.  In the early 1970s, the Dow Jones hit a bottom and reached all-time-highs in 1973.  Then, a resurgence in inflation forced the Fed’s hand, and the Dow cratered to new lows in 1975.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QIS6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F75fe596f-2b19-4079-b8df-3b90f35df519_634x439.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QIS6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F75fe596f-2b19-4079-b8df-3b90f35df519_634x439.jpeg)

With inflation cooling, a soft landing has now become the consensus view for the US economy.  The deeply inverted 2/10 yield curve continues to say otherwise.  Based on the lagged effects shown below, it remains uncertain if this time will really be different.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HMS-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4fa18401-dbf2-462d-9888-9f19922b6a13_530x488.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HMS-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4fa18401-dbf2-462d-9888-9f19922b6a13_530x488.jpeg)

Needless to say, a soft landing is being priced into the stock market.  This is the bull case.

The Bear Case is a hard landing, of course, which is now the contrarian view.  Until a clear catalyst for that Bear Case emerges and a recession becomes imminent (spike in unemployment, etc.), it is wise to respect the bullish uptrend while remaining vigilant for any shifts in market sentiment.
