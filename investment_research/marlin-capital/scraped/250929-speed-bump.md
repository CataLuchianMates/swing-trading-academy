#### **The Little Picture**

We were due for a pullback.

US stocks rallied into the September 17th Fed meeting, and then continued melting up post-Fed meeting. High Beta Momentum had its best 2-week stretch since the 2021 Covid Bubble.

[Remember](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-dreaded-september-ff0), the last 2 weeks of September are the worst 2-week stretch on the calendar, so some seasonal weakness is completely normal and expected here.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qRuv!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5a285cc-cc00-4382-bc0e-1c17c57f96a2_501x330.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qRuv!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5a285cc-cc00-4382-bc0e-1c17c57f96a2_501x330.jpeg)

We have some additional headwinds going on in the background as well.

First is Quarter End pension selling. Goldman models -$19B of pension selling in US equities for month-end, which ranks in the 89th percentile since 2000.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YTQ7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc7f379d0-94c8-453d-a48d-9dceea63d563_1023x614.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YTQ7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc7f379d0-94c8-453d-a48d-9dceea63d563_1023x614.jpeg)

Secondly, corporates are beginning to enter their buyback blackout periods ahead of their upcoming earnings. This is part of the traditional late September seasonal weakness.

#### **The Big Picture**

Markets don’t go up in a straight line, and we were due for a breather.

Despite last week’s momentum sell off, the action was not broadly bearish. We saw money rotate from Momentum/recent AI winners to Energy (XLE +5.4%) and Utilities (XLU +2.9%). Money rotation is a healthy part of Bull Markets.

Big picture, I remain bullish and don’t see this pullback as anything more than a speed bump. As Marlin Capital readers know, I believe we are currently in an AI Bubble, similar to the Dot Com Bubble of the late ‘90s. This has been my thesis since late May, and we continue to follow it to a T.

#### Two factors give me confidence that there is still more gas left in the tank for this AI Bubble:

**1.) The Fed is resuming rate cuts with no recession on the horizon**

Assuming no recession surfaces, this is a very bullish signal historically with an avg 1yr return of +15%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!90ch!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F55fe4fa4-0658-4469-965a-2e56d57d2d31_1215x847.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!90ch!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F55fe4fa4-0658-4469-965a-2e56d57d2d31_1215x847.jpeg)

**Put simply, Bull Markets don’t peak when the Fed is cutting rates & the economy still growing.**

The Fed has cut interest rates with the S&P 500 within 1% of ATHs 16 times in its history. **The S&P 500 was higher a year later all 16 times for an average return of +15%.**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Efy5!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcb7bcdf6-2450-49b8-9311-03006b95b973_898x578.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Efy5!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcb7bcdf6-2450-49b8-9311-03006b95b973_898x578.jpeg)

Stretch that to SPX within 2% of ATHs, and it’s happened 20 times.

**All 20 times, the S&P 500 was higher a year later.**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zq5s!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F35d0f4c2-ec02-4176-9105-c3c453eb8838_975x675.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zq5s!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F35d0f4c2-ec02-4176-9105-c3c453eb8838_975x675.png)

Again, Bull Markets do not historically end while the Fed is easing.

**2.) Equity positioning is STILL neutral**

As Marlin Capital readers know, positioning [helped us call the market bottom in April](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bottomed-out) (when it hit extreme underweight levels at -2.5z).

I believe it will help us call the market top for this cycle as well.

**And right now, positioning is telling us that we are simply not there yet.**

Positioning is not at all euphoric here (as you would expect near a market top). In fact, it’s neutral.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!es5q!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4878b50c-91c7-4a65-8f1f-2691f93e5083_1267x1209.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!es5q!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4878b50c-91c7-4a65-8f1f-2691f93e5083_1267x1209.png)

This is due to Discretionary Investors. Their exposure has remained underweight (36th %ile long), even as the typical drivers of positioning, such as earnings growth and technical momentum, suggests it should be MUCH higher.

**Big picture, -0.3z total positioning is not consistent with where prior Bull Markets have peaked.**  In fact, every Bull Market since 2015 saw Goldman’s Positioning Indicator reach at least +1.8z before peaking. I do not expect this cycle to be any different.
