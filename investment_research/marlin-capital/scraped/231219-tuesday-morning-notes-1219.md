# Tuesday Morning Notes 12.19

**Daily Morning Commentary:**

The S&P 500 is coming off a 7-week win streak (longest weekly winning streak since 2017) and starts the penultimate week of the year off in the green.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cjGg!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7585bb9e-26d8-4dff-b3e2-ef50e1dbc591_1067x801.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cjGg!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7585bb9e-26d8-4dff-b3e2-ef50e1dbc591_1067x801.jpeg)

The Magnificent 7 led the charge yesterday with Mega Cap Tech +169 bps on the day.

The Rates backdrop (10 YR yield now sub 4.0%) continues to be a constructive tailwind powering the market higher into year end.  As mentioned in “Bull On The Loose”, the historical seasonality picture is bullish from here into year end.

On the flip side, we saw some consensus shorts (which greatly outperformed over the last 2 weeks) lag yesterday, with GS Prime’s most short basket closing -107 bps on the day.  GS PB noted that last week single stocks saw its first net covering in 19 weeks, which was evident in the squeezy action in highly shorted names.

Corporate buybacks just completed the first week of the estimated buyback blackout window (est. to run through 1/19/24).  GS currently estimates that ~30% of the S&P 500 are in their blackout period with ~50% in blackout by the end of the week.

No change to current positions (TNA & PATH) or stop losses.
