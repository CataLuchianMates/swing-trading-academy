# Thursday Morning Notes 3.14

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The S&P 500 closed -0.19% yesterday in a mostly uneventful day with reversion in momentum, R2K > NDX, Non-Profitable Tech > Quality, Weak BS > Strong BS, and Most Shorted > Consensus Longs.

Tech dragged with semis under pressure with the QQQ finishing the day -0.77%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PceI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe62a5d66-b5bb-4054-a07c-817112bd2e14_1154x861.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PceI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe62a5d66-b5bb-4054-a07c-817112bd2e14_1154x861.jpeg)

Yesterday was an inside day for the QQQ, giving us clear levels to watch for today’s session.  A break above $442.68 = bullish, a break below $439.12 = bearish.  Below that, the key support level remains $433-$434 on both the daily and hourly charts.

US PPI data released this morning came in hotter than expected –

\*US FEB. PRODUCER PRICES RISE 0.6% M/M; EST. 0.3%

\*US RETAIL SALES INCREASED 0.6% IN FEB.; EST. 0.8%

We are set for a gap up despite the PPI numbers as the market continues to shrug off any potentially bearish news.  The FOMC meeting next Tuesday should tell us a lot more about how the Fed feels on the hotter inflation numbers that came out this week.

BAND – Big time reversal after breaking to new highs in the morning.  Reversed its +5% gains and stopped me out for a -4.9% loss.

PLTR – I’ve traded this name twice (both profitable trades) since earnings day.  Now, it looks to be setting up once again after the recent pullback.  Yesterday was an inside day for PLTR.

Long above $25.45

Stop below $24.59 (-3.4% risk)

CRWD – The initial reaction to earnings was a sell off/profit taking, but CRWD has found support at the 21 EMA and begun to turn back up.  This gives us a potential low risk entry here.  I will treat yesterday as an inside day and look to enter long above yesterday’s highs ($334.82).
