#### APLD

In the midst of yesterday’s chaos, Applied Digital announced some HUGE news.

**Polaris Forge 2 Deal**

[Applied Digital just announced](https://ir.applieddigital.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/132/applied-digital-announces-5-billion-ai-factory-lease-with) that they have entered into a lease agreement with a U.S. Based Investment Grade Hyperscaler at its Polaris Forge 2 Campus. The lease represents $5B in total contracted revenue over an estimated 15-year lease term and covers 200 MW. The Hyperscaler also holds a first right of refusal for an additional 800 MW, representing the full expansion potential of the 1 GW Polaris Forge 2 Campus.

We saw a delayed bullish reaction in APLD today with the stock closing +9%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Tz-0!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F851c4743-b2d8-4b7c-803f-a9bff71c2943_1137x752.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Tz-0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F851c4743-b2d8-4b7c-803f-a9bff71c2943_1137x752.png)

[Back in mid-August,](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-4-stars-of-q2-earnings?utm_source=publication-search) I highlighted the potential for an upcoming Polaris Forge 2 deal:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FycK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdfa73e54-61e7-4992-9eb4-59a916f65144_1192x753.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FycK!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdfa73e54-61e7-4992-9eb4-59a916f65144_1192x753.jpeg)

And now, the deal is done!

So let’s re-run the math:

**Polaris Forge 1**

CoreWeave will be paying Applied Digital $11B over 15 years ($733M per year) for 400 MW.

**Polaris Forge 2**

With this new deal, Applied Digital will be making $5B over 15 years ($333M per year) for 200 MW.

**Totals:**

With just these 2 deals, Applied Digital will be making ~$1.07B in ARR per year. This clears the $1B+ target I gave in my [original SMID Cap 6 report](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-smid-caps-with-massive-upside-potential) back in June.

Remember, this is a company that generated just $165M in annual revenue last year. This will be a > +600% increase in ARR for Applied Digital.

But I think the most important part of this deal announcement was the right of first refusal (often called a “right of first offer”) for an additional 800 MW. This essentially means that the hyperscaler will have “first dibs” on the additional 800 MW, allowing APLD to easily expand this deal. To me, this signals that the full 1 GW is pre-committed. In this AI CapEx boom, we have every reason to believe that this hyperscaler will scale up to the full 1 GW eventually.

And if that 1 GW “scale up” happens, let’s run the math:

Current Polaris Forge 2 terms = $333M per year over 15 years

**That’s $1.67M per MW per year.**

Using the $1.67M per MW per year…800 MW × $1.67M per MW per year = **$1.33B annually.**

$1.33B plus the current $333M per year = $1.67B per year

**If the hyperscaler exercises its right of first refusal for the additional 800 MW under the same terms, APLD could generate $1.67B per year ($20B over 15 years) in total for Polaris Forge 2.**

Combined with Polaris Forge 1’s $733M (from CRWV), this would total $2.4B+ in revenue per year.

**That would be a 14x in annual revenue from 2024.**

For my money, there is no better growth story in the market right now than APLD.

#### Earnings Season

In [“Risk Off”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70), I said earnings season would be the real tell for me on the market.

So far, it has been a mixed bag.

But a lot changed today. Thursday was a promising day for bulls (the first such day in weeks).

Starting with TSLA, the first of the Mag 7 names to report.

TSLA gapped down -4.3% this morning, but the morning dip was bought up throughout the session. The stock closed +2.3%, at highs, and formed a bullish engulfing candle.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7fuI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F25b9ae52-cb8b-4c37-8060-db44609b0f3e_1266x837.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7fuI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F25b9ae52-cb8b-4c37-8060-db44609b0f3e_1266x837.png)

[Back in Q1](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-4-trade-ideas-from-q1-earnings?utm_source=publication-search), I said that it’s sometimes even more bullish to see an earnings gap down/dip that gets bought right up because that shows there is strong demand to buy at lower prices (aka support). This was the setup on our HOOD $49.48 buy.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nxV4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb5c03762-75c2-445d-b5a5-fa6ace8b2cf1_1213x747.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nxV4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb5c03762-75c2-445d-b5a5-fa6ace8b2cf1_1213x747.jpeg)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Xj2J!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F704d550e-d50b-4f5e-8eb3-3f3e3fa1ee22_1208x754.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Xj2J!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F704d550e-d50b-4f5e-8eb3-3f3e3fa1ee22_1208x754.jpeg)

We might not be seeing very many earnings gap ups this quarter (yet), but bears had every chance to take some of these names like TSLA lower and fumbled that opportunity today.

In [“Weakness”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/weakness), I said that I like to “think of price action as a battle between buyers and sellers”. Bulls had been effortlessly dominating the price action battle since April, but the tide was noticeably starting to turn in the last 2 weeks.

The market looked like it was on the edge of a cliff. Speculative names were beginning to break down, volatility rising, breadth weakening, momentum turning, and then came today’s TSLA gap down on earnings. If Bears were going to really take control of the battle, today was their day to shine. Instead, today’s morning dips were bought and the SPX closed right at the highs of its October range.

**LRCX**

LRCX gapped down -2.3% today following those strong results, but buyers stepped in to buy the dip immediately. It took just 4 minutes for LRCX to flip green today.
