# Tuesday Morning Notes 4.29

Aggressively unchanged day to kick off the week yesterday with the SPX closing +0.06% and NDX -0.03%. It was a quiet day with volumes tracking down -20-30%, which speaks to the market normalizing after a month where we saw 3 of the highest volume sessions in market history/some of the most volatile swings ever.

Technically, some consolidation/backtesting is healthy action after last week’s huge rally. I talked on [Thursday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/day-3) about the super strong market breadth, which tends to be an extremely bullish signal for forward returns.

This was the 17th NYSE Breadth Thrust Signal since 1939. Since 1939, the SPX was higher 100% of the time (16 out of 16) in 130-190 trading days with an average gain of +17.1% and +19.6%.

A NYSE Breadth Thrust means that market breadth has improved from weak to strong over a short period of time. A BT occurs when the 10-day EMA of advancing issues divided by advancing issues + declining issues (Advances / Advances + Declines) moves from a weaker breadth reading of below 0.40 to a stronger breadth reading of above 0.615 within 10 trading days.

Again, the last 16 such signals dating back to 1939 preceded higher prices in 130 and 190 trading days. **Returns are much higher than average following a NYSE Breadth Thrust.**
