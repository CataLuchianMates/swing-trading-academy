# Thursday Morning Notes 11.30

**Daily Market Commentary**

The S&P 500 reversed its morning gap up yesterday, finishing the day -0.09%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K1v4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa241fe24-79b0-41ed-9010-10399d13b966_1038x779.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K1v4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa241fe24-79b0-41ed-9010-10399d13b966_1038x779.jpeg)

This is not the best looking candle for the SPX, as the index ran into heavy resistance at $4575-$4580.  Yesterday’s candle leads me to believe we may need some more consolidation/sideways chop in the indices in the near term.

With that said, we saw some outperformance from small caps/software/YTD laggards yesterday.  Lower yields, combined with recent dovish Fed commentary, has triggered a rotation out of YTD winners and into secular growth names.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!w22a!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F70d72100-32db-4fdf-ac4f-45814b66e986_1186x848.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!w22a!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F70d72100-32db-4fdf-ac4f-45814b66e986_1186x848.jpeg)

In my view, this rotation still has more to go.  I am playing this potential catch up trade/reversion trade via TNA (3x small caps) long.  My stop loss will remain below $27.50 (closing stop).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fb8K!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd3169ff6-4654-4ce3-9e74-835bca84f65f_970x724.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fb8K!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd3169ff6-4654-4ce3-9e74-835bca84f65f_970x724.jpeg)

Yesterday’s Q3 GDP report showed faster than expected real growth for the economy (5.2% vs 5% expected) and a lower pace of core PCE inflation (2.3% vs 2.4%).  Stronger growth, albeit backward looking, and lower inflation is a recipe for the goldilocks economy we currently find ourselves in, especially when set against what looks to be a definitive change in tone from Fed speakers.  This gives credence to the “soft landing” outcome for the global economy that a majority of market participants are expecting.

Yesterday, Mester stressed the importance of being nimble and responding to both sides of the dual mandate while nothing that going forward “monetary policy is in a good place for policymakers to assess incoming information on the economy and financial conditions.”  This is a considerable shift from her comment 2 weeks ago when she asserted “it’s not about cutting rates, it’s about how long we stay in a restrictive stance, and possibly go higher”.
