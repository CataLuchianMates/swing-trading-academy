# Thursday Morning Notes 6.13

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The S&P 500 rallied +0.85% yesterday, clocking its 28th all-time-high of 2024 so far.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IEXU!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3772919a-f16a-4e59-a107-5005ee7a194a_1194x887.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IEXU!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3772919a-f16a-4e59-a107-5005ee7a194a_1194x887.jpeg)

Yields eased 9bps to 4.31% following a very soft CPI with Core MoM coming in at +0.16% (the lowest reading since August 2021).  The Fed Meeting was largely a non-event, with the median dot plot now showing only 1 cut for 2024 (down from 3 in March) and now 100bps of cuts in both 2025 and 2026 (vs 75bp each in the March projections).  This would keep the cumulative amount of rate cuts across 2024-2026 unchanged at 225bps.

My big picture thoughts – There is nothing bearish about yesterday’s CPI data, Fed meeting, or price action.  It is hard to find a reason to be bearish right now with inflation continuing to ease and the market looking ahead to rate cuts beginning in September.  Historically, the Fed has cut rates 20 times with the market at ATHs.  All 20 times, the SPX was higher a year later (source – Ryan Detrick).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hjT9!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6b5b58f3-723b-488b-b457-e829bbdfe721_2048x711.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hjT9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6b5b58f3-723b-488b-b457-e829bbdfe721_2048x711.jpeg)

Mega Caps are breaking out to new highs as the biggest companies continue to get bigger.  AAPL just had its 2nd best two day run in over 15 years while MSFT broke out to new ATHs yesterday.  AVGO is set for a gap up this morning following earnings, propelling the Semi sector to new ATHs pre-market.

Not to mention, we are ~2 weeks away from entering the best seasonal period of the calendar year.  The 1H of July is the best 2 week period of the calendar year.  Moreover, the NDX has finished positive for 16 straight July’s with an average return of +4.6%.  It is difficult for me to come up with any reason why this July would be different.
