# Thursday Morning Notes 11.2

**Daily Market Commentary**

Yesterday’s Fed meeting played out as expected with no hike or hawkish surprise from the committee.  There were no major changes to the Fed statement apart from acknowledging the strength of the data in the Q3 GDP report.  What is there suggests a dovish bias to wait and see whether stronger activity impedes progress towards the Fed’s 2% inflation target or not.

All of this led to a broad rally in US stocks yesterday with the S&P 500 finishing the day +1.05%.  The index has now rallied back to its 200-day moving average (along with the 21 EMA).  $4250-$4260 is the key resistance level to watch in today’s session.

The recent bounce appears to be a “quality bid” this week as FAAMG has led the rally.  Six names contributed over 60% of the move in the Nasdaq yesterday (MSFT, AAPL, AMZN, NVDA, META, AMD).  Mega Caps will stay in focus today with AAPL set to announce earnings after today’s close.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0VXW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F81cf671c-051a-4018-a004-f84fa784b677_1129x535.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0VXW!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F81cf671c-051a-4018-a004-f84fa784b677_1129x535.jpeg)

Conversely, lower quality meme stocks and most short rolling backs both underperformed yesterday.  Said another way, the drop in yields yesterday is clearly a bullish catalyst for equities, but not to the point where investors are interested in lower quality/higher beta names.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cOl4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa6e28a1a-1b7f-45ef-b1a1-3d8e239ed0e1_1121x464.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cOl4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa6e28a1a-1b7f-45ef-b1a1-3d8e239ed0e1_1121x464.jpeg)

The preference for quality in yesterday’s rally is evident in the outperformance of strong versus weak balance sheet names.  Yesterday was the 2nd largest 1-day outperformance of strong balance sheet names in the last year.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wIj7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb8aa4dc7-5e5d-4041-b1be-ceb6c718406a_1234x649.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wIj7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb8aa4dc7-5e5d-4041-b1be-ceb6c718406a_1234x649.jpeg)
