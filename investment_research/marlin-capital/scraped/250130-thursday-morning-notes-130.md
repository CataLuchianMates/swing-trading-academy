# Thursday Morning Notes 1.30

**Daily Market Commentary:**

US stocks finished slightly lower yesterday (SPX -0.47%, NDX -0.25%) with the Fed holding rates unchanged as anticipated (+ some watered down language on inflation progress…Fed Funds futures now pricing 1st full cut in June). Overall, Powell said a whole lot of nothing during the press conference as he walked back some of the more hawkish language in the statement (market initially sold off on some of the more hawkish language in the statement but pared losses during Powell’s presser) .

After hours, we had MSFT, TSLA, and META earnings:

MSFT - Solid Q4 Revs & EPS beat + big enough rev guide, offset by higher CY25 total expenses. Azure growth came in a touch light +31% vs expectations of +32-33%.

In PR: “Already, our AI business has surpassed an annual revenue run rate of $13B, up +175% YoY.” On the call, MSFT guided for Q3 Azure growth to be +31-32% and FY operating margins up slightly. On CapEx, they guided Q3 and Q4 to be similar to Q2 and FY26 CapEx growth lower than FY25.

TSLA - Revs and gross margins came in slightly below and the 2025 delivery guide pretty vague (only says to expect growth, but not quantifying it). However, as most people know, the future story for Tesla is not predicated around car sales. The post-market dip was immediately bought up and we are set for a gap up in TSLA this morning.

META - Solid Q4 rev beat, but Q1 rev guide was below Street estimates ($39.5-$41.8B vs cons $41.67B). Zuckerberg commented on any impact on future CapEx DeepSeek might have:

"It's probably too early to have a strong opinion on what this means for CapEx. We still believe heavy CapEx will be a long-term strategic advantage. Maybe we’ll learn otherwise at some point, but it’s way too early to make that call."

We also got a nice beat and raise quarter by LRCX, which is boosting Semis this morning.

Big picture - we are off to a strong start in Q1 earnings and not seeing any spillover effect from DeepSeek / any companies scaling back on AI spending. As I mentioned earlier in the week, Monday’s selloff was clearly a case of “Sell First, Ask Questions Later” for Tech. It was definitely a panic move in many AI names with NVA shedding over $600B in value. Goldman’s Broad AI Basket had its worst day since the March 2020 Covid Crash (on a day where the SPX fell over -10%).
