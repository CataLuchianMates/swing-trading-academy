Technical Analysis

A muted market reaction so far to the slightly higher than expected CPI number for January. As of this writing, futures are basically unchanged/down slightly.

The S&P 500 rallied into today’s event to kick off the week yesterday, closing +1.14%. On a technical basis, the index found support at the 21 EMA last week ($4060).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aC2i!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F45e3c1a1-7cad-4d5f-b189-378fee4ed35e_1253x954.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aC2i!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F45e3c1a1-7cad-4d5f-b189-378fee4ed35e_1253x954.jpeg)

No key levels were broken in yesterday’s rally. I will continue to be patient until we break last week’s inside Week to the upside or downside. A break below $4060 is bearish. A break above $4176 is bullish.

Other key resistance levels ahead include $4155-$4160.

Key support levels below include $4090-$4100 and $4060.
