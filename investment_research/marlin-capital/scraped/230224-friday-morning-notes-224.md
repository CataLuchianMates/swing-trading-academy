Technical Update

The S&P 500 bounced higher yesterday, finishing +0.53% on the day. That move has all been erased and more this morning with futures pointing to a sharp gap down following higher than expected PCE.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!u9xX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fce8fcf91-290b-45a9-b2ec-6e76fd6574fe_872x646.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!u9xX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fce8fcf91-290b-45a9-b2ec-6e76fd6574fe_872x646.jpeg)

As of this writing, we are currently looking at a gap below yesterday’s lows. Technically, the stage is now set for a test of the 200 SMA ($3940). This is a critical support level for the S&P 500. A decisive break below $3940 opens the door for a further drop to $3900 and later $3800. If the index starts to slice through these key support levels, new Bear market lows can come much quicker than anyone thinks. Major caution is warranted here.
