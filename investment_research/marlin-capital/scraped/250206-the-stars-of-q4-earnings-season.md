# The Stars of Q4 Earnings Season

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8rpF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F26e11fd7-4a23-4f8c-b633-5914accbe3c2_1307x669.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8rpF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F26e11fd7-4a23-4f8c-b633-5914accbe3c2_1307x669.png)

As Marlin Capital readers [know](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-earnings-gapper-trade-setup?utm_source=publication-search), I firmly believe a company’s earnings report is the most important catalyst for its stock. The earnings report sets the tone for how the stock will trade the rest of the quarter. Often times, the momentum from a big earnings beat/guidance raise will carry over for the entire quarter (into the next earnings report). This is my favorite time to trade stocks because you have a fundamental catalyst, momentum, and volume.

**Fundamental Catalyst**

Earnings reports provide critical data to investors about a company's financial health. This information directly influences investor perceptions of the company's value and future prospects. A beat or miss will significantly alter the stock's perceived value instantly.  
  
**Momentum**

An earnings beat/guidance raise typically creates a surge of optimism. This optimism translates into buying pressure, which can lead to sustained upwards momentum in the stock. Conversely, disappointing earnings can lead to selling pressure and downward momentum. This is why the initial reaction to earnings can set the tone for weeks or even months, as traders and investors adjust their positions based on the new information.  
  
**Volume**

Earnings announcements bring increased trading volume. This increased volume typically leads to bigger and more volatile price movements, offering traders opportunities to capitalize on these swings.  
  
**Analyst Revisions**

Post-earnings, analysts typically revise their future earnings and revenue estimates based on the new data. If analysts raise their forecasts, it can further fuel positive momentum, as these upwards revisions often attract more buyers.  
 **Information Asymmetry Correction**

Prior to the earnings release, there is a lot of speculation and uncertainty. The earnings report reduces this asymmetry by providing hard data, which traders and investors use to realign their expectations, resulting in big price movements in the stock.

Now that I’ve covered the key reasons why I believe earnings reports are so important on Wall Street, let’s dive into the data for Q4.

[This morning](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/wednesday-morning-notes-25), I talked about how we are currently in a market of “haves” and “have nots”. We are seeing this clearly play out in Q4 earnings as well, where companies that beat estimates are outperforming more than usual (+1.3% vs historical avg of +1.0%), and companies missing estimates are being punished even more than usual (-4.3% vs historical avg of -2.1%).

* PLTR, NFLX, SPOT are the top Large Cap Liquids that have reported so far. You can throw META in there as well.
* If the China theme continues to be strong, TAL is 1 to watch closely.
