# The Moment of Truth

After trading higher for 14 of 15 weeks, the NDX stumbled last week – falling -1.5% - as rates backed up (US 10 YR 4.3%) amidst hotter inflation prints (CPI, PPI).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gzXb!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb8f2cc2d-c1bc-42cb-a74f-faac924903a6_977x719.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gzXb!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb8f2cc2d-c1bc-42cb-a74f-faac924903a6_977x719.jpeg)

Last week’s inflation data caused the Fed Funds Futures market to further push back the first Fed rate cut to the June FOMC meeting.  The futures market is now pricing in just shy of 4 rate cuts for 2024 (versus the consensus 6+ coming into 2024).

In tandem with the move in rates, we saw a sharp late week reversal in momentum as Goldman’s Momentum pair reversed 350 bps on Thursday/Friday, following a red hot stretch that saw the factor’s 14d RSI erupt to 87.  Below is the weekly scoreboard:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!L09K!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F78916bf1-df87-4e64-984c-fbe9fd170e6f_1085x2007.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!L09K!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F78916bf1-df87-4e64-984c-fbe9fd170e6f_1085x2007.jpeg)

Exiting the week, the tape certainly felt a bit more complicated and less directional as investors started to wonder about the catalyst path once we get past NVDA’s earnings print on Wednesday.  A handful of brand-name Tech stocks started to show a bit of technical fatigue – think ADBE below 50/100 day MA, GOOGL, MU, TMUS all below their 50 SMAs, and AAPL below its 200 day MA.  This came alongside a number of white hot AI names reminding us that momentum can work in both directions (think ARM & SMCI both turning in -20% days last week).

We also now enter a more difficult seasonal period for stocks.  Historically, the 2nd half of February has been a challenging period for the market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DjSN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5f0f294-b0fe-4dbd-a84d-07dcb32c1a24_1164x526.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DjSN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5f0f294-b0fe-4dbd-a84d-07dcb32c1a24_1164x526.jpeg)

In fact, the last 2 weeks of February are the worst 2 weeks of the year for the S&P 500 dating back to 1928.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6Gk7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fec1fd9d8-7c41-492d-bd3c-965171b0d891_1117x741.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6Gk7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fec1fd9d8-7c41-492d-bd3c-965171b0d891_1117x741.jpeg)

Zooming out, one feature of this run in tech has been that the market has not really pushed investors to do uncomfortable things / pay exorbitant multiples for AI/Tech exposure.  Over the last year, one could access the AI theme via the largest companies in the world trading at mostly reasonable PE multiples (think Mag 7 ex NVDA trading at 20-35 PE) – not all that euphoric relative to the 50-100x PE multiple of the Dot Com Bubble (or the 10-50x EV/S multiples of the Covid Bubble).

When compared to the Dot Com Bubble, valuations at the top are nowhere near as frothy as they were back then.  The 5 largest stocks traded at a 43x Fwd PE in March 2000, a 59% premium to the Mag 7’s current multiple of 27x.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9Q2-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0902c93-8341-4b91-bb25-624c257bf8eb_1222x939.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9Q2-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0902c93-8341-4b91-bb25-624c257bf8eb_1222x939.jpeg)

Along these lines, despite all of the excitement and energy in the Tech narrative this year, the NDX is ‘only’ up 5% YTD and the SOX is only off to its 4th best start in the last 6 years – hardly a ‘crazy start to the year’ by those measures.

In the upcoming holiday shortened week, all of the focus will be on NVDA earnings Wednesday.  The options market is pricing in a ~11% directional move on earnings – notable given the last 2 quarters NVDA hasn’t hit that mark despite beating EPS by 20-30% on both prints.  There is plenty of debate heading into the report whether this print will be a local top or a breakout moment for the stock and the AI trade in general (and perhaps, the market in general).

NVDA is now up >50% since last quarter’s earnings, surpassing both GOOGL and AMZN in market cap in the interim.  Investors are once again looking for Nvidia to deliver a clear beat and raise print.  For context, NVDA has beat revenue by ~$2B the last couple of quarters (vs consensus modeling $20B in Jan qtr revs), which will likely serve as a measuring stick for this print.  Commentary/guidance on the April quarter will be just as important (recall, NVDA guided Jan qtr +10% Q/Q) and the 2025 roadmap.

Recent history of NVDA prints have been more suggestive of local top moments than breakout moments.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8OfG!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F96e374dc-8b3b-4e07-b8cf-7d69ad559932_1137x847.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8OfG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F96e374dc-8b3b-4e07-b8cf-7d69ad559932_1137x847.jpeg)

The Q4 earnings season has highlighted the ongoing fundamental strength of Mega Cap/Mag 7 stocks.  In fact, if NVDA reports in line with consensus, the Mag 7 will have grown sales by 14% YoY and lifted net margins by 747 bps YoY, leading to earnings growth of 58%.  In contrast, the remaining 493 stocks in the S&P 500 grew sales by 2% YoY while net margins contracted by 110 bps and earnings fell -2% YoY.

Moreover, aggregate revenues for the Magnificent 7 are projected to exceed that of the entire Russell 2000 for the first time in 2024.
