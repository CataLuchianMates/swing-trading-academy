Technical Update

With the CPI tail risk now out of the way, we can focus more on the technicals. Inflation was last year’s story, and while I believe a recession will be 2023’s ultimate story, there is no news catalyst in that regard just yet.

Futures have been volatile this morning following the CPI report, but the key is that a 50 bps hike has now been taken off the table (by the futures market) after this report. We are set for a small gap up in the indices as of this writing.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sFUY!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff1c36272-fb74-4aa5-973f-5410fb3eff2d_961x720.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sFUY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff1c36272-fb74-4aa5-973f-5410fb3eff2d_961x720.jpeg)

Technically, the key level on the daily chart is the 200 SMA ($3987). This is where the previous Bear market rallies have topped out (after falsely breaking above).

In the absence of any negative news catalysts, it is possible to see a more sustained move above the 200 SMA this time around. The key for the SPX will be stabilizing above the $4000 level and holding the 200 SMA on any pullbacks. If this can happen, my focus on trading will be value oriented sectors (as discussed in last week’s report). Remember, if this rally truly is anything more than the previous 2-3 Bear bounces, it has hardly even begun. If that is the case, there should be more and more opportunities in individual stocks arising as the indices stabilize above the 200 SMA.
