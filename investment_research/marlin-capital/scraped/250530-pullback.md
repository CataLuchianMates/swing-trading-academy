# Pullback

The stock market reversed to close near unchanged yesterday and is following through to the downside so far this afternoon.

Reversal of yesterday’s morning gap up seemed warranted. The court ruling overturning the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA) basis for universal 10%+ tariffs is a setback to for the administration’s tariff plans, but will certainly not be the final outcome on trade. There is plenty more to the tariff story still to come.

On a technical basis, yesterday’s reversal was a bearish candle for the indices. Today, we are seeing more follow through to the downside with the QQQ currently down around -1.1%.

Underneath the surface, I am seeing much more bearish action in leading names and industry groups. Starting with the high beta leaders - Nuclear stocks (SMR, OKLO, LEU, etc.) along with Quantum Computing stocks (QBTS, IONQ, QUBT etc.) were 2 of the leading groups during the market runup. In our current market, stocks like these are a good barometer of where risk appetite stands because they are seen as more speculative plays. It is very common for high beta names to top and pull back before the indices start to. These higher beta groups have pulled back hard this week and are starting to appear like they may have formed a temporary top.

It is not just the action in high beta/speculative plays that are showing some red flags, though. We are seeing names I consider to be liquid leaders in the current market like NFLX, GEV, UBER, CRWD, etc. pull back sharply off ATHs. These names have been rallying hard for the past month +, but we finally saw some selling volume come in to these stocks during yesterday’s reversal.

The stocks in my Active Portfolio are also pulling back, and I consider these names to each be leaders.

Remember, it is a market of stocks. For me, the leading stocks/industry groups provide a clearer picture of market health than broad indices. Leaders lead, in both Bull and Bear Markets. Some of the greatest traders of all time like Nicolas Darvas, Bill O’Neil, Mark Minervini, etc. have all echoed the importance of following the leaders over the indices.

Looking bigger picture, I am not overly concerned about the action in the indices and leading names. It is natural to get a pullback and consolidation after a big month + runup.

However, I do believe we are potentially setting up for more downside early next week, based off the action in leading names and the reversal in the indices. It is possible we test recent lows (and the 200-day MA) at $5767 or shakeout below it.

Short-term, I am cautious here. Bigger picture, I remain bullish overall. I explained why in “The Big Picture”, as it all comes down to AI and the revolutionary impact we are going to see in the near future. We are at a point in time in history where you do not want to lose sight of the big picture, however loud short-term macro noise gets.
