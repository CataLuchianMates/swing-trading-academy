# Friday Morning Notes 3.10

Technical Update

Yesterday was a critical day for the market as the S&P 500 decisively broke below key support.

The S&P 500 lost the 200 day MA support level ($3940) yesterday, its first close below the line in nearly 2 months.

**US Jobs Report (3/10/23)**

* Payrolls +311k vs. +225k estimate.
* Unemployment rate 3.6% vs. 3.4% estimate.
* Average hourly earnings up 0.2% vs. 0.3% estimate.
* Average weekly hours 34.5 vs. 34.6 estimate.
* Labor force participation 62.5% vs. 62.4% estimate.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mgIA!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc31e49c1-3907-415a-9ceb-f52e98a27c21_2048x591.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mgIA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc31e49c1-3907-415a-9ceb-f52e98a27c21_2048x591.png)

We are set for a small gap up this morning after the jobs report data. With unemployment ticking up and the problems arising in Silicon Valley Bank, the odds of a 50 BP hike in March have now fallen below 50%.

This does not change my Bearish view, however, and more importantly, I will only be triggered on my short trade ideas on a break below yesterday’s lows. I am also comfortable with the risk in these 2 setups, and have (at least 3) more potential short trade ideas in individual stocks that I will begin talking about if these trades start to gain traction (adding progressive exposure).
