# Wednesday Market Notes 5.25

Technical Analysis

Some aggressive early day selling yesterday, but the S&P 500 finished the day down just 0.8%. It didn’t feel like that underneath the surface as growth stocks got hammered. For SPY, the $387 support level marked the low of the day. If that level gives way in the coming days, new lows should follow.

Given some of the extreme short term selling pressure readings we have gotten, it is not surprising to see the market at least attempting to bounce. However, the attempts are lackluster at best so far. I expect the dizzying intraday volatility to continue as liquidity dries up. This means big bounces and rug pulls, so it’s best to stay patient and trade lighter in an environment like this. If you weren’t entertained yet, QT starts next week, so the liquidity will be getting (much) worse.
