In [“Headwinds”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/headwinds), I walked through every major headwind plaguing this market right now, from the Iran war sending oil prices parabolic, to the AI labor market shakeup accelerating, to credit conditions deteriorating, to the overall technical picture confirming a downtrend.

All of those headwinds remain firmly in place heading into this week.

So, the 2 main questions I want to focus on tonight are simple. 👇

**How close are we to a bottom?**

**And where are we on my capitulation scale?**

These are the most important questions in the market right now, in my view. And the answers may surprise you.

In [“Headwinds”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/headwinds) last week, I laid out the 3 main things I am watching before deploying long-term capital. One of those was positioning data.

Positioning data was the main factor that helped us time the April 2025 Liberation Day lows ([within 2 days of the exact market bottom](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bottomed-out?utm_source=publication-search)). And right now, it is telling me something very important. 👇

**Not Much Reason to Predict a Bottom Here**

Let’s start with the technicals.

The S&P 500 and Nasdaq just broke below their 200-day SMAs for the first time since May 2025. As I highlighted in [“Rolling Over”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/rolling-over?utm_source=publication-search), the overall price action is not indicative of a market trying to bottom. The rounded top pattern I first identified continues to play out, and the moving averages are confirming the new technical downtrend.

On Friday, the SPX closed right at my first key support level (the $6450-$6500 anchored VWAP from Liberation Day lows).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KvB3!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5f88e95b-ee77-49de-8eb6-7c01468db9a3_1566x846.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KvB3!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5f88e95b-ee77-49de-8eb6-7c01468db9a3_1566x846.png)

A counter-trend bounce is always possible after a 4-week dump like this, but the overall technical setup does not point to a bottom just yet.

With that said, it is very important to remember that we are in a headline driven market right now. And that cuts both ways.

Despite all the negatives, it should NOT be lost on anybody that 1 positive headline from Iran could cause oil prices to tumble and equities to rally big. A year ago at this time, US stocks were melting down on tariff news. If you remember back to last year’s Liberation Day selloff, the SPX bottomed on the headline of a tariff pause.

To be clear though, that was after a ~20% SPX drawdown, when the administration was feeling real pressure from the stock market meltdown. Right now, we are at -6.8%. A -6% SPX drawdown is not unusual. These types of pullbacks happen basically every year, throughout history. Putting this little selloff into historical context, it is just a teeny tiny blip (see below).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wOg6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F57b0cb06-8687-4bde-8c2d-cb75655a5b94_1080x645.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wOg6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F57b0cb06-8687-4bde-8c2d-cb75655a5b94_1080x645.jpeg)

This is not the kind of stock market pain that forces any type of major policy changes.

Still, the important thing to remember here is that 1 headline could turn things around at any moment. This is why I think getting too bearish is also a trap here, despite the technicals pointing to more downside ahead.

So, if the technicals alone are not enough to call a bottom in a headline-driven tape, what IS?

For me, it all comes down to positioning.

**It’s All About Positioning, Not Sentiment**

Bearish sentiment has increased considerably. The AAII Bull-Bear Spread has dropped to -21.6, its lowest reading since April 2025.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!z2zS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fca4a2e73-d566-492a-905c-d7b113f26090_1281x729.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!z2zS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fca4a2e73-d566-492a-905c-d7b113f26090_1281x729.jpeg)

But, anyone who has experienced **real extreme fear** in the market (like when the Nasdaq was falling -13% in a day during the March 2020 Covid Crash and you couldn’t pay people to buy stocks, or at the end of the 2022 Bear Market when people were throwing in the towel and no one wanted META at $88 or NVDA at $12), knows this isn’t really it.

While sentiment indicators are useful to monitor (especially in the short term), I view Positioning as far more important than Sentiment. Positioning is the ultimate sentiment indicator, in my opinion, because it tells you what investors are actually doing. Not what they are saying. Not how they are feeling. What they are actually doing with their money.

One of my favorite quotes on this topic comes from famed value investor Benjamin Graham. 👇

> “In the short run, the market is a voting machine.”
>
> Benjamin Graham

People vote with their money. During real times of extreme fear, there is a genuine reluctance (for most every investor and most all funds) to buy. That reluctance shows up in the data. And when it reaches extreme levels, that is when sustainable bottoms form.

As I talked about in [“Headwinds”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/headwinds) last week, positioning data was the main factor that helped us time the April 2025 Liberation Day lows. We [called the bottom within 2 days](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bottomed-out?utm_source=publication-search). 👇

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nSvA!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F599424e1-945f-4c05-978f-e1e391869df2_1566x846.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nSvA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F599424e1-945f-4c05-978f-e1e391869df2_1566x846.png)

This is the same framework I am using right now, because I believe there are many similarities in these 2 market selloffs.

**Where Are We on the Capitulation Scale?**

Right now, total positioning does not reflect extreme fear or capitulation yet.

Goldman’s Positioning Indicator has fallen from +1.4z at the start of January to -0.3z now. 👇

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NfYz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F49759d42-9d60-4cf8-9caa-31bc658f66de_1080x1086.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NfYz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F49759d42-9d60-4cf8-9caa-31bc658f66de_1080x1086.jpeg)

This is a meaningful reset. But, it is still far from the levels that have historically marked SPX bottoms. Those lows have typically occurred closer to -1 standard deviation or worse (usually -2 standard deviations for major corrections). For reference, the Positioning Indicator hit -2.5z at the Liberation Day lows last April (when I wrote [“Bottomed Out”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bottomed-out?utm_source=publication-search)).

The current reading of -0.3z suggests investors are no longer aggressively long, but are not significantly underweight either. It is slightly below neutral right now. This is not capitulation.

The positioning reset has begun, but the process does not look complete to me here. We have yet to see any signs of genuine capitulation selling.

Under the surface, the systematic flows tell an improving (but not panicked) story.

CTAs (Commodity Trading Advisors) have flipped net short in the US. Over the last week, CTAs sold -$78B in US equities (and -$146B in the last 1M). These are big numbers.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wrkY!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd9273116-a330-4183-bbff-aedc4655d357_866x544.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wrkY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd9273116-a330-4183-bbff-aedc4655d357_866x544.png)

This suggests we are likely through the bulk of the mechanical selling in this cohort. Still, they have room to add more shorts if this downtrend persists.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bjeP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F68bbaa2f-bd6b-4202-be38-cb699f16e2e7_961x624.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bjeP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F68bbaa2f-bd6b-4202-be38-cb699f16e2e7_961x624.png)

At the same time, Vol Control Funds and Risk Parity Funds (which represent ~2/3rds of systematic funds) have not meaningfully reduced exposure yet. This creates an additional layer of potential selling pressure if volatility continues to rise.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!b9z3!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5e56e94d-a707-4e63-bd55-183f07cfd723_786x499.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!b9z3!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5e56e94d-a707-4e63-bd55-183f07cfd723_786x499.png)

Altogether, total positioning has improved, but is far from washed out. We are currently in the middle ground (slightly below neutral). And we have certainly seen no signs of capitulation or panic selling yet. 👇

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!a6x6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F66d0f75d-1dfb-4805-95ee-071101e455de_1064x638.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!a6x6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F66d0f75d-1dfb-4805-95ee-071101e455de_1064x638.png)

Beyond positioning, I am also watching oversold conditions closely. And right now, the market is not oversold yet. Only ~14% of S&P 500 stocks have hit oversold levels so far.

For context, that number reached >50% during last April’s Liberation Day selloff and >40% during the 2022 Bear Market. 👇

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lbem!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F09a2ce3b-6f06-4074-bf80-6598b6c8f17a_741x498.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lbem!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F09a2ce3b-6f06-4074-bf80-6598b6c8f17a_741x498.png)

14% is nowhere close to the kind of oversold washout we typically see at meaningful market bottoms.

#### **The Big Picture**

In my view, caution remains highly warranted here.

The bottom line is that we are currently in a broad de-risking phase for US equities, and there is no reason to fight the tape right now.

The macro environment has shifted meaningfully over the past several weeks. What started as isolated weakness in speculative names ([back in October](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70?utm_source=publication-search)) has now spread to the broader market. The war in Iran is keeping oil prices elevated, the Fed easing tailwind has all but evaporated, and credit conditions continue to deteriorate. The headwinds I laid out in detail last week remain firmly intact, and if anything, they are intensifying.

Until I see 1 of these 2 things happen, I am staying patient and protecting capital. 👇

1) Headlines start to meaningfully improve (particularly on Iran/oil, which is the primary driver of this selloff right now)

2) We reach signs of genuine capitulation selling, which for me means positioning reaching at least -1 std dev AND >40% of SPX stocks hitting oversold levels

We are not there yet on either front.

This is why I am fully prepared for more downside ahead. My base case remains a decline to $6300, which is another ~3% from here. Buying opportunities will come for those who are patient. But again, we are simply not there yet.

I have said it before, and I will say it again. Preparing a plan and executing a plan are 2 very different things. Here at Marlin Capital, we have prepared and built our plan. My downside levels are set. The key signals I am watching closest are clearly defined. When those signals arrive, I will be ready to act. Until then, I am protecting capital and staying patient. It is not time for execution just yet.
