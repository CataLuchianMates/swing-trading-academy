# Friday Morning Notes 7.21

Technical Update

It was not a positive start to big tech earnings season with TSLA and NFLX taking it on the chin yesterday. The QQQ finished -2.31% and closed below its 8 EMA for the first time in over a month.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cx5E!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1429f55a-8c13-4922-bdd1-921f0b6a91b2_1195x895.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cx5E!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1429f55a-8c13-4922-bdd1-921f0b6a91b2_1195x895.jpeg)

Tech, specifically big tech, has unquestionably led this YTD rally. Yesterday, we saw some money rotate into Dow 30 stocks, with the index closing green (+0.47%) on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DwuB!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe68ab29b-252c-4d13-8ea5-ab774828276e_1204x889.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DwuB!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe68ab29b-252c-4d13-8ea5-ab774828276e_1204x889.jpeg)

Meanwhile, the S&P 500 finished -0.66% on the day and remains firmly above all key moving averages.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Q9F0!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5596a676-f8d6-4938-b348-849e695d585b_1188x889.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Q9F0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5596a676-f8d6-4938-b348-849e695d585b_1188x889.jpeg)

Next week (with MSFT and GOOGL reporting) should give us a better idea if this rotation out of tech will lasting or if it is simply a 1 day event. Technically, the QQQ now appears the weakest of the 3 indices, but that can change quickly with a strong close to the week.

As for the market as a whole, it is difficult to get overly bearish until we see a broad market sell off that encapsulates all 3 indices (rather than a rotation out of tech and into Dow 30 like we saw yesterday).

We are set for a bounce back gap up this morning. I will be watching to see if the QQQ can close back above its 8 EMA ($378.6) to finish the week.

I am back to all cash after GTLB and AMD stops hit yesterday and not planning to place any new trades today. July monthly options expiration (see below) will likely increase the volatility in today’s session, especially in the afternoon.
