#### APLD Earnings

APLD is [1 of our Top Picks for 2026](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-picks-for-2026).

APLD is a stock we have essentially [owned since $12 due to our fundamental thesis](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-smid-caps-with-massive-upside-potential?utm_source=publication-search) that is now playing out EXACTLY as we predicted.

Applied Digital just reported Q2 FY26 revenue of $126.6M, up +250% YoY (crushing estimates of $88M). The company announced that its first 100MW data center (Polaris Forge 1) has reached ready-for-service status on schedule and is now generating initial lease revenue from CoreWeave.

Most importantly, its all about the pipeline for APLD.

Applied Digital has already secured ~$16B in lease revenue over 15 years from ~600MW capacity. This impressive pipeline makes APLD one of the big 3 industry leaders in the Data Center leasing space.

On the conference call, APLD CEO Wes Cummins said that Polaris Forge 1 and 2 can both scale to **1+ GW each**, and that with those 2 plus 3 more campuses, they see a “pretty clear path” to **5 GW of capacity by 2030–2032** (even without adding further sites). **Management now expects to surpass their long-term goal of $1B+ in net operating income within 5 years.**

#### **My Top 5 Ideas for January**

**OSS**

One Stop Systems is a Small Cap ($220M market cap) ruggedized AI and edge-compute hardware vendor serving defense, aerospace, and other harsh-environment applications.

The company just announced a new $1.2M order from a US defense prime contractor. The order involves designing and supplying rugged computing equipment for real-time 360-degree video monitoring systems for Army combat vehicles including Stryker, Bradley, and Abrams platforms, with prototype delivery expected in 3-6 months.

This is a BIG fundamental catalyst for a $220M mkt cap small cap company. Most importantly, the price action and volume are confirming this HUGE catalyst.

**Today was OSS’s highest volume day EVER** (IPOed in 2018). Yesterday was the stock’s 3rd highest volume day ever.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KLIT!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F778cefa6-6b14-4fcc-95b3-d53c411dad84_1266x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KLIT!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F778cefa6-6b14-4fcc-95b3-d53c411dad84_1266x844.png)

OSS averages ~600k shares traded per day, but each of the last 2 days saw >4.5M shares traded following this major fundamental catalyst (new order win).

Moreover, this will be OSS’s highest volume week every by far, with >12M shares traded on the week. The stock’s 2nd highest volume week every was way back in February 2021, with only ~7.7M shares traded.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4ziW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7559a63e-4fe2-4680-b054-33122884ac37_1266x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4ziW!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7559a63e-4fe2-4680-b054-33122884ac37_1266x844.png)

**AEVA**

AEVA was one of our former [SMID Cap 6 names](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-smid-caps-with-massive-upside-potential?utm_source=publication-search), and the only 1 of the 6 that we lost money on (although we were up >70% at one point for those who were less greedy than me on it). The stock has essentially been in its Stage 4 downtrend since June, but could finally be breaking the downtrend here.

It all starts with Tuesday’s HUGE fundamental catalyst.

**Tuesday was AEVA’s highest volume day EVER.** The stocksoared +34% after news dropped that Nvidia selected the company's 4D LiDAR technology for integration into its DRIVE Hyperion autonomous vehicle reference platform.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cnuT!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F663b25a6-7958-4975-a338-356a506a15d5_1266x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cnuT!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F663b25a6-7958-4975-a338-356a506a15d5_1266x844.png)

This is a MASSIVE catalyst for AEVA. Not only has Nvidia now legitimized Aeva’s 4D LIDAR technology, it also potentially opens the door for more deals with new automakers who were previously hesitant to engage with the tiny firm ($800M market cap). AEVA was 1 of our original [SMID Cap 6 picks](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-smid-caps-with-massive-upside-potential?utm_source=publication-search) for a reason. Even though the trade didn’t work out for us in the end, I picked AEVA because after all my research, I concluded that company had the best technology in the industry with its 4D LIDAR. As mentioned, Tuesday’s NVDA deal helps legitimize the company’s technology and opens the door for more potential automaker deals down the line.

Most importantly, the price action and volume are confirming this HUGE fundamental catalyst for AEVA. This will be AEVA’s highest volume week ever with >35M shares traded (2nd highest was back in June with ~21M shares traded).

**ALMU**

Semis continue to lead here early in 2026 with the SOX Index hitting new ATHs already on Tuesday (ahead of the Nasdaq, which last made ATHs in October).

For me, the most interesting Semi setup early in 2026 is ALMU. Aeluma is a recent Small Cap ($317M market cap) semi IPO that manufactures high-performance optoelectronic devices using compound semiconductor materials on large-diameter silicon wafers, enabling scalable, cost-effective production for mass-market applications. The company targets multiple high-growth markets including AI infrastructure, mobile and consumer electronics, automotive LiDAR, defense and aerospace, and quantum computing.

ALMU IPOed 1+ year ago, but is finally starting to gain some attention now after a long consolidation period. The stock is looking to break out of its IPO base on the weekly.
