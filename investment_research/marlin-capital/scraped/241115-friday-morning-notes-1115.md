# Friday Morning Notes 11.15

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Stocks pulled back yesterday with somewhat of an election trade unwind/temporary pause under the hood (renewables, tarriff risk > bitcoin sensitives, small caps). The SPX finished -0.61% on the day.

Powell’s statements yesterday afternoon were firm on rates “the economy is not sending any signals that we need to be in a hurry to lower rates…if data lets us go slower, seems like the right thing to do…we can approach decision carefully amid strong economy”.

We saw weakness in EVs on reports that Trump’s transition team plans to kill the $7500 consumer tax credit for EV purchases. TSLA -5% accounted for ~45% of the NDX move lower, while RIVN -13% and other EV makers sold off even harder. Biopharma/major vax names were weak on the back of the RFK appointment announcement.

We are getting some continued selling in the indices this morning, although TSLA, COIN, and PLTR (my Trump trades) are bouncing back. So far, this pullback seems relatively orderly after the big post-election rally. I continue to believe a year-end rally remains the most likely scenario for the market.

With that said, we might have some more selling today afternoon/early next week to test some key support levels. I am sticking with my positions LRN, TWLO, and APP (ALAB stopped me out for a -4-5% loss this morning). Not looking to add any more long exposure here just yet, but that time is likely to come next week in my view.
