# The Week Ahead

**CPI Tomorrow**

The January CPI report is due tomorrow at 8:30 AM EST.  This report is critical given its importance for the Fed.  Markets are expecting inflation to continue to decelerate on a YoY basis in January, though the MoM changes may come in hotter than recent months, indicating the path back to 2% is likely to have some speed bumps.

Consensus is expecting headline CPI to rise 0.5% MoM, which would represent a bounce from the -0.1% decline and the largest monthly increase since June, with the YoY rate slowing to 6.3% from 6.5%.

**The Technicals are starting to fade**

The YTD rally reached severely overbought conditions, and last week we saw the beginnings of a potential market roll-over.

On a weekly basis, the S&P 500 gave an inside week after failing near $4200 resistance for the 2nd week in a row.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ArS1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa618891b-23fc-47ac-a08c-218a49d99ebd_1029x643.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ArS1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa618891b-23fc-47ac-a08c-218a49d99ebd_1029x643.png)

A decisive break below last week’s lows ($4060) post-CPI would represent a bearish breakdown of the inside candle.

On the other hand, a break above last week’s highs ($4176) post-CPI would represent a bullish breakout, and open the door for a further rally to August highs ($4300).

Long story short, the technicals can still go either way here.  For the SPX, we have a potential bearish rejection and reversal near $4200.  Still, it is too early to get bearish on a technical level until we break last week’s lows.  Tomorrow’s CPI report is critical in determining the next direction for the market.

With that said, I have exited the remaining portions of my NVDA and MELI positions based off trailing stop losses.  I am now just holding CLH as we head into this week.

Many other stocks on my watchlist are now beginning to fade after the massive YTD rally.  Stocks like SHOP, SQ, TSLA, and ETSY helped to lead the rally off lows, but the technical setups no longer appear bullish heading into this week.  Time will tell if this is just a mini-pullback or a major bear market rally top.  Tomorrow’s CPI report will go a long way in determining that outcome.

One aspect of this YTD rally that cannot go unmentioned is the amount of institutional short covering we have seen.  The 4-week short covering flows are the largest since the meme stock frenzy of 2021 (GME, AMC, etc.), and 3rd largest of the last decade.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wT9a!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F46ae6f34-4ae0-4985-9e50-5fc14e0f74c3_1113x875.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wT9a!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F46ae6f34-4ae0-4985-9e50-5fc14e0f74c3_1113x875.png)

After the YTD rally in stocks, the S&P 500 currently trades at an 18x fwd P/E Ratio.  Historically speaking, this is rare territory for the index.  Over the past 50 years, the S&P 500 has only sustained a multiple higher than 18x during the Dot Com Bubble and Covid-QE Bubble.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FmBi!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4a8708b7-4188-4063-8a9d-723b2f8e2c8f_603x389.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FmBi!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4a8708b7-4188-4063-8a9d-723b2f8e2c8f_603x389.png)

The index has seen its P/E multiple expand this year despite earnings being revised to the downside.  So far in 2023, negative earnings revisions have not mattered for equities.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oWuT!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5b4ffaa7-e2a8-4faa-a073-d2737017fd49_1275x771.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oWuT!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5b4ffaa7-e2a8-4faa-a073-d2737017fd49_1275x771.png)

If earnings estimates continue to drop, it is hard to imagine equities will not be impacted.  The current estimate for forward earnings assumes 0% growth YoY, but a major earnings recession is not yet forecasted.  If current estimates prove to be too rich, the S&P 500 is likely trading above 20x fwd earnings.

---

Summary

* The technicals appear setup for a big move post-CPI. Below $4060 = bearish; above $4176 = bullish. I will be waiting to see which way we break post-CPI before placing any new trades. Both long and short setups will be on the table depending on the inflation data and price action.
* Institutional short covering has been major fuel for the YTD rally in stocks, reaching the 3rd highest levels over the last decade.
* After the YTD rally, stocks appear richly valued based off the 18x Fwd P/E Ratio of the S&P 500. Such a multiple has only been sustained during the Dot Com Bubble and Covid-QE Bubble.
* Negative earnings revisions have not impacted stocks so far this year. The more they fall, the less likely it is that this can continue.
