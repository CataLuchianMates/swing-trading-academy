# Wednesday Morning Notes 5.3

Technical Update

The S&P 500 pulled back yesterday into this afternoon’s FOMC decision and press conference, finishing the day -1.16%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vxet!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F123ccabd-0225-4958-94d3-0b5ee1bcfe3f_959x717.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vxet!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F123ccabd-0225-4958-94d3-0b5ee1bcfe3f_959x717.jpeg)

A late day bounce helped the SPX to close above the 21 EMA ($4109).

Technically, the SPX remains in the same range it has been unable to break for roughly 2 months. As long as price remains above all key moving averages in the chart above, this setup still leans bullish.

All eyes are on today’s Fed decision and press conference now.

Key resistance level to watch above include $4140-$4150 and $4190-$4200 (YTD highs).

Key support levels to watch below include $4110-$4100, $4040-$4030 and $4000.
