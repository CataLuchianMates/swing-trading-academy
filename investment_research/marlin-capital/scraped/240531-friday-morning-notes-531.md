# Friday Morning Notes 5.31

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The S&P 500 closed -0.6% yesterday with negative software earnings (CRM) weighing on the indices.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!t0fJ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1b8b9ecc-4afe-4ad1-b176-4d404eec3291_1215x888.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!t0fJ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1b8b9ecc-4afe-4ad1-b176-4d404eec3291_1215x888.jpeg)

We also got some softer Macro data yesterday (inline GDP / downward revision to Q1 Core PCE, and dovish comments from Fed Williams).  The 10YR yield eased 6bps to 4.54, helping the Russell 2000 outperform (0.95% on the day) and Most Shorted pocket +5.9%.

Retail was bid on earnings prints from FL (+15%), BURL +18%, BIRK +12%, though we also saw some negative earnings reactions in the group – KSS -23%, DG -8%.

Software finished -5.4% (biggest day of underperformance in 10+ years).  CRM -20% (worst session in 20 years), PATH -35%, OKTA -6% (despite beat/raise), NOW -12%, plus additional ugly prints after the close – MDB -24% (slight beat, but cut FY rev guidance), S -10% (cutting FY rev growth targets), and AI favorite DELL -15% (revs and EPS better than consensus, however missed on margins & expectations were very high).
