# Negative Feedback Loop

Slow fade for stocks today with the S&P 500 finishing -0.30%, NDX -0.21%, and RTY -0.69%.

Underneath the hood, momentum/high beta stocks were hit the hardest. Many of the biggest winners since April lows were down upwards of -8% + today (QS -15%; TSSI -13%; NVTS -12%; TMC -11%; OUST -11%; etc.).

**So, what now?**

U.S. stocks have been in a low volatility melt up since early April. As I highlighted in “Replay”, The Nasdaq has recorded its longest consecutive streak above its 20-day MA since the **Dot Com Bubble**.

**Market Feedback Tells All**

For me, it’s not about predicting the future. There is no crystal ball that will tell you the market’s next move.

The good news is: success does not hinge on predicting the market’s next move, but on understanding probabilities and responding to the real-time feedback the market gives you.

The feedback on my trades tells me all I need to know. **When my trades are making money and setups are plentiful, I place more trades. When my trades are faltering/not gaining any traction, or losing, I pull back on entering new trades.**

The feedback on my trades tells me how aggressive I should be. No predictions necessary.

Recent feedback on my trades have been mediocre/negative:

**OKLO**: we gained +8.4% overall, but the stock essentially had a -15% + drawdown in 2 days to force our exit. It has since recovered back to $70+ on positive news, but this was anything but a clean breakout.

**IREN**: this was a classic failed breakout. We made +6.7% overall. It's declined -13% since our exit.

So, to summarize, here is the feedback the market has given us on our recent trades in the last 2 weeks:

- Failed breakouts

- No follow through

- Names continuing to roll over after our exits

- Leadership thinning

- Ideas I skipped (like BZAI) not working

Overall, I'm happy with the way I've handled these trades from a risk management perspective. We exited TMC at breakeven, which would have otherwise hit my stop price for a -10% loss. We exited IREN for a +6.7% gain even though the breakout failed spectacularly. We took a -2.8% loss on RGTI instead of a -10% loss. We didn’t enter BZAI.

But, the negative feedback from the market is clear to me. Suddenly, we are in a much more difficult environment than we were a month ago. This means I pull back on entering new trades.
