# Friday Morning Notes 1.17

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Another choppy/tricky day for the market yesterday as the indices digested Wednesday’s post-CPI rally. Mag 7 weighed on the tape while the S&P equal weight outperformed, finishing +0.8%.

AAPL sold off -4% (worst day since August) on a mix of industry data (China) and TSM talking to Q1 smartphone seasonality, although neither datapoint felt particularly new/surprising. The stock is now -12.3% off its highs and down 10 of the last 13 sessions.

The rest of the Mag 7 all finished in the red as well despite a boost from TSM’s positive earnings update and dovish commentary from Fed’s Waller (3 rate cuts possible in ‘25?). NVDA continued to leak lower despite the positive catalyst from TSM.

Attention will now move to Trump’s inauguration on Monday and what executive orders he will implement quickly once he’s taken office. Today is January options expiration and it is a big one. GS estimates that over $4.3T of notional options exposure will expire today, including $1.8T of SPX options and $1.3T of notional single stock options. This options expiration will be the largest Jan expiration on record, and well as the largest non-quarterly expiration on record.

Active Portfolio -

We are still in a trickier/choppier tape, in my view, and today’s gap up open arguably makes the setup even more challenging. I am currently holding 2 longs (RBRK and MRVL) with closing stops in place. No changes for me there.

There are a couple more potential long trade setups catching my eye, but I am not looking to buy into today’s gap up just yet. I will look to add more longs if the market acts constructively, but as I mentioned, I still view this as a choppier, “less is more” type tape.
