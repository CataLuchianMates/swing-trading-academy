# The Week Ahead

The S&P 500 fell -2.4% last week and closed below its 200-day moving average for the first time since March.  Bond yields touched fresh highs as investors had to contend with rising geopolitical tensions and digest ongoing Q3 earnings.

Investors took down risk into the weekend with the SPX closing at lows.  The VIX held above 20 as some fear has returned to the market.  All eyes were on the 200 day moving average for the SPX, which was breached for the first time in 7 months.

What happens when we close below the 200 SMA in the SPX? Since 1998, the SPX has crossed below its 200 SMA 95 times.  The results 5 days later: 31x higher, 64x lower.  Avg move = -0.63%.

Tech remains stronger on a relative basis, still well above its 200 SMA (but in a similar downtrend on a technical basis).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!87Tq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9ae548a6-5f75-4b2c-ac24-e7f02487e82b_978x724.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!87Tq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9ae548a6-5f75-4b2c-ac24-e7f02487e82b_978x724.jpeg)

With that said, the QQQ has traded lower in 6 of the last 7 sessions (down -4.5% over this stretch) as the combination of higher rates, elevated positioning (tech as the “safe haven”), and negative data points (China policy regarding semis) have weighed on the space.  TSLA (-17% in 3 days) and NVDA (posted its worst week in >1 yr) were the laggards in the space last week.

We did not see many investors interested in buying the dip in the midst of yields breaking out and 39% of the S&P 500 reporting earnings this week: MSFT & GOOGL (Tues), META (Weds), AMZN (Thurs).

We will also get a heavy dose of economic data in the next week with PMIs, GDP, and PCE.

The big question heading into this week of earnings is what matters more for big tech: rates or fundamentals.  With AI excitement near all time highs and positioning still stretched, these stocks are mostly priced for blowout quarters.

Concentration in the Magnificent 7 as a % of total US single stock net exposure is still near record highs, as managers have been selling other stocks (not Mega Cap tech) at a much more rapid pace.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tfIr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2bf31525-e2d7-42b0-8133-93700f37b9fb_1220x644.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tfIr!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2bf31525-e2d7-42b0-8133-93700f37b9fb_1220x644.jpeg)

Positioning – US single stocks saw their largest net selling since July, as single stock shorts increased for an 11th straight week, on par with the longest shorting streak on record (according to GS Prime).  While shorts have dominated recent selling activity by Hedge Funds, last week’s long selling in Tech was the largest in 6+ months and point to risk off behavior in the sector ahead of a huge slate of Mega Cap Tech earnings this week.
