# Thursday Morning Notes 6.20

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Tuesday was a quiet day for markets with the S&P 500 +0.25%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vW4l!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc2e4e014-b58d-4087-adc4-bebe8e0e7e47_980x723.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vW4l!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc2e4e014-b58d-4087-adc4-bebe8e0e7e47_980x723.jpeg)

Markets continue to make new all-time highs even on the quieter days (volumes -9% vs 20dma).

The market continues to grind higher and volatility seems to be a thing of the past (at least, in the current environment we find ourselves in). In fact, we are currently in the longest stretch in the post-GFC era without a -2.05% daily selloff in the S&P 500.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HuFT!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6516f11a-3f5a-4412-8edf-cf9045632ab7_609x363.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HuFT!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6516f11a-3f5a-4412-8edf-cf9045632ab7_609x363.jpeg)

Breadth continues to be a main talking point for the current market environment.  NDX vs NDX Equal Weight is +12% over the last month and now +45% YTD (14d RSI = 81; in ‘very’ overbought territory).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Etzg!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F62a6cce0-f5b9-42e1-9721-eabba14eff6f_625x389.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Etzg!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F62a6cce0-f5b9-42e1-9721-eabba14eff6f_625x389.jpeg)

Goldman’s TMT Momentum Pair (Leaders > Laggards) is up 8.9% over the last 5 trading sessions, the best 5 day stretch in over 18 months.  This is to say, the stocks that have performed the best continue to lead (think Mag 7, AI leaders, etc.); and the stocks that have performed the worst continue to lag (think software, etc.).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tLeA!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff8c0f188-12bc-426f-aeaf-03a94f234975_625x446.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tLeA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff8c0f188-12bc-426f-aeaf-03a94f234975_625x446.jpeg)

We are hearing a lot of debate about “how much further” these leaders (Mag 7, AI leaders, etc.) can run.  While the group has performed extremely well, we are not yet at nosebleed valuations with the 10 largest stocks trading at a 30x P/E (vs 47x P/E at the peak of the Dot Com Bubble).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GbEq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F26d750ff-f93e-43d1-8c8b-25ecd6a9e12b_1135x685.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GbEq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F26d750ff-f93e-43d1-8c8b-25ecd6a9e12b_1135x685.jpeg)
