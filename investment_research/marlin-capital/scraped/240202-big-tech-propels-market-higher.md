**META –**

+20% on Friday

AI was the word of last night from Meta’s call (in addition to robust revenue trends +$50B buyback & 50c dividend) as the company laid out how the “year of efficiency” has reset the company as a leaner organization (with a nimble approach to iteration and innovation).  The resulting resources (additional capital generated) will be used to fund 5-10 year+ investment cycles around AI and the metaverse, which add to one of the (already) more robust product cycle stories in internet names right now.  Reels (daily watch time +25% YoY), Shops Ads ($2B annualized run rate), click-to-messaging Ads, Threads, WhatsApp Channels, etc.

Any negatives? Not really.  In fact, exiting the week, META is the Mag 7 name with arguably the most discovery value/perceived numbers upside amongst many investors (META trading at ~19x EPS ex/cash), though, a few potential bearish catalysts to monitor – ongoing uncertainty around the ROI of Reality Labs (losses to increase meaningfully in FY24)….durability of China based Ad Revs (~10% of total revs in 2023)…

META added $205B in market cap post-earnings on Friday, breaking the record for the largest single day gain in market cap for any stock in history

**AMZN -**

+8% on Friday

Despite Friday’s big move, the stock is still quietly $18 below ATHs…AWS re-acceleration (optimization -> resumption of cloud migrations), brewing AI story (both in AWS and consumer apps), margin execution opportunity, product cycle upside in advertising (core sponsored ads + video), and strong online sales with 3P revs +19% YoY.

Any pushback?  Not a whole lot.  While the Macro will always be an overhang/risk for AMZN, the beauty of the story is that AWS looks set to accelerate for several quarters in a row (off easing comps alone), which arguably makes this a bit of an open-ended convo as investors can credibly paint upside scenarios on where growth tops out.

**AAPL -**

-0.5% on Friday

The clear pushbacks are AAPL’s weaker F2Q24 guidance ($90B vs consensus of $96B), implying March iPhone revs down -$5B YoY and other products down ~10% YoY (vs consensus flattish into the print), lack of a clear acceleration narrative in Services into the March qtr and greater China revs – 13% YoY (competition concerns).

So what gets investors re-engaged for AAPL?  The Bull Case is anchored around AI upside to come (they said stay tuned for later this year), ongoing Services momentum (accelerated for a 3rd qtr in a row), GMs continue to inflect and the iPhone still grew +6% YoY in Dec qtr.  Specifically on iPhones, initial indications are that the iPhone 15 is resonating with consumers and channel inventories are lean, which could foreshadow strong iPhone 16 sales later this year (although the F2Q24 revenue guidance for iPhone missed - $46B vs cons $50B)

**GOOGL –**

Was it all that bad?  The stock reacted negatively last week, finishing -6% post-earnings .

The Bear Case – no real upside on topline vs expectations (& calling out a revenue base that is >$100B larger into 2024)…higher CapEx…ongoing debate on AI positioning vs value proposition…ongoing regulatory uncertainty.

The Bull Case – nothing to worry about (topline in re-accelerating on YouTube, Search, and Cloud)…Beat/Raise print where numbers went up…trading <17x GAAP PE with ~$85B of net cash… AI narrative is arguably the upside from here.

**MSFT –**

It’s difficult to come up with tangible reason to explain the initial negative reaction to MSFT’s print.  It feels mostly positioning/risk backdrop driven, but I suppose investors can debate: Azure mix (how to think about OpenAI vs core trends) + magnitude of EPS revisions (is it enough to support the below picture - MSFT valuation nearing ATHs).

From here, the market is likely focused on the likelihood of Azure to re-accelerate in 2H of FY, more confidence in the margin expansion story, and thinking about how the non-AI segments help to support growth.

**AMD –**

While lack of estimate revisions initially weighed on the stock post-earnings, (with outsized weakness in both Gaming and Embedded), the bulls likely got everything they wanted with strong consumer traction in MI300 (arguably threaded the needle with the $3.5B MI300 guide – nice raise, but arguably still enough upside to play for). Elsewhere, server GPU share gains story is still intact, GMs are set to expand on a sequential basis throughout 2024 from 52% in Q1 ’24 to 57% in Q4 ’24.

**QCOM –**

Solid beat in Dec qtr with revs $9.93B vs consensus $9.54B and mixed March guidance (revs touch below, EPS a touch above at midpoint at $2.3 vs $2.26 cons.).

**Look ahead –**

Now that we’re through Big Tech Earnings (67% of the SPX equity cap has now reported), we have a clearer picture on the general market: Positive reactions from META and AMZN, flattish reactions from MSFT and AAPL, and negative reactions from GOOGL and TSLA.  The only 1 of the Magnificent 7 still to report is NVDA.

These Mag 7 stocks continue to lead the market higher, and for good reason.  I have talked in the past about how Small Caps are historically undervalued (compared to Large Caps), but the picture below is the other side of that coin.  The earnings growth is all coming from Nasdaq 100 stocks with 12M Fwd EPS at all time highs, while 12M Fwd EPS for Small Caps plateaued in 2022 and is rolling over.

Technically, as I touched on in [Thursday’s Morning Notes](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/thursday-morning-notes-21), there was no real damage done from Wednesday’s post-FOMC sell off.  The dip was bought right up on Thursday as we rallied back above the 8 EMA, and continued with a gap up on Friday following META and AMZN prints.  The trend is firmly up and to the right, and there is 0 reason to fight this technical uptrend.
