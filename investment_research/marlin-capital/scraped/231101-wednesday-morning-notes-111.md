# Wednesday Morning Notes 11.1

**Daily Market Commentary**

US stocks continued their bounce yesterday with the S&P 500 closing +0.65% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kp9R!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbb7ed80a-2337-485e-99da-90e6eedd99b6_959x743.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kp9R!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbb7ed80a-2337-485e-99da-90e6eedd99b6_959x743.jpeg)

The 10 YR yield finished the day at 4.91% as the 5.0% level continues to provide heavy resistance.  This has allowed equities to bounce here (as the 10 YR has been unable to decisively break above 5% thus far).  A decisive move above 5% would trigger more downside for stocks, in my opinion.

Yesterday’s action felt squeezy at the top with Non-Profitable Tech and New Technology +2.5%, High Short +1.9%, and Renewables +2.15% (after a rough session Monday) vs Mega Cap weaker (-14 bps).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wc4V!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc3a74e29-4b69-46f0-8464-8a40d5c6d160_1290x2077.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wc4V!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc3a74e29-4b69-46f0-8464-8a40d5c6d160_1290x2077.jpeg)

The November FOMC meeting concludes today.  Fed officials have signaled that they will not be hiking rates today.  Based off recent Fed official comments, most would prefer not to hike again in this cycle.  The market is pricing in very little chance of a hike today and only a ~20% probability of a hike at the December meeting.

In the last 2 meetings, Fed Chair Powell has struck a more hawkish tone to make up for the Fed’s pause.  I expect Powell to maintain his hawkish bias in today’s presser with 1-year inflation remaining well above 2%.
