But the real damage was underneath the surface. Software got crushed, falling -8.9% on the month. The Global AI Technology ETF (AIQ) dropped -4.5%. Many individual names got hit even harder. MSFT -7%. PLTR -7%. APLD -22%. IREN -23%. GRAL -40%. EOSE -60%.

It was a very difficult month. And heading into March, it’s not getting any easier.

**The AI Labor Market Shakeup**

Block (XYZ) announced last week that it is laying off roughly 40% of its workforce. Over 4,000 employees gone. According to Goldman Sachs, the job cuts were concentrated in engineering roles, not sales or compliance.

In other words, Block is replacing engineers with its own internal AI platform. The stock initially popped +24% on the news.

Read that again. A major US tech company just announced it is cutting 40% of its employees, and the market rewarded it with one of the biggest single day rallies in the stock’s history.

CEO Jack Dorsey was explicit about the reason, citing AI. In his letter to shareholders, Dorsey wrote, “A significantly smaller team, using the tools we’re building, can do more and do it better.” He went further, predicting that **“within the next year, I believe the majority of companies will reach the same conclusion and make similar structural changes.”**

But here is what really matters. Block’s (very) positive stock reaction sends a very clear message to every other CEO in America. If you are struggling, cut your workforce significantly, attribute it to AI, and your stock will rally big. That is a powerful incentive. Whether or not this triggers a broader wave of AI-driven layoffs across corporate America is something I will be watching very closely the rest of 2026.

With all of this uncertainty, my strategy has never been more important. Over the past 5+ weeks, I have exited 23 different names and closed out an entire portfolio.

**What I am left with are leaders only.**  And they are still leading.

**The Punch Card**

I want to leave everyone with something I have been thinking about a lot lately.

Warren Buffett has a famous concept called the 20-slot punch card. If you could only make 20 investments in your entire lifetime, you would be incredibly selective. You would wait for the absolute best opportunities and size up aggressively when they arrived.

Stanley Druckenmiller learned a similar lesson from George Soros early in his career. In one of my favorite interviews, **Druck described how Soros would look at the same trade ideas Druck had, and simply bet far more money on them.**

Soros’ edge was not his analysis. It was his conviction and position sizing. Druckenmiller took that lesson and built his entire career around it. As Druck has said before, “only 1 or 2 times a year do you see something that truly excites you, and your track record on those specific trades is far superior to everything else you do.”

3 of the greatest investors/traders in history are all saying the same thing here. Big money is not made by trading more often. It is made by being selective and loading up on your best ideas.

Every year, there are maybe a dozen opportunities that can make your entire year. Those are the punch card trades. My job is to make sure we are positioned to capitalize when these opportunities present themselves.

A lot of days, I scan everything and walk away. That is not inaction. That is the discipline. In fact, that is my process working exactly as intended.

In April-October (and in the first 2 weeks of January), some punch card opportunities showed up for us. And, because I had the capital and the conviction from months of disciplined waiting, we were ready to swing. That is the exact same setup I am building toward right now.

**This is how the results get made.**

Right now, I am most focused on protecting capital in a [hard penny environment](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/hard-penny?utm_source=publication-search).

When the next wave of punch card opportunities shows up, I will be ready.
