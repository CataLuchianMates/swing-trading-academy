# Rough Waters in Q1

More risk off action to close out Q1 with the S&P 500 in line for its first quarterly decline since Q3 2022. The S&P 500 now stands ~10% below its February highs and down -5% YTD.

The Nasdaq is set to close Q1 down -8%, which would mark the worst quarter for the NDX since Q2 2022. Zooming out, this is rare territory for the Nasdaq. Only 5 times in the last 10 years (40 quarters) have we seen a -5% + quarterly decline for the NDX.

Starting with some positive news, investor positioning is no longer the headwind it was at the start of Q1. Total positioning declined further last week to -1.2 Z Score, the lowest reading since April 2023. While positioning is now firmly in “light” territory, it still remains above levels reached at the bottoms of other major drawdowns during the past 15 years (2022, 2018, 2020, 2015, etc.).

I have covered this total positioning washout in detail throughout Q1, as I firmly believe this has been the biggest driver of the current market correction. Institutional investors and Hedge Funds have de-risked at a record pace over the last 2 months.

This continued last week. Last week’s net selling in Tech was the largest in 6 months and the 2nd largest in the past 5 years (-2.8 Z score).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EI36!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8d49f31b-d5cd-41d6-ad58-5b60d9b18e99_790x518.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EI36!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8d49f31b-d5cd-41d6-ad58-5b60d9b18e99_790x518.jpeg)

Gross Exposure in Tech now stands at 16.4% (as a % total of US book), a 5-year low. Net exposure stands at 16.7%, which is in the 12th percentile versus the past 5 years.

One could argue the “why” for the massive de-grossing/de-risking we have seen (tariffs, recession/growth scare, etc.), but this selling is the “how” we have ended up here. Given the speed of this positioning washout, it is almost surprising the SPX is not down more than -10%. We have gone from ATH long positioning at the end of January to now underweight in 2 months.

Holding “up” the market (i.e., why is the SPX not down more than -10%?) have been retail investors/households. I talked about this dynamic in [“Red Wednesday”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/red-wednesday) and [“Price Tells All”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/price-tells-all).

Retail investors bought the dip this quarter and were net buyers all throughout Q1. In fact, as of 1 week ago, there were only 7 trading sessions in all of Q1 where retail were NOT net buyers.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uYok!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4b116ea4-6148-48e0-ae50-7f9e89b1b677_499x334.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uYok!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4b116ea4-6148-48e0-ae50-7f9e89b1b677_499x334.png)

This is a stark contrast to the unprecedented de-risking we have seen from institutions/funds. This is also reminiscent of Q1 2022 when the SPX started to decline following a roaring Bull Market where the “buy every dip” strategy reigned supreme. Investors who were conditioned to buy every dip during the Bull Market continued to do so at the start of the Bear Market (before eventually puking up these buys in Q4 2022).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Qrnj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc293f74c-ecf4-46ec-9f4c-dde1a93877fc_1572x633.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Qrnj!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc293f74c-ecf4-46ec-9f4c-dde1a93877fc_1572x633.png)

So far, the behavior from retail in Q1 2025 is very similar to Q1 2022. I talked about some of the other similarities we were seeing to the 2022 Bear Market at the beginning of this sell off in [“Red Flags Emerge”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/red-flags-emerge).

> “Based on elevated risk appetite and speculation (retail euphoria) in the market as well as some of the parabolic moves we’ve seen with potential climax/blowoff tops, there are definitely some parallels from now to that time (2022). [During that time](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/were-not-in-kansas-anymore?utm_source=publication-search), we had parabolic/climax tops in more speculative names (SPACs, ARKK, etc) before the indices topped.”

I have also noted the differences between 2025 and 2022, starting with the monetary policy environment. In 2022, the Fed was raising rates aggressively. Now, we are in a Fed cutting cycle.

In 2022, we saw a technical recession AND earnings recession. A recession is still NOT consensus in 2025, but economists have continued to raise their probabilities for one. Goldman now estimates a 35% probability that the US economy will enter a recession during the next 12 months (up from 20%).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HbcX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdde99017-19ec-4f7d-b3f3-1b592dfc93db_297x265.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HbcX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdde99017-19ec-4f7d-b3f3-1b592dfc93db_297x265.jpeg)

The historical equity market recession playbook implies a roughly 20-25% SPX drawdown from peak to trough. If followed, this pattern would suggest a further -17% drawdown from today’s price to a trough level of ~$4600. This would represent a 17x P/E multiple. During the last 3 major SPX drawdowns, the P/E multiple bottomed at:

15x (2022 Covid Bubble Bust)

13x (2020 Covid Crash)

14x (2018 December Rout)

[I have also noted](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/price-tells-all) that during recessionary Bear Markets, the duration of the selloff tends to be much longer. The median recessionary market correction lasted 282 days, almost 5x as long (in duration) as non-recessionary corrections.

In [“Price Tells All”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/price-tells-all), I talked about what my 2 main tells for calling an ultimate market bottom will be: (1) positioning and (2) earnings.

As I talked about above, positioning is no longer the headwind it was here in Q1. With that said, it can still go lower and stay low/underweight for a prolonged period of time (see 2022).

Further, retail positioning and the “buy the dip” behavior from retail in Q1 is still a major headwind because it indicates a lack of real fear commonly seen at/near market bottoms. Retail buying the dip is NOT typical behavior near market bottoms. There have been 0 signs of capitulation or panic in 2025, which tells me we are not there yet positioning wise. In my view, we will need to see retail turn to sellers (like Q4 2022) before forming an ultimate market bottom.

Earnings are the real wild card now. In [“Price Tells All”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/price-tells-all), I pointed out the consistent negative earnings revisions we are seeing (14 straight weeks of negative revisions). Even with these negative revisions, though, there is still no earnings recession priced into the market at these levels. Consensus is still expecting positive EPS growth in 2025 (+3% YOY).

If these estimates continue to decline and we do see an earnings recession, I believe we will likely see a -20% + market correction before a bottom is formed. History supports this notion, as I pointed out above (-20 - 25% in recessions).

This is why earnings are the real wild card here, as they will determine how deep this sell off ultimately gets. Analysts have continued to revise estimates down more each week, but we have not seen any major banks stick their necks out and predict an earnings recession (yet).

My initial price target for the S&P 500 remains $5200. This is essentially my “Bull Case”, as I believe this would be the earliest we could see the market begin to bottom. In a non-recessionary scenario (with no earnings recession), it is very plausible the market could begin to bottom there.

In a recessionary scenario (with an earnings recession), my price target is $4600-$4800. This would imply a -20-25% peak to trough decline, in line with the average recessionary market drawdown.
