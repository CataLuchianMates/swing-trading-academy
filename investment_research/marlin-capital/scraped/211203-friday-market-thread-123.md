**My Strategy**

The end of QE has not historically correlated to the end of bull markets.  It does, however, create a tighter liquidity environment and signals a more “risk off” market.  During a risk off environment, more speculative assets are unloaded for safer assets.  It does not get much more speculative than unprofitable/barely profitable tech companies at all time high valuations.  So, my portfolio is very vulnerable and I must act to limit the drawdown.

I vividly remember the December 2018 market meltdown.  This was triggered by the Fed becoming hawkish, and the fear that Jerome Powell would begin to raise interest rates too quickly.

The good news is, I recall a few great buying opportunities (like SHOP in the $120’s) I was able to take advantage of.  If we are patient, I believe tremendous buying opportunities will present themselves in my highest conviction names.  However, that does not mean I will just sit by and watch 20%+ further downside play out.

I’m coming into the day with my long portfolio fully hedged with an ARKK short position.  I will be looking to add some potential shorts to become at least 60:40 Short:Long, for now.

An oversold bounce near the 8 EMA, followed by a rejection, would provide a good place to add to the ARKK short.
