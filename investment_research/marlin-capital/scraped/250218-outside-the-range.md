# Outside the Range

Last week was a big one for US markets with the S&P 500 finishing +1.5% despite Wednesday’s hotter than expected CPI reading. This is the 3rd and latest example in the last few weeks of a big market gap down on bad news being bought up (DeepSeek gap down, Tariff news gap down, and Hot CPI gap down).

For the QQQ, we finally broke through the 2 month trading range we have been in and closed last week at the highest levels in the index since mid-December.

US stocks continue to be led on the heels of secular growth/AI adopters (META up 20 days in a row) and big inflows from retail traders (Memes +8% last week, China +8%, and 12M losers +3%). Retail has had a net buy imbalance for the last 20+ days, including the top 3 largest buying days on record.

January and February are the largest months of the year for inflows into 401k and 529 allocations, but this dynamic will start to fade now as we enter the the 2nd half of February. Typically, the 2nd half of Feb into early March tends to be a more challenging period for stocks.

With that said, it is hard for me to be bearish here with the QQQ breaking a 2 month trading range to the upside and a plethora of post-earnings names setting up for continuation rallies (in my view).

Speaking of earnings, Q4 has been a strong earnings season so far with 55% of companies reporting beating street wide earnings estimates by >1 standard deviation (higher than the historical avg of 49%). 10% of companies have missed estimates by >1 standard deviation (less than the historical avg of 13%).

Companies beating earnings estimates by at least 1 std dev have outperformed the S&P 500 by +83bps in the trading session directly after reporting (vs historical avg of +102bps of outperformance). Companies missing estimates by at least 1 std dev have underperformed the SPX by -166bps (vs historical avg of -211 bps of underperformance).

In terms of trading, Marlin Capital readers are aware of [my favorite way to trade earnings gappers on day 1](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-earnings-gapper-trade-setup?utm_source=publication-search). In many quarters, we see some profit taking right after the opening bell following a large gap up on earnings. This allows the inside 5/15/1 hr setups to form and (hopefully) gives us a low risk/high reward entry.

This quarter, though, we are almost exclusively seeing the top earnings gappers bottom during the first 5 minutes of the Day 1 gap up trading day. 12 out of the top 16 Q4 earnings gappers moves I have studied have bottomed on the first 1 minute candle of the Day 1 gap up (9:30 AM EST). The other 3 bottomed at 9:31 and 9:32 (3x).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2FS4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F279e576f-9951-483d-90dc-88e6384ee586_921x422.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2FS4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F279e576f-9951-483d-90dc-88e6384ee586_921x422.png)

This is an interesting development and a notable change from most quarters. This is also why I bought HOOD at Thursday’s open rather than wait for a potential inside setup to form. With earnings gappers mostly rallying right after the bell in Q4, I want to buy my top earnings gappers names in the first minute of the trading day.

The strategy: On top earnings gappers, buy at open or on any big downtick at 9:30, reassess at 9:35 and 9:45. Position size as if -10% is your risk if the trade fails.
