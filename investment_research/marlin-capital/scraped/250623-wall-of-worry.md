# Wall of Worry

US equites slumped out of the gate this morning in reaction to the news that the US struck Iranian nuclear facilities over the weekend, but the market has already more than recovered all of the initial weakness. The market is hopeful that the US strike will not prompt a larger escalation, but Iran has vowed to respond, so there is plenty of uncertainty still in the picture.

Crude Oil initially rallied past last week’s high of $80 /bbl, boosted in part by speculation that Iran could close the Strait of Hormuz after the country’s parliament approved the move. This is a move that would disrupt 20% of global oil supply (and ~5% of US oil supply).

Polymarket currently assigns 26% odds that Iran will close the Strait of Hormuz before July and a 45% chance they will close the Strait before the end of 2025.

Yet even with these weekend’s negative headlines, US equity indices are opening this week in the green. This entire year has been marked with global uncertainty. The Trade War narrative that dominated in Q1 has now shifted to discussions of a War in the Middle East. Considering how strong returns had been coming into 2025 (recall that the SPX generated back to back years of a Sharpe Ratio >2 for the 1st time since the 70s), and with how much has been thrown at the market in 2025, I find it nothing short of incredible that the SPX and NDX are in the green YTD.

The main reason we find ourselves positive (in the SPX & NDX) about halfway into 2025, in my view, is the overarching Artificial Intelligence narrative. The SPX and NDX have now gone sideways for the last 3 weeks+, consolidating a few percent below all-time highs. Against this choppy backdrop, names levered to AI have already broke out to new ATHs (Goldmans’s AI Basket), leading the market.

The AI theme has seemingly defeated every Bear Case that has been thrown at it in 2025 (DeepSeek, MSFT CapEx angst, ROIC woes, Tariffs, Recession). Readers of Marlin Capital know how bullish I am on the future of AI (it is really going to change EVERYTHING and EVERY industry). This is the big picture view I keep harping on and why I believe it’s important to look past the Macro headlines that will inevitably dominate in the media.

Bigger picture, everyone knows exactly where I stand. Before all is said and done, I believe AI will spark a major asset bubble on par with the late 90s Internet/Dot Com Bubble. This is the cyclical nature of markets (the Boom and Bust asset cycle). Throughout history, we have seen the biggest technological advancements spark asset bubbles. This is because technological breakthroughs generate mass excitement which leads to increased speculation and risk taking. During the Dot Com Bubble, the Nasdaq rose +353% over 17 months following the 1998 correction (and +180% from the ATH breakout following the ‘98 correction).
