# A Stock Picker's Paradise

The S&P 500 finished slightly positive last week with risk appetite dampened by Middle East tensions, port strikes, the looming US election, China policy, etc.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DNx6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc8178557-b979-4a7e-9701-2774e84321d3_1283x695.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DNx6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc8178557-b979-4a7e-9701-2774e84321d3_1283x695.jpeg)

The SPX pulled back -1.4% on Tuesday as news hit that Iran fired nearly 200 missiles directly at Israel.  Markets stabilized on Wednesday however, most likely because worst case scenarios failed to materialize.

The big data release of the week was Friday’s NFP report, which was much better than expected.  Headline NFP came in at 254k vs 150k consensus and Unemployment came in at 4.05% unrounded vs 4.2% consensus.  September’s 254k jobs added was the most since March, while August was also revised higher.

Friday’s jobs report pushed the market higher (SPX +0.9%, NDX +1.2%) while the US 10YR Treasury yield reached its highest intraday level (3.98%) since August 8th.  The stronger than expected employment report resulted in markets pricing out 13bps of cuts through year end.

After concerns around slowing activity/growth flared up in August/September, recent economic data has taken a turn for the better.  Good news is good news for stocks as long as inflation doesn’t flare up again (CPI release coming this Thursday).  Historically, stocks and rates have shown positive correlations (i.e. good news is good news) when inflation was low.  I believe this is the type of market environment we are currently in now with the Fed easing.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hdZ8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F32f7ce61-ce1d-4df8-b445-2b204564bbe1_1133x687.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hdZ8!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F32f7ce61-ce1d-4df8-b445-2b204564bbe1_1133x687.jpeg)

We are now entering a very busy stretch for the market from now until Mid-November.  Earnings season kicks off this week while the US election is now less than a month away.  Historically, the US election is a risk clearing event for the market, regardless of the winning party.  We have traditionally seen volatility rise into the event and subside after.

The November/December following U.S. elections tend to be positive, regardless of who wins.

On tap for this week: FOMC minutes (wed), US CPI (thurs), Initial Jobless Claims (thurs), TSLA robotaxi product launch (thurs).  Earnings include DAL & DPZ (thurs) + Bank stocks kickoff their earnings on Friday (BK, BLK, FAST, JPM, WFC).  We also have bond auctions (3yr Tues, 10yr Wed, 30yr Thurs) + a slew of Fed speakers.

The upcoming earnings season is set to be a great environment for stock pickers, in my opinion.  The options market is pricing in the biggest post-earnings implied move at the single stock level since 2021.  Interestingly, at the index level, implied volatility on the S&P 500 remains relatively low, suggesting that the real action is going to be more at the single stock level.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qFMB!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8b59c910-44be-424d-b80c-990908bcf6df_1160x590.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qFMB!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8b59c910-44be-424d-b80c-990908bcf6df_1160x590.jpeg)

Following a Macro driven market in 2022-23, where multiples predominantly drove market returns, earnings have contributed much more to market returns in 2024.  According to BofA, earnings have driven 45% of the 12M returns for the SPX as of September.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!snfK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbb64203e-a1ca-45a7-a167-eadb17b4154e_1054x627.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!snfK!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbb64203e-a1ca-45a7-a167-eadb17b4154e_1054x627.jpeg)

With the easing cycle having started, I expect earnings to contribute an even bigger portion of returns going forward.  Part of the reason I believe this will be the case is because market have largely frontloaded this Fed easing cycle.  Historically, Fed rate cuts triggered market rallies led by valuation expansion.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ovG2!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbdb78278-139a-41dd-9f98-a92665392077_1284x923.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ovG2!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbdb78278-139a-41dd-9f98-a92665392077_1284x923.jpeg)

This time, however, we have seen valuations already expand into this Fed easing cycle, suggesting the market frontloaded this cycle.  In fact, 2024 has the highest P/E at the start of a Fed cutting cycle in 60+ years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VQkE!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0e1566be-18f4-4a2f-9d39-a810336a629b_750x409.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VQkE!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0e1566be-18f4-4a2f-9d39-a810336a629b_750x409.png)

With valuations on the high side vs other instances in history, I believe earnings become that much more important.  For these reasons, I expect this earnings season to be a stock picker’s paradise.

Earnings trades will be my focus from here.  We hit big on a couple earnings trades this last quarter with SN and NMM hitting 20%+ gainers, plus SEZL from my Top 6 Small Caps picks going on to run +100% following its blowout quarter.  I believe we will get some great new trade opportunities here in the coming weeks as earnings season kicks off.
