# The Week Ahead 8.1

Technical Analysis:

Stocks rallied hard following last week’s Fed meeting. A more dovish Powell ignited the rally in equities and bonds. Fed Funds futures shaved about 60 BPs off the peak future rate for this cycle following Wednesday’s meeting, the key reason for this squeeze higher in stocks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mIfD!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F48b8f823-bd89-4f76-92f1-61284965e00d_731x472.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mIfD!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F48b8f823-bd89-4f76-92f1-61284965e00d_731x472.jpeg)

As I have discussed, this is where I do not quite see eye to eye with the market’s viewpoint. Regardless, many investors and traders were prepared for a big sell off last week with big tech earnings and the FOMC supposed to be the catalysts. Instead, the market ripped higher, causing max pain to market participants who expected the next shoe to drop.

Now, we have gotten through several key obstacles and have a quieter road ahead. With that said, we are now reaching overbought levels where the previous 2 Bear Market rallies failed.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rIvQ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcd360cbe-e420-4b74-bfda-551cee128287_1075x1075.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rIvQ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcd360cbe-e420-4b74-bfda-551cee128287_1075x1075.jpeg)

Sentiment has also improved considerably as many more traders believe the bottom is in after last week’s big tech earnings misses failed to take down the market. We’ve gotten through the difficult parts of earnings season & likely hit peak inflation (and peak Fed hawkishness?), so what can possibly take down the market now? This sounds the exact opposite of the consensus heading into last week, which was that the FOMC and big tech earnings would ignite the next wave down.

Support levels below: $310-$312, $300-$301, $290-$292
