Why?

A few key reasons 👇

**1.) It has been 6 straight up months for the S&P 500, which is the longest win streak since the 2021 Covid Bubble.**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yeIZ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F35fd74c1-92bc-47ef-be92-0181bd77d442_1428x1026.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yeIZ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F35fd74c1-92bc-47ef-be92-0181bd77d442_1428x1026.jpeg)

The Dot Com Bubble only reached 5 straight green months following the 1998 correction.

Many pundits are predicting a Santa Claus Rally to end the year, which would make it 8 straight up months for the SPX (with November and December green). This would be the longest win streak since 1996.

Moreover, the S&P 500 has now spent 125 consecutive days above its 50-day MA, its longest streak since 2011.

Needless to say, the risk/reward heading into November is simply not as strong as it has been.

**2.) Breadth has deteriorated, which means the overall health of the market has deteriorated.**

Breadth has started to deteriorate, and this is a big concern for me. I voiced my concerns about the breadth deterioration last week in [“Trick or Treat”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/trick-or-treat). Please review if you have not already.

Tech is leading, but not much else is following right now.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zAbf!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0c017b2f-6b20-4fee-af09-9544609401d9_1143x799.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zAbf!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0c017b2f-6b20-4fee-af09-9544609401d9_1143x799.jpeg)

With that said, weakening breadth can last a long time before the indices peak. During the Dot Com Bubble, many individual stocks began peaking as early as 1 year before the SPX eventually topped.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EB2z!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F15efdd40-bf86-4e96-b25a-9002d69a849a_1558x353.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EB2z!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F15efdd40-bf86-4e96-b25a-9002d69a849a_1558x353.png)

The same thing happened during the 2020-21 Covid Bubble. In [“Weakness”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/weakness), I talked about ZM topping in October 2020. This was 14 months before the SPX topped for the cycle. ARKK topped 10 months before the SPX topped, etc.

Just because many stocks are beginning to drop out of the Bull race, does not mean a market correction is imminent any day now. This type of bifurcated market can last awhile, and often has in the past.

**Another Potential Headwind: Put-Call Skew**

Last week, Put/Call Skew in the Mag 7 complex inverted for the first time since December of last year (i.e. implied volatility of calls traded over puts). This phenomenon has only happened a handful of times. The move implies investors are overwhelmingly positioned for continued upside.

Historically, such low skew reading have tended to coincide with short-term consolidation or reversals as optimism peaks. The average 1M SPX return following inversion is -2.34% with 0/5 instances seeing a positive 1M return.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!17HB!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F59a5efed-6320-405e-bdfc-2398e26c7e42_1284x994.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!17HB!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F59a5efed-6320-405e-bdfc-2398e26c7e42_1284x994.jpeg)

**Earnings Season**

With breadth narrowing and less names participating, it is critical right now to focus on those leading stocks that are still making new highs. These are the names that should continue to participate as long as the market melt up continues.

Luckily for us, Earnings season is the ideal way to tell which stocks are leaders and which stocks are laggards. Even within the Mag 7 complex, we got a glimpse of that bifurcation with AMZN soaring and META plummeting post-earnings.

Overall, Q3 Earnings Season has been strong so far. 70% of the S&P 500 has reported to date, and Q3 EPS growth is tracking ahead of expectations at +8% YoY (vs +6% exp). The frequency of beats this quarter is the 2nd highest on record (surpassed only by the Covid reopening period).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!c9p1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdf69ae6c-5f13-4b93-8777-897a738ce9d0_1156x738.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!c9p1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdf69ae6c-5f13-4b93-8777-897a738ce9d0_1156x738.jpeg)

Management guidance has been solid as well.

To summarize, there has been 0 deterioration in the fundamental story here, especially the AI Bull thesis. In fact, on a fundamental basis, the AI Bull thesis is as strong as ever. In [“Trick or Treat”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/trick-or-treat), I covered the incredible CapEx numbers from META, MSFT, and GOOGL.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kTvB!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2ea2ca15-32e5-4c29-ace5-0c87dee727cb_1205x690.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kTvB!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2ea2ca15-32e5-4c29-ace5-0c87dee727cb_1205x690.jpeg)

On that note, CapEx to sales ratios have risen to their highest levels since the Dot Com Bubble. But, CapEx to Free Cash Flow levels are nowhere near Dot Com Bubble levels. By this metric, AI CapEx Spending does not even look like a Bubble yet and has more room to grow.

My sense coming out of Mag 7 earnings is that these companies would much rather overspend on AI investment than risk missing the AI boat. Mark Zuckerberg said as much in a recent interview.
