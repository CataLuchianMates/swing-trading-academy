**What’s Driving This Rally?**

Repositioning.

Positioning has been the dominant force behind the market action all year, overshadowing any tariff headlines or economic data.

**As I have detailed each week at Marlin Capital, the market’s movements since January have been driven by historic shifts in investor positioning.**

I recapped this dynamic in detail in last Monday’s report [“Repositioning Rally”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/repositioning-rally), but I will summarize below:

* At the end of January, everyone was “all in” US equities. The SPX was at record highs and total investor positioning (Institutional + Foreign + Retail) was at all-time highs. Life was good.
* Then, the looming Trade War began to spook investors (particularly institutional & foreign). Hedge Funds & Foreign Investors dumped US stocks at a record pace in February and March. This massive selling led to the -21% SPX selloff, as total investor positioning reached ~15yr lows heading into Liberation Day.
* Days later we got the 90-day tariff pause announcement (just as everyone was underweight US stocks). The VIX peaked, triggering this repositioning and short covering rally we have seen for the last month.

**So, where do we stand now?**

Total investor positioning is just below neutral, with room for further buying ahead.

**Q1 Earnings**

76% of the S&P market cap has now reported. So far, Q1 Earnings season has been better than feared with Q1 EPS growth tracking in the +12-14% range vs +6-8% expected.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1Zel!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa3597ddf-8bab-48b6-b451-400f2b16c645_1095x902.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1Zel!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa3597ddf-8bab-48b6-b451-400f2b16c645_1095x902.jpeg)

Better than expected margins have driven the positive surprise so far, with the average earnings surprise tracking at +5% vs an average sales surprise of +1%. As expected, companies are hesitant to provide forward guidance, and those that do are largely maintaining their existing guides.

Big picture, here are my 3 key points to be aware of from Q1 Earnings Season:

1.) Backwards looking Q1 results have been better than expected

2.) Company forward guidance (or lack thereof) reflects the elevated level of uncertainty

3.) Forward EPS Revisions have been negative

While forward EPS revisions have been overwhelmingly negative (in terms of breadth), the overall EPS picture remains much steadier than previous recessionary periods. In other words, at the benchmark level, forward earnings estimates do not mirror historical years with recessions.

**HOOD**

Robinhood has emerged as 1 of the clear FinTech leaders, with +50% YoY revenue growth and +106% YoY EPS growth for Q1. The company also announced record net deposits and an increased share buyback program.

The initial stock reaction in HOOD was down on Thursday (-4%), but as we saw in APH, it is not uncommon to see a delayed bullish reaction in earnings winners.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fS_5!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Facfb78c5-3dbb-47c6-a8f8-1ba839bc324d_1107x653.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fS_5!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Facfb78c5-3dbb-47c6-a8f8-1ba839bc324d_1107x653.jpeg)

Sometimes, it is actually even more bullish to see a Day 1 earnings selloff that gets bought up quickly. This tells you that demand for the stock is very high (as we saw with APH), as the post-earnings dip does not last long and gets bought up right away.

We saw HOOD stock bounce back +4.23% on Friday, forming an inside day candle. My long entry this week will be on a break above Friday’s highs ($50.07), with my stop loss set below post-earnings lows ($46.50).

Again, my thesis here is that when the post-earnings dip in a leading stock gets immediately bought up (see APH), it means demand for the stock is extremely high. This is a common theme in leading stocks throughout history: dips don’t last long.

**SPOT**

Similar to APH and HOOD, Spotify sold off on its 1st trading day following earnings results. However, as I mentioned above, when the post-earnings dip in a leading stock get immediately bought up, it means demand for the stock is extremely high. It means bears fumbled their chance to take the stock lower, most likely because there is simply too much buying demand.

In SPOT, the post-earnings dip lasted all of 1 day as it filled the gap on day 2 and broke out to new ATHs on Friday (day 4).
