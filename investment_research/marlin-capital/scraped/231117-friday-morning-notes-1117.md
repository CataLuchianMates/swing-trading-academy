# Friday Morning Notes 11.17

**Morning Market Commentary**

Yesterday was another quiet day for US stocks with the S&P 500 finishing +0.12% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uV7W!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc64c95be-39bb-4727-a0ed-63e74e19bac6_1192x900.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uV7W!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc64c95be-39bb-4727-a0ed-63e74e19bac6_1192x900.jpeg)

Technically, this looks like some healthy consolidation for the market after a big squeeze.  The fact that the indices have not pulled in further after such an aggressive rally/extension from key moving averages should be seen as a bullish sign for more shorter term upside.

Mega Cap tech reclaimed its spot as the top performing group in yesterday’s session (+0.97%), followed by Artificial Intelligence (another big YTD winner).  Liquid Most Short, Most Short Rolling, China ADRs, and Non-Profitable tech (all YTD laggards) were the worst performers on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lKz-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5b5fbc1d-f9ad-4ebe-b4a6-34cb2e1e069d_850x810.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lKz-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5b5fbc1d-f9ad-4ebe-b4a6-34cb2e1e069d_850x810.jpeg)

The lags in monetary policy look like they are starting to catch up with the economy, in my opinion.  This is visible in the recent economic data and corporate earnings calls.  Continuing claims are now at their highest level in 2 years (1865k…higher for 8 straight months), retail sales broke a 6 month positive winning streak, and industrial production and the NAHB housing market index hit their lowest levels YTD.

The poor growth data combined with recent lower inflation prints has sent yields lower, and reflexively, stocks higher (SPX’s November is on track to be the best month of 2023…+7.5%).  With that said, it appears overly optimistic to bank on the correlation between declining yields and rising stocks continuing to drive us higher.  Shortly put, “bad” economic data will no longer be “good” for equities for much longer.  Until then, we are still in a “bad is good” regime with stocks in rally mode and the widely expected “soft landing” (3 out of 4 FMS investors expect soft landing, per BofA) in arm’s reach.

CTAs have been very active over the past 2 weeks and are a key reason the market squeeze.  Over the last 10 days, CTAs have bought nearly $70B of US equities.  This is the largest 10d buying in GS Prime’s records (data goes back to 2016).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!j4AI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7d771b21-2c9a-4297-a80d-978d1dd7e241_1126x675.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!j4AI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7d771b21-2c9a-4297-a80d-978d1dd7e241_1126x675.jpeg)

CTAs have officially flipped, covering all shorts, and are now long equity exposures of +17B of S&P.
