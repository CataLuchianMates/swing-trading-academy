# Rolling Over

US stocks got hit hard on Thursday. The S&P 500 fell -1.5%, the Nasdaq dropped -1.7%, and the Russell 2000 led to the downside at -2.2%.

Oil surged nearly +10% after Iran’s new supreme leader declared the Strait of Hormuz will remain closed, and 3 more tankers were attacked overnight in the Persian Gulf. The IEA called it the largest supply disruption in history. Brent crude is now back above $100.

[On Tuesday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/fork-in-the-road), I said to wait and let the market show its hand. 👇

**Thinking in Probabilities**

Professional traders think in terms of probabilities, not absolutes.

There are no absolutes in the stock market. No matter what anyone says, the future is unknowable.

What professional traders DO is gather evidence, assess probabilities and risk/reward, stack layers of probability on their side, and put themselves in a position to succeed.

One of my favorite quotes on this topic comes from Colm O'Shea in Hedge Fund Market Wizards. 👇

> “Fundamentals are not about forecasting the weather for tomorrow, but rather noticing that it is raining today. The great trades don’t require predictions. The Soros trade of going short the pound in 1992 was based on something that had already happened — an ongoing deep recession that made it inevitable that the U.K. would not maintain the high interest rates required by remaining in the ERM. Afterwards, everyone said, “That was incredibly obvious.” Most of the great trades are incredibly obvious.”
>
> - Colm O’Shea, “Hedge Fund Market Wizards” (2012)

Read that again. “**Fundamentals are not about forecasting the weather for tomorrow, but rather noticing that it is raining today.**”

That is exactly what I am doing right now. I am not predicting exactly what the market will do next week or next month. What I am doing is looking at the evidence in front of me (21/60 EMA bearish cross, Financials & many sectors breaking down, VIX at 27, oil above $100, financial conditions tightening, credit conditions deteriorating, etc.) and acknowledging that it is raining. There are storm clouds currently present.

One thing all professional traders share in common is that they respect risk. And it is very clear why this is a necessary quality for sustained success in this game. If you do not respect risk, you will eventually blow up. It's not a question of if. It's a question of when.

Trust me when I say this. You should never underestimate the stock market. Every cycle, we see new traders pop up during the bull market, and get absolutely crushed (giving all their gains back and more) during their first bear cycle. Why? Because a bull cycle rewards bad habits. Buy every dip, don’t use a stop loss, never sell, use insane leverage. It doesn’t really matter when the market is ripping higher every day.

But, trying to use a Bull Market playbook in a Bear cycle will get you punished.

This is exactly why I have said before, “buy and hold” is strictly a Bull Market strategy. During Bear cycles, you should expect most individual stocks to decline 50-80% + (per the Minervini Rule).

> “When a secular leader makes a major top, there’s a 50% chance it will drop 80% and an 80% chance it will drop 50%”
>
> - Mark Minervini

I do not like holding stocks through those kinds of massive drawdowns, hoping and praying they might one day recover. Hoping and praying is not a strategy for sustained success.

This is why one of the most important things for a trader is assessing and identifying the current market environment, and adjusting your strategy to fit. And sometimes (like right now), that strategy is simply capital preservation.
