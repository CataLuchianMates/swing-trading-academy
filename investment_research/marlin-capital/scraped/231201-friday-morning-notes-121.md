# Friday Morning Notes 12.1

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The S&P 500 sold off initially yesterday, but recovered its losses by the close, finishing +0.38% on the day.  The low of the day was $4537, as the index found support at its 8 EMA level.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Y5k_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbf2c919b-5b2f-4596-9a25-5492ed519840_1200x885.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Y5k_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbf2c919b-5b2f-4596-9a25-5492ed519840_1200x885.jpeg)

November was a month that saw the biggest monthly decline in US 10 YR yields since the 2008 GFC (~70 bps retracement peak to trough).  Equities rallied on the backs of this, with the SPX finishing +8% on the month.

The biggest winners of November included a slew of YTD laggards – non-profitable tech, meme stocks, new tech, etc.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DN30!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F523ee7f1-5155-411d-8b16-f56eaefe99dd_1131x542.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DN30!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F523ee7f1-5155-411d-8b16-f56eaefe99dd_1131x542.jpeg)

Financial Conditions eased ~90 bps in November, which is the most on record (data going back to 1982).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kGn7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb7e352b0-7e13-4869-96ed-4dc5cd6c1345_1146x739.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kGn7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb7e352b0-7e13-4869-96ed-4dc5cd6c1345_1146x739.jpeg)

Fed’s Daly yesterday – “not thinking about rate cuts at all right now.”

“Premature to declare victory over inflation.”

Fed’s Daly says he does not see recession on horizon.

After the bell, we saw a handful of better than expected software earnings with ESTC, IOT, PATH, and PD all set for gap ups this morning.  This comes after CRM, SNOW, WDAY, CRWD earlier in the week.

The AAII Bear Index fell to its lowest level since 2018 this week (meaning the bearish sentiment is now at its lowest level since Jan of 2018).  The 4-week change in the AAII Bear Index is one of the largest drops we have seen in the last 20 years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XTaL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8ff64d80-ba59-42ab-ae0d-196a52ba551b_1146x1410.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XTaL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8ff64d80-ba59-42ab-ae0d-196a52ba551b_1146x1410.jpeg)

This coincides with the grind higher in the GS Monetary Policy Optimism Factor (ie more dovish outlook), which is now at 3 year highs.
