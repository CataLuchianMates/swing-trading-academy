# My Top Lessons from 2021: Part 2

[Part 1: Top Lessons from 2021 as an Investor](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-lessons-from-2021-part-1)

---

Below, I dive into my top lessons as a trader from 2021 and my trading strategy for 2022.

Surprise - Number 1 is patience.

Overall, I am quite happy with my trading performance in 2021.  As we close in on the final day of 2021, I find myself up 70.54% for the year in my Individual account.

My goal is to outperform the top performing index each year, so I am happy to have achieved my goal in 2021. Throughout the year, I had a few great trades.

Still, there were a lot of lessons to be learned this year.  2021 taught me to be more patient as both an investor and as a trader.  As a trader, there are times to get aggressive and times to simply be in cash. Trader Julian Komar highlights this theme below:

> “The stock market may offer you 1 or 2 times a year to make some serious money.  Your goal as a trader must be to use these periods and not lose too much money in between.” – Julian Komar

Legendary traders Bill O’Neil and Jesse Livermore were also big proponents of only trading the market during certain periods. The passage below describes how O’Neil and Livermore waited for pivotal points to enter the market and get aggressive.

The key, in my opinion, is to know when to get aggressive versus when to be conservative or 100% cash.  Even in a Bull Market, the market only offers several times I year I want to really be aggressive.

Let’s take a look at the chart of TSLA, a favorite of many traders for its spectacular momentum runs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!r845!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9e5ae2b3-e47f-4445-a15a-70750e6c8734_1224x754.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!r845!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9e5ae2b3-e47f-4445-a15a-70750e6c8734_1224x754.png)

I’ve marked up the chart above to help demonstrate what I mean.  In 2021, there were only 2 instances to get aggressive in TSLA stock.  The key is fully participating in these moves, and avoiding the consolidation/choppiness phases.  Consolidation phases are healthy for a stock long term, but difficult to trade.  Many traders underperform because they overtrade the stock, and participate in the choppy A-B-C-D etc. moves that are marked above.

The Tesla chart above is just one example of how winning stocks move.  A typical winning stock will have 1-3 major stock advance phases over the course of the year, with the rest of the time being consolidation.  Legendary trader Dan Zanger says it best in a 2005 interview below.

> “I really have found that the market may have two big moves during the year.  They may last 6-8-10-12 weeks, and other than that, they just exhaust themselves after that.  The rest of the time, the market is just consolidating the gains.”

As a trader, my strategy is to be aggressive during the run ups and avoid getting chopped up in the consolidation/down moves.  For a trader, being aggressive means to be heavy with position sizing.  Let’s take a look at this strategy in action for me in 2021.

Over the course of 2021, I had only three A+ trade setups arise.  I went over some of what constitutes an A+ trade setup for me in the [RBLX idea](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-trade-idea-for-q4) I gave to premium subscribers in early November (an addition, would be a strong general market). As I mentioned, I used these three A+ opportunities to get heavy in each trade.  For me, these means using options to get leverage.  I do not often trade options, but I will for A+ and A trade setups that I believe will have huge momentum moves.  In each trade below, I was predicting a 100%+ move in the calls, and took advantage of my opportunities.

I go over all 3 of 2021’s A+ trades below:

---

**LC**

I posted about LC being my Top Trade idea for Q3 [here](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-stock-picks-and-best-trade).  You can go over some of what the rationale behind the trade was here, but to summarize, it was basically a play on the multiple re-rating I believed we would see following a massive growth acceleration.

In this trade, I got heavy on Day 1 (7/29) as the stock gapped up over 40% on massive volume.  My first price target was $30.  I opted for buying shares and the Oct $25 calls for $2.00, as the calls would be worth $5.00 (150% gain) if my $30 target was hit.  The October expiration calls were giving this trade a whole quarter to play out, which was more than enough time in my opinion.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3Epv!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe622e31c-d3d5-49cf-8d14-86c7a82c9f07_1595x963.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3Epv!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe622e31c-d3d5-49cf-8d14-86c7a82c9f07_1595x963.png)

As you can see in the diagram above, the period from when I originally entered long to the period where my $30 price target was hit is where I want to be heavy in the name.  You must always analyze the trade based off how much potential is left.  From my cost basis of ~$22 to $33 is a 50% move.  This is the exact type of move I predicted.

At $33, it is no longer an A+ trade setup anymore.  Once it has blown through my price targets, I am scaling out of the trade and only holding a small remaining position.  In the case of LC, I got heavy in the calls at $2.00 and sold them for an average of $5.80 (190% gain). As a trader, you must be patient, until it is time to get aggressive. Then, do not hesitate.

My first price target was a test of ATHs ($147.50).  Even after all this great news, AFRM was still over 30% off ATHs.

**RBLX**

My final A+ trade setup of the year was in RBLX, which I posted about in detail [here](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-trade-idea-for-q4).  I opened a position in Day 1 post-earnings (11/15), and added to my position on Day 2 and Day 3.  I also added on the breakout of $110, making it a very heavy position.

My first price target was $140, as I mentioned in my report.  I bought shares and the Jan $110 calls for $10.40, predicting that these could trade up to $30 each when RBLX hit my $140 target.

I sold all of these calls for $20-$30 as the stock topped out near my first price target.  Once again, RBLX achieved the >40% move I was predicting and the calls were a 100%-200% gainer. I expected the move to take 1-2 months, but it essentially took just 1 week to move >40%.  This is where a good trader takes profits , scales out of the trade, and holds a small remaining position.

Of course, RBLX topped right when ARKK and many growth stocks started a violent move downwards, so it was not difficult to exit my small remaining position on the way down as it made an ugly topping candle.

**Recap**

In my opinion, the two keys to successful swing trading are patience and position sizing.  Like a lion stalking its prey, you must patiently wait for the opportunity to strike.

My biggest lesson to take from 2021 is NOT to overtrade.  As I mentioned above, the market may give you 1-3 times a year to get aggressive.  I took advantage of the 3 A+ trade setups I found in 2021, leading to my outperformance.  However, I also took too many papercuts and small losses on low conviction trades along the way.  Of course, my position size was much smaller in these trades, but these losses add up. My performance in 2021 would have improved had I not made so many needless trades.

Moving forward, I will continue to be even more patient, and grade each setup I am considering taking.  In 2022, I am eliminating any trades that boast less than a “B” setup.  Many traders feel the need to trade every day, or every few days, as they false believe it will improve their performance.  For me, it is about being patient for those golden opportunities that arise.  There is plenty of money to be made in the market during the run ups OR meltdowns.  I have no edge in trading the chop that inevitably occurs in each stock and each index.

There is a reason that legendary trader Bill O’Neil only bought stocks breaking out of large bases.  The idea behind a base is that after a strong runup, the stock begins consolidating its gains as it takes a breather and prepares for a move in either direction.  O’Neil stressed the importance of not buying before the proper entry point, as buying within the base can frustrate you.  In some instances, it can also break down, as I predicted we would see in many growth names in [early December](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/technical-analysis-long-term-holdings).

My objective, moving forward, is to continue to reduce my activity in rangebound markets and stocks.  This is a valuable lesson for all traders, in my opinion.  Be patient most of the year.  Get aggressive 1-3 times per year.
