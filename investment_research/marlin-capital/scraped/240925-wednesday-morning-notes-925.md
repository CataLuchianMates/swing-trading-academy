# Wednesday Morning Notes 9.25

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Markets continue to chop around near highs with the SPX closing +0.25% yesterday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!L53A!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa90985ec-7f35-4f3f-9922-cae1f175b8fb_1206x882.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!L53A!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa90985ec-7f35-4f3f-9922-cae1f175b8fb_1206x882.jpeg)

NVDA led the way, rallying +4% on a Barron’s report that CEO Jensen Huang is “done selling stock for now”.

China ADRs soared after the PBOC unleashed stimulus (KWEB +10% / FXI +10%).  Hedge Fund short covering in the space was real as China ETF call volume hit a near 2-year high.  My take – this looks like more of a relief/short covering rally than an all clear signal for money managers to reengage.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!V56j!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F874a7ef9-6e59-45cd-bc01-a3990120ef9a_1290x832.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!V56j!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F874a7ef9-6e59-45cd-bc01-a3990120ef9a_1290x832.jpeg)

Elsewhere, the market didn’t love the largest drop in US consumer confidence in 3 years as labor market weakness continues to weigh on confidence.  We also saw a pretty sizable miss in the Richmond fed factory index (-21 vs -12 expected).

Active Portfolio –

No changes from yesterday.  It was another huge day for SEZL +8% and we are up well over +100% on this long position heading into Wednesday.  I’ve sold half.

My stops are in place on my other positions and I am letting these trades play out.
