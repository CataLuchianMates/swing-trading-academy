# US Tech Wreck

41% of the S&P 500 is set to report earnings this week, but the big news this weekend was out of China.

DeepSeek, a Chinese AI startup that’s just over a year old, has achieved a seemingly impossible technological breakthrough in the AI world. The Chinese startup has introduced AI models with comparable/better performance to the world’s best chatbots at seemingly a fraction of the cost.

Needless to say, the fact that a 1-year old startup can challenge the Trillion dollar US Big Tech industry in AI is not a welcome development to US equity markets. DeepSeek’s innovative approach is showing the world that its possible to build high performance AI with significantly lower CapEx (cheaper hardware, less chips, less power, etc.). The exact cost for DeepSeek to build these models are unknown/unproven (and it should be noted that China might not exactly be truthful with such claims…but it can be inferred that a 1-year old startup did it with significantly less resources than US Big Tech behemoths).

The DeepSeek story is forcing investors to question everything they know about AI, Mega Cap Tech, and the entire AI supply chain (How did a 1-year old startup just best Microsoft in AI??). This is leading to concerns about how to justify valuations in the Tech world, particularly the Giants like Nvidia, Microsoft, Meta, etc. (which have had multi-year run ups primarily due to being the most dominant AI players in the world). If AI models can perform comparably at a fraction of the cost, it is hard to justify the enormous CapEx on AI infrastructure from US Tech. DeepSeek’s massive breakthrough could push US Tech to reassess their spending strategies, leading to a big reduction in AI infrastructure investments.

DeepSeek’s breakthrough is raising obvious concerns about the whole AI Value Chain. There are so many questions this morning (Is Chinese Tech in the process of passing US Tech/becoming the dominant nation in AI?), but judging by US Equity Market Futures, the market is in a clear “Sell First, Ask Questions Later” mode.

Perhaps the best news for US Tech is that Tesla, Meta, Microsoft, and Apple are set to report earnings this week. So, we only have a few more days to speculate before Big Tech hopefully gives us some clearer answers. The most important factor, in my view, will be CapEx guidance from the Mag 7 and how CEOs/CFOs respond to questions about DeepSeek’s breakthrough. If we start to see CapEx numbers revised lower, the whole US equity market is extremely vulnerable.

This week is shaping up to be a HUGE one for investors as AI sentiment has now soured. We know that Artificial Intelligence is going to revolutionize our world in the years to come, but DeepSeek’s breakthrough is forcing us to question everything we thought we knew about Artificial Intelligence. If DeepSeek can turn water into oil, why are US companies investing so much on AI infrastructure? (chips, hardware, etc.). And, why are US Tech stocks priced at such high valuations if they are losing their dominance?

Sometimes in the market and in the Tech world, unforeseen events can happen that change everything. These types of surprises happen and it is our job as investors and traders to deal with them, since no one can accurately predict the future. This is a true Black Swan event, as even Nancy Pelosi was investing heavily in AI Infrastructure/Chip stocks just a month ago. The US just announced Stargate last week, a potential $1T+ investment in AI Infrastructure.

As mentioned, the US market is in a clear “Sell First, Ask Questions Later” mode this morning with futures pointing to a huge gap down. This is not surprising because there are so many questions emerging right now, but again, 4 of the 7 Mag 7 names will report earnings this week and give their view/guidance.

In my view, is it more likely than not we will see a multi-day selloff here into TSLA, META, and MSFT earnings after hours Wednesday. Big Tech will have a chance to flip the script later this week, but for now, the AI narrative has changed overnight with DeepSeek’s massive technological breakthrough. It is important to remember how far US Tech has run on the notion that they were the dominant players in the AI revolution, but DeepSeek is now forcing us to question that dominance.

This Black Swan event will obviously be painful for me this morning as I come into the day holding 4 long positions in my Active Portfolio + my Top 33 AI names are mostly all set for big gap downs. As I mentioned, though, all you can do as an investor/trader is react knowing that these types of things will happen to you if you are in the game long enough.

Starting with my Active Portfolio, it appears my 4 longs will gap below my stops this morning.

My stop was originally set at $165 here for -3.7% risk but I will be closing out this position at the open for a smaller loss. Maybe this gap down is immediately bough up in Tech, but my priority is risk management now (and for me, this is too big of news for us to see an immediate bottom in Tech).

While it is common to at least see an initial bounce following a big gap down, I am choosing to remain disciplined here. In the case of MRVL and AVGO gapping below my stops, these are immediate sells for me as I do not want to take on any more risk there. For RBRK, my stop is at breakeven on the rest ($66.40). For SNOW, I will be closing out above my stop because of the momentum killing gap down catalyst (and I want to move to 100% cash in this portfolio).

For my 2025 Top Picks Portfolio, this will obviously be a painful day as AI stocks are set to gap down big this morning. As mentioned, I see this as a potential multi-day selloff, but am hopeful that Big Tech can calm the markets with strong guidance/reaffirmation this week. If we see Big Tech revise CapEx guidance lower/show some worry over the DeepSeek news, this is where I believe it will turn into a major selloff for US stocks (and possibly a top for Big Tech for awhile). If this happens, I will be ready to react in my AI Portfolio (most likely by hedging with some US Tech shorts/long Chinese tech). For now, I will have to just take this selloff on the chin.
