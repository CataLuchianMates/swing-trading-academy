# Growth Scare

In [“The Big Unwind”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-big-unwind), I talked about how I believe the current “US growth scare” headlines will start to dominate the newsflow, and that was certainly the case today. The Atlanta Fed just revised their Q1 2025 GDP growth estimate lower once again, from -1.5% just last Friday to -2.8% today.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!B3ln!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2941b1a8-689d-4967-97ca-6d5affdf187c_745x609.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!B3ln!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2941b1a8-689d-4967-97ca-6d5affdf187c_745x609.jpeg)

In just the last 4 weeks, the Atlanta Fed’s Q1 GDP estimate has dropped from +3.9% to -2.8%. This is an unprecedented revision lower.

A -2.8% GDP would obviously be the beginning of a recessionary number (need 2 consecutive qtrs for recession) and would be the US economy’s worst GDP reading since the heart of the Covid Pandemic (Q2 2020; -29.9%).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pgg7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4019097f-7e83-4179-8ebe-4bf000443832_773x462.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pgg7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4019097f-7e83-4179-8ebe-4bf000443832_773x462.png)

This has emerged as a potential catalyst for continued selling, which I believe will ultimately take us into correction territory in the S&P 500 (-10% + in the SPX; $5530).

I’ve talked a lot about the parallels we are currently seeing to the late 2021/2022 market top (mostly in the charts), with the exception that the 2022 Bear Market had a much worse Macro picture (Fed raising aggressively, 10% CPI, etc.). While we did technically have a recession in 2022 with 2 consecutive quarters of negative GDP growth, the numbers were -1.6% and -0.6% (and unemployment did not really rise at all). So while it meets the textbook definition, it was a very shallow recession.

From a US economy standpoint, a -2.8% GDP number would be worse than any reading we saw in 2022. However, we also had an earnings recession in 2022 in both the Mag 7 and S&P 493.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5qZo!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5fda2858-0cbf-4a22-96df-46b212fead92_542x636.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5qZo!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5fda2858-0cbf-4a22-96df-46b212fead92_542x636.jpeg)

In [“The Big Unwind”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-big-unwind), I talked about how we are not currently seeing the negative earnings revisions typically required for a Bear Market (-20% +). That has started to change a bit, as we are now seeing a lot of negative EPS revisions across stocks as well. Consensus EPS growth for 2025 has come down in recent weeks (but still expecting positive growth in ‘25).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JGgL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa1d40f48-b090-41bb-aa1a-28e2d5839e9f_728x578.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JGgL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa1d40f48-b090-41bb-aa1a-28e2d5839e9f_728x578.jpeg)

Revisions to 2025 EPS have now gone negative for the year. Every sector but financials is seeing negative revisions now in 2025.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0Bz8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdd395488-7447-436c-8c95-d81a44ca927e_1114x1682.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0Bz8!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdd395488-7447-436c-8c95-d81a44ca927e_1114x1682.jpeg)

For me, Earnings will be the key factor in determining how deep this market selloff will ultimately get. Consensus is still expecting +9% EPS growth for the S&P 500 in 2025. If this number continues to get revised lower and reaches into negative territory (earnings recession territory), I believe we will be looking at a potential Bear Market (-20%), or close to it. On the other hand, if earnings remain positive and there is no major change in 2025 estimates, I believe this will be closer to the -10% variety correction.

A lot can change from now until the end of 2025 with the Trump administration’s multiple trade wars and ongoing tariff situation. If deals are resolved, this would obviously be a bullish catalyst for SPX earnings. However, if it drags on, we would possibly see earnings to continue to be revised lower.

Anyone whose been reading Marlin Capital since 2022 knows I am a big proponent of studying market cycles and the boom and bust asset cycle. The boom and bust asset cycle has been observed throughout history in all financial assets. Towards equity market tops and the tops of asset bubbles, we typically see euphoria and greed.

We certainly saw euphoria here at the beginning of 2025 with retail investors buying 28 straight days from 1/10 to 2/20, including 4 of the 5 largest retail buying days in history.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3ycQ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1694c4a3-dc25-488d-9173-ca42bf4107d3_255x477.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3ycQ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1694c4a3-dc25-488d-9173-ca42bf4107d3_255x477.jpeg)

Overall, we saw all-time high long positioning across retail, institutions, and foreign investors. This long positioning has basically all been unwound in the last month (especially in crowded longs), and we are just above neutral now.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!s9Vp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2f3e1923-8750-4947-892d-3763a3a3924b_1557x1422.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!s9Vp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2f3e1923-8750-4947-892d-3763a3a3924b_1557x1422.png)

If we are in the Bear phase of this market cycle, we will certainly see positioning go negative for a period of time (like in 2022). Even in shallow market corrections, you will typically see positioning go negative (just not for as long a period of time as deeper Bear Markets like 2022).

[I talked on Saturday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-big-unwind) about how I believe Market Positioning > Market Sentiment, because positioning tells all. Current market sentiment is very bearish, but positioning is not. Current positioning does not reflect extreme fear or panic in any way. It is slightly above neutral.

At the bottoms of Bear cycles, we typically see extreme fear. In my view, the best way to identify extreme fear is seeing very light/underweight market positioning and capitulation/panic selling. I do not believe sentiment indicators capture extreme fear accurately like positioning does. We are not seeing anything close to extreme fear or capitulation at this time.

**Summary Points**

* I will be most closely watching future earnings revisions and market positioning to help me identify a potential SPX bottom in 2025.
* My Base Case remains a -10% correction for the SPX, but we could certainly reach -20% + if we see an earnings recession.
