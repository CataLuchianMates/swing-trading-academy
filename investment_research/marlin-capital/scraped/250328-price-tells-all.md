# Price Tells All

More risk off action again today as the early week oversold bounce to the 200-day MA has failed.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9GnX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0f50c153-a859-4ac4-adbf-9d88b12e599b_943x705.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9GnX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0f50c153-a859-4ac4-adbf-9d88b12e599b_943x705.jpeg)

Legendary trader Paul Tudor Jones once said “Nothing good happens below the 200 day moving average”. While the early week rally back to the 200-day sparked some hopes that the worst of this correction might be behind us, the rest of this week has been a very bearish development. History supports Paul Tudor Jones’ notion that nothing good happens below the 200-day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fz2a!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe623a7e6-e358-4b0a-b169-7175f5b9b988_1179x570.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fz2a!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe623a7e6-e358-4b0a-b169-7175f5b9b988_1179x570.jpeg)

This is why I have repeatedly warned about the technical damage we were seeing across the market during this correction. In [“Do or Die Time”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/do-or-die-time), I wrote:

> “During bull markets, you always get shakeout corrections to the 200 day. You often overshoot the 200 day to the downside. However, these corrections are usually bought up very quickly and the long term trend does not break (i.e. no death crosses).
>
> During this pullback, we are seeing trend breaks, names establishing themselves below the 200 day, and Bearish crosses. This is different than August (and any other pullback we’ve seen since the Bull Market began). This is what I mean when I say the health of the market is currently weaker than during any other pullback we’ve had since the Bull Market began in 10/22.”

There is a big difference between a routine correction during a Bull Market and a Bear Market. For me, the technicals are the best way to identify the trend (i.e., is it a bull market correction, or is it a bear market?). **Price tells all.**

Right now, price is saying that is is NOT a routine correction during a Bull Market. Price is saying that this is a Bear Market.

The leaders (Mag 7) of the Bull Market have already broken down and given Death crosses. Semis have broken down and Death crossed. Tech (XLK) has broken down and Death crossed.

On a technical basis, the uptrends that began in late 2022 have ended. These are now downtrends. There is no argument that can be made.

In “[Big Picture, Small Picture”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/big-picture-small-picture), I talked about how the 1st market bounce attempt typically fails after the SPX enters into a correction so quickly (<20 days). Historically, this gives way to a 2nd wave down in the market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0FrF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F37fc27ac-2bbb-468d-a406-ae8483e935fe_1176x441.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0FrF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F37fc27ac-2bbb-468d-a406-ae8483e935fe_1176x441.jpeg)

The 2nd wave down historically ranges between -3% and -15%, with an average of -10%.

As I talked about on [Wednesday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/red-wednesday), a 2nd wave down of -10% would take us to the $5200 region in the S&P 500, which would be a -15% total drawdown from ATHs.

Based off price action, positioning, etc., this is where I believe we are headed in the SPX ($5200).

This is not to say that I believe $5200 will be the ultimate bottom. As I have discussed in detail, positioning and earnings revisions will be the 2 main factors I will be monitoring to try to call a market bottom.

In terms of positioning, we are not seeing signs of an ultimate bottom yet. We are simply not seeing any signs of capitulation or panic selling. As I talked about on [Wednesday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/red-wednesday), retail has continued to buy the dip during this correction. This is more reminiscent of Q1 2022 than Q4 2022 (when the market bottomed).

In terms of earnings revisions, we have now reached 14 consecutive weekly declines in Citigroup’s US Earnings Revision Index. This is now the longest streak since 2022 (which, as I have covered, did see a technical recession AND an earnings recession).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vnfh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff20b97c6-56f0-416e-bb26-ba5d3c301da6_1284x1102.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vnfh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff20b97c6-56f0-416e-bb26-ba5d3c301da6_1284x1102.jpeg)

Again, I have [talked about the fact](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bounce-phase) that recessionary bear markets tend to reach -20% peak to trough, and tend to last much longer in duration than non-recessionary ones.

Big picture, I do believe we are headed for a -15% + S&P 500 correction minimum here ($5200) just based off price action and positioning. From there, earnings revisions and positioning will be the key tells for me to determine if we are nearing an ultimate bottom.
