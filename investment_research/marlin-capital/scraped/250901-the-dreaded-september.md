# The Dreaded September

It’s that time of year – the dreaded month of September.

September is notoriously the worst month for US stocks (by far), with a -13.5% annualized return for the SPX.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Vizn!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb08683ce-45f9-4a29-912c-4cabd6e03e0a_1284x605.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Vizn!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb08683ce-45f9-4a29-912c-4cabd6e03e0a_1284x605.jpeg)

Dating back to 1928, the S&P 500 is up just 44% of the time in September for an average loss of -1.17%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wnc-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F574cee52-145f-4501-874e-dd8f0ff80b4d_858x650.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wnc-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F574cee52-145f-4501-874e-dd8f0ff80b4d_858x650.png)

Recent September’s haven’t fared much better. 8 of the last 15 have been negative for an average return of -0.94% (again, the worst month over the last 15 yrs).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4knu!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5e8eaf7-5e1e-4682-b1a3-a73694d471e3_1968x1043.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4knu!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5e8eaf7-5e1e-4682-b1a3-a73694d471e3_1968x1043.jpeg)

Digging deeper into the seasonality of September, we find that the market weakness tends to be backloaded.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mr_F!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F621e1e82-175b-40f4-8d2f-cdbc66a2340d_842x491.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mr_F!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F621e1e82-175b-40f4-8d2f-cdbc66a2340d_842x491.png)

While the first half of September is not great historically (5th worst 2-week period), it is still moderately positive since 1950. On the other hand, the last 2 weeks of September are the worst 2-week period on the calendar for the SPX.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qRuv!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5a285cc-cc00-4382-bc0e-1c17c57f96a2_501x330.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qRuv!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5a285cc-cc00-4382-bc0e-1c17c57f96a2_501x330.jpeg)

### Systematic Flows Are a Big Red Flag

All year, I have highlighted how Systematic Flows have been a MAJOR driver of the market’s moves. In Q1, they amplified the tariff-driven Bear Market. Then, massive Systematic Buying fueled this entire +34% rally off April lows.

Now, for the first time since that rally off April lows began, Systematic flows are once again a headwind for US stocks. Here’s why 👇

**CTA positioning in US equities officially sits in the 100th percentile and is maxed out.**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2vfp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F87d3d07e-4c61-4058-b057-52b0f50856b5_1290x972.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2vfp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F87d3d07e-4c61-4058-b057-52b0f50856b5_1290x972.jpeg)

This is suddenly a major downside risk now, with Goldman projecting ~$74B of US equities to sell in a down tape.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DrBm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffed71408-7ec3-4fd5-984a-d9f8b55ecee6_1290x960.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DrBm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffed71408-7ec3-4fd5-984a-d9f8b55ecee6_1290x960.jpeg)

**Vol Control**

Remember how I emphasized what a big tailwind Vol Control Buying would be [in early July](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/volatility-melt?utm_source=publication-search)? Well, that Vol Control buying is finished. Vol Control Funds are now fully loaded, long ~$171B of US equities.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!q0gR!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F669492fd-a1a9-483a-a3d3-f086bc106def_1290x931.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!q0gR!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F669492fd-a1a9-483a-a3d3-f086bc106def_1290x931.jpeg)

In total, Systematic Positioning sits in the 90th percentile versus history.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nfC_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F86c9fc2f-fb8d-4517-9928-57e994ab4323_1290x1008.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nfC_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F86c9fc2f-fb8d-4517-9928-57e994ab4323_1290x1008.jpeg)

To summarize: not much room for buying in this cohort, but plenty of room for selling (in a down tape).

**My Big Picture Thoughts Heading Into September**

I remain cautiously bullish here. With that said, I do not believe now is the time to be getting aggressive on the long side. The recent market action has been choppy at best. Couple that with weak September seasonality and the potential for big systematic selling ahead (in a down tape), and there is definitely plenty of risk for a patented September sell off.

It’s not all bearish, though. Bulls have some things going for them here as well. #1 is the upcoming Fed rate cut, which is a [major tailwind for equities](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/august-finale) (and particularly Small Caps). On that note, the Russell 2000 Index of Small Caps continues to set up for a multi-year base breakout as we head into September.

Further, the S&P 500 is now up +10.1% YTD through August. This typically bodes well for the rest of the year. The best September-December returns happen in years when the SPX has rallied 10-20% YTD through August, with a 91.3% positive rate in such scenarios.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zSj1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb520039-5119-4056-a5ee-4bae7ebebf9a_1284x528.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zSj1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb520039-5119-4056-a5ee-4bae7ebebf9a_1284x528.jpeg)

Big picture, I remain bullish into year end and would view any September weakness as a buying opportunity. With that said, the near-term outlook is tricky here. As mentioned, some caution is warranted here as risks are elevated (particularly the risk of big systematic selling).
