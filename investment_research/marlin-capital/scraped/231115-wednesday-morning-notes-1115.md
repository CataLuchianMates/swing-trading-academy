# Wednesday Morning Notes 11.15

**Morning Market Commentary**

US stocks staged a broad rally following yesterday’s softer than expected CPI report, with the S&P 500 finishing +1.91% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9_zJ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc57632d4-8b4b-47d8-9cdc-1929c53f9660_1163x879.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9_zJ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc57632d4-8b4b-47d8-9cdc-1929c53f9660_1163x879.jpeg)

The rally was broad based, with the bottom of the barrel/YTD laggard themes at the top of the board.  Renewables +7.5%, Non-Profitable Tech +7.5%, High Beta Momentum +7.5%, Regional Banks +7%, Commercial Real Estate +6.8%, Most Short +6.9%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oSzt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F47efbdfe-a81a-485f-ba8f-2886ed532da8_1168x860.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oSzt!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F47efbdfe-a81a-485f-ba8f-2886ed532da8_1168x860.jpeg)

Moreover, the Russell 2000 Index of Small Caps had its largest 1 day rally of the year (and 2nd largest in the last 3 years).  Low Profitable Small Caps had their biggest up move of the year.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_VPE!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc6fc7392-746e-40bb-a478-b036eb34ca3f_1206x639.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_VPE!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc6fc7392-746e-40bb-a478-b036eb34ca3f_1206x639.jpeg)

With everyone trying to catch a part of yesterday’s squeeze, IWM saw its highest call volume ever.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ERvR!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3d9f577b-695e-490d-b493-6a84ba0ceeb5_1145x665.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ERvR!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3d9f577b-695e-490d-b493-6a84ba0ceeb5_1145x665.jpeg)

The Magnificent 7 Basket of Mega Cap Tech is also breaking out, reaching all time highs following yesterday’s inflation numbers.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!X4Cb!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faa194bab-66f3-4e28-9c28-ce805fa2d7cd_1194x722.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!X4Cb!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faa194bab-66f3-4e28-9c28-ce805fa2d7cd_1194x722.jpeg)

However, as mentioned, yesterday’s rally was broad based and not confined to Big Tech/YTD winners.  Another welcome sight for bulls - The S&P Equal Weight (+2.8%) outperformed the S&P Market Cap Weighted by 92 bps (recall S&P cap weighted index is outperforming its equal weighted index by ~15% YTD, the widest margin of outperformance in 30+ years).

CTA Update – Data from GS Prime shows that this cohort is still short (~$6B), but likely to finally flip long in Wednesday’s session.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!i_V9!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F610f47d6-108b-4cea-9b63-913331468bf7_1131x861.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!i_V9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F610f47d6-108b-4cea-9b63-913331468bf7_1131x861.jpeg)

Following yesterday morning’s CPI report, 10 YR yields retracted further below 4.5% to mid-September levels.  The market is now pricing in 2 rate cuts by July ’24 (1st in May/June ’24) and ~100% certainty that the Fed is done raising rates for this cycle.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BWRC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3c1f4918-8508-4203-9d05-fdb3fa0dcdcb_1127x351.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BWRC!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3c1f4918-8508-4203-9d05-fdb3fa0dcdcb_1127x351.jpeg)

Yesterday’s inflation report prompted CNBC’s Jim Cramer to declare that “Fed Chair Powell has successfully engineered a soft landing for the US economy” (and similar sentiment from other Wall Street pundits).

A soft landing is very much consensus ([3 out of 4 fund managers surveyed by BofA share this view](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/tuesday-morning-notes-1114)), and yesterday’s CPI report will certainly do nothing to change that.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!D6UV!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F63096f2a-ee26-4737-9997-75bf00e6b391_774x438.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!D6UV!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F63096f2a-ee26-4737-9997-75bf00e6b391_774x438.jpeg)

Yesterday’s US FCI (Financial Conditions Index) easing of -23 bps was historic, ranking in the 30th biggest 1D easing event going back to 1990.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1hN-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5ca049ee-f49b-4592-9567-5b4e0f43affa_1172x832.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1hN-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5ca049ee-f49b-4592-9567-5b4e0f43affa_1172x832.jpeg)

We are officially at the point now where the GS FCI is lower (easier) on the year, and has erased just about all tightening that occurred over the last couple months (in Sept/Oct).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vu8i!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F427bce99-e2e2-450d-920c-23103245262d_1149x787.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vu8i!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F427bce99-e2e2-450d-920c-23103245262d_1149x787.jpeg)
