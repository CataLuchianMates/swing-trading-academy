**Active Trading Portfolio**

I received a question about position sizing on the trades I place in my Active Portfolio. Below is my detailed answer on how I determine the position sizes on my trades:

In this hypothetical example, let's say you are trading with a $100k account.

First off, everyone must decide for themselves how much they are willing to risk in a trade (based off their own risk tolerance, goals, etc.).

For me, a normal trade is risking 1% of my account. So in our $100k account example, a normal trade would be risking $1k.

On a high confidence A+ trade, I may risk up to 2-4% of my account. Again, this is not a decision I can make for any individual without knowing their risk tolerance, age, goals, full financial situation, etc. This is simply how I trade for myself (and I would characterize my risk tolerance as high).

Let's take our recent ORCL long trade as an example. We entered long on 6/24 at $212.03. Our stop was at $202.53 for $9.50 risk per share (-4.5% risk).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9Xm_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F80b4e8eb-7f18-4166-bbc2-7e7d887fa9c4_1284x428.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9Xm_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F80b4e8eb-7f18-4166-bbc2-7e7d887fa9c4_1284x428.jpeg)

On a normal trade in a $100k account, I would risk $1k on this trade. This tells you how many shares to enter. The math:

$1,000 / $9.50 = 105 shares

105 shares x $212.03 = $22,263

You are entering 22.3% weighting of your account in this trade, risking 1% of your account.
