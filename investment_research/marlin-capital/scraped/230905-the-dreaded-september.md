# The Dreaded September

It’s that time of year – the dreaded month of September

September is the worst month of the year historically for US stocks dating back to 1928.  On average, the SPX is up 44% of the time in September for an average return of -1.6%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bwdu!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F41c33689-743c-40a7-9012-924079d65d90_806x554.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bwdu!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F41c33689-743c-40a7-9012-924079d65d90_806x554.jpeg)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8PIw!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5e83aeb7-87f3-4ade-b459-d167836e3b85_1225x803.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8PIw!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5e83aeb7-87f3-4ade-b459-d167836e3b85_1225x803.jpeg)

Much of this weakness is typically loaded in the 1st half of the month.  In fact, the 1st half of September is the 2nd worst 2 week trading period for the SPX dating back to 1928 (only 2H of February is a weaker period).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Cvrw!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5c2b62d7-ebba-4a0c-b001-932e62064053_1284x963.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Cvrw!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5c2b62d7-ebba-4a0c-b001-932e62064053_1284x963.jpeg)

Over the last 10 years, the NDX has finished September positive only 3 times for an average loss of -1.8%.  September has closed negative for the SPX for 3 straight years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pXpN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5d282da-dbfa-4895-8d6c-36386c85f24e_1171x729.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pXpN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5d282da-dbfa-4895-8d6c-36386c85f24e_1171x729.jpeg)

With that said, September tends to have better returns when the SPX is up YTD (through August), and especially when the index is up 10-20% YTD through August.  Historically, the best setup for both September and the rest of the year is when the SPX rallies 10-20% from Jan-Aug.  This is the scenario for 2023.

A rally of 20% or more on the SPX through August may be too extreme (based off the data) for the index to continue its winning ways in September and over the rest of the year.  Seasonality data shows that September performs the best when the SPX has rallied 10-20% YTD through August.  A January-August return of 17.4% in 2023 fits the bullish sweet spot scenario and bodes well for September.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JX1B!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc334fa20-3035-481b-9f3a-91b306071f12_1218x513.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JX1B!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc334fa20-3035-481b-9f3a-91b306071f12_1218x513.jpeg)

It also typically bodes well for the rest of the year.  The best September-December returns happen in years when the SPX has rallied 10-20% YTD through August, with a 91.3% positive rate in such scenarios.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zSj1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb520039-5119-4056-a5ee-4bae7ebebf9a_1284x528.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zSj1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb520039-5119-4056-a5ee-4bae7ebebf9a_1284x528.jpeg)
