# Friday Morning Notes 12.8

**Daily Market Commentary:**

US stocks finished higher on Thursday with the S&P 500 closing +0.85% on the day.  With yesterday’s close, the index has bounced back above its 8 EMA.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!su17!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feb153674-3bd5-4de0-858d-068d94737956_968x712.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!su17!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feb153674-3bd5-4de0-858d-068d94737956_968x712.jpeg)

The S&P has traded in a 1% high vs low band for the last 2 weeks which is the tightest trading range in the last 2 years.  Stocks drifted higher yesterday led by Mega Cap Tech bid +2.3% driven by enthusiasm around Google’s AI Gemini release (stock +5%, a top 10 day of outperformance in >10 yrs with >600k calls hitting the tape).  AMD finished +10% having one of its best trading days of the year.

As such, we saw the NDX outperform the RTY by 67 bps and saw hedge funds chasing within tech.  I am still holding 1/3rd of my remaining TNA long with a stop below $30 on the final portion.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lFqR!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F809dfaf5-fefa-422a-b9a3-08ad7855f8eb_955x715.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lFqR!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F809dfaf5-fefa-422a-b9a3-08ad7855f8eb_955x715.jpeg)

Outside of tech, we saw a violent squeeze across travel and leisure ignited by JBLU +15% with the company stating demand remains healthy and that close-in booking have outperformed for both holiday peak and non-holiday travel period.

Nonfarm Payrolls (Nov) +199K vs 180K Est.

USA Unemployment Rate For November 3.7% Vs 3.9% Est.; 3.9% Prior

US Average Hourly Earnings (MoM) (Nov) +4% vs +4% Est.

November unemployment rate fell to +3.7% vs. +3.9% est. & +3.9% in prior month

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!L7cv!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fafb064be-268e-4837-ab90-5596638656c5_936x574.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!L7cv!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fafb064be-268e-4837-ab90-5596638656c5_936x574.jpeg)

Quick thoughts - this morning’s NFP report should do nothing to change the soft landing/Goldilocks economy narrative driving the market higher. This was better than expected, and could have been much worse for the near term direction of the market. This should help ease some of the anxiety we saw in the market the last couple weeks.
