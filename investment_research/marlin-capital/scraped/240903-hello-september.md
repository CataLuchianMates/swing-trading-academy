# Hello September

We head into September 2024 with the SPX up +19% and NDX +18% on the year.  Q3 has been a trickier tape, though, with the NDX -1% through the first 2 months (vs REITs, Utilities, Financials all up +10%+ thus far in Q3), indicative of the less directional Tech trade since the NDX peaked in mid-July.

Last week was a huge one for Tech investors, even if the volumes didn’t muster up to the story lines.  NVDA earnings and price action largely supported the view that the GenAI trade has become a bit more two sided.  The good news was Nvidia’s Datacenter revenues grew +155% YoY on a ~$100B run-rate base.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!t7ah!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5277637f-55d8-4423-81e4-f41ae353a272_547x347.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!t7ah!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5277637f-55d8-4423-81e4-f41ae353a272_547x347.jpeg)

The bad news was the narrower EPS beat and guide and slight Gross Margins miss (+ OpEx ramping), arguably muting the NVDA “Wow” factor for the first time since the AI trade began.

To be clear, the NVDA story is still far from derailed.  Bulls are shifting their focus to the upcoming Blackwell product ramp into CY25 and Jensen Huang left plenty of breadcrumbs on the earnings call re: generative AI usage and engagement expansion to sovereigns, enterprises, and startups.  Not to mention, Axios reported last week that 200M people now use ChatGPT each week, a double since last November.  With that said, there is simply less momentum in the AI theme these days as investors look to AI monetization clues to balance the growing CapEx burden.

Other themes and sectors have taken the baton in Q3 and are already racing ahead to new highs, as I highlighted last week.  Industrials, Utilities, Financials, Health Care, Consumer Staples, and Materials each hit new YTD highs last week (while the QQQ remains -5.4% off 2024 highs heading into this week). The Equal Weighted S&P 500 (RSP) finished last week at ATHs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YTGq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F99c12c0e-f6b7-4f46-9c81-0878f5c46b58_1197x882.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YTGq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F99c12c0e-f6b7-4f46-9c81-0878f5c46b58_1197x882.jpeg)

September is a trickier month for the market as a whole, with volumes typically ramping up versus August and July summer price action.  Over the last 10 years, only 2 September’s (2016 & 2019) have finished positive for the NDX.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZAQK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffd370718-532c-48d2-b23d-fe5bec70569b_1120x698.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZAQK!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffd370718-532c-48d2-b23d-fe5bec70569b_1120x698.jpeg)

September is notoriously the worst month for US stocks (by far), with a -13.5% annualized return for the SPX.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Vizn!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb08683ce-45f9-4a29-912c-4cabd6e03e0a_1284x605.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Vizn!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb08683ce-45f9-4a29-912c-4cabd6e03e0a_1284x605.jpeg)

Digging deeper into the seasonality of September, we find that the market weakness tends to be backloaded.  While the first half of September is not great historically (5th worst 2-week period), it is still moderately positive since 1950.  On the other hand, the last 2 weeks of September are the worst 2-week period on the calendar for the SPX.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qRuv!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5a285cc-cc00-4382-bc0e-1c17c57f96a2_501x330.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qRuv!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5a285cc-cc00-4382-bc0e-1c17c57f96a2_501x330.jpeg)

Where do I stand on this? The S&P 500 is just 21bps away from previous ATHs set on 7/16, while the equal weighted SPX (RSP) has already raced ahead to new highs.  I think we are likely to hit new highs this week, triggering some FOMO when the new all-time high headlines hit.  The pain trade for equities remains higher into mid-September after US systematic strategies overshot exposure to the downside during the recent pullback.  The greenlight has been given to get back in the pool, and this unemotional demand should provide a big tailwind for the market over the next couple weeks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jIb4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1be27aeb-68ff-4945-8a88-4d6b9b9daabf_506x348.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jIb4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1be27aeb-68ff-4945-8a88-4d6b9b9daabf_506x348.jpeg)

GS Corporate Buyback Desk sees very strong corporate demand in the way of Buybacks into the upcoming blackout window (estimated September 13th), providing another big tailwind for stocks over the next couple weeks.

With these flow tailwinds and the S&P 500 sitting just 21bps from an ATH breakout (plus the RSP and many sectors already breaking out), I see the market setting up for a strong 1st half of September.  After that, some of the tailwinds dissipate (CTA + Corporate demand) into the weakest 2-week period on the calendar.

If the next couple weeks plays out the way I am looking for, it will certainly make sense to play some defense into September end. With that said, we will cross that bridge if it comes to it as I prefer to take it 1 week at a time. For this week, I remain bullish on the market here.
