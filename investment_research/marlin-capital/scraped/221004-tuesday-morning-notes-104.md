# Tuesday Morning Notes 10.4

Technical Analysis

The market kicked off the week with a solid bounce yesterday, allowing the indices to shake off some of their oversold conditions. Today’s gap up will do even more in that area as the indices are set to open up +2%.

As I described in yesterday’s morning notes, the counter-trend moves are even faster and more violent when the VIX is elevated. In the first 2 waves down, such bounces typically failed between the 8 and 21 Daily EMAs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zdUj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F735da423-4bb1-4c7f-a93b-580e2be0ff2d_962x722.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zdUj!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F735da423-4bb1-4c7f-a93b-580e2be0ff2d_962x722.jpeg)

If the current bounce follows this pattern, we should see this bounce fail in the $275-$285 area. We are set to open near $279 in the QQQ, so the indices are starting to run into some resistance with today’s large gap. It is still possible to continue to squeeze higher with a possible overshoot to the upside, but for those looking to add a hedge/short position, this bounce may provide the best opportunity.
