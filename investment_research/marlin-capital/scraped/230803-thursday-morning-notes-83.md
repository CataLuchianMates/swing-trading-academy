# Thursday Morning Notes 8.3

Technical Update

The S&P 500 sold off -1.38% on Wednesday in its worst day since February.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ga69!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7de714d6-e519-42bf-9e0b-1692e4dfd1a8_978x720.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ga69!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7de714d6-e519-42bf-9e0b-1692e4dfd1a8_978x720.jpeg)

The index closed right at key 21 EMA support ($4510) after briefly breaking below in yesterday’s session. We are set for a gap below $4510 support this morning.

This would be the first break below the 21 EMA for the SPX since May. In April and May, we spent a maximum of 2 days below the 21 EMA at a time before rallying back above.

This technical uptrend, which started in March with the Fed’s regional banK bailout and massive balance sheet injection, is now under pressure. A close below $4500-$4510 opens the door for a further pullback to the 60 EMA ($4380-$4400).

AAPL and AMZN report earnings after the bell, so this is also likely to play a big factor in where we go from here. Some names in my watchlist are still acting well (CLS, COUR), but I don’t plan to try to fight the general market conditions. Ideally for bulls, we would need to see the SPX rally back above the 21 EMA to close out this week. Otherwise, I believe a 60 EMA test ($4380-$4400) is likely on deck.
