# Tuesday Morning Notes 11.5

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Markets were little changed yesterday as the world awaits the US election today. The S&P 500 finished the day -0.28% on -15% volume (vs YTD avg).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JpF4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F51011292-7b88-4aac-a2bd-5633949f95b5_1195x885.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JpF4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F51011292-7b88-4aac-a2bd-5633949f95b5_1195x885.jpeg)

Recent polling data shows this election to be extremely close with a focus on several key swing states (Pennsylvania, Georgia, North Carolina, Michigan, Arizona, Wisconsin, and Nevada).

Volumes were down ~15% vs yearly avg yesterday. The day after the election often shows the most significant jump in volume as results are finalized. Notably in 2016, we saw an increase of 68% vs FY ADV, followed by another +72% the subsequent day. 2020 also showed a spike in volumes on the day after the election although it was much lower when compared to FY ADV as the Covid crisis caused historically high volumes that year.

The Volatility market is currently pricing in a 191bp move for the S&P through Wednesday’s close.

The final Polymarket map into Election Day assigns a 58.4% probability of a Trump victory vs a 41.7% probability of a Harris victory.
