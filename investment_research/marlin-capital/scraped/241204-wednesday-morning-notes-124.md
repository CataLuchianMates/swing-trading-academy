# Wednesday Morning Notes 12.4

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The S&P 500 closed out another day in the green yesterday for the 10th day out of the past 11 (has only happened 20 times in the past 25 years). Positive momentum in Tech continued with the NDX closing Tuesday at fresh record highs.

There are 18 trading days remaining in 2024. The seasonals are strong with inflows typically pouring in the 2nd half of December and into early January (the January Effect). Many don’t believe in the power of seasonals, but it is traditional for the largest capital in the world to be deployed into the US equity market during the first (and last) few weeks of the year.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jzxD!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe761b885-268e-485d-9e5e-5e526dd58dfd_496x304.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jzxD!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe761b885-268e-485d-9e5e-5e526dd58dfd_496x304.jpeg)

In my view, it is not wise to be bearish US equities in late December/early January unless there is some type of major catalyst causing selling. In the absence of that, you are going to see large capital being deployed (some passive, some active) into a lower liquidity trading environment. The bar to be bearish during these weeks is VERY HIGH. I remain bullish into year end and into early January.
