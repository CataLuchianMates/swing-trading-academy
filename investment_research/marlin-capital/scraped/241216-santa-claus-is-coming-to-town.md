# Santa Claus is Coming to Town

2 weeks left in 2024 and what a year its been.

The market’s positive momentum from 2023 carried over into 2024 with the Mag 7 leading the S&P 500 to 38 ATHs during the 1st half of the year. AI mania continued with NVDA maintaining its crown as king of the US stock market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!c7pG!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5e9c6849-1cae-4cbd-8608-dc072adc9b72_612x360.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!c7pG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5e9c6849-1cae-4cbd-8608-dc072adc9b72_612x360.jpeg)

In the 2nd half, we have since transitioned to a broader based rally with Small Caps, Deregulation, Equal Weight SPX, etc. joining the party.

Dips in the market were shallow and short lived. Looking back, the most consistent buyer of US stocks this year was corporates. Companies repurchased over $1T of their shares in 2024, making this a top 3 buyback year in the history of the US stock market. As authorizations continue to ramp, I believe $1T annual corporate bids will be the new norm going forward.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!x51O!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4f48f9ea-fdc4-403a-8c1b-c8c0389da985_585x451.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!x51O!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4f48f9ea-fdc4-403a-8c1b-c8c0389da985_585x451.jpeg)

August 5th was perhaps the most memorable day of 2024, as Japan’s Nikkei closed down -12.4% (worst day since Black Monday 1987). The VIX touched 65 in response while the S&P 500 declined -3%, making 8/5 the worst day of 2024.

The Fed responded with a 50bp rate cut in September (the 1st cut since the -100bp cut to 0 in March 2020). The Fed followed with another 25bp cut in November and Fed Funds Futures are currently pricing in a 98% chance of a 25bp cut this week (December meeting).

It is worth noting that the Fed has cut rates with stocks near all-time-highs on 20 different occasions throughout history. The SPX was higher a year later all 20 times.

On November 5th, Donald Trump was elected the 47th president of the United States. The VIX has since collapsed and the market has continued making new ATHs. Reminder, the 1st year of Trump’s presidency (2017) was the lowest volatility year in 6 decades.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rr6_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff0f5d059-1ec2-4196-88b0-a681ec532faf_567x392.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rr6_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff0f5d059-1ec2-4196-88b0-a681ec532faf_567x392.jpeg)

Following Trump’s victory, the S&P 500 broke through $6k on hopes of a lower corporate tax rate, deregulation, and a more business friendly regulatory environment. We also saw Trump’s win greatly boost Crypto, as the president elect has embraced the asset class and promised to make the US the Crypto capital of the world. Bitcoin is now trading above $100k for the 1st time in its history, up ~$30k since election night.

Bears have continued to point to stretched valuations as a top concern with the SPX trading at a 22x Fwd P/E, an elevated number versus history. My view is that lofty valuations alone will not break this Bull run. Bears will need an earnings downturn/an economic data decline to disrupt this market. As it stands, I am not seeing either of those things currently on the horizon. Realized YoY EPS growth has continued to come in better than expected in Q3:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3Z5A!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3a93fbd8-9daa-49c6-b4eb-b16e00fa767a_930x579.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3Z5A!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3a93fbd8-9daa-49c6-b4eb-b16e00fa767a_930x579.jpeg)

This week, the Fed’s December meeting concludes on Wednesday. A 25bp cut is effectively priced in to the market with a 98% probability. With that said, Powell’s commentary and the Fed’s economic projections are both potential catalysts for the market.

This is the last real hurdle for the market in 2024, and it is shaping up to be a fairly anti-climactic Fed meeting. The 2nd half of December is historically the 2nd strongest period of the year for US equities, and the SPX has been positive 83% of the time in election years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dCer!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3ce9d0a4-625f-48f3-a710-88a13e124e18_1153x795.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dCer!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3ce9d0a4-625f-48f3-a710-88a13e124e18_1153x795.jpeg)

I have [talked in detail about the reasons why](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-final-act-of-2024) I remain bullish the US market until at least mid-January (Inauguration Day). I believe we are following the melt up path and without a negative catalyst to disrupt us, I see the trend continuing higher from here.
