Notice the peak in Polymarket recession odds in the chart above (~67% on 4/7). **This day (4/7) also coincided with the lows in the S&P 500 AND the peak in the VIX.**

The S&P 500 bottomed on the morning of April 7th when we got the initial fake report about a 90-day tariff pause. We ended up getting the official 90-day tariff pause news that Wednesday (2 days later), but the SPX never revisited Monday’s lows.

The VIX also peaked on the morning of April 7th at 60.13.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!v4Bt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F97ba507d-bf52-4bfc-b4ae-a85fe567aa8b_1182x888.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!v4Bt!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F97ba507d-bf52-4bfc-b4ae-a85fe567aa8b_1182x888.jpeg)

The VIX now appears to have formed a clear topping pattern, in my view. This signals a major decline in market fear.

As I highlighted at the time, this was the likely to be the moment of peak fear/peak recessionary fear. [Polymarket](https://polymarket.com/) odds confirm this fact, as fears of a recession have since dropped (to now 53% odds).

At the time, we were also seeing clear signs of capitulation selling. Total positioning reached -2.5z, (essentially nearing 15-year lows), as Hedge Funds dumped stocks at a record pace.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!k82M!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F67e04538-9aa4-4376-aca9-8e2b59bc2c99_1290x1134.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!k82M!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F67e04538-9aa4-4376-aca9-8e2b59bc2c99_1290x1134.jpeg)

My earlier view was: with the VIX & recession fears peaking, alongside positioning potentially bottoming, it is a favorable technical setup for a market bottom beginning to form.

Positioning has now become a big tailwind, as it appears to have most likely bottomed at -2.5z (near 15yr lows). Systematic funds and CTAs are now turning into buyers from a completely washed out level.

CTAs have now begun buying from a -$26B net short position.

Reminder that this cohort chases trends in equity markets. The “dumping” of the last few weeks is over, and sustained rallies will only extend demand.

Moreover, with the market rallying, deeply underweight Hedge Funds will likely be forced to add long exposure (i.e, “reposition” after dumping everything). These positioning factors are all working to create a strong tailwind for the market to rally.

**Character Change**

I have also talked about how markets often begin to bottom when news is at its worst (and top when news is at its best). TSLA earnings yesterday looked like a character change for the market, with the stock rallying on bad news (poor earnings).

The VIX topping confirms this change in character for the market. While US/China trade headlines continue, their impact is diminishing, as evidenced by the VIX’s declining sensitivity to negative news. The headline roulette news flow is having less and less of an effect (i.e., even the bad headlines are not causing too much fear anymore). Again, this is a change in character for the market.

**Super Strong Breadth**

Over the past 10 days, we have witnessed 2 days of exceptional market breadth, with over 96% of S&P 500 stocks advancing. Historically, this has been a very bullish signal for future returns (h/t subutade).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K-Tm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7c482a23-3049-42e4-a1d8-1ef252adad3b_2048x1260.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K-Tm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7c482a23-3049-42e4-a1d8-1ef252adad3b_2048x1260.jpeg)

After this has happened in the past, the market was consistently higher 2+ months later (outside of the 2008 GFC Bear Market).

Today, the Zweig Breadth Thrust triggered. This indicator, developed by legendary trader Marty Zweig, is one of the most historically accurate technical indicators ever created.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JkJU!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F09598c22-b949-4518-aba1-2c7ac187922b_2048x1503.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JkJU!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F09598c22-b949-4518-aba1-2c7ac187922b_2048x1503.jpeg)

This is now the 17th Zweig Breadth Thrust that’s ever triggered (h/t subutrade). Following the previous 16, the S&P 500 was higher 6 months and 1 year later 100% of the time (for an average gain of +16.3% and +23.8%, respectively).

Remember, it’s a market of stocks. When 96% of stocks are being bought, this is no doubt extremely bullish. The historical data confirms this as well, as strong breadth (especially the Zweig Breadth Thrust) has been an incredibly accurate indicator for future returns.

The technical setup is now quite strong, driven by a peaking VIX, bottoming positioning, and exceptionally strong breadth. I believe the S&P 500 could rally toward the top of my $5,700 trading range, with the 200 SMA at $5,747 acting as potential resistance. Systematic buying and forced long exposure from underweight funds are likely to further support this move.
