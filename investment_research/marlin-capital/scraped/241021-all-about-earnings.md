# All About Earnings 

US stocks grinded out another weekly gain last week, now up 6 weeks in a row.  Banks were the big winners (regional banks +7%), while Small Caps, Most Shorted, Value Stocks, and 12M Laggards also led the tape higher.

In the 1 month since the Fed’s first rate cut of this cycle (a 50bp cut on 9/18), US 10YR yields are up nearly ~50bps (in a straight line up) and the market has traded decidedly pro-cyclical (Tech, Industrials, Financials, Materials pacing the indices higher vs Healthcare, Staples, REITs lagging).  This is largely what one would expect in a backdrop of Fed easing, the US economy remaining stable, and recessionary fears not currently a factor.

Speaking of recessionary fears (or lack thereof), just 8% of respondents to BofA’s October ’24 Fund Manager survey say a recession is the most likely outcome for the global economy in the next 12 months.

The aforementioned cyclical rally has continued into the beginning of earnings season with early price action across the market largely fitting this theme (think EPS reactions across Banks/Fins or TSM or industrials like FAST/ALV/UAL).  For this week, the focus on the cyclical theme shifts to names like TXN and LRCX as Tech investors look for clues of how real this cyclical appetite is in Semis.

We are entering the heart of earnings season now, with 19% of the SPX market cap set to report this week and 42% set to report next week.  Overall, 61% of the SPX market cap will report in the next 2 weeks.

It is a quiet week ahead for Macro data, headlined by Beige Book Weds, New Home Sales Thurs, U Mich Sentiment + Inflation Exp Friday as well as a handful of Fed speakers (note we got into the blackout here starting Saturday).

Today is also peak corporate blackout window with this flow dynamic set to improve materially next week.  US corporates have been the largest net buyers of US equities in 2024 and look ready to return the blackout window with dry powder.

Overall, US corporates authorized $1.1T worth of stock YTD, the largest authorizations year on record (according to Birinyi).  US authorizations are +0.9% higher than the largest YTD year (2022) and +21% higher than last year (2023 YTD).
