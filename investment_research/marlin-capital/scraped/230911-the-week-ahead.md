# The Week Ahead

September has lived up to its reputation as the weakest month for stocks so far with the SPX down -1.1% and the QQQ down -1.4% through the first 1/3rd of the month.

The indices are currently at a pivotal juncture technically as we enter this week. The S&P 500 closed Friday just below the 21 EMA resistance level ($4463).  The low of last week was $4430, just above the key 60 EMA support level.

Key support levels to watch in the SPX include $4420-$4430, $4400, and $4330-$4350.

Key resistance levels to watch in the SPX include $4465-$4475, $4500, and $4540-$4550.

The QQQ has a very similar technical setup heading into this week, closing Friday right at its 21 EMA.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zPQq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa4a6b96b-4fa8-4acf-ad16-2b4d24166728_1206x884.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zPQq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa4a6b96b-4fa8-4acf-ad16-2b4d24166728_1206x884.jpeg)

One big contributor to the market’s struggles in September is AAPL.  AAPL has been a big factor in the market’s YTD rally, but the stock struggled following last week’s news that Beijing ordered some central government employees to stop using iPhones.  The daily chart appears to be setting up bearishly for more potential downside. Apple is hosting its annual iPhone event this Tuesday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!me4K!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe49daa2f-6ed2-4223-8213-85bc0fa31d22_978x718.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!me4K!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe49daa2f-6ed2-4223-8213-85bc0fa31d22_978x718.jpeg)

Meanwhile, small caps (IWM) are already deep below their 21 and 60 EMAs, and currently nearing their 200 SMA support levels.  Small caps are often seen as a barometer of future economic sentiment, so this is a potential cause for concern (even though they have lagged YTD).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9WmA!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3cda6d85-cbaf-484b-9fc3-4593bfc87c58_990x718.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9WmA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3cda6d85-cbaf-484b-9fc3-4593bfc87c58_990x718.jpeg)

It will be important to the overall market for IWM to hold 200 SMA support ($182).

With so much general market uncertainty, I am currently only holding 2 long positions in COUR and CLS.

COUR

This long trade has worked well since the inside day break entry on August 30th at $17.02.

Late last week gave us an opportunity to take some profits on the daily chart as we got quite extended outside the upper Bollinger band.  I locked in 1/3rd profits on Thursday and am holding my remaining shares into this week.  Friday closed with a potential short-term topping candle/shooting star.

With how extended we still are from the 21 EMA, a bigger pullback would not be surprising here.  I am raising my stop to $17.32, ensuring a profitable trade on the last 2/3rds.

On the weekly chart, I still see plenty of upside to $20-$25 in the near term.  It will likely need the support of the indices for such a rally, but very attainable if we get it.
