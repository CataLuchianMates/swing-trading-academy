# Wednesday Morning Notes 2.8

2.8.23

Some things I would like to address -  
  
The discord group has been inactive for some time now.  I had previously kept it open so that subscribers could still easily ask questions.  I have now discontinued it to avoid any confusion stemming from it.   
  
Despite my best efforts in late 2022, I was unable to confirm the Substack identities/payment status of a number of discord accounts.  This was problematic because, there were members in the group who were intentionally attempting to avoid payment.  There were also many paying members who did not have a discord and asked that all content be posted directly on Substack.   
  
This is precisely why everything is posted on Substack, in 1 organized format for all (and **only**) paying subscribers to see.  This includes all trade ideas/setups.     
  
One example is the CLH long idea [posted here on 1/16](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/is-it-different).  In this case, I gave the exact setup I was planning on personally buying ahead of time (before the market even opened).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ka-k!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5b471e9-541c-4442-847f-8b7391a05d6a_787x479.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ka-k!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff5b471e9-541c-4442-847f-8b7391a05d6a_787x479.png)

At that point, it is the responsibility of each individual to make a personal decision for themselves on how they wish to proceed.  I have given the exact setup (with defined risk) that I will be trading.  In [future posts](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/friday-morning-notes-127) (after the setup triggered), I go on to talk about how I am personally managing the position (where I’m taking profits, where I’m moving the stop loss to, etc.).  If you are still looking for more guidance than this, Marlin Capital is not the service best suited for you.

In this newsletter, I talk about how I **personally** trade my portfolio.  The trades/setups that I discuss may not be suited for everyone.  That is for each individual to decide for themselves.  I discuss them because I believe they are suited for me and my strategy.   
  
I also must address this…For anyone claiming I have been on the wrong side of this market rally, this is simply false and a lie.  
  
First off, I have **only** talked about long trade ideas in 2023.  I have **ONLY** entered long trades in 2023. I have **NOT** shorted a stock since **Q3 of last year**.  In [“We’re a Long Way From Kansas Now (1/8)”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/were-a-long-way-from-kansas-now), I wrote “it is important to avoid the trap of becoming more bearish the lower the market gets. The time for extreme bearishness was 1 year ago.”   
  
In [“The Week Ahead (1/23)”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-week-ahead-595), with the SPX at $3972, I discussed my view that the index was setting up for a test of Dec highs ($4100).  In the report, I gave 3 long trade ideas (and **only long** trade ideas).  In the same report (1/23), I highlighted NVDA as “one of the best looking charts in the tech space”.  The stock is up 22.7% from that day’s open.  
  
So, it should be very clear which side of the market I have been trading on.  Since Q4, I have been positioned in either 100% cash or mostly cash with some longs (**no shorts**). YTD, I have discussed 7+ long trade ideas and a 0 short trade ideas.   
  
Yes, my bottom price target on the SPX (for the low of **this cycle**) is $2900-$3300.  That is still the case.  However, the future is not a pre-determined path that is destined to only play out 1 way (no matter what).  If the Fed surprises and cuts rates to 0 tomorrow, that path would drastically change.  If Russia decides to nuke Manhattan tomorrow, that path would drastically change.  A trader must be open and willing to adapt to rapidly changing information.  
  
I have never recommended trading solely based off one’s opinion.  I have never recommended trading solely based off Macro.  Or Fundamentals.  Or technicals.  These are all tools I use **together**, with the goal of achieving the highest portfolio returns that I possibly can.  
  
I have also never recommended trading solely based off my analysis.  I have always stressed the importance of doing your own research & due diligence.  The purpose of Marlin Capital is to be part of that research & DD process.

If you are looing for a trader with a crystal ball and perfect market timing, you are in the wrong place. If you are looking for a financial advising firm to tell you exactly what to do, or someone to blame for hitting the buttons, you are in the wrong place. I can surmise that there are a few readers here that are in the wrong place. I will see too that this gets sorted out and they find their way.

To the vast majority of you, I apologize for this distraction.
