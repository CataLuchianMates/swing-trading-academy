# Is The Bottom In?

Despite a strong rally in the Nasdaq today, volume was lower than Friday.  Also, net lows have continued their bearish trend.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NdsB!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5dec1428-edea-4153-b9e6-7ee21ee8617b_1822x923.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NdsB!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5dec1428-edea-4153-b9e6-7ee21ee8617b_1822x923.png)

We basically have two forces at play right now.  First, we have already seen historic levels of selling pressure, on par with the worst markets of the past 25 years.

As of last week, 42% of Nasdaq stocks had been cut in half.  This number has been higher in only three instances since 1999: the Dot Com Bust, 2008 Great Financial Crisis, and March 2020 Covid crash.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5wI3!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F76267521-4694-492b-8c48-8ea22e8628ad_550x375.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5wI3!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F76267521-4694-492b-8c48-8ea22e8628ad_550x375.png)

We also began to see certain sentiment indicators at extremely bearish levels.  Jim Cramer even made a notable “Short ARKK call” on his CNBC show “Mad Money”, which has proven to be the bottom in ARKK so far.

As of last week, Small Cap P/E valuations (S&P 600) were near 20 year lows.  The post-Covid re-rating in small caps has already been fully wiped out.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hRAP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F10359faa-adcc-4770-bf96-9cec3cdc8357_437x332.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hRAP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F10359faa-adcc-4770-bf96-9cec3cdc8357_437x332.png)

We also saw a surge in small traders buying puts, at one of the highest levels in the last 20 years.  This has been a good contra-indicator in the past, leading to above average returns 2 and 3 months later.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!clk8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5f32f5c8-265a-432d-8c05-f8ef0a1cb373_807x545.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!clk8!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5f32f5c8-265a-432d-8c05-f8ef0a1cb373_807x545.png)

All of these examples are indicators of excess pessimism and extremely bearish sentiment.  These are the type of indicators I look to buy into in my long term account, as I did last week.

The other force we have in play, however, is the Macro Environment we currently find ourselves in.  It is a challenging one for stocks.

Let’s start with valuations.  The S&P 500 Forward P/E Ratio is still above 20x, a multiple we did not see prior to real rates going negative.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!y02l!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F86e334d1-cfea-4f3c-b567-c1ef4f80c396_338x301.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!y02l!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F86e334d1-cfea-4f3c-b567-c1ef4f80c396_338x301.png)

Now, real rates are likely headed to the 0.5-1.5 range.  Notice what happened in 2018 above.  2018 is important because the Fed hiked rates 4 times that year, and we had QT for the entire year.  As you can see, 2018 was a year of multiple compression as real rates soared above 1.0.

It is hard to imagine we see anything but a year of multiple compression this year into an aggressive Fed tightening cycle, QT, and slowing growth.  I believe we will see the S&P 500 trade at a multiple in the 14x-18x P/E range by the 2nd half of 2022.

Software valuations are now within their normalized range, but they are at the very high end of the range.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sV5h!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcf8ea9ad-99ef-41b6-a71b-f292a6532e0c_678x361.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sV5h!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcf8ea9ad-99ef-41b6-a71b-f292a6532e0c_678x361.png)

All of this is important to note because over a 1 year time frame, changes in multiples are the #1 driver of stock performance.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HLAq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa3715650-d432-4475-ac1c-92bf6bf29844_884x557.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HLAq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa3715650-d432-4475-ac1c-92bf6bf29844_884x557.png)

Quite simply, it will be difficult for stocks to perform well if valuations are compressing.  The smart money is on multiples compressing throughout 2022, as this is the historical norm for Fed tightening cycles

The market is now pricing in 5 rate hikes, but certain investment banks have called for up to 7.  We will also have to deal with QT in H2, which leads to [more market volatility](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-thoughts-post-fed-meeting-de7).

**Summary**

We basically have 2 forces at play in the market: (1) Historically high levels of selling pressure, excessive pessimism.  (2) A challenging Macro backdrop for stocks, high valuations.

Ultimately, as I said above, I believe the S&P 500 will find itself at a 14x-18x Fwd P/E by/in the 2nd half of 2022.  Big picture, any type of large rally here will lead to valuations expanding beyond 20x, leading to more potential downside in H2.

As for the near term, I believe there is potential for this rally to continue as negative sentiment continues to get washed out.  First up for ARKK is a test of the 21 EMA.

I will be closely monitoring how the ETF reacts to this resistance level in the coming days.  A close above the 21 EMA would signal a short term trend change.
