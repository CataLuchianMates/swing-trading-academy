# Do or Die Time

The whipsaw market action continues this afternoon with a big index gap down following this morning’s ADP report. Private employers added just 77k jobs in February, far below expectations.

However, around 10 AM EST, we got news of potential tariff delays, and the market bounced well of its lows. We are retesting those lows now early this afternoon. As I said yesterday, this remains a daytrader’s market with lots of intraday volatility and headline driven action.

Looking bigger picture, both the S&P 500 and QQQ are sitting right at 200 day support. This is critical support for Bulls.

For the SPX, $5730-5745 is the key support zone.

Today is now the 3rd bounce attempt for the indices once we dipped into those support zones. We are back at the support zones now early this afternoon. If these lows can hold up, it opens the door for a larger bounce into the declining EMAs. Many names are where you might expect them to bounce based off oversold conditions (SPX red 8 of last 9 days, RSI ~30, etc.).

For the S&P 500, we reached a -6.7% pullback at Tuesday’s lows. We had a -9.7% pullback for the S&P 500 in August before going on to make new highs and resuming the Bull Market.

I talked yesterday about how I’m seeing more red flags right now than I saw during the August pullback. Here’s what we didn’t see in August that we’re seeing now:

Leading stocks breaking below the 200 day and establishing themselves below

4 of the Mag 7 names are currently below their 200 day moving averages (NVDA, MSFT, GOOGL, TSLA).

NVDA was the leader of this entire Bull Market. Now its establishing itself below the 200 day for the first time since its uptrend began and appears headed for a Bearish cross (death cross). This would be the first Bearish Cross for NVDA since the Bull Market began in October 2022 (NVDA was at $10.81).

During bull markets, you always get shakeout corrections to the 200 day. You often overshoot the 200 day to the downside. However, these corrections are usually bought up very quickly and the long term trend does not break (i.e. no death crosses).

During this pullback, we are seeing trend breaks, names establishing themselves below the 200 day, and Bearish crosses. This is different than August (and any other pullback we’ve seen since the Bull Market began). This is what I mean when I say the health of the market is currently weaker than during any other pullback we’ve had since the Bull Market began in 10/22.

It’s basically “do or die time” now for bulls. We are currently at key support and the next couple weeks will decide if this pullback was just a shakeout to the 200 day in the indices (like August), or a breakdown and bigger correction. Names like NVDA, MSFT, Semis, Small Caps, etc. do not want to go much lower than this. They are already establishing themselves below the 200 day and giving Bearish crosses, and they must bounce back above soon.

For Semis, this chart is starting to look strikingly familiar to the [ARKK chart in 2022](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/history-repeating?utm_source=publication-search). Big up move, long consolidation period, break down. These are the classic stages of an asset cycle/bubble. Stage 4 would be the decline.

Speaking of classic bubbles, we saw many in speculative names and a good majority have already round tripped (which is [historically what happens when a bubble pops](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/history-repeating?utm_source=publication-search)). Again, we saw this in 2021 months prior to the indices breaking down in 2022 (SPACs and more spec stocks peaked first).
