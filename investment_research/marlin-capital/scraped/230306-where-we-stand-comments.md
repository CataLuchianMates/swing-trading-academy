## David Marlin Q&A (Comments)

**Q:** Great analysis David , as always. What are your thoughts to why is SPX so strong vs rates, regardless of whether this is a bull or bear mkt. Last few years, we were told it was Tina behind the rally in SPX. now that Tina, is not the factor, what is the narrative behind long SPX at these levels?

**David Marlin:** Thank you! My view is that the market has rallied in the face of higher rates because

(1) economic data has generally been stronger than expected so far (specifically the labor market).  Expectations were quite low heading into the year.  This, along with inflation generally surprising to the downside over the past ~6 months, has allowed the soft landing narrative to take hold.  The lagged effects of monetary policy are difficult to predict.  

(2) Positioning.  I have been posting data showing just how offsides asset managers were coming into 2023.  The consensus view among institutional investors was a 1H sell off in the market this year.  The amount of institutional short covering we have seen this year is among the highest levels recorded over the last decade +.  We saw this in the rallies last year, but the data shows that this short squeeze has been even bigger.

---

**Q:** Thanks for the analysis.  I feel like this "next leg down" thesis keeps being put off as the market is bolstered by hope of a "soft landing" on the legs of more resilient than expected consumer and job markets.  Q4 2022 earnings weren't particularly good, but not a disaster.  Maybe the bottom drops out in Q1 2023 earnings?  If we are headed for new lows, what do you think is/are the catalyst(s) and when?  I am positioned very conservatively waiting for the things you detailed above to come to fruition, but am nervous with FOMO the new bull market at the same time.

**David Marlin:** Thank you.  I think there are many potential catalysts for the next wave down, but rather than guessing, I will list the most likely ones that I foresee.  

1. Earnings misses and warnings from major companies 
2. Inflation re-accelerating to the upside.  We saw some of this in January but would likely need to be multiple months to really spook the market. 
3. The Fed raising more than expected and rate cuts being priced out of 2023
4. Economic data beginning to greatly deteriorate, putting to bed the idea of a soft landing
