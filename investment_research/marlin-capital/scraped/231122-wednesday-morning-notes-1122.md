# Wednesday Morning Notes 11.22

**Morning Market Commentary**

US stocks traded slightly lower on Tuesday with the S&P 500 finishing -0.2% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xOXz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F31bc33fa-68cd-4b41-8fdf-6ab23ea32580_1269x951.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xOXz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F31bc33fa-68cd-4b41-8fdf-6ab23ea32580_1269x951.jpeg)

Mega Caps weighed on the tape (combination of AMZN -1.5% on the back of insider selling headlines + MSFT -116bps on Altman drama + NVDA -85bps in high anticipation of earnings).

We also saw disappointing top-line momentum on consumer/retail prints (LOW -3%, KSS -8.6%, and BBY -68bps all lowered their respective top-line guides).

Price action reflected some reversion of Monday’s price action with lower quality themes selling off: Most Short -3%, Non-Profitable Tech -2.9%, China ADRs -2.7%.

Yesterday’s trading volumes were very light (as expected during this holiday shortened week).  28.96M options traded yesterday, the 3rd lowest daily options trading volume YTD (42.63m average).

The FOMC Minutes (released yesterday) were a non-event, as expected.  We got a repeat of the “proceeding carefully” language that Powell used in the press conference when talking about the path of policy in the near-term and confirmed that all participants were on board with that.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PMc1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F49c4a5d3-82b9-4030-a758-c07a6796bd27_760x641.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PMc1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F49c4a5d3-82b9-4030-a758-c07a6796bd27_760x641.png)

NVDA Earnings Results – NVDA beat and raised above consensus (big upside on GMs).

Oct qtr revenues of $18.12B vs cons $16.1B with Datacenter Revs $14.2B (+41% Q/Q) vs cons $12.9B (+25% Q/Q)…

EPS $4.02 vs cons $3.37 – this is a ~19% beat on EPS (vs 30% beat last qtr and 19% beat two qtrs. ago).

Gross Margins 75% vs cons 72.4%...

Inventories $4.78B (vs $4.32B last qtr).

Jan qtr total revs guided ~$20B vs cons $17.9B (up 10% Q/Q) and GMs 75.5% vs cons 72.4%.
