# August Finale

Another low volume, choppy session for US stocks today as investors hold off on big bets ahead of NVDA earnings tomorrow.

On the Macro side, we have GDP on Thursday and the Fed’s preferred inflation gauge, PCE, on Friday morning. This week’s potential catalysts are backloaded, so it’s no surprise to see the market consolidating after Friday’s Jackson Hole fueled rally.

#### **3 trading days left in August.**

It is well documented that September is the worst month of the year for the US stock market. The average gain/loss is -0.94%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nkvy!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcadc4110-4207-4dcd-9e65-f7fa9e660c24_1968x1043.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nkvy!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcadc4110-4207-4dcd-9e65-f7fa9e660c24_1968x1043.jpeg)

As you can see, 8 of the last 15 September’s have finished negative.

This year, it’s all about the Fed. The market is pricing in near certainty of a 25bps cut in September after Powell’s Jackson Hole Speech.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!H_PM!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F72f03658-4842-453a-86e2-dc9c87193fa6_1060x180.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!H_PM!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F72f03658-4842-453a-86e2-dc9c87193fa6_1060x180.jpeg)

**Historically, rate cuts are very favorable for equities, particularly one like this.**

Here’s what the data says 👇👇👇

There have been 9 instances since 1990 where the Fed cut rates with the SPX at/near ATHs (within ~1%). The market has been higher a year later in every instance (7 so far, still pending the 2024 cuts).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FxN6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F91098858-56e5-4524-b617-9e6accadff29_680x324.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FxN6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F91098858-56e5-4524-b617-9e6accadff29_680x324.jpeg)

When the Fed resumes cutting after a 6+ month pause, it is a very favorable backdrop for equities (assuming no recession).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SEIx!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb8e50f1c-6be4-4afd-9c03-cc3226c10107_1215x847.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SEIx!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb8e50f1c-6be4-4afd-9c03-cc3226c10107_1215x847.jpeg)

This time around, the Fed is set to resume cutting into solid economic growth. When coupled with the AI CapEx surge, this is a very bullish backdrop for equities, in my view.

Following Powell’s speech on Friday, we saw a rare 90/90 day for the market. This means >90% of NYSE stocks rose and >90% of trading volume was higher. Historically, these 90/90 days have been bullish in the past with the SPX higher 1 year later ~92% of the time.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tKMo!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe09b049a-a3ea-4dc0-8c9d-74bd61144d69_975x647.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tKMo!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe09b049a-a3ea-4dc0-8c9d-74bd61144d69_975x647.png)

High participation + significant volume supporting those advances = Very bullish signal

In [“Small Cap September”,](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/small-cap-september) I talked about the Russell 2000 setting up for a potential big picture breakout.

I like the consolidation action so far this week in the IWM, and I continue to believe we are on the verge of a BIG breakout here.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GiK3!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F206ec4c3-f3f8-4240-a62d-698445475781_1132x652.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GiK3!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F206ec4c3-f3f8-4240-a62d-698445475781_1132x652.png)

Again, all eyes are on NVDA tomorrow and this is a potential market moving event. But as we head into the dreaded month of September, I see too many bullish signals right now (strong breadth, R2K breakout, Fed cutting, etc.) for me to think 2025 will follow the traditional seasonal lull.

I remain bullish US equities here, even as we head into \*gasp\* September.
