# My Thoughts Post-Fed Meeting

In its most anticipated meeting in ages, the Federal Reserve approved its first interest rate hike in more than three years.  The Fed approved a 0.25 percentage point rate hike, the first increase since December 2018.  Officials indicated an aggressive path ahead, penciling in rate hikes at each of the 6 remaining meetings this year, pointing to a consensus funds rate of 1.9% by the end of 2022.  The committee has forecasted a rate of 2.8% by the end of 2023/24.

At his post-meeting news conference, Fed Chairman Jerome Powell hinted that the balance sheet reduction could start in May, but there has been no official decision yet on the matter.  Powell went on to say “The committee is determined to take the measures necessary to restore price stability. The US economy is very strong and well-positioned to handle tighter monetary policy.”

This certainly seems hawkish, but the market ripped higher today.  So, was this all priced in?  Overall, I believe the market did an effective job of pricing in the Fed’s plans prior to today’s meeting.  However, the actual effects of the policy are a different story, and I believe it will continue to be a difficult environment for stocks throughout 2022.  Historically, the combination of high inflation and aggressive tightening is the worst one possible for stocks.

One key to the market’s rally today may have been the fact that Fed Chair Powell downplayed the risk of a recession within the next year or so.  The problem is, the fixed income market does not agree with Powell’s stance here.  After the Fed’s press release today, the 5/10 year yield curve is now inverted.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QqX2!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F27353970-88ca-4bca-8f99-865fbde0f5a7_889x959.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QqX2!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F27353970-88ca-4bca-8f99-865fbde0f5a7_889x959.png)

It is likely the 2/10 year yield curve will also invert at some point this year, as it continues to flatten.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ujzv!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7538a846-3ff9-4b6a-8097-04f8b33f0dd7_1449x647.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ujzv!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7538a846-3ff9-4b6a-8097-04f8b33f0dd7_1449x647.png)

Additionally, the Fed cut its forecast on GDP from 4% to 2.8%, reflecting an expectation of decelerating economic growth.  The University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment is the lowest its been in over 10 years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9paV!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2a9ffcbb-ccf8-4725-9012-3d250326b026_625x483.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9paV!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2a9ffcbb-ccf8-4725-9012-3d250326b026_625x483.png)

These are all leading recessionary indicators that are beginning to flash.  I am inclined to believe the data in this case, as the Fed (by nature) tends to be more reactionary than predictive, which is part of the reason they were unable to get ahead of inflation before it spiraled out of control.

Then, there is also the question of how Quantitative Tightening will affect stocks.  Based on the limited history we have to go off of, [I concluded that it causes more volatility](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-thoughts-post-fed-meeting-de7?s=w).  In the last QT cycle (‘17-19), the market experienced 3 corrections of 10% or more from the time QT began to the time it finished.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9JK9!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd8c95a5a-05b5-4b61-952d-88f078a0acd5_579x415.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9JK9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd8c95a5a-05b5-4b61-952d-88f078a0acd5_579x415.png)

Increased volatility is what one would expect - as liquidity is being extracted from the market, it causes the magnitude of price moves to be amplified.  If history is any indication, the 2H of 2022 is likely to be a bumpy one for stocks as QT is implemented and a series of rate hikes starts to take its toll on the economy.

So, where do we go from here?  There are a few scenarios I could see playing out here.  One, is an immediate move lower over the coming weeks before putting in a capitulation bottom.

The second, is a relief rally that may already be underway.  In the Dot Com Bust, the Nasdaq had multiple rallies that lasted 4+ weeks before the ultimate bottom was put in.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!i__T!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F36efe03c-5871-4b91-8ecf-9ff2a12774e1_587x398.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!i__T!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F36efe03c-5871-4b91-8ecf-9ff2a12774e1_587x398.png)

To this point, we have not gotten these longer duration rallies that many other Bear Markets experienced.  In this scenario, I believe the counter-trend rally would likely stall sometime before May, before the indices go on to break these temporary lows.

There is a third scenario, of course, where the market has already bottomed.  Without a pivot from Powell, I do not view this scenario as likely at all.  If the Fed stays the course with 7 rate hikes in 2022, QT in 2H, and 3 additional rate hikes in 2023, the odds of the market having already bottomed are close to 0.  Optimists will say that Powell has pivoted in the past.  It’s true - he did in 2018, which put a bottom in the market.  But, this was after a tightening cycle that included 9 rate hikes and 2% inflation.  Now, we have 8% inflation and a much stronger economy (according to Fed Chair Powell).

I believe that he Fed is motivated to truly hike 6 more times this year in order to tame inflation.  The only way I see the Fed being knocked off this path, is if the committee suddenly fears that more tightening will cause a recession.  For the market, it is pick your poison.  Neither scenario is a positive one for stocks, but such is the nature of the predicament we are in.

My Strategy: In my Active Portfolio, I will continue to listen to what the price action tells me about which scenario may come next.  First up for ARKK (my proxy for growth stocks), on a technical basis, is a test of the 21 EMA.  All Bear Market rallies since November have been rejected at this level, as the ETF has failed to close above this line in 4 months.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7-SN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F193d9a10-205d-4fd9-8e75-4afee5bf3cc1_1925x1021.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7-SN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F193d9a10-205d-4fd9-8e75-4afee5bf3cc1_1925x1021.png)

A close above would finally signal a short term trend change, which would open the door for a larger and longer duration rally.  Another rejection would provide enticing short opportunities in ARKK and related names .

As for my IRA, my plan is to continue to be patient with my cash position (37% Cash).  Big picture, I believe there is a high risk of a recession over the next 2 years, and I believe the economy is already beginning to deteriorate.  If this is the case, better opportunities will surely arise, and I have no problem waiting patiently for them.
