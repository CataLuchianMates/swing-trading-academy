# The Week Ahead

The S&P 500 rose 2.3% last week, good for the single best week of 2024, led by a dovish Fed and a continued broadening of the AI narrative.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!laiN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fafb6f87c-bf2c-40b2-bd50-2a5cd62c5aa2_602x1039.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!laiN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fafb6f87c-bf2c-40b2-bd50-2a5cd62c5aa2_602x1039.jpeg)

**My key takeaway from last week’s FOMC meeting:**  Despite a forecast that runs a bit “hotter-for-longer”, the Fed is sticking to what they penciled down in December: 3 cuts in ’24 (and a moderation of QT).  The Fed was clearly not influenced by the January/February inflation data (which surprised to the upside), which arguably lowers the bar for a rate cut come June.

Moreover, the market has not been disrupted by the prospect of a slower cutting cycle than initially expected.  Consider this – the S&P 500 is now 450 pts higher than it was when the market discounted nearly 7 rate cuts back in January.

With the Fed out of the way for now, the technical uptrend remains strong.  The S&P 500 is showing no signs of slowing down on the weekly and daily charts.
