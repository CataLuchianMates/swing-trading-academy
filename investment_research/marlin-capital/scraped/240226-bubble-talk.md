# Bubble Talk

We are seeing lots of comparisons being made between now and the late 90s Dot Com Bubble.  The S&P 500 made new all-time highs this week on the backs of NVDA earnings.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HaYp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffa8dc999-00a8-4ae5-a117-ab02cb21196f_967x723.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HaYp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffa8dc999-00a8-4ae5-a117-ab02cb21196f_967x723.jpeg)

As the push and pull between bulls (who point to the AI Revolution) and bears (who are prone to the “Bubble Talk”) continue, it remains to be seen just how this part of history will end.

It was all about NVDA last week after the company impressively beat an elevated bar and posted standout data center numbers (+27% QoQ & 409% YoY).  While the stock certainly benefitted from a relatively attractive tactical setup (-9% in 4 days prior to EPS), the company earned its flowers as Nvidia beat revenue by $2B+ on a 77% gross margin. For NVDA, those gross margins are among the top 3 highest in the semi space.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3Ncr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff0384520-5cdb-40cf-b176-28a5b010803d_592x487.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3Ncr!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff0384520-5cdb-40cf-b176-28a5b010803d_592x487.jpeg)

Speaking of standout data center numbers, consider this for context.  **From 2017-2021, Nvidia was consistently doing $4B-$10B in annual data center revenues.**  **In 2024 and 2025, the market is expecting $100B+ in data center revs**.  On that note, Nvidia’s share of the Data Center market has grown from 15% five years ago to 75% today.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jPDn!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F31be297a-cd47-4f84-9b1b-634b131f8975_604x484.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jPDn!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F31be297a-cd47-4f84-9b1b-634b131f8975_604x484.jpeg)

This helped the stock add a record ~$277B of market cap on Thursday, the largest 1 day gain in market cap for any company ever.  The company briefly eclipsed $2T in market cap on Friday, becoming the fastest company to ever do so (by far). For context, NVDA went from $1T to $2T market cap in just 4 months, a feat that took both AAPL and MSFT 2+ years to achieve.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ehNK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff30999af-b86e-4e83-8bb1-1ebbca948469_1284x1563.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ehNK!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff30999af-b86e-4e83-8bb1-1ebbca948469_1284x1563.jpeg)

Management noted that accelerated computing and **generative AI have hit a tipping point and the demand is surging worldwide** across companies, industries, and nations.

Incredibly, the company is cheaper than it was for much of 2023 (33x Fwd PE vs ~50x a year ago).  **More incredibly, the Fwd PE (33x) is the same (32x) as it was in October 2022, when the S&P 500 bottomed and NVDA was valued at $280B in market cap.**  On Friday, the stock briefly eclipsed $2T in market cap, a 7x gain from those October 2022 lows.  Yet, the stock is not any more expensive on a forward Price to Earnings multiple.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6klI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcb084c7a-27d7-4618-af3f-8b831e511e0d_1141x728.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6klI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcb084c7a-27d7-4618-af3f-8b831e511e0d_1141x728.jpeg)

This is because of the incredible earnings growth Nvidia has exhibited over the last 2 years, and will exhibit (according to consensus projections) over the next 12 months.  In Q1, half of the Magnificent 7’s earnings growth can be attributed to NVDA.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YBl9!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F73a39ee2-cae1-44d6-ad7f-0ef68f000b21_1284x709.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YBl9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F73a39ee2-cae1-44d6-ad7f-0ef68f000b21_1284x709.jpeg)

Understandably, this has helped drive gains across the semiconductor space as the SMH (Semi Index) extended its YTD gain to +20% last week while the Russell 2000 is still negative in 2024.

The main reason for the Mega Caps continued outperformance over Small Caps is once again earnings growth.  On a Price-to-Earnings-to-Growth (PEG) Ratio, Magnificent 7 valuations are still significantly lower than the rest of the S&P 500.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!awGa!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F949340d7-a47c-4491-a6c0-d6e6892da820_873x613.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!awGa!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F949340d7-a47c-4491-a6c0-d6e6892da820_873x613.jpeg)

This is because the earnings growth projections are much higher for the Magnificent 7, with a projected +25% YoY EPS growth in 2025 versus +8% for the remaining 493.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yqVt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F27d37c17-2fd6-43f5-9298-903f04c2bfef_902x609.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yqVt!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F27d37c17-2fd6-43f5-9298-903f04c2bfef_902x609.jpeg)

Besides “Bubble Talk” and concentrated positioning in Mega Caps, bears will argue multiples are high (SPX trading at ~21x), and the Fed might not be the tailwind many thought they would be as the numbers of projected rate cuts this year have halved (3 vs 6).  Moreover, they can point to Palo Alto Networks (PANW) cutting their revenue guidance as a sign that some tech companies are reigning in their spending (how much of this is going towards AI budgets?).

Of course, bulls will point to this time being different as these companies are earnings real revenues, valuations are not in Bubble territory, and AI seems to have more tangible use cases which can be implemented in existing businesses.  As I pointed out [last week](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-moment-of-truth), the 5 largest stocks traded at a much higher multiple in 2000 (43x) than they do now (27x).  Additionally, we’ve already had a major bust (bubble burst) in unprofitable companies (see ARKK, Unprofitable Tech, SPACs, etc.). This is the Bubble I first wrote about in late 2021 ([“We’re Not in Kansas Anymore”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/were-not-in-kansas-anymore)).  The drastic rise in interest rates caused the Covid Bubble to burst, and the peak to trough drawdown exceeded 80% in ARKK/Unprofitable Tech/SPACs.

The question now, is “Are we in an AI Bubble?” As I mentioned above, the more speculative stocks/ARKK/Unprofitable Tech remain deeply entrenched in Bear Markets.  The Russell 2000 index of Small Caps is still down 27% from its recent peak.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rH4L!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa8997583-7b32-4278-8baa-34dde2b08d7d_1284x1000.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rH4L!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa8997583-7b32-4278-8baa-34dde2b08d7d_1284x1000.jpeg)

In my view, this is because (1) interest rates are high and these companies are much more sensitive to rates, and (2) the AI Boom/Revolution is making the largest companies in the world even more dominant.  In [June 2023](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-chase-is-on), I wrote this:

> “In my view, the AI theme is making the largest companies in the world even more dominant.  The barriers to entry in the AI sector are enormous when factoring in IP, capital intensity, and established industry relationships.  The investments that companies will need to make to be successful in AI are significant, as new entrants will be competing against the likes of Trillion Dollar tech behemoths like Microsoft, Google, Nvidia, etc.  There are only a handful of companies in the world that can afford such an investment.
>
> In this way, it is reasonably similar to the Cloud, where Google, Microsoft, and Amazon have become the 3 most dominant players because they have the capital to afford competing in such a capital intensive industry.“

For my favorite AI stocks (and ways to play the AI boom) outside of the obvious (Mag 7), be sure to read my “[Top 10 Stocks to Watch For 2024](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/top-10-stocks-to-watch-for-2024)” List.

So, to summarize my view on the question “Are we in an AI Bubble right now?”

My big picture sense here is that earnings and revenue growth have driven more durable moves and that the resilience of the economy suggest a healthier foundation and backdrop. Additionally, the current market leaders (Mag 7) are as strong as they’ve ever been while leveraging their scale and elite corporate management teams to execute effectively.
