# Wednesday Morning Notes 8.23

Technical Update

The S&P 500 finished -0.28% on Tuesday following a bounce to kick off the week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Uk0B!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa991942d-ca25-4d60-8f8b-f01cd23c1fe2_964x717.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Uk0B!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa991942d-ca25-4d60-8f8b-f01cd23c1fe2_964x717.jpeg)

This was an overall bearish looking candle as the index was rejected at the $4415-$4420 resistance level I mentioned in yesterday’s morning notes. The high of the day was $4418 before the index closed back below the key $4400 level (60 EMA).

The technical setup in the SPX remains bearish until we can break and close above the 60 EMA.

Some of the top names on my earnings watchlist continue to act strong. Below are the names I will be most interested in if the indices show signs of bottoming out.

CLS,

COUR,

VRT,

RELY,

ROVR,

UPWK

My verdict - I’m still playing defense here until we get more bullish confirmation from the indices. If the SPX begins to break down, it is unlikely for any of my watchlist setups to work.

NVDA earnings tomorrow is a big potential event for the market. I’ll remain in defense mode until we see how it shakes out.
