# Thursday Morning Notes 1.2

**Daily Market Commentary:**

First trading day of 2025.

The market ended 2024 with a selloff as the SPX finished New Year’s Eve -0.43% and QQQ -0.85%.

The month of December saw a significant decrease in market breadth. ~70% of the trading sessions in December saw more SPX named declining vs advancing (1m avg SPXAD -100). Negative breadth of this magnitude (1m avg SPXAD -100) suggests the SPX should be down -10-15%. And yet, we are only -3.6% off ATHs in the S&P 500.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!e6ll!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5f80aae9-631b-4ec7-9f70-0c6fde0b647f_1272x649.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!e6ll!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5f80aae9-631b-4ec7-9f70-0c6fde0b647f_1272x649.jpeg)

Another way of looking at the negative breadth is comparing the return of the SPX equal weight vs the SPX market cap weight index. This is the (by far) the largest underperformance of stocks relative to the stock market in 2024.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uK1d!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F59849ff3-9029-4efb-a713-03decc919f61_1218x658.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uK1d!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F59849ff3-9029-4efb-a713-03decc919f61_1218x658.jpeg)

These are typically signs of an unhealthy market. But, with pension rebalancing and end of year shenanigans/window dressing (funds trying to lock in performance/sell losses/front run potential January tax selling), any bit of selling (triggered by the Dec FOMC meeting) can be exacerbated in a low volume/liquidity environment. This is why I said on NYE that I would not read too much into this current selloff.

We are set for a gap up this morning to kick off 2025. I head into 2025 with no current active portfolio positions and no plans to add any today. I am not interested in buying into today’s gap up off support. We often will need to see a re-test of support during the regular trading session. I will want to see how this shakes out before getting involved.
