# Thursday Morning Notes 9.21

Fed Recap & Technical Update

Yesterday’s Fed decision went as expected, with the committee leaving the Federal Funds rate unchanged.  The 2024 picture laid out was a bit more hawkish than expected with the dot plots showing only 50 bps of cuts in 2024.  US 10 YR yields soared to new cycle highs at 4.39%, the highest since 2007.

During the press conference, Fed Chair Powell was very much more hawkish than anticipated.  Unlike recent past meetings, Powell did not embrace “immaculate disinflation” or a “soft landing”.  At one point, Powell made a point to say that a soft landing is not the FOMC’s baseline expectation.  He further went on to say that he expects labor market weakness ahead and that the full effects of rate hikes are not being seen yet.

This is a wake up call for a market that has been pricing in a soft landing for about a year now, and certainly all of 2023.  In recent past meetings, Powell embraced the possibility of a soft landing, as everything has gone better than expected so far.  Yesterday, we saw him change his tune and pour water over the soft landing fire.

The indices sold off accordingly, with the S&P 500 closing the day at lows (-0.94%).  This is a decisive close below the 60 EMA and a technical breakdown.

We are set for a continuation gap down this morning in the indices.  Key support levels to watch below include $4350-$4360, $4330, and $4200.

Big picture, the SPX appears to have made a lower high with a technical breakdown below key support yesterday.  A move to the 200 day moving average near $4200 is looking more likely based off the current picture.  I also expect volatility to remain elevated with quick snapback rallies and bounces, which would be a better time to look for short trades.

COUR – as of now, this looks like it will open slightly below my stop price ($17.84) and trigger my sell on the remaining portion for around a 5% gain on the rest.

CLS – I will be selling this long at the open.  With the market breakdown, I no longer see a favorable risk/reward setup for longs right now.
