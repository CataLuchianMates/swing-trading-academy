**The MOVE Index**

I have been talking about signs of credit stress for several weeks now, and the Bond Market is confirming those concerns.

The MOVE Index (think of this as the VIX for the bond market) measures volatility in US Treasury options. When the MOVE Index is spiking, it tells you that the Bond Market is pricing in greater uncertainty and stress. This is not a signal equity investors should simply ignore.

Right now, Bond volatility is pointing significantly lower for equities from here. 👇

**The Valuation Trap**

I am seeing a lot of talk out there right now about valuations getting cheap. And on the surface, it is true. Many stocks have come down significantly from their highs, particularly in the Tech space.

But, in my view, this is one of the most common and dangerous misconceptions in all of investing. Cheap valuations are to be expected during a bear cycle.

During bear cycles, cheap stocks get cheaper. Then they get cheaper some more. During bull cycles, expensive stocks get more expensive. Then they get more expensive. Trends can (and usually do) last much longer than you think.

This is why I have said before, valuations do not matter on the way up, and they do not matter on the way down. This is because stocks do not simply trade at “fair value” indefinitely. Instead, they swing from overvalued to undervalued, depending on where we are in the cycle.

Like a pendulum.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EzVA!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0e7a221f-d418-446c-8149-e11c4370a5bd_2816x1536.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EzVA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0e7a221f-d418-446c-8149-e11c4370a5bd_2816x1536.png)

Howard Marks described this brilliantly in *The Most Important Thing*:

> “The mood swings of the securities markets resemble the movement of a pendulum. Although the midpoint of its arc best describes the location of the pendulum ‘on average,’ it actually spends very little of its time there. Instead, it is almost always swinging toward or away from the extremes of its arc.”

For me, this is one of the most important concepts in all of investing. And it is directly related to what we are seeing right now.

So, where are we on the pendulum right now?

As I have talked about since October (starting with [“Risk Off”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70?utm_source=publication-search) and [“Weakness”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/weakness?utm_source=publication-search)), the price action has been consistent with the end of a bull cycle and the beginning of a bear cycle. The most speculative stocks (IONQ, OKLO, and many others) were the first to peak.

Historically, [the most speculative names peak 9-12 months before the indices do](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/grinch-selloff?utm_source=publication-search). We saw this play out during both the Dot Com Bust and the Covid Bubble Burst.

This time around, the most speculative stocks began peaking in October 2025. And today, the Nasdaq just hit its -10% correction level.

This is why, coming into the year, I said we were in the final innings of this bull cycle. Historically, the indices may have 1-2 more waves up after the spec leaders top (but it’s a much more difficult environment), and this was my expectation for 2026.

But importantly, as I have stressed all year, stocks peak at their own pace during the final innings. That is exactly why we came into 2026 with 34 longs, and are now down to 9. This was always our plan for 2026 (though I will say, I did not expect it so early in Q1).

To be clear, my exit sales are purely technical based. I do not hold stocks that I believe to be in their Stage 4 downtrends, and I never will. Why? Because, per [the Mark Minervini 50-80% rule I have talked so much about](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/cold-as-ice?utm_source=publication-search), you should expect individual stocks to decline 50-80% during their Stage 4 phases. I do not have the stomach to hold through such drawdowns, hoping and praying they might one day recover. So, I exit at the first signs of Stage 4 behavior.

This is exactly what we have done in 2026. We started the year with 34 longs. We are now down to 9.
