# Rebound

Big rebound day for the market yesterday with the S&P 500 finishing +2.51%, NDX +2.63%, RTY +2.76%. We are seeing strong follow through so far today with the indices tacking on another +2%.

Yesterday was good enough for the 3rd best day for the SPX in the last 2 years.

Breadth was super strong: There have been 25,525 trading days since 1928. Yesterday, more stocks in the S&P 500 advanced than on all but 7 of those days (h/t Jason Goepfert).

Today’s follow through rally comes after Trump’s comments yesterday that he plans to be “very nice” to China in any trade talks and that tariffs will come down substantially in any trade deal. Trump also took a softer tone towards Fed Chair Powell, saying that “he has no intention of firing Powell” but would like him to “be a little more active in terms of his idea to lower interest rates”.

We also got our first of the Mag 7 earnings yesterday with TSLA after the bell.

TSLA - Expectations were clearly low for TSLA into this print, especially after the well below consensus delivery numbers they provided on April 2nd. It appears these subpar Q1 numbers do not matter too much to the market with the stock +6% today.

For Q1, EPS of $0.27 missed consensus estimates of $0.43 while EBITDA missed at $2.81B vs $3.12B projected. GAAP gross margins were fairly in line, which was a little better than whispers going in. TSLA punted on the guide for now, saying they will revisit the guide with their Q2 update.

From a numbers standpoint, there is not a ton to like about this TSLA report. With that said, expectations were about as low as could be going in. Moreover, long term TSLA investors know this story is really about FSD (full self driving), Robotaxis, and the Optimus Humanoid robot. The quarter to quarter car sales are very much secondary to these aforementioned growth stories. Big picture, Tesla is not seen as an accurate barometer of the economy as a whole. This is more of a one-off report versus the rest of the Mag 7.

Some other solid earnings that came out after the bell yesterday - PEGA, VRT, APH, MIND
