**Watchlist:**

Rate sensitive high growth tech stocks have been less in favor than late cycle stocks. Unless the CPI comes in soft and/or the Fed pivots more dovish next week, I expect this to continue.

Some of the strongest sectors include homebuilders, building materials, semis, etc. As the saying goes “there is always a bull market somewhere”. This is the market discounting the end of QE and rate hikes. If I am stopped out of my ARKK short position, my focus will be shifting the funds to the leading stocks in the leading industries. Note, that high growth tech is NOT a leading industry in this market and is still in a downtrend. I believe volatility will continue to affect growth stocks as the market continues to discount a shrinking Fed balance sheet and future rates hikes.

In my active portfolio, I prefer to trade the strongest stocks. At this point, this would only include stocks at or near all time highs. The strongest stocks in this market have solid earnings, strong balance sheets, great buyback programs, and are less sensitive to a change in rates. Right now, the market values these characteristics much more than revenue growth at a premium price. We have no reason to fight the tape.
