# Wednesday Morning Notes 2.22

Technical Update

The S&P 500 sold off hard to start the week on Tuesday, finishing the day -2% (it’s worst day of 2023). I mentioned last week several reasons to be cautious from a technical standpoint. Yesterday’s action represents a big breakdown on the daily chart.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_ZOt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F312639ab-e55c-407a-9b40-3064ac38667c_962x718.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_ZOt!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F312639ab-e55c-407a-9b40-3064ac38667c_962x718.jpeg)

Not only did we break and close below the 21 EMA, the index actually closed 2 pts below the 60 EMA and 3 pts below the $4k support level.

We are set for a small gap up today back above $4000. A reversal and break below $4k opens the door for a further decline to the 200 SMA ($3942). This is the next critical support level for the market. If the 200 SMA ($3942) cannot hold as support, we can see $3900, $3850-$3860, and $3800 in a hurry.

I am currently just holding CLH long position with a stop at $130.
