# Party Like Its 1999

Another day, another record high for the S&P 500.

The NDX just notched its 62nd consecutive day above its 20-day MA. The last time this happened was…January 1999 (during the Dot Com Bubble).

Remember the parallels I drew to 1998 as we started to recover from April ‘25 lows?

In [“The Big Picture”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-big-picture), I compared the Q1 ‘25 Tariff Tank Bear Market to the 1998 market correction (a -20% market drawdown during the Dot Com Bubble). This comparison has played out to a T so far.

The “AI Bubble” has a nice ring to it. Marlin Capital readers know my stance on where we’re headed next.

Underneath the hood, we’re seeing a throwback to 2021 with highly shorted meme stocks like GPRO, DNUT, KSS, etc. exploding. Goldman’s most short basket has been 9x more volatile than the SPX over the last 5 sessions.

It’s early, but Q2 earnings are off to a good start. Only 124 companies have reported earnings so far this season, but the current EPS beat rate stands at 85% (normal is 66%) and the sales beat rate is 77% (normal is 63%). The average stock has gained +0.72% on its earnings reaction day.

GOOGL numbers were solid with a top and bottom line beat. 2025 CapEx guide hiked from $75B to $85B which is a big positive for Tech. Search was better than feared (Google search is not dead yet) with +12% growth (vs whispers of 10%), YouTube +13% (whispers 10-11%), and Cloud +32% (Q1 +28%, whisper 25-30%). Overall, a very solid report for Google.

TSLA - Q2 numbers were undeniably bad but I’m not going to spend too much time on this report. Most investors are aware that TSLA does not trade off car sales at this point. It’s all about the future - Robotaxis, Optimus Humanoids, and AI.
