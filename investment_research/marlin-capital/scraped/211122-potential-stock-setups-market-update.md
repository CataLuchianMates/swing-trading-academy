# Potential Stock Setups & Market Update

Below is a list of potential stock setups and a recap of the broad market. I will start by recapping the action in the general market.

The QQQ has broken out to new highs and remains strong.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vfCG!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1ed95c50-0dc1-42d6-8c85-6db6244d86d7_951x727.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vfCG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1ed95c50-0dc1-42d6-8c85-6db6244d86d7_951x727.png)

The action underneath the surface is what is concerning.  The indices continue to be propped up by several names (Big tech, etc.) and the majority of stocks in the market continue to struggle.

The percentage of stocks above their 10D MA is down to its lowest level since the recent SPX low on October 4th.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!U_75!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0f4d8f0-f574-4a14-892e-f93019ee0c9e_618x409.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!U_75!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0f4d8f0-f574-4a14-892e-f93019ee0c9e_618x409.png)

Readings of the 10D & 50D MA below 20% and 40%, respectively, suggest an oversold market.  We are not there yet, but this speaks to the weakness underneath the surface.  When technicians use the term “breadth”, this is what they are referring to.

Let’s take a look at ARKK, a good proxy for growth stocks:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ggJt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb46b74d3-74dd-4ea2-9750-03c53f46787c_961x729.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ggJt!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb46b74d3-74dd-4ea2-9750-03c53f46787c_961x729.png)

This is an ugly chart that looks like it will head lower.  ROKU, PINS, ZM, Z, TDOC, PTON, DKNG, and RDFN are all down over 50% from their highs.

In a truly healthy market, you expect to see broad based participation.  That is not what we are seeing at the moment.

Weak breadth does not necessarily mean that we will have a broad market correction, but it is reason enough for me to be cautious heading into this week.  When I’m cautious, I want to focus on managing my existing positions and not adding any new exposure until I see improving technicals in the market.

Thanksgiving week tends to be positive for the market, but this traditionally comes on low volume.  With low volume and not a lot of action, it is important for traders not to “overtrade” this week.  Low volume stock movements tend to be choppy and lacking a clear direction.  This is another reason I will remain cautious in putting on more exposure in my Active Portfolio this week.

Friday’s daily candle for GOOGL is a lot like PFE.  Both stocks reached ATHs in the morning and faded in the afternoon.  Again, this indicates to me that more time may be needed.  The weekly chart for GOOGL still looks terrific, with 3 very tight weeks right at all-time highs.  This is another Megacap stock that I want to continue to hold as long as the market is uptrending.  As a general rule for my active portfolio, I exit a stock on a break of the 21 EMA.  This rule should allow you to stay in stocks in uptrends, and avoid the most pain during downtrends.

As mentioned above, I want to avoid “overtrading” this week and putting on unnecessary exposure.  For one, the market breadth is deteriorating and less and less setups are emerging.  The setups I am most bullish on continue to be Megacap tech stocks.  For the most part, this will continue to be my focus until I see setups emerging elsewhere.

---

My general feeling is that most of the stocks on my Watchlist would look better with a week of rest.  The market has had a big move since the Oct. 4th low, and some consolidation or a low volume pullback would be healthy.  The strongest stocks in the market look extended and in need of consolidation, while the weakest stocks are screaming “Avoid”.

One of the key aspects of a successful trader is knowing when to be aggressive and knowing when to be defensive.  At the beginning of this week, I am not seeing any reason to be aggressive.  I am seeing far more reasons to remain cautious.  The limited number of stocks that have shown strength are extended and in need of a Time or Price correction.

With that said, I will manage my 3 existing positions and keep an eye on the Watchlist.  With respect to my Long Term portfolio, I will continue to be patient and wait for a larger market correction that I believe will occur in early 2022.
