Marlin Capital Strategy:

We had a wild weekend of headlines (SWIFT, nuclear war threats, etc.) after the Russian invasion of Ukraine. As I mentioned last week, volatility and wild swings are here to stay. Needless to say, I have no expertise in predicting what an unstable dictator may or may not do in the future. Anyone who says otherwise is lying, in my opinion. No one truly knows whether this will escalate, by how much, or how it will end.

In times like these, it is helpful to remember that cash is a position. In my opinion, stocks (in general) are simply not cheap enough here, with the Macro & geopolitical backdrops, to do any heavy buying for the long term. In my view, the amount of uncertainty we currently face is not fully priced into stocks. At these valuations, there are not a ton of favorable R/R opportunities. This is why I will continue to be patient at this juncture, both as a trader and as an investor.
