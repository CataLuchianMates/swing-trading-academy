# Thursday Morning Notes 5.29

US stocks are holding onto early gains so far today (well off the morning highs) with the big news that the Court of International Trade blocked Trumps tariffs imposed under the International Emergence Powers Act (IEEPA).

Goldman notes that the Trump administration can impose an across the board tariff and country specific tariffs under other legal authorities (ex. Sec. 122 and Sec. 301). This ruling represents a setback for the administration’s tariff plans and increases uncertainty, **but is unlikely to change the final outcome for most US trading partners.**

* They could cxl replace 10% across the board with 15% under sec 122
* They could launch an investigation under rule 301 against trading partners
* Launch up to 50% tariffs on unfair import rules under rule 338 (act of 1930)

The bottom line is that the White House is likely to reinstate tariffs following the appeal or through other tariff authorities. Overall, the consensus from my morning research is that the Trump administration will find other ways to impose tariffs, and this is definitely not the end of the story.

The market (perhaps surprisingly) gapped up over >1% on this news, but the indices have already given up a good portion of those gains in the first hour of trade. From my perspective, this news is not very impactful, so it is not surprising to see the morning gap up faded.

With that said, stocks have been in rally mode and were setting up for higher prices regardless of any news flow (in my view). I remain bullish here overall, but am not seeing good r/r opportunities to buy any new positions today after the gap up and early fade.
