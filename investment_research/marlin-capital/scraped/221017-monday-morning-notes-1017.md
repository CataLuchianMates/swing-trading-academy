Technical Analysis

The wild market swings continued on Friday, with the indices giving up a large portion of Thursday’s rally.

The QQQ closed the week near lows, but we are set for a sizable gap up this morning (over 1.5%). Such wild volatility makes it difficult to hold any type of trades (long or short) overnight. The current environment remains best for day trading and intraday/short term trading. With the VIX elevated above 30, it is wise for any trader to be using smaller position sizing to account for higher risk.

MS Mike Wilson:

U.S. stock market bear MIKE WILSON of Morgan Stanley said this morning US stocks could rally in the short term as the 200-week moving average provides a "serious floor of support" until companies flag an earnings capitulation, or a recession arrives. A team led by Michael Wilson say inflation has likely peaked and "could fall rapidly next year." Note that history points to the next 3-4 months as the window to fully discount the earnings recession. Expect major earnings cuts in 4Q rather than 3Q and the strategists see the S&P 500 rising to as high as 4,150 points in this rally -- suggesting an upside of 16% from its latest close. However, if the index falls below the 200-week moving average, there could be no rally and stocks could decline straight to 3,400 or lower

Darda Comments at MKM

Darda comments @ MKM - What we have is an ongoing multiple compression prompted by higher market interest rates so it is no surprise that we got new lows in the equity markets as we got a peak high in Fed funds policy rate expectations and a new high in the 10-year. For the market to turn we will need to see a peak in the Fed funds rate and a concomitant easing in the 10-year Treasury yield. This morning, the 10-year is lower and futures are up. This is different from the last cycle which came with falling Treasury yields due to growth scares. Historically, if you look at market drawdowns due to recessions they fall 22%-30% so at just over 25%, you can make an argument that the bulk of the fall is behind US but it would be unprecedented for the market to bottom in front of the actual recession. That said, in the short term we may be setting up for a rally here. We are bullish on treasuries here and much more cautious on the equity markets still. We like health care and low valuation tech and would use a barbell strategy to go long materials and financials and short discretionary and energy on an equity risk premium basis as the Fed continues to focus on lagging indicators.
