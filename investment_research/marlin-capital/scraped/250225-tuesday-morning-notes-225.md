# Tuesday Morning Notes 2.25

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Very bearish price action to kick off the week yesterday with momentum/retail favorites like PLTR, HIMS, etc. continuing to get clobbered. While the recent declines have felt very “unwindy”, we have yet to see any type of major outflows overall. As I talked about over the weekend, institutional investors have been sellers while retail investors have poured record amount of money into stocks over the last month. Overall, equity positioning across all cohorts remains quite elevated here (86th percentile versus history).

With the recent price action in the market, I keep coming back to the late 2021-early 2022 comparison because it feels very similar to me. Growth and crowded names are all getting hammered in tandem while the indices are barely off ATHs. The charts in some of these names look like classic blowoff/climax tops after parabolic upside moves (HIMS, PLTR, OKLO, etc.). In no way are these normal looking pullbacks to me. Again, very similar looking action to 2021-22.

While the charts and price action are reminiscent, the key difference to me is once again the Macro and fundamental picture. Stemming from the Fed’s extreme tightening policy shift in 2022, we did indeed see an earnings recession (-14% in Mag 7 and -4% in the S&P 493).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VOV5!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feb174649-ca48-4896-9703-c2d588fd4e65_542x636.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VOV5!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feb174649-ca48-4896-9703-c2d588fd4e65_542x636.jpeg)

Many have theorized (and I would agree) that Nvidia and the release of ChatGPT (as well as the Fed’s shift towards easier policy after the regional banking crisis) saved the US from a technical recession (although, by some classic definitions, we did actually see a recession). The point is, we are not really in a similar situation now to 2022 from a Macro and fundamental standpoint. The Fed is on pause, the next move will be a cut, and earnings are still expected to continue to grow at a solid pace this year.

However, you simply can’t ignore the price action, and this is very ugly. One of the ways people get hurt the most in these types of sell offs is buying the dip too early in select names because they are already well off their highs (i.e., a name that -30% from ATHs). For the S&P 500, we are only about -3% off ATHs. If the SPX is to see a -10%+ correction, the selloffs in names that have already been hammered will actually accelerate to the downside.

A -10%+ correction is my base case for the SPX if NVDA is unable to shift the tide with a blowout earnings tomorrow. In that scenario, I would not expect any stocks to be spared and you should expect selling in most all names to accelerate. At the index level, I am still not seeing a Bear Market (-20%+ downside) on the horizon this year. We would need to see economic data and earnings start to deteriorate in a meaningful way for that to play out.
