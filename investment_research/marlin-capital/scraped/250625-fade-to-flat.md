But overall, the action was quite mixed today with Small Caps finishing negative (-1.2%) and breadth weak.

I am reminded by the 2018-19 analog I called out a couple weeks ago in [“Mixed Action”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/mixed-action). The 2018-19 market rebound is the closest historical correlation to our current 2025 market rebound.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!a8g9!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa0a26eae-30dd-49c3-9224-36288d348466_1019x652.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!a8g9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa0a26eae-30dd-49c3-9224-36288d348466_1019x652.png)

In [“Mixed Action”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/mixed-action), I wrote:

> Looking closer at the 2018 non-recessionary Bear Market and recovery, **it was not until stocks officially reached ATHs that we saw our first real pullback in the market.**

In the 2018-2019 market recovery, the S&P 500 very briefly recovered to new ATHs following a -20% market correction/Bear Market. Almost immediately after hitting new highs, we had an ensuing -7.6% pullback.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lsQ0!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffabac82e-8f85-4575-8934-0f58a122c2fc_1110x651.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lsQ0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffabac82e-8f85-4575-8934-0f58a122c2fc_1110x651.png)

Do we see something similar this time around? Trap new ATH breakout buyers after a big run (>30% since April lows), and shake them out with a mini correction. I’m keeping an open mind to that possibility here.

As I talked about in [“OpEx Chop”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/opex-chop), we do have 1 end of June headwind to deal with in pension selling.

> Goldman Prime models pensions to sell -$20B of US equities before the end of June (90th percentile among all buy and sell estimates in absolute $ value terms of the past 3 yrs).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!z6tg!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6d1699d7-e6d2-4ec8-a66f-8114dde3b5bc_688x434.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!z6tg!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6d1699d7-e6d2-4ec8-a66f-8114dde3b5bc_688x434.png)

But then, we enter July, which is the strongest month of the year for US stocks. 17 of the last 18 July’s have finished positive for the Nasdaq.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!n8Oe!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F764a6e23-a926-4bf8-8ef7-e33daa9dcceb_1404x879.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!n8Oe!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F764a6e23-a926-4bf8-8ef7-e33daa9dcceb_1404x879.png)

Big picture, I remain bullish. Zooming out, monthly and quarterly charts look absolutely fantastic here. I will be covering my top monthly charts in this weekends report.

Smaller picture, the trend remains up, but we are seeing some mixed action. This keeps me a little cautious in the near term.
