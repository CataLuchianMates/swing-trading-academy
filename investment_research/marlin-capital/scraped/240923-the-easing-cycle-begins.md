# The Easing Cycle Begins

Amidst a 3% GDP growth and a stock market already in arm’s length of all-time highs, the Fed delivered a 50bp cut last week.  Stocks rallied initially after the 2 PM statement release, but failed to hold their gains on Wednesday and finished lower.  Despite the dovish 50 bp cut, 2 comments during the press conference were seen as hawkish.

First, Powell emphasized multiple times that the 50bp cut is not an expected pace going forward, but more of a catch-up to the July decision.   Second, Powell was making comments around a higher neutral rate than pre-Covid.

Powell made it clear that the 50bps cut was not a reaction to recessionary risks.  In fact, he mentioned multiples times in the press conference that the US economy is strong and the labor market is cooling, but off overheated levels.

Despite the initial negative reaction to Powell’s hawkish press conference Wednesday, the market settled in and digested the news.  We saw a huge rally on Thursday followed by a flattish session on Friday.  Overall, the S&P 500 rallied +1.5% from Wednesday’s close as the economy remains on solid footing.

Historically, there have been 20 times where the Fed has cut rates with stocks near all-time highs.  The S&P 500 was higher a year later all 20 times.

There were 5 times over the past 40 years when the Fed eased and a recession did not imminently follow.  On average, the S&P 500 rallied +17% in the 12 months after the first cut.

The big judgment call now is whether or not we see a recession.  When the Fed begins the cutting cycle, it all comes down to the landing.  So long as the economy can avoid a recession, returns are overwhelmingly positive from here.

There is no question that the setup is more demanding now with incoming economic data more important than ever.  The consensus call is for a Soft Landing/no recession.  52% of Fund Managers surveyed by BofA believe there will be no recession for the US economy in the next 18 months.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gG-M!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F464fc33c-36fa-49bd-a40c-c551fd4ca581_1050x556.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gG-M!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F464fc33c-36fa-49bd-a40c-c551fd4ca581_1050x556.jpeg)

For now, it’s a Bull Market and the primary trend remains higher.  As I mentioned, the setup now is certainly more challenging as any bad economic data will spook the market and trigger a “Hard Landing” panic trade/flight to safety/defensives (especially with Soft Landing as the consensus view).  With that said, we are in a good position for a continued rally in stocks so long as we can avoid those negative/bad economic data surprises.
