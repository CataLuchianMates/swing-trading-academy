# Leaders Shine Bright

The Nasdaq bounced back by grinding out a small gain last week (+1.1%), finally snapping a 3-week losing streak.

Heading into the week of 2/23, I would characterize myself as cautiously optimistic. Here’s why 👇

Last week, the market leaders really separated themselves.

When you see entire groups and themes start to break out in unison, it makes it that much more likely the breakouts will stick.

[On Thursday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/hard-penny), I theorized that this recent 4+ month market pullback might be a Bear Trap, similar to the first major pullbacks during the Dot Com and Covid Bubbles.

If that is the case, and the market does have another wave up here, my mindset is very clear:

**Stick with the leaders.**

Leadership has narrowed significantly from 2025 to 2026, particularly in the AI trade. Many of last year’s biggest winners are in clear Stage 4 downtrends, particularly the speculative/high beta plays. With that said, there are some very clear leadership groups that have emerged during this 4+ month market pullback. These are the themes that I will continue to focus on moving forward.

**Here are the 2 leading themes I see in the market right now, and my favorite ways to play each theme** 👇

#### Memory

Memory has been the hottest theme on Wall Street all year. When you dig into the fundamentals, it’s easy to see why.

Last year, I said Data Centers were the #1 theme to own for this AI Bubble.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Gr5w!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6f69dded-0e59-47bc-9ad4-51861dcbd552_1196x690.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Gr5w!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6f69dded-0e59-47bc-9ad4-51861dcbd552_1196x690.jpeg)

**This year, it’s Memory.**

Memory is the standout theme so far in 2026, both in terms of price action and fundamentals. The Memory market is undergoing an incredible fundamental shift driven by AI, and the stocks are leading as a result (MU, SNDK, WDC, etc.).

For decades, memory was a boring, cyclical commodity business. Prices went up, and prices went down. Due to AI, that cycle is over. What has replaced it is a permanent, structural shift driven by AI. Memory manufacturers are now redirecting their production capacity toward high margin AI memory products, and as a result, it is completely transforming the fundamentals of these companies.

Here is the simple reason why: AI systems are incredibly powerful, but they have a problem. They are limited by data. The chips themselves can crunch data/numbers at mind-boggling speeds, but only if that information can be fed to them fast enough. Memory bandwidth has now become the #1 bottleneck for AI, not compute power. Jensen Huang said it himself at CES 2026.

To put this bottleneck into perspective, **a single next-generation NVIDIA server rack now requires more memory than entire data centers needed just a few years ago.** Because of this, there is an incredible demand for memory, and it is producing a major supply shortage.

The result is a pricing environment unlike anything this industry has ever seen. According to [TrendForce](https://www.trendforce.com/presscenter/news/20260202-12911.html), DRAM prices surged +90-95% in Q1 2026 alone, the largest quarterly increase **ever**. NAND [prices climbed](https://www.trendforce.com/presscenter/news/20260202-12911.html) +55-60% QoQ, another record. Meanwhile, global DRAM and NAND inventory have collapsed to historic lows. Put simply, this is the most extreme supply-demand dislocation the Memory industry has ever seen.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cnbd!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fecdfbaac-8668-4d1a-9f8f-156cb2a6483a_851x586.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cnbd!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fecdfbaac-8668-4d1a-9f8f-156cb2a6483a_851x586.png)

The best part is that this supply shortage does not look like it will be going away anytime soon. Data centers are now projected to consume 70% of all high-end memory produced in 2026 (leaving PCs, smartphones, and consumer electronics with the rest). This is a massive, permanent shift in the Memory market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9ZeR!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8c5242e1-434b-4ea9-9165-8381b563c240_1305x792.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9ZeR!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8c5242e1-434b-4ea9-9165-8381b563c240_1305x792.png)

**Most importantly, the bullish price action in Memory stocks is confirming this MASSIVE shift in fundamentals.**

#### Optical Connectivity

Optical Components has also been one of the hottest themes on Wall Street in 2026. And just like the Memory Supercycle, once you understand what is driving it, the fundamental case becomes impossible to ignore.

The AI buildout has entered a new phase. For the past few years, the conversation was all about chips, GPUs, and compute power. But here in 2026, the bottleneck has shifted. The question is no longer just “How do we get more GPUs?” It’s: “How do we move data between them fast enough?” That is where optical connectivity comes in, and that is why this theme has been so hot.

Traditional copper cables have hit a wall. They physically cannot move data at the speeds AI clusters now require without massive signal loss, overheating, and power consumption so extreme it becomes unworkable. Optical connections (fiber and light-based data transmission instead of electrical signals through copper) solve all of these problems at once. They are faster, more power efficient, and can handle the bandwidth that next-generation AI systems demand.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!a67L!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F44e41aea-244b-43a9-897c-471cd5484778_826x230.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!a67L!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F44e41aea-244b-43a9-897c-471cd5484778_826x230.png)

Hyperscalers are now upgrading their entire data center fabrics to 800G and 1.6T optical speeds, a generational upgrade cycle that is just beginning to ramp. Shipments of 800G optical modules alone are projected to more than double in 2026, while 1.6T modules are expected to grow 4-8x.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qz7S!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd8d4c11b-0fa6-401b-af5c-f170cde38028_743x832.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qz7S!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd8d4c11b-0fa6-401b-af5c-f170cde38028_743x832.png)

What makes this theme so powerful is the combination of explosive demand growth and limited supply (just like Memory). The companies that make optical transceivers, fiber, and the test equipment needed to run these networks are seeing order backlogs most industries could never dream of.

This is why the Optical stock momentum has been so strong in 2026. The market is repricing many of these stocks from legacy tech to critical AI infrastructure. Margins are expanding, guidance beats are accelerating, and Street estimates continue to get revised higher as analysts scramble to play catch-up.

**Just like Memory, when the fundamentals are accelerating, guidance beats are historic, and the technicals confirm the thesis, that is when you get the most powerful and sustained moves in stocks.** This is exactly the setup we have in Optical right now.
