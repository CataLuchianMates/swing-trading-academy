# Friday Morning Notes 3.31

Technical Update

The S&P 500 rallied once again yesterday, closing right at the $4050 resistance level I discussed in yesterday’s morning notes. Next up in the way of key resistance is $4075-$4080.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_5Cz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fecc6dbfe-994e-41df-ac53-41b604797d92_972x728.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_5Cz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fecc6dbfe-994e-41df-ac53-41b604797d92_972x728.jpeg)

If $4075-$4080 resistance breaks, the technicals point to a test of $4200 and YTD highs.

Support wise, the $3990-$4000 levels should hold on any pullback for this setup to remain bullish.

Some are asking how the market can remain so resilient in the aftermath of several large scale bank failures. In my view, the answer lies in chart 4 of BofA’s flow show report below. In March, the Fed’s balance sheet expanded at its fastest pace since the March 2020 Covid Crisis, even slightly besting the Fed’s policy response to Lehman Brother’s failure during the 2008 GFC. Full report and analysis coming this weekend.
