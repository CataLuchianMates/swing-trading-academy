# Not Enough

Marlin Capital Readers,

The QQQ has been stuck in a range for nearly 5 months now. The index just closed 1 point lower than it did on October 8th. Let that sink in. ~5 months of chop, and we’re right back to where we started October.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yF4h!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3997f60c-867b-4583-9855-88867d17ea97_1566x846.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yF4h!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3997f60c-867b-4583-9855-88867d17ea97_1566x846.png)

NVDA reported a record quarter after the bell last night. Big revenue beat. EPS beat. Guidance beat. But it wasn’t enough, and the stock still sold off -5.5% (dragging down the QQQ -1.2% along with it).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0QXJ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8c260ed2-ec3b-435f-b019-4298b5137d5b_1267x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0QXJ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8c260ed2-ec3b-435f-b019-4298b5137d5b_1267x844.png)

That was not the reaction bulls were looking for. And it raises a very important question about what is actually driving this market right now.

Below, I break down the full NVDA print, the Jensen Huang quotes every investor needs to hear, and a critical piece of market psychology (from Howard Marks and a legendary Wall Street trader story) that I believe might explain what is happening in this tape. I am also updating all my trading positions and sharing my big picture views heading into March.

Let’s start with the NVDA numbers 👇

NVDA reported after the bell last night, and the numbers were objectively excellent. Q4 revenue grew +73% YoY to $68.1B (beating consensus by $2.1B, or +3.2%), adjusted EPS hit $1.62 vs. $1.52 expected (+6.6% beat), and Data Center revenue surged to $62.3B, up +75% YoY. Gross margins expanded to 75.2%, still climbing. And to top it all off, Free Cash Flow reached a record $34.9B, up +124% YoY.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jrib!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7a98218f-1385-4488-89d8-2e117f2f8a12_1600x1134.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jrib!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7a98218f-1385-4488-89d8-2e117f2f8a12_1600x1134.png)

Then came the guidance. NVDA guided Q1 FY2027 revenue to $78B, which was +7-10% above even the most optimistic Street estimates heading in. By any objective measure, this was a huge beat and raise quarter across the board.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NG3C!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F066ff0b4-e629-46d6-a3e9-003e7288b74a_885x412.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NG3C!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F066ff0b4-e629-46d6-a3e9-003e7288b74a_885x412.png)

Jensen Huang used the conference call to hammer home his long term AI thesis. Some of his quotes deserve the full treatment here. On the greater AI picture, Jensen said:

> “This is not a one-year cycle. Computing power is becoming the primary competitive advantage for companies, and the demand for accelerated compute is going to be relentless for many years…In this new world of AI, compute is revenues. Without compute, there's no way to generate tokens. Without tokens, there's no way to grow revenues. So, in this new world of AI, compute equals revenues.”

On the “agentic AI inflection point” (think Claude Cowork, Perplexity Computer, Openclaw):

> “The ChatGPT moment of agentic AI has arrived. The agentic AI inflection...it literally happened in the last two, three months. The world is now awakened to the agentic AI inflection. The agents are super smart, they're solving real problems…Claude Code, Claude Cowork, and OpenAI Codex have achieved useful intelligence. Adoption is skyrocketing and tokens are profitable, driving extreme urgency to scale up compute.”

Later in the call, Jensen predicted that Physical AI would be the next major inflection point (following agentic AI):

> “The wave that we're seeing now is the agentic AI inflection, and the next inflection beyond that is physical AI, where we take AI and these agentic systems into the physical applications such as manufacturing, such as robotics. That's a giant opportunity ahead.”

These are powerful statements from the most influential man in AI. And he backed them up with numbers. Networking hit $11B in Q4 alone (up >260% YoY). Sovereign AI surpassed $30B in FY2026, more than tripling. Physical AI already contributed over $6B in revenue. The breadth of Nvidia’s AI ecosystem is staggering. There’s a reason they are the undisputed AI king.

But, despite all of this, the stock still fell -5.5% today.

So, what gives?

There are a few scuff marks worth acknowledging. First off, Nvidia’s RPO (remaining performance obligations) posted its first QoQ decline in roughly 3 years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!X6rJ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6db3ff35-68a5-4951-98ea-2869d65c9b7c_1600x1134.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!X6rJ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6db3ff35-68a5-4951-98ea-2869d65c9b7c_1600x1134.png)

NVDA characterized this decline as demand converting into revenue faster (not a falloff in bookings), but the market may be treating it as a softer signal on forward visibility. When your stock is up 18x over the last 3.5 years, everything must be perfect (and RPO was not).

Second, the DeepSeek narrative continues to linger in the background. We are seeing some investors questioning whether AI models becoming more efficient means less hardware demand over time (Jensen would argue the opposite). There is a growing concern that AI infrastructure spending might be peaking and/or that customers are overextending their balance sheets. Overall sentiment around AI ROI (not specific to NVDA) has shifted to “show me the money”. The market wants to see companies generating real revenues from AI, not just spending more and more on infrastructure.

But for me, the most important takeaway from this NVDA report is the same thing I say every earnings season. **It is not the numbers that matter most. It is the market’s reaction to the numbers.**

NVDA just delivered a record quarter with a massive guidance raise, and the stock sold off. That tells me **a lot** about the current market environment.

And it reminds me of one of my favorite Howard Marks quotes.

> “Investing is a popularity contest, and the most dangerous thing is to buy something at the peak of its popularity. At that point, all favorable facts and opinions are already factored into its price, and no new buyers are left to emerge…For this reason, fundamentals don't have to deteriorate in order for losses to occur”

To summarize Marks’ viewpoint, stocks often peak when the newsflow/fundamentals are at their best. Not when things are falling apart, but when things look perfect.

Why is this the case? It goes back to something I talk about all the time. Price action is a constant battle between buyers and sellers. A stock does not simply go up because of revenue beats or profit margins. **A stock goes up because there is more buying demand than selling demand.**

On that note, I am reminded of a great story my former boss on the trading floor used to tell. It is a famous piece of Wall Street lore, and it is attributed to Doug Kass, the founder of Seabreeze Partners.

The story takes place in 2012 at a major investment conference. At the time, Apple was the undisputed king of the market. The fundamentals were flawless. The iPhone was dominating. Every analyst on the Street had a “Strong Buy” rating on it. AAPL was a consensus long trade, and the stock looked unstoppable.

Kass stood before a room full of professional investors and fund managers and asked a simple question.

“How many people in this room are currently long AAPL?”

Virtually every hand in the room went up.

Kass looked around at the sea of raised hands and calmly said:

**“That is exactly why I’m short. There is no one left to buy.”**

*Note: Kass’ timing on this trade was near perfect. It is one of the most famous examples of a “sentiment top” in market history. AAPL peaked at $705 around the time of Kass’ speech. 7 months later, Apple fell to a low of $390 (a -45% decline).*

This story ties right into Howard Marks’ investing philosophy. When everyone is already in the trade, when the fundamentals are great, when the newsflow couldn’t possibly be any better, the stock becomes vulnerable. Not because anything is “wrong” with the company. But because, if everyone in the room already owns the stock, there is no marginal buyer left to drive the price higher.

To be clear, NVDA *only* finished -5.5% today. The stock closed Thursday right where it did a week ago. And it is far from a breaking down here. But, I have real concerns that the buying demand has been exhausted, at least for now. Everyone owns NVDA. This is among the most crowded trades out there, and has been for a few years.

Looking at the big picture, NVDA has not moved in 7 months now. The stock just closed the day right where it did in July 31st (~7 months ago).

#### **The Big Picture**

So, NVDA could not take the market higher (at least not yet).

The QQQ has been rangebound for nearly 5 months now.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yF4h!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3997f60c-867b-4583-9855-88867d17ea97_1566x846.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yF4h!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3997f60c-867b-4583-9855-88867d17ea97_1566x846.png)

NVDA 7 months.

Bears have had every opportunity to seize control, but they have not been able to do so. My [bear trap thesis](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/hard-penny?utm_source=publication-search) is not dead yet. But most importantly, this very much remains a hard penny environment for me.

Today, we saw a rotation into Small Caps and beaten down software names, which is certainly interesting. But I would not be getting too bullish here if Semis start to falter. The SOX closed right at its 21 EMA ($8100), which is a key level to watch. If SOX establishes itself below $8100, it will likely drag the broader tech complex down along with it.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Yr9W!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd6d02a24-dd19-44d6-992b-4da5cfb04e9a_1267x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Yr9W!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd6d02a24-dd19-44d6-992b-4da5cfb04e9a_1267x844.png)

Unfortunately, there is no crystal ball that will tell you the market’s next move. What I do know is that the most speculative stocks are in Stage 4 downtrends, Bitcoin & Software are in Stage 4, and now even Semis (the leader) look questionable after the NVDA print.

We have also had 5 [Hindenburg Omens](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/mixed-market-a68?utm_source=publication-search) in the last month and a [“Bear Market level” of single-stock blowups](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-clusters?utm_source=publication-search). Most importantly, this is a [hard penny trading environment.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/hard-penny?utm_source=publication-search) And if Semis start to break lower here, it could be a dangerous time for the entire Tech/AI trade.

Nothing has been able to take this market significantly higher (or significantly lower). The range continues to tighten. And in my experience, when the QQQ gets this tight, a decision is coming soon.

My [bear trap thesis](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/hard-penny?utm_source=publication-search) is still alive, but I do not see much reason to add significantly more risk until the indices make that decision. Patience here. I will let the market tell me when it’s ready. If we do get a real, sustainable market breakout here, there will be plenty of opportunities ahead in the coming months. Just remember how many great opportunities there were from April-October. This is what a real, sustained uptrend looks like.
