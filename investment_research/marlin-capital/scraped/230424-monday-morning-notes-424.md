# Monday Morning Notes 4.24

Technical Update

The choppy market action continued on Friday with the S&P 500 closing out Friday +0.09%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Evqm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Febe24855-d9bb-4312-9038-97d4ab255f3c_1192x888.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Evqm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Febe24855-d9bb-4312-9038-97d4ab255f3c_1192x888.jpeg)

The market has traded in a narrow range for all of April and much of late March. We have a big week of mega cap tech earnings and economic data ahead, so this could provide a potential catalyst for more defined action.

Technically, this setup still leans bullish with price above all key moving averages. With that said, the SPX has (thus far) been unable to break through key resistance levels $4150-$4200. Momentum stalled out the last 2 weeks, but bears have been unable to take the market down decisively. Until we see the SPX start to break some key moving averages and support levels below, there is no reason to get bearish here from a technical perspective.

Key support levels to watch below include $4130-$4135, $4100, and $4075-$4080.

Key resistance level to watch above include $4160, and $4190-$4200 (YTD highs).

JPM POSITIONING INTELLIGENCE: Given the propensity for the pendulum of sentiment and flows to swing from one extreme to another, a lack of exuberance lately suggests that equities could grind higher until “greed” sets in more firmly or a more definitive negative catalyst shows up. That said, the positive flows and more neutral positioning does suggest that there’s greater potential for negative catalysts to drive markets lower than was the case heading into the banking crisis last month.
