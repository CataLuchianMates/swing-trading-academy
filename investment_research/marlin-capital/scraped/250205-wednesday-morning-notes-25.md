# Wednesday Morning Notes 2.5

**Daily Market Commentary:**

US stocks finished broadly higher yesterday with outperformance in energy, discretionary, and tech (PLTR +23% on beat & raise qtr, SPOT +13% on better Q1 guide).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rix0!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa26165ba-65b7-4640-ac94-122135f1c2e9_1284x2147.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rix0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa26165ba-65b7-4640-ac94-122135f1c2e9_1284x2147.jpeg)

GOOGL results after the bell were mixed, with Ad revs better but cloud revs a bit disappointing. Positioning score was an 8/10 going in, so expectations were high for this GOOGL print. The stock is set for a -7% gap down this morning, dragging down the indices along with it.

The direction of the overall market is best described as choppy over the last 2 months (since early December). During that time, the QQQ has traded in a roughly 25 point range ($505-$530).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1gsX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb7fb3879-d638-489e-8e21-501a5a4dc2b2_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1gsX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb7fb3879-d638-489e-8e21-501a5a4dc2b2_1396x653.png)

We are seeing stocks go their separate ways based off earnings results, where stocks that beat estimates are outperforming more than usual and stock that miss are underperforming 2x as much as usual.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EExt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F29c881e5-3e0e-47c0-99fa-c7e14927ba54_450x308.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EExt!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F29c881e5-3e0e-47c0-99fa-c7e14927ba54_450x308.jpeg)

Even in Big Tech, it increasingly feels like a “haves” or “have nots” in this group, which is a different flavor to much of the last 2 years. At the moment, META and AMZN are the most loved stocks, whereas NVDA (churning below its 200 SMA), AAPL (big underperformance in Jan), and MSFT (below its 200 SMA, approaching 7yr low relative P/E multiples) are not.

With that said, it’s hard to see this choppy market action as overly bearish. Bears had multiple opportunities in Monday gap downs (on news) to break the range to the downside, but bulls stepped in twice to buy the dip. I continue to believe we will ultimately resolve to the upside out of this range, but in the meantime, I think it’s best to buy on dips and sell into strength (and not buy breakouts).

We are set to open right in the middle of the 2 month trading range in the QQQ today with GOOGL pulling down the indices.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YulH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F28c6b540-4242-43d5-985b-294da15a7a50_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YulH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F28c6b540-4242-43d5-985b-294da15a7a50_1396x653.png)

My focus will continue to be on earnings gappers and names with price, volume, and fundamental catalysts. Yesterday, I talked about my top 2 earnings trades setups so far: VIAV and IBM.

Both stocks gave us morning dips yesterday to get long. I bought IBM at $259.5 and $258.14 and VIAV at $12.16.
