# A Long, Cold Winter

The recent action in ARKK closely resembles the topping of the Nasdaq Bubble in 2000.  Look at these charts side by side.

When you zoom in even closer, the similarities become more evident.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NTUR!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd1da5a98-f1df-4c4a-8862-224578150a7b_1100x272.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NTUR!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd1da5a98-f1df-4c4a-8862-224578150a7b_1100x272.png)

Many people dismiss such a comparison, but I believe it is crucial to study market history and cycles to help learn from the past.

These charts look the same because this is topping action after a parabolic run.  In simpler terms, this is what a bubble bursting looks like.

If you want to know how we ended up here, please read [“We’re Not in Kansas Anymore”.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/were-not-in-kansas-anymore)  In my report, I dive into how the Bubble formed and when it started to pop.  Overall, investor risk appetite peaked in February along with many of the most speculative stocks.

The scary part for Bulls is that valuations are still historically high.  The fact is, many growth stocks are still at, near, or above PEAK pre-Covid multiples.  I emphasized peak because these were all-time high multiples, and many stocks are right back to those spots.  The issue is that this was during a monstrous bull market rally.  The market was on fire in February of 2020.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xrST!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fae732f9c-6007-4a06-be66-526b1faff8ce_1298x671.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xrST!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fae732f9c-6007-4a06-be66-526b1faff8ce_1298x671.png)

Look around you.  Does this look like a bull market to you?  No?  That’s because it’s not - it’s a Bear Market.  Bear Markets do not typically end with stocks returning to “high” valuations.  Bubbles don’t usually end when stocks are simply expensive.  They end when stocks are cheap.

High growth software multiples are still 54% above Pre-Covid levels, according to Jamin Ball.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0rt2!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F447d2ab7-5812-422f-98f6-91340123afbe_638x378.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0rt2!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F447d2ab7-5812-422f-98f6-91340123afbe_638x378.png)

If you want to see what cheap valuations look like, you really have to go back to the Covid crash lows. Stocks have not been truly “cheap” since the March 2020 Covid crash.  In terms of multiples, these were historically cheap valuations, in line with what we have seen during major market correction.

There have been other SaaS multiple compression sell offs that usually ended with most growth stocks trading under 10x NTM, such as December of 2018.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bvqk!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9723e3e6-a52e-4cff-bd97-eef807e28003_1329x931.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bvqk!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9723e3e6-a52e-4cff-bd97-eef807e28003_1329x931.png)

If the market wants to really panic, we will see growth stocks end up with cheap multiples of under <10x NTM revenue.  For many, that is still over a 40% decline from here.

As I have stated previously, my **Bear Case** in ARKK is a decline to the $44 level.  That would still include roughly 50% downside from here, meaning many individual names would likely decline greater than 50%.

**Price Targets**

Throughout this correction, my **Base Case** in ARKK has remained a move to the $78 level.  Now that we are approaching this target, I can safely say I need to adjust my Base Case.  The reason is pretty straightforward – this is simply not bottoming action.  In fact, the recent price action points to an acceleration in selling.  The $78 level, therefore, is now my **Bull Case**.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZT5T!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F44bf9031-bccf-414a-b6c6-679fc95330c4_2048x987.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZT5T!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F44bf9031-bccf-414a-b6c6-679fc95330c4_2048x987.png)

I am not sure exactly what my **Base Case** is now or how much ARKK will decline.  Below support levels include $80, $78, $73, $65.

**Watch the Nasdaq**

Ultimately, my point is that it’s going to be more important now to watch the general market.  Coming into today, the Nasdaq is roughly 7% off ATHs, approaching official correction territory.  This is great news if you are a growth stock investor.  As I have said, I see this ending with the general market correcting 15-20%.  This is in line with historical norms of market corrections into an aggressive Fed tightening cycle.

Expecting growth stocks to bottom while the indices get hit is not realistic.  If the decoupling of growth stocks and the general market is over, this is great news.  Now, we may be able to watch the Nasdaq’s correction to give us hints on when to buy stocks.  Here are some key levels below to watch:

Nasdaq: $14,591 – 10% from highs → official Correction à -3.4% from yesterday’s close

Nasdaq: $13,780 – 15% from highs → 8.7% from yesterday’s close

Nasdaq: $12,970 – 20% from highs → official bear market → 14% from yesterday’s close

Now that the correction has started to hit the indices, it has become more important to watch the Nasdaq than ARKK.  This is because, as I mentioned, I do not expect ARKK to bottom much before the general market does.

As for my strategy now as ARKK approaches my $78 price target, I am unlikely to do any heavy buying in my favorite names there.  This is because the correction in the indices has essentially just started, and I see more downside in all my long term holdings as long as the indices are in a correction.

At this point, I will likely look to buy when the Nasdaq nears my $13,800 price target.  This is 8.7% below yesterday’s close.  At $13,800, the Nasdaq will have corrected roughly 15% peak to trough.  Once again, this is in line with historical corrections stemming from an aggressive shift in Fed policy change.  I do not envision a much bigger correction in the indices than 20%, as this is not a 2008 GFC or 2020 Covid Crash situation. There is no Black Swan event, and I liken this sell off to what happened in December 2018 for the general market, only this time many growth stocks started from incredibly high valuations.

As for the price targets I gave recently in my top [individual stock picks](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/technical-analysis-long-term-holdings-655), I am expecting most to now head lower than the targets I gave.  Again, these stocks will continue to be under pressure if the Nasdaq is in the midst of a correction.

Additionally, these price targets are support levels, but it certainly does not mean each stock will bottom there.  The fact that some are now approaching those targets already before ARKK has reached its target tells me they are heading lower.
