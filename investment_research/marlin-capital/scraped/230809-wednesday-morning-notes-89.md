# Wednesday Morning Notes 8.9

**Technical Notes**

The SPX briefly broke recent support lows in yesterday’s session ($4470), but reclaimed them by the Tuesday’s close.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!W76R!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5a73e3c3-001a-4a08-9ea7-7652dc719317_1197x889.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!W76R!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5a73e3c3-001a-4a08-9ea7-7652dc719317_1197x889.jpeg)

This has been a fairly orderly, overbought pullback in the indices so far.  The technicals are telling me that this dip will eventually be bought, especially looking at weekly charts.  On the weekly, this is not a bearish setup for the S&P 500.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yA53!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F65c079df-f607-4a36-bd45-7cb1868b02d5_962x723.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yA53!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F65c079df-f607-4a36-bd45-7cb1868b02d5_962x723.jpeg)

There is no reason to worry technically when looking at the SPX chart unless we start to break $4400 decisively.  With that said, we do have some reasons for additional caution.  The indices may need some more basing action, which would make it difficult for new long trades to work.

Many names on my watchlist are still acting strong.  These high relative strength stocks are the names to monitor while the indices pull back, as they will often provide the best returns when the general market pressure eases.  Below are the top names I’m watching.
