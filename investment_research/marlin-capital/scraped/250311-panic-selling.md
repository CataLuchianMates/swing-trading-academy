# Panic Selling?

Huge downside day for US markets today with the Nasdaq falling as much as -4.9% and the S&P 500 declining -2.7% overall.

My Base Case for this pullback has been a -10% correction [since the S&P 500 was -2% off highs.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/red-flags-emerge) We are basically there now with the SPX down -8.7% and reaching -9.5% at today’s lows. The Nasdaq is down -13.5%.

However, it is hard for me to call a bottom here without capitulation or really any signs of panic. Yes, sentiment is low (but [I talked about why that is not an accurate indicator of extreme fear/panic for me](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-big-unwind)).

Let’s take a trip down memory lane to the 2022 Bear Market Bottom.

This is what capitulation looks like.

By definition, capitulation means investors are panicking and throwing in the towel, selling heavily, and moving to cash because they don’t want to lose any more money, etc. That is not what we are seeing right now.

As I talked about [this morning](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/positioning-cleanse), we have indeed seen a major and necessary positioning cleanse (from ATH longs to just below neutral in 5-6 months). However, “just below neutral” is obviously not capitulatory or panicky.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gg0j!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc808cb7a-1932-43a0-8127-fd39f2154fd0_1324x1176.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gg0j!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc808cb7a-1932-43a0-8127-fd39f2154fd0_1324x1176.jpeg)

It is simply “neutral”. The excess has been cleansed, but real capitulation is accompanied by underweight positioning (not simply “neutral”).

Even in the December 2018 market correction (Trump’s first China Trade War, SPX -19%, NDX -23%), we saw overall positioning reach -2 Z Score. This is what I want to see here in 2025 before calling a bottom, because I think we are ultimately at the point in the cycle where this is what we will need to see to form a bottom.

It is also hard for me to call a bottom when the charts are telling me we’re going lower, and that is what I am seeing in the technicals right now.

I talked about how we were basically at [Do or Die Time](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/do-or-die-time) for the indices and some key stocks [late last week](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/do-or-die-time), as they were sitting right at key long term support. They have all broken down in unison now.

To put it simply, stocks and sectors are more damaged from a technical standpoint than you want to see in a routine market correction (-10%).

Again, it is normal to see shakeouts to the 200 SMA in Bull Market pullbacks/corrections. We saw such shakeouts a few times throughout this Bull Market which began in late 2022.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kbNr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe1ee88bb-a2cc-41af-9f7d-1ba039d14533_1111x658.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kbNr!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe1ee88bb-a2cc-41af-9f7d-1ba039d14533_1111x658.png)

What we did not see, though, is [Death Crosses](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/do-or-die-time) in key leading names and sectors. Since my report last Thursday, we have followed through to the downside. Everything I talked about being at key support has broken down (SPX, GOOGL, AMZN, XLK, QQQ, Mag 7, etc.).

The Nasdaq is now establishing itself below the 200 day moving average for the 1st time since February 2022 (the market bottomed 10 months later in October 2022).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Gddg!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4f9e30eb-ed68-4470-b2bb-5d5553be659a_1109x650.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Gddg!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4f9e30eb-ed68-4470-b2bb-5d5553be659a_1109x650.png)

The first -10% of the market correction is now complete. However, this price action looks more to me like the 2022 Bear Market than a regular -10% market correction. Based off the current price action and lack of real fear, I am expecting more downside here in the market.

The biggest 2 parallels I can draw to now are the 2022 Bear Market and the 2018 correction. The 2022 Bear Market was a long, drawn out Bear Market that played out over 8 months. It is important to note that the 2022 Bear Market was accompanied by a technical recession (albeit unmemorable/shallow) and an earnings recession for the S&P 500.

The 2018 correction was much quicker. While the China trade war did play a factor, this 2018 correction was really all about the Fed. We definitely reached some panic selling in December 2018 before finding a bottom. However, its important to note that the 2018 correction was not accompanied by a recession.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gb4M!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fffa64d2a-e015-4cbe-a669-ccc4dbe01418_1108x647.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gb4M!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fffa64d2a-e015-4cbe-a669-ccc4dbe01418_1108x647.png)

This is where we are now. Recession vs No Recession will be a huge factor in determining where the market will bottom here in 2025 (including Earnings Recession or No Earnings Recession). If we do see a recession and earnings recession, I would be looking for a similar drawdown to the 2022 Bear Market (-25% in SPX, -35% in NDX).

Non Recessionary Bear Markets, on the other hand, tend to be more shallow (like 2018; -19% in SPX, -23% in NDX). However, we still saw capitulation selling (extreme light positioning) at the 2018 lows, which is the other thing I want to see before calling a market bottom in 2025. Again, it is my belief that we are now at the point in the cycle (the Bear cycle) where we need to see capitulation selling to bottom.

With that said, if you are a long term investor, there are worse strategies than buying the SPY and QQQ when they are down -15% + . Of course, we could always reach -35% in a Bear Scenario, which is why its best to layer in your buys in different tranches. But, if you were to only buy the SPY and QQQ when they are down -15% + (layer in it different tranches), you would likely make over +10% annualized over a long period of time.

If you are a trader, this is a much more difficult environment as volatility is extremely high. We will see a big short squeeze bounce at some point here in the near future, as we are reaching very oversold levels. For the QQQ, we are now 10 days outside of the lower Bollinger band.

I’m looking for a 1-3 day bounce here pretty soon (if not starting tomorrow). From there, I will be looking to add some shorts. If we get a decent long opportunity in the next day or 2, I will consider taking it for a 1-2 day trade.

My Big Picture View is that we are headed for more downside here before this correction ends. The big thing I want to see before calling a market bottom is capitulation selling (extreme light positioning). In my view, you ultimately need to see capitulation selling for a Bear Cycle to end and a new Bull Cycle begin.
