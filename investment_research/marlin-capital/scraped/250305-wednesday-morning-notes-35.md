# Wednesday Morning Notes 3.5

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Wild day yesterday with whipsaw type action as the S&P 500 and QQQ tested their 200 day moving averages.

In the QQQ, we tested the 200 SMA for the 1st time since the August pullback. It was also the highest volume day since the August lows.

It is not surprising to see the indices try to stage a bounce at 200 SMA support. This is the 1st target I talked about for this pullback. However, this is not my final target for the correction. My base case is still a -10% correction for the SPX.

Small Caps and Semis have already broken below the 200 SMA (which they did not in August). NVDA has also broken below its 200 SMA, which it did not in August.

High beta is having its worth month since the 2020 COVID crash.

Underneath the surface, this is a more bearish environment for stocks than we saw during the August correction. Net new lows are currently higher than at any other point since the October 2022 market bottom. The overall health of the market, in my estimation, is worse than the August pullback. This is why I expect us to ultimately break below the 200 SMA during this correction before bottoming.

As I have discussed, this market pullback has been a complete positioning washout with crowded trade unwinds. Hedge Fund long exposure was in the 94th percentile at the end of January. It is now in the 67th percentile after an unprecedented month of Hedge Fund de-risking.

I will keep monitoring and sharing the positioning updates as they come in, as I believe this captures extreme fear most accurately and is perhaps the best indicator of a bigger picture market bottom.

With all that said, we did see bulls take a stand at the 200 SMA yesterday and it is very possible we see a multi-day bounce here (due to oversold conditions, sentiment washout, etc.). I wouldn’t buy into a gap up in this market, but I’m also not looking to short with how oversold we are (32 RSI in QQQ). We will need to see a larger bounce for me to be interested in opening any short positions.

This is a difficult market to trade with Trump tape bombs and headlines really driving the action. It is definitely a day traders market right now, which to me is more of an algos market (as they react to the news headlines before you can even read it). With the VIX elevated, you can expect larger and faster intraday moves. I will continue to stress risk management right and caution right now until we see conditions begin to improve.
