US stocks rallied for a 2nd consecutive day on Wednesday with the S&P 500 gaining +0.7% and the Nasdaq leading the way at +1.2%.

Oil fell -2.5% to ~$99 while Energy names sold off hard. Today’s follow through had a re-grossing feel to it with Momentum, Memory, and the Mag 7 leading the charge. The market was aggressively pricing in an end to the Iran conflict.

Yesterday’s massive rally was particularly notable. The S&P 500 finished +2.9%, marking the first time in 90 trading days that the index posted a gain of at least +2.85%.

But here is the thing about outsized upside shocks like yesterday’s rally. They do not have a great historical track record.

Dating back to 2006, similar occurrences (where SPX finishes > +2.85%) have generally been followed by choppier price action and weaker risk-adjusted returns over the subsequent 1-2 weeks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hDBs!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F76e0b33f-be51-4dcd-957e-8372926a8a8e_785x364.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hDBs!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F76e0b33f-be51-4dcd-957e-8372926a8a8e_785x364.png)

**Here is what stands out in the data 👇**

(1) The market tends to give it back. Average returns are negative at every single time horizon from 1 day out to 10 days. Historically speaking, the initial pop does not usually stick.

(2) Weakness tends to deepen with time. The first few days tend to be choppy, but the real damage typically shows up after Day 4. The Day 6-8 window is historically the ugliest stretch.

(3) Volatility increases with a lag. The big drawdowns tend to come later. The worst outcomes build over time, with historical max drawdowns ballooning from single digits early on to -20%+ within 2 weeks.

(4) The upside is capped, meaning positive follow-through beyond a week is rare. Almost all of the historical upside at longer time horizons comes from a single outlier event (2018, and this was after a -20% drawdown and extreme underweight positioning -2.5z).

My takeaway is straightforward. These massive upside shock days (like yesterday) have historically been characteristic of volatile, choppy downtrends, not the start of a new sustained rally. They tend to show up in the middle of the storm, not at the end of it. The data says we are not in the clear yet, and to expect more volatility from here, not less.

To be clear, this does not mean yesterday’s rally is guaranteed to fail. But it does show that the odds do NOT favor chasing it (at least, not right away).

Then tonight happened. 👇

**The Trump Address**

The market was expecting President Trump’s address to the nation tonight to be de-escalatory. The last 2 days of price action were built on that assumption.

Instead, we got the opposite.

Here are the summary points of President Trump’s address tonight **👇**

* The Iran War will last another 2-3 weeks
* The US will hit Iran “extremely hard” over the next 2-3 weeks
* The US will strike Iranian power plants if no deal is reached
* The US “will bring Iran back to the Stone Age”
* Core strategic objectives are “close to completion” in Iran
* The US will not import oil from the Strait of Hormuz in the future
* “Iran’s navy is gone and their air force is in ruins”

US oil prices surged above $104/barrel immediately following the speech.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!T-pD!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6f48f8fc-9c75-4c5a-9552-a828afa8d90c_1267x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!T-pD!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6f48f8fc-9c75-4c5a-9552-a828afa8d90c_1267x844.png)

This is not exactly what the market was pricing in.

The last 2 days were built almost entirely on de-escalation optimism. Today’s follow through rally was the market doubling down on that bet (ahead of Trump’s speech). And tonight, the President told the nation that the US will be hitting Iran “extremely hard” over the next 2-3 weeks. That is the definition of headline whiplash.

As a reminder, the US stock market is closed on Friday (4/3). Tomorrow (Thursday) is the final trading day of the week. With oil surging above $104 after hours on the speech, Thursday’s open is likely to look very different from today’s close.

Now, I have said this many times before, and it remains the single most important framework for navigating this current environment. **It is not the news that matters. It is the market’s reaction to the news.**

Trump’s speech tonight was undeniably a bearish setback. Oil is spiking. The war headlines are not done yet. But here is what I will be watching very closely in the coming days: does the market make new lows? Or do we form a higher low?

If the S&P 500 does NOT make new lows on this re-escalation, that tells you something very important about the underlying bid in this market. It would mean the market absorbed a major escalatory headline and held (for the first time). This would be a major change of character. That kind of resilience, in the face of bearish news, is one of the most bullish signals you can get.

On the other hand, if we gap down hard tomorrow and follow through to the downside in the coming days (eventually taking out Tuesday’s lows), that is the market telling you it is not ready. And when the market speaks, you must listen.

As I talked about in [“Headline Roulette”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/headline-roulette?utm_source=publication-search), this is a headline-driven market. And in a headline-driven market, your job is not to predict the headlines. Your job is to stay nimble and react to the market’s reaction. That is what we do here at Marlin Capital, and that is how we will continue to navigate this challenging/whiplash environment.

The coming days will tell us a lot. Stay nimble.
