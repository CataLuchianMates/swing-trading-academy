**Active Portfolio**

**OSCR -**

**Exited after hours for -10.8% loss**

[My top trade idea for July.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-trade-idea-for-july) It was not a bullish day for OSCR as it tried to breakout over $22 but failed and formed a bearish engulfing candle. In hindsight this was a sell signal, but no one could have predicted the negative news that has now taken it down by ~9% after hours.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4Z5p!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F60b2dbd4-1d2e-4223-86cc-9555891bbca5_1121x647.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4Z5p!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F60b2dbd4-1d2e-4223-86cc-9555891bbca5_1121x647.jpeg)

I knew $18.80-$19 was the key support level to watch going in, and would have even considered buying at that spot if we pulled back orderly. But, not when we slice through support after hours on negative news. News catalysts change everything, and sometimes they go against you.

This OSCR trade failed for me and I took the -10.8% loss. It was a fantastic setup going in and had everything I look for (and more importantly, fulfilled the formula that has brought me the biggest winners in my career). But, there is always risk in trading no matter how strong a setup looks. You can not predict the news cycle. I’m at peace with my losses knowing I did my research and managed risk. From there, all you can do is accept the result.
