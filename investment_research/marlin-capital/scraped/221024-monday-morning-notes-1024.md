# Monday Morning Notes 10.24

Technical Analysis

US stocks closed strong last week with a big Friday rally. The QQQ notched its first close above the daily 21 EMA in over a month.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qf8r!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcfc5c1a5-4fe6-465a-9661-c2f7b524802e_1020x772.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qf8r!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcfc5c1a5-4fe6-465a-9661-c2f7b524802e_1020x772.jpeg)

As long as the 21 EMA support ($274-$275) holds up on any pullbacks, this rally can continue to the $285-$290 region. The 60 EMA at $287 looks like a realistic target for this bounce. Key resistance levels ahead include $278-$280, $284-$285, and $287-$290. The key support level (as mentioned above) remains $274-$275 and $270.

Keep in mind 46% of the S&P 500 market cap reports earnings this week, so there are no shortage of potential catalysts to whipsaw the market. We should be in for plenty of earnings based volatility and wide gaps day-to-day.
