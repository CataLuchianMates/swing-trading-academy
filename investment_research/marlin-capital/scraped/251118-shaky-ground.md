# Shaky Ground

We kicked off the week with another classic “risk off” day for US stocks with markets bleeding lower after a green open. The S&P 500 closed below its 50-day moving average for the first time since April 30th, snapping its 5th longest streak since 1950 (201 consecutive days).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7fLp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7ed34f74-d95a-4a29-bd71-e7a2b4439ab0_1012x675.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7fLp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7ed34f74-d95a-4a29-bd71-e7a2b4439ab0_1012x675.png)

There was no rotation out there at all on Monday, as over 400 SPX names finished red on the day. Just straight up selling across the board.

The Russell 2000 Small Cap breakout has failed at 3+ year resistance levels.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7ApH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc349dc65-6cb7-4125-b9c0-79b412e335ee_1266x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7ApH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc349dc65-6cb7-4125-b9c0-79b412e335ee_1266x844.png)

High flying momentum stocks have cratered.

Non-Profitable Tech is down -20% on the month, speculative corners (Drones/Quantum/Space/Nuclear) are all >30% off highs, and Crypto continues its own unwind. Last week was the biggest week of outperformance for Mega Cap Tech vs Speculative Tech since February (when the market was first shifting into risk off mode during the Q1 decline, and there was plenty more to go at that point.). This is a good reminder that when the market shifts to “risk off” mode, speculatives are the first to be unwound (and they will be unwound the hardest).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pBDJ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd07b6b0d-76da-4e58-96c8-5295c348b635_1097x1053.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pBDJ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd07b6b0d-76da-4e58-96c8-5295c348b635_1097x1053.jpeg)

Many former leading stocks are now establishing themselves below their 50-day moving averages for the first time since April. Bitcoin is establishing itself below its 200-day moving average (& heading for a death cross), a glaring “risk off” signal. Bitcoin and the QQQ have been highly correlated in recent years, so it looks like one of these might be lying. With the overall action out there, my guess is that it’s the QQQ.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!S0Yp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F41cf850d-b80a-44c5-869a-189610d706f7_1553x679.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!S0Yp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F41cf850d-b80a-44c5-869a-189610d706f7_1553x679.png)

With the S&P 500 closing below its 50-day moving average, this could lead to more selling, especially in the systematic trading community. Remember, [we rode huge systematic buying tailwinds back in July](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/volatility-melt?utm_source=publication-search). But now, there are big potential headwinds on the horizon from this cohort.

According to Goldman Prime, the CTA short-term SXP flip level sits at $6725. We just closed below today. If we stay below $6725, Goldman estimates that we could see -$165B of systematic equity selling (a 1 sigma event). CTAs are currently long $212B of US equities. Put another way, CTAs just started selling last week (but only sold -$13B), and are still near max long right now.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hWve!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2afbb02d-268e-4ee5-aff6-1d4e20c1d480_1148x741.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hWve!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2afbb02d-268e-4ee5-aff6-1d4e20c1d480_1148x741.jpeg)

This is a substantial risk if the SPX can’t rally back above $6725 in short order. In other words, if the SPX establishes itself below its 50-day MA, we could be looking at more downside ahead.

NVDA earnings post-market close on Wednesday is the focus the rest of the week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mZkZ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F94e79b3f-4f55-45a0-8988-8fca2a15e185_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mZkZ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F94e79b3f-4f55-45a0-8988-8fca2a15e185_1920x1080.jpeg)

September NFP (est nonfarm payrolls +80k, unemployment at 4.3%) and NVDA earnings post-close Wednesday (EPS est $1.26) will be the 2 biggest focus points this week. From GIR on NVDA “we expect investors to focus on (1) incremental details on $500B datacenter rev forecast; (2) OpenAI deployments in terms of timing and revenue in 2026; (3) Rubin ramp in 2026”.

Maybe NVDA can save the entire market with a blowout report on Wednesday, but I’m certainly not holding my breath on that one. There is too much technical damage out there right now. Nothing we saw today was indicative of any type of bottoming action (in individual names or the indices). The market remains firmly in “risk off” mode heading into Tuesday.
