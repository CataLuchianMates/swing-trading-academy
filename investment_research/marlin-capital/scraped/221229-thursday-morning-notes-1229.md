# Thursday Morning Notes 12.29

Technical Update

The SPX broke down through the key $3800 support level yesterday, closing the day at $3783.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4THh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3f995259-4318-4d3d-9126-d9533e1748f5_959x725.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4THh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3f995259-4318-4d3d-9126-d9533e1748f5_959x725.jpeg)

It should be emphasized how low the trading volume has been the last 2 days. I would expect this to continue into year end.

We are set for a sizable gap up this morning back above $3800. Technically, the setup remains bearish on all time frames. However, I continue to believe the most likely scenario into year end is a close between $3800-$3900. A directionally sustainable move typically (but not always) requires higher trading volumes.
