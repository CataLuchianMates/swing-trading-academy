# Buckle Up

Marlin Capital Readers,

The S&P 500 has been stuck in a range for 4 months now. If you were just watching the indices, you might think this market has been boring.

It has been anything but.

Underneath the surface, we are seeing some insane stuff. Anthropic has been nuking Software stocks seemingly every single day. The Software ETF IGV is now trading at levels we saw all the way back in February 2021.

On the other hand, leaders are leading.

**We are currently up >60% on 4 different longs in our [2026 Portfolio](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/marlin-capital-live-portfolio-dashboards) in just under 2 months (+ 2 exit sales for +153% and +66%).**

**Split Market**

Underneath the hood, we are seeing some of the most dramatic bifurcation I can remember.

As I have talked about extensively over the past several months, many stocks have entered (or are entering) their [Stage 4 downtrends](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/grinch-selloff?utm_source=publication-search). This is classic late cycle market behavior, exactly what I described in detail back in [“Weakness”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/weakness?utm_source=publication-search) and [“Risk Off”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70?utm_source=publication-search) last October (when all this began).

But here is the important part 👇

This does NOT mean the indices and the final leaders won’t have 1 more wave up. In fact, this is exactly what happened during both the Dot Com and Covid Bubbles. The most speculative names topped first (6-14 months before the indices), breadth deteriorated underneath the surface, and yet the indices and true leaders pushed higher for 1 final leg before it was all over. The first pullback “bear trap” thesis I laid out in [“Hard Penny”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/hard-penny?utm_source=publication-search)) is still very much alive.

#### **My Strategy**

My strategy in this market has been very clear.

Across [my 2 portfolios](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/marlin-capital-live-portfolio-dashboards), I am only holding stocks that I believe to still be in their [Stage 2 uptrends](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/grinch-selloff?utm_source=publication-search). Any stock I believed to have entered a [Stage 4 downtrend](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/grinch-selloff?utm_source=publication-search) has been exited. That is why I closed out the [Trump Trades Portfolio](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/marlin-capital-live-portfolio-dashboards) (PLTR, TSLA, COIN). This is why I exited 22 names from the [2026 Portfolio](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/marlin-capital-live-portfolio-dashboards) over the past several weeks. I am not looking to be aggressive in names that I believe may have topped for the cycle.

What I am left with are the leaders. And right now, leadership has narrowed dramatically.

**VRT**

Vertiv is the Large Cap leader in the space ($97B mkt cap vs $12B for MOD). They are a full-stack Data Center infrastructure story (while MOD is more narrowly focused). Power, thermal management, liquid cooling, prefabricated data center systems, and services, all under one roof. If you are building a AI data center, you are almost certainly buying from Vertiv (at least, that’s what the numbers say).

The most recent earnings report (2/11) was a monster. Q4 organic orders came in at +252% YoY with a book-to-bill of 2.9x, meaning for every $1 of revenue Vertiv shipped, it booked nearly $3 of new orders. The backlog now sits at $15B, up +109% YoY and more than double where it was just a year ago. And the forward guidance was equally impressive. FY 2026 EPS guided to ~$6.02, ~13% above prior Street consensus, (and representing +43% YoY EPS growth).

VRT reacted exactly the way you want to see following these earnings, surging +25% on the day of the report. The stock has been consolidating in a bull flag pattern since, but started to break out today.

#### **The Big Picture**

For me, this remains a challenging and choppy market environment. The bifurcation underneath the surface is unlike anything I have seen outside of a late-cycle topping process. The data backs that. We have had 5 Hindenburg Omens and a [“Bear Market level” of single stock blowups](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-clusters?utm_source=publication-search).

Yesterday was the 5th Hindenburg Omen in the past month. As I covered in [“Mixed Market”,](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/mixed-market-a68?utm_source=publication-search) Hindenburg Omens are rare and historically bearish signals. 5 in a single month is a very bearish stat.

In my view, this remains a hard penny environment. As a result, I am being more selective and prioritizing capital preservation above all else.

But, tomorrow night has the ability to change everything (at least in the short term).

NVDA reports earnings after the bell tomorrow.

If the numbers are strong and more importantly, the market reaction is positive, perhaps we can finally get some upwards momentum to break out of this 4M range (i.e. a trending market). This is the **Bull Case** scenario. If that is the case, I will look to add more long exposure from the names on my watchlist.

The **Bear Case** is that the numbers are not quite good enough, NVDA sells off, and it drags the indices, Semis (including Memory), and the entire AI complex down with it. In that case, it is very possible we are heading to a deeper correction for Tech.

The market is coiled tight right now and getting ready to make a move.

Buckle up for tomorrow.
