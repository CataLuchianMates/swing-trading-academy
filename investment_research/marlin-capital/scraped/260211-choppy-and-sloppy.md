# Choppy and Sloppy

The sloppy market price action continued on Tuesday after a 2-day index bounce (on Friday and Monday).

The QQQ has traded in an ~50 point range for the last 4+ months. The index just closed exactly where it did on October 9th.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kLnd!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F060e73af-4e77-4a62-95ef-12c67ae91ecb_1566x846.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kLnd!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F060e73af-4e77-4a62-95ef-12c67ae91ecb_1566x846.png)

Sellers had their chance to really take control late last week, but fumbled during this recent bounce.

To be clear, this does not mean we’re out of the woods. It simply means that we are back in the lower/middle of this 4 month trading range (aka the chop zone).

[As I have talked about in great detail](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/wild-times), underneath the surface, stocks are diverging and going their own separate ways. Many high beta former leaders appear to have entered their Stage 4 downtrends. On the other hand, defensives, industrials, consumer staples, etc. are leading the market and breaking out to new highs.

This is what I would consider a “risk off” rally. [Historically](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/wild-times), this is the type of market behavior you see in the final innings of Bull Markets.

Big picture, I find this to be the trickiest part of the market cycle to trade. Stocks are going their own separate ways, making it much more difficult as a portfolio manager. My strategy is to abandon any stock that has shown key signs of topping behavior, and to focus on the leaders.

**Active Trading Portfolio**

If a choppy market is your Super Bowl, this is the right environment for you. For me, it is not

In fact, a choppy market is by far my least favorite type of environment to swing trade. Why? Because is no real rhythm to action. At least in a downtrend/bearish market, there is a bearish rhythm to the action. A choppy/directionless market is much tricker to trade because you don’t have any rhythm at all.

We are currently seeing big waves of selling (like early-mid last week), but then no real follow through to the downside. The dip is bought. Then another wave of selling comes. The dip is bought again. Then another wave of selling comes.

So, how will I attempt to combat this?

I’m fully keyed in on earnings setups now. In a choppy/directionless market (which we have been in for 4+ months), names with defined catalysts are my priority. This strategy worked out well for us in January with OSS and MRAM (although the environment has deteriorated since). Earnings names have a clear, identifiable reason for sustained buying momentum to continue, even if the indices continue to chop around. Ideally, these types of stocks that will be able to trade with more rhythm and momentum.

**ENPH**

We bought ENPH today at $51.23.

**All Active Portfolio buy and sell alerts are posted in real-time in the [Marlin Capital Live Trading Room](https://substack.com/chat/408010/post/3d65a58d-fce3-4f48-a8ed-ff9cc56f669e).**

Enphase is a beaten down Solar Energy company that is showing signs of life after a beat and raise quarter.

Q4 revenue came in at $343M, a solid +2% above consensus after **a long string of negative revisions**. Non‑GAAP EPS came in at $0.71, another solid beat (+20%).

Most importantly, Enphase raised Q1 revenue guidance to ~$285M, well ahead of Street estimates (~$262M). The company’s “long string of negative revisions” appears to be over. Enphase framed Q1 as the “demand low” with improvements throughout 2026 (especially 2H). On the Conference Call, management said that they “believe **Q1 marks the low point for underlying demand with improvement expected through 2026.”**

The stock is responding well to this report. In fact, the stock appears to be mirroring the fundamentals “bottoming”. ENPH gapped up big following this EPS report, finishing the post-earnings session with its 2nd highest volume day ever (behind 1 time in June 2020).

ENPH has been consolidating in a nice bull flag pattern since. And now, the stock appears to be entering a Stage 2 uptrend, with a golden cross likely to come tomorrow (21 EMA > 60 EMA > 200 SMA).

This stock has been beaten down for so long, and now, it is entering a Stage 2 uptrend on an earnings catalyst (fundamentals bottoming) and volume. This is not a time for shorts to press their luck.

I am looking for a breakout of this post-earnings bull flag. On a move above $53, ENPH can see $60. My closing stop (1 HR closing) will be placed below $47.5 for ~7.3% risk on this trade.

**My Watchlist:**

**DOW**

Dow Chemical.

These are the type of names that are leading this market right now. DOW declined -40% in 2025, but has emerged as one of the market leaders so far in 2026 (up +38% YTD). Like ENPH, DOW is another turnaround story.

DOW has rallied big following its earnings report on 1/29. And for me, this move might just be getting started.

Q4 itself was weak (revenue miss, EPS miss, operating EBITDA down sequentially), but the price action in DOW shows that this type of quarter was already more than priced in.

Most importantly, management announced a new “Transform to Outperform” plan (which appears to be the real catalyst for the move). The company is targeting $2B+ in near-term EBITDA improvement (2/3rd from productivity, 1/3rd from growth) with $500M expected in 2026.

As I mentioned, DOW was crushed in 2025 (down -40%). A credible, quantifiable multi‑year plan with clear targets is exactly what value‑oriented investors and “ turnaround investors” (activists) tend to reward in cyclicals.

And this is exactly what we are seeing in DOW. Similar to ENPH, the price action is mirroring the “fundamentals bottoming”. The stock has already given a bullish Golden Cross (21 EMA > 60 EMA > 200 SMA) and entered its Stage 2 uptrend.

**SLB**

SLB (formerly Schlumberger) is an energy company that provides technology to find, drill, and produce oil and gas.

The stock is forming a bull flag consolidation pattern following a strong beat and meet quarter.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!376Y!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcdbb8277-8dc8-410d-aa8e-630547e0729d_1266x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!376Y!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcdbb8277-8dc8-410d-aa8e-630547e0729d_1266x844.png)

Q4 numbers beat expectations on both revenue ($9.75B vs. $9.55B est.) and EPS ($0.78 vs. $0.74), with 9% sequential revenue growth, 83 bps EBITDA margin expansion, and $2.3B FCF.

Forward guidance was in-line with consensus. Most importantly, the company highlighted its new growth leg: its Data Center Solutions business. SLB’s Data Center business is a new growth driver that will repurpose its power, cooling, and digital expertise for AI‑heavy data centers, with a goal of reaching a $1B revenue run‑rate by end‑2026.

As I mentioned, the stock is consolidating in a nice bull flag pattern following this EPS report. In my view, this rally appears to have more legs.
