## David Marlin Q&A (Comments)

**Q:** Thanks for this report, David. One point that caught my attention during the press conference is that the plan Powell laid out was for the most optimistic scenario (continued inflation at high levels, wage growth, improved labour market, etc.), not taking into consideration some of the risks he mentioned during the press conference. Should Omicron or another variant have significant economic impacts, Powell left himself plenty of room to delay the tapering and rate hikes.  Therefore any “bad” economic news (ie employment) would be positive for high growth stocks as it would imply a delay in the announced plan. This also gives the FED time to see if the inflation is transitory or not and if supply issues resolved themselves (with downward pressure on prices). Although he completely  dismissed the hypothesis, I believe political factors played into Powell’s decision (to calm senators that will confirm his nomination as Fed chair). Should that be the case this would also be favorable as it may imply a future delay (once he’s confirmed).  Not saying we’re gonna see ATH in high growth stocks any time soon, but it’s nice to see  (interpret in this case) some positives elements.

**David Marlin:** I agree with you here.  Right now, the roadmap they laid out is for 3 rate hikes in 2022, 3 more increases in 2023, and 2 in 2024. That would push the rate to 2.1% by the end of 2024.  That’s a total of 8 rate hikes through 2024.  I think the potential 3rd scenario of the Fed not fully following through on this roadmap is also a definite possibility, which would obviously be bullish for stocks.  The economic data between now and May could certainly change expectations as well, so it will be interesting to watch play out.  I think the market ultimately needs to price out the Fed’s current roadmap, but the possibility of a Fed pivot would provide a nice bullish surprise as well.
