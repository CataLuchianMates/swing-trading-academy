# Home on the Range

All the pieces for a rangebound market remain in place.  One could even make the argument for a slight upward bias to the action based off extremely negative sentiment, short discretionary positioning, and a large pile of cash on the sidelines.

1.       Money market fund flows have now reached all-time highs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ku1S!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5b938e9a-eed2-4f8e-b882-d3365a867d29_1201x781.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ku1S!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5b938e9a-eed2-4f8e-b882-d3365a867d29_1201x781.png)

Demand for cash remains strong due to the prevailing risk environment, as well as the higher yields currently offered by US Money Market Funds.

We are seeing investors flee to safer assets (versus riskier assets) at a fairly extreme pace.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qJRx!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcc5a1587-b6c4-40e1-aa00-f7c78394fa00_1245x965.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qJRx!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcc5a1587-b6c4-40e1-aa00-f7c78394fa00_1245x965.png)

2.       Fund managers are as bearish as ever.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-KKE!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F76d46c74-ac31-45cb-87f3-44a536c4bf37_802x420.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-KKE!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F76d46c74-ac31-45cb-87f3-44a536c4bf37_802x420.png)

Investor sentiment is currently near levels seen at major Bear Market lows of the last 20 years.

3.       The Fed is backstopping the banking system and offsetting QT.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qEly!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F841690e2-5c82-4822-9b7d-ef07383418bc_742x412.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qEly!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F841690e2-5c82-4822-9b7d-ef07383418bc_742x412.png)

With the recent bank turmoil, we have seen an injection of $440B of reserves on the Fed’s balance sheet, effectively reversing 6 months of QT (or 1/3rd of the Fed’s ~$1.3T of QT since the end of 2021).  While the Fed may continue QT in absolute terms, the net effect may be further balance sheet expansion from here.

4.       There is still some hope for a soft landing.

Fed Chair Powell refused to give up hope at last week’s press conference, where he was directly asked about how the recent bank turmoil affects the possibility of a soft landing.

> “It's too early to say really whether these events have had much of an effect… The question will be, how long this period is sustained. The longer it's sustained, then the greater will be the likely declines in tightening credit standards, credit availability, so we'll just have to see. I do still think though that there's a pathway to that. I think that pathway still exists and we're certainly trying to find it.”

Hedge funds are not buying this possibility, though, as their trading activity reflects a clear “Recessionary Rotation”.  Hedge funds are rotating into safer/strong balance sheet TMT stocks and defensives while rotating out of cyclicals.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!estX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd2fb4a76-4756-42c5-8590-f404453fe386_612x373.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!estX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd2fb4a76-4756-42c5-8590-f404453fe386_612x373.png)

Historically speaking, it is not uncommon for the S&P 500 to trade in a broad range until the recession begins.  This is when market declines typically accelerate.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NGI8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F09126850-750d-4ff3-bd02-258393cd5426_1085x690.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NGI8!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F09126850-750d-4ff3-bd02-258393cd5426_1085x690.png)

However, I would note that a sustainable rally (“New Bull Market”) is historically dependent on financial conditions materially easing.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7p17!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff40685c2-4e9a-4429-bfe8-2f6d353877b1_1266x820.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7p17!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff40685c2-4e9a-4429-bfe8-2f6d353877b1_1266x820.png)

Previous market bottoms have historically correlated with a peak in Goldman’s Financial Conditions Index.

5.       Unemployment remains historically low.

In my view, the resilience of the stock market has very much to do with the resilience of the labor market.  Households directly own 38% of US equities and have yet to turn into net sellers during this Bear Market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nBVj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F30cd1d72-65be-4802-98f5-52f3c504ff24_595x699.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nBVj!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F30cd1d72-65be-4802-98f5-52f3c504ff24_595x699.png)

Goldman Sachs projects households will become net sellers of ~$750B of equities in 2023.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7Mlc!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbeb916f7-d997-4dcb-9166-c6f5de3b4558_589x697.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7Mlc!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbeb916f7-d997-4dcb-9166-c6f5de3b4558_589x697.png)

In my view, we will likely need to see unemployment spike higher before that happens.

These projections are based off higher “risk-free” returns and lower personal savings rates (see below), which tend to be associated with decreased net equity demand from households.

As mentioned, inflation is clearly taking its toll on the US consumer, as the personal savings rate has declined to near 40-year lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!UXpW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcb013ea9-d94b-44f7-ae76-df1d8f103f89_597x472.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!UXpW!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcb013ea9-d94b-44f7-ae76-df1d8f103f89_597x472.png)

6.       Market breadth has been very poor as the generals refuse to go down easy.

Consider this: the MAGMA + NVDA cohort has added a collective ~$620B of market cap since the regional bank issues began, which compares to ~$950B of collective market cap lost by the remaining 494 companies in the S&P 500.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9hdv!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff1822fc5-a39f-47b9-af8b-86af61cc86a3_617x386.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9hdv!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff1822fc5-a39f-47b9-af8b-86af61cc86a3_617x386.png)

MSFT and AAPL alone comprise more than 13% of S&P now. No two names have been this dominant since T + IBM back in 1978.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZDRS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F021ac7fe-f204-46b5-a9bd-bb7374baf878_583x387.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZDRS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F021ac7fe-f204-46b5-a9bd-bb7374baf878_583x387.png)

Moreover, strong balance sheet stocks are outperforming weak balance sheet stocks at the greatest pace since March 2020, indicating that investors are fleeing to perceived “safer stocks”.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Lb3V!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6029f7ed-9d9e-4761-9fca-2e163469554c_1201x459.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Lb3V!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6029f7ed-9d9e-4761-9fca-2e163469554c_1201x459.png)

7.       The price action is range-bound, with the indices simply refusing to break down key support and failing at key resistance.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KGIq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F440cbeae-54fd-4a3a-8d13-f3ffaec9abbe_1114x833.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KGIq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F440cbeae-54fd-4a3a-8d13-f3ffaec9abbe_1114x833.png)

The S&P 500 has spent much of 2023 in a 200-point range between $3850-$4050.  The factors mentioned above continue to create a “hard penny” trading environment.  This is a term coined by legendary trader Mark Minervini to describe when a swing trader should get aggressive versus stay conservative.

> “Instead of bull or bear, I like to characterize the market as hard penny vs. easy dollar.  My rule of thumb: reserve aggressive trading for easy dollar environments.” - Mark Minervini

The wild card of all this range-bound action, as I pointed out in [“Things are Breaking"](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/things-are-breaking), is that things are indeed starting to break.

However, with the Fed stepping in to backstop the banking system, a crisis has been averted for now.  Along with each of the other factors mentioned above, this may help to put a temporary floor in the market until the recession starts, unemployment spikes substantially, or an unforeseen credit event materializes.
