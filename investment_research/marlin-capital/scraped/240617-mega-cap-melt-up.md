# Mega Cap Melt Up

The major US indices powered higher once again last week with the SPX +1.6% and NDX +3.5%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oJhr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8da4c63a-548f-4238-8dae-907d02bbf1f5_1197x887.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oJhr!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8da4c63a-548f-4238-8dae-907d02bbf1f5_1197x887.jpeg)

We saw a welcome surprise in the inflation narrative with Core CPI coming in well below expectations (+0.16%).

The biggest companies in the world continue to dominate the action.  Last week, we saw AAPL and MSFT break out to new ATHs, along with ORCL and AVGO post-earnings.

If it feels like Mega Cap Tech stocks are taking turns going on huge runs, that is because they are.  META +25% in Jan/Feb. AMZN +15% in Feb. GOOGL +17% in Mar/Apr.  MSFT +15% and AAPL +25% in May/June.  And of course, NVDA has been ripping higher all year (aside from a small hiccup in April).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BFF_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff8323cea-ed97-4ec7-b148-e77a50bc377c_999x630.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BFF_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff8323cea-ed97-4ec7-b148-e77a50bc377c_999x630.jpeg)

The 1 constant we have seen in the AI trade throughout this bull run has been the outperformance of Semiconductors.  While this outperformance is extraordinary, we have seen Semis go on massive runs before.  For reference, SOX outperformed the SPX by +385% during the Dot Com Bubble.  We are at +71% in favor of SOX during the current bull run.  This is to say that is plenty more juice left in the Semi space if the AI Bubble were to reach Dot Com levels.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!al98!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa8529279-d886-4f69-8857-274d8d791b8f_1050x675.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!al98!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa8529279-d886-4f69-8857-274d8d791b8f_1050x675.jpeg)

Speaking of the 90s, 2024 has had a 1995-like flavor to it so far.  1995 was the last time we got a soft landing.  The Fed cut from 6.00% to 5.25% and the S&P 500 saw 77 ATH closes.  YTD, the S&P 500 already has 29 ATH closes (the best start to an election year ever).  The Fed is projected to begin cutting rates in September and a soft landing is the consensus view on Wall Street.  The 2 paths are tracking pretty well so far (and remember, 1995 was just the appetizer for the epic rally that ran through 1999).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xkrn!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcdd64793-ef64-492b-8f38-5e459619877b_954x628.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xkrn!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcdd64793-ef64-492b-8f38-5e459619877b_954x628.jpeg)

There are plenty of stats surrounding the narrow breadth of the current market rally.  Only 49% of S&P 500 companies are currently above their 50-day moving average (meaning more than half of the SPX is currently in a short-medium term downtrend).

Understandably, this has raised some questions around relative performance and a lack of participation from Small Caps/outside of the Mag 7.  On Friday, the NDX outperformed the NDX Equal-Weight by 105bps, the 3rd time this year we have seen this spread end a day at this level.  At an 84 RSI, the NDX / NDXE pair is among the most overbought it has been in ~20 years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TUis!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F097bd046-3804-48dd-966d-31088c1c1d83_1171x730.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TUis!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F097bd046-3804-48dd-966d-31088c1c1d83_1171x730.jpeg)

The simple answer to the weak market breadth question has been the same all year:  Investors continue to pay up for real Earnings growth.  Outside of MSFT, NVDA, AMZN, GOOGL, and META, there is no real earnings growth in the S&P 495.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yxCd!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6e159646-5657-461e-bc19-5666528cd690_601x419.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yxCd!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6e159646-5657-461e-bc19-5666528cd690_601x419.jpeg)

The outperformance of US Tech stocks has been justified by superior earnings growth.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GV_6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feaa8d86b-5e38-4607-aa6d-42811fc08f31_952x620.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GV_6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feaa8d86b-5e38-4607-aa6d-42811fc08f31_952x620.jpeg)

With that said, the spread between Mega Cap Tech EPS growth and the rest of the S&P 500 is expected to narrow beginning in 2025 and that trend is projected to continue in 2026.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GwaQ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9e5d217e-2caa-49bf-b937-7e70d90155d1_598x425.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GwaQ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9e5d217e-2caa-49bf-b937-7e70d90155d1_598x425.jpeg)

The other major factor at play in the outperformance of Mega Cap Tech is stock buybacks.  As I wrote in “The Week Ahead 6/10”, Apple’s stock buyback program is 169% larger than the size of the average S&P 493 company. Good luck betting against those market dynamics.

With that said, buybacks are set to be on hold for the next month as the Buyback blackout period begins.  GS estimates that the upcoming blackout period will run from 6/14-7/19.  Companies enter their blackout periods ~4-6 weeks ahead of earnings and exit blackout 1-2 days post earnings.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sFVE!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F90754e2f-e8f5-425d-bac1-973c9892fa07_979x384.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sFVE!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F90754e2f-e8f5-425d-bac1-973c9892fa07_979x384.jpeg)

This coincides with some relatively weak seasonality in the back-half of June.  The last 2 weeks of June are typically choppy as some end of Q2 rebalancing takes place among fund managers.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dElv!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F96ebb291-9375-4715-a920-ad2bfc460a63_1102x750.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dElv!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F96ebb291-9375-4715-a920-ad2bfc460a63_1102x750.jpeg)

With that said, once the calendar turns to July, we enter the best seasonal 2-week stretch of the year for US stocks.  The NDX has finished positive for 16 straight July’s (last negative July was 2007) for an average return of +4.6%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!S48e!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0e8631c3-386b-4ec0-8e92-07a0d0c663b1_987x664.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!S48e!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0e8631c3-386b-4ec0-8e92-07a0d0c663b1_987x664.jpeg)

In my view, we could be setting up for something similar here in 2024.  We enter the week of 6/16 in overbought territory for both the SPX and QQQ.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-fbW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F30833b40-f480-4299-b719-63d01eb3ed50_1168x640.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-fbW!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F30833b40-f480-4299-b719-63d01eb3ed50_1168x640.jpeg)

The last 2 times the SPX and QQQ had daily RSI’s this high, it was followed by a -3-4% pullback in the indices over the ensuing several weeks.  A similar pullback would be a healthy occurrence here in 2024 as it would allow the market to shake off the overbought conditions.
