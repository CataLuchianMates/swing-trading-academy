# The Week Ahead

**Daily Market Commentary**

US stocks closed out last week on a high note with the S&P 500 finishing Friday +1.18%.  The index tested key 200-day moving average support near $4200 multiple times in the week before bouncing big following Friday’s stronger than expected jobs report.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zUP-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F51d3096a-6156-48c6-892e-4c3ec0c93e64_958x713.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zUP-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F51d3096a-6156-48c6-892e-4c3ec0c93e64_958x713.jpeg)

The QQQ, which was the stronger index during the recent market pull back, bounced +1.68% on Friday and closed above its 8 and 21 EMAs for the first time since the beginning days of September.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2hT-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2fa32f79-6eb5-46d9-a6d7-d3d92aa92bd5_974x720.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2hT-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2fa32f79-6eb5-46d9-a6d7-d3d92aa92bd5_974x720.jpeg)

On the 1 HR Chart, the QQQ looks strong after breaking out of its recent consolidation/basing period off September lows.  While the SPX went on to make new lows in October, the QQQ never breached September lows here in October.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!UZVP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffc62cf1d-7619-4218-b29c-d56f5925b1bd_1364x644.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!UZVP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffc62cf1d-7619-4218-b29c-d56f5925b1bd_1364x644.png)

SPX futures are lower on Monday morning with the US Bond market closed for Columbus day.  Globally, markets are mostly lower after the weekend Hamas attacks in Israel and the ensuing Israeli response.  Crude is up +4% on the backs of the news while Europe is mostly lower with energy outperforming.

Main events for this week in the US include PPI on Wednesday, CPI on Thursday, and the kickoff of earnings season on Friday.  Today we have several Fed speakers scheduled (Logan, Barr, Jefferson).

Earnings season will come into focus here with Bank earnings beginning on Friday.  Consensus expects SPX EPS growth to have troughed in Q2 before an improvement this quarter.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3wsG!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8b51eb16-8f14-4e03-86a5-c7e83ce9cec2_1259x429.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3wsG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8b51eb16-8f14-4e03-86a5-c7e83ce9cec2_1259x429.jpeg)

Earnings season is my favorite period to trade stocks as we typically see some of the best setups arise.  For new members, please read my report [“The Earnings Gapper Trade Setup”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-earnings-gapper-trade-setup) to learn about one of my favorite trade setups involving earnings gaps.
