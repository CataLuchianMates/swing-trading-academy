# Big Tech Blowouts

Today was action packed with the Fed meeting and MSFT + META earnings after the bell.

Starting with the Fed meeting, it went as expected with the committee leaving rates unchanged.

The big news was Powell’s hawkish tone in the press conference. The Fed chair remarked that “inflation is further from our goal than employment”, and that large cuts are unlikely unless the labor market weakens significantly.

Powell’s comments sent the probability of a September rate cut tumbling. Polymarket odds of a 25bps cut in September fell from 57% at the start of the presser to now 43%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WVvk!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F68850ba6-5e52-48ed-a328-83f107babcff_1284x1727.jpeg)](https://polymarket.com/event/fed-decision-in-september)

Selling pressure in the stock market ensued, sending the major averages into negative territory. The S&P 500 finished the day -0.12%.

But Microsoft and Meta earnings saved the day after hours (and the market, which had been starting to roll over in recent sessions).
