## David Marlin Q&A (Comments)

**Q:** Hi David, I am a recent subscriber to your service and I wish I had joined before the meltdown began. I used to have a big position in AFRM but got stopped out with a huge loss unfortunately. What are you thoughts on interest rate changes and general slowdown in economy going to affect AFRM's balance sheet? There is a lot FUD out there regarding BNPL in general. Thanks!

**David Marlin:** It is certainly the biggest risk in my mind as we have not seen the company have to deal with a tightening credit cycle.  However, to be a good long term investment, AFRM must be able to handle higher interest rates and all parts of the credit cycle.  Overall, I trust the management team as they outlined this risk in the 10k and how they plan to deal with it.  Affirm maintains an interest rate hedging program to reduce exposure to broad changes in prevailing interest rates.  This eliminates some, but not all, interest rate risk.  CEO Max Levchin was one of the cofounders of PayPal and I trust his plan to be able to handle tighter credit cycles.
