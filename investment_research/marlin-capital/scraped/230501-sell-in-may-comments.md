## David Marlin Q&A (Comments)

**Q:** David, are you still thinking 2,900-3.200 on the S+P is plausible?  or at least a test of last Fall's lows?  This market seems to shrug off all hits...what possibly justifies the valuation?  Still waiting for that golden buying opportunity you have been talking about for over a year.  Thanks.

**David Marlin:** Yes $2900-$3300 remains my base case scenario.  As I’ve written about in detail, my base case includes a recession within the next year.  As of now, I feel confident in saying that a recession is not priced into the market (and this is confirmed by future EPS projections showing this quarter will be the “trough”).  The economic data has been more resilient than expected to this point, leading many to believe that a soft landing could be ahead (see CMG CEO comments).  However, my stance on this has not changed and is only confirmed by the issues in the banking industry.  I fully believe we will see a recession priced in and future earnings estimates drop as the economic data deteriorates.  We are not at that point yet, despite what the leading indicators are showing.
