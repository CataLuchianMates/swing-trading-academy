# Bounceback

US stocks finished higher on Wednesday led by Tech on a combination of Semiconductor strength and a relief rally in Software. The Nasdaq staged its biggest day of outperformance this month.

Does this rally have legs, or is it just another dead cat bounce?

Here’s how we’re handling it at Marlin Capital. 👇

#### **[Current Marlin Capital Holdings](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/marlin-capital-live-portfolio-dashboards)**

No new trades for me so far this week.

Here’s where I stand on the market right now. 👇

First off, let me refresh everyone on the 5 key warning signs I’m seeing right now:

**1.) This is the big one for me. The most speculative leaders of the 2025 AI rally topped back in October.**

Names like OKLO, IONQ, RGTI, etc. (the spec leaders of the AI rally) peaked back in October (along with the Nasdaq), and have been in Stage 4 downtrends ever since.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lhQq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F19faa8b5-ffe6-4d15-983c-922861c58101_1456x436.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lhQq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F19faa8b5-ffe6-4d15-983c-922861c58101_1456x436.jpeg)

[Historically, this is how market topping processes begin.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-marlin-capital-2026-preview?utm_source=publication-search)

**2.) We have seen a major increase in blowups and bear clusters.**

Many individual stocks are getting absolutely crushed. We are seeing huge single-day drops (-7% +) across multiple names (115+ to be exact).

This kind of carnage beneath the surface is a big red flag. In fact, these kinds of extreme short-term “damage” days have **\*only\*** occurred during major Bear Markets since 2000.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Yl0j!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb902768-0e98-42b4-bd74-bd9d37031f6c_1749x984.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Yl0j!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb902768-0e98-42b4-bd74-bd9d37031f6c_1749x984.png)

**3.) We have seen a cluster of Hindenburg Omens (4+) in the last month.**

[A Hindenburg Omen](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-clusters) is a technical indicator that signals deterioration in the market’s health. Getting 4+ of these signals in a short window is rare, and historically precedes negative returns.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-S4r!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5e197af-92a5-4772-aed3-a43142ea7f82_1496x1778.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-S4r!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5e197af-92a5-4772-aed3-a43142ea7f82_1496x1778.jpeg)

*h/t subutrade*

**4.) Low correlation and high dispersion among stocks.**

Stocks are moving independently of each other, rather than together. This creates a choppy, unpredictable environment.

Historically, these type of low correlation/high dispersion markets tend to resolve to the downside. The median 2M return for the SPX after such incidences (99th %ile SPX dispersion) is -5.2%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Uiya!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb7dd68f-abf3-4579-912a-492ec9e0c68c_804x370.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Uiya!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb7dd68f-abf3-4579-912a-492ec9e0c68c_804x370.png)

**5.) The Mag 7 is struggling, sitting right at its 200 SMA.**

The very Mega Caps that drove this huge bull run are now sitting at critical support levels. If they break below, it is a very bad sign for the overall health of the market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kqe1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7abed1d6-c906-40a7-bf5a-10b158ace304_1267x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kqe1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7abed1d6-c906-40a7-bf5a-10b158ace304_1267x844.png)

The warning signs are present.

Anyone who has traded through various cycles quickly learns that it is too costly to ignore warning signs. I’ve seen way too many traders get stubborn and fight the tape when red flags emerge. It can be an account killer. So, I am personally very quick to exercise caution when I see red flags like these stacking up.

**The Bull Case**

At the same time, I want to keep an open mind on the market here. It’s important not to get too bearish **before** the indices actually break down.

There is definitely a Bull Case here as well.

To start off, the Fed is cutting rates into a growing economy with strong corporate earnings. Historically, equities don’t top when the Fed is cutting rates and there is no recession.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!90ch!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F55fe4fa4-0658-4469-965a-2e56d57d2d31_1215x847.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!90ch!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F55fe4fa4-0658-4469-965a-2e56d57d2d31_1215x847.jpeg)

Cycle tops typically come when the Fed is raising rates or when we're heading into a recession (like 2001 and 2022). Neither is the case right now.

Moreover, as I highlighted Monday in [“Whirlwind”,](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/whirlwind) there are still plenty of stocks in Stage 2 uptrends. We own 14 of them in our 2026 Portfolio right now (although a few of them are under pressure).

**Final Thoughts**

There are plenty of reasons for caution right now. The warning signs I laid out above are real, and I'm certainly not ignoring them. With that said, I am also keeping an open mind here.

Would it surprise anyone if the SPX broke this ~4 month range to the upside?

Think about it. The Fed is cutting rates, AI is changing the world, earnings remain strong, and there is no recession. This all sounds like a recipe for higher stock prices.

If the market does stabilize here and starts to move higher, I am currently compiling a list of names I think can run the hardest (again, market willing). These are names with strong fundamentals and healthy Stage 2 continuation patterns. I’m not ready to release the full list yet tonight, but I will leave everyone with the 1 stock at the top of my Watchlist right now. It is emerging as a clear leader coming out of the Q4 Earnings season, and could be actionable in the coming days (market willing).

**AEIS**

Advanced Energy Industries is an AI power company that provides precision power conversion and control solutions used in AI-driven data centers and advanced semiconductor equipment. The company just announced a tremendous quarter, and the stock is setting up for a potential ATH breakout.

Let’s start with the Q4 earnings. Advanced Energy reported Q4 revenue of $490M, driven by strength in semiconductor, industrial, and medical markets. The star of the report was Data Center revenue, which reached a record $178M (+101% YoY). In total (for FY 2025), revenue growth exceeded +20% YoY and EPS grew +70% YoY, while gross margin expanded 240 bps to 38.7% and operating cash flow hit a record high ($235M).

Advanced Energy also raised both Q1 ‘26 and FY guidance. The company guided to $500M in Q1 rev (versus consensus of $475M, ~5% above), while EPS guidance came in ~13% above consensus. Management now sees 2026 Data Center revenue growing above >30% (from 25-30%) and expects total revenue to grow in the high teens, with semiconductor business strengthening in the 2H driven by advanced logic and **memory capacity investments.**

Meanwhile, the stock is consolidating in a tight bull flag following these tremendous earnings results (+ guidance raise).

In my view, AEIS is a clear AI Power leader benefitting from growth in Data Centers and Memory. The stock is setting up for a post-earnings breakout. On a bull flag breakout, AEIS can see $400+. On a long entry, my stop would go below $292.
