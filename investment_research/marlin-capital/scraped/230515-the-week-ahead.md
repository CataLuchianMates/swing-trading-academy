# The Week Ahead

The Macro

Consensus estimates are currently projecting a -4% peak to trough EPS decline for this cycle.

This would be (by far) the shallowest EPS drop of any recession in the modern era.  Recessions since 1970 have averaged a -24% EPS decline peak to trough, with none shallower than -15%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IvQs!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F23bc7096-53e2-4627-8093-7c95016f46dd_624x327.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IvQs!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F23bc7096-53e2-4627-8093-7c95016f46dd_624x327.png)

Meanwhile, the Fixed Income market is pricing in rate cuts starting in September.  The odds of July rate cuts have risen to around 50%.  The Fixed Income market and equity markets remain disassociated based off current pricing.  It is rare for the Fed to cut rates with inflation so high and unemployment so low.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!CYca!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fead4ca95-a781-4d60-87bc-93129b1f8e74_850x508.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!CYca!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fead4ca95-a781-4d60-87bc-93129b1f8e74_850x508.png)

The current FOMC is not projecting any rate cuts this year, but the Fixed Income market sees some type of credit/major event (occurring before September) that forces the Fed to cut rates.  Meanwhile, the equity market is pricing in no recession/the mildest in 50+ years with a small blip in earnings.

Historically, the Fed has never hiked this much without a recession occurring.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oLhu!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fef27b68b-a986-4035-a22f-0738b75ef2bd_1131x477.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oLhu!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fef27b68b-a986-4035-a22f-0738b75ef2bd_1131x477.png)

Fed Chair Powell has said he still believes this time could be different, pointing to the continued strength of the labor market for his reasoning.  The equity market believes him, but the fixed income market does not.

Financial Conditions remain in check, even as regional banks are dropping one by one.  The Financial Conditions Index shows no signs of breaking out to the tightening side, which has allowed equities to stabilize YTD.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kT8D!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb7d7b74c-ce19-41e1-bcb0-a90cc5130cf5_1213x1171.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kT8D!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb7d7b74c-ce19-41e1-bcb0-a90cc5130cf5_1213x1171.png)

Positioning

Positioning is a tale of two sides.  Systematic funds remain “all in” while active managers remain “all out”.  Active managers refuse to take on any new risk in the current environment.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lXjE!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd00a13d7-7fd2-485d-9d48-81300adb7174_1233x921.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lXjE!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd00a13d7-7fd2-485d-9d48-81300adb7174_1233x921.png)

Meanwhile, systematic funds, which primarily employ trend following strategies, are long to the max.

Retail investors seem to be spooked by the current issues in the banking sector as they have been rotating money into money market funds and other high yield instruments.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3Ikl!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F43e7fa4d-f13e-4927-8a67-562d80ee9e63_1236x757.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3Ikl!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F43e7fa4d-f13e-4927-8a67-562d80ee9e63_1236x757.png)

Technicals

In regards to the current market landscape, one can make an argument for both sides of the coin.  Meanwhile, the S&P 500 has not moved at all since April.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sVZL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7fdf1abf-2fae-459d-b560-f2dba6874738_961x721.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sVZL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7fdf1abf-2fae-459d-b560-f2dba6874738_961x721.png)

The SPX closed the first trading day of April at $4124.  The SPX closed on Friday (5/12) at $4124.

As I noted in Friday’s morning notes (via SocGen), the S&P 500 YTD performance (+7%) is entirely based on 3 stocks – NVDA, MSFT, GOOGL.  Without these stocks, the S&P 500 is actually down -2% YTD.

If your trading strategy is tailored to choppy markets, 2023 has been your Super Bowl.  For me, it is easy to sit this action out until there is a more consistent direction across the board. Knowing your strategy, the current environment, and when to get aggressive/passive is the key to any successful trader.
