# Thursday Morning Notes 2.15

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Equities mostly picked back up where they left off pre-CPI yesterday with the S&P 500 finishing the day +0.96% and closing back above its 8 EMA.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jVmT!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2d02a603-d266-4772-9fa6-a59f7b9d0faa_1136x847.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jVmT!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2d02a603-d266-4772-9fa6-a59f7b9d0faa_1136x847.jpeg)

The Russell 2000 and China Internet names led the way yesterday.  Non-Profitable > Quality.  Most shorted > Least shorted.  Low quality themes were back at the top of the board with Bitcoin Sensitive equities +12%, Retail Memes +6%, Non-Profitable Tech +4%, etc.  The focus was on micro > macro (ETFs were 27% oft eh tape vs 37% on CPI day and a YTD avg of 32%).

More Macro catalysts to watch for the rest of this week – retail sales and jobless claims today, empire manufacturing, PPI, and option expiry on Friday.

PLTR – still holding half my long position.  The action was strong here yesterday with the stock bouncing right back to recent highs.  My stop remains below $21.5 and my big picture target on the remainder of this position is $30.
