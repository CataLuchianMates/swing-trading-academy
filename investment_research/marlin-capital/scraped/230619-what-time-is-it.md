# What Time Is It?

Is it time to position for a reversal in the recent S&P 500 rally?

With the S&P 500 continuing to soar further into bull market territory after its 20%+ rally from last October’s lows, nervousness surrounding an impending reversal, especially as we move closer to the typically quieter months of July and August, appear to be rising.

Following the most recent lag of this rally, many of the technicals and positioning metrics I track are starting to look very stretched.  For example, the S&P 500’s 14D RSI has hit 78, the most tactically overbought level in 3 years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!A8yh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F43b5207d-c342-40cc-80c8-a8f2358ba07e_728x359.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!A8yh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F43b5207d-c342-40cc-80c8-a8f2358ba07e_728x359.png)

While the RSI can always get more overbought, it is important to note that levels this high have often led to a pullback (at least in the short-term) in the past.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9Xzs!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F486e2e6a-0ec0-469f-b0e8-2cbeeda28b2f_744x431.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9Xzs!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F486e2e6a-0ec0-469f-b0e8-2cbeeda28b2f_744x431.png)

On the positioning side, while average investor positioning in US equities still only looks modestly overweight vs history, the increase in positioning we have seen over the last month has hit some extreme levels in the context of the last decade.

Over the past month, we have seen a sharp increase in positioning, with broad-based bullishness increasing across Hedge Funds, Retail, Asset Managers, CTAs, and ETF flows.

As shown in the chart below, the 4wk change in JPM’s Tactical Positioning Monitor (TPM) just reached the most extreme level we have seen in 7+ years (since early 2016).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!L6AY!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb31fd3a0-cdba-406b-be54-8fed3e2280c5_817x372.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!L6AY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb31fd3a0-cdba-406b-be54-8fed3e2280c5_817x372.png)

The obvious question that arises from this data is whether this presents a major risk to the market or not.  Looking back at how the S&P 500 performed after JPM’s 4wk TPM hit >1.5z+, it was roughly flat on average over the next 2 months.  The SPX tended to increase slightly in the short term before ultimately pulling back.

With that said, over the past 2 years, there has been a tendency for the S&P 500 to fall about 5% over the next 1-2 months, with most of the losses starting about 2.5 weeks after the TPM first hit +1.5z (which it first did on Friday, June 2nd).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9PlX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa56a40e4-e462-4b49-8199-cf885aaca4fa_814x349.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9PlX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa56a40e4-e462-4b49-8199-cf885aaca4fa_814x349.png)

Some of the other investor sentiment and positioning metrics I track are similarly showing a sharp increase in investor bullishness lately.  As I [mentioned last week,](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/price-drives-the-narrative) price action typically drives the narrative.  It is no surprise, then, that Goldman’s Sentiment Indicator, which measures stock positioning across retail, institutional, and foreign investors over the past 12 months, has reached an extreme Z-Score of 1.2 (indicating extremely stretched positioning).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JPPq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F40f45f68-d142-4019-a496-f806342f17d7_1024x998.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JPPq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F40f45f68-d142-4019-a496-f806342f17d7_1024x998.png)

Moreover, the AAII Net Bull Ratio recently rose +23%, the highest level in nearly 2 years.

The CBOE Put/Call Ratio also fell to a 1 year low of 0.68 last week.

In [“Price Drives the Narrative”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/price-drives-the-narrative), and publicly [on Twitter,](https://twitter.com/Marlin_Capital/status/1668734503731298309) I have discussed how both S&P 500 and IWM Call Volume have skyrocketed to new record highs.  My research shows that this record call option participation has come from both institutions and retail (and only 38% of last Thursday’s record call volume was from 0dte, with most transactions taking placed in longer contracts).

Specific to retail, small traders’ equity call option flows (Options customers trading <10 contracts) saw a sharp rebound during May and June.  This call option flow has increased substantially in recent weeks and is now hitting levels not seen since the S&P 500 topped (in January 2022) and the Bear Market began.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ku3h!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feabd3372-c3a6-455b-a264-60b8fc228c4f_459x387.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ku3h!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feabd3372-c3a6-455b-a264-60b8fc228c4f_459x387.png)

Meanwhile, the VIX hit 13.5 last week, its lowest level since February 2020 (a month before the market crashed due to the Covid-19 Pandemic).  This indicates a lack of fear by market participants.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OIme!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F02b8ebba-0314-4426-9aca-e31fb6b8f1b9_1005x651.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OIme!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F02b8ebba-0314-4426-9aca-e31fb6b8f1b9_1005x651.png)

Speaking of the VIX, Wall Street’s Fear Gauge has followed its traditional seasonality patterns very closely this year.  If this pattern continues, mid-late June and July are historically the least volatile months for stocks with quieter summer trading in full effect.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4tWk!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F16219b7c-cd25-464f-819f-4e05b723d872_502x328.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4tWk!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F16219b7c-cd25-464f-819f-4e05b723d872_502x328.png)

The overlay chart above suggests we could continue to see more subdued volatility during the next ~2 months or so before volatility picks up in late August/September.  If this is the case, a major reversal/significant correction period would be unlikely before then.

With so many technicals and positioning metrics now starting to look stretched, and the Fed/CPI catalysts now in the rearview mirror as we enter a quieter summer period, the question is - does it make sense to watch for a reversal in the indices here?

As shown through a number of historical backtests of the extreme positioning/sentiment signals cited above, the short term (subsequent 1M) returns for the market are modest after levels like current ones have been hit.  With that said, the 3M-6M returns appear fairly attractive when backtested.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!RPoS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F31561c36-5e76-44f8-b607-34404141e25e_745x385.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!RPoS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F31561c36-5e76-44f8-b607-34404141e25e_745x385.png)

Further, unlike April and May, the most recent leg of this rally has been supported by much stronger market breadth.  As shown below, we have seen a sharp increase in the percent of stocks in the market trading above their 50D/200D moving averages over the last month (from <30% to 70-90% levels).  This suggests the recent leg of the rally has had much wider participation across the market than was the case a couple of months ago.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-0xg!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fac1ab938-7dca-45fa-a4de-9b784beddb93_939x497.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-0xg!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fac1ab938-7dca-45fa-a4de-9b784beddb93_939x497.png)

**My Strategy:**

In my view, the risk/reward for new long entries up here is skewed to the downside.  Consequently, I am not planning to enter any new longs until some of the short term overbought/extreme readings are shaken off.

With that said, I continue to see no reason to try to time a major reversal when the market remains in an upwards trend, especially during a typically dull period for stocks and volatility.  As mentioned above, this lull period typically extends from now until mid-late August, which has historically resulted in lower volume and reduced volatility.

In my view, the manner in which the market and individual stocks pull back in response to the aforementioned overbought and extreme sentiment/positioning readings will provide valuable insight.  If the pullback exhibits constructive behavior and shows signs of holding support at key levels, I will be looking to add more long exposure.  The most ideal scenario would involve a rotation to YTD laggards (defensives, etc.) while the most overbought names and sectors (Mega Cap Tech, etc.) pull back and hold key support levels.
