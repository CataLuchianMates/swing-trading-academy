# Wednesday Morning Notes 12.18

**Daily Market Commentary:**

It’s December Fed Day. Global equities are choppy this morning ahead of the FOMC event with some reversion from recent price action (TSLA -2.5% and NVDA +3.7%).

The FOMC is widely expected to cut rates by 25bps today’s meeting (98% probability). More important will be the Fed’s commentary and Powell’s press conference at 2:30 PM EST. I expect the main message coming out of December’s meeting to be that the FOMC will likely slow the pace of rate cuts moving forward.

Currently, the market is pricing only a ~17% chance of a rate cut in January. This will be, in my view, the most important number to watch today from the FOMC as we should get more clarity on which way the Fed is currently leaning for January.

The S&P 500 is currently implying a 0.6% move today for the FOMC meeting (QQQ ~0.9% / IWM ~1.45%). This is the lowest for a Fed Day since the beginning of 2022 (average of the past 4 meetings has been ~0.9% IV for the SPX).

Active Portfolio -

No planned actions for me today. I am not looking to open any new positions ahead of this afternoon’s Fed meeting. All stops in place on existing positions and letting these trades play out.

If we have a positive market reaction coming out of today’s FOMC, I am seeing some new potential long setups emerge into year end. But, we will need to clear today’s hurdle first for me to be interested.
