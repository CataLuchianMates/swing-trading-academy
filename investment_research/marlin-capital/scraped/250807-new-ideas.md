**AMD**

Remember [during Q1 earnings](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-4-trade-ideas-from-q1-earnings?utm_source=publication-search), when I said sometimes it can be even more bullish for a stock to sell off and get bought right back up after earnings.

That would be the idea with this AMD setup. The stock was a longtime dog, but recently turned into a leading semi name. Now, let’s see if the post-earnings weakness gets bought right up and it retains its newfound leader status.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zpvw!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa568b46d-82d8-4ed6-9fc6-ecbcb1b1f30c_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zpvw!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa568b46d-82d8-4ed6-9fc6-ecbcb1b1f30c_1396x653.png)

Along those lines, we have a few recent leaders that are set to gap down on earnings tomorrow: JOBY, SYM, and TSSI. Let’s see how these stocks react to their gap downs tomorrow. Earnings gap downs are a great litmus test to gauge demand.

When a leading stock gaps down following earnings, you get a front-row seat to see how much buying demand really exists in the stock. Does the gap down flip momentum towards selling pressure, or will dip buyers step in to bid the stock right back up?

In terms of earnings gap ups tomorrow, DASH, BROS, and DUOL are my top 3 to watch.
