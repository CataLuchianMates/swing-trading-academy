I can’t imagine we will see Powell do anything to surprise the markets tomorrow, as this is simply not the way he has operated as Fed Chair.

There were no signs of a pause in the market rally to kick off the week yesterday, with the S&P 500 finishing +0.93% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!769q!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F40f62875-5b25-495f-a7f9-0f57b092bc51_958x721.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!769q!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F40f62875-5b25-495f-a7f9-0f57b092bc51_958x721.jpeg)

We are set for a continuation gap up this morning in the index after CPI numbers.

I will be looking to sell 1/4th of my ARKK long at today’s open, as we are set to open above my initial price target of $44. If we rally to $45 today, I will be looking to take another 1/4th off and leave half my position remaining.

For my IWO long, my initial price target is $243, where I will look to take 1/4th profits. My 2nd price target is $245 and YTD highs.
