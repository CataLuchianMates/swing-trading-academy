# The Setup into NVDA Earnings

While the SPX and NDX both registered new all-time highs last week, alongside their 4th weekly gains in a row, it was a bit of an unusual trading week as key Large Cap names lagged (META, AMZN, UBER all down on the week).

Volumes for the indices dried up late in the week, offset by a meaningful step up in activity in low priced stocks.  Last Thursday set a new record high in Off-Exchange volume with 52% of all US equities traded coming Off-Exchange.  This is commonly viewed as a proxy for retail trading activity and surpassed the prior retail peak (Meme Stock Mania) of January 2021 (~50%).

With this newfound optimism among retail traders, overall market sentiment is back in elevated territory.

The market backdrop remains upbeat as we head into a Micro catalyst driven week (led by NVDA earnings, also PANW, CRM, and SNOW).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!V0h_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbc9e72f0-95b7-447b-a021-d5d07e89323d_1290x720.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!V0h_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbc9e72f0-95b7-447b-a021-d5d07e89323d_1290x720.jpeg)

The market tehnicals remain favorable here (above all key EMAs, buybacks, fund flows, and retail) and the market temperature has become relatively accommodating for quality, secular assets (alongside easing inflation & growth).

Further, the earnings revision story for Tech (particularly Mega Cap Tech) remains best in class YTD.  Analysts have lowered 2024 EPS estimates for the S&P 493 by -2%, but lifted estimates for the Magnificent 7 by +8%, meaning the aggregate 2024 S&P 500 EPS forecast has been flat.  Investors have fully taken notice and are paying up for this growth, as P/E valuations of the 10 largest $SPX stocks remain elevated compared to the rest of the market (but still nowhere near Dot Com Bubble levels).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jkTk!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc085ebe8-2b70-4b26-9c2a-1c5fbcc5328b_989x694.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jkTk!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc085ebe8-2b70-4b26-9c2a-1c5fbcc5328b_989x694.jpeg)

With that said, NVDA still has to deliver this week and I view this earnings print as up there (or even more important) with a CPI or NFP print in terms of market focus/importance.

All eyes will be on NVDA’s earnings this Wednesday night.  The stock is up +250 since the last print and is currently sitting within a few percent of ATHs, while options are implying a 8-9% move on the print (~$200B mkt cap swing).  On 2 of the last 3 prints, NVDA barely moved, but last quarter was a little different as the stock sold of -9% into the print and gapped up big post-earnings.

It has been 1 year since NVDA’s blowout quarter (1 of the best quarters in Wall Street history, in my view), which ignited the AI led rally.  Since then, NVDA’s market cap is +1.5T, its LTM EPS +617% YoY, and mentions of AI are +186% during corporate earnings calls (as everyone tries to ride the backs of NVDA’s wave).

GS currently characterizes positioning in NVDA as 9/10, indicating that a simple beat/raise print may not be enough with such elevated expectations.

Heading into this week, I am currently long ONON, LSPD, JMIA, VITL, RMD, and ENVX after all these setups triggered.
