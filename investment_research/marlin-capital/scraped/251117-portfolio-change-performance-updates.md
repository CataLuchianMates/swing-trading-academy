#### **On Exiting:**

We sold 70% of the SMID Cap 6 portfolio [on 10/17](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/smid-cap-6-update) when the “risk off” warning signs first began to surface. Our 70% sell was more predictive in nature (for the reasons I talked about in [“Risk Off”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70?utm_source=publication-search)).

Friday’s exit sales were purely technical based. 5 out of our 6 holdings closed below their 10-week EMAs (with the exception of GRAL). We followed [our plan](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/wreckage) and closed out the whole portfolio on these sell signals. While short-term oversold (& we could certainly be nearing a technical bounce), I don’t really feel the need to risk more gains here into year-end. We sold 70% a month ago, and were holding the remaining 30% so long as we held above weekly support. We broke those key weekly support levels last week. So we followed our plan and exited. Most importantly, we were able to lock in another year of outperformance (+64% gains vs +14% YTD for SPX).

I’m going to keep harping on this Bill O’Neil quote because I very much share the same selling philosophy:

> **The common fear is “what if it goes up more without me?”** That may happen, but remember, your goal is not to sell at the very top price. That’s almost impossible to do. Rather, **you just want to participate in the majority of the move.”**
>
> - William O’Neil

Two further points on this 👇

1.) As O’Neil says, it is impossible to time the top. You are either going to be selling too early or too late. You are not going to sell at the very top. You must be at peace with that fact. There is no such thing as perfect market timing.

2.) In my view, the feeling of FOMO (ie “what if it goes up more without me?”) comes from a sense of dissatisfaction with your profits. As traders & investors, we have all felt this feeling at least once before.

Here’s a common scenario that causes FOMO: you made big gains on a % basis, but maybe you didn’t enter big enough (so your $ profits feel less than they should be). You nailed some huge winning stocks, but the $ profits do not feel like quite enough (because you didn’t buy enough at the right time). So to make up for it, you may be holding out for even more gains. The problem with that is, you are holding out for more gains at the wrong time. And now, you are taking heavy/unnecessary risk at the wrong time.

In my experience, the best way to remedy this is to simply learn and be prepared for next time (not to chase more profits at a questionable time). Next time, make sure you are taking heavy risk when the risk/reward skews positive. For the SMID Cap 6, the time to get heavy was in early June. When APLD was at $13. When NBIS was sub $50. When GRAL was at $39. We just ran for 5 straight months. 3/6 of our holdings hit >100% gains. The entire portfolio gained +64% in 5 months (and at 1 point, was >80% gains). And now, we have “risk off” market signals after an extended run. In my view, this is not the time to be getting heavy/greedy. The time for that has long past.

So my best advice to avoid FOMO, is to simply learn from your experiences so you are ready to fully capitalize for next time. There is always a next time. Be prepared for it. This means getting positioned before the major market rally, not after.

If you bought well and sat tight, you are probably thinking “I don’t want to lose my hard earned profits”, rather than “what if it goes up more without me and I miss it”. My point is, if you did everything right (getting heavy in early June and selling into the big run ups), than you should not feel any FOMO. If you are happy with your profits, than you should not feel FOMO. We made a lot of profits, outperformed, and achieved our goals with the SMID Cap 6 portfolio. If the market environment flips back to bullish tomorrow and all 6 of our stocks double in the next month, than so be it. You still can’t take those +64% gains away from us. No matter what happens from here, that is some real, big money we made in this portfolio. When I worked on the trading floor, I had a mentor that would always say “lock in profits, otherwise it’s **NOT** your money”. Now that we’ve fully sold, it’s fully our money.

#### General Market Analysis

The price action remained “risk off” across the board last week.

Bitcoin has already broken down well below its 200 -day MA (and 10 week MA), a huge overall “risk off” indicator. Historically, Bitcoin and the Nasdaq are highly correlated.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!S0Yp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F41cf850d-b80a-44c5-869a-189610d706f7_1553x679.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!S0Yp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F41cf850d-b80a-44c5-869a-189610d706f7_1553x679.png)

High beta/growth stocks are breaking down below their 10-weeks. Entire sectors are breaking down.

Market cycles take place at an accelerated pace nowadays (much faster than ever before). In Q1, we saw an entire Bear Market cycle take place in the span of ~2 months. The SPX dropped -21%, QQQ -26%, and most stocks fell upwards of -50%. This was an entire bear market cycle wrapped up into ~2 months.

In the old days, market cycles would seemingly play out over a much longer time frame. Of course, every cycle is different and we can still have long bear markets (like we did in 2022). The 2022 Bear Market Cycle took place over 9+ months for the indices, but many individual stocks were in Bear Cycles for 1-2 years. 2022 was a different animal, though. We had a economic recession (& earnings recession), and the Fed was raising rates at the fastest pace in decades to combat 50+ year high inflation. We had a lot of bearish catalysts for that prolonged 2022 downturn. We do not have that right now.

With that said, the exact reasons for the selloff do not really matter to me when the price action is what it has been (unequivocally “risk off”, with topping patterns across more speculative assets & crypto).

My big picture thesis remains the same here. In my view, we are in the overall latter stages of this Bull Market. For many stocks, the Bear cycle has already begun. This means they have more than likely topped for this cycle (names like OKLO, SMR, IONQ, QUBT, etc.). This is typically how Bear cycles begin, as not all stocks top at the same time. Topping is a process, not an event.

In recent market cycles, the indices (SPX, NDX) have typically had at least 1 more wave up after the most speculative stocks begin to top. I think we will continue on this pattern during this cycle. I do NOT believe the SPX and QQQ have topped, and I expect them to see new highs after this sell off.

With that said, I view this as the most challenging part of the market cycle to trade. Many stocks are in their bear phases already. Many other stocks are still in their bull phases. Even for the stocks that are still in their Bull phases, they are not trading with any rhythm due to overall “risk off” environment. Volatility is high and stocks are trading fast and loose. The daily ranges are too big for swing traders to be able to manage risk comfortably. Further, I believe the market remains vulnerable to a bigger drawdown so long as breadth remains weak. Eventually, they will come for the leaders (and they already have, to some degree).

For me, this is not the part of the market cycle where I am looking to get aggressive at all. This is the part of the market cycle where I prioritize risk management, because it is very easy to give back gains here. I’m sitting in 100% cash in my Active Trading Portfolio heading into this week. I continue to expect tough sledding and a difficult market environment ahead.
