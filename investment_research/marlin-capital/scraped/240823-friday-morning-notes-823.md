# Friday Morning Notes 8.23

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Jittery day for the market yesterday ahead of today’s Jackson Hole. The SPX finished -0.89% while the NDX closed -1.7%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wMbL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F99526a5c-756e-46f6-97c0-3a47fae397a4_1219x886.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wMbL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F99526a5c-756e-46f6-97c0-3a47fae397a4_1219x886.jpeg)

There is certainly some justified anxiousness out there given Powell’s past remarks at Jackson Hole have moved the market. The market clearly has today priced as a real event with the 1 day straddle currently implying a ~1% move. The SPX has been up 9 of the last 10 days and flows have shown longs being added in pockets of the market that had been unwound in July/early August.

I expect Powell to express some more confidence in the inflation outlook and to put a bit more emphasis on downside risks in the labor market than his July press conference, in light of the data released since then. A dovish surprise could be any hint of the Fed Funds rate is inappropriately high (opening the door for a 50bp cut in September). A hawkish surprise could be highlighting instead that broad financial conditions are quite easy.

We are set for a +0.9% gap up this morning in QQQ. I will not be looking to buy into this morning’s gap up with Powell on deck, but my guess is that today’s speech leans dovish given the labor market weakness in most recent data. This is now a bigger concern than inflation, and I expect Powell to emphasize that in today’s speech.

Active trading account - I am holding long AXON, SN, SHOP, and SPOT into Friday with stops in place.
