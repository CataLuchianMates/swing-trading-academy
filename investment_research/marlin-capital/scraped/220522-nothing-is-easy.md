# Nothing is Easy

The S&P 500 is now 18% below its late November highs.  The current decline, both by price and time, is consistent with the equity market pricing in an imminent recession.  In past reports, I have discussed the key reasons why I believe a recession must be fully discounted by the market before we can see a bottom for this cycle.

The -18% selloff we have seen in the S&P 500 is well beyond the usual range of “non-recessionary” corrections.  The current selloff is now approaching the “recession median” correction of 24%.

Additionally, the current market selloff is already into its 4th month and approaching durations only seen during recessions.  If history is any indication, this Bear Market is only about 1/3rd of the way to the finish line in duration.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uPGI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8776f171-8234-4dca-9902-fd765b1d0cbe_877x464.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uPGI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8776f171-8234-4dca-9902-fd765b1d0cbe_877x464.png)

The key question of course becomes, “When is a recession fully priced into the market?”  In our previous 4 Bear Markets, US stocks bottomed with the S&P 500 trading at or below 14x forward earnings.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0S7J!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9ab377e0-e612-4c80-97bd-733fb250b302_891x415.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0S7J!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9ab377e0-e612-4c80-97bd-733fb250b302_891x415.png)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2rIh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F634bfddb-8d69-4f47-8bbb-a9b48f6f52f3_419x145.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2rIh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F634bfddb-8d69-4f47-8bbb-a9b48f6f52f3_419x145.png)

The S&P 500 currently trades near 16.5x forward earnings, suggesting stocks are not yet cheap enough to have discounted the worst.  It should also be noted that the “Earnings” part of the equation is not set in stone.  In fact, analysts are becoming more uncertain about future earnings, indicating there could be more earnings cuts to come. The last 3 times we have seen such uncertainty among analysts coincides with the last 3 major Bear Markets in US stocks (Dot Com Bust, GFC, Covid Crash).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bz7l!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F42dfb683-dae6-46db-ae72-d36aab65c907_1125x981.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bz7l!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F42dfb683-dae6-46db-ae72-d36aab65c907_1125x981.png)

This is important to note because a lower “Earnings” denominator equates to a more expensive Forward P/E Ratio for the S&P 500.  If we assume analysts are not yet done cutting their forward earnings estimates, the S&P 500 is likely still trading above a 17x multiple (despite the official number).

Financial conditions have tightened considerably in recent months, as seen below:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LAP-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff95c7b7e-c0db-4cc9-bc76-a814338dc02f_1100x840.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LAP-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff95c7b7e-c0db-4cc9-bc76-a814338dc02f_1100x840.png)

This is a good start, but financial conditions are likely to continue getting tighter throughout this year.  Former Fed President Bill Dudley discussed his thoughts in a recent Op-ed:

> “The Fed has to be happy with the fact that financial conditions have tightened…they’re getting traction…they still have to do what they said they’re going to do.”

Despite the fact that financial conditions have already tightened substantially, the Fed must follow through with their plan, or they risk undoing all their progress so far.

More important for stock investors to note is that equity prices are one of the 4 components of the Financial Conditions Index.  In laymen’s terms, this means a rising stock market would work against the Fed’s quest to tighten financial conditions.  Yes, this also means a declining stock market would be beneficial for the Fed’s current goals.  This is also why the Fed will likely use any rally in equities as an opportunity to get even more hawkish, essentially capping the upside of the market.

This is precisely why I remain confident that a “Powell Pivot” is still a long way off.  In fact, Fed Chair Powell issued another beatdown for US stocks last week with his hawkish comments, even as the S&P 500 approached official Bear Market territory:

> “No one should doubt our resolve to do that (reduce CPI to 2%) …There could be some pain involved.”

These comments are reminiscent of Fed Chair William McChesney Martin’s comments in 1969, as the Fed attempted to lower the then 6% inflation rate:

> “We’re going to have a good deal of pain and suffering before we can solve these things.”

In my view, Jerome Powell cannot pivot until the Fed’s job is complete, unless something “breaks” in the economy first.  So, what does this mean for the stock market? In my view, it means equities are likely to remain under pressure until the market is comfortable that the Fed has a handle on inflation.  This could be a grueling process.

Now that we’ve established the Fed is unlikely to pivot anytime soon, the question is, “How low can stocks go”?  As mentioned above, I believe the S&P 500 Fwd P/E Ratio will continue to contract until the multiple sits at under 14x.  For the S&P 500, this would mean a move to the $2900-$3300 range, as discussed in [“Let’s Talk About It”.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/lets-talk-about-it?s=w)

**Bottom Signals?**

Below, I highlight some other key signals I am watching for clues as to where this Bear Market might bottom.

First off, I believe we need to see a significant flight to safe assets such as US Treasuries.  Legendary investor Howard Marks emphasizes the importance of knowing where investor appetite towards risk stands at all times.  The below chart is a good barometer of where investor risk appetite stands.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!337G!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F763a4604-68bb-4e8f-bb69-912d088a3834_731x499.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!337G!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F763a4604-68bb-4e8f-bb69-912d088a3834_731x499.png)

Investors have certainly become more risk averse during this selloff, but we are still not even close to the levels seen during the 2018 rate shock and March 2020 Covid crash.  One clear sign of capitulation is a rush to safe assets at the expense of risky assets.  We are not there yet, but I will continue to monitor and post these flows moving forward.

Another key sign to watch is the number of net new highs/lows.  Last week, the number of net new 52-week highs on the NYSE fell to -560.  This reading is consistent with a minor market correction, but is still far from extreme.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rrKm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd594632d-66b2-4297-bf02-f1313a8dbf14_1200x776.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rrKm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd594632d-66b2-4297-bf02-f1313a8dbf14_1200x776.png)

We are starting to see this play out, as the selling extended heavily into retail stocks last week.  One by one, the selling pressure is rotating through industry group after industry group.  In the end, no stock is likely to escape the Bear unscathed.

Another sign to look for in capitulation selling, or climax selling, is a VIX spike above 45.  The VIX is Wall Street’s “Fear Gauge”.  Since the product was invented, no major market selloff or Bear Market has bottomed without the VIX spiking above 45 at least once.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sB2c!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fca66ec6f-f70b-42ec-b73e-f57ad88c1172_1329x625.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sB2c!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fca66ec6f-f70b-42ec-b73e-f57ad88c1172_1329x625.png)

Additionally, while investor sentiment is highly depressed, one must consider the problems of “sentiment indicators”. The way in which investors are feeling does not matter until those feelings turn into actions. Overall equity positioning is down significantly, but does not indicate any type of major panic outside of a typical market correction.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mnRc!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0078b089-f047-47f9-abde-d7e623657710_994x617.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mnRc!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0078b089-f047-47f9-abde-d7e623657710_994x617.png)

**Summary**

* Equities are likely to continue to remain under pressure until the Fed regains control over inflation. My best guess would be a CPI reading below 5% before the market starts to feel comfortable.
* A recession is in the process of being priced into markets.  My best guess for the price at which a recession would be fully discounted is the $2900-$3300 range for the S&P 500.
* History says we are only about 1/3rd of the way through the median Bear Market in duration.  Counter-trend rallies are a large part of what makes Bear Markets drag on.  We have only had 1 major one so far, but history says we will have more to come.
* Bottoming signals are not flashing yet.
