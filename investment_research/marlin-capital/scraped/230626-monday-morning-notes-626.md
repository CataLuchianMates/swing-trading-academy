# Monday Morning Notes 6.26

Technical Update

S&P 500 futures are largely unchanged this morning after weekend headlines were dominated by the Russian turmoil with the Wagner Group and what it could mean for Putin/the war in Ukraine. The market pulled back from heavily overbought conditions last week, closing below its 8 EMA for the first time in over a month.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gqwz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2349c2e6-f0c4-4813-9402-e1a855725268_965x726.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gqwz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2349c2e6-f0c4-4813-9402-e1a855725268_965x726.jpeg)

My target on this pullback for the SPX has been the 21 EMA, at minimum ($4300). We open Monday’s trading just 40 points away (~1%) from this key support level. If the 21 EMA breaks decisively, a further pullback to the 60 EMA could be in the cards. The SPX has been supported by the 60 EMA all year, excluding the week before the Fed bailout of SVB and regional banks. This Fed bailout has marked the SPX bottom for 2023, which is no surprise when considering it marked the end of the QT cycle thus far.

Top Long Trade Idea for this week:

Long MDB over $397. Stop below $380 (4.3% risk)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!liKu!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F013717ef-60ee-438f-bc11-c757d8295808_958x717.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!liKu!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F013717ef-60ee-438f-bc11-c757d8295808_958x717.jpeg)

MDB is 1 of the strongest looking charts after strong earnings results propelled the stock higher. It is consolidating/flagging nicely and showing great relative strength.

Friday’s candle was an inside day candle. My long entry will be a break above Friday’s highs ($397) with a stop below Friday’s lows.
