# What's Next?

The Nasdaq is now officially in a Bear Market, down 21% from its November peak.  The technicals paint an ugly picture, with an h-sell pattern beginning to play out.  The composite is now at its last line of support, while net lows continue to dominate.  A breakdown looks imminent here.

Of course, in a brutal Bear Market, it is entirely possible we break those support levels, as we did in the Dot Com Bust and GFC.  Still, if you have a long term investment account where you can stomach some short-term volatility, these are potential support area I look to buy into.

Another aspect that I have discussed in the past is the possibility of a longer Bear Market, where there is no V-shaped bottom that we have become accustomed to.  The average Bear Market, since 1929, has lasted a duration of 20.6 months (on average).  Currently, we are about 4 months into the Bear in the Nasdaq (12+ months in ARKK, many growth names).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mFNI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe233a239-b56a-4829-9ec9-23c767e08938_544x291.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mFNI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe233a239-b56a-4829-9ec9-23c767e08938_544x291.png)

The reason a longer duration Bear Market is becoming a higher probability scenario is because of the deteriorating Macro picture.  I have talked much about the Macro picture in the past, and my skepticism that the Fed will be able to tame inflation without creating a recession.  The Fixed Income market seems to agree with that statement, as the 10yr/2yr Yield Curve is getting dangerously close to inversion.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qqmL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F64f2740d-1937-4958-9ff5-060bc6d51f0a_1167x435.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qqmL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F64f2740d-1937-4958-9ff5-060bc6d51f0a_1167x435.png)

The yield curve inversion has preceded each of the last 6 recessions dating back to 1980, with no false positives.  It has typically preceded a recession by 8-24 months.  A yield curve inversion in 2022 now seems inevitable.

Meanwhile, the geopolitical situation continues to cause more and more stress to the global economy.  Unfortunately, as is evident in all time high gas prices in the US, the situation will only exacerbate inflation.  The Fed must raise rates quick, or they raise the risk of facing a potential recession without much room to cut.

Valuations have certainly taken a sharp dive since post-Covid peaks, but we are still not yet at previous “cheap multiples”.  Let’s take a look at software stocks, for example.

The current EV/NTM revenue multiple in the software industry is 9.2x, according to Clouded Judgment’s Jamin Ball.  In 4 previous Saas corrections highlighted below, we bottomed at an average of 6.3x.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qYC5!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff761bb72-c733-48fd-ae4d-d35298630609_1329x931.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qYC5!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff761bb72-c733-48fd-ae4d-d35298630609_1329x931.png)

The most important comparison here is 2018, in my opinion.  In 2018, software stock multiples bottomed at 7.1x.  If we were to follow a similar path here, multiples would compress another 23% or so.  In previous writeups, I have compared today’s Macro backdrop to 2018, with both years having the combination of an aggressive Fed tightening year and QT.  However, 40 year high inflation and the economic fallout of a war in Europe adds two distinct challenges for the Fed to navigate here in 2022.

Finally, I believe we are yet to see the type of climax selling typically associated with major market bottoms. The climax selling indicator and panic indicators remain low, as seen below.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1G8e!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5055566b-bea3-4036-a651-68a3e953d708_695x513.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1G8e!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5055566b-bea3-4036-a651-68a3e953d708_695x513.png)

We have not yet seen capitulation selling in the market.  The percentage of Nasdaq stocks above their 200 day MA is currently at 16.5%.  We typically see this number drop below 15% in capitulation moves.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!42mQ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F755bb4cb-50e3-46b5-b689-54003805599b_900x396.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!42mQ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F755bb4cb-50e3-46b5-b689-54003805599b_900x396.png)

Another sign to look for in capitulation selling, or climax selling, is a VIX spike above 45.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zndt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7de423ee-4625-428d-937e-b153d742c39e_1595x977.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zndt!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7de423ee-4625-428d-937e-b153d742c39e_1595x977.png)

A final sign to look for in capitulation selling is a 90% downside day.  A 90% downside day is when 90% of all stocks in an index decline.  When there is a rush to the exits, everything is sold or puked out. I believe we will see a complete washout day like this in the not too distant future.
