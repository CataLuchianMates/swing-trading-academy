# Tuesday Morning Notes 5.2

Technical Update

The S&P 500 made an intraday high of $4186 yesterday before reversing and closing -0.04% on the day. Yesterday’s high was 9 points below the YTD high of $4195.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sovQ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F635c7c65-8bd7-483d-892d-bf63f82f7f48_958x724.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sovQ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F635c7c65-8bd7-483d-892d-bf63f82f7f48_958x724.jpeg)

Technically, this is a bullish looking chart for the S&P 500. This is despite fewer groups keeping up (see below) and the equal weighted SPX lagging behind.

Yesterday’s action was fairly muted and came on low volume. We may see more of the same today as investors await tomorrow’s FOMC decision.

Key resistance level to watch above include $4190-$4200 (YTD highs).

Key support levels to watch below include $4150-$4160, $4130, $4100, $4040-$4030 and $4000.
