# Thursday Morning Notes 7.25

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Huge sell off yesterday with the S&P 500 breaking a 356 day streak without a -2% (or greater) downside day – the longest streak since 2007 (943 days).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6aNf!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5fbc2676-8d61-4282-8a45-78cf6541e3ec_961x718.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6aNf!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5fbc2676-8d61-4282-8a45-78cf6541e3ec_961x718.jpeg)

The SPX fell -2.3%, its worst day since Dec ’22.  The NDX fell -3.7%, its worst day since Oct ’22.

The NDX closed at lows of the day, taking the index down to ~8% below ATHs.  For context, the April ’24 drawdown was ~8% and the Oct ’23 drawdown was -11%.

The Mag 7 declined -6% (worst session since Nov ’22), AI winners fells -5% to -10%, and the VIX closed above >18 for the first time since April.

Yesterday had a different type of feel to it and could certainly lead to an extended flush lower.  While tough to assign a silver bullet for the price action, the main catalysts appear to be (1) underwhelming EPS reports from TSLA (-12%) and GOOGL (-5%) vs. high expectations; (2) lack of incremental AI spending expectations form GOOGL/TSLA (CapEx guides were not raised); (3) Order growth deceleration from AI leader VRT (-13%), which weighed on other AI infrastructure plays (NVDA -7% / AVGO -7.5% / DELL -8%); (4) and continued downside momentum from heavy rotational pressure out of Big Tech the last 2 weeks.

Technically, the damage is done as the QQQ broke and closed below its 60 EMA for the first time since April.   We got a bearish 8/21 EMA cross, with the 8 EMA closing below the 21 EMA for the first time April.  Next up in the way of key support to watch for the QQQ is $457-$460 (100 EMA level).  14d RSI is at 36 so we are starting to get oversold, but we can always go lower (as we did in April).

The SPX is a bit stronger technically, still above its 60 EMA ($5400), which is the key support level to watch today.
