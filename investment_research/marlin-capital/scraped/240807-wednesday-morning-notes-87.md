# Wednesday Morning Notes 8.7

**Daily Market Commentary:**

US stocks rebounded yesterday as investors took a pause on some Macro concerns.  The SPX finished +1.04%, NDX +1.02%, and RTY +1.23%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FMv4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc2c5cc90-7b47-46a6-9eea-4083bb7d19ab_958x713.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FMv4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc2c5cc90-7b47-46a6-9eea-4083bb7d19ab_958x713.jpeg)

While some of last week’s Macro data points certainly raised some concerns, there seemed to be a pretty large overreaction early in the week (i.e. people calling for emergency rate cuts).

While the Macro data coming in is clearly weakening and increasingly surprisingly to the downside, it does not call for any type of emergency action by the Fed.  In my mind, it falls way short of that.

Hedge Funds were buyers of Monday’s panic dip.  According to GS Prime, US stocks saw their largest 1-day net buying in 5 months.  8 off 11 sectors were net bought, led by Tech and Defensives (Health Care, Staples, Utilities).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_ldB!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fddd903fc-78ec-4313-9d07-08bfb8409653_419x336.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_ldB!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fddd903fc-78ec-4313-9d07-08bfb8409653_419x336.png)

In all, JPM estimates that institutional investors poured in $14B of net buying on Monday.

Consumer Discretionary, Real Estate, and Financials were the only sectors net sold on Monday.

Nearly all Tech subsectors were net bought by Hedge Funds, led by Semis, Semi Equipment, and Software.  The Prime Book is still underweight Tech by -12.6% vs. the SPX (around the most underweight in more than 10yrs), but they have been using this dip as an opportunity to add back to Tech.

On the other hand, retail traders were aggressive net sellers on Monday.  Much of the selling flow was concentrated in the first hour of trading (which was the low).  The retail order imbalance ended the day at -$1B (-2.5z below the 12M average).

In my mind (and as I stated yesterday), the VIX has very likely topped (barring a huge black swan event).  I would look for a higher low to be formed in the indices and would use that as a buying opportunity.  My focus will be earnings gappers.  I have been keeping a list of “the best of the best” earnings gappers – names that announced great earnings/guidance this quarter and held up the best through the recent market volatility.  Below is my full list as of now:
