# The Trend Is Your Friend

Many of my most [recent reports](https://marlincapital.substack.com/archive?sort=new) at Marlin Capital have centered around Inflation, the Macro picture, the Monetary Policy landscape, etc. These are the issues that have plagued the US equity market all year.

My views on such topics have not changed, so there is no need to “beat a dead horse” in this week’s report. Instead, this week’s report will be focused on certain actionable insights that I will be using for stocks coming out of a Bear Market.

Patience is a requirement for success as a stock trader.  There are times to be very aggressive and focus on capital growth, and times to be very conservative and focus on capital preservation.  There are times to be incredibly active, and times to do absolutely nothing.  It is critical for a successful trader to know which market conditions are most and least favorable for their specific trading strategies.

As a Swing Trader, I primarily look to follow established trends.  Legendary investor Paul Tudor Jones is a diehard proponent of trend following:

> “I teach an undergrad class at the University of Virginia, and I tell my students, “I’m going to save you from going to Business School. Here, you’re getting a hundred-grand class, and I’m going to give it to you in two thoughts, okay? You don’t need to go to Business School, you’ve only got to remember two things. The first is, you always want to be with whatever the predominant trend is. You don’t ever want to be a contrarian investor.””

The first step in successful trend following is identifying the predominant trend.  There are many technical indicators I use to help me do this, but I am going to simplify things for my readers below.

**Medium Term Trend:**

60 EMA

**Long Term Trend:**

200 Day Moving Average (SMA)

In an uptrend, the stock price should be **above** its:

* 60 EMA,
* 200 SMA,
* AND the Stock’s 60 EMA should be **above** its 200 SMA.

In a downtrend, the stock price should be **below** its:

* 60 EMA,
* 200 SMA,
* AND the Stock’s 60 EMA should be **below** its 200 SMA.

This has not stopped many pundits from trying to call a “bottom” along the way.  Such predictions mean very little to me, because my strategy as a trader is not to perfectly time the “bottom”.  My strategy is to let the trend develop, then jump on and ride the momentum.  Legendary trader Jesse Livermore best captures my sentiment on “bottom fishing” in [“Reminisces of a Stock Operator”](https://www.amazon.com/Reminiscences-Stock-Operator-Edwin-Lef%C3%A8vre/dp/0471770884):

> “One of the most helpful things that anybody can learn is to give up trying to catch the last eighth or the first. These two are the most expensive eighths in the world. They have cost stock traders, in the aggregate, enough millions of dollars to build a concrete highway across the continent.”

So then, when is my “ideal time” to buy for a Swing Trade? To answer this question, I must talk a bit about Stan Weinstein’s “Stage Analysis”. Stan Weinstein is a legendary trader who became famous for his stage analysis trading strategy, which he talks about in his book “[Secrets for Profiting in Bull and Bear Markets](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1556236832?ie=UTF8&tag=nextbigtrade-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=9325&creativeASIN=1556236832)”.

Stage Analysis centers around the premise that assets move in cycles.  These cycles contain boom and bust phases. No asset moves in one direction forever.  These cycles also contain periods of consolidation.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IzQU!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8a140162-701e-465a-b546-f5899e44ebcd_490x232.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IzQU!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8a140162-701e-465a-b546-f5899e44ebcd_490x232.png)

Stage Analysis is a useful tool for traders to study and familiarize themselves with. I will give a quick summary of each of the stages below.

**Stage 1: Basing Stage**

In this stage, the stock is consolidating or basing with no defined trend or direction.  The stock is chopping around in a general price range.

**Stage 2: Advance Stage**

This is the phase where the stock breaks out of its consolidation/basing pattern and begins to advance.  The trend flips to a defined uptrend.

**Stage 3: Topping Stage**

This is the phase where the stock begins to top out after its advance.  This can occur very quickly, or over a longer period of time.  In any case, the uptrend will be under pressure at this point.

**Stage 4: Decline Stage**

In this stage, the stock begins to decline after topping out.  The trend flips to a defined downtrend.

In the context of this analysis, my ideal time to buy for a Swing Trade (for stocks coming out of a Bear Market) is during the Stage 1 breakout/at the beginning of Stage 2.

Until more stocks start hitting my ideal buy points, as highlighted above, I will largely remain a spectator. In my professional opinion, the real money in stocks is made in buying and holding during the Stage 2 Advance Phase. This is where I make my living as a trader. In the meantime, my main focus is preserving capital and taking certain low risk counter-trend entries as they present themselves. A trader must never be in a rush to make money, especially if the market conditions are not conducive to their strategy.

**Summary Points**

* My mindset as a trader right now: waiting for the right pitch.  The real money in stocks is made in buying and holding stocks during the Stage 2 Advance Phase.  This is the time to get aggressive.  There is no other time I look to get aggressive.
* Right now, this is a Day Trader’s market.  The conditions remain mostly unfavorable for Swing Traders.  We are potentially seeing the beginnings of certain Stage 1 bases across the market.  For me, this continues to mean patience.
* My ideal “Buy Point” for swing longs is during the Stage 1 breakout/at the beginning of Stage 2, with a confirmed uptrend flip.  We are not seeing these indicators flip yet, even in the names/ETFs that have bounced the most.
* I will continue to remain focused on counter-trend and short term trades until the conditions improve.  These are typically smaller trades with tighter stops.
