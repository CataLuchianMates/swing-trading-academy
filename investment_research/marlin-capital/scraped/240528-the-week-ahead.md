# The Week Ahead

After 4 straight weeks of gains, the Dow Jones suffered its worst week since March 2023 last week.  The Russell 2000 index of small caps also saw its first weekly loss in the last 5.  The Nasdaq sharply outperformed on the week (hitting a new record, 5th straight week of gains) led by another blowout quarter for NVDA, while the S&P 500 managed to rally on Friday to finish green for the week.

NVDA saved the tech heavy indices after beating on estimates and rallying over 9% on Thursday (adding $220B in market cap).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QFXH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F84975b0e-08c2-4e68-991c-5a645ff62c8e_1203x882.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QFXH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F84975b0e-08c2-4e68-991c-5a645ff62c8e_1203x882.jpeg)

The company posted quarterly revenues of $26B, +18% QoQ and +260% YoY.  Operating margin reached 65%, up 3 percentage points over the previous quarter.  Net income reached a new record $14.9B in Q1, and increase of 628% YoY, and the company announced a 10x1 stock split (effective June 7th).

According to estimates, NVDA currently controls over 75% of the AI chip market (on 80% gross margins).  NVDA is now trading at a $2.5T valuation for the first time in its history, larger than the market caps of Tesla and Amazon combined.  The company is also larger than the entire German stock market.

When NVDA made its Bear Market low in October 2022, the company had a market cap of $280B and Fwd P/E of 26x.  Today, NVDA is worth $2.5T and has a Fwd P/E of 38x.  Along that 18 month path, their earnings and free cash flow grew roughly 10x.  Following last week’s rally, NVDA is still trading 13% below its 3-year median on NTM P/E.

Big picture, I would argue that the Q1 reporting period justifies the claim that the Magnificent 7 remains the most durable collection of stocks in the world.  Along those lines, the 10 largest stocks in the S&P 500 currently trade at a 25x P/E multiple compared to 18x for the S&P 490.

Moreover, the 10 largest S&P 500 companies represent 26% of the S&P 500’s total earnings (a 14 year high).  The market caps have followed suit.

A related point on the dominance of the Mag 7 – AAPL just announced a $110B buyback program in its Q1 report.  If you strip out the Magnificent 7 from the index, the average market cap for the S&P 493 is just $65B.  Yes, you read that right.  AAPL’s stock buyback program is 169% larger than the average S&P 493 company.  For Bears of Mega Cap tech, good luck fighting against those market forces.

Another bullish sign for the rest of the trading year – success breeds more success.  May 23rd was the 100th trading day of 2024 and the S&P 500 rallied 10.4% through 100 days.  When the SPX is up 10% or more over the first 100 days of the year, the rest of the year is up 76% of the time for an average return of 7.1% (9.3% median).  This would equate to $5640 to $5750 for the SPX into year end.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2z2Z!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7c56e017-2fa7-4872-a365-a2fe347e2d9d_1231x984.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2z2Z!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7c56e017-2fa7-4872-a365-a2fe347e2d9d_1231x984.jpeg)

In election years, the historical data gets even stronger.  When the SPX trades higher over the first 100 days of a Presidential election year, the rest of the year tends to be strong with the index up 93% of the time for an average return of 10.1% (SPX $5800) and a median return of 8.9% (SPX $5730).
