## David Marlin Q&A (Comments)

**Q:** Hello David! Saying "0 reason to fight the fed" is in contradiction with "0 reason to fight the rally" imo. The Fed wants inflation down to 2%, is opened to 2 million job loss, wants to see rates higher or held for longer, or financial conditions tightening via more restrictive bank lending standards. Can you explain how this rally is not somehow defying the fed goal?

**David Marlin:** It is more important to watch what the Fed does, rather than what they say.  The Fed’s actions since March have had an easing effect on financial conditions.  The financial conditions chart I posted shows how FCI have actually been easing since October.  The Fed has had opportunities to pull the strings tighter since then, but has elected not to do so each time. 

This is why, despite the Fed’s tough talk on everything you mentioned, the market simply doesn’t believe them and is pricing in an 89% chance of rate cuts by the end of 2023.  This is because, despite the tough Fed talk, the market expects the Fed to fold at the first sign of distress (similar to the SVB and regional banking crisis).  In fact, the market is currently questioning whether or not the Fed will raise rates again this year.  This is why I wrote “we do not know what the Fed will do”, as there is a disconnect between what they have said and what the market is pricing, speaking to the “credibility issue” the committee has.
