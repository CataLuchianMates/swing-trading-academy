# Friday Morning Notes 2.17

Technical Update

Technically, yesterday’s close is a bearish one for the market. The S&P 500 finished the day -1.38%, closing below $4100 (and right at the $4090 support line).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6uzA!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F05d735e7-93e6-4de8-a862-7ce37ef8a0dd_1231x937.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6uzA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F05d735e7-93e6-4de8-a862-7ce37ef8a0dd_1231x937.jpeg)

We are set for a continuation gap down this morning following yesterday’s bearish close. The SPX is set to open below $4090 support and right at/near the inside week low ($4060).

Caution is warranted here. The SPX has spent basically the entire year above its 21 EMA, but we are set to open below the blue line this morning. Further, we have the first bearish MACD cross in the index since early December.

If the $4060 support level breaks, I will be looking for a further pullback to $4000 and the 60 EMA at $3995. In my view, it is still too early to get super bearish here technically, but we have the beginnings of a potential topping pattern. A bearish close today could confirm the pattern.

Key support levels below include $4060, $4020-$4030, and $3995-$4000.

Key resistance levels ahead include $4090-$4100 (previous support) and $4140-$4150.
