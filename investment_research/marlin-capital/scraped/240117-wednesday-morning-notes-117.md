# Wednesday Morning Notes 1.17

**Daily Market Commentary:**

US stocks kicked off this holiday shortened week with an uninspiring and choppy session on Tuesday with the SPX finishing -0.37% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Aoc-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F25723b7a-cd99-443f-96ac-b1462782f7a9_952x699.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Aoc-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F25723b7a-cd99-443f-96ac-b1462782f7a9_952x699.jpeg)

We saw a continuation of strength in select Mega Caps and Semis (mainly NVDA, MSFT, and AMD).  AI and Expensive Software led the way while China ADRs, Renewables, and Liquid Most Short were the weakest performers.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ET4a!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1d5955fa-1dc8-4977-b338-29c81674ba4f_1154x545.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ET4a!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1d5955fa-1dc8-4977-b338-29c81674ba4f_1154x545.jpeg)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Dmlr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fefa5463c-0071-42ac-b14a-4385829dfec2_1058x490.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Dmlr!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fefa5463c-0071-42ac-b14a-4385829dfec2_1058x490.jpeg)

Remarks by Fed’s Waller were not well received by the market – “no reason to move as quickly, cut as rapidly as in past”.  There was only a small move in market assumed March cut expectations (now ~68% likelihood of March cut), but a slowing pace of cuts is something the market is now warming up to.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SU3R!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F87576371-7c4b-46d2-9813-ef546fc9fe36_1058x477.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SU3R!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F87576371-7c4b-46d2-9813-ef546fc9fe36_1058x477.jpeg)

Waller not pushing for imminent cuts – “time will tell” – certainly opens up the door for a 1st cut to come later than March and also for the pace of cuts to be quarterly, rather than 3 (or more) consecutive cuts that the market is anticipating.

The idea of a slower pace of cuts is something the market is now considering.  Case in point – the RTY is lower 10 out of the last 12 sessions while the equal weighted S&P 500 (RSP) is suffering significantly more damage than the SPX.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0l3f!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb1eab400-f453-4e21-b7f2-3a741b862821_978x720.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0l3f!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb1eab400-f453-4e21-b7f2-3a741b862821_978x720.jpeg)

The US Empire Manufacturing Index declined by much more than expected in January to the lowest level in the series’ history outside of April 2020.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!CZVu!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F49b515c7-bb5f-42d4-a38c-6d5887e8f847_2048x923.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!CZVu!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F49b515c7-bb5f-42d4-a38c-6d5887e8f847_2048x923.jpeg)

The composition of the report was weak on net, with declines in new orders and shipment components and a small increase in the employment component.  While this data point is not necessarily the one to flip the market trend, especially while initial claims still hover near multi-year lows, it’s not a data point that makes you want to step in and immediately buy a broader market that’s currently trading at 20x PE (ranking in the 87th percentile vs history).

**Potential Earnings Gapper Trade Setup –**

PI stock is set for a +13% gap up this morning after releasing upbeat preliminary Q4 Results (+5% revenue guidance raise).  I will be looking for an earnings gapper entry on this one in the first 30 minutes after market open.
