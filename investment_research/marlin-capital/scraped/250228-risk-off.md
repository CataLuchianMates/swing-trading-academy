# Risk Off

Huge risk off moves.

That’s really the best way to describe the price action in the stock market right now.

I [talked on Sunday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/red-flags-emerge) about the flow dynamics being very bearish here. Institutions were selling while retail was buying (with demand fading).   
  
Institutions have continued to sell this week. Some new data in from Goldman Prime this morning: Hedge Funds have de-risked at the 2nd highest level on record in February (behind only the Jan ‘21 Meme Stock Mania).

Retail turned to net sellers on Monday and Tuesday (-$1B, -$428M) before returning to net buyers on Wednesday (+$1.2B). I will get the data in for Thursday’s bloodbath soon, but I’m guessing we saw plenty of retail selling.  
  
With institutions already selling and now retail beginning to sell following record high inflows, it creates the perfect storm for the market pullback we’ve seen.  
  
For the QQQ, we sliced through the 50-day MA on Monday. Yesterday, following the AI King Nvidia’s earnings, we broke the inside day support level ($511.36) lower and closed -2.8%. We closed Thursday right at 2025 lows and the Nasdaq is now down -2.1% YTD.

The QQQ is now -7.5% off its 52-week highs. For the SPX, we are -4.6% off highs.  
  
So far, this is not even a major correction in the indices. A -5% correction in the SPX happens basically every year. A -10% correction happens in most years and a -20% Bear Market occurs once every 4ish years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6Czi!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5ced9ce9-8066-4bae-8467-20cd8f236270_537x209.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6Czi!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5ced9ce9-8066-4bae-8467-20cd8f236270_537x209.png)

With that said, this price actions feels very different to me than any other correction we’ve had since this Bull Market began in October 2022. It looks different too.

The comparison I keep coming back to is the [December 2021 Bull Market Top](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/were-not-in-kansas-anymore?utm_source=publication-search), because we are definitely seeing similar price action now to back then.

December 2021/Jan ‘22 ended up being the top of the Covid-QE Bull market before a -25% Bear cycle in 2022. The reason these times look similar to me is because of the massive carnage in individual names, particularly high flying momentum stocks. Many names have put in parabolic upside moves followed by Climax Top patterns, and are already down upwards of -30 and -40% by the time the SPX has pulled back -5% (again, just like in 2022).

Many stocks have likely topped for this Bull Market cycle, in my opinion. This is what the charts are telling me right now.

Goldman’s Meme Basket (popular/crowded retail stocks) is having one of its worst 5 day moves on record.

This is where it signals a strategy shift for me in my [Small Cap 6 Portfolio](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-small-caps-with-massive-upside). I do not want to hold a Small Cap portfolio during a technical downtrend in the Russell 2000. Small Caps are known to pull back 80%+ during Bear cycles.   
  
Each of my Small Cap 6 companies announced great earnings, but stocks often top out when the newsflow is at its best (and bottom when news is at its worst).   
  
It’s time to ring the register heavy in the Small Cap 6. As of this writing, my Small Cap 6 portfolio (equal weighted) is up +99.4% overall in just over 7 months, good for an annualized gain of +226.7%. We had 3 multi-baggers out of 6 stocks (in 7 months), an incredible accomplishment.

SEZL: +235%

INOD: +214%

AEYE: -37%

I will be taking the profits here +99.4% and officially closing out all of my [Small Cap 6 Portfolio](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-small-caps-with-massive-upside). This is why I always stress taking profits on the way up (and I did in my names that hit +100%, +200%, +300%, +400% at century marks), because you never know when the Bull cycle will end.

I certainly could be wrong here and this Russell 2000 pullback could end today, and my names go on to make new highs immediately. But for me, this is a risk/reward and highest probability decision with the Russell 2000 breaking its weekly uptrend. I believe these names would be quite vulnerable in a -10-15% market correction, and could certainly correct upwards of 50% during a Bear Cycle. This is just the way that Small Caps trade.

The most important thing for me, though, is that I’m more than happy with +99.4% gains in 7 months and want to lock that in now. I can always rebuy at a later date if I choose to.

For my Active Portfolio, this is obviously a much more difficult trading environment than we have been in. We could certainly have some short trades start to emerge here. In the 2022 Bear Market, I had some hugely profitable short trades (+30%+) in names like HCP, GTLB, CFLT during the Software Bubble bust.

Overall, I believe a -10-15% correction in the market indices will end up being a great buying opportunity in the right names. This is my Base Case right now following NVDA earnings and I will continue to preach patience here, as I do not believe we are done selling yet.

As stated, I would be prepared for more downside here. Legendary trader Mark Minervini has a famous rule called the 50-80 rule:

“When a secular leader makes a major top, there's a 50% chance it will drop 80%, and an 80% chance it will drop 50%.”

We saw this play out in the 2022 Bear Market with the ARKK bubble declining -80% and never fully recovering (or even close) this cycle. Many of last cycle’s leaders like ROKU, ZM, TDOC, etc. never recovered to new highs during this Bull cycle. Most never will.

Overall, I believe this market correction (it can not even be considered a mini correction in the SPX yet) will bring about many great buying opportunities for the next Bull cycle. My Base Case is a -10%-15% correction in the SPX, which would bring us near $5500 (another 5%+ downside). For now, I think you can use oversold bounces to de-risk and prepare for more downside.
