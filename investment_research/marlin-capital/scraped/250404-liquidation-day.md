# Liquidation Day

The massive market unwind continued today with US stocks taking another **huge** leg lower (SPX -6%, QQQ -6.2%, IWM -4.5%). The S&P 500 just suffered its worst 2-day losses since the March 2020 Covid Crash.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1m24!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa7a55209-632f-4324-95be-50b7b76c7beb_1121x643.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1m24!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa7a55209-632f-4324-95be-50b7b76c7beb_1121x643.jpeg)

This week’s selloff was a textbook example of the “h sell” pattern playing out. [I sketched it out for everyone on Wednesday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-trade-idea-for-q2-840), setting a $5200 measured price target for the SPX on the breakdown.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rdHq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F49beee31-4e2b-4130-8165-f06eec2dcc12_1111x651.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rdHq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F49beee31-4e2b-4130-8165-f06eec2dcc12_1111x651.png)

The “h sell” pattern is basically the only setup you need for shorting in Bear Markets.

As I talked about [Wednesday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-trade-idea-for-q2-840), it is fairly common for the initial breakdown of “h” lows to be a “bear trap” of sorts. On Monday, we got a brief breakdown of “h” lows, followed by a multi-day bounce into Wednesday’s Liberation Day. This multi-day bounce ended up being a u&r trap, as many bulls anticipated that Liberation Day would be a risk clearing event for the market.

Instead, we got the big gap down yesterday and subsequent re-pricing of equities.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pPPS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb238b118-6419-4346-8fc2-bc18783d4763_1111x651.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pPPS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb238b118-6419-4346-8fc2-bc18783d4763_1111x651.png)

The measured target on this h sell breakdown was $5200, which we sliced through today. We ended up closing at $5074 (basically at lows).

Now what?

The S&P has already hit my first 2 downside targets ([$5500](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off?utm_source=publication-search), then $5200). We are currently -17.5% off all-time highs. The Nasdaq has officially hit Bear Market territory, down -22.9% from highs. The Russell 2000 is down -26% from ATHs. The Mag 7 Index is -29% off highs. Semis are down -39%.

What’s next? The technicals continue to scream caution. Both the S&P 500 and QQQ are now printing death crosses for the first time since March 2022 (market bottomed 8-9 months later).

[Early on,](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/do-or-die-time) I emphasized how critical it is to identify the difference between a routine Bull Market correction (like August 2024) and a Bear Market selloff (like 2022). For me, this is all revealed in the charts. [Price tells all](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/price-tells-all).

In February, leading stocks and industries began to establish themselves under their 200 SMA’s for the first time since 2022, and began printing death crosses. This is why [I turned bearish at that time](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/red-flags-emerge?utm_source=publication-search). To me, the Death Cross signals the end of a technical uptrend.

Now, the broader indices have followed suit. Historically, S&P 500 death crosses only appear during selloffs exceeding -20% (which is only a little bit more at this point, we are already -17%).

We are clearly in a Bear Market, so the next step will be officially reaching that status in the S&P 500 (a decline to $4918). In my view, another -3% decline to reach -20% seems like a formality at this point.

**The 2 Phases of This Selloff**

The first leg of this decline was a [positioning cleanse](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/positioning-cleanse?utm_source=publication-search), flushing out massively over-leveraged (ATH) long positioning. Everyone was essentially “all in” the market at the end of January (institutions + retail + foreign investors).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!P51K!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F31e5d97c-b3dc-44cb-8c00-aad4e80de202_704x425.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!P51K!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F31e5d97c-b3dc-44cb-8c00-aad4e80de202_704x425.jpeg)

The second leg, post-Liberation Day, reflects a fundamental re-pricing due to a suddenly bleaker economic outlook. As I talked about [yesterday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/searching-for-the-bottom), S&P 500 EPS estimates have **only** been revised down -2% YTD.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!z0ta!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbe4249bc-d3d3-435e-ab3e-5f48e48ecf0f_840x586.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!z0ta!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbe4249bc-d3d3-435e-ab3e-5f48e48ecf0f_840x586.jpeg)

The post-Liberation Day equity collapse suggests the market is getting ahead of future EPS cuts. In my view, we are seeing stocks preemptively adjust for EPS downgrades that I believe are inevitable.

**Hedge Funds Capitulating while Retail Buys**

[Yesterday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/searching-for-the-bottom), I talked about the massive positioning divergence we saw in March from Hedge Fund and Retail. Hedge Funds sold a record amount of stocks, while Retail bought a record amount of stocks.

This dynamic continued during Thursday’s -5% market selloff.

Hedge funds capitulated en masse, logging their largest single day net selling on record.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dQFS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5fe2dbb4-5857-40ad-a6d3-b41dd29ef2ed_872x601.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dQFS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5fe2dbb4-5857-40ad-a6d3-b41dd29ef2ed_872x601.jpeg)

Meanwhile, retail investors charged in the opposite direction, posting their largest buying day ever.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!awJT!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F60f378a6-dd43-40e0-941d-ebcb6f749988_717x339.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!awJT!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F60f378a6-dd43-40e0-941d-ebcb6f749988_717x339.jpeg)

Again, this is [why I have basically said to ignore Sentiment Indicators](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-big-unwind?utm_source=publication-search) like the CNN Fear & Greed Index (which has been at “extreme fear” throughout this entire -17% selloff). Positioning is the only true sentiment gauge. The market is a voting machine, and people vote with their money.

We have seen a broad based positioning cleanse already (from ATH longs to underweight), but yesterday’s data stops short of full capitulation. Hedge funds are showing signs of panic (HUGE selling & shorting), but retail are not. Extreme fear, in my view, comes with heavy, synchronized selling across the board, not this split-screen dynamic.

Given that Retail has bought on most days YTD, I do not believe the individual investor has even started reducing positions (let alone capitulating). A capitulation of retail bullishness is far away.

The “Buy the dip” behavior from retail is why [I have compared](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/searching-for-the-bottom) the current dynamic to Q1 2022.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Mzog!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6ae56396-5d45-4e44-8b2b-ec8d795fc39a_1572x633.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Mzog!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6ae56396-5d45-4e44-8b2b-ec8d795fc39a_1572x633.png)

However, this has been a much faster sell off than we saw in 2022. The Nasdaq is already down over -21% now, while 2022 was much more of a slower burn (10+ month Bear Market). Based off the price action, the current correction reminds me more of December 2018 or March 2020 (which were much quicker, sharper selloffs).

This is why it is important to stay flexible and open minded. Each cycle is different.

Polymarket now pegs 2025 US recession odds at 57%, a cycle high. In a consumer-driven economy where ~68% of GDP hinges on consumer spending, expectations can become self-fulfilling. When businesses and households cut back on spending anticipating an economic downturn, it can become a self-fulfilling prophecy, leading to a recession.

Should economic conditions deteriorate significantly from here, I believe negative earnings revisions will deepen, and my $4,600 SPX target becomes increasingly likely. We also may go ahead and see the market start to price in a recession here right away (again, $4600 = PT in this scenario), before any major deterioration of data.

**Final Thoughts**

The technicals, positioning, and economic tea leaves all point to more downside ahead. I believe we are likely to reach official Bear Market territory in the S&P 500 (-20%; $4918), while the Nasdaq, Russell 2000, Mag 7, Semis, etc. are already there.

My recessionary price target remains $4600.

As we approach my price targets (already under $5200 initial), long term investors should consider starting to buy in retirement accounts (particularly SPY and QQQ).
