# Thursday Morning Notes 5.4

Technical Update

As expected, the Fed delivered a 25 BP hike yesterday to bring the target rate to 5-5.25%. The S&P 500 initially rallied on the news, but reversed in the last 30 minutes of the trading day, closing at lows (-0.70%).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fIJI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff83d3780-b40f-42ea-b529-7595a5958f94_1274x956.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fIJI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff83d3780-b40f-42ea-b529-7595a5958f94_1274x956.jpeg)

We are now set for a small gap down this morning. Technically, the index is right back at the $4100 level we have chopped around for over a month now. Meanwhile, the action in financials and regional banks post-FOMC should be seen as concerning. As for the S&P 500, the index remains in “no man’s land” until some key support/resistance levels start to break decisively.

Key resistance level to watch above include $4100-$4110, $4140-$4150 and $4190-$4200 (YTD highs).

Key support levels to watch below include $4040-$4030 and $4000.
