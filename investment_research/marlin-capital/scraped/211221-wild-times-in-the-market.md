# Wild Times in the Market

If you thought the action in the stock market has been unusual lately, that’s because it has been.

The average Nasdaq stock is 43% off 52 week highs, but the index is not even in a correction (-10%) yet.  The only time in recent history we have seen similar action was during the Dot Com Bust.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yjsm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0c59cf52-b782-45ee-9559-33094c6bdd67_1588x927.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yjsm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0c59cf52-b782-45ee-9559-33094c6bdd67_1588x927.png)

BofA Securities has said that long-duration tech is currently trading like the post-Dot Com Bubble in 2000 and 2001. Indeed, many of the Dot Com darlings broke down months prior to the Nasdaq peaking on March 10th, 2000.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EB2z!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F15efdd40-bf86-4e96-b25a-9002d69a849a_1558x353.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EB2z!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F15efdd40-bf86-4e96-b25a-9002d69a849a_1558x353.png)

In the Dot Com Bust, more speculative stocks who had nothing more than a business plan (i.e. Pets.com) peaked first while leaders like Cisco and eBay had one last melt up.

This sounds eerily familiar.  In this cycle, pre-revenue SPACs melted up on 2030 projections and fancy buzzwords.  Just like the Dot Com Bust, though, these were also the first stocks to peak.  QS and LAZR, two of the biggest SPAC darlings, peaked in December of 2020.  Similar to 2000, we saw this cycle’s leaders (NET, UPST, etc.) continue to melt up until they finally peaked months later.

So, what can we take from this?  History does not repeat, but it often rhymes.  As you can see below, during the Dot Com bust, the Nasdaq did not close above its 40 week EMA until it had officially bottomed.  Moreover, on the weekly chart, the index continued to respect its downtrend for over 2 years.  A downtrend is characterized by a series of lower highs, and lower lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ebFF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4115af65-cb67-45aa-8dd1-7d3f62461d35_1022x724.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ebFF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4115af65-cb67-45aa-8dd1-7d3f62461d35_1022x724.png)

As you can see with ARKK below, its 40 week EMA is officially trending down for the first time since this parabolic post-Covid run.  Since its peak in February, ARKK has made a series of lower highs, and lower lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EAGj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F136db62c-56ee-470a-b61d-3986c8548479_1001x735.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EAGj!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F136db62c-56ee-470a-b61d-3986c8548479_1001x735.png)

Bottom line, ARKK is in a larger downtrend and the action we have seen in recent months is reminiscent of the Dot Com Bust.  I will trust that this Bear Market has ended when ARKK breaks its weekly downtrend.  Until then, I will continue to be patient and wait for lower prices for buys in my long term account.
