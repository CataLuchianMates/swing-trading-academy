# Friday Morning Notes 1.10

Daily Market Commentary:

First off, I would like to extend my thoughts and prayers to my friends and readers in the Southern California area. Please don’t hesitate to reach out with anything.

The US market finished flattish on Wednesday ahead of this morning’s NFP report.

DECEMBER NFP REPORT:

The US economy added 256,000 jobs in December, well above expectations of 164,000.

The unemployment rate fell to 4.1%, below expectations of 4.2%.

We now have a stronger than expected labor market with rising inflation. Following this morning’s NFP Report, the Fed Funds Futures market is now pricing in just 1 single rate cut in all of 2025: a 25 bps move down to 4.00-4.25% by year end.

Breaking: U.S. 10-YEAR TREASURY YIELD RISES TO 4.79%, HIGHEST SINCE OCTOBER 2023

Rising real yields have been weighing on equities heavily in recent weeks, and this morning’s jobs report is only adding fuel to the fire.

We are set for a gap down this morning in U.S. equities in light of this morning’s stronger than expected jobs report.

Active Portfolio -

I came into this morning holding only 1 long position (AVGO) and have already been stopped out at $225 with today’s gap down. I will look to re-enter on a deeper pullback, which it appears the market may be setting up for.

No other planned trades for me today. I will be off the desk today (in the process of moving) so won’t be watching price action, but I would not be looking to add any positions today regardless.
