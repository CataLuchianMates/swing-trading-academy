# Heading Into April

Marlin Capital Readers,

The S&P 500 is nearing correction territory as well (down -9% from highs).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jcep!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F67fdffa1-5f95-40ff-9bdb-22ff1bbc5727_1267x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jcep!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F67fdffa1-5f95-40ff-9bdb-22ff1bbc5727_1267x844.png)

The S&P 500 is now down -7.2% YTD. The Nasdaq is down -9.9% YTD.

On Friday, the SPX posted its lowest close in 232 days. An estimated -$4.8 trillion in market cap has been erased since the onset of the Iran War. Global market expectations have shifted dramatically from rate cuts to rate hikes.

The Iran War is getting all the headlines right now, and rightfully so. Oil shocks create uncertainty, and markets hate uncertainty. Historically, oil shocks have weighed heavily on corporate earnings. In 5 previous oil supply shocks since 1973, S&P 500 earnings grew by a median of just ~1% during the next 12 months.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zwqu!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F063e6165-3af1-40af-bf31-3b8c68e7a1e1_1003x888.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zwqu!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F063e6165-3af1-40af-bf31-3b8c68e7a1e1_1003x888.jpeg)

But the truth is, this “risk off” price action did not start with the Iran War. It started back in October, when the most speculative leaders began topping. I have been talking about this since [“Risk Off”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70?utm_source=publication-search) and [“Weakness”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/weakness?utm_source=publication-search) back in October.

Historically, this is how market topping processes always begin. [The most speculative names roll over first](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70?utm_source=publication-search). Then the weakness spreads, and the indices follow.

This is exactly what has played out in late 2025/early 2026, almost textbook.

This is why we have been playing defense all year here at Marlin Capital. [We came into 2026](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-marlin-capital-2026-preview?utm_source=publication-search) saying that this year would be significantly more challenging, and that we were in the final innings of the AI Bull Market (that began in 2022).

**Our defensive posture has translated to another quarter of outperformance. 👇**

We are currently up +12% adjusted in the [2026 Portfolio](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/marlin-capital-live-portfolio-dashboards) vs. -7.2% for the SPX.

And we tacked on 3 more Multibaggers in Q1 (SKYT, SNDK, VIAV), **bringing our tally to 14 Multibaggers in the last 16 months‼️**

The question now is what comes next. And that is exactly what I want to walk through tonight. 👇

During bull markets, you always get shakeout corrections (routine corrections) to the 200-day. You often overshoot it to the downside. But, those corrections are usually bought up quickly and the long-term trend does not break.

What we are seeing right now is different. We are seeing trend breaks. We are seeing names establish themselves below their 200-days. We are seeing bearish death crosses forming (21 EMA < 60 EMA < 200 SMA) across the market.

This is no longer routine market correction territory. This is bear market behavior.

The leaders of this Bull Market have already broken down. The Mag 7 is now -19.8% from its highs, nearing official bear market territory.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LcLI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fafaf3a0a-3451-401b-a138-40b79bfb0141_1267x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LcLI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fafaf3a0a-3451-401b-a138-40b79bfb0141_1267x844.png)

The Tech Sector (XLK) has broken down and is heading for a death cross.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QpEv!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd3ce58cc-fc79-4392-8a5f-86fa7c076949_1267x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QpEv!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd3ce58cc-fc79-4392-8a5f-86fa7c076949_1267x844.png)

When the leaders break down, that tells you something very important about the overall health of the market. These are the stocks that carried the entire bull market higher. And right now, they are confirming the same bearish signals I have been tracking since October.

**Updated Capitulation Watch**

In [“Capitulation Watch”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/capitulation-watch) last week, I walked through the positioning data in detail and concluded that we were far from the kind of widespread panic selling that has historically marked sustainable bottoms.

With another week of heavy selling in the books, we are getting closer. But, we are still not there yet.

Goldman’s Positioning Indicator has fallen further, from -0.3z last week to -0.9z now.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cSP4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe9fd4404-3e57-4a98-87b3-589e6cba6ba6_1126x836.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cSP4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe9fd4404-3e57-4a98-87b3-589e6cba6ba6_1126x836.jpeg)

That is a meaningful move in just 1 week. But for reference, this indicator reached -2.5z at the Liberation Day lows last April ([when we called the bottom within 2 days](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bottomed-out?utm_source=publication-search)). Historically, major SPX bottoms have occurred at -1.5 standard deviation or worse, and typically closer to -2 standard deviations for significant corrections.

As I laid out in [“Capitulation Watch”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/capitulation-watch), these are the 2 things I am watching before deploying long-term capital. 👇

1. Headlines start to meaningfully improve (particularly on Iran/oil, which is the primary driver of this accelerated selling pressure right now)
2. We reach signs of genuine capitulation selling, which for me means positioning reaching at least -1 standard deviation AND >40% of SPX stocks hitting oversold levels

We are not there yet on either front. But this is a fluid situation, and that point is important to remember. 1 positive headline could change the equation greatly, which is why I think it’s crucial to stay nimble here despite the bearish price action.

If we hit below $6300 **AND** my capitulation signals start firing at the same time, that is where I will look to **start** deploying capital (in my long-term account).
