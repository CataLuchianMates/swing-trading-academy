# Friday Morning Notes 4.19

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Yesterday made it 5 consecutive sessions in the red for US stocks with the SPX finishing -0.22%.

 We saw more risk-off price action within Tech with Semis weaker following TSM -4.8% on a mediocre earnings print (lacked the ‘wow factor’ + some debate about trajectory of FY outlooks, ASML down another -2% following weaker top line results yesterday).  Semis are now down -12% from highs and below the 50-day moving average (and 60 EMA) for the first time since last Fall.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0cZY!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd756a92e-6a33-4443-b6e2-952e0a04393f_1203x891.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0cZY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd756a92e-6a33-4443-b6e2-952e0a04393f_1203x891.jpeg)

After the bell, NFLX dropped -4% on a solid sub and EPS beat.  In the face of a higher bar, investors were not overly impressed and the company said that it will stop reporting quarterly membership numbers next year (possible red flag).

After 5 consecutive negative sessions for the SPX, a possible short-term bounce could be in order here.  With that said, a lot of work still needs to be done technically to flip the switch from the newly bearish trend.  No planned trades for me today.
