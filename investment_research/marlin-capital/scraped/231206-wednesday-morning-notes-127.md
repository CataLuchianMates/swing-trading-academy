# Wednesday Morning Notes 12.7

**Daily Market Commentary:**

US stocks finished flat (-0.06%) on Tuesday as the S&P 500 continues to hug its 8 EMA.

11.9B shares traded across all exchanges.  SPX volumes were -7% d/d and -2% vs 20d MA.

Mega Cap Tech took back the lead in yesterday’s session after a week + of rest/underperformance.

AAPL had its biggest daily gain since early May, which felt like a combination of (1) better Foxcon numbers out of Taiwan and (2) positive note from BofA.

Mega Cap Tech stocks have sharply underperformed the broader market in the past 2 weeks.  Amid the group’s recent underperformance, hedge funds have begun aggressively reducing net longs in Mega Cap Tech.  According to GS Prime Book, the Mag 7 has been net sold in 15 of the past 17 sessions, driven almost entirely by long sales.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!A06y!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F06ce14c6-0a5f-4d00-8c64-05760a4d0c63_942x1469.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!A06y!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F06ce14c6-0a5f-4d00-8c64-05760a4d0c63_942x1469.jpeg)

JOLTS – 8.73M vs 9.3M consensus.  More signs of cooling in labor demand as the number of job openings in October fell to 8.73M, the lowest since the reopening of the US economy in Q1 2021.

Yesterday’s action following the worse than expected JOLTS job openings shows how we are still in a “bad news is good” regime for the stock market.  History says be careful what you wish for, while the market continues to say this is a problem for another day.

TNA - took a much needed rest yesterday after a sharp 2 day rally. I am still holding 2/3rd of my long entered last week
