# A Huge Week Ahead

The S&P 500 snapped a 6-week winning streak last week, finishing lower by -0.96%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vEp4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F331560f9-00ac-42ed-83cd-920582147911_1248x1543.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vEp4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F331560f9-00ac-42ed-83cd-920582147911_1248x1543.jpeg)

Large Caps outperformed Small Caps on the week, led by TSLA (+22%). The EV car maker posted better than expected quarterly earnings (especially on gross margins) and the stock responded with its best daily gain (+22%) in more than 11+ years on Thursday.

Tesla’s post-earnings rally helped to keep the SPX from a steeper decline, which speaks to the type of market environment we are in/will be in for the next couple weeks with earnings. I expect to see individual stocks to go their separate ways based on their Q3 earnings reports, and the Mag 7 names will undoubtedly be dragging the broader indices along with them. 5 of the 7 Mag 7 names report this week: AAPL, AMZN, GOOGL, META, MSFT.

Hedge Funds have snapped up shares of Mega Caps this month, but aren’t quite as confident as they have been in previous earnings seasons. Mag 7 long positioning is overall much lighter compared to Q1 and Q2 ‘24.

Cleaner positioning is a good thing heading into Q3 earnings, but the challenges are still there for each company. It’s hard to find a print that investors feel ‘great’ about leaning into right now - META (elevated topline expectations + ‘25 CapEx), AMZN (Q4 guide debate), GOOGL (YT decel + regulatory overhang), MSFT (lack of Azure visibility + limited EPS revisions as of late), AAPL (muted iPhone demand, Q4 guide unknown).

Thus far through Q3 earnings, Tech stocks are pacing the index. 78% of Tech stocks that have reported so far have beat on EPS compared to 51% for the S&P 500.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!AJun!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F428b3332-d4fc-4573-a5f8-8b07382062c6_1213x503.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!AJun!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F428b3332-d4fc-4573-a5f8-8b07382062c6_1213x503.jpeg)

We will get A LOT more color this week with 42% of the S&P 500 market cap set to report. All there is to do now is sit back and watch. My trading focus for this week will be solely on potential [earnings gappers](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-earnings-gapper-trade-setup?utm_source=publication-search).

The other factor at play in markets here is the upcoming November 5th election. The latest election odds from Polymarket have Trump with a 65.8% chance to win.

This is easily explainable. If there is 1 thing that markets dislike, it is uncertainty. We have a closely contested election here in 2024 and uncertainty about who will be the president of the US for the next 4 years. BofA Quant Strategy has modeled a -4.7% hit to EPS with a Harris victory (led by raising the corporate tax rate from 21% to 28%), and a +4% boom to EPS with a Trump victory (led by lowering the corporate tax rate from 21% to 15%).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5Ps-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faca5eb2d-077e-4350-9c84-bdd0a3223c4d_1284x1696.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5Ps-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faca5eb2d-077e-4350-9c84-bdd0a3223c4d_1284x1696.jpeg)

In my view, any potential downward bias in markets heading into the 11/5 election will largely be offset by 41% of the SPX reporting earnings. This is the main factor that will move markets this week.
