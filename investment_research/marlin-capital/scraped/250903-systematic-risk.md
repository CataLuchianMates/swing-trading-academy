#### Systematic Downside Risk Remains

As I talked about in [“The Dreaded September”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-dreaded-september-ff0), CTAs are basically max long now.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!p7-2!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fae014a11-d825-418a-b0b0-34ccc408a722_1290x972.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!p7-2!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fae014a11-d825-418a-b0b0-34ccc408a722_1290x972.png)

If we slip below the key CTA trigger levels, we will see big systematic selling.

This means that the risk is assyemtrically to the downside. Over the next 1 month, Goldman models…

* Up tape: +$3.5B of CTA buying
* Flat tape: -$3.9B of CTA selling
* Down tape: -$73.7B of CTA selling

If we slip below the key CTA trigger levels, we will see upwards of -$73B of CTA selling.

**I estimate the sell trigger level is below $6300 in the SPX.**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Y1o7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc25abd1b-2885-46db-aadd-527fad670854_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Y1o7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc25abd1b-2885-46db-aadd-527fad670854_1396x653.png)

If we break below $6250-$6300 support, this is where we will see big systematic selling come into play.

The good news is, as long as we remain above this key support level ($6250-$6300), systematic selling will not become a major factor. Remember, CTAs are trend followers. They are basically max long now because the market has been in a healthy uptrend. They do not flip to sellers until the trend flips (estimated trigger below $6250-$6300).

The bad news is, systematic buying is done either way, as they are max long already. Even in a up tape, Goldman estimates only +3.5B of CTA buying in the next month (which will not move the needle for the market).

Systematic flows have been a HUGE tailwind for the market since April lows. Now, it is no longer a tailwind (and in fact, is is a major downside risk in a down tape).

As long as we remain above $6300 support, we should be safe from this big downside risk. However, the lack of systematic buying is keeping the tape choppy and rangebound. As I said in my weekly preview, I do not believe now is the time to be aggressive trading-wise.
