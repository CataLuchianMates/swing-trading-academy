# Wednesday Morning Notes 9.27

Technical Update

The S&P 500 continued its descent lower on Tuesday, finishing the day -1.47%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8dno!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2cbc5f1b-70df-4246-91d3-444637170c7d_977x721.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!8dno!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2cbc5f1b-70df-4246-91d3-444637170c7d_977x721.jpeg)

It has been a straight line down following last week’s FOMC meeting, where a hawkish Powell threw water on the soft landing fire that became consensus this year.

We head into Wednesday in a difficult situation for market participants here. The door remains open for a waterfall sell off, but we are very oversold and a sharp multi- day short covering rally is possible at any time.

My initial target for the SPX on this sell off remains the 200 SMA at $4190-$4200. We are set for a small gap up this morning. Key resistance levels to watch above include $4300, $4340-$4350, $4370-$4380, and $4400.

Key support levels to watch below include $4260-$4270 and $4190-$4200 (my initial target for this correction).
