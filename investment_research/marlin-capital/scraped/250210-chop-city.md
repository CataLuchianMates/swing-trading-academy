# Chop City

Last week was another choppy one for US stocks with the the S&P 500 falling -0.2%. Stocks opened the week sharply lower in response to the announcement that President Trump would be implementing 25% tariffs on Mexico and Canada along with 10% tariffs on China. By the end of Monday, however, Trump agreed to postpone tariffs on Mexico and Canada for 30 days, which helped stocks to recover some of their early losses by the end of the week.

Friday was especially weak for the indices with the Nasdaq declining -1.4% after Amazon earnings failed to impress. Earnings season has been a mixed bag for the whole market, including Big Tech. MSFT, GOOGL, and AMZN have each seen initial sell offs following their results. To this point, below is the YTD breakdown of the Mag 7 with 4/7 now in the red for 2025.

This choppy/directionless environment has made for difficult trading as it has become a big challenge for stocks to sustain rallies/breakouts. There have been no “easy days” as markets have been lunging from headline to headline. Despite the increased degree of difficulty, the S&P 500 still sits just -2% off all time highs.

Looking forward, I absolutely still believe we are in a Bull Market and we are simply in a choppy period for the market. I do believe, however, that one should reasonably expect less consistency than we have become accustomed to the last 2 years. History tells us that the 3rd year following 2 years of consecutive +20%+ gains tends to be much more challenging. Only once in history have we seen 3 straight years of >20% returns, so a more normal return profile in 2025 seems like the best bet.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jxn-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa6f240e6-aa9d-4545-81e9-0338b980df17_1109x714.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jxn-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa6f240e6-aa9d-4545-81e9-0338b980df17_1109x714.jpeg)

With regards to trading, I believe the key here is to stay patient and mostly preserve capital during these difficult trading environments so that you be in a position to attack the 3-4 big trends that will inevitably show up.

In my Active Swing Trading account, my trades have mostly failed to gain any traction and this is the only feedback from the market that I need to tell me to remain light in my positioning. I believe we are still in an environment where dips in strong names with earnings catalysts and volume can be bought while rips should be sold (quicker to take profits in a market like this).

I head into this week holding just a half position in VIAV in my Active Swing Trading Portfolio and no plans to add any new longs today with the market gapping up +0.8%. Buying directly into a gap up in a choppy/rangebound market is the last thing I want to be doing here.

While it has been difficult for stocks to trade with any rhythm and consistency during this time, we are still seeing some strong earnings moves out there. Last week we had PLTR up >20% (from my Trump trades and Top ‘25 picks portfolio) as well as a huge move on earnings in AMSC +30% (from my Small Cap 6 and Top ‘25 picks portfolio).

We have another big week of earnings ahead of us with some major growth and Software names set to report. Earnings gappers will continue to be my focus in my Active Portfolio, but I will be looking to be even more selective than usual in this environment.
