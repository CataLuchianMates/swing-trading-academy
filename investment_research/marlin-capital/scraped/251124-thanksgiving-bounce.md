# Thanksgiving Bounce

US stocks rallied today (SPX +1.5%) with markets rebounding to kick off this Thanksgiving holiday week.

This comes after last week’s slide, which helped knock the air out of the 2025 AI Bull run. For last week, the S&P 500 fell -1.95%, the Nasdaq declined -2.74%, the Dow fell -1.91%, and the Russell 2000 dropped -0.78%.

The Russell 2000 fell for its 4th straight week, its longest run of weekly declines since March.

#### Monday’s Bounce

After today’s bounce, the S&P 500 is now just -3.1% off ATHs.

Today’s market rebound was all about December rate cut odds. Fed members Williams (voter) and Waller (voter) both announced their support of a December rate cut, lifting [Polymarket odds for a December cut](https://polymarket.com/event/fed-decision-in-december) from 33% to 83%.

#### Healthy or Unhealthy Pullback

In the major market indices, this has been a healthy pullback so far.

For many individual names, that has not been the case.

82% of the S&P 500 (412/500) have already hit Bear Market territory (-20% from ATHs) during this pullback.

In “How to Make Money in Stocks”, the great Bill O’Neil says that during market corrections, growth leaders should pull back up to 2.5-3x the major market indices. Anything more than that would be considered unhealthy. During this mini-SPX correction, most growth leaders are already down over -30% from highs (>7x the major market indices). Many have already declined 10x the SPX or more.

To me, this price action is more reminiscent of the 2022 Bear Market than any routine market pullback we have seen. The data confirms this as well.

And on that note, as I have highlighted several times, the 2022 Bear Market price action was very reminiscent of the 2001 Dot Com Bust. History always has a way of repeating.

Important to note, I am only comparing the price action in these instances. The 2022 Bear Market had massive Macro headwinds (Fed hiking aggressively, 40+ yr high inflation, etc.) that we do not have right now.

Crypto has also been slaughtered, which has become an important “risk off” signal during recent cycles. In fact, Bitcoin has been the leading indicator for every major S&P 500 correction dating back to 2017. Historically, Bitcoin has topped 6-8 weeks before the S&P 500.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fz26!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fef780be4-8e72-44ba-95e4-db072e8bfee2_2048x1301.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fz26!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fef780be4-8e72-44ba-95e4-db072e8bfee2_2048x1301.jpeg)

Here in 2025, Bitcoin topped on 10/6 at ~$126k and the SPX topped on 10/29 (3 weeks later).

Digging deeper into the numbers, we have had 8 Bitcoin corrections of -30% or more since 2017 (we are in our 8th right now). The previous 7 were all followed by an SPX correction of -10% + within 9 months.

In my view, this simply means that “risk off” price action has a way of crossing over into different asset classes. This has been observed throughout history, but the Bitcoin to SPX/NDX correlation really hammers this point home for me.

#### My Big Picture Thoughts

As I mentioned above, I view this current overall price action as most similar to 2022. With that said, the key difference is that 2022 had significant Macro catalysts for a long Bear Market (Fed hiking aggressively, 40+ yr high inflation, slight recession).

This time around, the Fed is cutting rates and the economy looks fine. That is why it is difficult to pinpoint this market weakness to any 1 reason. There are many different factors at play (AI anxiety/ROI & valuation concerns, Fed possibly moving slower than expected, labor market concerns, tariff concerns), but no clear, major catalyst.

Big picture, I do NOT see the catalysts in place for a major Bear Market like 2022. I have learned to never to get too bearish when the Fed is cutting rates (and there is no recession). This is why I do not believe the SPX has topped for this cycle yet.

Right now, I believe the indices are in a normal/healthy market pullback phase while many names are likely already in their Bear phases. I continue to expect another leg up for the indices (where many of the former leaders down >40% will bounce big, but ultimately form lower highs, confirming their Bear phases).

#### Is the healthy SPX pullback over?

So far, the market has staged a big 2-day bounce from oversold levels. That’s all.

It’s a solid start, though. The SPX closed back above its 60 EMA (medium term support) today, as well as its 5 and 15 EMA levels (shorter term support).

Further, we finally saw a market gap up hold today and didn’t close the session weak (which is a slight change of character to take note of).

But for me, the uptrend doesn’t officially start until the first higher low is put in.

Here’s how it usually plays out:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fGk1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2399c75e-ca54-47fe-9520-ba2ea7277300_1252x676.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fGk1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2399c75e-ca54-47fe-9520-ba2ea7277300_1252x676.png)

This bounce attempt should be followed by higher lows/successful re-test of Friday’s lows. Then, the first higher high comes (signaling a new uptrend has begun).

This means that we will need to see Friday’s lows hold in the indices/individual names on the next market pullback. This would be the higher low we are looking for.

We will also need to see breadth improve to confirm the action, which we did today.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FnK8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9a741ab6-9103-45fd-88f5-ec22e6fbf80b_1150x800.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FnK8!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9a741ab6-9103-45fd-88f5-ec22e6fbf80b_1150x800.png)

Again, it’s a start. But right now, breadth is merely bouncing from washed out levels, so we can’t read too much into this just yet.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aO9M!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F79ebcee3-8c7d-46ca-9e84-92fcf3545a5c_722x485.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aO9M!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F79ebcee3-8c7d-46ca-9e84-92fcf3545a5c_722x485.png)

Similar to the SPX, we will ideally want to see breadth indicators form a higher low on the next market pullback.

For me, I do not look to get involved until the first higher low is put in place. This is what I will be looking for in order to start entering new trades.

My goal is not to “catch the bottom”. I’ve never caught an exact market bottom, yet have outperformed during every Bull Market cycle I have traded through. This is because my edge does not come from calling the lows (ie predicting the future). It comes from identifying the market environment we are in and capitalizing on the trend. And as of right now, we are currently still in an uncertain market environment. For me, that means that cash is still the best position until a reliable trend develops. If/when we get solid evidence of a higher low in the indices and market breadth, new trades will be back on the table for me. Until then, I will continue to exercise patience.
