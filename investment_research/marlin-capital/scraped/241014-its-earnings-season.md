# It's Earnings Season

The S&P 500 moved to record highs last week, boosted by some upside surprises to kick off earnings season.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oWek!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe7e32a8e-4b7d-4625-9fe8-9ec330c06dc7_946x698.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oWek!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe7e32a8e-4b7d-4625-9fe8-9ec330c06dc7_946x698.jpeg)

JPM and Wells Fargo rose on Friday after reporting smaller than expected declines in Q3 profits.

Breadth was strong to close out the week with the SPX Equal Weight outperforming its Market Cap weighted Index by 40bps.  The Russell 2000 led the way on Friday, closing +2% thanks to a cooler PPI number.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sVFC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F126d4b96-e5d1-471a-8775-225c221ccb71_1197x881.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sVFC!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F126d4b96-e5d1-471a-8775-225c221ccb71_1197x881.jpeg)

Extended valuations are the only thing bears are consistently pounding the table on, as economic data points have remained “good enough”.  Last week, the Labor Department reported a surprise jump in weekly jobless claims to 258k, the highest level in 14 months.  As expected, the market was largely unfazed by this due to the special circumstances that went into this data point (Hurricane Helene, port strikes, etc.).

Earnings are running the show now, and the bar of +4% EPS growth YoY should be a relatively easy one to clear.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!G7m_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F62d49083-02c6-411c-aa80-b798cddb67a3_978x811.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!G7m_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F62d49083-02c6-411c-aa80-b798cddb67a3_978x811.jpeg)

Recall, for Q2 ’24 earnings the bar was set at +9% YoY EPS growth and realized came in at +11% YoY.

The bar is not high for Q3 earnings. As long as companies have managed through Macro headwinds and see early signs of improvement from lower rates, many stocks should get rewarded and there will be plenty of trading opportunities. Estimates have fallen over the last 3 months, which should lead to another healthy beat for the SPX in Q3.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Q6oj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5b9874b5-dbfc-49f4-896a-0980e9868604_1284x466.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Q6oj!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5b9874b5-dbfc-49f4-896a-0980e9868604_1284x466.jpeg)

I am expecting at least a 2% beat for SPX earnings in Q3 ($62, or +6% YoY vs +4% consensus).

This week, Bank earnings continue with a few Tech reports mixed in starting Wednesday (ASML, TSM, NFLX, ISRG the headliners).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kiIv!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F16f291c9-95ea-401b-bfb4-23043f967745_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kiIv!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F16f291c9-95ea-401b-bfb4-23043f967745_1920x1080.jpeg)
