# Wednesday Morning Notes 4.5

Technical Update

The market finally pulled back a bit yesterday, with the SPX finishing -0.58% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!r-uD!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd429bfdb-93b0-493a-add7-f891b712a094_871x651.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!r-uD!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd429bfdb-93b0-493a-add7-f891b712a094_871x651.jpeg)

As I mentioned heading into yesterday’s action, we were overbought on the short-term time frame, and a pullback to $4050 would be healthy here. The SPX held $4100 support on yesterday’s pullback, closing above the level with a bounce in the final 10 minutes.

Technically, this setup remains bullish here with nothing out of the ordinary so far from this pullback. Key support levels include $4075-$4080, and $4050, and the last line of technical support at $3990-$4000. As mentioned, a brief pullback to $4050 would be healthy in the context of this recent uptrend.

If the index refuses to pull back that far and starts rallying further, key resistance levels ahead include $4150-$4160 and YTD highs at $4190-$4200.

**Trade Idea**

Long PANW

Entry on break above $199

Stop below $194 (2.5% risk)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ngRS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5144071e-3e11-4829-b8cf-7a6098144578_868x655.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ngRS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5144071e-3e11-4829-b8cf-7a6098144578_868x655.jpeg)

With the market still acting well and above all key moving averages, there are certain setups emerging here. Leadership is thin, but PANW is boasting a strong technical setup with double inside days after a big move up. My entry will be triggered on a break of both inside day highs ($199). My stop will be below the inside day lows ($194), creating a low risk (2.5%) setup to start out.

I’m seeing a couple other potential long setups emerging as well, but in my view, this appears PANW be the best low risk/high reward setup in a leading tech name. I will wait to see how this trade acts (if triggered on break above $199) before looking to get more aggressive exposure wise.
