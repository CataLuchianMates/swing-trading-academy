Friday’s jobs report was firmer than expected with nonfarm payrolls rising +199k (vs street 185k) in November, or 161k excluding the temporary boost from the return of striking auto workers – beating consensus by 14k and leaving the 3 month average unchanged at +204k from the previously reported value (following 35k of downward revision to prior months).  It is worth noting, however, that the breadth of job gains remained narrow with Healthcare (+93k) and government (+49k) again driving the majority of hiring in November.

On the surface, last week’s labor data looked optimistic.  Unemployment ticked lower, average weekly hours worked ticked higher (34.3 -> 34.4), productivity for the quarter hit the highest level since the early post-pandemic comps (5.2%), NFPs surprised to the upside, and the household survey printed 747k.  These healthy numbers came to fruition in spite of robust tightening in financial conditions throughout the late summer and early fall (peak tightening was Oct 27th), which would indicate the economy continues to be insulated from higher rates.

Under the surface, though, the data is a bit wobbly and doesn’t portray exuberance.  For instance, when government employees and workers returning from labor strikes are stripped out, NFP was 150k.  Further, average hourly earnings MoM came in a touch hotter than expected.  However, the devil in the details of the data did not deter market participants on Friday.  Net flows into global equity funds were positive for the week.

According to GS Prime, hedge funds were net buyers last week or the 1st time in 4+ weeks, driven by both long buys and short covers (1.5 to 1).  CTA positioning is at its highest level in 8+ years after $225B in buying over the last month.

**Trade Idea – Long UiPath (PATH)**

PATH checks all the boxes I look for in a momentum trade, starting with its high volume earnings catalyst.

Automation software company UiPath (PATH) reported results ahead of analysts' expectations in Q3 FY2024, with revenue up 24% year on year to $325.9 million.  The company expects next quarter's revenue to be around $383.5 million, in line with analysts' estimates.  It made a GAAP loss of $0.06 per share, improving from its loss of $0.10 per share in the same quarter last year.
