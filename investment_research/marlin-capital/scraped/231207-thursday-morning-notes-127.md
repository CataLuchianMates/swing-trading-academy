# Thursday Morning Notes 12.7

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Another quiet day for US equities on Tuesday with the S&P 500 closing -0.39% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zVRJ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F915582b6-9bf1-42b2-85e0-b5045e8cb489_1197x882.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zVRJ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F915582b6-9bf1-42b2-85e0-b5045e8cb489_1197x882.jpeg)

The index closed just below its 8 EMA ($4557).  Moreover, the SPX gave its first bearish MACD cross since October 20th, which was followed by a week of heavy selling.  MACD has been in the bullish zone and pointing up since early November throughout this whole rally, so this flip is something to take note of.

The QQQ is further along with MACD firmly in the bearish zone and trending down while price struggles to hold above its 8 EMA.  These bearish flips should tell you that an additional layer of caution is warranted here after the 4+ week rally.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OjEg!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa13a37f8-4a27-42e4-9dd2-a24202c8e8f0_1210x892.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OjEg!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa13a37f8-4a27-42e4-9dd2-a24202c8e8f0_1210x892.jpeg)

Under the surface, the rotation continues (TMT Most Short +1.5%, Mag 7 -1.1%).  The leg lower into the bell was a function of mega caps weighing on the indices and the catch up trade (Small Caps) running out of some gas.

With yesterday’s reversal in Small Caps (TNA), I believe it is prudent to reduce long exposure here.  I will be selling another 1/3rd of my TNA long (locking in over +7% gains) and will be holding the final 1/3rd of my TNA long with a stop below $30.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PO90!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F221e5243-2977-473d-8202-7ac95f57d78c_955x721.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PO90!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F221e5243-2977-473d-8202-7ac95f57d78c_955x721.jpeg)

Tomorrow’s NFP report will be a key catalyst for the market, especially after this week’s JOLTS report shows a deteriorating labor market.  The economic surprise bucket shows labor decelerating ahead of expectations and beginning to flip into the negative surprise territory.  This could be confirmed by tomorrow’s NFP report.
