**NVDA Earnings:**

NVDA trading slightly negative this morning (-3%) following earnings last night:

Beat and guided above on the topline+ announced a new $50B buyback.

Revs of $30B (+122% YoY) vs cons $28.9B (+114% YoY)

Data Center Revs of $26.3B (154% YoY) vs cons $25.2B

Gross Margins 75.7% (down -3.2% QoQ) vs cons 75.5%

EPS of $0.68 (a 5% beat, compared to 8-20% beats in last 9 qts) vs cons $0.65

Guided FQ3 Revs at $32.5B (+79% YoY) vs cons $31.7B (+76% YoY)

Guided Gross Margins at 75% vs cons 75.3%

Big picture thoughts – Overall a very solid quarter for NVDA, although the magnitude of the beat and guidance raise is a bit less than previous quarters.  Guidance was a bit light versus the $2B+ beat the market has become accustomed.

Perhaps the one glaring weakness in this report is the gross margin compression (which was not totally unexpected as the tailwinds seen earlier in the year abate and product mix evolves).  Management reiterated its FY25 Gross Margin outlook of mid-70s range.  This fell ~30bps shy of consensus, which is essentially the only “miss” I can find in this report.  OpEx came in a bit higher than expected.  But big picture, NVDA delivered once again and I would expect to see the stock bought up on any major weakness.
