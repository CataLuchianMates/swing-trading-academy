2024 was a terrific year for US equities with the S&P 500 closing +24% and NDX +27% (higher for the 19th time in 22 years). This was the first time since 1998 and 1999 that the S&P 500 produced 2 consecutive years of +20% total returns.

The 2024 market rally was characterized by a ‘broadening out’ of market gains, with both the SPX and R2K Tech Indices both finishing the year +20%. This was a refreshing change after 2023, when the NDX outpaced both the SPX and R2K Indices by 25-30%.

Tech stocks led the charge in 2024, with only 3 down months (matching the 2021 Bull Run we saw) and just one -10%+ drawdown (in July), as an accommodative Macro backdrop (rate cuts + stable economic growth) continued to catapult stocks higher. The top theme was once again Generative AI - both at the infrastructure layer (NVDA, MRVL, TSM, ANET up 75%+) and, increasingly, the application layer (APP, CRM, META, NOW strength, particularly in the 2H).

Despite the wire to wire Tech rally, under the hood, it was a tale of 2 halves: Big Tech dominated in the 1H and Small Tech took over in the 2H. Mega Cap traded up +42% in the 1H of the year (and tacked on another +11% in the 2H), whereas, Russell 2K Tech was up just +7% in the 1H of the year (but nearly +20% in the 2H of the year).

Looking towards 2025, the market's bullish momentum is up for debate as Tech P/E valuations are currently at 20+ year highs. With that said, the key foundational elements for the 23-24 Bull Run are once again intact into the New Year (GenAI, Macro, balanced positioning, Earnings growth, etc.).

**Are valuations too high?**

The S&P 500 began 2024 with the forward P/E of 19.5x and ended the year with a 22.5x forward P/E. Whether you were looking at P/E, EV/Sales, ERP, CAPE, or most any other traditional metric for valuation, the starting point in 2024 was objectively demanding. In the end, that never stood in the way of Bulls in 2024. If anything, the 2024 market rewarded those who were willing to buy higher highs.

Now, we head into 2025 with an even more demanding P/E multiple for the S&P 500 (22.5x). These are levels we have only seen a couple other times in history (1998-2000 Dot Com Bubble and 2020-21 Covid-QE Bubble).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vsU-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5093707b-444b-4817-b1a2-c33d76499c6d_1157x680.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vsU-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5093707b-444b-4817-b1a2-c33d76499c6d_1157x680.png)

As I think about how much these historically high valuations will ultimately matter for the market in 2025, I am reminded by legendary investor Howard Marks’ Pendulum Theory. This is a theory that I myself subscribe to, as my own experiences and research very much confirm Marks’ theory. Marks describes the market as oscillating like a pendulum due to human emotions, swinging from extreme to extreme. The pendulum's swing influences asset prices, leading to overvaluation when optimism peaks and undervaluation when pessimism is at its height. Directly from Mr. Marks:

> “The mood swings of the securities markets resemble the movement of a pendulum. Although the midpoint of its arc best describes the location of the pendulum 'on average,' it actually spends very little of its time there. Instead, it is almost always swinging toward or away from the extremes…The pendulum swings between greed and fear, from credulousness to skepticism, from optimism to pessimism, and from recklessness to caution.”

So, while there may be a “Fair Value” one can assign for the market (and individual stocks) based off extensive research, number crunching, etc., the market (and stocks) will actually spend very little time there (at Fair Value). It will typically be in an overvalued or undervalued phase depending on various factors, but most importantly, where we stand in the market cycle. This is why I have said before, valuations do not matter on the way up, and they do not matter on the way down. During a Bull Market, valuations can always go higher. During a Bear Market, they can always go lower.

Both aforementioned times the Forward P/E valuation reached these current levels (the Dot Com Bubble & Covid Bubble), there was ultimately a catalyst/a reason why valuations peaked. During the Dot Com Bubble, it became clear that most companies would not be able to meet lofty Wall Street expectations, and a Hard Landing/Recession ensued. The Covid-QE Bubble peaked when the Fed signaled it would need to aggressively raise rates in order to curb 40+ year high Inflation. Both these cases had clear catalysts for valuations to peak and the pendulum to begin swinging back the other way.

Valuations, in and of themselves, are not what end Bull Markets. I do not believe we will see a peak to this current Bull Market simply because valuations are extended. History shows us that there would need to be a catalyst for the pendulum to begin swinging the other way. While I can think of some potential negative catalysts for the market and valuations to top in 2025, my experience tells me it is better to react than predict in these cases.

As for the comparisons between now and the Dot Com Bubble, there are some similarities, but ultimately there are many more differences. Similarly, we have a new technology (AI vs the Internet) that is going to change our world in ways we are only just beginning to comprehend. Life changing technologies often fuel speculative asset bubbles.

By now, it is very much consensus that a booming AI industry is driving the next technological revolution. For investors, however, the most important question to ask is whether the expectations embedded in financial markets today project a realistic path ahead. This was, after all, the great undoing for the late 90s Internet Bubble.

The key difference now relative to 2000 is how delivered earnings growth has supported this massive stock rally. Take Nvidia, the AI King, for example. Wall Street’s estimates for Nvidia’s FY 2026 earnings have increased from $0.62 in Nov. 2022 to $4.07 in Nov. 2024, meaning that its huge 2-year stock rally was almost entirely driven by earnings growth estimates rather than by market sentiment alone.

During the Late 90s Internet Bubble, we saw a major decoupling of stock prices from earnings. Back in March 2000, for example, Cisco was the poster boy of the Dot Com Bubble with a forward P/E of 125x. By contrast, Nvidia’s fwd P/E has hovered around 35x despite the massive stock advance.

In other words, what we are seeing today is more reality based vs hope based (while the Dot Com Bubble was more hope based). Rather than seeing AI as a Bubble that is about to burst, I see it as a multiyear investment cycle with plenty of growth still ahead. To theorize what other companies the market could begin to celebrate as ‘AI Winners” in 2025, it is imperative to understand the AI value chain and which bucket companies fall into.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!r1s9!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0b77230c-6c4f-4c2f-92d9-ce72aa1cd95b_1089x410.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!r1s9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0b77230c-6c4f-4c2f-92d9-ce72aa1cd95b_1089x410.jpeg)

**AI Hardware:** This segment includes the companies that design, produce, and manufacture the chips and hardware necessary for AI computation. Examples include NVDA and AVGO.

**AI Hyperscalers:** These companies provide the infrastructure needed for AI operations, like cloud computing and data centers. They also build large language models that can be used/licensed by other companies. For example, Amazon’s Web Services business or Google Cloud,

**AI Developers:** Think ChatGPT, Grok, Perplexity, etc. These can range from small app builders to Tech giants (like Microsoft) that leverage hyperscaler technologies to provide solutions for end users.

**AI Integrators:** These are organizations that have the capability to build/customize, use, and integrate their own AI solutions.

**AI Essentials:** Companies in this category support the AI ecosystem by providing essential services or materials. These companies enable the whole AI value chain to work, whether that is energy, air conditioning, power, raw materials, or even the data to train the models

With the AI trade poised to continue to broaden out in 2025, I believe we are likely to see smaller companies in each bucket outperform. Proven AI winners like NVDA, AVGO, MSFT, GOOGL, etc. should continue to be liquid leaders, in my view. But, my focus will primarily be on smaller/newer companies in 2025. I believe there will be many opportunities for skilled stock pickers to outperform in 2025 by owning and trading non-Mega Cap, AI beneficiaries.

Big picture - AI is poised to change our world in ways we are just beginning to understand. Below, I highlight my top 5 predictions on how we will see AI evolve in 2025.

**My Top 5 Big Picture Predictions for AI in 2025:**

1. **AI agents and chat bots will continue to dominate**

   AI agents, capable of autonomously performing tasks like scheduling, data analysis, and even content creation, will continue to grow. These technologies will only become more sophisticated and personalized in 2025.
2. **Significant Advancements in multimodal AI**

   AI systems will show significant advancements in understanding multiple types of data simultaneously (text, images, audio, video)…Think Voice to AI technology. This will lead to better human-AI interfaces, particularly in fields like customer service, education, and entertainment.
3. **AI in media and entertainment will spark debates about ethical AI/and copyright/originality**

   As AI becomes more capable of generating new content like art, music, articles, or even entire films—it will challenge existing notions of copyright and originality. The debate will center around whether AI-generated content should be copyrighted and who owns this content (the creator of the AI, the user, or the AI itself?), and how to determine originality when AI can mimic styles or create entirely new ones.
4. **The rise of physical AI robots**

   This is not specifically something that I see becoming mainstream in 2025. However, I do believe that the concept/idea and development of physical AI robots will gain significant traction this year. These physical robots will be developed to perform a variety of tasks, from household chores to industrial manufacturing, and even in healthcare for surgery.

   [![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ziDh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F121eeb8f-7e75-4aea-9cd9-548eb7369c6f_1201x1422.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ziDh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F121eeb8f-7e75-4aea-9cd9-548eb7369c6f_1201x1422.jpeg)
5. **AI fakes will become a bigger issue and will spark questions surrounding what is legal vs illegal and how world governments should handle AI fakes**

   Deepfake technology, where AI can generate or manipulate video, audio, and images to make them appear authentic, will escalate in 2025. This can affect everything from personal reputation to political campaigns, etc. and will prompt legal and ethical debates about the use of AI in 2025.

Overall, my 2025 predictions paint a picture of AI evolving and becoming more embedded in daily life and business operations, as well as a broader focus/debate on managing its capabilities responsibly/ethically.

**My Big Picture Market Views for 2025:**

Big picture, I expect the valuation gap between Mega Cap Tech and the broader SPX to narrow in 2025. As I mentioned, earnings growth projections are expected to narrow between the Mag 7 and S&P 493 in 2025, so there will be opportunities for a catch up trade in 2025. We nailed the catch up trade in Small Caps with my [Small Cap 6 Portfolio](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-small-caps-with-massive-upside) in the 2H of 2024, and I remain bullish Small Caps for 2025.

While I can think of some potential negative catalysts for the market and valuations to peak in 2025, I am not predicting any of these right now based on my research. In my view, a 2025 Bear Case scenario would hinge on rising unemployment and/or re-accelerating inflation, which would force the Fed to stay put (or even raise) rates. The most resilient part of the US economy during this Fed rate hiking cycle has been the health of the consumer and the continued strength of the labor market. So long as the labor market remains strong and unemployment subdued, I expect another strong year for the consumer in 2025.

As for corporates, all signs are pointing to a very strong year in 2025. Consensus analyst estimates for 2025 are predicting an acceleration of YoY EPS growth in 2025. In 2024, we saw +7-11% YoY EPS growth per quarter. In 2025, analysts are expecting to see +12-17% EPS growth per quarter.

Absent one of the negative catalysts I mentioned, I will be looking for another positive (+10%+) year for the US market in 2025.

Generative AI should remain the dominant theme, but I expect it to be more of an evolving AI landscape where new/smaller winners continue to emerge (like we started to see in the 2H of 2024).
