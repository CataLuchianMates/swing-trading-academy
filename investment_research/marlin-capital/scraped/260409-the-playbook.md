#### **The 2026 Portfolio**

The 2026 Portfolio is my 1-year hold flagship portfolio. It is designed as my top picks/favorite holds for the year. But as I said [at the time of its release](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-picks-for-2026?utm_source=publication-search), this is NOT simply a buy and hold portfolio this year. In my [2025 Year-End Update report](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/2025-year-end-update?utm_source=publication-search), I cautioned that 2026 would require closer monitoring than the prior year’s portfolio. I said it would not be a “set it and forget it” portfolio like 2025.

Why? Because 1-year is actually NOT that long for the market.

That has proven to be exactly right. My thought process was that 2026 would be a much more challenging year after 3 consecutive years of >15% gains (only time in history we reached 4 was during the Dot Com Bubble). And as I highlighted in detail, we were seeing some late cycle market behaviors such as the most speculative names peaking and entering stage 4 downtrends (in October) + breadth deteriorating (the 6+ hindenburg omens).

I started the year with 35 longs and 2 shorts. Then I took it all the way down to 2 longs and 2 shorts. And now, I’m back to >60% long (14 longs) and 0% short. That is active management on a longer timeframe, not passive investing.

My framework is fundamental + technical. The fundamentals help me identify the best companies and industries. The technicals tell me when to own them and when to step aside.

**To be clear, my view is that the technicals should confirm the fundamental story.**

When the technicals are confirming the fundamentals (Stage 2 uptrend, healthy price action, rising moving averages), I hold. When the technicals are breaking down (Stage 3 topping patterns, Stage 4 downtrend), I sell, regardless of how strong the fundamentals still look.

Here’s why. I have talked about this Jeff Bezos quote before, and it is one of my favorites:

> “The stock is not the company. And the company is not the stock.”
>
> - Jeff Bezos

This is 100% true. A great company can be a terrible stock to hold if the price action is deteriorating and we are in a Bear Market. William O’Neil built his legendary trading career out of this philosophy. His philosophy centered on the idea that a good company is only a good stock if its price is actually appreciating. There is no such thing as a good stock. Only a stock that goes up.

That is why I exited many names this year, even though I remained bullish on many of those same companies long-term. The technicals told me to step aside, so I listened.

My sell rules are clear for these types of 1-year hold portfolios. Any stock I believe to have entered (or be entering) its Stage 4 downtrend gets sold. I covered the Stage Analysis framework in depth in [“Grinch Selloff”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/grinch-selloff?utm_source=publication-search) for those who want to go deeper. I would describe my sell rules as discretionary, although I do use some proprietary AI quant tools as part of my greater technical analysis (i.e, what stage a stock is currently in, whether it’s a quant buy, sell, or hold, etc.). But, this is just part of my technical analysis process, not the whole process.

In hindsight, I was wrong on my stage analysis on some names (GLW, TER, etc.). For leaders like GLW, TER, etc., these were not stage 3 tops or stage 4 downtrends. These more were likely consolidation phases within stage 2 uptrends (while the market and basically every non-leader was breaking down, which made it quite difficult).

At the same time, I was right on my stage analysis on other names. GRAL, EOSE, AVAV, etc. have gotten creamed since my exits. These were stage 4’s. Overall, this was a very unique and tricky market period to trade through. I can’t say I’ve traded through a market period quite like this one in my 10+ year career.

So, I was right and I was wrong. That’s trading (and life), so you better get used to it.

The good news, as I talked about last night, is that I protected my YTD gains (+12% YTD vs SPX -1%). And now, I can simply re-buy my favorite names. Which is exactly what I did this morning (adding 12 new longs). I am once again holding my 14 favorite long picks right now. This is exactly what I do when I change my view. I re-position.

To put it simply, my top picks are not going to breakout to new highs and run big without me. I will participate in the big move. But first, I need to confirm the uptrend so that I am protected on the downside. I need to confirm that we are in a Bull Market first. Confirmation is the price of having downside protection.

Why do I need to be protected on the downside?

See the 2022 Bear Market.

See the 2008 GFC.

See the 2000-02 Dot Com Bust.

Rest assured, we will see another major Bear Market like that again one day. **You can sleep peacefully at night knowing we will be 100% cash or net short at Marlin Capital when that time comes.**

So today, I re-entered my favorite names. And with that, every name in the 2026 Portfolio is a stock I currently believe to be in a Stage 2 uptrend with accelerating fundamentals. These are the strongest names in the strongest groups. Memory, Optical Connectivity, Data Center Cooling/Power, and AI Infrastructure.

The technicals are confirming the fundamentals in all these names. That is exactly the setup I want to be invested in. I’m currently ~60% long as of today’s close.

#### **The Active Trading Portfolio**

The Active Trading Portfolio is a completely different strategy.

This is shorter-term swing trading. Holding periods range from 1 day to a few months. The goal is to capitalize on the sharpest momentum moves in the market, and to do it with disciplined risk management.

Typically, my trades in this portfolio will have a <10% stop loss in place (for equity trades. For options trades I size for 0, meaning my risk is -100% loss). This is part of the disciplined risk management strategy I employ here.

My approach in this portfolio is entirely driven by the market’s positive feedback loop. In strong, trending markets (what I call an “easy dollar” environment), breakouts are working, momentum stocks are running big, and the tape is rewarding aggression. In those environments, I size up, I press winners, and I let my positions run. The big trades we hit on OSS calls (+340%), MRAM (+50% in 1 week), and AEVA (+31%) in January were all in an easy dollar trading environment.

In these types of environments, my bread and butter setup is the earnings/news catalyst gapper. Kristjan Kullamagi calls these “Episodic Pivots” (and it’s his favorite setups as well). I covered this in [“Headline Roulette”.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/headline-roulette?utm_source=publication-search)

On the other hand, in choppy, treacherous markets (what Minervini calls a “hard penny” environment), my approach shifts dramatically. Breakouts fail. Even the best setups (A+ earnings/news gappers) fail. Stocks chop around violently. You are fighting hard for every penny of profit and getting frustrated. In those environments, I stay in cash and wait for better days.

Right now, the Active Trading Portfolio is still in cash. But those “better days” I mentioned above appear to be on the way. There are plenty of setups I see emerging right now. Ideally, we will see some flagging/consolidation of today’s move to allow the moving averages to catch up.

As I have talked about before, in a strong market, there will be no shortage of setups. For the last couple months, there were very few setups. There was a real shortage. Now, all of the sudden, that looks to be changing.

We may now be entering a trending period for the market here, which means I would shift into active trading mode in this portfolio. For new subscribers, I recommend reading [“September Lull”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/september-lull?utm_source=publication-search) where I give a detailed breakdown of how winning stocks typically move, and [“Volatility Melt”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/volatility-melt?utm_source=publication-search) where I cover position sizing in the Active Trading Portfolio.
