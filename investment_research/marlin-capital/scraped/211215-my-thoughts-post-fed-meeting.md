# My Thoughts Post-Fed Meeting

We saw a relief rally in the markets post-Fed meeting.  I thought Powell handled the Q&A quite well and walked a tight line.  Overall, Powell was certainly not MORE hawkish than the market expected.  The market accurately priced Powell’s performance going into the FOMC meeting, at least in terms of Fed rate hike probabilities.  Powell did not act in a manner that surprised the markets, and that is why I believe we are seeing a relief rally.

Still, the Fed is going to be moving fast in 2022, and I’m not sure the market has fully internalized that reality yet, at least in rate sensitive sectors like high growth tech.

The recent sell off in growth stocks has occurred entirely because of the market discounting the end of asset purchases **in the future** and rate hikes **in the future**.  The actual liquidity dry up and economic slowdown has not even begun to go into effect yet.  As of this moment, the Fed is still in a highly accommodative stance and we still have very easy money policy.

**There are pretty much 2 options I see from here:**

**1.**       The market rallies into the Fed raising rates 2 meetings from now (May) and sells off when the policy goes into effect.

**2.**       The market continues to sell off into the rate raise (May) and bottoms by the time the policy actually goes into effect.

There is no escaping the eventual selling in tech though, if the Fed is indeed going to hike 6 times by the end of 2023.  Valuations are still too high historically for tight money policy and an aggressive tightening cycle.

My point is, we can either sell off now or later, but in my opinion, we have not reached a point where 6 rate hikes through 2023 have been adequately priced in, at least in rate sensitive stocks.  The only way out is if the Feds deviates from this roadmap, but that would really just be kicking the can further down the road.

As I’ve said for weeks, we are in a bear market.  These stocks need long bases to repair themselves on a technical basis.  I would not be expecting any type of V-Shaped recovery in rate sensitive stocks into a fed tightening cycle.

With that said, perhaps Powell has soothed the markets enough to put in a short term bottom.  I am keeping an open mind to this possibility, even though I continue to believe ARKK has not completely bottomed in this cycle.  If we follow scenario 1 I mentioned above and have put in a short term bottom for the next few months, I certainly will not remain short all the way through.

As you can see in the chart below, ARKK put in a short term bottom in May of 2021 before making new lows this December.  This is around 7 months of consolidation before the eventual move lower.  In scenario 1 I mentioned above, we could see a short term bottom into the May Fed meeting (around 5 months).

In the last consolidation, we saw a 37% bounce from the trough to the peak.  This time, a 20-30% bounce in ARKK (over the next 5 months) is certainly possible if Scenario 1 plays out. A 30% bounce from the recent low ($89.03) would take us to the ~$116 level, which is still below the recent November 4th highs.

The initial relief rally is not surprising to me, as I mentioned this morning.  This is why I was progressively locking in some profits on my ARKK short yesterday and today.

As I mentioned, I am going into the future believing either of the 2 scenarios I mentioned above may play out.  I will adjust my portfolio as 1 of the 2 scenarios becomes more likely to play out based off the market’s price action.  At this point, it is still too early to say.
