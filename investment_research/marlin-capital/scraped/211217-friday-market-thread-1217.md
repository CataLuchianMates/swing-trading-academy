**12:55 EST:**

Keep these 2 scenarios I outlined handy. In both cases, we are likely to see new lows. It is just a matter of when. With that said, these are certainly 2 very different scenarios from an active management standpoint.

**1.**       The market rallies into the Fed raising rates 2 meetings from now (May) and sells off when the policy goes into effect.

**2.**       The market continues to sell off into the rate raise (May) and bottoms by the time the policy actually goes into effect.

The 3rd scenario is that the Fed will walk back its plan for 6 rate hikes thru ‘23 and 8 rate hikes thru ‘24. Although the 3rd scenario could certainly play out, it is more of a hope than an investment strategy & I don’t expect it to be priced in by the market until/if the Fed actually pivots.

**4:25 EST:**

Today, we saw a 6% bounce in ARKK at the $89 support level. It’s hard to get overly excited about a 6% bounce off 52 week lows in a 40% drawdown bear market. Many growth stocks did not even finish green today. Still, Bear Market bounces can be quick and huge. The Nasdaq had many bounces over 20%+ during the Dot com bust:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!F2Kx!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5c0458b5-ddea-429e-8433-b58c960a676a_560x370.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!F2Kx!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5c0458b5-ddea-429e-8433-b58c960a676a_560x370.jpeg)

So, is that what’s happening right now? As you can see below, the weekly chart is in a clear downtrend, but we did get support 3 weeks in a row around $90. The evidence is mounting that ARKK does indeed want to bounce here. Again, in Scenario 1, we would be bouncing to the $110-$120 range.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Nlat!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F02847ed9-6a75-43ce-8f0e-9e08b9db577b_1082x843.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Nlat!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F02847ed9-6a75-43ce-8f0e-9e08b9db577b_1082x843.jpeg)

As for this being the ultimate bottom, remember, stocks will be in for a challenging year in 2022. A BofA study showed that in aggressive Fed tightening years, equities are negative roughly 80% of the time historically. Now, we have the end of QE to deal with in addition to an aggressive Fed tightening year. These events are bad for equities in general, but even worse for growth stocks. New lows are likely to come unless the Fed pivots.

But haven’t we sold off enough? Well if we look at software valuations (Jamin Ball Chart below), they are now back to where they were pre-Covid.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!m56p!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0b5d8ff4-0f6d-4eed-9132-f79005844706_1125x2436.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!m56p!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0b5d8ff4-0f6d-4eed-9132-f79005844706_1125x2436.png)

Stocks had historically high valuations back then, as they do right now, if you ignore the Post-Covid bubble.

In basically every bubble, stocks do not go from being in a Bubble to simply having high valuations. We have already seen TDOC, for example, go back below its post-Covid valuation, despite obvious advancements in the telehealth industry. Stocks overshoot to the upside and the downside. In terms of valuations, we have certainly not overshot to the downside yet.

In terms of learning about market cycles, bubbles, etc. my favorite book is Mastering the Market Cycle by Howard Marks. I recommend the book to anyone looking to learn more about market cycles (which are very important, in my opinion).
