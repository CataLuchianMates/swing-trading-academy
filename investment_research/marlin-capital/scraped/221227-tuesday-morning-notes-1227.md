# Tuesday Morning Notes 12.27

I hope everyone had a wonderful holiday weekend!

Technical Update

The S&P 500 finished off last week with a small bounce, closing at $3844.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!m4Ij!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe28c08ff-c883-406d-a4be-6e409a6adcfd_1123x842.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!m4Ij!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe28c08ff-c883-406d-a4be-6e409a6adcfd_1123x842.jpeg)

Technically, we have a potential Bear flag forming with key support at $3800. Futures have already given up most of their overnight gains as we head into today’s open. Still, it is possible to see a rally to $3900-$3908 resistance, as long as the $3800 support level holds. If these resistance levels break, I will be looking for the bounce to top out somewhere in the $3930-$3960 region.

If the key $3800 level breaks, support levels below include $3740-$3750 and $3698-$3708. $3703 is the 200 week moving average, a technical level of key historical significance that [I have discussed in the](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-pendulum-swings-both-ways) [past](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-pendulum-swings-both-ways).
