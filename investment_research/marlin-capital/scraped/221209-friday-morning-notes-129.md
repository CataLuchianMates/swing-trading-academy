# Friday Morning Notes 12.9

Technical Analysis

The S&P 500 bounced yesterday, closing just above the $3960 resistance level (21 EMA).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!h4aG!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc2576a9a-a7c9-45e9-bbc4-314d1a84381a_1191x903.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!h4aG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc2576a9a-a7c9-45e9-bbc4-314d1a84381a_1191x903.jpeg)

It appeared as if the market might want to make a run to the $4000 resistance level, but hotter than expected PPI data for November put a damper in those plans. Now, we are set for a gap down towards the $3940 level.

Support levels below remain the same - $3918-$3920, $3914, and the all important $3900-$3906 level.

Resistance wise, keep an eye on the $3960 and $4000 levels above.

Keep in mind next week is action packed, with November CPI data being released on Tuesday Morning and the Fed decision coming on Wednesday afternoon.
