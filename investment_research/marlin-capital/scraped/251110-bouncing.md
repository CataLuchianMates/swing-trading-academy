# Bouncing

Despite a modest rebound on Friday afternoon, US stocks declined sharply last week. The Nasdaq recorded its largest weekly pullback since April (Liberation Day).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gIaD!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa2d88b07-9b67-40c9-9d6d-cda2b32b2f2a_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gIaD!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa2d88b07-9b67-40c9-9d6d-cda2b32b2f2a_1396x653.png)

Here at Marlin Capital, we saw the writing on the wall for this impending market decline.

**Bad Breadth**

It all starts with bad breadth.

In the past few weeks, we saw market breadth narrow to some of its weakest readings ever. Underneath the surface, most stocks were already pulling back aggressively while the indices appeared fine.

As I highlighted over the past few weeks, weakness was spreading rapidly throughout the market (like a disease). Many more stocks were declining than advancing (see new lows vs highs, etc.), but the Mag 7 was keeping the indices afloat.

That changed last week.

The Mag 7 finally cracked, pulling down the broader indices. The SPX finished -1.6%, QQQ -3%, and IWM -2%.

MSFT hit an 8 day losing streak, its longest since 2011. META has followed through to the downside post-earnings. NVDA (the AI king) pulled back over -15% at Friday’s lows.

We also saw weak earnings reactions across the board in Tech, even on strong numbers (PLTR, DASH, ANET, etc.). When strong earnings can’t drive prices higher, it is a concerning sign for the market.

**Breadth Matters Both Ways**

If you’re an objective analyst, then weak breadth should be just as important to you as strong breadth.

Towards the very beginning of this rally, strong market breadth (the Zweig Breadth Thrust) gave us confidence that the bottom was in coming off the April lows. This allowed us to get heavily positioned in the early stages of this rally.

This is why we take extreme breadth readings (both bullish and bearish) very seriously here at Marlin Capital. As I always say, it’s a market of stocks. To gauge the overall health of the market, you need to look at the entire stock universe (not just 7 stocks). The S&P 500 is market cap weighted, so it is very easy to miss the forest from the trees if you are stuck on the indices.

Speaking of bad breadth, last week triggered the **4th Hindenburg Omen** of the past month.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Jjb6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F76d3fab7-c963-4c24-a149-1d24864940fc_2048x1164.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Jjb6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F76d3fab7-c963-4c24-a149-1d24864940fc_2048x1164.jpeg)

The Hindenburg Omen is a technical indicator that signals immense market weakness underneath the surface. It is triggered when a significant number of stocks hit both 52-week highs and 52-week lows simultaneously (indicating weak market breadth).

Historically, forward returns are extremely weak following these signals. The past 30 times this happened, the SPX fell 83% of the time over the next 2 months for an average return of -6.4%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-S4r!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5e197af-92a5-4772-aed3-a43142ea7f82_1496x1778.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-S4r!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5e197af-92a5-4772-aed3-a43142ea7f82_1496x1778.jpeg)

Again, if you’re going to cover the bullish Zweig Breadth Thrust, then you can not also in the same breath (no pun intended) dismiss the bearish Hindenburg Omen’s. This indicator is essentially the opposite of the Zweig Breadth Thrust.

In my view, a stock trader ought to act like a detective. Gather all the evidence. Evaluate it objectively. Arrive at your conclusion.

For us, the evidence has been crystal clear here. The market shifted into clear **risk off** mode over the last few weeks (following Trump’s China Tariff tweet).

Weak breadth. 📉  
Poor earnings reactions. 📉  
Most stocks breaking down, while only a handful were holding up. 📉

The writing was on the wall for an SPX pullback.

**Friday’s Bounce**

Friday’s afternoon bounce was promising, but a few hours of strength does not erase several weeks of risk off price action.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nKxc!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe4b3e700-24db-4dc7-9588-6287006fb681_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nKxc!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe4b3e700-24db-4dc7-9588-6287006fb681_1396x653.png)

It could be a major low (both short-term and/or long-term), but we need more evidence to make that determination. We need bullish follow-through early this week. Specifically, we need breadth to start improving. We need to see a contraction in new lows (vs highs) and an improvement in the advance/decline line. We need to see risk appetite start to recover.

**Government Shutdown** **Ends**

Today, we received big news concerning the end of the Government Shutdown.

Fox News just reported that Republicans and enough Senate Democrats have reached a deal to end the government shutdown after 40 days.

This could help lift sentiment early this week. Historically, market returns are above average (with an ~88% success rate 2-12 months out) following the end of a government shutdown.

**Active Trading Portfolio**

As a trader, I’m simply not a bottom picker.

We have largely sat out the last few weeks once the market shifted to risk off mode. This is when I shift into capital preservation mode, trying to preserve both my financial AND mental capital.

We have kept our drawdown low, stayed disciplined, and avoided the mental/emotional drain of trading a bearish tape. Trying to trade in a market environment that is not conducive to your strategy is a good way to lose money and frustrate yourself.

Many traders try to time bottoms, and here’s what often ends up happening:

They fail the first few times. This creates a feeling where they basically need to nail the actual bottom to make their initial losses back. And by that point, they’re trapped emotionally. In a major market correction, their losses can snowball out of control. Sometimes, by the time the real bottom comes, they are exhausted from trying and failing so many times.

If you are prone to FOMO or are overly reactive, this kind of bearish/choppy environment will eat you alive.

We don’t play that game.

We sit out, stay patient, preserve our financial and mental capital, and wait for the environment to improve. If you do not have a gambler’s mentality, you should really have 0 problem waiting for an environment that is better suited for your strategy. That time will come, so what’s the big rush? Do not succumb to FOMO. Know your strategy. Know your edge. And know the environment. You do not always need to be trading.

**Big Picture**

My big picture market view remains the same here. So far, we have gotten the -5% Nasdaq pullback I was anticipating. Meanwhile, many higher beta names have already fallen -30%+ from highs.

I continue to believe the indices will rally back to highs once this risk off phase exhausts. In my view, we are likely closer to the end of this index pullback than the beginning. For the SPX, my big picture target remains $7300 following this recovery.

During this upcoming market recovery, I believe many of the beaten-down former leaders will likely bounce big, but will NOT fully recover back to highs. I believe they will end up forming a lower high (which will eventually be shortable).

I continue to believe many names have already entered their bear phases and have most likely peaked for this cycle. In my view, we have already hit “peak risk taking” during this cycle, and I expect tougher sledding ahead no matter what. With that said, as long as the major indices are in an uptrend, there will be new long opportunities ahead. My plan is to focus on the leading earnings names that are still making new highs. These are the leading stocks in the market, not the former leaders from July/August that are now down -40%.
