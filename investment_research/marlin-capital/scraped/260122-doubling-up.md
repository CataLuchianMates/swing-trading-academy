#### Market Analysis and Watchlist

Market momentum wobbled the last couple days in response to a bunch of different geopolitical headlines. Today’s big one: \*TRUMP: HAVE FRAMERWORK FOR FURUE DEAL ON GREENLAND WITH NATO (BBG).

With geopolitical headlines like this, all that really matters to me is the market reaction. If you remember back to mid-October, it was a Trump post (threatening 100% tariffs on China) that started the whole “risk off” period for stocks. Many speculative/high beta names (like OKLO, IONQ, etc.) declined >50% in ~2 months following Trump’s post. And yet, Trump’s threat (of 100% tariffs on China) has amounted to nothing. Nothing actually came of this headline.

My point is, even a small, seemingly insignificant geopolitical headline can shake the trees. On the other hand, even a massive, ultra-significant geopolitical headline can have very little effect on the market. In my view, trying to predict the impact of different geopolitical headlines is a futile exercise. The market will tell you when to be concerned or not to be concerned. For example, even though the mid-October Trump threat (of 100% tariffs on China) was nothing, it started a major “risk off” event/period for stocks. Here at Marlin Capital, we shifted to “risk off” mode in October-late December, not because of a headline/prediction, but because it is exactly what the market told us to do.

As for the current market and current headlines, it’s still early, but the action/response is not overly bearish to me (especially when you look at the Russell 2000 Index of Small Caps). **The R2K has now outperformed the S&P 500 on all 13 trading sessions so far in 2026.**

**Small Caps outperforming is NOT a “risk off” signal. In fact, it is a “risk on” signal.**

My point is, it’s hard to get too bearish with Small Caps outperforming and breaking out of a multi-year base.
