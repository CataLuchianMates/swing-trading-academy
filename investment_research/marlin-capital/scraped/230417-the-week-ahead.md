# The Week Ahead

Stocks continued to rise last week as we saw headline inflation surprise to the downside once again. On Wednesday, [March CPI](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/wednesday-morning-notes-412) came in lower than expected at 5.0% YoY (versus 5.2% exp).

In response to [Wednesday’s](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/wednesday-morning-notes-412) lower than expected CPI number, the 2Y Treasury Yield continued to decline, supporting the expectation that the Fed will cut rates 3 times before the end of 2023.  The futures market is currently pricing in 3 rate cuts by the end of the year, and does not expect the Fed to follow through with their “tough talk” on keeping rates higher for longer.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PXe9!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F85cc18bc-c29e-419f-b24e-c32cc2c6de69_606x425.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PXe9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F85cc18bc-c29e-419f-b24e-c32cc2c6de69_606x425.png)

The US 2Y Yield, which is (historically) a reliable indicator of where the Federal funds rate will peak, is suggesting that the Fed will likely stop at 5% for this cycle. The 2Y yield peaked at 5.05% on March 8th, and has since declined to 3.96% as of Friday 4/14 .

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4q_w!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb54e98c8-e3ba-413b-a89c-2860f91134d2_1200x770.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4q_w!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb54e98c8-e3ba-413b-a89c-2860f91134d2_1200x770.png)

A falling 2Y is also one of the Macro indicators on my “[Bear Market Bottom Checklist”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-market-bottom-checklist).  Prior to each of the last 6 Bear Market lows dating back to 1980, the US 2Y yield peaked and fell at least 50 bps.  Historically, the Fixed Income market anticipates Fed rate cuts before they happen (and before the market bottoms).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!l9Is!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F80b2d96b-020e-4e0a-8a52-4823c478e15b_739x511.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!l9Is!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F80b2d96b-020e-4e0a-8a52-4823c478e15b_739x511.png)

Based off current pricing, the Fed Funds Futures market sees the final Fed rate hike coming in May. On average, equities continue to rise (historically) following the last Fed rate hike of the cycle.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BuZM!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6548264b-9a16-41c4-bfc7-a4b0c8e4d5f9_1284x1029.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BuZM!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6548264b-9a16-41c4-bfc7-a4b0c8e4d5f9_1284x1029.png)

However, stocks have historically declined following the first Fed rate cut.  The futures market believes the first Fed rate cut will occur late this summer (see FFR futures chart above).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2EPx!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd735ed8c-2c65-407f-9cb9-d57346633833_1241x1019.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2EPx!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd735ed8c-2c65-407f-9cb9-d57346633833_1241x1019.png)

Meanwhile, we saw a 3rd straight week of Fed balance sheet contraction (-$119B in total) as QT has effectively resumed (at least for the time being).  This could become a big headwind for stocks again, so it will be important to continue to keep an eye on the below chart over the coming months.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!l0qS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7f34aaf5-4217-4ee4-9e93-b450ae86d2c3_850x549.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!l0qS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7f34aaf5-4217-4ee4-9e93-b450ae86d2c3_850x549.png)

The market has remained stable, while positioning and sentiment signals suggest that fears of a wider spread banking crisis have been fully alleviated due to the Fed’s intervention.  Positioning has been moving back up over the last 2 weeks, but is still not back to neutral following the regional banking crisis.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vofG!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F779970d8-901a-4d6b-b32c-d531ec9dca47_1192x826.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vofG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F779970d8-901a-4d6b-b32c-d531ec9dca47_1192x826.png)

Meanwhile, positioning in S&P 500 futures remains heavily short, helping to further squeeze the market higher.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-go4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe88b4aae-b33b-4102-bd64-175c4c160d9b_1284x1009.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-go4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe88b4aae-b33b-4102-bd64-175c4c160d9b_1284x1009.png)

This cautious and short bias positioning has acted as a cushion for stocks, suggesting a lot of bad news is already priced in with the Fed backstopping the banking system.

Meanwhile, consensus is expecting S&P 500 EPS to fall by -7% YoY in Q1, which would represent the trough in SPX earnings for this cycle.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zek0!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9d64fb96-c241-402a-ab18-833535983d94_608x431.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zek0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9d64fb96-c241-402a-ab18-833535983d94_608x431.png)

In my view, these future projections remain a major risk to the market, as they are unlikely to be met if the [economy continues to deteriorate](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-3-factors).

Big Tech earnings kick off this week with Netflix reporting on Tuesday and Tesla reporting on Wednesday.  These reports and guidance (if any) are likely to contribute heavily to the market’s swings this week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MY_I!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F12d95fc3-6df4-442b-92c8-e5f2697d99a3_1920x1080.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MY_I!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F12d95fc3-6df4-442b-92c8-e5f2697d99a3_1920x1080.png)

Technically, the market has remained strong and continues to climb the proverbial “wall of worry”.  With that said, many stocks on my watchlist continue to fail and chop at key breakout points, as the action in individual names is still not ideal.  Buyers have remained active into market weakness, preventing a flush and creating choppy price action for many stocks.

As we head into this week, the S&P 500 continues to boast a more bullish technical setup despite Friday’s reversal.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3-AL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F89cffc22-fc42-4114-a959-44004dab709d_959x721.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3-AL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F89cffc22-fc42-4114-a959-44004dab709d_959x721.png)

For the SPX, $4150-$4160 remains the key resistance level to watch ahead, along with $4190-$4200 (YTD highs).

This week is also April Options Expiration (OpEx), indicating that we could see increased volatility.  Since the beginning of the Bear Market in January 2022, I have found that OpEx week has been positive only 20% of the time, while the week after OpEx has been positive 64% of the time.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZGHU!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fddaaf5c5-b0d4-4d3e-afb5-f8f72fc5b986_462x423.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZGHU!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fddaaf5c5-b0d4-4d3e-afb5-f8f72fc5b986_462x423.png)

With that said, we have seen better performance on OpEx weeks recently since this rally off lows began. We have seen 2 large squeezes on OpEx week since October ‘22 lows.

If the market remains stable, we could see some bullish post-earnings trades in individual names arise this earnings season.  These are some of my favorite trade opportunities and provided me with some of my best trades.
