# Bear Market Bottom Checklist

In this week’s report, I will cover 8 Technical and Macro indicators that have typically occurred before prior Bear Market bottoms.  I believe that studying past market cycles and utilizing a data driven approach is the most effective way to navigate this treacherous market environment.

I have compiled a list of 8 indicators that have historically signaled Bear Market bottoms in the past 7 instances, dating back to 1973.  There are 3 Technical and 5 Macro indicators that I believe will be the most important factors to watch moving forward.  Below is my full Bear Market Bottom Checklist.

**Technical Indicators:**

1.       **S&P 500 Golden Cross: 21 EMA > 60 EMA > 200 SMA**

I have discussed my definition of a Golden Cross [here](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-trend-is-your-friend).  In laymen’s terms, this is when the short term, medium term, and long term trend lines are all in unison and point up.  The Bullish Golden Cross is a technical indicator that signals the downtrend has ended, and we have officially entered a technical uptrend on all three time major time frames.

In the prior 7 major bear markets dating back to 1973, there are no examples of an SPX golden cross resulting in new lows for the market.  **In other words, there were no false positive examples of a bullish Golden Cross.**  Each time the S&P 500 gave a bullish golden cross, the market had already bottomed.  An example in the 2008 Bear Market is attached below.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vtIM!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4c66a357-f5af-4d9e-bfd1-375230110a94_1327x637.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vtIM!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4c66a357-f5af-4d9e-bfd1-375230110a94_1327x637.png)

We have not yet seen a bullish golden cross in 2022.

**2.       VIX spike above 45**

The VIX is Wall Street’s Fear Gauge.  The product was invented in 1987 to measure the level of risk, fear, or stress in the market.  Since the invention of the VIX, no major Bear Market has ended without a VIX spike above 45 first.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sB2c!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fca66ec6f-f70b-42ec-b73e-f57ad88c1172_1329x625.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sB2c!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fca66ec6f-f70b-42ec-b73e-f57ad88c1172_1329x625.png)

The VIX spike is a sign of capitulation or panic selling that typically occurs before the market can start to bottom. We have not yet seen a VIX spike above 45 in 2022.

**3.       ERP increased 75 BPs**

Prior Bear Markets were associated with a rising Equity Risk Premium (ERP), as increased risk in stocks leads to investors requiring a higher ERP (to compensate for such risk).  Historically, equities have not bottomed until sufficiently pricing in a downturn via higher ERP of at least 75 bps.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!UEt2!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb768732d-e232-4499-91a8-a7ab1f2e1184_735x449.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!UEt2!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb768732d-e232-4499-91a8-a7ab1f2e1184_735x449.png)

This time around, the ERP for the S&P 500 has only risen about 20 bps from its 12 month low in September 2021 (<2 months before the SPX peaked).

**Macro Indicators:**

**1.       Fed cutting rates**

In each of the previous 7 Bear Markets dating back to 1973, the S&P 500 did not bottom until AFTER the Fed began cutting rates.  On average, the index bottomed 11 months following the first rate cuts.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HjFe!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fddf821cb-21d9-4b0b-9cb5-828bd98415e5_739x487.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HjFe!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fddf821cb-21d9-4b0b-9cb5-828bd98415e5_739x487.png)

BofA forecasts the first rate hikes to come in Q3 2023, while many Federal Reserve members have adamantly expressed that there will be no rate cuts in 2023.  The trajectory of future rate cuts will depend on inflation and the labor market, above all else, as I have highlighted in [past reports](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/back-to-reality).  In any case, I do not believe rate cuts are a realistic proposition until late 2023 at the earliest.

**2.       Unemployment Spikes/Jobless Claims Spike**

The labor market is a very important factor to watch for its effect on equities, as I have highlighted [here](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/back-to-reality) and [here](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/inflation-versus-unemployment-research).  [Last week](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/back-to-reality), I showed that the 2000 and 2008 Bear Market Bottoms closely followed the peak in jobless claims.  I have also [written about](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/inflation-versus-unemployment-research) why the labor market is especially important to watch in periods of high inflation.    If 2022-23 Unemployment trends follow the average of the previous 4 major inflation spikes (>8% CPI), Unemployment should spike to peak of 6-8%. Recall that on average, we saw a 93% increase in the Unemployment Rate from the time CPI spiked above 8% to the cycle peak in Unemployment.

Additionally, rising unemployment is typically a necessary ingredient before the Fed begins to cut rates.  In 6 of the last 7 major Bear Markets (excluding 1987), the Unemployment Rate ticked higher substantially prior to the market bottoming.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!--O0!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F295767f1-b40c-451b-9c22-cbc0bc8a8449_766x418.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!--O0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F295767f1-b40c-451b-9c22-cbc0bc8a8449_766x418.png)

The same is true for claims, with 6 out of the last 7 Bear Markets (excluding 1987) seeing a major YoY surge in jobless claims prior to the market bottoming.  Currently, the YoY change in jobless claims remains negative.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!C0x6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe3da8fa5-9a15-4a34-8571-efee1f5517d1_1268x734.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!C0x6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe3da8fa5-9a15-4a34-8571-efee1f5517d1_1268x734.png)

**3.       2/10 Yield Curve Steepens**

A steepening 2/10 yield curve is a sign of improving economic conditions and/or a dovish Fed pivot.  In each of the last 6 Bear Markets dating back to 1982, the 2/10 yield curve had steepened before the market bottomed.  If the yield curve was inverted, as it is currently, it un-inverted prior to the market bottoming.  In other words, the market never bottomed as long as the yield curve was continuing to invert further.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!n_us!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc0b63b7d-c488-4ae3-ae27-c199cf00a4b4_715x471.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!n_us!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc0b63b7d-c488-4ae3-ae27-c199cf00a4b4_715x471.png)

Last month (August ‘22), the 2/10 yield curve inverted further, implying the fixed income market has not fully priced in the economic downturn to come.

**4.       2 YR Yield peaks and begins to fall**

I have [highlighted why](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/late-summer) Marlin Capital subscribers should continue to watch the 2 YR yield closely, as chart overlays have long suggested a higher 2 YR yield results in lower equity prices/valuations.  This is confirmed by my analysis of the previous 6 Bear Markets (once again, my data source does not go back to 1973 on this one).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!l9Is!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F80b2d96b-020e-4e0a-8a52-4823c478e15b_739x511.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!l9Is!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F80b2d96b-020e-4e0a-8a52-4823c478e15b_739x511.png)

In the prior 6 Bear Markets, the 2 YR yield fell at least 50 bps from its 6 month highs before bottoming.

In our current cycle, the 2 YR yield is rising to new highs, and just hit its highest level since November 2007.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Pci0!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F29d3f833-7198-4cfa-b077-faba945dfa87_906x450.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Pci0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F29d3f833-7198-4cfa-b077-faba945dfa87_906x450.png)

**5.       US ISM PMI bottoms**

Historically, the S&P 500 has not bottomed until PMI bottoms and begins to improve.  In 6 of the last 7 Bear Market (excluding 1973), the PMI improved vs. its 12 month lows prior to the SPX bottoming.  In other words, as long as PMI continues to decline, it is difficult for stocks to fully bottom.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YZ3p!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd9df12b9-ae9d-43bf-adbc-679aa5871699_733x503.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YZ3p!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd9df12b9-ae9d-43bf-adbc-679aa5871699_733x503.png)

Currently, the leading indicators for PMI (including Housing NAHB) are pointing to a further decline in PMI over the next 12 months.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7-Y7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6c7d6ed9-0cca-4469-885b-e9474bd8377a_1034x546.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7-Y7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6c7d6ed9-0cca-4469-885b-e9474bd8377a_1034x546.png)

**Summary Points**

In a Bear Market, it is better to be late than early.  The historical signals that flashed prior to previous Bear Market bottoms have not yet signaled in 2022.  The indicators are to pointing to more downside for stocks ahead.  My base case for the S&P 500 remains an ultimate bottom in the $2900-$3300 range.
