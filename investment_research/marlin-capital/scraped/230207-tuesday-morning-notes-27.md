# Tuesday Morning Notes 2.7

Technical Update

The S&P 500 pulled back yesterday to start off the week. The $4100 support level held on its first test, making a low of $4093 on the day before closing at $4111.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IoTD!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc50d5614-bbc7-4803-b8fe-a4e3a04730ad_948x723.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IoTD!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc50d5614-bbc7-4803-b8fe-a4e3a04730ad_948x723.jpeg)

In my view, the technical setup remains bullish here as long we hold $4000 on the SPX. A decisive move below the $4k mark would open the door for the next wave down. Until then, the trend is your friend, and the trend is up.

On a pullback, key support levels to watch below include $4100, $4050-$4060, $4030, and $4000.

Key resistance levels ahead include $4150 and $4190-$4200.
