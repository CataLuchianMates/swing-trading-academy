# 2026 Portfolio Update

Marlin Capital Readers,

A lot has happened since my last report on [Sunday night](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/headline-fatigue). Tonight, I am making significant changes to the 2026 Portfolio.

But first, let me give you the lay of the land.

**The Ceasefire**

After weeks of escalation (and a terrifying threat this morning that “a whole civilization will die tonight”), President Trump announced a 2-week ceasefire with Iran tonight. Pakistan brokered the deal, and Iran accepted.

S&P 500 and Nasdaq futures have popped over >2% on the news.

Is this the definitive turn we’ve been waiting for? I believe it could be. Here is my thinking. 👇

For the past several weeks, the Iran conflict has been the single biggest overhang on this market. Oil spiked. Volatility surged. Risk assets got crushed. I responded by raising cash aggressively in the 2026 Portfolio. That was painful, but it was the right call.

**Because, most importantly, we protected our YTD gains (+11% YTD vs SPX -3%).**

Capital preservation is the job when the tape is telling you to step aside. Now, the tape is telling me something different.

With the ceasefire in place, the war risk is coming out of the market. Oil is plunging. Futures are ripping. And most importantly, the S&P 500 and Nasdaq both look set to reclaim their 200-day SMAs at the open tomorrow.

**My Strategy**

Over the past ~2 months, I have been in full capital preservation mode. I told everyone that I would be patient and that I would focus on protecting capital. That discipline allowed us to preserve our gains (and relax) while waiting for the fat pitches to come back.

But discipline is not just about knowing when to step aside. It is about knowing when to step back in.

To be clear, I am not immediately going “all in” here. But I will be redeploying capital into my highest-conviction names.

As I talked about in [“Hard Penny”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/hard-penny?utm_source=publication-search), this is how I like to handle a market transition period. I start with some positions and let the market give me feedback. If the feedback is positive (the gap up holds, the 200 SMA reclaim sustains, follow-through buying emerges), I add more. Progressive exposure.

Here is exactly what I am doing. 👇

**Closing Both Shorts**

I will be closing both our short positions (QUBT and NNE) at tomorrow’s open.

The ceasefire changes the risk/reward on shorts for me. I am not going to fight a 200 SMA reclaim on a major geopolitical catalyst. These shorts served their purpose over the past several weeks.

Overall, we gained >20% on both these short trades.

**Current Holdings**

VIAV and GEV are the 2 longs I held through this entire period. Both are leaders in their respective groups (Optical Connectivity and AI Power), and both held up remarkably well through the worst of the selloff. That relative strength is exactly why I kept them.

**Adding 12 New Longs**

Tomorrow at the open, I will be adding 12 new long positions to the 2026 Portfolio at ~4-5% weighting each.

These are my highest-conviction names across the 4 themes I have been most bullish on all year: Memory, Optical Connectivity, Data Center Cooling/Power, and AI Infrastructure. These are the strongest names in the strongest groups. The fundamentals are accelerating. The technicals are confirming. And with the geopolitical overhang lifting, these are the names that can run the hardest.

I covered the Memory and Optical Supercycles in detail in [“Leaders Shine Bright”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/leaders-shine-bright) :

[“Memory Supercycle”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/leaders-shine-bright) — why Memory is the hottest theme on Wall Street in 2026

[“Optical Connectivity Supercycle”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/leaders-shine-bright) — why Optical Components are the infrastructure backbone of the AI revolution

After tomorrow’s additions, the 2026 Portfolio will move to >60% long and <40% cash (and 0% short). I am still keeping some cash on the sidelines intentionally. If the market gives me positive feedback over the coming days and weeks (the ceasefire holds, the 200 SMA reclaim sustains, leadership broadens), I will add the remaining positions.
