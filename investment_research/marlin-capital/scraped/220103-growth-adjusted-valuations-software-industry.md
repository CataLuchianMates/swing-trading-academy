# Growth Adjusted Valuations: Software Industry

Let’s take a look at where valuations stand in the Cloud Software space to begin 2022.  I use a growth adjusted valuation ratio to help balance valuations and future growth expectations.  It will be interesting to continue to watch valuations in the Software industry as the year goes on.

The most expensive stocks, by this metric, are NET, TEAM, CFLT, ZS, and BILL.

The cheapest stocks, by this metric, are PATH, DOCN, PLTR, TWLO, and OKTA.

---

**My Takeaway:**

Historically speaking, none of these valuations are “cheap”.  I will continue to steer clear of the most expensive stocks as I believe these are the most vulnerable in a rising rate environment.

The cheapest stocks comparatively, like PATH, DOCN, etc. are still not cheap enough for me to buy purely based off valuation.  I expect valuations to continue to compress in the Software space in the first half of 2022.  As I mentioned, I view the most expensive stocks as the most vulnerable to multiple compression.

In 2021, I consolidated my Long Term account to my 12 highest conviction positions.  In the Software Industry, I am most bullish long term on the growth of Cybersecurity and Big Data.  Currently, CRWD and DDOG are my top 2 picks in the aforementioned industries (and have been for awhile, I bought CRWD both in the $60s).

In the above linked report, I called out Asana and Monday.com as stocks with intriguing financials, but companies that I do not see myself holding in 2028.  This is generally how I feel about many stocks in the Software space right now.

As for which names I am watching, my top 2 in the software space are SentinelOne (S) and Snowflake (SNOW).  Once again, I’m sticking with the high level software themes I am most bullish on (Cybersecurity and Data).  SentinelOne and Snowflake are the two companies projected to growth the fastest over the next two years (in the whole Software industry).

On a growth adjusted basis, SentinelOne is trading at a below average multiple compared to its peers.  If the stock falls into the bottom five (<0.85x) in the above chart, I may start to consider opening a long term position.  As for Snowflake, its valuation severely limits its potential upside in my opinion.  I do not see the stock as having an attractive R/R at these levels (85x NTM Gross Profit).
