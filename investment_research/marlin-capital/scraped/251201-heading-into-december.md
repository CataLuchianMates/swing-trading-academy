# Heading Into December

November was an eventful month for US stocks with the major market indices staging their first real pullback since April 7th lows.

At November lows, the SPX pulled back -5.8%, the NDX fell -8.9%, and the RTY dropped -9.4%.

Despite the early-mid month pullback, we closed out November on a high note with the SPX rallying back to within ~1% of ATHs after a +3.7% advance last week.

#### The Big Picture

First off, let’s take a look at the big picture.

Here at Marlin Capital, we have been comparing the Q1 ‘25 Tariff Tantrum to the 1998 LTCM market correction [since May](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-big-picture). This comparison has played out to a T so far.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dWiq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6abcbdee-0384-4ca3-aa19-409978f0fc6e_1284x2331.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dWiq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6abcbdee-0384-4ca3-aa19-409978f0fc6e_1284x2331.jpeg)

That is - a big, Macro driven market correction in the face of a generational technological advancement.

In 1998, that generational technological advancement was the Internet, which gave us the Dot Com Bubble.

In 2025, that generational technological advancement is Artificial Intelligence. This has given us the AI Bubble.

So, where are we right now in the AI Bubble heading into December 2025?

This chart from Fidelity came across my desk last week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nt7C!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F36ff5a8d-b901-443d-b001-050bbf052aeb_799x482.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nt7C!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F36ff5a8d-b901-443d-b001-050bbf052aeb_799x482.png)

Based on this overlay, it has the S&P 500 peaking for the cycle in October 2026 (~1 yr from now). This would mean we currently have 1 year left in the AI Bubble.

From my analysis, I believe we are in a similar spot right now.

Coming into November, the S&P 500 had just rallied 6 straight months off April lows. November was the first negative month for the SPX (-0.5%) since April. This was our first real market pullback since we bottomed at $4835 on April 7th.

Prior to November, we had been a clean, low volatility uptrend for 6 straight months off April 7th lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9emR!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3d4ff74e-97a6-4e27-8449-2759a3989767_1012x675.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9emR!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3d4ff74e-97a6-4e27-8449-2759a3989767_1012x675.png)

During the Dot Com Bubble, the same exact thing happened following 1998 lows. The S&P 500 rallied for 5 straight months in a clean, low volatility uptrend before the first down month/real pullback.

At this point in the Dot Com Bubble, the SPX had about 1 year remaining before peaking in 2000. I have marked up the 1997-2002 SPX monthly chart below:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9nLz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fefe99be4-391a-4aa1-9007-76bf34463b0d_1012x675.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9nLz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fefe99be4-391a-4aa1-9007-76bf34463b0d_1012x675.png)

Based off my Dot Com Bubble comparison, I believe we are currently in March 1999. The SPX peaked 1 year later, in March 2000.

As you can see above, following the first down month/pullback off 1998 lows, the SPX would go on to make new highs. But, the clean uptrend was over. We were entering a choppier and much more volatile point in the market cycle.

Additionally, March 1999 is the period where the most speculative names began to peak. This was what I would consider “peak risk appetite” for the cycle, as the highest beta stocks never recovered back to highs like the SPX did.

As I have talked about before, the most speculative names historically top 8-14 months before the SPX does. This happened during the 2022 Covid Bubble/Bust as well, with SPACs, ARKK, ZM, ROKU, FUBO, etc. peaking up to 1 yr before the SPX did.

This is the part of the Bull Cycle that I believe we are now. In my view, the S&P 500 is well on its way to making new highs following this mini-correction (and we likely have another year + of this Bull Cycle). My big picture SPX target remains $7300. But, not every stock will go on to make new highs. There are some former leaders that have most likely peaked for the cycle. Again, this is consistent with what we saw in prior/comparable Bull cycles.

At this point in the cycle, I believe it is best to focus on the top names. These are the names that will have the potential for a real blowoff top if the SPX hits my big picture target ($7300+).

As I talked about last week, the good thing about this mini-market correction is that it came during Q3 earnings season. This gave us a chance to see which earnings gaps the market really liked originally. And then, we were able to see which of those earnings names held up the strongest during the November market weakness.

There are tons of stocks setting up right now. Breadth broadened out considerably last week. In fact, we could be looking at a potential Zweig Breadth Thrust this week (more on that this week if it triggers).
