# Losing Steam

The AI Trade got beat up today with markets focused on CoreWeave, which cut its annual revenue forecast last night. CoreWeave now sees 2025 revenue coming in at ~ $5.1B, trailing the average analyst estimate of $5.29B.

CRWV attributed the revenue warning to a delay at a third-party-data-center partner (RTRS). The specific bottleneck involved “power-shell” data centers where physical infrastructure was not completed on schedule.

While long-term AI demand remains strong, CoreWeave’s revenue warning highlights how shorter-term AI CapEx could potentially be throttled by constraints in power, labor, materials, etc. CRWV finished -16% today on these disappointing results. In a market where AI stocks could do no wrong a month and a half ago, this is a stark change of character for a former AI leader.

CRWV’s revenue warning dragged down the entire AI trade today, specifically Data Center companies (NBIS -7%, APLD -9%, IREN -5%, CIFR -5%, WULF -14%).

Nebius announced solid earnings results this morning, but finished -7% on the day.

The company announced a fresh $3B deal with Meta over the next 5 years. Management also significantly boosted guidance related to power capacity, seeing contracted power at more than 2.5 GW at the end of calendar year 2026, up from 1 GW previously.

Based off these projections, I see a path to $7B+ in ARR for Nebius in the next 5 years. NBIS is already at $4.4B+ in ARR once the Microsoft and Meta deals kick in. This is a company that did $146M in rev this quarter (~$600M annual run rate). We could be looking at a 10x in ARR here.

A month and half ago, this stock would have been up +20% on this type of report. But now, things have changed.

When the Bearish disease starts to spread across the market, fundamentals and positive news don’t seem to matter much.

We trimmed 70% of the SMID Cap 6 when we first noticed the market environment changing, and we are left with a decision on the rest now. As I talked about last week, I only make trades on the SMID Cap 6 Portfolio based off weekly and monthly charts. Last Friday’s afternoon bounce was promising as we formed some nice weekly hammer candles (potential bottom candles). I had hoped those weekly lows could hold this week, but so far, they are not. As of now, my plan is to exit any SMID Cap 6 name that closes below last week’s lows this Friday. Stay tuned for updates.

I have covered this phenomenon in detail here at Marlin Capital, but I am going to keep harping on it:

**When the most speculative groups top, it is the beginning of the Bear Cycle (in my view).** This is because the most speculative groups topping is the moment of max risk taking for the cycle. **It is the moment of max bullishness.**

Think of the market like a pendulum. It swings from 1 extreme to the other. From max bearishness to max bullishness. April Liberation Day was max bearishness. We hit -2z total positioning, and there was panic in the air. There was a rush to the exits. Futures were getting halted down every night.

Since those April lows, we have swung to another extreme. AI Stocks entered a Bubble, which has been our big picture thesis all along here at Marlin Capital. Stocks like OKLO (pre-revenue) 10xed off April lows, rallying from $17 to $193 in <6 months.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!haSL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcde1c82f-d0eb-4591-a651-80225f1e70d9_1566x846.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!haSL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcde1c82f-d0eb-4591-a651-80225f1e70d9_1566x846.png)

These are the kind of gains you only see in a Bubble.

Bullishness and risk taking were steadily increasing every month. This trend continued from May into September, and then accelerated even more. Things were starting to get crazy. Stocks were doubling in a month. Our SMID Cap 6 portfolio hit >80% gains overall in <4 months.

And then, something changed in October. A switch flipped. I don’t usually like to attribute it to 1 event (because the market cycle is the market cycle, and it is going to play out no matter what eventually), but Trump’s-China tweet changed changed the collective risk tolerance of the market. It is surprising to me, because I believe in TACO (Trump Always Chickens Out). It’s all a negotiating tactic for this administration, so why trade based off it? But, perhaps it was a stark reminder to everyone about how quickly the rug could be pulled at any moment. In any case, and most importantly, it did change something: the collective market risk tolerance. **It was the moment of max risk taking for the cycle.**

Again, let’s think of the market like a pendulum. A pendulum does not ever halt in one spot. It is always moving in 1 direction or the other. We are either in a Bull Phase or a Bear Phase. This is true for every asset in the universe.

This is a much trickier part of the Market Cycle to trade. Some stocks have now entered their Bear Cycles. Others (including the major indices), are still very much in their Bull Phases. As I have covered in detail in the past month +, market tops usually take awhile to play out. Historically, it starts with the most speculative groups (usually pre-revenue companies) peaking. We saw it during the Dot Com Bubble with names Pets.Com, TheGlobe.com, etc., and then again in 2021 with pre-revenue SPACs. In both of those cases, former Bull Cycle leaders (specifically the most spec groups) peaked up to a year before the SPX eventually did.

As mentioned, for many stocks, we are in the beginning of their Bear Cycles. But for many others, they are sill in their Bull Phases. To simplify things, this is exactly what “weak breadth” means to me. A month and a half ago, every stock was in its Bull Cycle (including the highest beta names). Now, many are no longer. This shows up in the breadth data that I have been highlighting for the last month +.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QO4u!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2142bde0-d676-4f69-9ab4-bd68b8827703_1206x774.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QO4u!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2142bde0-d676-4f69-9ab4-bd68b8827703_1206x774.jpeg)

This is why I believe it is critical to focus on the right stocks during this time. Personally, I prefer to focus on stocks that are still in their big picture Bull Cycles. Eventually, names that have already peaked (and have entered their Bear Cycles) will see big counter-trend bounces, and these bounces will be tradeable. However, I generally prefer to focus on trading stocks that have time-frame continuity (big picture, medium picture, and small picture uptrends).

In my view, breadth will likely continue to be narrow during this part of the cycle. Many stocks have dropped out of the Bull Race this quarter. My focus will continue to be on names with earnings and volume catalysts. Here are some of the top standouts for me so far.

#### My Big Picture Views and Summary Points:

* We are past the point of max bullishness and risk taking for this cycle.
* The most speculative groups have topped for this cycle.
* Many stocks have entered their Bear Phases. I expect that they will eventually have big bounces, but will ultimately form lower highs.
* Many other stocks are still in their Bull Phases.
* This is the meaning of weak breadth. It is a split market.
* The indices are still in their Bull Cycles. History shows that it could 12+ months from the time the most spec stocks peak and the time the indices peak. This would mean ~October 2026.
* I still expect the SPX to hit $7300+ before peaking for this cycle.
* I will be focusing on earnings leaders for new trades.
