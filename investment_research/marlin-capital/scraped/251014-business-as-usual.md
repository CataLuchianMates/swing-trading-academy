#### Quant Data on Friday’s Selloff

Friday’s selloff was a ~5 standard deviation event compared to the calm markets experienced in the prior few months.

To put Friday’s move in context, the -2.7% decline in the SPX came in an environment where SPX realized volatility over the prior 3 months was just 8.7%, and 2-week realized was just 6.3%.

The good news is, out of the blue shocks in low volatility markets like this rarely snowball into large Bear Markets (at least in the following few months).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2NT5!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fee2945dd-d7ff-42a8-b540-c9a55ed40009_1142x689.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2NT5!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fee2945dd-d7ff-42a8-b540-c9a55ed40009_1142x689.jpeg)

These type of shock selloffs are relatively rare. Since 1960, there have been only 20 prior SPX declines in environments of sub 15% realized vol that were bigger than 4 standard deviations. Recent episodes include the Yen carry trade unwind last August and Fed meeting selloff last December. In 70% of those events, the SPX was higher 1 and 3 months later. And on average, it took just 8 days to regain the pre-selloff highs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aqHV!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd57fcaf4-a7c2-4621-8526-419d0ef51ad0_1230x1364.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aqHV!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd57fcaf4-a7c2-4621-8526-419d0ef51ad0_1230x1364.jpeg)

In most scenarios, it took just 1-4 days to regain the pre-selloff highs.

In my view, we are likely on our way to something similar this time around starting with today’s strong rebound.
