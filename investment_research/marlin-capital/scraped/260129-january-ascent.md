#### **Market Analysis and Watchlist**

Today was another choppy day for the indices with the SPX closing flat. There were no surprises from today’s Fed meeting with the FOMC leaving rates unchanged (10-2 vote). Financial conditions tightened slightly, but it was essentially a non-reaction for a Fed day (which was expected).

On the earnings front, today was a big one with META, MSFT, and TSLA reporting.

META was the clear standout, delivering a blowout Q4 earnings beat after the bell, with EPS of $8.88 (vs. $8.16 exp, an +8.2% beat) and revenue of $59.9 billion (vs. $58.4 billion exp, a +2.7% beat).

MSFT traded down in the AH session despite strong headline beats on the top and bottom lines. Investors appear to be focused on a slightly softer Azure guide versus the recent run-rate and a conservative full‑year operating margin outlook.

TSLA results were mixed (as always). The conference call was dominated by AI, with Elon Musk repeatedly emphasizing a future of “amazing abundance” driven by full self-driving (FSD), the Cyber Cab robotaxi network, and the Optimus humanoid robot. Tesla even announced that they will be ending Model S/X production to convert Fremont capacity into an Optimus factory (targeting 1M robots per year). Musk framed Tesla’s core focus going forward as autonomy, FSD, large AI compute and custom chips, robotaxis, and Optimus at scale.

**RR**

Richtech Robotics is a Small Cap (~880M mkt cap) Robotics and AI company that develops, manufactures, and deploys advanced robotic solutions for the service and industrial sectors.

Yesterday, the company announced a major partnership with Microsoft focused on integrating Azure AI into its ADAM robot, enhancing vision, voice recognition, and autonomous reasoning capabilities.

RR stock responded to the huge Microsoft news with a +45% rally on ATH trading volume (171M shares traded).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mujn!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fca5b93aa-66bd-44e8-8ca3-6aa38fca5fd8_1266x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mujn!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fca5b93aa-66bd-44e8-8ca3-6aa38fca5fd8_1266x844.png)

Today, the stock pulled back and closed -8% after announcing a dilutive $38.7 million private placement of 8.5 million shares. It is not uncommon for a Small Cap company to announce a stock offering following a huge rally and volume spike on news. This is simply management striking while the iron is hot. Often times, if the news is big enough, the stock can shake the offering right off (and RR closed well off its lows today). In my view, this Microsoft partnership news qualifies as “big enough”.

Today was also an inside day for RR. A break above Wednesday’s highs at $5.16 would be a potential buy signal for me. On a breakout through $5.5 (yesterday’s highs), RR has a clear path back to ATHs ($7.4).

On a long entry, my closing stop would need to go below $4.05. Given that this would be a very high risk trade (~20% risk), this is most definitely a scenario where I would prefer buying RR calls rather than shares.
