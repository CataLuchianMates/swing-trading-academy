# First Dip 

The stock market rally finally hit a wall today, triggered by the big spike in Treasury yields. The SPX finished -1.61% while the QQQ declined -1.39%.

The 10-yr yield settled the cash session at 4.60%, up 12 bps, while the 30-yr bond yield settled the day at 5.09%, up 13 bps.

Yields took a turn for the worse in the afternoon following a $16B 20-yr bond auction that saw some relatively soft dollar demand, evidenced by a 2.46 bid-to-cover ratio that fell short of the prior 12-auction average of 2.58.

We saw selling pressure pick up in stocks following the afternoon auction. The spike in yields triggered renewed growth concerns, hitting Small Caps the hardest (RTY -2.8%), and ultimately contributing to losses in 10 of the 11 SPX sectors.

Technically, the S&P 500 just closed below its 8 EMA (green line below) for the first time since April 21st, one month ago.

We had spent a month riding the 8 EMA higher throughout the entirety of this +20% rally off lows. Now, we have finally broken below this key support level for the first time in a month.

The next key support levels I am watching below include:

(1) The 21 EMA at $5740-$5750

(2) The unfilled China Trade deal gap near ~$5700

(3) The 60 EMA at $5680

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HjfC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F77fb500f-9504-4858-9aac-dd55aaad8650_1219x1015.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HjfC!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F77fb500f-9504-4858-9aac-dd55aaad8650_1219x1015.jpeg)

At the beginning of the week, I mentioned that I see a potential -3-5% S&P 500 dip as buyable. As of today’s close, the SPX dip is currently sitting at -2%.

A further decline to 21 EMA support would be a -3.7% dip. If we reach 60 EMA support, the dip would be -4.8%.

Bigger picture, I remain bullish here, and continue to view a potential -3-5% dip as buyable. I am targeting at least a decline to the 21 EMA at $5740-$5750, which should suffice to shake off the overbought conditions and reset sentiment. With that said, it is also certainly possible we test the 60 EMA at $5680 if yields continue to rise sharply.
