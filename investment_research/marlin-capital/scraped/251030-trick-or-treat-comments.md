## David Marlin Q&A (Comments)

**Q:** We’ve had other bouts of bad breadth without a top / bear market, right? Would you say it’s a necessary but not causal condition?

**David Marlin:** Yes I think that’s a fair assessment. Not every bout of bad breadth is a top/beginning of a bear market, but every top (since 2000) started with bad breadth. With that said, with a couple of the stats I went over, it shows current breadth is historically bad and we have either not seen this before or only seen it during market tops.

---

**Q:** What do you think the odds are, at this point, of entering a downturn/correction within the next couple of months? How long do you think it would last? Do you think we should we trying to take profits at this point and try to convert to let's say 50% cash? I remember you mentioned in past posts that when the Fed cuts rates at ATH, he have historically been high one year later, so I am wondering if this means the downturn here would only last a few months.

**David Marlin:** It’s tough to say but there are definite red flags/cracks showing up. So I would say the odds are significantly higher than they have been at any point since April lows.  The SPX is mostly the Mag 7 and would be much more insulated from any drawdown. In Bear cycles, the average stock will decline upwards of -50%.

---

**Q:** Thanks for the insightful writeup.  Do you ever play the short side of the market?

**David Marlin:** Thank you! Yes, our top trade idea for Q2 was a short (APP short), which we gained >20% on a week on. And we also shorted frequently during the 2022 Bear Market and had some big trades (HCP, MSTR, GTLB, etc).
