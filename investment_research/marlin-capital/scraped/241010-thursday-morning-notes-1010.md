# Thursday Morning Notes 10.10

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The S&P 500 clocked its 44th ATH of the year yesterday, finishing +0.71% on the day.

We saw some continued outperformance in software (+2%), while lower quality pockets of the market also outperformed on the day.  Market volumes were below average once again as investors were awaiting this morning’s CPI report.

September CPI –

September CPI inflation falls to 2.4%, above expectations of 2.3%.

Core CPI inflation rises to 3.3%, above expectations of 3.2%.

Slightly hotter CPI than expected, which has market futures in risk off mode a bit this morning.  With that said, this is nothing to get overly bearish about in my opinion, as this is still the lowest headline CPI print since February 2021.  This data does shift the FOMC probabilities, and I would expect the Fed’s next move to a be a 25bp cut rather than another 50bp cut.

The other data point we received this morning was Initial Jobless Claims, where we saw the biggest weekly jump since July 2021.

Weekly Initial Jobless Claims: 258K, [Est. 231K, Prev. 225K]

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EhP-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F60608ecd-5d90-4e79-9494-3ab80ed22cd8_937x503.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EhP-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F60608ecd-5d90-4e79-9494-3ab80ed22cd8_937x503.png)

However, this spike in initial claims data looks to be largely driven by Hurricane Helene.  For that reason, I am not expecting the market to get too caught up on this data point. This can/will be chalked up to a one off driven by special factors.

Active Portfolio –

SN – Still holding the final portion of my long position here with my trailing stop set at $104.52.

NMM – Stopped out on my final 1/3rd of this long yesterday at $61.39.  Position closed on the rest for +11% gains.

MU – This stock has acted weaker than expected post-earnings.  I’m still letting this trade play out with my stop set at $99.39 for -4.5% risk.

No new planned trades for today. We will get a better idea of how the market digests this morning’s data following today’s close, but I do not anticipate this data reversing the market’s primary uptrend going forward.
