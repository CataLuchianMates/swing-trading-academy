**Investing is Different than Trading**

This is a good time to remind everyone that investing is NOT the same as trading.

> “The company is not the stock, and the stock is not the company.”
>
> - Jeff Bezos

I am very adamant about separating these differing strategies (trading and investing), because you need to effectively define the specific strategy of a portfolio for it to be successful.

This is why, at Marlin Capital, we have separate portfolios for our long term investments and our trades (which go in the Active Portfolio).

**My Strategy as an Investor:**

Buy and hold innovative, game-changing type companies.

It’s that simple.

Innovative, game-changing type companies make for volatile stocks when they are in their high growth phases.

We [nailed over](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-small-caps-with-massive-upside?utm_source=publication-search) +200% gains on SEZL, WGS, and INOD (round 1) in the original [Small Cap 6 Portfolio](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-big-unwind?utm_source=publication-search). We [invested in PLTR](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/activate-the-trump-trades?utm_source=publication-search) last year at $60. I invested in [CRWD in 2020](https://talkmarkets.com/content/technology/momentum-is-on-crowdstrikes-side-will-it-last?post=282372). I’ve made over 10x bagger returns through the years in NVDA and SHOP (investing in 2018).

How did I do it?

By studying the industries. Studying the companies. Studying the fundamentals and valuations.

I owned shares of these companies. I did not trade these stocks.

**Timing the market is NOT part of investing in companies on a fundamental basis.**

I’ve also owned shares of TRVG and sold at a -70%+ loss. I owned shares in ROKU and sold at a -45% loss. I owned shares [in AEYE](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-big-unwind?utm_source=publication-search) for a -37% loss.

You are going to be wrong investing in companies, too. Warren Buffet lost -67% in Paramount Global stock. He lost 40% in Conoco Phillips. No one bats .1000 in this game.

But you don’t need to. When you hit on a long-term investment in a growth company, it will be a multi-bagger. The most you can lose is -100%. But you can and will make +200%+ on your winning investments. One Multi-Bagger (NVDA) more than makes up for every loss I’ve ever had in my career.
