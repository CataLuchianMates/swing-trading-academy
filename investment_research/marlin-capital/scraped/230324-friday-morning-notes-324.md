# Friday Morning Notes 3.24

Technical Update

The S&P 500 struggled to hold its early gains yesterday, finishing up the day +0.3%. The index failed to close above the 21 and 60 EMAs after rallying above the levels intraday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MzEE!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1a4a5d01-4b0d-404a-8563-e36ad60b7013_958x732.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MzEE!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1a4a5d01-4b0d-404a-8563-e36ad60b7013_958x732.jpeg)

Still, the 200 SMA support level held yesterday afternoon and index was able to close 15 points above support.

We are set for a gap down this morning below the 200 SMA. The market has been chopping around the $3900-$4000 level for some time now, frustrating impatient bulls and bears. This setup leans bearish, but in my view, it is too early to get super bearish until we close decisively below yesterday’s lows ($3919).

Key support levels below include $3930-$3935, $3915-$3920, $3900, and $3850-$3860.

Key resistance levels above include $3960-$3965, $3975-$3980, and $4000.
