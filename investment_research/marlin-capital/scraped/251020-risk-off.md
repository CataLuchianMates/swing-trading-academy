# Risk Off

We saw risk off price action in US stocks last week with volatility rising. The VIX hit a high of 29 (highest since April) before falling back to 21 to finish the week.

Rising volatility is always a bearish signal for equities. For position and swing traders like us, the ideal bullish environment is a low-volatility, steady grind higher in stocks.

This was the type of bullish environment we had over the past several months (since late April). But, that has all changed in an instant now…

This means it’s our job to adjust.

Here’s how we’re handling the increased market volatility at Marlin Capital 👇👇

#### **Cracks in the Price Action**

The most important thing for a stock speculator is staying objective and analyzing what you see. The market does NOT care what you want to see happen. The market is going to do exactly what it’s going to do, and it’s your job to position yourself for it.

With that said, let’s review some of the concerns I’m seeing right now:

It all starts with weakening breadth. I’ve talked before about how topping is a process (not an event), and not all stocks peak at the same time.

Last week, some of the highest flying momentum names of this rally put in huge extensions and *possible* climax tops.

Stocks like OKLO, EOSE, IREN, IONQ, etc. had parabolic runs and just formed huge weekly topping candles (while being completely outside their upper bollinger bands).

Momentum has been stalling across the board in former leaders/groups, particularly the most speculative ones. This is classic “Risk Off” price action.

#### **Watch the Speculatives**

In my view, the most speculative stocks are the best indicator of risk appetite. Way back in late 2021, during the [Covid Bubble Bust,](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/were-not-in-kansas-anymore?utm_source=publication-search) I highlighted how the most speculative areas of the market (SPACs) topped many months (9+) before the market indices did.

The same thing happened during the Dot Com Bubble, too.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EB2z!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F15efdd40-bf86-4e96-b25a-9002d69a849a_1558x353.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EB2z!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F15efdd40-bf86-4e96-b25a-9002d69a849a_1558x353.png)

My research shows that Bubbles throughout history have peaked this way (with the most speculative groups/names topping first).

Back in [2021-22](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/were-not-in-kansas-anymore?utm_source=publication-search), the most speculative groups were SPACs and pre-revenue companies. During this AI Bubble cycle, I believe the most speculative groups are Quantum Computing and pre-revenue names like OKLO (nuclear), QS, etc.

Historically, when we these most speculative groups start to peak, it means the market topping process has begun. Right now, the action in the most speculative names is signaling “risk off”. And if these groups have topped for the cycle, it means we have already hit peak “risk appetite” during this AI Bubble. Historically, this is how the market topping process begins.

#### **It’s Not All Bearish, Though**

Long-term readers of Marlin Capital remember my “Bear Market call” in December 2021. Many names (like ARKK) would go on to decline -70% + after my bearish call, and the SPX ended up declining ~24%.

In some ways, the 2025 AI Bubble has had similar qualities to the 2020-21 COVID Bubble.

**But the biggest difference between back then and right now is THE FED.**

In late 2021 the Fed was aggressively raising rates (too late, I may add) to curb 40+ year high inflation.

Here in 2025, the Fed is cutting rates into solid economic growth during a strong Bull Market.

I’ve highlighted these stats many times before, but they are worth rehashing now:

**The SPX is overwhelmingly positive when the Fed cuts and there is no recession:**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!90ch!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F55fe4fa4-0658-4469-965a-2e56d57d2d31_1215x847.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!90ch!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F55fe4fa4-0658-4469-965a-2e56d57d2d31_1215x847.jpeg)

**Moreover, the SPX has a perfect track record when Fed cuts at ATHs (ie, when the Fed cuts during a strong bull market):**

The Fed has cut rates with the S&P 500 within 1% of ATHs 16 times in its history. **The S&P 500 was higher a year later all 16 times for an average return of +15%.**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Efy5!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcb7bcdf6-2450-49b8-9311-03006b95b973_898x578.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Efy5!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcb7bcdf6-2450-49b8-9311-03006b95b973_898x578.jpeg)

Stretch that to SPX within 2% of ATHs, and it’s happened 20 times. **Again, all 20 times, the S&P 500 was higher a year later.**

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zq5s!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F35d0f4c2-ec02-4176-9105-c3c453eb8838_975x675.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Zq5s!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F35d0f4c2-ec02-4176-9105-c3c453eb8838_975x675.png)

**Don’t fight the Fed.**

This phrase is sometimes said as a joke, but there’s plenty of truth to it. And the stats prove it.

**Historically, it does not pay to be bearish when the Fed is easing.**  This is the simple fact of the matter.

The big caveat, as mentioned above, is when the economy is in / enters a recession. Recessionary rate cuts are a whole different type of monetary policy. The good news is, these are not the type of rate cuts we’re getting right now. Unless the economy significantly deteriorates from here, the Macro picture should continue to a big tailwind for stocks.

Equity Positioning is also not indicative of a major market top, and never has been during this post-April Bull Cycle.

Last week saw the largest decrease in equity positioning since Liberation Day. Total positioning is now back to neutral.

#### **So what’s next?**

Earnings season will be the real tell for me.

**The last 2 earnings seasons (since April lows) were incredibly bullish with big earnings gaps everywhere.**  We took full advantage here at Marlin Capital with plays like HOOD, APH, SPOT, APLD, NBIS, TEM, ALAB, etc.

Do we start to see that change in Q3?

Do we start to see stocks selling off after strong earnings this time?

Luckily for us, we don’t have to wait long to find out. The real action starts this week with 18% of the SPX market cap set to report.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PXCL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faacea355-adf5-44bf-9f28-354844ae2a1a_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PXCL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faacea355-adf5-44bf-9f28-354844ae2a1a_1920x1080.jpeg)

APH, GEV, VRT, TSLA, QS (spec pre-rev), LRCX, and INTC are all going to be huge tells for me. Not necessarily the earnings numbers (unless there is a big surprise), but the market reaction.

We are at the point in the market cycle where any major downside move could be the top for certain individual stocks. In my view, we may start to see some weaker names drop out of “the bull race” this quarter. This earnings season should separate the men from the boys.
