### Expectation Breaker

It’s true, we have been highlighting the [“Risk Off”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70) price action ever since it began in October.

Still, this recent wave of bearishness is surprising to me. I was not expecting all this year-end weakness after the Fed announced [QE lite](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/qe-lite) last week and the RSP and IWM responded with breakouts. It looked like the SPX was setting up for one as well.

But, you must be able to change your opinion quick in the market, because the market does NOT care 1 iota about what you are expecting. This is why it is best to leave your ego at the door.

**This week’s price action is what I like to call an “expectation breaker”.**

For me, the most surprising thing about the current selloff is that there is no key reason or catalyst for all this weakness. There was no obvious reason for AI stocks to begin peaking in October.  **But they did.**

Which is why, this is a good lesson for everyone (including myself):

**There does not always need to be a clear, identifiable reason for stocks to sell off.**

And most importantly, it does NOT matter one bit.

When I worked on the trading floor, we had a running joke for whenever someone would ask: “Why is X stock is down today?”

The answer: “Because there’s more sellers than buyers!”

This is the only answer that you would hear (in a sarcastic/joking tone). And put simply, it is the correct answer.

You can argue and hypothesize the exact reason for “why” until you’re blue in the face. But, the literal reason for a stock being down is that there is more demand to sell than there is demand to buy.

**A stock declines because there is more selling demand than buying demand.**

[As I have talked about before](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/weakness), I like to think of price action as a battle between buyers and sellers (Bulls and Bears).

Bulls had been effortlessly dominating the price action battle from April lows up until mid-October. It was a rip-roaring Bull Market.

But then, something changed.

All of the sudden, a switch flipped.

And now, the tide has turned. Bears have taken control.

I talked about some of the warning signs [immediately as they occurred](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70), specifically the weekly and monthly topping candles in names like OKLO, IONQ, etc.
