# Tariff Storm

US stocks fell sharply on Friday after Trump threatened 100% tariffs on China.

The market reaction was quick and painful. The S&P 500 fell -2.7% for its worst day since April 10th.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ccum!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7d18a640-4d13-4e88-9306-484bc65bc133_1132x652.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ccum!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7d18a640-4d13-4e88-9306-484bc65bc133_1132x652.png)

Friday’s price action erased a month’s worth of gains for the S&P 500. The market closed out the week right where it opened all the way back on September 10th ($6550).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!thNd!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6582ba65-2edf-4506-8b94-1cadb11043d2_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!thNd!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6582ba65-2edf-4506-8b94-1cadb11043d2_1396x653.png)

So, was Friday’s sell off just a quick blip, or are we heading for a deeper market correction?

Detailed analysis and **Marlin Capital strategy** below…

Friday’s tariff news fueled worries around a replay of April. We all remember the **Q1 Tariff Tank**, which I have compared to the 1998 market correction (a deep, Macro-driven market sell off in the face of a technological revolution/market bubble).

But this time around, it might all be over in a weekend.

The big news this weekend is Trump unsurprisingly walking back his “hardball” 100% tariff threat.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lHYF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7f30d8ac-2bd0-4c9c-bac8-f375123de068_1515x676.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lHYF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7f30d8ac-2bd0-4c9c-bac8-f375123de068_1515x676.jpeg)

My first reaction to Friday’s sell off was “Really? We’re doing this again?” Not because a full-scale Trade War with China wouldn’t be disastrous for US equities. But because just like the “Liberation Day” big board of tariffs, it is not realistic/feasible.

When in doubt, just remember TACO (Trump always chickens out).

Yes, Trump always chickens out, because this is all a negotiating tactic. A bargaining chip. It is never meant to be implemented. The extreme tariff threats (like 100%) are just posturing in the first place. Trump is well aware of how disastrous this would be for the US economy and stock market (as well as China).

This is “The Art of the Deal” 101. This is how Trump negotiates. There will be no “130% tariff” on China.

So, why the huge market selloff on Friday?

Truth be told, we are on Year 5 of the market overreacting to Trump tariff threats. Trading algorithm’s react instantly, and do not subscribe to the TACO trade.

But beyond that, we were due for a sell off. The 50% Nasdaq surge from April lows has been extraordinary, and has come without a real pull back. Prior to Friday, it had been 133 days since the last -3% dip in the SPX (in the 95th percentile versus history).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!RRZX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6a21bd6a-329d-41f7-b4ec-f72e3cfbb6b7_1067x552.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!RRZX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6a21bd6a-329d-41f7-b4ec-f72e3cfbb6b7_1067x552.jpeg)

Things were getting a little too frothy. Total call volume just reached a new record high with 69M calls traded.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9IcG!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fda6c5237-75a9-4b3a-97c5-8890fa20b9da_1091x752.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9IcG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fda6c5237-75a9-4b3a-97c5-8890fa20b9da_1091x752.jpeg)

And we just saw record buying from Retail Investors.

Over the last month, retail investors bought over +$100B of US stocks, the largest 1M buying EVER.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!g0j8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F15d744f3-8c18-4502-addd-a0855491d500_1076x621.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!g0j8!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F15d744f3-8c18-4502-addd-a0855491d500_1076x621.png)

Friday’s news triggered heavy index-level hedging and record options volumes. Total US options volume hit new all-time highs, eclipsing >100M contracts for just the 2nd time ever (April 4th was the other, where SPX fell -5.97%).

Total put option volume across all underliers marked the 2nd highest daily total on record.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!49nR!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F400d3fa6-402b-4099-ba96-2abfb125f3e3_1089x696.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!49nR!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F400d3fa6-402b-4099-ba96-2abfb125f3e3_1089x696.jpeg)

In early September, I talked about the potential of systematic selling as a big risk for the market. That risk never played out, as the SPX stayed firmly above the CTA sell trigger. But heading into this week, systematic selling is once again a key risk for the market. GS estimates that systematic funds are currently long $220B of US equities, near 8-year highs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TYcP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5e33b7b1-6cdc-4d55-bd6c-67d0c293f3f7_1012x657.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TYcP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5e33b7b1-6cdc-4d55-bd6c-67d0c293f3f7_1012x657.jpeg)

For the CTA sell trigger level, Goldman has the first sell trigger at $6500-$6580 (partially breached on Friday, and we saw some CTA selling). The next threshold is around $6300, which is -4% below current levels.

All this to say, **there are still some shorter-term selling risks present heading into the week of 10/13** (US/China headline risk and systematic fund unwinds if SPX falls below $6500).

#### **The Big Picture**

Big picture, my views have not changed at all. 5 years from now, all this tariff news will be forgotten. But Artificial Intelligence will define this era.

We are still in the early stages of a technological revolution, the likes of which we have only seen a few times in history. AI is going to drastically change our world in the next decade. In the next 5-10 years, we are will see massive technological breakthroughs in industries like healthcare, transportation (autonomous), robotics, security & defense, etc. This could potentially drive a golden age for our economy. This is what is responsible for this >50% Nasdaq rally since April.

My view remains that we are currently in an AI Bubble. This means that eventually, the Bust phase will come, and the leaders of this cycle will fall 50-80%+. But, that day has not come yet, in my view.

**In my view, this is not “the Big Sell”. This is not the end of the Bull Market, or the top of the AI Bubble.**

The trend remains up and to the right, and 1 day of headline induced selling does not change that. I see no evidence that this is the beginning of a Bear Market (or the beginning of the end of this Bull Market). Simply put, we are not seeing that in the overall price action. I reserve the right to change my opinion if the price action significantly deteriorates from here, but that is precisely what it would take to change my view.

-3-5% market dips are common in Bull Markets. The fact that we have not had one since April speaks to the incredible buying demand we have seen over the past ~6 months.

Big picture, my target remains $7300 for the SPX.
