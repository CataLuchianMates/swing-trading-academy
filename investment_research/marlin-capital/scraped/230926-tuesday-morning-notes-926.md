# Tuesday Morning Notes 9.26

Technical Update

The S&P 500 bounced +0.4% from oversold territory to kick off the week yesterday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Kb_U!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F775cb65c-b315-497f-92d2-bf73e0dfdd79_958x723.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Kb_U!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F775cb65c-b315-497f-92d2-bf73e0dfdd79_958x723.jpeg)

Technically, we still remain in short-term oversold territory heading into Tuesday. With that said, the bigger picture remains bearish with the SPX setting up for its first bearish 21 and 60 EMA crossover since March.

We are set for a gap down this morning. Key support levels to watch below include $4300, $4260-$4270, and $4190-$4200 (my initial target for this correction).

Key resistance levels to watch above include $4340-$4350, $4370-$4380, $4400, and $4430-$4440.
