# Moment of Truth

Marlin Capital Readers,

The Fed spoke today, and the market did not like what it heard.

US stocks sold off sharply on Wednesday with the SPX falling -1.4%, the Nasdaq dropping -1.5%, and the Russell 2000 plunging -1.6%. The selling was broad-based and aggressive. Today was the worst rate-day decision for the S&P 500 since December 2024.

But the Fed decision was only part of the story. This morning’s PPI report came in scorching hot, with wholesale inflation surging +0.7% month over month (more than double the +0.3% consensus estimate). Core PPI hit +3.9% YoY, the highest reading in over a year. This set the bearish tone before Powell even spoke.

**The Fed Meeting**

The FOMC voted 11-1 to hold rates steady at 3.5%-3.75%. The hold itself was widely expected. But the details underneath were more hawkish than what the market wanted to see. 👇

The dot plot shifted meaningfully in the hawkish direction. The median end-of-2026 rate estimate stayed at 3.4%, which implies ~1 cut this year. But as Powell pointed out during the press conference, there was “meaningful movement” toward fewer cuts. 4-5 members moved from projecting 2 cuts to just 1. In December, only 7 of 19 members saw 1 or 0 cuts in 2026. That number is now 14 of 19. That is a major shift.

The Fed also raised its 2026 inflation forecast to 2.7% PCE (up from 2.4% in December), driven largely by surging oil prices from the Iran war. Powell acknowledged the difficulty of the current environment, saying the Fed is balancing downside risks to the labor market (which call for lower rates) against upside risks to inflation (which call for higher rates or holding). He called it a “very difficult situation.”

The Iran war remains the wild card. The Fed’s statement explicitly noted that “the implications of developments in the Middle East for the U.S. economy are uncertain.” In the presser, Powell said it was “too soon to know” the full impact. Rising oil prices are the #1 threat to the inflation outlook right now, and until the geopolitical picture stabilizes, the Fed’s hands are effectively tied.

The bottom line for us is simple. The market [came into 2026](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-marlin-capital-2026-preview?utm_source=publication-search) projecting 2+ rate cuts this year (substantial monetary easing). And now, the Fed is telling us that even 1 cut is no longer a sure thing. That is a major hawkish shift, and the market has reacted accordingly.

The fixed income market has also reacted accordingly. Following today’s FOMC, 2 YR Treasury yields broke out to new 2026 highs (~3.8%). The 2 YR is the most sensitive part of the curve to Fed policy, and today’s surge shows that bond traders are aggressively repricing for a "higher-for-longer" scenario.

#### The Moment of Truth

This brings us to the single most important moment for the US stock market in 2026 so far. 👇

The S&P 500 and QQQ both closed right at their 200-day SMAs today. As I laid out in detail last week in [“Fork in the Road”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/fork-in-the-road), the 200-day SMA is the last line of defense. It is the level where the biggest decisions get made (routine market correction vs bear market).

Last week, that test passed (initially). ES and NQ Futures bounced off their 200 SMAs on Sunday night, and the big question was “will this bounce hold?”

The bounce did not hold. We are right back at the 200 SMA now, and this time, the macro backdrop is a little bit worse.

So what’s next?

As I talked about in [“Rolling Over”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/rolling-over?utm_source=publication-search), traders should always think in terms of probabilities. Because, to be clear, there are no certainties in this business. Right now, based on everything I am currently seeing, the Bear Case is my higher probability scenario.

I have talked about the Bearish warning signs building [since October](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70?utm_source=publication-search) (when the specs topped). Most recently, I have highlighted the [7 Hindenburg Omens](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/unwind?utm_source=publication-search), [Bear Market level of individual stock blowups](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-clusters?utm_source=publication-search) (aka Bear Market price action), and the fact that total [positioning is not at all capitulatory](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/headwinds) (it is still neutral). In fact, [positioning remains a headwind](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/headwinds) for the market here with systematic funds beginning to de-risk substantially (from a high level) this week.

Add another data point to the Bear Case as well. 👇

Historically, Oil prices have surged >50% above trend 7 times since 1970. All 6 of the previous times were followed by a recession. We just got the 7th here in March 2026.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9EV2!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc4905390-90ce-4db6-bb8f-babeb24f31b7_1070x753.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9EV2!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc4905390-90ce-4db6-bb8f-babeb24f31b7_1070x753.jpeg)

And with all that said, the Bull Case (ie the Bear Trap) thesis is not dead yet. Despite all the bearishness, we still have not broken below the all important 200 SMA. And to be clear, it is still possible to see the market carve out a bottom here. As I pointed out in [“Hard Penny”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/hard-penny?utm_source=publication-search), this is exactly what happened during the first major index pullback of the Dot Com Bubble. The SPX tested (and briefly undercut) the 200-day SMA before ripping higher for 1 final wave (although many stocks had already topped for the cycle by that point).

A good bear trap sucks everyone in, and I am certainly not immune to that. **This is why I am not getting locked into a bearish view until bears actually fully take control and break through.** Price action is a battle, and we can not declare a winner prematurely.

But, the big difference between last week and tonight is that the cash market is now testing the 200 SMA (not just the futures market). The S&P 500 and Nasdaq are sitting at those key support levels right now. The market’s key decision should be made in the next week or so.

If the 200 SMA breaks, my next downside target for the SPX is the $6,450-6,500 area. This is the anchored VWAP from the Liberation Day lows.

Beyond that, my eventual downside target remains ~$6,300 (an official -10% SPX correction). If we get there, that is where I will be looking to deploy long-term capital in my Long Term Investments Portfolio (formerly Trump Trades).

This is a massive moment for the market. And we are about to find out which path it takes. 👇

**[2026 Portfolio Update](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/marlin-capital-live-portfolio-dashboards)**

Current weightings - I am currently ~30% long, 10% short, and 60% cash.

I am still holding 9 leaders long. The portfolio is up slightly on the week (though our 2 shorts are helping as well). This is what makes the current setup so fascinating.

With the SPX and QQQ sitting right on their 200 SMAs and the macro backdrop deteriorating, I would normally expect to be out of most of my long positions. In a typical market environment, that would probably be the case.

But, my leaders are not acting like the indices right now. They are still leading, and for the most part, bucking the indices. This is exactly why I made the decision, [when the 2026 Portfolio was released](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-picks-for-2026?utm_source=publication-search), to manage each position individually this year. As I have highlighted in detail over the past few months, it is typical for individual stocks to top at their own pace in late cycle markets (the transition from bull to bear market). This is what we are seeing here in 2026. In fact, this is what [we have been seeing since October when the most speculative leaders first topped](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70?utm_source=publication-search). As I said at the time, that was the moment of peak risk taking/speculation for the cycle.

The beauty of my [Stage Analysis Framework](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/grinch-selloff?utm_source=publication-search) is that it allows me to manage each position individually. The stocks I am holding are still uptrending despite the broader market deterioration.

And none of them demonstrated that more clearly today than MU.

MU just reported earnings after the bell, and the numbers are staggering. Just as we knew they would be. EPS grew +626% YoY and beat estimates by +37%. Revenue hit $23.9B vs. $20.1B expected (a +24% beat, and +196% YoY). Free cash flow hit a quarterly record of $6.9B. And the Q3 guidance? Revenue of $33.5B vs. $24.3B expected (a +38% raise). EPS of $19.15 vs. $12.05 expected (a +59% raise). Gross margins guided to ~81%.

The fact that MU is delivering numbers like this while the indices are testing their 200 SMAs tells you everything you need to know about the quality of the names we are holding.

Still, the stock finished the after-hours session negative. As I always say, it’s not the numbers that matter most, it’s the market’s reaction to the numbers. And there is an aura of bearishness hanging over the entire market right now.

In any case, I am going to keep managing each position individually. Maybe the final market leaders will top if the SPX follows my Bear Case and breaks below the 200 SMA and towards $6300. This is certainly a plausible scenario, and one that I am prepared for. If this is the case, I will exit my positions (on an individual basis) when they begin to break down and I view them as transitioning to Stage 4.

**The Big Picture**

We are at a crossroads for the market here. The indices are currently sitting at their 200-day SMAs. This key test is going to define the next several months for the market. And we are watching it unfold in real time.

The macro backdrop just got a little worse today with the hawkish Fed and hot PPI reading. To be clear, the Bear Case is my higher probability scenario here (and why I am positioned defensively).

But, the Bear Trap thesis is not dead until the 200 SMA is decisively broken. And right now, we are sitting right on top of it.
