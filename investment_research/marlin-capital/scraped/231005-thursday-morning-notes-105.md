# Thursday Morning Notes 10.5

**Daily Market Commentary**

The S&P 500 bounced off 200 SMA support on Wednesday, closing +0.81% on the day.  Yesterday was an inside day for the SPX, meaning we did not break through the previous day’s lows or highs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZvYa!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa44fb6f7-17d5-4e04-9dae-b779870f0256_1204x892.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZvYa!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa44fb6f7-17d5-4e04-9dae-b779870f0256_1204x892.jpeg)

Key levels for Thursday – above $4,269 = bullish, below $4220 = bearish.

We came into Wednesday short-term oversold with a sub-35 RSI.  Under the surface, we may have also seen enough damage near term.  The % of SPX members below their 50-day moving average is coming into contact with previous short-term bottom levels.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hZzq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3be07aac-6981-44b3-9cb9-dc9d3fb91694_1172x806.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hZzq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3be07aac-6981-44b3-9cb9-dc9d3fb91694_1172x806.jpeg)

The % of SPX members below their 200-day moving average says we can still go much lower over the medium/long term (sub 20% has been a decent benchmark).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PJSu!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd27f3038-ea33-4bd8-b725-05c6078ab5b6_1180x806.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PJSu!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd27f3038-ea33-4bd8-b725-05c6078ab5b6_1180x806.jpeg)

Similarly, the % of companies under the hood that are short-term oversold (RSI sub-30) stands at 28%.  Levels where short term bounces are common, but not suggestive of any type of major washout low just yet.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!eUrI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdc79b345-5c2c-410b-9bf2-c1e561409242_1178x794.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!eUrI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdc79b345-5c2c-410b-9bf2-c1e561409242_1178x794.jpeg)

Tech led the way on Wednesday with the QQQ finishing the day +1.36%.  This was also an inside day on the daily chart for QQQ.

Key levels for Thursday - above $360.62 = bullish, below $355.30 = bearish.

On a shorter time frame (the 1 HR Chart), the QQQ did not make new lows in October while the other indices did.  $361 and $363 are the key short term levels to watch in the QQQ.

Meanwhile. Small Caps (IWM) came within $2 of YTD lows on Wednesday before forming a possible short-term bottoming/reversal candle.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2BSO!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F282f5994-0005-45e3-ad7e-82c9b0b385fe_1002x721.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2BSO!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F282f5994-0005-45e3-ad7e-82c9b0b385fe_1002x721.jpeg)

The 10-YR Treasury yield, which is important to watch right now, declined intraday after hitting a high of 4.88.  It is now sitting at 4.735%.  The move in rates yesterday allowed for the intraday bounce in equities.
