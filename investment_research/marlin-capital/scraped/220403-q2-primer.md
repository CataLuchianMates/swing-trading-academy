# Q2 Primer

**Rule #1**

First off, I would like to share with everyone my favorite quote from Warren Buffett.

> “Rule number 1: **Don’t lose money**. Rule number 2: Don't forget rule number 1.”

Keeping losses small, while avoiding large portfolio drawdowns, is truly one of the main pillars of any successful trader or investor.

As I discussed in Q4 & Q1, the troubling Macro backdrop and monetary tightening will create a difficult environment for stocks throughout 2022.  Both quarters have been difficult for traders and investors alike, and I expect these conditions to continue throughout Q2.

Consumer sentiment is now nearing 2008 levels as inflation takes its toll on the consumer.  The last time the consumer sentiment reading was below 60 was in August of 2011, and since 1978, there have only been three other periods where this reading dropped below 60 (1980, 2008, and 2011).

S&P 500 performance following these occurrences is mixed.  In 1980 and 2011, the S&P 500 was higher over the next year.  After the prolonged period with a sub-60 reading in 2008, the S&P 500 dropped more than 34%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Idqh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdbc40fe6-8d09-4077-92e9-29b285ce624e_1284x617.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Idqh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdbc40fe6-8d09-4077-92e9-29b285ce624e_1284x617.jpeg)

The key difference between 2008 and the 2 other periods was that sentiment remained subdued for a prolonged period of time in 2008, while sentiment was quick to bounce back in the other 2 periods.

Here in 2022, the key for the market may once again prove to be how quick consumer sentiment can bounce back.  This factor is now highly dependent on whether the Fed is able to get a handle on inflation or not.

Last week, another warning sign for the economy was delivered in the Fixed Income market with the 2/10yr yield curve inversion.  The yield curve inversion has preceded each of the last 9 recessions, with an average of 18 months from inversion to recession.

**Stay Patient**

As mentioned, I believe each of these factors will continue to make it difficult for stocks in Q2.  This is why my key for Q2 will again be patience, and following Warren Buffett’s #1 rule of not “losing money”.

In Q1, I was able to capitalize on several short trades (HCP, GTLB, ARKK, etc.).  Additionally, I was able to keep all my losses very small, which is evidence to me that I am executing my strategy.

I believe there will continue to be more opportunities in Q2, likely both on the long and short side.  Traders who stay patient, avoid getting “chopped up”, and avoid fighting the tape, will be in the best positions to take advantage.

**Don’t Fight the Tape**

This brings me to my next key for Q2, which is not to fight the tape.  This means not to trade against the trend, no matter how illogical something might seem.  Even if you are bearish on a stock like GameStop or AMC, shorting without the proper technical signal can be catastrophic.  As traders, we must wait for the proper technical signals to act on our theses.

**Follow the Leaders**

The list of leaders in our current market is quite thin, but there are a couple of strong industry groups for traders who are long focused.  From a sector standpoint, energy (XLE) and utilities (XLU) continue to stand out. My focus for long trades in Q2 will be on stocks within the leading industry groups such as energy, commodities, miners, utilities, and shipping stocks.

This means I am not focused on tech in my Active Portfolio, at least coming into Q2.  The Nasdaq remains under its 200 day moving average, continuing to show relative weakness versus late cycle sectors.

**High Valuation underperformance to continue?**

The stocks with the highest valuations and the biggest losses will continue to be the most vulnerable in Q2, in my opinion.  These types of stocks could continue to provide solid short opportunities this quarter.

The historical precedent for buying stocks at >20 P/S is not a strong one for bulls, and I am not seeing any logical reason for it to be different this time with 40 year high inflation, 11 year low consumer sentiment, slowing growth, and rising interest rates.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aF9-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F32ba86ff-326f-48aa-ae50-b35799de65e1_943x529.webp)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aF9-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F32ba86ff-326f-48aa-ae50-b35799de65e1_943x529.webp)

With that said, it is important keep in mind my above point of not fighting the tape.  If my thesis is correct, I will continue to make money after entering shorts with the proper technical signal (a la HCP shorts, etc.).  If my thesis is wrong, and these stocks rally back to 50x sales, I will not lose any money as I do not fight the tape.  This is how a trader can put themself in the best opportunity to succeed, whether they are right or wrong.
