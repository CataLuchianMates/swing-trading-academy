# Recession Roulette

The S&P 500 has dropped -21% from its highs in under 2 months.

We have had a massive positioning unwind, led by historic Hedge Fund de-risking. Total positioning has declined from ATHs at the end of January (+2.6z) to now -2.5z in a little over 2 months.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IXD8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbddacd98-f7af-417f-ab93-6a64a5b31ecf_1284x1182.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!IXD8!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbddacd98-f7af-417f-ab93-6a64a5b31ecf_1284x1182.jpeg)

Many stocks have been exhibiting Bear Market behavior all year, despite the indices peaking in mid-February.

The question now is: “What’s priced in here after a roughly -20% market drop”?

My answer: plenty of fear and bearishness (based on the technicals and extreme underweight positioning), but not a recession.

As evidenced by the extreme underweight positioning (-2.5z) and certain technical indicators (the VIX, RSI, etc.) I have covered, my analysis suggests that we are seeing some telltale signs of capitulation selling. This is welcome news, as it has been established as [1 of my 2 keys](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-2-keys) for calling a market bottom.

Capitulation is 1 piece of the puzzle. The other is earnings.

[This morning](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/whipsaw), I talked about JPM revising FY25 earnings down due to tariffs.

> From JPM - “We reduce FY25 SPX consensus by $29.20 given the impact from tariffs on Canada, China, and Mexico. If we include an additional reduction of $20 to include the impact from RoW, we reduce by ~$50. Current consensus for EPS is $268 avg.”

Other sell-side firms are starting to follow suit, with Barclays now revising earnings down -5% as well.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!14lz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3dabef30-5897-47db-902e-8091bb2ab451_889x511.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!14lz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3dabef30-5897-47db-902e-8091bb2ab451_889x511.png)

Goldman has also revised their FY25 EPS estimate down to $253 (+3% YoY), with a recession case scenario of $220.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!64v3!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcdfa718a-2a50-4529-b40c-22c14fabb631_1074x692.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!64v3!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcdfa718a-2a50-4529-b40c-22c14fabb631_1074x692.jpeg)

Let’s say overall earnings get revised down -5% from $268 consensus right now. This would bring us to total SPX Earnings in the $250 area (which mirrors Goldman’s estimate), still reflecting +3% EPS growth YoY.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Rr1g!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fad2912b0-0e5f-410d-886d-96e4769d6aaf_715x389.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Rr1g!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fad2912b0-0e5f-410d-886d-96e4769d6aaf_715x389.jpeg)

At a $250 earnings level, with the SPX at ~$4980, our forward P/E stands at around 20x right now. This is historically high, (in the 85th percentile versus the past 25 years).

In fact, it is actually higher than any other time outside of the Dot Com Bubble and Covid Bubble.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Jajl!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc09026ac-6ff6-4e82-9fa7-050182565419_1084x866.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Jajl!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc09026ac-6ff6-4e82-9fa7-050182565419_1084x866.jpeg)

As I talked about in [“The 2 Keys”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-2-keys), the S&P 500 has never bottomed from a Bear Market at 20x fwd P/E before. The highest Bear Market bottom was a 16x fwd P/E in 1998.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KDAw!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F74701e94-f19d-4f04-b30e-fba1a688f070_672x410.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KDAw!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F74701e94-f19d-4f04-b30e-fba1a688f070_672x410.png)

Now, let’s say the market trades down to its 10 year average P/E ratio (18x). At an 18x P/E and $250 earnings, we arrive at $4500 in the SPX.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!eerj!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1a80d4d2-4b09-408b-a18c-189bce75ab32_715x389.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!eerj!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1a80d4d2-4b09-408b-a18c-189bce75ab32_715x389.jpeg)

At 16x P/E (the 30yr avg, and the lowest P/E for a Bear Market bottom ever), we are looking at $4050 SPX.

Now, for the **big risk**? Earnings can go even lower than $240-$250 the longer tariffs are in place, particularly the China tariffs.

According to JPM, a 60% tariff on China would be a $15 drag on EPS by itself (-5.6% drag).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LWDN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff1328497-1515-4bff-a442-ba7902248231_707x498.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LWDN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff1328497-1515-4bff-a442-ba7902248231_707x498.jpeg)

Today, President Trump raised the China tariff to 104%. This would translate to a $26 EPS hit, bringing consensus SPX EPS down to $242 (just from current China tariffs alone).

In [“The 2 Keys”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-2-keys), I talked about how in a typical recession, we always observe a coinciding earnings recession. The median recessionary earnings decline is -13%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ii06!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd5663729-9b96-4060-bf45-1682e47f945d_1038x960.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ii06!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd5663729-9b96-4060-bf45-1682e47f945d_1038x960.jpeg)

Even in the extremely shallow 2022 recession, we saw a -4% earnings recession.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!obRK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fac1bea34-0bca-4f0e-85f0-12d897fa394e_395x306.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!obRK!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fac1bea34-0bca-4f0e-85f0-12d897fa394e_395x306.png)

Goldman has stated that their “Recession Case” is $220 EPS (as mentioned above), which reflects a -11% YoY decline. Such a drop (-11%) would be in line with historical recessionary norms.

My conversations with other analysts/PMs suggests a recession is starting to become a fairly consensus view (absent significant progress on trade). Nomura’s top economist, Charlie McElligott, believes that tariffs will push us into a recession, and that they will be a “substantial growth drag”.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SkxC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdaaacd7a-0e33-4314-9317-17ca4946dbef_471x297.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SkxC!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdaaacd7a-0e33-4314-9317-17ca4946dbef_471x297.jpeg)

**So, what’s priced in at the current $4980 SPX level?**

A 20x forward P/E ratio (on $250 earnings, +3% YoY), which is still an expensive market (throughout history). It sounds odd to call the market expensive at -20% off highs, but as I mentioned above, valuations got historically elevated this cycle. This elevated P/E was driven by optimism in Big Tech & AI and expectations of double digit EPS growth (+13%) this year. Now, tariff-related growth drags have lowered EPS estimates, and in some cases, flattened them completely.

I think we will continue to see earnings revised down to $240-$250 EPS across the board, which means we are currently trading at around 20x P/E.

The risk: prolonged tariffs, particularly on China, could force earnings all the way down to $220.

That is exactly why this report is titled “Recession Roulette”. Attaching a normal P/E multiple like 18x or 16x on $220 earnings suggests the market would have to see -20% + downside from here.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0zwA!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F755f2360-de5a-4877-b2c7-4f51dac218ca_715x389.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0zwA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F755f2360-de5a-4877-b2c7-4f51dac218ca_715x389.jpeg)

This is a double whammy, because we still need to see the typical Bear Cycle multiple compression we are already getting, but that could come **in addition to** falling earnings. This is the “Recession Roulette” risk I alluded to, and why I do **not** believe a recession is currently priced into the market.

With earnings season about to kick off, this will be a pivotal quarter for US stocks. The big concern into Q1 Earnings is that despite last quarter’s numbers likely looking completely fine, the worries are about guidance due to the forward visibility concerns from global trade and the impact on consumers. So, if corporates use this environment to guide down or even profit warn, we are likely looking at a deeper selloff to come.

**Final Thoughts**

My $4600 price target on the S&P 500 is looking more and more likely due to falling earnings estimates. In a recession scenario, we could see an extended Bear Market ,with potential declines to the lower $4000 area. Volatility should remain high through earnings season, with huge market swings both ways.
