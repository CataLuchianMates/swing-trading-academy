# What’s troubling the market?

Daily Market Commentary

The S&P 500 sold off hard on Tuesday, breaking through September lows and $4240 support.  The index made a low of $4216 on the day as we near the critical 200 day moving average at $4202.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!t6Dr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F65e2e5ee-b523-48f7-bb4e-6b1e7d57cec4_970x723.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!t6Dr!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F65e2e5ee-b523-48f7-bb4e-6b1e7d57cec4_970x723.jpeg)

I talked yesterday about the importance of the $4200 level, as the 200 day moving average has not been breached since the March regional banking crisis.  Back then, the Fed stepped in and put a bottom in the market.

With its intervention in March, the Fed started a prolonged period of easing financial conditions.  Financial conditions are critical for stocks.  The below chart shows the staggering difference in equity performance when financial conditions are easing versus when they are tightening.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WApv!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6865868b-e00d-4d50-860c-43aa68f67c4f_806x481.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WApv!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6865868b-e00d-4d50-860c-43aa68f67c4f_806x481.jpeg)

This has clearly been a Macro focused tape as the move in rates has been too much to overcome for equities.  10YR yields were in breakout mode yesterday with the 10YR now sitting at 4.8%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XRKI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F35573f6f-e236-4777-b848-0d847e824b45_1250x1200.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XRKI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F35573f6f-e236-4777-b848-0d847e824b45_1250x1200.png)

If you want to explain the recent market sell off, look no further than the below chart.  Stocks were able to rally all year on easing financial conditions but have sold off hard post-September FOMC as financial conditions have tightened considerably (and are now nearing their tightest levels of 2023).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WnIH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbaab6cd4-4963-4090-9aa2-e6ce6350109d_1290x1705.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WnIH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbaab6cd4-4963-4090-9aa2-e6ce6350109d_1290x1705.jpeg)

The weakest parts of the market finished at the bottom of the barrel yesterday (non profitable tech -6%, crypto exposure -5.6%, high short interest -4%, high retail activity -4%), which felt like some catch down action after utilities got smoked to start the week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xCjD!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa38d5fa3-9331-4483-8ab6-2cb8e413e7aa_1245x2204.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xCjD!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa38d5fa3-9331-4483-8ab6-2cb8e413e7aa_1245x2204.jpeg)

Tech remains stronger on a longer time frame, as the QQQ is still well above its 200 day moving average and even below September lows (for the moment).  September’s lows - the $351 support level is key to watch in the upcoming sessions.  I see the technical setup as bearish for QQQ here, especially considering the move in rates and the weak market internals.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!k9vQ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffd8d6009-bf9d-4e83-aa62-37abc55e9f87_977x721.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!k9vQ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffd8d6009-bf9d-4e83-aa62-37abc55e9f87_977x721.jpeg)

Other parts of the market are also struggling.  The S&P Equal Weighted Index has now joined the Russell 2000 Index of Small Caps in the red for 2023 (both down -1% YTD).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NjQ8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F65fc0863-876f-4c50-a1e9-7edbbd7688fd_962x723.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NjQ8!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F65fc0863-876f-4c50-a1e9-7edbbd7688fd_962x723.jpeg)

Despite the recent market correction, there is still no real sign of panic yet with the VIX at 19.48.  The VIX just broke 20 for the first time in 94 trading sessions yesterday (its longest streak below 20 since 2018).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1Kf5!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0ee0e7d5-a5ce-4bf7-a242-c61cd4481534_1290x791.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1Kf5!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0ee0e7d5-a5ce-4bf7-a242-c61cd4481534_1290x791.jpeg)

**Bottom line** – equities are likely to remain under pressure until rates stop moving higher + financial conditions stop tightening (rates are a big part of the FCI equation).
