# The Bear is Back

The initial wave of the Bear Market (The “Covid Bubble Bust”), [which I began writing about in late 2021](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/were-not-in-kansas-anymore), was triggered by an interest rate shock.  During this period, it became clear that inflation was becoming a major crisis (i.e. not transitory) and the Fed had to play catch up with policy.

Since the October 2022 Bear Market lows, the stock market has experienced a noteworthy rally, with key indices bouncing as much as 40% off lows. This resurgence was underpinned by a more resilient economy and labor market than most expected.  Earnings have remained steadfast, defying the concerns of a weakening economy as leading indicators suggested.

However, the action in stocks/”New Bull Market” narrative was highly questionable.  In fact, this would mark the weakest Year 1 performance of a New Bull Market in over 4+ decades, raising concerns about the market's breadth and the sustainability of the market rally.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Oca5!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F28e8418f-9374-482a-9df0-8e619957b589_809x428.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Oca5!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F28e8418f-9374-482a-9df0-8e619957b589_809x428.png)

As the economy continued to outperform expectations and stocks rallied, sentiment rebounded, and a "soft landing" scenario emerged as the consensus narrative.  This is, after all, the only way the “New Bull Market narrative” could realistically play out.  Historically, stocks have never hit their lows before an impending recession.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!h0jh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff9ca5306-2845-48dc-97dd-dbcc79d9e05b_559x492.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!h0jh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff9ca5306-2845-48dc-97dd-dbcc79d9e05b_559x492.jpeg)

This “soft landing” narrative has fallen to the wayside in recent months, with a new deck of recessionary fears rising.  This has been showing in the price action of stocks, with the indices topping in late July & the biggest underperformers being leveraged balance sheets / low quality / economically sensitive & high debt / small caps / credit cycle downturn stocks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KyYx!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0d192c43-3730-432c-9206-2dd9c637b03a_504x510.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KyYx!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0d192c43-3730-432c-9206-2dd9c637b03a_504x510.png)

Speaking of small caps, the Russell 2000 (IWM) has now made fresh new Bear Market lows, dropping below its October 2022 lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yNQp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5eb84e2b-77f5-4952-b4e7-c79dbce132c0_472x457.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yNQp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5eb84e2b-77f5-4952-b4e7-c79dbce132c0_472x457.png)

We are currently nearing 500 straight days since the Russell 2000 last made 52-week highs.  This is closing in on the worst drought in the index’s history, showing how brutal this Bear Market has been (and continues to be) for many companies.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aOJa!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbfa981f3-084c-4334-95a7-471574194761_796x579.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aOJa!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbfa981f3-084c-4334-95a7-471574194761_796x579.png)

Much of the market’s weakness has been masked by the performance of Mega Cap Tech (the Magnificent 7 – AAPL, GOOGL, MSFT, AMZN, META, TSLA, NVDA).  This theme is best shown in the underperformance of the equal weight S&P 500 (RSP).  The equal weight S&P 500 is now down -5.4% for the year, which is its largest underperformance versus the S&P 500 market cap-weight (SPX) in 30+ years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KB-9!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F733f14d1-8edf-449c-80f3-5decb8801eb7_1211x723.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KB-9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F733f14d1-8edf-449c-80f3-5decb8801eb7_1211x723.jpeg)

In fact, the % of companies outperforming the S&P 500 (over the trailing 12 months) is currently at 5 year lows.  This is not what one would expect to see in the beginning of a healthy new Bull Market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1V_o!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8020091c-2859-452e-a09a-31ed80fac7a7_289x175.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1V_o!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8020091c-2859-452e-a09a-31ed80fac7a7_289x175.png)

As I have covered [in recent reports](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-key-story-19e), markets came into Q3 earnings with crowded leadership in Mega Cap tech and AI theme names.  Hedge Fund exposure to the Magnificent 7 currently remains near all-time highs, as money managers refuse to venture outside the perceived safety trade of Mega Cap Tech.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qMsa!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff4c0c4fe-40e8-4d33-ac2c-6e0ebc190a86_1236x779.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qMsa!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff4c0c4fe-40e8-4d33-ac2c-6e0ebc190a86_1236x779.jpeg)

So far through Q3 earnings, these crowded Mega Cap Tech names are being punished for small EPS/Rev beats/not being rewarded by the market for them.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aJpL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F88baa94a-d010-45a6-8040-e3d6a7408578_503x434.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aJpL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F88baa94a-d010-45a6-8040-e3d6a7408578_503x434.jpeg)

This has pressured the S&P 500 over the last 2 weeks, with the index breaching its 200-day moving average for the first time since the March regional banking crisis (YTD lows).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oQhf!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0518669a-7e4f-4c7b-8fd7-e4ecb7e36415_1166x888.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oQhf!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0518669a-7e4f-4c7b-8fd7-e4ecb7e36415_1166x888.jpeg)

With the recent market correction, the S&P 500’s gains for the year have been cut to 7.2%, down significantly from the 20% advance the index was sitting on in July.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zvYN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe1e71066-b0a2-4a1d-ab37-ddc437c28a63_257x334.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zvYN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe1e71066-b0a2-4a1d-ab37-ddc437c28a63_257x334.png)

The recent action in the Fixed Income Market is also consistent with the market beginning to price in a recession.  The yield curve has steepened 88 points and is now the closest it has been to fully un-inverting during this cycle  As I wrote about in [“Bear Market Bottom Checklist”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-market-bottom-checklist), the yield curve un-inversion has preceded the last 6 Bear Market bottoms dating back to 1982.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ILaX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe6c6ce07-8338-4bc7-b0a3-637a39c115c5_715x471.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ILaX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe6c6ce07-8338-4bc7-b0a3-637a39c115c5_715x471.jpeg)

**My view:**

The next wave of the Bear Market has begun, which appears to be a recessionary trade.  Most stocks are currently at their lows of 2023 after a 1st half rally.  Many have already reached their new Bear Market lows, breaking below October 2022 levels.  It should be clear that the 1st half/Q4 ’22 rally is not consistent with a “New Bull Market”.

With that said, I still believe there is a legitimate question as to whether the S&P 500 will make new lows (below $3500) for this cycle.  This will be dependent on the economy, which has been more resilient to higher interest rates (to this point) than I expected.

If it is a milder recession, I believe it is more likely for the SPX to successfully re-test October 2022 lows / make a higher low (above >$3500).  In a deeper recession, new lows for the SPX (below <3500) would be more likely.

The key metric to hone in on is labor.  My belief is once jobs go, the consumer and earnings will crack, and the whole cycle will go.  In my view, this will determine how deep the recession is and where the stock market bottoms.
