# Thanksgiving Week

US stocks finished last week higher with clear signs of a continuing rotation to Small Caps (RTY +4.3% vs SPX & NDX +1.7%).

The best performers on the week were Liquid Shorts & Non-Profitable Tech up +8-10%, while the worst performers were Mega Caps, Defense, and China ADRs down -0.3-0.7%.

Mega Cap Tech has had a massive run over the past couple years, and it appears we are finally seeing some money rotate down the market cap and up the risk spectrum. It is visible in price action and money flows, where institutional investors have been selling/trimming Mega Cap Tech to buy Small Caps/pro-cyclicals pockets/Trump winners.

Looking ahead to this week, it is expected to be a low volume/quiet trading week with Thanksgiving on Thursday. Typically, Wednesday and Friday are particularly quiet trading days during Thanksgiving week.

When you have a low volume/low liquidity week, it is typical to see the market drift higher (or lower) based on the prevailing market trend/sentiment. We are very clearly in a bullish uptrend across the board right now (and sentiment is very high), so that is good news for stocks this week. With that said, it is not atypical to see whipsaw/choppy action (at least in the indices/low volume names) when there are low trading volumes/liquidity, so be aware that we may be in for a week of choppier price action.

Overall, Thanksgiving week tends to be bullish with 70% of historical returns positive during this week. Moreover, seasonality trends point to a strong finish to 2024 from this week forward.
