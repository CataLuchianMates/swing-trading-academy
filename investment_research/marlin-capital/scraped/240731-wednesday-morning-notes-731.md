# Wednesday Morning Notes 7.31

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Another tough day for Tech yesterday with NVDA leading the sell off, down -7% as Jensen Huang failed to provide much new info during Monday night’s keynote (there was also a Reuters report about how Apple used Google chips to train its models, suggesting some increasing competition coming for NVDA).

NVDA is now down -23% from its peak after briefly becoming the largest company in the world.  All the Mag 7 stocks are in correction territory heading into Wednesday’s session.

The S&P 500 has held support at its 60 EMA ($5400) so far during this sell off.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MvDC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1a2832b7-6092-4082-98de-8e31dbdbcec2_1147x861.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!MvDC!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1a2832b7-6092-4082-98de-8e31dbdbcec2_1147x861.jpeg)

Despite the negative price action, SPX breadth was actually positive yesterday with 330 names finishing higher on the day.  Equal weight SPX and RTY both finished up +40bps on the day.

The move lower was led by Tech (as it has been since the July peak), particularly Mag 7 and Semis (now down -17% from the July peak).

The QQQ closed right at its 100 EMA ($457) support yesterday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ewqf!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faa0e5cdd-02d2-461a-90e2-a81588478b85_1166x873.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ewqf!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faa0e5cdd-02d2-461a-90e2-a81588478b85_1166x873.jpeg)

We are set for a huge gap up this morning (+2% in QQQ) ahead of today’s FOMC meeting.  AMD came through for Semis after hours yesterday with a better quarter and guide than expected.  The stock is set to gap up +7% this morning.  MSFT earnings disappointed with a miss on Azure, but the stock has already recaptured most of its losses pre-market and is now set to open down only -1-2%.

WGS – From my [Top Small Caps picks](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-small-caps-with-massive-upside), gapping up over 20% this morning following a terrific earnings and Whole Genome Sequencing product enhancements.  Following AEYE’s +38% rally on Friday, it’s looking like we have struck gold on yet another Small Cap!

PRG – Terrific move out of Thursday’s inside day.  We’ve tagged my initial $45-$46 targets.  I’ve sold 1/3rd of my position and moving my stop to breakeven ($43) on the other 2/3rds.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!W758!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd52bd8eb-cf99-4612-864b-4cb3e1fd6a4e_1192x884.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!W758!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd52bd8eb-cf99-4612-864b-4cb3e1fd6a4e_1192x884.jpeg)

My next target is $50, but we may need to see some consolidation/flagging first.

NOW – Tagged its 8 EMA during yesterday’s Tech sell off, but did not hit my $783 stop loss.  My stop will remain there and I am letting this trade play out.
