# The Week Ahead

US equities sold off sharply following last week’s FOMC decision, leaving the S&P 500 now 6.2% off its July peak.  This is the first >5% pullback in the SPX since mid-March and was arguably overdue following the YTD rally.

In my view, the current pullback is likely to extend when looking at the charts.  The S&P 500 closed last week at lows after attempting a bounce on Friday.  Technically, the index has now made a lower high followed by a lower low to confirm the current downtrend.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nMs7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fca1d3858-8c3c-4848-8ec8-24bdd2e70896_1203x888.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nMs7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fca1d3858-8c3c-4848-8ec8-24bdd2e70896_1203x888.jpeg)

My initial target for the S&P 500 on this pullback remains the 200-day moving average at $4190.  This would be another -3% lower for the index.

Meanwhile, the Russell 2000 index of small caps paints an uglier picture for the health of the market.  The IWM has definitively broken below its 200-day moving average and appears headed towards YTD lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Dw8F!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb517fcf8-147f-4c39-b79c-daede4f9df10_953x726.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Dw8F!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb517fcf8-147f-4c39-b79c-daede4f9df10_953x726.jpeg)

Positioning wise, hedge funds have been pressing shorts for 3 straight weeks and 5 of the last 6.  In cumulative notional terms over the past 6 weeks, the amount of hedge fund short selling in US stocks ranks in the 98th percentile vs the last decade.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tAAN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe57008d6-9227-41e0-bf36-abda7b811039_1210x484.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tAAN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe57008d6-9227-41e0-bf36-abda7b811039_1210x484.jpeg)

This should serve to make the market even more volatile with potential violent short squeezes that we have seen at points during 2023.

With that said, it is an overall bearish picture as we head into this week despite the indices flashing short-term oversold readings.  I am sitting in all cash heading into this week and looking for a potential oversold bounce to consider any new short positions.
