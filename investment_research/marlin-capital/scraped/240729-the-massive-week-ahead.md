Overall, Small Caps had another strong week of inflows while the de-risking continued in Large Caps (recall, [last week’s inflows to Small Caps](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-turning-point) were the highest in 17 years).  Big Tech has now retreated nearly ~15% from highs while Goldman’s Hedge Fund VIP Long Tech basket (crowded HF longs) is down in 8 of the last 10 sessions.

As we enter a huge week for the market (FOMC + Big Tech Earnings), alongside cleaner investor positioning, there seems to be a growing acknowledgement around the more “complex” market that looms exiting Q2 earnings (slowing topline growth in Mag 7, upcoming Election, Rate Cuts, GenAI ROIC debates).

The gap between Mega Cap EPS and the rest of the SPX is projected to narrow in the coming years, and the market seems to be starting to price this in (GOOGL and TSLA earnings declines, outperformance from Small Caps & SPX Equal Weight, profit taking in YTD winners/Mega Cap).

So far, the Mag 7 is not off to a hot start in Q2 with TSLA declining over 10% and GOOGL over 5% following their respective reports.  This led to Wednesday’s worst day for Tech stocks in 2 years (Oct ’22).

Some quick 1-liners previewing this week’s upcoming Mag 7 earnings:

MSFT – GS desk views positioning as a 7.5 out of 10…Investors are looking for a 3-handle growth on Azure + early FY25 CapEx views + reiterating initial FY 25 guide

META – GS desk views positioning as a 7.5 out of 10…Investors are looking for a Q2 revs beat + refreshed GenAI product/Revs vs cost framework

AMZN – GS desk views positioning as an 8 out of 10…Investors are looking for the AWS acceleration story, EBIT upside in international + North America/Ads momentum

AAPL – GS desk views positioning as an 8 out of 10…Investors are looking for a less impactful quarter/guide. It’s all about the narrative, buybacks, and early 2H iPhone commentary

While these are the big names set to report, this week offers a loaded slate of earnings for companies of all sizes.  [Potential earnings gappers](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-earnings-gapper-trade-setup?utm_source=publication-search) will be my focus this week in my trading account.

We also have the FOMC meeting on Wednesday, with rates unlikely to be cut this week.  The market has 2+ rate cuts price in down the stretch of the year (the Sep meeting and 1 in the Nov/Dec meeting).  For this week, Fed Chair Powell’s commentary and any updates/revisions to the statement (on unemployment, inflation) will be the keys to watch for.

The below chart will be worth stashing on your desk as we near the start of the Fed easing cycle.  Equities tend to perform well after the Fed easing cycle starts (with Small Caps outperforming), unless growth is weak.  The key judgment here is, rate cuts are a big positive for stocks so long as we avoid a recession.
