# Bounce Phase

The first half of March is in the books and it has been a volatile stretch, to say the least. The S&P 500 officially entered correction territory (-10%) last week as investors continue to digest the implications of tariffs/policy uncertainty. A -10% correction was my first and initial target for the S&P 500 [since February 23rd.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/red-flags-emerge)

The S&P 500 has declined -10% in 20 calendar days, which is the 5th fastest correction in the last 75 years (fastest ever was 8 days during the Covid Crash). Despite the oversold rally last Friday, US stocks closed out yet another week in the red. On the week: SPX -2.3%, NDX -2.5%, RTY -1.8%.

As I have covered in detail over the past month, the key driver of this market correction has been a massive positioning unwind. We exited January at all-time high long positioning across institutional, retail and foreign investors (everyone was “all in” the market). Since then, Hedge Funds have de-risked at a record pace.

Overall positioning is now nearing “light” territory, which speaks to just how sharp and fast this de-grossing has been in a short amount of time.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JOHz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F66612eb1-fbac-479e-ac79-d8756f2c27de_915x804.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JOHz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F66612eb1-fbac-479e-ac79-d8756f2c27de_915x804.jpeg)

The obvious catalyst for the de-risking, particularly among hedge funds, is the current trade policy uncertainty and how it will weigh on the economy. During Trump’s first term and, we saw equity positioning reach “light” territory (-1 Z score) during the China trade war. DB estimates a similar positioning undwind to -1 Z score (the bottom of the band) would take the S&P 500 down to $5250.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VGkf!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5207ae0e-ded8-4a98-8ef6-d5ea0fea4438_1263x836.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VGkf!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5207ae0e-ded8-4a98-8ef6-d5ea0fea4438_1263x836.jpeg)

The question now, with the massive positioning unwind we have **already** seen, is how close we are to a bottom/did Friday’s rally begin the bottoming process? From a technical standpoint, as I have covered in detail, the major indices have already suffered a significant amount of damage (more than we witnessed during any other “corrections” since the Bull Market began). At a minimum, this kind of technical damage takes time to repair.

With that said, we reached deeply oversold levels last week in the indices and among many individual stocks (most oversold since 2022). As mentioned, this has been an incredibly fast market correction (5th fastest ever), without more than a 1-day rally attempt throughout.

I talked last week about the likelihood of a multi-day market bounce coming after 11 straight days outside of the lower Bollinger Band for the QQQ (this is very extreme). It appears Friday was the beginning of this rally attempt and we are seeing some follow through early this morning.

To help matters, CTAs flipped short last week and are now short -$20B S&P 500. After Friday’s rip in the market, CTAs will likely be forced buyers of the S&P 500 for the 1st time in weeks. This is the trend following community. If the rally continues, demand will continue to grow.

For the SPX, I am targeting a potential bounce back to the 200 SMA at $5740 (another +1-2%). This is where technical resistance (former support all throughout the Bull Market) will start to play a factor.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bqYT!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb414c387-293f-4c2a-8b07-e5396e7a94a7_980x717.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bqYT!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb414c387-293f-4c2a-8b07-e5396e7a94a7_980x717.jpeg)

Big picture - in my view, it will take more than just an oversold market to get a sustainable market bottom (i.e., more than just a tradeable rally). History supports this notion as well (on a technical basis).

Friday was a 90% upside day in the NYSE. Since 2007, the first 90% upside day after a -10% market correction has only signaled a market bottom once (Dec. 2018). In every other case (11 total), we either re-tested the lows or made new lows in the following weeks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Dw_G!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6eb3f5f7-3832-4758-be6e-5dd031cd3600_2048x1120.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Dw_G!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6eb3f5f7-3832-4758-be6e-5dd031cd3600_2048x1120.png)

This supports my view that once we enter a market correction, the first big rally attempt (90% upside day) is typically just a dead cat bounce (11/12 times since 2007, 92%).

Looking bigger picture, I continue to believe that earnings revisions are the most important variable to watch now. Goldman has revised their 2025 and 2026 earnings estimates to $262 and $280 respectively (down from $268 and $288), reflecting growth of +7% for both year.

Despite all the uncertainty, we have not seen any major research firms come out and cut their estimates into negative territory (i.e. an earnings recession). With that said, we are indeed seeing earnings estimates revised lower across the board (and I don’t think we are quite done yet).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fxsi!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F14e3db4b-10e0-4560-a6bc-c054e44b0b4d_1293x901.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fxsi!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F14e3db4b-10e0-4560-a6bc-c054e44b0b4d_1293x901.jpeg)

There is still plenty of risk for another wave down in the market. While the recent market correction is consistent with the SPX pricing in more recession risk, we can clearly see based off the Fixed Income market pricing and earnings estimates, that there is certainly no recession priced into the market at current levels.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4sBn!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe1976ef9-7411-42c7-9af5-f7c7fb60d414_767x525.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!4sBn!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe1976ef9-7411-42c7-9af5-f7c7fb60d414_767x525.jpeg)

As I talked about last week, recessionary corrections/bear markets tend to be much longer and deeper than the current market correction. JPM currently assigns a 40% probability of a recession in 2025. If a recession starts to become the consensus view among market participants, I feel confident in saying that we will have another wave down in the market.

Bottom line, I am looking for a short term oversold rally back to the 200 SMA in the SPX ($5740), which will likely be led by stocks that have sold off the most (i.e., the most oversold stocks). Following this bounce, I believe we are setting up for at least a re-test of lows (in a more bullish/no recession scenario, it is possible these lows hold and we start to form a bottom), or another wave lower (bear case, in a recession scenario).
