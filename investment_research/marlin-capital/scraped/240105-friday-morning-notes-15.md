# Friday Morning Notes 1.5

**Daily Market Commentary:**

US stocks were choppy on Thursday with the S&P 500 closing -0.34% at $4688 (just below the key 21 EMA support level).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jHDH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F75e9ae68-c9e8-40fe-b887-e9355ab3054a_961x721.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jHDH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F75e9ae68-c9e8-40fe-b887-e9355ab3054a_961x721.jpeg)

Yields inched higher with the market repricing early rate cuts (following yesterday's stronger than expected ADP & fewer jobless claims).  Amazon struggled yesterday after negative third party data channel checks (specific to AWS).  Apple down another -123bps yesterday on its 2nd downgrade of the week (Piper Sandler).  MBLY also dropped -24% on a negative pre-announcement related to inventory issues which brought investors to question the broader read-through to auto semis given a cut of this magnitude speaks to the risks to the autos that many investors are worried about in the 1H (ASPs, volumes, inventories, etc.).

This morning’s jobs report - US economy adds 216,000 jobs in Dec (well above the roughly 170,000 jobs economists expected), ending 2023 with a bang. Unemployment rate held at 3.7% vs 3.8% expected. Wage growth a bit hot as well, accelerated to +4.1%, up from +4% in Nov & above Street’s 3.9% forecast.
