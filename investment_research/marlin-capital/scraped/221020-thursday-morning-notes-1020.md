Technical Analysis

We had a choppy day in the market yesterday, with the QQQ once again topping out at the daily 21 EMA level ($274-$275). This is the key level for bulls to get over for a larger continuation rally. A decisive move above $275 puts a potential rally to the 60 EMA ($287-$290) into play.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K3l2!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5a186c44-84f9-4962-85be-62fd4e34e438_1053x811.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K3l2!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5a186c44-84f9-4962-85be-62fd4e34e438_1053x811.jpeg)

Some (like Mike Wilson) are calling for an even bigger rally before the next move down begins.

Also important to note that monthly options expiration are tomorrow, which should only add to the volatility and choppiness of the market. In certain past monthly options expirations, a big move to start the week was completely retraced as the index closed (more or less) right where it started the week. In this case, that would be a weekly close tomorrow of around $3640 for SPX.

Record amounts of puts have been bought the last several weeks, as I have called attention to in the morning notes. The max pain for tomorrow’s monthly options expirations seems to be a further rally or chop and premium burn for both sides.

$268-$270 remains the key support level to watch on the downside.
