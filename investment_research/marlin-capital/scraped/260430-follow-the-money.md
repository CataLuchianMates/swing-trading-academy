**My bottom line:** This is exactly why we hold great companies through all the noise. This one is going in the books as one of our best trades of the entire cycle‼️

#### GLW

Corning posted another strong quarter. But the stock did not love it initially.

Q1 core sales came in at $4.35B (+18% YoY, beat est. by 1.8%) and core EPS of $0.70 (+30% YoY) met consensus. Optical Communications was once again the star, with sales ripping +36% YoY to $1.8B and segment net income +93%. Solar sales surged +80% YoY. Op margin expanded +220bps to 20.2%.

This is GLW’s 8th straight quarter of YoY sales beats. The Springboard plan continues to fire on all cylinders. But the biggest news, in my view, is the new hyperscaler LTAs.

GLW just signed 2 new long-term agreements with hyperscalers, each of similar magnitude to the original Meta deal (~$6B each). These deals lock in fiber demand visibility through 2030, with risk/reward shared via take-or-pay structures. This is massive for the durability of the AI optical thesis.

So why the negative reaction in GLW? This looks like some normal post-earnings profit taking to me. GLW had soared +287% over the last year heading into the print. The Q2 guide came in line with consensus ($4.6B sales, $0.73-0.77 EPS midpoint vs. $0.74 cons), which was somewhat underwhelming.

**My bottom line:** In my view, this is simply healthy digestion for GLW. This was a good earnings print overall. May 6th is the Springboard upgrade event, and that should be the next catalyst for the stock. We will continue holding GLW in the [2026 Portfolio.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/marlin-capital-live-portfolio-dashboards)

#### TER

Teradyne delivered a record Q1. Yet the stock got crushed -19% today.

On the surface, the numbers were great . Revenue of $1.28B beat consensus by 6% and grew +87% YoY. EPS of $2.56 crushed estimates by 20%. Gross margin hit a record 60.9%. Semi Test crossed $1B in a quarter for the first time ever. AI is now ~70% of total revenue, up from 60% just last quarter.

This was a blowout print. So what happened?

**Q2 guidance.**

TER guided Q2 revenue to $1.15-1.25B, which implies a sequential decline from Q1’s record #. Management did not raise the FY26 target ($6B rev, $9.50-11.00 EPS). After a +400% long-term run on AI backlog momentum, the lack of a guide raise was a disappointment for traders who were expecting a beat and raise on top of a beat and raise.

**My bottom line:** At this point in the cycle, the market wants to see beat **and RAISE** quarters. Not beat and meet quarters (which is what TER did here). But for me, this does not look like a demand issue for TER. And I expect them to comfortably beat in the next couple quarters after not raising expectations here. For me, the fundamental story here is still intact, and I will be holding for now.

Q2 revenue is expected to be “at least as good as Q1.” This is a company that just signed a 2.45GW Oracle Jupiter deal (one of the largest islanded microgrids ever) and has >50% of its data center backlog from hyperscalers, neoclouds, and colos.

#### Mag 7 Earnings

Tonight after the bell, 4 of the Mag 7 reported: GOOGL, MSFT, META, and AMZN. While we do not have any positions in these names (and don’t plan to), these are the companies funding the AI buildout, and their CapEx commentary is what drives every name we own.

Here is what you need to know from these reports. 👇

#### GOOGL

Google delivered a clean beat across the board. Q1 revenue of $109.9B grew +22% YoY, the 11th straight quarter of double-digit growth. Operating income up +30%. Net income up +81%. Google Cloud revenue grew +63% YoY to $20B, with operating margin expanding from 17.8% to 32.9%. The cloud backlog nearly doubled sequentially to $462B.

**And most importantly, GOOGL raised its FY26 CapEx guidance to $180-190B (from $175-185B), with 2027 CapEx set to “increase significantly” from there.**

This is a green light for the entire AI infrastructure trade. $462B in backlog. $180-190B in CapEx. And 2027 going higher. Every optical, memory, networking, data center, and power name we own benefits from these dollars getting deployed.

#### MSFT

Microsoft delivered another solid quarter. Revenue of $82.9B grew +18% YoY. EPS of $4.27 was up +21%.

The AI business hit a $37B annualized run rate, growing +123% YoY. Azure grew +40%, coming in ahead of expectations. Microsoft 365 Copilot paid seats hit 20M, up +250% YoY.

**And most importantly, Microsoft expects to spend ~$190B in CapEx in calendar year 2026.** Q4 CapEx alone is expected to be over $40B.

Demand continues to exceed available capacity at least through 2026. Azure is expected to accelerate further in H2 2026. **“Demand exceeds supply.”** That is the most bullish phrase in the entire AI theme right now. MSFT is essentially printing money and reinvesting it all into AI infrastructure.

#### META

Meta announced mixed results, with Q1 revenue of $56.3B up +33% YoY. Family of apps ad revenue grew +33%. Q2 guidance of $58-61B exceeded expectations.

But the headline that matters for our portfolio is the CapEx raise.

**META raised FY26 CapEx guidance to $125-145B (from $115-135B)**, citing “higher component pricing, particularly memory” and additional data center costs. When META cites memory pricing as a reason for raising CapEx, this is our Memory Supercycle thesis showing up in real-time.

#### AMZN

AMZN closed out the night with a strong report. Q1 revenue of $181.5B grew +17% YoY. Operating margin hit a record 13.1%.

AWS revenue accelerated to +28% YoY growth, the fastest in 15 quarters, hitting a $150B annualized run rate. AI revenue is growing triple digits YoY.

And most importantly, Q1 CapEx came in at $43.2B (+77% YoY), with the FY26 CapEx track running near $200B.

**Combined with the other 3 hyperscalers, we are looking at well over $650B in combined 2026 CapEx from just these 4 companies.**
