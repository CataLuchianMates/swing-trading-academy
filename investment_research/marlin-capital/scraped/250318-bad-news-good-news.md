# Bad News, Good News

The market indices are selling off once again so far today after an initial +0.64% S&P 500 bounce to kick off the week yesterday. I talked yesterday about how I was looking for an oversold bounce to the $5740 region in the SPX (200 day MA). We didn’t quite tag the 200 day yesterday, but we came close by hitting a high of $5704. This was followed by a gap down and sell day so far today.

Here’s the bad news - the S&P 500 and Nasdaq are both now setting up for Bearish crosses on the daily timeframe. These would be the first bearish crosses in the indices since the 2022 Bear Market.

This is a bad sign for the market indices because Bearish crosses have typically, throughout history, signaled the end of Bull Markets. Since the March 2009 S&P 500 lows, we have had 6 Bearish crosses.

In all 6 cases, the S&P 500 did not bottom until at least -15% declines. So far, we have only reached a -10.5% decline so far in 2025.

In 4 out of 6 cases, the S&P 500 went on to reach Bear Market declines (-20% +). The other 2 declines were -17.2% in the 2011 market correction and -15.2% in the 2015 market correction.

As I have talked about before, it is normal to see routine market corrections (-10% +) during Bull Market uptrends . Historically, it happens at least once a year. We saw a -10% SPX correction like this last August.

However, routine market corrections like this typically do not have Bearish crosses on the daily timeframe. In these routine market corrections, you will ordinarily see a shakeout to the 200 SMA (or just below), before a relatively quick bounce back above. Once the S&P 500 has established itself below the 200 day MA and stayed below long enough for a Bearish cross to occur, it has historically led to larger declines (-15% +) than we have seen so far in 2025 (10.5%).

We have already seen the leaders of the Bull Market (the Mag 7) break down. META is the only Mag 7 name currently above its 200 SMA, while the other 6 look to now be setting up for Bearish crosses.

This is the Bad News.

The Good News is, the positioning headwind I have talked so much about for the last month + looks to be in the top of the 9th inning. From Goldman Prime’s note this morning:

> “During time’s of market volatility, “what inning are win” is a constant question on the desk….Based on the work from the GS Futures strat team one could argue that the systematic de-risk in US equity futures is at the top of the 9th, and the “systematic tail” now exists to the upside

Over the next 1 month in a +2 standard deviation tape gs futures strats model that CTAs would need to purchase over $45B+ of SPX equity risk. This would be the largest 1m forward demand we have seen since 2023 (SPX finished +5.7% that month). Even in a risk off tape, the CTA community has already derisked SPX so much, that there is almost 0 incremental supply.

In laymen’s terms, CTAs flipped short at the end of last week and will be forced to buy if the market can continue to bounce. Even in a down market, they will not be selling any more as they are already “max short”.

This is no longer a headwind, and would be a potential major tailwind for stocks in a rallying market. The other tailwind for the market, positioning wise, is month end pension buying.

At the end of March, Goldman estimates pensions are modeled to buy +41B of US equities and sell the equivalent of bonds (for quarter-end pension rebalance). This is the 5th highest buy estimate of all time dating back to 2000, as stocks have underperformed bonds by almost 7% MTD.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EXTZ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa6c82065-2f37-48b6-9eac-26a8828c0f9b_1070x754.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EXTZ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa6c82065-2f37-48b6-9eac-26a8828c0f9b_1070x754.jpeg)

As I talked about yesterday, this entire sell off has been a total positioning and risk unwind. With the amount of de-risking that has already taken place, along with quarter end pension rebalancing, positioning is starting to look like more of a tailwind here (or at least, the end of March is).

Overall, no changes to my big picture view that we have likely not seen lows yet. With that said, the end of March will have some bullish positioning tailwinds at play, which makes me much more bullish for a further bounce. If CTAs being forced to buy, along with quarter end pension buying, things could get squeezy.
