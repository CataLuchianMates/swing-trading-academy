# Friday Morning Notes 9.6

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Price action was mixed yesterday (SPX -0.3%) with some Mega Cap strength led by AMZN (most citing Yipit data overnight) and TSLA.  Volumes were overall quieter on the fade lower after a busier start to the week.

AVGO results last night were a mixed bag and are providing a downside catalyst for Semis (and Tech) this morning.  There was a slight beat on headline results but Semi Solutions Revs missed ($7.27B vs cons $7.41B).  Stock is down over -8% this morning 1 hour into the trading day.

The big news this morning was the NFP report.  The US economy added 142k jobs in August, well below expectations of 164k.  The unemployment rate came in at 4.2%, in line with expectations of 4.2%.

Underneath the surface, this Jobs report is even weaker than it looks.  Jobs growth is only being seen in part-time/foreign born workers:

Full time workers: -438k

Part time workers: +527k

Foreign born workers: +635k in August

US born workers: -1.33M in August

The June and July jobs reports have also been revised lower by a combined 86k jobs.  The June jobs report was revised lower from 179k jobs added to 118k.

The July jobs reports was revised lower from 114k jobs added to 89k.  This means that the July jobs report was the weakest in terms of jobs added since December 2020.

This also makes it difficult to trust the accuracy of today’s data (for August), as it is likely that this morning’s 142k number will end up being revised lower next month.  This has been the theme for the past several months as the Labor market has begun to show some cracks.

None of this is fooling the market though, as the recent price action has shown a distinct flight to safety/recessionary trade in both the US and Europe.  Defensive sectors are the top performers over the past 1, 3, and 6 months (in the US and Europe) while growth proxies/cyclicals have underperformed.
