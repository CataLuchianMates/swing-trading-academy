# Friday Morning Notes 1.13

Technical Update

Inflation data came in as expected yesterday, and the market responded with a choppy session. The SPX managed to close +0.34% on the day, ending the day 1 point below its 200 day moving average.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QUDh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2a4f05a5-d401-40fa-aa7e-6deb7a9b8331_959x729.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QUDh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2a4f05a5-d401-40fa-aa7e-6deb7a9b8331_959x729.jpeg)

We are set for a gap down in the indices this morning. The key resistance levels to watch remain the 200 SMA at $3984, the key psychological $4000 level, and prior Bear rally highs at $4100.

Support levels below include $3940-$3950 (previous resistance) and $3890-$3900, where both the 21 and 60 EMAs currently reside. I will continue to watch this Bull & Bear battle play out from the sidelines until some of the aforementioned key levels are decisively broken.
