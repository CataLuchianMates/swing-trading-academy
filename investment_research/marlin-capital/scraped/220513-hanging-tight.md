# Hanging Tight

First off, an update on the general market.  The tech heavy Nasdaq has now reached its 200 week EMA, a level of key significance that has acted as support in past market corrections. As it stands, the Nasdaq is down roughly 30% from its late November peak.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Natu!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feb8316fa-9b6c-4bc8-a00f-a36d0d8df3cf_1327x627.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Natu!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feb8316fa-9b6c-4bc8-a00f-a36d0d8df3cf_1327x627.png)

The S&P 500 has a bit more downside to go to reach its 200 week EMA, as you can see below. The index is currently down just over 18% from its November peak.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!x09q!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fde026450-dba1-411e-a1e2-09d666d56894_1331x629.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!x09q!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fde026450-dba1-411e-a1e2-09d666d56894_1331x629.png)

While these levels have provided support in the past, I believe we are currently in a Bear Market that is not yet finished (similar to 2000 and 2008). There are many reasons to believe 2022 will continue to be a treacherous environment for equities..

**Rapidly Tightening Financial Conditions**

Financial conditions have tightened considerably in recent weeks, but we have not even crossed over to the dark side yet (see below).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zWja!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F59591870-961f-43a2-bf0e-14f3812912bc_1024x802.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zWja!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F59591870-961f-43a2-bf0e-14f3812912bc_1024x802.png)

Thus far, we have seen 75 BPs of rate hikes in the US.  In total, we should see over 200 BPs of rate hikes this year, the fastest “lift off” since the early 80s.  In the 2nd half of 2022, we will embark on the fastest Quantitative Tightening cycle ever.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rIX6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5afdc6e2-b00a-43d4-902a-1109e3e6b2c0_1233x821.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!rIX6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5afdc6e2-b00a-43d4-902a-1109e3e6b2c0_1233x821.png)

We are already beginning to see some of the effects of tighter monetary policy, as indicated by the Credit Conditions Index below.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_bl1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8d4c35b3-8973-49dc-81ab-1b352a091416_649x565.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_bl1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8d4c35b3-8973-49dc-81ab-1b352a091416_649x565.png)

The Credit Conditions Index tends to lead equities. With the indicator still down, it indicates more downside for the S&P 500.  Additionally, credit conditions are likely to continue to deteriorate as the Fed attempts to tame inflation by any means necessary.

**More Room for Multiple Compression**

Rapidly tightening financial conditions are likely to continue to pressure equity valuations.  The S&P 500 currently trades at a 16.7x Fwd P/E Ratio, leaving more room for multiple compression ahead.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!chKp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F651cf8e4-b3df-48f6-9b41-458eade83e65_2047x892.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!chKp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F651cf8e4-b3df-48f6-9b41-458eade83e65_2047x892.png)

I broke out the last 4 “Bear Markets” for a clearer view.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6ABr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd7285c35-88a7-4b18-a95f-86e7d708c3c2_419x145.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6ABr!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd7285c35-88a7-4b18-a95f-86e7d708c3c2_419x145.png)

As you can see above, the last 4 “Bear Markets” saw the S&P 500 bottom while trading at or below a 14x forward earnings multiple.  In the current environment, this would imply a minimum of 16% downside for the S&P 500, indicating a decline to below $3300.

I believe we are likely to hit the 14x P/E range in 2022 because (1) the Fed needs to lower asset prices to fight inflation; and (2) recessionary fears will likely find their way to the equity markets.

**Recession Shock?**

As I have discussed in [past reports](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/lets-talk-about-it?s=w), I believe a recession will ultimately need to be priced into the equity markets before we can see **the** bottom for this cycle.  This remains a major risk for equity investors right now.  The probability of a recession is beginning to rise slowly, but it is not yet priced into equity markets.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3fq7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1f2516c6-2994-4b22-9992-67a5f0d49d40_488x299.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3fq7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1f2516c6-2994-4b22-9992-67a5f0d49d40_488x299.png)

US GDP declined -1.4% annualized in Q1, but recessionary fears have remained largely subdued by equity investors. BofA is predicting a global earnings recession by 2023. The last three times we have seen negative global earnings growth, it has led to a recession in the US (see below).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ma3O!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0b1a8d45-6674-477f-8acf-d7ac1407380e_470x317.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ma3O!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0b1a8d45-6674-477f-8acf-d7ac1407380e_470x317.png)

In my view, equity investors should begin preparing for a recession before the market starts to.  I believe this is the next logical point for us to reach during this cycle, and I see it as the final step of this Bear Market.

As I mentioned in [“Let’s Talk About It”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/lets-talk-about-it?s=w), the last 4 recessions have resulted in an average decline of 39.9% for the S&P 500, which would imply a move to the $2900 region this time around.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!b1w4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff9d24c04-488a-47b1-9dfa-0ce3569f9c11_1170x665.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!b1w4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff9d24c04-488a-47b1-9dfa-0ce3569f9c11_1170x665.png)

In my view, the most likely place for the S&P 500 to bottom is in the $2800-$3300 region.  At these levels, the index would trade below a 14x Forward P/E, a multiple that could finally be considered “cheap” on a historical basis.  Additionally, the 30-45% peak to trough decline would be in line with previous “recession shocks” that have hit the market.  This is where I would look to use most of my cash in my long term retirement account.

**Summary**

* In my view, the $2800-$3300 levels are the most likely bottoming points in the S&P 500.
* Risk remains to the downside with a Recession not currently priced into the equity market.
* This is a process.  These things take time.  Be patient.  Those who are in a hurry to make money will continue to be punished by this market.
