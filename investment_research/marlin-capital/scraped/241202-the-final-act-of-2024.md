# The Final Act of 2024

The S&P 500 finished off November at all-time highs, rallying +5.7% on the month. This was the best monthly performance for the SPX since November 2023. The NDX had its best monthly performance (+5.2%) since June, and the RTY had its best monthly performance (+10.8%) since December 2023.

So where do markets go from here?

I remain bullish into year end given the positive earnings season we have had, strong technicals, dovish Fed, and supportive Macro environment. If you ask me to put a number on where we finish off 2024, I see $6200-$6300 on the SPX as a very achievable target. This implies 2.8% - 4.4% upside from Friday’s close.

As I’ve written about in length over the last several months, I continue to believe Small Caps are in a position to outperform from here. Market concentration is at historically high levels after the huge Mega Cap rally we have seen over the past couple years. The 10 largest stocks in the S&P 500 currently account for over a third of the total market cap.

In November, we finally saw the rally broaden out, with Small Caps outperforming Mega Caps (the Mag 7).

I have been anticipating Small Caps to turn the tables since July, when I released the [Small Cap 6 report](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-small-caps-with-massive-upside). A main part of my thesis was that the Fed would soon be starting its easing cycle, which is the part of the monetary cycle that historically favors Small Caps.

The true catalyst for the Russell 2000 breakout in November, though, was Trump’s victory in the US election. I talked about why I believe Trump’s administration will be bullish for Small Caps in [“Activate the Trump Trades”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/activate-the-trump-trades). Truth be told, these are both major tailwinds for Small Caps moving forward (monetary and fiscal policies), as well as the bullish technical breakout (4 month base) in the Russell 2000.

Make sure you continue to own the [Small Cap 6](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/6-small-caps-with-massive-upside) moving forward. These stocks outperformed while the index consolidated for months into November, and I expect them to continue to outperform ahead. Each of them reported outstanding earnings this season, and the market rewarded them handsomely.

If you are a new subscriber to Marlin Capital premium, my suggestion would be to initiate positions on -10%+ pullbacks. Small Caps are inherently volatile and higher beta stocks, and double digit pullbacks come fairly often in these names. Given my thesis that Small Caps will continue to outperform and the bullish uptrend the Russell 2000 now finds itself in, I view these random -10%+ pullbacks as buyable (in the Small Cap 6).

Another “table turning” theme to be aware of is the recent outperformance of Software vs. Semis. Semis, led by NVDA, AVGO, and friends, were the “meat and potatoes” of this Bull Market for the last couple years. This trade flipped in November, though, with Software outperforming Semis (proxied by the JPPQSFSM Index) by +19.1%. Since September 1st, the IGV (Software ETF) is +20.7% while the SOXX (Semi ETF) is -6.8%. After such an extended period of Semi outperformance over Software, I do not believe this “table turning” trade is over. I am expecting Software to continue to outperform into year end, and potentially beyond.

Stronger Software earnings this week would catalyze this trade even more. We have a number of big Software names reporting this week, headlined by ZS, CRM, OKTA, S, PATH, DOCU, GTLB, IOT, VEEV, and ASAN.

Elsewhere, we have now entered the 2024 Holiday Shopping Season. Mastercard estimates that US retail sales are off to a strong start (in store and online), up +3.4% YoY on 2024 Black Friday. Online retail sales increased +14.6% YoY (an acceleration from +8.5% YoY in 2023), while in-store sales were up a more modest +0.7% YoY (vs +1.1% YoY in 2023).

Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL) has seen continued growth so far this season. Adobe estimates that in 2024, BNPL will represent $18.5B spending, and +11% YoY increase from 2023. Overall, it appears we are pacing for a robust holiday season for consumer spending. The biggest beneficiaries appear to be the top e-commerce retailers (AMZN, SHOP, WMT, etc.), benefitting from the growth of online shopping, and BNPL companies (AFRM, SEZL, etc.).

Before getting into my Active Portfolio, a quick note on positioning among institutional investors. Despite sky high sentiment, institutional positioning is NOT currently a headwind for the market heading into December. The fact that we have not seen institutional investors sharply chase the market higher since the election, and given positioning currently remains below YTD highs, I believe the grind higher into year end can continue.
