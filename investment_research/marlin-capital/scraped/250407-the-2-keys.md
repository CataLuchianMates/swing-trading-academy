# The 2 Keys

In this report, I’ll unpack the recent market turmoil, analyze the key drivers behind last week’s equity collapse, and share my views moving forward.

[Early on](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/panic-selling?utm_source=publication-search), I talked about the 2 main factors I would be focusing on to call a big picture market bottom:

1. ) Capitulation Selling Signals; and

2.) Earnings Revisions.

**First Signs of Capitulation and Panic**

[On Friday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/liquidation-day), I talked about how the data fell short of capitulation/widespread panic selling (i.e, when everyone is panicking and heading for the exits all at once, not just hedge funds). To Friday, we had not yet seen that type of panicky behavior from retail investors. In fact, we had actually seen the opposite, as retail bought the dip at a record pace last Thursday .

That finally changed on Friday. We just saw a capitulatory day from retail (and basically the whole market), with the highest total # of shares traded across all exchanges ever.

We have now begun to see some clear signs of capitulation beginning to take place in the market.

Let’s start with Hedge Funds. [I have been covering](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off) their record de-risking/de-leveraging since late February.

The month of March saw record selling from Hedge Funds as they prepared for “Liberation Day”. Not only has that continued into April, it has actually accelerated (remarkably). This Hedge Fund selling hit a fever pitch last week.

Thursday marked their largest single day of net selling ever.

For the full week, we saw record weekly shorting (ETFs) AND individual stock liquidations from Hedge Funds.

This is full on Hedge Fund capitulation.

Retail investors, who had been buying the dip all year (including Thursday’s post-Liberation Day meltdown), finally flipped to heavy sellers on Friday.

From JPM - “Retail traders are finally terrified. As of noon today, they net sold -$1.5B, the largest level for this time of the day on record. Nearly the entire amount come from single stocks (-$1.6B) and are distributed across several names, with TSLA continuing leading the way (-$160Mn), followed by AAPL (-$110Mn).”

This was not the "split screen" dynamic I talked about [on Friday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/liquidation-day). This was a unified panic with everyone heading for the exits all at once. And **this**, is how I define “capitulation selling”.

Putting it all together, Goldman’s total market positioning indicator (institutional + retail + foreign) has now tanked to -2.5 Z score (at the end of Friday).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5xL_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4402a6a9-7297-4c00-aad4-8f92f27d7faa_1284x1182.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5xL_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4402a6a9-7297-4c00-aad4-8f92f27d7faa_1284x1182.jpeg)

Goldman’s Positioning Indicator combines 9 measures of positioning across institutional, retail, and foreign investors and has historically been a statistically significant signal for S&P 500 returns.

Take a moment to notice how quick this positioning unwind has occurred. We have gone from ATH long positioning to severely underweight in ~2 months. This is how you get the type of crashy market environment we have seen.

This is the exact type of total capitulatory unwind I have been waiting for. We are basically at December 2018 lows now and within total positioning ranges of several other Bear Market bottoms. Could positioning still go lower? Of course. But for me, this data now indicates that we have finally reached extreme fear/panic.

The technicals confirm the panic. The VIX, also known as Wall Street’s Fear Gauge, spiked to 45 last week (its highest since the 2020 Covid Pandemic). Last week was nothing short of historic with the 2nd highest weekly VIX spike since the 2008 GFC (only behind the March 2020 Covid Crash).

In [“Big Picture, Small Picture”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/big-picture-small-picture), I talked about how we would likely be starting the 2nd wave down in the selloff soon (the “the double dip” phase after the initial market rebound attempt fails). The market has now dropped -12% on this 2nd wave down so far, already exceeding the typical 9-10% average for such moves (though within the 3-15% range). Looking at similar episodes, the current -12% decline in “Wave 2” puts us close to the lowest we saw in other periods.

**The Earnings Risk**

While capitulation selling has begun to arrive, my 2nd main factor (Earnings Revisions), remains a **huge** risk.

The S&P 500’s 12M Forward P/E ratio currently sits at 18.6x, well above historical Bear Market bottoms. In fact, looking at the last 10 times the S&P 500 was down this much (-17.5% +), the highest SPX forward P/E for a bottom was 16x in 1998. The current P/E (18.6x) is still 14% above the priciest P/E bottom, and 30% above the median P/E bottom.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!eNnV!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd55352dd-a089-439d-88c0-4da4a1fe8157_672x410.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!eNnV!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd55352dd-a089-439d-88c0-4da4a1fe8157_672x410.png)

This also assumes earnings hold steady, of which I am very skeptical. Live Betting Market Polymarket now pegs US 2025 recession odds at 67%, a new cycle high.

In recessions, earnings typically fall somewhere in the ~13% minimum range.

Yet, current forecasts show only a -2% revision from highs (and a projected +10% YoY growth rate for EPS in 2025).

Again, [on Friday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/liquidation-day) I talked about how I believe the post-Liberation Day equity collapse is the market rapidly re-pricing and getting ahead of future EPS cuts. Yet even after the 2 day market crash to close last week, the below “EPS vs P/E matrix” from BofA shows that deep cuts are **not** priced in.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DOLk!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc9060df6-058d-480a-9d4d-cc753147b50c_446x271.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DOLk!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc9060df6-058d-480a-9d4d-cc753147b50c_446x271.png)

The consensus 12M fwd EPS estimate is just below $270 right now, which is calling for +10% EPS growth in 2025.

Now, let’s say EPS estimates get revised down to $250 (a -7.5% negative revision), which, by the way, would still signify positive EPS growth in 2025 (of +3%). At $250 EPS, even an 18x P/E would take the SPX to $4500. As I mentioned above, though, a Bear Market has never actually bottomed above a 16x P/E (which would take the SPX to $4000).

Now, imagine we actually get a recession and earnings recession in 2025 instead. $230 EPS would be a minor earnings recession (-5.3% YoY). At $230 EPS and a 16x P/E, we are looking at a sub-$4000 bottom for the SPX.

**The Bottom Line**

I’ve emphasized 2 key factors for calling a potential market bottom: capitulation selling and earnings revisions. The first is here. We now have our first clear signs of capitulation and panic in the market.

The second remains uncertain. From a fundamental perspective, I simply can not say that stocks haven’t fully discounted the worst. Bear markets have never bottomed at 18.6x forward earnings, and I suspect the "E" in P/E will need to adjust downward as well (so we are actually likely above 18.6x P/E).

While the technical setup hints at opportunity, the risk of earnings deterioration keeps me from calling lows. As I said Friday, I believe we are likely to reach official Bear Market territory (-20% +) in the S&P 500 ($4918), possibly coming tomorrow. Based off my analysis, my $4600 price target is looking more and more likely.

While I believe deteriorating fundamentals could push the market lower, the technical setup (capitulation, VIX spike, positioning unwind) suggests we are nearing a point where buying makes sense for the long haul. History shows that long term investors should buy during bear markets, especially amid capitulation selling (45+ VIX, -2.5 Z Score positioning, etc.). You may not nail the exact bottom, but this is why layering in progressively makes sense, especially in long term accounts like IRAs.

Stay disciplined and stick to your plan. I will be continuing to monitor by bottoming signals to help guide me in calling the ultimate market bottom.
