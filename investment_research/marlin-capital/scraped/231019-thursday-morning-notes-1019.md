# Thursday Morning Notes 10.19

Daily Market Commentary

US stocks sold off on Wednesday with the S&P 500 finishing the day -1.34%.  Over the last week, the SPX has been rejected multiple times at 60 EMA resistance ($4380).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WwFo!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7df6a619-9779-4d66-88f9-40f45f6ea905_953x715.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WwFo!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7df6a619-9779-4d66-88f9-40f45f6ea905_953x715.jpeg)

With yesterday's move, the SPX is now firmly below its 21 and 60 EMAs.

The tech led QQQ has been the stronger index, but was unable to escape yesterday's selling.  The QQQ finished the day -1.31% and broke below its 21 and 60 EMAs.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wE7f!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffe8f7220-f0bd-42bd-aafc-e81ac5dd723e_950x709.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wE7f!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffe8f7220-f0bd-42bd-aafc-e81ac5dd723e_950x709.jpeg)

The main focus yesterday was the continued higher for longer rates narrative as 10 YR yields charged towards 5% (touched highest level since 2007 yesterday) and the 30 YR was as high as 5.02% intraday.  The issue for stocks is that yields continue to move higher after a string of firmer than expected economic data (CPI last week, retail sales this week).  December rate hike probability has moved up accordingly to ~40% from <16% just 8 days ago.

We saw some short covering propel the market higher over the last couple weeks, but that bid was nowhere to be found yesterday (high short interest -3.5%, recent IPOs -3.7%, crypto exposure -4.2%, high retail activity -4.5%).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K1ew!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F69d3b11a-809c-4d00-bf9b-3e4fb6ad7e87_1217x2156.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K1ew!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F69d3b11a-809c-4d00-bf9b-3e4fb6ad7e87_1217x2156.jpeg)

Earnings have become the focus now with TSLA and NFLX reporting results after the bell yesterday.

TSLA – Q3 EPS of $0.66 vs consensus $0.74.  Total gross margins also came in slightly lighter than expected (17.9% vs 18% expected).  Investors were worried coming into this report with an increase in TSLA shorts over the last few weeks.  The stock is set for a gap down this morning following these results.

NFLX – The stock is set for a gap up this morning after reporting a big subscriber beat.  Revenues came in line at $8.54B vs consensus $8.53B.  Much better Q3 print on net adds +8.76M vs cons 6.2M.  For Q4, NFLX guided for $8.69B vs cons $8.76B & EPS $2.15 vs cons $2.17.  NFLX is also reporting boosting some prices in the US, UK, and France, which the market likes.
