# Good Bye Inflation

**Daily Market Commentary:**

US stocks are in rally mode so far this morning after CPI came in lighter than expected for February:

CPI (MoM): 0.2% vs 0.3% est.

CPI (YoY): 2.8% vs 2.9% est.

Core CPI (MoM): 0.2% vs 0.3% est.

Core CPI (YoY): 3.1% vs 3.2% est.

As I talked about on Monday, a technical oversold bounce was very much imminent after the QQQ spent 11 straight days outside of the lower Bollinger Band (usually the 8th or 9th day outside is where I look for an oversold bounce).

While lighter CPI was the catalyst, this does not change anything from a Macro standpoint. It is also not surprising to anyone as the Trump administration’s current policies are designed to stifle inflation (at the cost of economic growth?). The Truflation Index is currently at its lowest level in 4+ years (1.35%).

I believe most everyone is expecting inflation to fall off a cliff here. The big question is why. Is it due to a looming recession and slowing/negative economic growth? Or did the Trump administration just successfully solve inflation without damaging economic growth?

If it is due to a looming recession, history says we are still pretty early in this sell off. Below is a chart from 2022 showing the duration -10% + corrections in the S&P 500 by # of trading days (ignore the “current”, this chart is from 2022. The current 2025 selloff is 21 days long).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-jND!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F79a78dc2-c1d6-4285-af50-b38e4f008a8f_877x464.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-jND!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F79a78dc2-c1d6-4285-af50-b38e4f008a8f_877x464.jpeg)

As you can see, the median non-recessionary SPX correction lasted 60 days. The median recessionary market correction lasted 282 days, almost 5x as long (in duration).

Over the weekend, President Trump declined to rule out a recession and dismissed the recent market drop during an interview on Fox Business, warning that it will take a little time before his policies begin benefitting the economy. Some have theorized that a recession is his goal (to reset inflation and interest rates). Again, if that is the case, history says we are in the early innings of this market selloff.

The other main factor at play during this market selloff is the positioning unwind I have talked about in detail. Hedge Funds have been unloading for the last month +, and that has continued over the last couple trading days. In fact, it has actually accelerated.

Over the last couple trading days, Hedge Funds de-grossed at their fastest pace in 4+ years (since the 2020 Covid Pandemic).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YrQe!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8c7c4a36-9a1d-4167-9ded-d26e8a703504_847x591.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YrQe!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8c7c4a36-9a1d-4167-9ded-d26e8a703504_847x591.jpeg)

You would think after the last month + of Hedge Fund unwinds, that we are almost done there. However, Goldman Prime put out a note yesterday estimating that we are still in the middle innings of this de-grossing episode because gross leverage remains elevated versus history. This is a dynamic that will continue to weigh on the market, particularly during technical bounces like today (funds will use bounces to de-leverage).
