# Bottomed Out?

The US Stock Market staged a historic rally today with the S&P 500 finishing +9.52%, its best day since Oct 2008. The Nasdaq added > $2T in market cap, finishing the day +12%.

Today marked the highest volume day on record, eclipsing 30B total shares traded and breaking Monday’s record of 29B (which broke Friday’s record). The top 3 highest volume days in stock market history have all come within the last 4 trading days.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JAy3!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F504380d6-ffd7-4ebd-9efa-0720f40b7bd7_1209x597.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JAy3!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F504380d6-ffd7-4ebd-9efa-0720f40b7bd7_1209x597.jpeg)

The big news: President Trump paused the higher reciprocal tariff rates for 90 days on all countries aside from China (the cumulative tariff on China is being increased to 125%, effective immediately). The 10% blanket tariff remains in effect for everyone.

This is exactly what Bulls have been hoping for. On Monday, rumors of a 90 day tariff pause (turned out to be fake news at the time) ignited a massive market rally and reversal off Monday’s big gap down. While the headline ended up being a couple days premature, it offered a preview of the type of squeeze positive trade news could unleash. At [the time](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/whipsaw), I wrote:

> “Following the “90 day pause” fake headline yesterday morning, we saw a flurry of short covering in Macro products (Index + ETFs), a possible sign of how squeezy things could get if we ever start to get some real positive headlines. At one point yesterday, ETFs were 42% of the tape (finished 39%) vs the YTD avg of 28%.”

Fast forward to today, and we got the real headline: an official 90 day tariff pause. In response, the S&P 500 delivered its best single day performance since Oct 2008 on a potent mix of short covering and renewed economic optimism.

**Does this mean the Bear Market bottom is in?**

As Marlin Capital readers know, I have been tracking 2 critical indicators to call a market bottom:

(1) Capitulation signals; and

(2) Earnings

In ["The 2 Keys”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-2-keys), I talked about how we were seeing some classic signs of capitulation selling, as evidenced by -2.5z total positioning, the VIX spike above 45, biggest 2-day SPX market crash since 2020, etc.

We saw record de-leveraging AND short selling from Hedge Funds last week, (as well as a capitulatory day from retail on Friday).

This depressed/overly bearish positioning (-2.5z) ended up setting the stage for today’s explosive squeeze. When the news of the 90 day tariff pause hit the tape, we saw huge algo buying and shorts scrambling to cover, amplifying the ferocity of today’s snapback rally.

With today’s massive market rally, the VIX just had its biggest 1-day decline ever (-36.4%), as shown below:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!e2jR!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff56262c5-1669-4924-b0fb-ea034bd0827f_1238x292.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!e2jR!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff56262c5-1669-4924-b0fb-ea034bd0827f_1238x292.png)

As you can see, 4 out of the other 6 highest 1-day VIX declines were associated with a local peak in the volatility index. Returns were generally mixed following these episodes on a shorter time frame. However, 3+ months later, the market typically outperformed (with the 2008 systemic Bear Market/GFC being the clear outlier). Outside of the GFC, there was generally not much more SPX downside following 5 of the other 6 largest 1-day declines in the VIX.

In my view, it is hard to see the VIX spiking back above Monday’s highs after today’s “resolution” on trade. It is likely, in my opinion, that we have reached peak trade uncertainty. Overall, my capitulation box for calling a market bottom has already been ticked.

**Earnings Relief**

Now for earnings, my other key.

[Yesterday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/recession-roulette), I talked about how EPS estimates for 2025 had been falling towards flattish-down YoY, largely due to tariff uncertainty. The risk was that we could have a potential earnings recession alongside an economic recession (the latter becoming consensus). JPM, Nomura, and Goldman had baked a recession into their base cases. Live betting market Polymarket pegged the odds of a recession in 2025 at 67% over the past couple days.

Today’s news flipped the script. Goldman walked back their recession call 73 minutes after making it their baseline case, citing the tariff pause as a game changer.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ywjD!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1b29bc45-1b5a-4ce5-9473-818c21b373a6_1236x1064.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ywjD!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1b29bc45-1b5a-4ce5-9473-818c21b373a6_1236x1064.jpeg)

Polymarket recession odds have now plummeted from a cycle high of 67% to now 49%, which reflects this rapid shift in expectations.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FVGm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F706196f5-32a7-47ba-acc8-7da0152e0de1_1284x1376.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FVGm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F706196f5-32a7-47ba-acc8-7da0152e0de1_1284x1376.jpeg)

The equity market reacted accordingly as well. I have talked about how in my view, following Liberation Day, stocks were in the process of being re-priced for a recession. Following today’s tariff pause news, the market basically priced that recession back out today, with the SPX finishing just below pre-Liberation Day lows.

In my view, a trade war confined to China will not tank the US economy like a full blown global trade war would have. We saw a US-China Trade War during Trump’s 1st term, and stocks were essentially in a Bull Market throughout (except for Dec ‘18 and March ‘20). The economy performed well throughout (outside of the Covid shutdown).

Yes, the current tariff rate on China is now up to 125%. However, I do not see this rate sticking (and neither does the market, in my estimation). Every major economy has secured some form of tariff relief except China. It is now the World vs China, and that isolation gives the US some leverage to use. I believe China will be more willing to come to the negotiating table following today’s news. My guess is a 10-30% tariff rate on China will end up sticking, which would be a manageable hit to EPS.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!G7PF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1bcb933a-6c16-40e2-9952-792c8ce1ba9e_707x498.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!G7PF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1bcb933a-6c16-40e2-9952-792c8ce1ba9e_707x498.jpeg)

Depending on implementation, we would end up shaving $2.60 to $7.80 off EPS. Again, this is a very manageable number versus the $220 in Recessionary Scenario Earnings risk I highlighted [last night](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/recession-roulette). That nightmare scenario is now off the table, and it is a massive relief to the market.

**Is the Bottom In? My Take**

Today’s news changes everything for me.

As I talked about, I believe the market was in the process of pricing in a recession and potential earnings collapse following the -21% S&P 500 drop.

That changed today, and I do not see stocks necessarily needing to revisit Monday’s peak trade uncertainty lows. I also do not see completely washed out positioning needing to go any lower, as todays news brings some stability for traders.

In my view, we are now past the point of peak trade policy uncertainty.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_YIY!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F26cb6dfd-963f-40ce-b5be-dae6e4aab369_887x582.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_YIY!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F26cb6dfd-963f-40ce-b5be-dae6e4aab369_887x582.png)

We are now past the point of peak FCI tightening.

We are now past the point of max fear around tariffs.

We are now past the point of peak recession/earnings recession fears (at least, for now).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ACbl!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F31d2791a-4814-48a9-9e1c-f441b5b8d42f_1284x1376.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ACbl!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F31d2791a-4814-48a9-9e1c-f441b5b8d42f_1284x1376.jpeg)

And, we are now past the point of max Hedge Fund shorts (after today’s squeeze), (and very possibly hit a capitulatory lows on total positioning (-2.5z)).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kUpq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F012483bd-2e10-4476-b74e-5d465c8be453_1284x1182.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kUpq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F012483bd-2e10-4476-b74e-5d465c8be453_1284x1182.jpeg)

I believe the VIX has likely topped (with its 2nd biggest 1-day decline ever today), while positioning has very possibly bottomed (following today’s repositioning rally), which would make new market lows unlikely.

With that said, I am still anticipating a very volatile earnings season. We could see a flurry of pulled guidance or conservative outlooks as management teams recalibrate.

Still, my view is that the big picture changed today. The market no longer needs to price in a doomsday recession/earnings decline scenario, which is the way things appeared headed. I no longer see us breaking below Monday’s lows, because I saw this as more of an Event Driven Bear Market than a Systemic Bear Market.

Does that mean we will blast off to new all-time highs? No. I expect some eventual backfilling of today’s rally, like we typically saw in the 1-2 weeks following the other 6 largest 1-day VIX declines. But, the foundation feels much firmer now, and we can all breathe a little sigh of relief.
