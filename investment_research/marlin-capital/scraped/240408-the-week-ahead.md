# The Week Ahead

The S&P 500 and Nasdaq both fell around -1% last week while 10-YR Yields popped 20bps.  Rate cuts projections were slightly reduced and the likely start time for cuts was pushed back a touch.

The Nasdaq was briefly down -3% from its intraday high last week, the largest pullback we have seen during this run.  This raises the question: is this just a shakeout during a steady climb higher, or is this a signal of a more significant shift in the market?

We saw some volatility/fear finally hit the market last week as the VIX (Volatility Index/Fear Gauge) saw its largest weekly surge since August of 2023.

In my view, a few key factors contributed to the recent pullback and surge in market volatility.

1. Mid-April Tax Selling

The approaching Tax Day (April 15th) often prompts investors to engage in strategic selling to cover their tax bills.  Historically, this seasonal trend has created (temporary) downward pressure on the market in mid-April.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WV8g!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F156412c6-7418-4022-9ab2-8e94711723a6_962x751.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WV8g!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F156412c6-7418-4022-9ab2-8e94711723a6_962x751.jpeg)

2. Corporate Buyback Blackout Window

Most S&P 500 companies are currently in their "blackout period" on their share buyback programs.  This pause in buyback activity has reduced a key source of market demand/support (see last week’s chart on corporate buyback contributions to returns).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HoD8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2a2bed64-65b1-4c08-af8e-6a0dd1add332_964x418.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HoD8!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2a2bed64-65b1-4c08-af8e-6a0dd1add332_964x418.jpeg)

 3. A Hotter Economy than Expected

Last week’s jobs number shows that US economic data is still largely surprising to the upside.  This has not been a positive development for a market that had 6 rate cuts penciled in at the start of 2024 (now less than ~3).  Last week, Fed Speaker Neel Kashkari (non-voter) floated the possibility of 0 rate cuts in 2024.

The major market indices bounced back on Friday and closed the week on a bit of a high note (despite falling overall for the week).

Technically, Friday’s bounce back in the indices qualified as an inside day for the SPX and QQQ.

As you can see in the chart above, this is nothing more than rangebound/choppy action for the QQQ.  There is no reason to overreact to any movements within this range, as a larger move cannot come until we see a directional break from this range.  This is also the type of market (choppy/rangebound) where I look to reduce my trading activity, which I have been doing for the last few weeks.

Energy stocks took center stage last week amidst a backdrop of rising oil prices (this may be my least favorite sector to trade historically).  Energy was the only sector to end green (while healthcare and real estate were the biggest laggards).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0_ep!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb9e82c5c-9c63-4d24-b617-64c8a4332658_1052x645.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0_ep!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb9e82c5c-9c63-4d24-b617-64c8a4332658_1052x645.jpeg)

Meanwhile, MU and DELL took the lead in last week’s AI trade, showcasing great relative strength while NVDA (SMCI, AMD, etc.) each experienced a small pullback.

MU is my only current long as we head into the week of 4/8.  I am holding 2/3rd of my original position after taking profits at my $130 target.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BZPh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F342d2c65-a8dc-413e-988c-df03b682396e_974x718.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BZPh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F342d2c65-a8dc-413e-988c-df03b682396e_974x718.jpeg)

Technically, MU continues to look strong here despite the general market pressure we saw last week.  I have moved my stop loss on my remaining position to $119 and plan to let the rest of this trade play out.  Market willing, my bigger picture target for MU remains $150.

As I wrote Friday, I believe caution is warranted here, but it is also too early to get bearish based on the technicals/trend.  We are currently in a 6+ week trading range in the QQQ (highlighted above).  $436 and $433-$434 are the key support levels to watch as we head into this week.  A decisive drop below both of these support levels would signify a bearish break of the range and would likely lead to a larger correction.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0zMD!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9d857e1a-8877-4d51-9818-83fc17b83d90_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0zMD!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9d857e1a-8877-4d51-9818-83fc17b83d90_1396x653.png)

On the other hand, a break above key resistance ($443 and $446-$447) would signify a bullish break of the rangebound market we have experienced for the last 6 weeks, and would likely lead to another leg higher in the indices.

My trading activity level will remain low as long as the QQQ stays rangebound.  With that said, I will be fully ready to capitalize on the breakout/breakdown when that time comes.
