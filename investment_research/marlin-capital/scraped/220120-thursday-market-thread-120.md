Active Portfolio:

Short ARKK (60% of original position)

---

As Marlin Capital subscribers know, I have been very patient throughout this brutal sell off in growth stocks. I have held off on new buys, because I continued to see more downside.

Yesterday, the Nasdaq closed in correction territory, about 11% off ATHs. This is what I have been waiting for, as I believe this sell off most likely ends with a 10-20% correction in the indices. For the time being, the S&P 500 is lagging the Nasdaq, down about 6% off ATHs. I expect the correction to run deeper in the Nasdaq, but ultimately, I believe the SPY will also reach correction territory before we bottom.

I have outlined my strategy to buy in 3 tranches below:

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Qs1-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4950d1ae-a68a-4d69-a2c3-941f6fbe0d3b_1100x668.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Qs1-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4950d1ae-a68a-4d69-a2c3-941f6fbe0d3b_1100x668.png)

The Nasdaq is set to gap up this morning, which is not surprising given the amount of oversold indicators flashing. However, I do not see any bounce lasting beyond 1-2 days before we resume our current downtrend. Violent bounces are a hallmark of Bear Markets, which makes trading the short side so much more difficult.

I will continue to be patient, and wait for my spots outlined above. As I have mentioned, I believe all these prices will be seen at some point in 2022, so I am in 0 rush and have 0 FOMO about missing some type of major V-shaped bottom. The technical damage to a number of stocks (i.e UPST, ROKU, etc.) is so extensive it can only be compared to the Dot Com bust. Bottoming after such a massive decline is a long process that normally starts with a long basing period. The idea of growth stocks forming a V shaped bottom and catapulting back to old highs, as the Fed aggressively raises rates, is a fantasy scenario. Reality is, ARKK and many growth stocks will take years to fully recover from this crash, and some will surely never see their old highs again.
