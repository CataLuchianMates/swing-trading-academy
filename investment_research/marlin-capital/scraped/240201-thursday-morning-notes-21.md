# Thursday Morning Notes 2.1

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The month of January closed out with the S&P 500 having its worst 1 day move (-1.61%) since September 21st (-1.64%) after Jerome Powell rose the bar for a March cut during his FOMC press conference last afternoon.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6f4r!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3d524de5-e71b-4eee-b149-94e6324c67ac_1033x767.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6f4r!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3d524de5-e71b-4eee-b149-94e6324c67ac_1033x767.jpeg)

Technically, there is still not much damage yet after yesterday's sell off.  We have broken and closed below the 8 EMA, but as you can see in the chart above, the 21 EMA has acted as support (twice since December) throughout this rally.  This is the most important key support level to watch today ($4800-$4820).

Fed Chair Powell stated during the presser “(I) don’t think it’s likely Fed will cut in March”.  US stocks sold off into the close and ended the day at lows after Powell’s presser.

Markets were already off to a rocky start to the session with tech under pressure as tech heavy weights MSFT, GOOGL, and AMD failed to impress lofty investor expectations on their earnings prints (mag7 took a 3 sigma trip lower, finishing -3.1%).  Things were also ugly in the land of regional banks with NYCB -40% weighing on a guide cut + dividend cut.

We have another slew of earnings this afternoon (AAPL, AMZN, META) and NFP tomorrow.

Potential earnings gapper trades to watch this morning – NXT, ALGN
