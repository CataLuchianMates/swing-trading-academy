# Tuesday Morning Notes 12.12

**Daily Market Commentary:**

A quiet start to what will be the last big week of Macro in 2023 (CPI, FOMC Weds).  The S&P 500 finished +0.39% on the day with the big underperformer being Mega Cap Tech.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OitX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbefe79d5-d6e5-4905-90ce-ea2910a7f606_1210x887.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OitX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbefe79d5-d6e5-4905-90ce-ea2910a7f606_1210x887.jpeg)

Despite the NDX finishing +85bps on the day, the Magnificent 7 closed -144bps.  Breadth across the SPX was robust with 390 names closing up on the day and only 109 closing down.  The SPX equal weight outperformed by +55bps.

Mega Cap Tech underperformance yesterday felt like a continuation of last week’s flow picture with capital being deployed down the quality curve into year end with Growth, Software, Crypto, etc. being the biggest beneficiaries.  There was a >3% spread yesterday between the Profitable Secular Growth Basket and the Mega Cap Tech basket.  Semis strength was also a big standout yesterday with big days from AVGO +9%, MU +4%, AMAT +5%.

Small Caps (TNA) finished the day +0.53% and appear to be setting up constructively for a continuation move higher.  I am holding 1/3rd of my TNA long (from $28.75) with a stop on the final portion on a close below $30.

I entered PATH long on yesterday’s gap down (at $23.93) with a closing stop below $22.50.  Yesterday was an inside day for PATH, giving us key levels to watch today.  A move above $24.66 (yesterday’s highs) is bullish and could be a spot to add additional long exposure in the name.
