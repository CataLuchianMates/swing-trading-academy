# The Patience Game

Stocks finished sharply lower yesterday (SPX -2.24%, NDX -3.04%, RTY -0.9%) on a combination of:

(1) Semis under pressure (NVDA finished -7% on new export curbs to China, which the company said will lead to a $5.5B write-down + ASML -5% on an earnings miss and lighter guide); and

(2) Powell’s hawkish afternoon commentary, with his lasting remark rattling markets: “For the time being, we are well positioned to wait for greater clarity before considering any adjustment to our policy stance.” Translation - don’t expect any rate cuts until the data deteriorates.

This should come as no surprise to Marlin Capital readers, as the Fed is data dependent and they do not cut rates preemptively. I wrote about this on Monday in [“Choppy Waters”:](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/choppy-waters)

> “The Fed can only cut rates once the hard data allows them to, and we are not at that point yet.”

The selling was orderly in the morning, but accelerated during Powell’s hawkish speech. Despite the sharp move lower, we saw no real pickup in volumes. SPX volumes have dryed up significantly, with yesterday finishing -38% vs the 10d average.

Mega Caps continue to stand out as underperformers, as increased cyclical concerns seem to be weighing on AAPL/AMZN/META in particular. We have not observed these stocks act defensively at all throughout this drawdown. In fact, the relative valuation of Big Tech vs. the S&P 500 is now at 10 year lows:

We are also seeing the health of the consumer deteriorate, by some metrics. In March, the number of Americans planning a vacation in the next 6 months was the lowest in 15 years (excluding Covid).

This tells me consumers are bracing for an economic downturn, which can often becoming self-fulfilling. Polymarket recession odds stand at 56% as of this morning.

The US Stock Market is closed tomorrow (4/18) for Good Friday. That means today is monthly OpEx. We have over $2.6 trillion of notional options exposure expiring today, including $1.2T of SPX options and $480B of notional single stock options.

Big picture, my views remain the same here. This is still a cash market for me. It is important to be patient/selective during these types of market environments. This is how I have played it, and how I will continue to play it until conditions materially improve.

I have placed 2 trades since the beginning of March:

* SPXL long for a +2.7% gain
* APP short for a +21% gain

Over that time, the S&P 500 is down over -11%.

As my readers know, I do not place trades simply for the sake of placing trades. I only place trades when I see a favorable risk/reward setup, high probability of success, and a conducive environment.

I will leave everyone with this quote by 1 of the legends of the trading game, Mark Minervini:

> “In the stock market, you have the luxury of being able to stay on the sidelines, free of charge, observing and waiting for the most opportune moment to wager. You get to see the market’s “cards” before you bet, free of charge. This is a wonderful advantage, yet few exploit it.
>
> While you do nothing, less skilled opponents are laying the groundwork for your success, and you get to wait and watch for free.
>
> What a deal!”
>
> -Mark Minvervini, “Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard”
