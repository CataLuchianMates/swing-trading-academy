# The Worst Third

The stock market took a sharp dive last week following the Federal Reserve’s decision to hike interest rates by 75 bps.  At his press conference, Fed Chair Jerome Powell signaled 2 main things

(1) Monetary policy will remain restrictive for a prolonged period of time (at least until inflation is fully under control).

 (2) A soft landing appears increasingly unlikely for the economy. The FOMC does not expect to be able to avoid a recession.

These two points are anything but new revelations for Marlin Capital subscribers, but the market has continued to lag behind in Fed funds futures pricing (and, reality).  Now, the moves in the the fixed income market are starting to signal a more severe recession is on the horizon.  The US 2/10 YR Treasury Yield curve hit its deepest inversion in 4 decades (1981) following the Fed meeting.  Historically, this has been the most accurate indicator of a recession, as I have [discussed before.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/back-to-reality)  The 2/10 YR yield curve inversion has preceded each of the last 6 US recessions dating back to 1980.

The US 2 YR yield, [which typically peaks before the stock market bottoms](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-market-bottom-checklist), is now at its highest level since August 2007.

Each of the above factors should continue to act as major headwinds for equities and valuations.  Speaking of valuations, higher interest rates typically lead to lower equity multiples.  The move in the Real Rates is signaling that there is more multiple compression is ahead for the S&P 500 (specifically, a sub-14 P/E target, a multiple that has not been seen since March 2020 Covid lows).

So, what does it all mean for stocks?

In my view, the S&P 500 and Nasdaq have each begun their 3rd waves down, as demonstrated in my chart below.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!RPP8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F107ee3a4-dd0a-4830-8f3a-d2b64750ca69_1031x649.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!RPP8!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F107ee3a4-dd0a-4830-8f3a-d2b64750ca69_1031x649.png)

Historically, Bear Markets tend to be back-end loaded, with the largest declines coming in the last third (of time duration).  The final third has been the largest decline in 11 out of 16 Bear Markets since 1929.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-LKm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0584bb1-0b3b-4cb5-bf4b-c4ebe4b8f1d9_1210x1064.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-LKm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb0584bb1-0b3b-4cb5-bf4b-c4ebe4b8f1d9_1210x1064.png)

In our 1st wave down, the QQQ declined -22.4%.  In the 2nd wave down, the QQQ declined -27.5%.  Currently, the QQQ has declined -18.7% in the 3rd wave so far.  A -30% decline, in line with the pattern described above, would take the QQQ to $234 (a -15% drop from current levels).  A 40% Wave 3 decline would take us to $200 (a -27% drop from current levels).

My target is somewhere in between those levels.  In my view, the decline in the QQQ 3rd Wave down will take us to the $210-$230 region.  For the S&P 500, my target on this 3rd wave down remains $2900-$3300.  **Such declines would confirm full retraces of the post-Covid Bull Market**.  As I described in “[History Repeating](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/history-repeating)” in my historical analysis of past asset bubbles, a full retrace has always occurred in the “Bust Phase” of the Bubble.

Of course, this does not mean the S&P 500 is heading straight down to $3000.  Bounces will continue to occur, but they will likely be shorter in nature.  Dead Cat Bounces during Wave 1 Down and Wave 2 Down typically lasted 1-4 days, as the bounces tend to be very short in duration when the market is in selling/liquidation mode.  When the market is in selling/liquidation mode, the counter-trend bounces will be even more violent because the VIX is elevated.

The VIX is now near the 30 level, indicating heightened fear, uncertainty, and risk in the market.  A VIX of ~30 implied a ~2% daily move in either direction, as more volatility leads to a larger magnitude of moves.

As I talked about in “[Bear Market Bottom Checklist](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bear-market-bottom-checklist)”, a VIX spike above 45 is one of the technical indicators I use to assess capitulation, and has historically occurred before major each Bear Market bottom throughout history.

---

**Summary**

* The moves in the Fixed Income market are a major concern, as they are signaling a more severe recession is on the horizon.
* S&P 500 P/E valuations remain too expensive for where real rates stand, posing a major risk for further multiple compression.
* In my view, we are in the 3rd Wave Down of this Bear Market.  Historically, the final third is where the largest declines come.  I am targeting a 30-40% decline in the indices during this 3rd Wave Down, taking us to $2900-$3300 in the S&P 500 and $210-$230 in QQQ.
* Expect heightened volatility and larger daily moves during this wave.  Counter-trend bounces are likely to be quicker and even more violent.
