# Friday Morning Notes 5.17

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Quiet day for the market indices yesterday with the S&P 500 and NDX both closing -0.21%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Krss!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0d24e6c6-bdb8-4eac-b923-6deaf42e6b18_973x729.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Krss!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0d24e6c6-bdb8-4eac-b923-6deaf42e6b18_973x729.jpeg)

The biggest retailer in the world (WMT +7%) delivered solid results and made no concerning comments regarding the US consumer.  Consumer staples was the best performing sector yesterday  +148bps.

HF VIP Longs vs Most Short bounced back to close +253bps yesterday.  As of Tuesday, that pair trade was down as much as -12% with Meme Stock mania causing an aggressive squeeze.

The NDX finally closed red (-21bps) after 6 straight up days, matching the longest streak of the year.

A couple of my trade setups triggered long yesterday (JMIA, RMD).  VITL will be buyable today through the $37.86-$38 resistance level.  ONON (inside 15) and LSPD (inside 5) gave textbook day-1 earnings gapper setups with low risk this week.
