# Bigger Than the Internet

Marlin Capital Readers,

US equities just finished another week higher (SPX +0.5%, NDX +2.4%) as investors digest a strong start to Q1 earnings season alongside US/Iran negotiation headlines.

Here is the most important thing to know about the Iran conflict right now. 👇

**The market has moved on.**

For me, at the end of the day, it’s pretty simple. You have to be long stocks when stocks are going up to make money. The market has moved on, and the price action is unequivocally strong.

**And the #1 biggest reason the market has moved on, in my view, is the historic earnings boom we are seeing.**

To be clear, this massive earnings ramp is very much driven by one singular thing.

**The 2 most important letters in the alphabet right now.** 👇

AI.

Over the long term, it’s all about earnings. Stocks follow earnings. Always have, always will.

And right now, earnings are pointing to plenty more upside ahead for US stocks.

The only thing we can really compare the current AI buildout to is the Internet in the late 90s. But even the internet did not drive an earnings inflection like this one (during the dot com bubble).

**This is bigger.**

Earnings estimates are growing faster today than they did in the mid-90s or in the late internet bubble years. The only period in modern history that saw a greater earnings inflection was the Covid recovery period, and that was a one-time bounce off a global economic shutdown.

Don’t underestimate how powerful this catalyst is for the market and market sentiment: **AI is driving a historic boom in corporate earnings and productivity.**

#### The Rocky Short-Term Setup

At the risk of sounding bearish, there is one caveat I want to flag here.

We are getting extremely overbought after this massive lockout rally, particularly in Semis (the leaders).

The SOX has rallied for 18 consecutive sessions. That is the longest streak on record.

The SOX 14-day RSI is now at 85. Only 3 times in history has it been higher.

77% of SOX constituents are now overbought, the 2nd highest reading in the last 20 years. Only 5 other times have we seen >70%. After similar readings, short-term returns were generally muted (2-week avg -1.9%, 1-month avg +0.2%), but returns 5+ months out have been extremely positive.
