# It’s Systematic

US stocks finished ~70bps lower yesterday, finally snapping the longest S&P winning streak in 20+ years (9 days). It was a very quiet session with trading volumes tracking -32% vs 20d avg.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nuuS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc09bb59f-fac9-449b-96a2-a4d4eb7d1f8f_1191x882.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nuuS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc09bb59f-fac9-449b-96a2-a4d4eb7d1f8f_1191x882.jpeg)

On the data front, ISM Services was better than feared, but prices paid were the highest since February 2023. There were 13 mentions of tariffs in the April press released, compared to 13 in March and 5 in January.

US stocks are pulling back ~1% so far this morning. All eyes are on tomorrow’s Fed meeting now. The Fed is certainly a potential bearish catalyst for stocks, but if the market can avoid this landmine, I remain bullish here in the near future. Here’s why…

It all starts with Systematic buying.

Systematic Fund flows remain extremely favorable here. According to Nomura, US equities benefitted from $61B+ of mechanical/systematic flows last week. My research shows that this reallocation buying is still in the early stages and systematic demand will continue to be substantial moving forward.

This is certainly the most bullish force out there currently. As I’ve talked about in the past, the beauty of the Feb/March positioning washout is the tailwind their eventual re-risking can provide when momentum flips.

Overall, Systematic strategies (CTAs, Vol Control, and Risk Parity) are historically low on equities exposure (4th percentile vs history) from the brutal volatility shock in recent months.

Within the Systematic space, the next 1w+ is all about reallocation buying from Vol Control Funds (as we see large $ notional to buy due to a number of substantial 1d market moves dropping out of the 1m trailing rVol sample in the days and weeks ahead).

According to Nomura, this should mean +$30B+ of US equities to BUY over the course of the next week (almost regardless of the SPX daily chg magnitudes).

This projected 1w buying from Vol Control Funds is in the >95th percentile vs history. When modeled/projected 1w buying from Vol Control is this substantial (using >95th percentile vs history), S&P 500 forward returns, hit rates, and excess returns are all quite strong from 1W, 2M, and 1YR out.

Then we have CTAs, who are projected to be another large source of equity buying demand moving forward. Due to the magnitude of the volatility shocks over the past 1M+, overall CTA long exposure remains extremely low versus history (in the 22nd percentile).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!U5ul!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa34260be-3520-4f4b-bf0e-2b6733e501bb_633x430.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!U5ul!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa34260be-3520-4f4b-bf0e-2b6733e501bb_633x430.jpeg)

According to Goldman Prime, CTAs are modeled to buy in all scenarios over the next 1 week and next 1 month:

Over the next 1 week…

* Flat tape: Buyers $13.4B in US
* Up tape: Buyers $18.9B in US
* Down tape: Buyers $7.8B in US

Over the next 1 month…

* Flat tape: Buyers $18.4B in US
* Up tape: Buyers $37.3B in US
* Down tape: Buyers $1.2B in US

As we have now established, systematic buying demand is going to be a major tailwind for the market moving forward.

Discretionary institutional buying demand is also likely to be a tailwind for markets, in my view. Discretionary institutional exposure is historically low following the record pace of de-risking/de-leveraging we saw in February, March, and early April. I have covered this phenomenon in detail at Marlin Capital over the past several months.

In my view, discretionary Hedge Funds are done selling/shorting. They are now forced to chase the market higher or risk being locked out of this rally. We have already seen Hedge Funds “net up” marginally over the past several weeks as the market has rallied (a lot of which was done through short covering), but they remain extremely underweight US stocks here.

When you pair these 2 dynamics (systematic buying demand + potential “netting up” from discretionary funds), institutional buying demand is a very clear tailwind for the market moving forward.

Retail has provided a steady bid all year (other than 1-2 capitulation days around Liberation Day, similar to the March 2020 crash). There is no reason to expect retail to become sellers now after the market has recovered all its post-Liberation Day losses, in my view.

That just leaves foreign investors, who were big sellers in Q1. But similar to discretionary funds, they risk being underexposed to the U.S. if this rally continues. They have already de-risked substantially, and find themselves underweight US equities.

I would also add that as we push through the meat of Q1 earnings season, the share buyback window will open back up in the next ~2 weeks, adding more mechanical buying demand to the market. Many notable companies have increased their buybacks this quarter (NFLX by +$15B, Visa by +$30B, Citi by +$20B, etc.). In fact, the Q1 earnings season has already seen a surge in buyback announcements to new record highs.

Overall, I believe it will be difficult for sellers to build any meaningful momentum here without a notable catalyst. The good news for bears is that they have some potential catalysts in the near future, starting with tomorrow’s Fed meeting. It is very possible for an overly hawkish Powell to turn the tides of momentum.

**Final Thoughts**

Absent a negative catalyst, I remain bullish here due to outsized institutional buying demand moving forward (particularly systematic). During the -21% SPX decline, there was huge selling from institutions and foreign investors. That selling demand is now turning into buying demand, and has gone from a major headwind to major tailwind.
