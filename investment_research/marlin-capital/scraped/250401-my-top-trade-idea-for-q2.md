# My Top Trade Idea for Q2

The S&P 500 and QQQ briefly broke new correction lows yesterday before bouncing back +2-3% so far.

Technically, this mini bounce does nothing to change my view. We still have an h sell breakdown developing for each of the 3 major indices (SPX, QQQ, and IWM).

So far, this is pretty normal action on an h sell - to narrowly cut through lows with a false breakdown, then trap both shorts and longs. Typically, the 2nd breakdown is the ideal 1 to short if you’re interested in trading to the short side (or on this bounce).

In my experience, these h sell false breakdown bounces typically last 2-5 trading days. We can break down tomorrow, or this bounce can continue for a couple/few more days, but I am ultimately expecting this mini bounce to fail

Technically, the h sell breakdown points to $5221 for the SPX. My breakdown target is $5200 for this next wave down.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!15B1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0822e888-d5f3-455f-a272-bea5dcd2763b_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!15B1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0822e888-d5f3-455f-a272-bea5dcd2763b_1396x653.png)

As I talked about yesterday, $5200 would be a -15% peak to trough move for the S&P 500. The Russell 2000 already reached Bear Market territory at yesterday’s lows (-20.2%), while the Nasdaq reached -15.4% (and would likely reach Bear Market territory -20% + if the SPX declines to $5200).

In a non-recessionary scenario, it is certainly very possible the market begins to bottom around $5200, in my view.

That is why the key market debate is now “recession vs no recession”. Today, the Atlanta Fed just revised its Q1 GDP estimate even lower to -3.7%. This would be a pretty severe contraction and the worst since the heart of the pandemic (Q2 2020)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0deu!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff118236c-b5d5-42d0-ac48-9f2f221436df_1079x751.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0deu!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff118236c-b5d5-42d0-ac48-9f2f221436df_1079x751.jpeg)

If the Atlanta Fed’s GDP estimate proves accurate, I would definitely be looking for the S&P 500 to hit my recessionary price target of $4600.

As far as my take on “recession vs no recession”, I have been in the “no recession” camp since the start of the year. With that said, I believe the risks are becoming very elevated now. [Yesterday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/rough-waters-in-q1), I talked about Goldman assigning a 35% probability of a recession in the next 12 months. For me, it is closer to 50/50.

In the 2022 Bear Market, I was firmly in the recession camp for a myriad of different reasons I talked about throughout that year. We did have a technical recession back then. (Although a shallow one, I personally believe the release of ChatGPT (sparking the huge AI wave in big tech NVDA, MSFT, etc.), as well as the Fed’s intervention during the March 2023 regional banking crisis, saved what could have been a much deeper recession.)

Here in 2025, it is hard for me to get as good a read on the economy. This is because of the current uncertainty on Fiscal Policy from the Trump administration, which is basically at record highs right now.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vIgA!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8622c4b1-a56f-4f5a-9da7-a61c48fb12af_1079x708.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vIgA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8622c4b1-a56f-4f5a-9da7-a61c48fb12af_1079x708.jpeg)

For me, I look at the Trump tariffs as more of a negotiating tactic than a longstanding policy change, so it is hard for me to predict the medium-long term effects (without knowing how long they will be implemented for).

Some have theorized that the Trump admin is engineering a recession in order to get the Fed to cut rates. Such a move would reset the system with lower rates and lower inflation (with a goal of creating an economy less reliant on government spending/debt).

This theory is also a possibility, and one that must be recognized. If the Trump admin is indeed engineering a recession, these tariffs would certainly be 1 way to do it (remember, the Trump 2018 tariffs were much lighter and less widespread).

This is why I personally stand at 50/50 on recession vs no recession debate. If the Trump tariffs are indeed a longstanding policy change, we will see a recession (in my view). Put simply, if the president’s goal is to engineer a recession, then we will see a recession.

If the 2025 tariffs are simply a negotiating tactic and we make quick progress on trade deals, then I believe we will avoid a recession. I have seen plenty of interesting takes on the debate, but I don’t believe anyone outside of Trump’s inner circle really has a confident read on this.

Overall, my price target for the S&P 500 remains $5200 on this h sell breakdown. From there, I believe we will likely bounce (in either scenario).

In a non-recessionary scenario, this could end up being the market bottom. In a recessionary scenario, we will likely have 1-2 more waves down, and reach Bear Market territory ($4600 PT for me).

**Short Trade Idea**

APP

Here, we have a Parabolic Bubble Top, followed by a potential h sell developing.

We also have an unfilled earnings gap at $168-$175.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wbXQ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2d7ebe4b-9cf9-4121-84b3-6041b4b3e775_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wbXQ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2d7ebe4b-9cf9-4121-84b3-6041b4b3e775_1396x653.png)

During Bull Markets, I always talk about the importance of Earnings Gaps in trading. The strongest stocks typically gap up big on an earnings beat and guidance raise, and put in a momentum move higher without ever filling these gaps. APP was 1 of these stocks, gapping up big in November to $230 and continuing higher to $525 (a +128% move in 2 months).

Now, during the Bear Phase of the cycle, I look for these unfilled earnings gaps to ultimately be filled. It is rare, throughout history, for a stock to ever have unfilled gaps in its chart. This would suggest a PT of $168-$175 for APP, a -37% drop from current prices.

On the weekly chart, we had a Parabolic Bubble move from $39 at the start of 2024 to a high of $525 on February 10th (a > +1200% move in 13 months).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wrTZ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F092119b9-ac89-490a-a853-daaf3a243128_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wrTZ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F092119b9-ac89-490a-a853-daaf3a243128_1396x653.png)

In 2022, [I studied](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/history-repeating?utm_source=publication-search) the biggest asset Bubbles throughout history, and found that the key theme was the Bubble fully retracing its Parabolic move during the Bear Phase. You can read about that [report here](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/history-repeating?utm_source=publication-search) (to update, ARKK fully retraced its parabolic move during the Bear Phase as well).

For APP, I believe the Parabolic upside move began with the $116 IPO Base breakout to weekly highs. My study on Bubbles would suggest a $116 PT on a full retrace scenario for APP during the Bear Phase (although, it does not *always* happen with individual stocks).

I believe we will see APP fill this gap to $175 during this cycle. My price target is $168-175, with a Recessionary Bear Case price target of $116 (full retrace in $4600 SPX scenario).

I am looking to play this potential APP Bubble Bust downside move to the short side. The potential reward on this trade is too great to pass up for me. It is a high risk trade, however (as all short swing trades are). My stop loss on the trade will be a close above $352, which equates to 24.8% risk on this trade. On trades like these, it is critical to size appropriately. I am risking -24.8% to make +37% - +58%.

This is a High risk, high reward trade. For a trade like this, you might find it better to buy puts to manage risk. I would basically assume a 100% loss on a losing trade ($352 stop trigger), and only buy the amount of puts I would be willing to lose if the trade fails.

For me, I will be looking to short APP stock tomorrow. I will be risking -25% to make +37-58%. Again, this is a high risk trade, so be sure to size appropriately.

For me personally, the risk reward is there, so I will be taking the trade.
