# The Path Forward

**Conflict in the Middle East**

The Israel/Iran escalation brings into focus the playbook for geopolitical shocks, which historically entails sharp equity selloffs but also surprisingly quick recoveries. In the past, the market has often rallied back from the initial geopolitical shock without any clear signs of de-escalation.

The historical pattern is for the S&P 500 to pull back about -3-6% in 3 weeks, but then rally all the way back in another 3 weeks. 2 recent examples that followed this pattern in textbook fashion were the Russia attack on Ukraine in early 2022 and the Hamas attack on Israel in October 2023.

In present context, total equity positioning is already underweight and a -6% selloff would likely need it to fall all the way back down towards the bottom of the 15-year range.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pqDs!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3aa38989-c4eb-4b88-b366-2c1037e02b4c_1290x1068.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pqDs!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3aa38989-c4eb-4b88-b366-2c1037e02b4c_1290x1068.jpeg)

Ultimately, the economic backdrop has eventually always dominated in the end. So the question is, will this Middle East conflict derail the 2025 Bull Run? **I believe it is unlikely absent a significantly larger escalation which starts go greatly impact the economic outlook.**

**Positioning**

I touched a bit on total positioning above (it remains underweight US stocks here). Global equities have now been net bought for 6 straight weeks, but last week’s buying came at a much slower pace.

Systematic strategies have continued to raise their equity exposure slowly, but remain underweight (-0.41sd, 27th percentile) currently. Discretionary positioning is now approaching neutral territory after some of the forced buying/chasing of the last few weeks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gpYT!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F12baad92-9b8d-4161-9a76-7e57c48778fa_1178x878.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gpYT!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F12baad92-9b8d-4161-9a76-7e57c48778fa_1178x878.jpeg)

For systematic strategies, it is expected that they will continue to raise their equity exposure in the coming weeks. Backwards looking vol signals continue to be impacted by the April volatility shock, and as these days finally roll out of the sample, we will see Vol Control Funds forced to buy. I highlighted this phenomenon in more detail in [“Clear Summer Skies”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/clear-summer-skies).

Buybacks remain strong as well, a crucial pillar of the robust demand support for equities. 90% of the S&P 500 remains in an open window for buybacks currently.

**My Big Picture View**

Big picture, the conflict in the Middle East does nothing to change my views on the market. I remain bullish overall and expect this geopolitical shock dip to ultimately be bought.

In the short term, volatility is back and may be here to stay for a bit longer as headlines continue to come in from the ongoing conflict. We had our first -1%+ down day for the SPX since April on Friday, and we are already bouncing back near +1% so far this morning. Remember, volatility works both ways.
