# Friday the 13th

The market is selling off hard this morning following the overnight escalation between Israel and Iran, with major Israeli strikes on Iranian nuclear facilities and Iranian commanders/scientists. The US says it was not involved but was aware. Oil surged up to +13% on the news and has now stabilized around +6-8%. Iran is said to be preparing retaliation, while Israel says the operation could continue for another 2 weeks.

**My initial concerns/takeaways for the market:**

1. Consumer Discretionary sectors will feel pressure due to rising oil prices, acting as another tax on consumers already impacted by tariffs
2. Defense and energy stocks emerge as clear beneficiaries
3. Tech/Nasdaq are likely the least affected
4. Europe and Small Caps should feel bigger impacts (UAE stocks -3%)

In recent days, I have been talking about the mixed price action we were seeing underneath the surface (while the indices chopped around). Lower quality/highly shorted stocks were squeezing higher, while higher quality names like Big Tech and former leaders were underperforming. It almost seemed as though the indices were looking for a reason to sell off, and we got one with last night’s Israel/Iran escalation news.

In the short term, last night’s geopolitical news introduces some volatility back to the market (VIX +11% so far this morning). We have not had a -1% SPX day since April.

Bigger picture, major geopolitical events have historically always been solid buying opportunities for the S&P 500. The average SPX drawdown for major geopolitical events since 1941 is -5%, while the median is -3%.

**Average Days to Bottom:** 22 days

**Average Days to Recovery:** 47 days

**Median Days to Bottom:** 10 days

**Median Days to Recovery:** 19 days

The average days to reach a market bottom is 22 days and average days to full recovery is 47 days. The median days to reach a market bottom is ~10 days and median days to full recovery is 19 days.

So, if history is any indication, it says we can expect a potential 3-5% SPX drawdown over the next 2 weeks/next month.

So far, at today’s lows, we had a -1.2% SPX drawdown. As mentioned, geopolitical shock events like this typically take a little time to play out for equities (2-4 weeks median/average) and do not usually reach an immediate bottom on day 1 (although this has happened in a few instances before).

In this case, Israel has said the operation could continue another 2 weeks while Iran is said to be preparing retaliation. This introduces a layer of headline/gap risk for the market over the next couple weeks, as last night’s news is certainly not the last of this story.

Big picture, I do not believe this Israel/Iran geopolitical event will be any different. Long term, I see this as a buying opportunity for the market. The question is how deep and how long will the sell off be.

After the major rally we have had off April lows, a -3-5% SPX drawdown would be healthy to reset sentiment (which I mentioned yesterday had gotten very overheated in the short term). While it is possible we just bottom here on day 1 and continue the market rally back to ATHs, I do not view today as a tremendous buying opportunity just yet with headline/gap risk very elevated over the next couple weeks.

Big picture, I remain bullish the market here and would view a -3-5% dip over the next couple weeks as buyable. My best guess is that is the most likely scenario for stocks, but as always, things can change quickly.
