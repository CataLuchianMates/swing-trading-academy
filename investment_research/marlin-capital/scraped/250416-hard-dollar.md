# Hard Dollar

We are seeing some retracing of the recent market bounce so far today (SPX -1.2%), which is to be expected. The S&P 500 rallied +12% off Monday’s lows to yesterday’s close.

The big news causing today’s market drop is the US blocking NVDA from selling H20 chips to China, which NVDA reported will lead to a $5.5B write-down. According to a note by Bloomberg Intelligence, NVDA’s writedown suggests that the company may miss out on $14B-$18B in revenue for the year.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zEji!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5e054a48-3785-4aa6-8599-35df1a437643_950x277.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!zEji!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5e054a48-3785-4aa6-8599-35df1a437643_950x277.png)

Not helping matters for Semis is ASML earnings, as the company missed on Q1 bookings noting “increased uncertainty” driven by tariffs.

As I highlighted last week, the 90-day tariff pause announcement removed the extreme Bear Case scenario (deep recession + $220 EPS) from the market, which has allowed stocks to stabilize since last Wednesday.

But now, reality is setting in. And the reality is, there is not much fuel for a sharp rally back to SPX $6000+ with companies guiding down/removing guides, stagnating EPS growth (+3% YoY proj), and worsening economic data. Recession odds now stand at 54%, according to Polymarket.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XNoH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb5f6e8da-1be0-4e6d-aa14-00e811f10867_1284x1481.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!XNoH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb5f6e8da-1be0-4e6d-aa14-00e811f10867_1284x1481.jpeg)

As I talked about on Monday, I believe these factors create a clear ceiling/lid for the market (~$5750).

Trading volumes are also back to reality/normal after recording the 3 highest volume days in stock market history within the last 8 trading days. ETFs had their lightest notional session since before Liberation Day yesterday, with $217B worth of ETF shares changing hands, reverting closer to the YTD average of $185B.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!V5LF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F99577a52-6e70-4bd8-b5d9-1045b78e4159_781x490.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!V5LF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F99577a52-6e70-4bd8-b5d9-1045b78e4159_781x490.jpeg)

Volumes normalizing are another sign that we are past last week’s capitulation type action in the market. Today’s market decline (on bad NVDA news) is much more orderly than the type of panic/capitulation selling we were seeing last Monday.

With that said, my views remain the same here. I do not believe we are in a V-shaped bottom type of environment. Some retracing and backtesting is to be expected, and that is exactly what we are seeing so far today. This aligns with my view of a rangebound market for the foreseeable future.

I believe the VIX has likely peaked while positioning has likely bottomed, creating a floor for the market. At the same time, based off historically high valuations (>20x Fwd P/E), deteriorating fundamentals/stagnating growth, and continued trade uncertainty, I see a clear ceiling in the market as well.

Trading in this choppy, “hard dollar” environment will continue to be a real challenge. I do not have much interest in trading through this chop at the moment. With that said, Q1 Earnings Season is now underway and stocks with clear fundamental and volume catalysts will be in play. Opportunities will arise here, provided we avoid any new tariff tape bombs. These Earnings Beats + Guidance Raises will be my sole focus for new trades for the next month +. In a “hard dollar” environment, it is critical to be more selective in entering new trades. Stocks with a fundamental catalyst will have high volumes and will therefore be more resistant to general market chop.
