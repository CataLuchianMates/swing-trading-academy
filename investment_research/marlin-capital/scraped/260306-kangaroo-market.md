# Kangaroo Market

US stocks bounced around all day Thursday before eventually finishing lower (SPX -0.6%, QQQ -0.3%). At one point today, the QQQ was down -1.4% before recovering most of its losses by the close.

This is what traders call a Kangaroo Market.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ph1l!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2d9b5900-1937-4e6d-8513-0715dac85e15_800x533.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ph1l!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2d9b5900-1937-4e6d-8513-0715dac85e15_800x533.jpeg)

Up, down, up, down.

No follow through in either direction. Every time you think a move is starting, it reverses. This type of market frustrates both Bulls and Bears, and it is exactly the environment we have been in for months now.

**The Bull Case Is Still Alive**

To be clear, I am not calling a Bear Market (yet). I am keeping an open mind, because the Bull Case is certainly not dead here.

The Bear Trap thesis I laid out in [“Hard Penny”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/hard-penny?utm_source=publication-search) remains valid. In both the Dot Com and Covid Bubbles (our 2 best historical comps), the most speculative names topped first, breadth deteriorated underneath the surface, and yet the indices pushed higher for 1 final leg before the real top came. That is still a very real possibility here in 2026. It’s usually not as simple as “the first real pullback is the top”.

There is also 1 historical data point worth paying attention to. 2026 is a midterm election year. Historically, midterm years are positive for US stocks (though worse than the other 3 years). But here is the key detail. The weakness in midterm years has historically been concentrated in the first half of the year, with the real strength showing up in the second half (particularly Q4).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!isE6!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F64de05a2-4fa8-4067-bb63-c478ce455380_900x531.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!isE6!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F64de05a2-4fa8-4067-bb63-c478ce455380_900x531.png)

If this pattern holds, the chop and pain we are experiencing right now could simply be the “first half weakness” phase that midterm years are known for, before giving way to a much stronger second half. That would fit perfectly into both the Bear Trap thesis and the historical midterm year pattern.

But ranges do not last forever. The longer a market coils like this, the more energy builds up, and the more explosive the eventual move tends to be.

Right now, the market is a coiled spring. The bearish evidence is real and growing louder. Speculative leaders peaked months ago. 2026 leaders (the final market leaders) are deteriorating after massive runs. Semis are deteriorating. The indices remain below key moving averages.

The bullish evidence is also real. The Bear Trap thesis from both of our historical comps. The Fed is cutting rates into a growing economy. And midterm election year seasonality overwhelmingly favors a positive second half/Q4.

Something has to give. And when it does, the move is going to be significant.

You want to stay ready and locked in for the real opportunities (not exhausted and frustrated).
