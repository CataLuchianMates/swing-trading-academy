# Floor to Ceiling

Markets were sharply lower to kick off the week yesterday with the S&P 500 finishing -2.4% (its 3rd decline in last 4 sessions, NDX down 4/4). Breadth was widely negative with 463 names in the S&P finishing lower (~93% downside day) and Big Tech leading to the downside once again.

Looking at the big picture, we have bounced +9% off April 7th lows following the 90-day tariff pause announcement.

But now, reality has set in.

And the reality is, as of this moment, we are trading at ~21x forward P/E with deteriorating fundamentals and stagnating earnings growth.

Yesterday, Morgan Stanley said President Trump’s trade war has created “COVID-level uncertainty” for investors and corporations.

The data proves this to be true. So far through Q1 earnings, we have the fewest companies issuing guidance since COVID (13% vs 40% avg). Only 8/59 SPX companies to report have issued any guidance at all, and none have guided above consensus.

We have the worst 3M guidance ratio (# above vs below consensus) since COVID.

Company mentions of “better” or “stronger” versus “worse” or “weaker” are at 2008 GFC levels.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ItYS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0346dbda-8106-44cb-8322-9993c97f90d3_563x500.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ItYS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0346dbda-8106-44cb-8322-9993c97f90d3_563x500.jpeg)

As we can clearly see, this is the most uncertainty for companies since COVID times. Uncertainty is the enemy of financial markets. So, why did the market bottom from the Covid crash (when things were still quite uncertain)? Very simple answer - the Fed.

As it stands right now, the Fed is not supportive of higher equity prices. In recent days, we have seen Trump/Powell tensions continue to grow, with Trump calling for preemptive rate cuts and saying the economy will slow unless Chair Powell lowers rates.

The Fed does not cut rates preemptively, though. This is not in their nature. The Fed will cut when the hard data allows them to (which often causes them to be late), but we are not at that point yet. One can argue that is not the best way for the Fed to operate, but this is the way they have always handled things.

As long as the Fed remains on hold and fundamentals continue to deteriorate (as we’re seeing in Q1 earnings season), I feel confident in saying that there will be no V shaped recovery like COVID.

For these reasons, I believe we have a clear ceiling in the market.

Perhaps the bigger question: Is there a floor?

My analysis leads me to believe that there is a floor in the market because of completely washed out positioning and signs of technical capitulation (VIX spike above 60, -2.5z total positioning).

Historically, these are the times when long term investors should look to buy, as it indicates a ton of bearishness and fear is present/has already been priced in.

Moreover, the current Bear Market is already quite advanced in terms of the drawdowns. Compared to the 2022 Bear Market, the average stock has suffered a similar amount of damage. As I have highlighted, the 2022 Bear market was slow moving and took place in 10+ months.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hdA9!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F919ff120-6fc5-42b5-8e4f-67a4ab338051_822x1461.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hdA9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F919ff120-6fc5-42b5-8e4f-67a4ab338051_822x1461.jpeg)

Here in 2025, we reached comparable drawdowns in ~2 months (for the indices, many individual stocks have exhibited bear market behavior all year).

In 2022, we also had a shallow recession and a shallow earnings recession (which is no guarantee here in 2025).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2kCI!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8e005a83-4b58-4555-86d4-a9b76df6bf6f_395x306.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2kCI!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8e005a83-4b58-4555-86d4-a9b76df6bf6f_395x306.png)

For these reasons, I continue to believe we do have a potential floor in the market. However, my view on this is not without risks.

The main risk remains an economic recession, which typically coincides with an earnings recession. Again, I do not believe a recession is priced in here at 21 fwd P/E and positive EPS growth still projected for 2025 (+3% YoY).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gT-y!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc81d75c1-7d50-4f7f-ba10-9e46e0bf6243_1074x692.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gT-y!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc81d75c1-7d50-4f7f-ba10-9e46e0bf6243_1074x692.jpeg)

To be clear, a recession is by no means off the table because of the 90-day tariff pause. As it stands, Polymarket recession odds for 2025 are currently at 56%. What the 90-day tariff pause did, is take us from a sure recession to a possible recession. This allowed the market to stabilize and removed the extreme Bear Case scenario (<$220 EPS) that was in the process of being priced in.

An economic recession & earnings recession remains the risk to my view, and it would likely mean the floor is lower than $4835 (current lows).

The good news is, we will start to get more clarity on the state of the economy as Big Tech and the rest of the SPX reports Q1 earnings. These reports will be critical for the market, and will likely decide the direction the indices take from here.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TcyT!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F11863f9c-7556-4eef-8f0f-23208818885e_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TcyT!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F11863f9c-7556-4eef-8f0f-23208818885e_1920x1080.jpeg)

As it stands, my big picture view remains the same ($5000-5700 SPX trading range for the foreseeable future). However, when times are this uncertain, I think it is important to stay flexible and not married to any 1 view. The majority of the S&P 500 reports earnings within the next 2-3 weeks, so we are once again entering “do or die” time for the market. Stay tuned for my analysis on this critical Q1 earnings season moving forward.
