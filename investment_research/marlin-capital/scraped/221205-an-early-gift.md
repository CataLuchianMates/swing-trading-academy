# An Early Gift

The key takeaway from Fed Chair Powell’s [speech last week](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/speech/powell20221130a.htm) is clear: despite a very tight labor market (3.7% Unemployment) and recent easing of financial conditions, the Fed intends to downshift.

If the central story plaguing the market this year has been the Federal Reserve, there is no question that this dynamic just got a bit friendlier.  As I pointed out in [“Scary Season”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/scary-season), each time financial conditions have eased this year, the Fed has pulled the strings back tighter.  In fact, Powell reportedly [changed his speech](https://fortune.com/2022/08/30/stock-market-pain-jerome-powell-jackson-hole-speech/) at Jackson Hole (8/26) due to FCI easing.  Many expected him to deliver a similar message this time around, but he did not.

In this regard, Powell’s speech last week was a clear change in tone from the Fed.  For the first time within this tightening cycle, the recent easing in financial conditions did not require Powell to beat the market back with a warning message.

With Fed Chair Powell casually transitioning the Fed tightening cycle to its final phase, the market reacted as if it just received a large-scale regime change, sharply cutting out the remaining tail risk of overtightening and the ensuing economic collapse outcome.  In response to the Fed Chair’s speech, bond yields fell, the dollar weakened, and equities rallied.

**The Big Picture - Recession Indicators Blinking Red**

Big picture, a 50 bp rate hike into the most inverted 2/10 yield curve in 40 years is not a major policy pivot.  The 2/10 yield curve inversion has preceded each of the last 6 recessions.

Weakness in the US economy is already showing up in the manufacturing sector.  The Chicago PMI slid all the way to 37.2 in November, within 20 bps of Covid lows.  A sub 40 PMI reading has correlated with each of the last 7 recessions.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Id_x!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F10021d4b-49cf-4ce4-adf7-d38ff4e02f60_767x481.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Id_x!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F10021d4b-49cf-4ce4-adf7-d38ff4e02f60_767x481.png)

Moreover, the ISM Manufacturing Index dropped below 50 for the first time since the pandemic.  The difference between new orders and inventories has historically foreshadowed where manufacturing production is headed, and it is currently near prior recession levels.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!doum!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8dd99a61-cdde-42e3-b987-d1cf7876d513_1095x603.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!doum!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8dd99a61-cdde-42e3-b987-d1cf7876d513_1095x603.png)

In Bear Markets, the S&P 500 typically bottoms close to inflection points in economic data.  The ISM Manufacturing Index is just one example data point, as highlighted below.

As mentioned above, leading indicators are pointing to a steeper drop in ISM Manufacturing in 2023. The economic data continues to worsen and does not appear close to a trough, so it is hard to argue for a sustained market bottom.

**Earnings Estimates Priced for a Soft Landing**

With this recent rally, the market is pricing in a soft economic landing (no recession).  The S&P 500 is currently trading at an 18x Forward P/E multiple.  An 18x multiple does not feel like the launchpad for the next 10 year bull market, as it is already expensive for where real rates currently are.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bgbg!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd9e31cd5-91b0-4984-a629-0aef2d7fb29a_415x322.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bgbg!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd9e31cd5-91b0-4984-a629-0aef2d7fb29a_415x322.png)

As I have [discussed in the past](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/better-early-than-late), I believe future earnings estimates remain too high (meaning the P/E multiple is actually higher than 18x).  Based off current estimates, the consensus is calling for $225-$230 of EPS in 2023 (+4% YoY growth).

As illustrated in the graphic below, the average recession results in a -15% decline in EPS. If we see a similar result in this recession, the SPX is currently trading above 20x forward earnings. Even in a mild recession scenario of -12% EPS growth (highlighted below), the SPX is currently trading above 20x NTM earnings.

Based off this data, it is safe to say there is nothing more than a mild earnings slowdown priced into future estimates.

Leading indicators are pointing to an earnings recession next year, but the estimates have not caught up (down).  Both factors I mentioned above (1. Inverted yield curve & 2. Manufacturing weakness) have historically led to weakness in the economy and corporate earnings.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hysL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4ca996e0-54a6-4b64-9846-c1b17ac0be99_1255x1465.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hysL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4ca996e0-54a6-4b64-9846-c1b17ac0be99_1255x1465.png)

Additionally, stocks can not yet be viewed as cheap compared to bonds. Typically, equities do not bottom until they are **the** more attractive asset class versus fixed income. Due to the brutal year bonds have had, equities are actually more expensive (not cheaper) than bonds and credit than they were at the start of the year.

**Technical Update**

Technically, this is around the point where Bear Market rallies typically fail.  On the weekly time frame, we have reached some key resistance points.  The SPX closed above its 40 week EMA by 30 points last week ($4036).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!onEN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4d2707d3-00e7-4630-8bff-22161c476cc2_1029x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!onEN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4d2707d3-00e7-4630-8bff-22161c476cc2_1029x653.png)

On a timing basis, we are now entering the 9th week of this rally.  The previous 2 Bear Market rallies failed after 6 and 10 weeks, respectively.

On the daily time frame, the SPX also closed above its 200 day moving average ($4046).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GlZX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F137d58b9-0f64-4c03-b31e-e6d747fca754_1031x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GlZX!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F137d58b9-0f64-4c03-b31e-e6d747fca754_1031x653.png)

During the March Bear rally, the index peaked 6 trading days after the initial break above its 200 day.  In total, the rally lasted for 11 trading days above the 200 day moving average.  A similar pattern this time around would point to a peak in the $4200-$4300 range on Wednesday, before a drop below the 200 day ($4046) next week.

A trader must be prepared and ready for all possible scenarios. It is important not to fight the market in this environment. Despite worsening economic data, the market has staged a powerful rally to squeeze overeager traders.

I will be looking to short ES Futures on the reversal, but will remain patient until sufficient evidence that the peak is in arises. For patient bears looking to short, I believe this rally will turn out to be an early holiday gift.

**Summary**

* In my view, the market is pricing in a soft landing with an 18x P/E multiple on future estimates that are unlikely to be met.
* A hard landing remains my Base Case, with a price target of $2900-$3300.
* The most reliable leading indicators are pointing to a recession in 2023.
* The technicals are suggesting this rally will likely peak and fail in the next 1-2 weeks.
