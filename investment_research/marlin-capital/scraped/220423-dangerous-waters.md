# Dangerous Waters

The indices broke lower last week, just when it looked as if we might get a tiny lull in the action through Earnings Season.  Interestingly, the indices broke lower in the session after Tesla announced outstanding Q1 results.  This is an ominous sign for future Earnings reactions throughout the Q1 season.  There are a handful of ominous signs for the market right now, including the chart setups.

Let’s start with the Daily Chart of the Nasdaq.

There is a very high probability that the $12.5k support level is tested in the coming days.  If this level breaks and the composite starts to make new 52-week lows, here is what I believe will happen next.

**3 Key Levels to Watch**

Below is the weekly chart of the Nasdaq, going back to 1999.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7UaB!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F13258d28-2aa3-4d9f-9251-55bd6604d482_1331x613.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7UaB!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F13258d28-2aa3-4d9f-9251-55bd6604d482_1331x613.png)

As you can see in the above chart, the 200 Week EMA (blue line) has acted as a key support level in past market corrections.  In the Dot Com Bust and 2008 GFC, the 200 Week EMA support level broke, and gave way to longer and more painful Bear Markets.  The 200 Week EMA currently sits at $11,280 (12% below current Nasdaq price).

Below is the monthly chart of the Nasdaq, going back to 1999.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mXbh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8e7b968e-cf52-4261-9c02-b8169f67a35b_1335x625.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mXbh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8e7b968e-cf52-4261-9c02-b8169f67a35b_1335x625.png)

In the above chart, the orange line is the 40 Month EMA.  This level has also acted as a key support level in past market corrections.  Once again, this key EMA has not been violated since the 2000 and 2008 Bear Markets.  The 40 Month EMA currently sits at $11,754 (8% below current Nasdaq price).

One final long term chart to look at is the Nasdaq Quarterly, going back to 1999.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5o1Y!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faaf38cbb-6d5f-4185-85ed-2024df19e0c3_1333x629.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5o1Y!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faaf38cbb-6d5f-4185-85ed-2024df19e0c3_1333x629.png)

In the above chart, the purple line is the 20 Quarter EMA.  This is a final key support level to watch, as this level has also not been breached in the ’00 Dot Com Bust and ’08 GFC.  The 20 Quarter EMA currently sits at $10,723 (16% below current Nasdaq price).

These key levels suggest another 8-16% downside in the Nasdaq Composite, at minimum.  The evidence indicates that we are likely to visit these support levels in the coming days/weeks.  The million dollar question is: What will happen next?

**Warnings From the Past**

There are a number of parallels to both 2000 and 2008 that can not be ignored.  I have been Bearish on equities since late November because of the many warnings signs that were present.  Now, there are a whole new set of warning signs that I see.

First off, central banks globally have already hiked 75 times YTD, the highest net % since 2008.  In fact, 2022 promises to be an even faster tightening cycle globally than 2008 was.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mF9k!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff8cfa5fd-46a8-4634-9bf0-0ba8085b5be9_566x392.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mF9k!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff8cfa5fd-46a8-4634-9bf0-0ba8085b5be9_566x392.png)

In the US, the Federal Reserve has set a target for 7 rate hikes in 2022, and many participants have opined that multiple 50 Basis Point hikes will be necessary.  To make matters worse for equity investors, the Fed will also be embarking on the fastest QT cycle we have ever seen.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YWLK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcb70782c-6f15-4c06-83d8-e81607767eee_570x438.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!YWLK!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcb70782c-6f15-4c06-83d8-e81607767eee_570x438.png)

As you can see in the above chart, 2022 is projected to have a much faster QT cycle than the last one (2018), where the slope of the balance sheet was mostly flat or slightly down. As I discussed in [“How Late Is It”,](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/getting-late?s=w) 2022 projects to be about 2x as fast as 2018 in terms of balance sheet shrinkage. Additionally, this time around, we are following up the fastest QE cycle in history with the fastest QT cycle in history.  I have talked about how I expect Quantitative Tightening to affect equity markets in past reports [here](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-thoughts-post-fed-meeting-de7?s=w), [here](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-thoughts-post-fed-meeting-290?s=w) and [here](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/getting-late?s=w).

Several of the moves in the Fixed Income Market should also serve as major warning signs to equity investors, starting with the velocity of the move in 2 Year rates.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lDqF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7731b9ee-46d8-4968-9b31-d00266d6cba3_1232x599.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lDqF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7731b9ee-46d8-4968-9b31-d00266d6cba3_1232x599.png)

This is the fastest move in 2 Year rates since 1987.  Any parallel to 1987 should scream “Caution!”, as this was the year that featured one of the biggest market crashes in history (“Black Monday”).

The recent spike in 10 Year yields should also serve as a warning sign to equity investors.  History warns of such events, as detailed below.

Bond market traders have already entered panic mode, as the Vanguard Total Bond Market Index is approaching March 2020 Covid lows in a brutal sell off.

As you can see, the fixed income market is suggesting that everything is NOT okay.  Can you blame them?  The track record of past Fed tightening cycles in the US suggests that the Fed faces a significant challenge in achieving a soft landing.  As you can see in the below chart, Fed tightening cycles have historically been followed by recessions shortly after they end.  11 out of 14 tightening cycles since WWII have been followed by a recession within 2 years, implying a success rate of just 21%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1LBi!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F09f4562c-7a64-40ab-a45f-2b78616cae37_1176x733.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1LBi!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F09f4562c-7a64-40ab-a45f-2b78616cae37_1176x733.png)

With the speed and magnitude of this tightening cycle (along with 40yr high inflation), it is fair to assume the odds of a soft landing drop substantially (from 21%).  More on that unsuccessful track record below.  Fed tightening cycles historically end in “financial events” that have ranged from bad to catastrophic.

**Recap**

This will continue to be a treacherous environment for equities, for the reasons outlined below.

1.       Fastest QT cycle ever

2.       7+ Rate Hikes

3.       40 yr high inflation

4.       Slowing Growth

5.       Above Average Valuations Historically

6.       Warnings Signs from Fixed Income Market

I expect the Nasdaq to pull into the key support levels mentioned above.  It is unclear what will happen from there, but there are a number of warnings signs and parallels to 2000 and 2008 that should not be ignored.

In 2000, we spent 32 months below the 40 Month EMA.  In 2008, we spent 10 months below the 40 month EMA.  Both of these scenarios show what is possible in markets. Neither scenario should be dismissed as a potential possibility.

**Marlin Capital Strategy**

In my Active Portfolio, I plan to continue looking to the short side for trades, as I believe the market will be in “Sell the rip” mode until inflation begins to improve.  Big picture, I expect this to be a long and painful process for equities.

In my IRA, I plan to deploy at least half my cash (currently 38% total) at the key support levels mentioned.  Historically, these moving averages have provided terrific buying opportunities.  I plan to keep anywhere from 15-20% cash to prepare for the scenario that we break these levels and enter a longer Bear Market a la 2000 or 2008.
