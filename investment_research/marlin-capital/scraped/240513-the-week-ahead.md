# The Week Ahead 

The S&P 500 rallied back towards all-time highs last week, recording its 3rd consecutive week of gains.

Last week was the lowest volume week of 2024 thus far and the lowest volume week we’ve had since Thanksgiving (holiday shortened week).  With the NDX now just ~1% off ATHs after the recent bounce back, current investor sentiment is looking a bit fatigued.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gNCR!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2cccb653-c159-48dc-a8e8-9a2d9d6a9021_605x413.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gNCR!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2cccb653-c159-48dc-a8e8-9a2d9d6a9021_605x413.jpeg)

The NDX is now up +8% YTD, which would lead most to believe that Tech is off to a great start to the year.  However, through a different lens, the median NDX stock is only up +3% this year and the NDX Equal Weighted Index is only up +2.4% YTD (a very average start to the year relative to the NDXE’s history).  This tells a mediocre picture for Tech’s performance when compared to the top performing assets of 2024 so far (Copper +21% YTD, Gold +15%, Crude & Energy +14%, Japan/Nikkei +14%, Utes +14%, Europe +13%, Financials +11%, China/Hang Seng +11%).

Last week did not offer investors many new data points nor plot twists to the picture.  The meat of earnings season (Big Tech, majority of mega caps) is now behind us as we enter a pivotal Macro week (PPI + CPI readings this week).  Equities are trading in a comfortable goldilocks scenario, with growth resilient (but not too hot to be inflationary), earnings are solid enough, and investors are looking ahead to Fed rate cuts in the 2nd half of 2024.  Markets are currently anticipating the 1st Fed rate cut to come in September and a potential 2nd cut to come in December.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Uulm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1f8b35a0-50e7-44cc-8386-33508fbe600e_1284x663.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Uulm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1f8b35a0-50e7-44cc-8386-33508fbe600e_1284x663.jpeg)

The market has continued to perform despite the momentum in real rates (10Y hovering around 4.5% all year) and elevated geopolitical uncertainty (Middle East).  The performance of unprofitable Tech stocks have suffered the most amid this dynamic of higher sustained real rates.  In my view, this is why we have continued to see Small Caps/Unprofitable Tech stocks underperform their larger, more established counterparts.

With that said, market breadth is showing some bullish signs for Small Caps (and the entire market).  The SPX, NYSE, and Midcap Advance/Decline lines all made new all-time highs last week.  The Advance/Decline line for Small Caps is on the verge of new 52-week highs (and right at 2024 highs).  This is a bullish sign for a potential summer rally in the group.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Y8li!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F668d1d08-5de3-482c-9fcb-aa5ebec328e2_1284x1350.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Y8li!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F668d1d08-5de3-482c-9fcb-aa5ebec328e2_1284x1350.jpeg)

One dynamic that has stood out through Q1 earnings season so far is the outperformance of Semis vs Software (Semis are +15% YTD vs Software down on the year).  We are yet to see much of a bid in the Software space (positioning remains low relative to other Tech subsectors).  The AI software story remains nascent at best, as investors continue to flock towards the more obvious AI stories (i.e. Semis).

Overall, AI hype has cooled somewhat, but remains strong.  Jefferies compiled the number of times AI was mentioned in earnings calls across the B2C internet universe.  They found the focus on AI has remained elevated in Q1, but down slightly from the previous quarter (Q4 ’23).

This week is a Macro heavy tape with PPI on Tuesday, CPI on Wednesday, and Initial Jobless Claims on Thursday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hKBs!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd5e47197-38b8-4176-8960-1e1daabea71b_684x495.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hKBs!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd5e47197-38b8-4176-8960-1e1daabea71b_684x495.jpeg)

The Earnings slate quits down a bit this week, but plenty of interesting names are still set to report (STNE, BABA, SE, NU, MNDY, DT, CSCO, WMT, AMAT, etc.).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hlZt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3b4db45c-f421-4a81-a1db-673c15a57628_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hlZt!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3b4db45c-f421-4a81-a1db-673c15a57628_1920x1080.jpeg)

So far through Q1 earnings season, the average S&P 500 stock has declined in the trading session immediately following its report.  This has been the 8th worst quarterly performance for the average SPX stock post-earnings announcement since 2006.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tRlq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe0bf29ac-d7ed-4301-aff4-dc30c1634393_568x447.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tRlq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe0bf29ac-d7ed-4301-aff4-dc30c1634393_568x447.jpeg)

While not the best quarter for my Earnings Gapper setups (at least, on day 1), we are still seeing some potential future leaders emerge from the Q1 earnings season.  My top names to watch from earnings season so far are APP, ASPN, TMDX, PI, SG, and PINS.  I will be stalking these names for potential long entries as well as any new Earnings Gapper setups that present themselves this week.

As for the general market, we have a couple potential tailwinds working for us moving forwards.  #1 is stock buybacks.  S&P 500 stock buybacks are now at their highest level in 6 years with the majority of SPX companies now in their open window.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3jOm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffd281ecf-6ecf-489f-9b42-918e3294857c_1284x697.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3jOm!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffd281ecf-6ecf-489f-9b42-918e3294857c_1284x697.jpeg)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dv3p!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F51de3868-3579-49d5-b9f3-53fc9bd55924_1032x444.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dv3p!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F51de3868-3579-49d5-b9f3-53fc9bd55924_1032x444.jpeg)

#2 is seasonality, particularly in election years.  Presidential election years have often seen big summer rallies throughout history.  In presidential election years, the SPX is up 75% of the time from June-August for an average return of 7.3%.
