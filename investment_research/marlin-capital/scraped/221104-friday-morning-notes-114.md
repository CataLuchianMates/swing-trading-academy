# Friday Morning Notes 11.4

Technical Analysis

SPX WEEKLY

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OamW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff55394a3-33e5-4e2e-8e85-dcd4d0bc1f20_1061x655.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OamW!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff55394a3-33e5-4e2e-8e85-dcd4d0bc1f20_1061x655.jpeg)

This is the most important chart to watch today as we close out the week. The S&P 500 is sitting right at its 200 Week EMA after a 3 week rally and Fed induced pullback. A break back under the 200 Week EMA ($3685) opens the door for new lows in the coming weeks. As I described in “[The Pendulum Swings Both Ways](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-pendulum-swings-both-ways)”, the next significant break of this support should begin the extension to the downside. Based off my historical research, we are likely heading for a 20-30% downside extension below the 200 week EMA. Full analysis [linked here](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-pendulum-swings-both-ways).

A close above the $3685-$3700 level today is critical for bulls. Until we break below the 200 Week EMA again, there is still a window of opportunity for this rally off lows. In my view, superior swing short trade opportunities will come after this support breaks. Until then, traders may have to deal with more chop to frustrate both longs and shorts.
