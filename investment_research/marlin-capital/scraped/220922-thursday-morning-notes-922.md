# Thursday Morning Notes 9.22

Technical Analysis

US stocks experienced whipsaw action yesterday, with traders overwhelmed by a flood of headlines following the Fed’s decision to hike interest rates 75 bps and Jerome Powell’s press conference. The Powell presser signaled 2 main things: (1) policy will remain highly restrictive for a prolonged period of time, (2) and a soft landing appears increasingly unlikely. The S&P 500 finished near session lows and extended its drop from January highs to more than 20% once again.

On a technical basis, volatility expanded following the 2 PM EST decision, with 2 fakeout moves outside the range (first down, then up). The QQQ broke down in the late afternoon and closed at lows. We’ll find out if this is the “real move” in the coming days/weeks, but it certainly seems like it, and is also logical because of the move higher in Fed funds futures, yields, and the USD. $280, $275-$276, and June lows ($269-$270) are the key support levels to watch below. I will be looking to add short exposure if this breakdown can stick, most likely via leveraged ETFs (TQQQ, SOXL, etc.) and ES futures.
