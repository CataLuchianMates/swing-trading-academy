#### Full NVDA Breakdown

Let’s dig a little deeper into the NVDA print. 👇

The numbers were spectacular across the board.

Q1 revenue grew +85% YoY to $81.6B (beating consensus by $2.4B, or ~3%), adjusted EPS hit $1.87 vs. $1.77 expected (+5.7% beat), and Data Center revenue surged to a record $75B, up +92% YoY. Gross margin held firm at 75.0%. Free cash flow reached a record $49B.

The guidance was also impressive. Q2 FY2027 revenue was guided to $91B, well ahead of the ~$86B consensus heading in.

But the more important story was the qualitative shift in how NVIDIA is framing its business.

For the first time, NVIDIA broke out Data Center into two distinct segments: Hyperscale ($38B, ~50% of DC revenue, +12% QoQ) and ACIE ($37B, +31% QoQ). ACIE stands for “AI Clouds, Industrial, and Enterprise.”

That ACIE segment growing +31% QoQ is the most important new datapoint in the entire report.

AI demand is no longer just hyperscaler capex. It is broadening into a second, more fragmented AI factory market spanning AI-native clouds, sovereign builds, enterprise, and industrial. That is a structurally broader and longer-duration cycle than a market built only on a handful of hyperscaler customers.

Jensen Huang used the conference call to drive his point home. Some of his quotes deserve the full treatment here.

On the parabolic state of demand right now: 👇

> “This was an extraordinary quarter. Demand has gone parabolic. The reason is simple: agentic AI has arrived. AI can now do productive and valuable work. Tokens are now profitable. Model makers are in a race to produce more. In the AI era, compute capacity is revenue and profits. NVIDIA is the platform of this era.”

On where AI spending goes from here: 👇

> “First of all, we should be growing faster than hyperscale CapEx... They’re $1 trillion this year. I have every expectation it’s going to grow from here for fundamentally good reasons. This is the way computing is going to work in the future. If they don’t have the compute, they won’t have the revenues. It is very clear. Compute is revenues. Compute is profit.”

On the broadening AI customer base (the ACIE thesis): 👇

> “The first category is hyperscale, and the CapEx is at $1 trillion and it’s growing towards $3 trillion-$4 trillion. The second category is all of the AI native clouds... enterprise, 250,000 enterprise companies around the world... sovereign AI clouds... The second category is extremely diverse... and in the future, it’ll be hundreds of thousands of companies.”

On the cycle duration: 👇

> “AI infrastructure spending is on track to reach $3 trillion-$4 trillion annually by the end of this decade.”

In my view, this is the type of conference call that’s worth listening to more than once. Here are my key takeaways from tonight’s NVDA call. 👇

**(1) The AI Supercycle is broadening, not peaking.**

The biggest fundamental change in this quarter vs. last quarter is the explicit disclosure that AI demand is widening into AI-native clouds, sovereign builds, and enterprise factories. ACIE grew +31% QoQ. That broadening is a longer-duration setup than a hyperscaler-only cycle.

**(2) Agentic AI is the new inflection point.**

Jensen called it “the ChatGPT moment of agentic AI” back in February. This quarter, the language is more confident, the demand is parabolic, and the tokens are profitable.

**(3) Compute equals revenue.**

This is the most important framework Jensen articulated. “If they don’t have the compute, they won’t have the revenues.” This is how Jensen is selling to enterprises and sovereigns, and it is the reason CapEx is still accelerating, not peaking.

**(4) $145B of supply commitments.**

This is the hard data sign that NVDA management is still planning for sustained demand, not peak demand. You do not commit $145B of inventory and prepaids if you think the cycle is peaking.

**Here is the bottom line for Marlin Capital subscribers. 👇**

We do not own NVDA, but every datapoint in tonight's report directly reinforces the themes we do own. The Memory names are the HBM under every Blackwell and Rubin GPU. The Optical names are the networking fabric Jensen called out (Spectrum-X now larger than every Ethernet network peer combined). The Power and Cooling names are the physical AI factory buildout (partner sites over 10 MW nearly doubled YoY to 80+). The AI Data Center names like APLD and NBIS are the ACIE segment Jensen just named as the next leg of growth. In my view, NVDA's print and commentary just made every one of these AI themes more durable.
