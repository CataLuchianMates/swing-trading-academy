# Friday Morning Notes 1.20

Technical Update

The market continued to its downside reversal off the 200 day MA yesterday, with the S&P 500 closing -0.76% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!STdb!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2df615a8-2ec4-4198-924e-cb179308c251_859x661.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!STdb!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2df615a8-2ec4-4198-924e-cb179308c251_859x661.jpeg)

The low of the day was $3886, as the index failed to decisively break below the $3890 support level. We ended up closing the day at $3898, as we are now below the 21 and 60 EMAs on the daily.

We are set for a small gap up this morning after NFLX kicked off big tech earnings last night. Today is options expiration (including LEAPS), so this should further add to the volatility in the session. Max pain for both sides appears to be a close around $3935, effectively burning premium for calls and puts.

The technical setup remains bearish to me here as long as price stays below the recent highs ($4015). We will need to firmly break $3890 support for the selling to accelerate, in my view. This would open the door for a further drop to $3700.

Resistance levels to watch today include $3935, $3950, $3970, and $4000.

Key support levels to watch include $3890-$3900, $3840-$3850, and $3800.
