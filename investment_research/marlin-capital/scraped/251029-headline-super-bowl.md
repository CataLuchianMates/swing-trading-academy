# Headline Super Bowl

October 29th will be the headline Super Bowl with the FOMC decision and MSFT, META, and GOOGL earnings post-close.

#### **Wednesday’s Fed Meeting**

Tomorrow, the Fed will be cutting rates with the S&P 500 at ATHs (just like they did in September), so it’s a good time to rehash these stats:

The Fed has cut rates with the S&P 500 within 1% of ATHs 16 times in its history.

**The S&P 500 was higher a year later all 16 times for an average return of +15%.**

**INTC**

The next trade for us was INTC, which we bought at $39.96 this morning.

Intel was a company left for dead years ago, but the Trump administration investment revitalized this stock. INTC has been a leading Semi stock since that 9/18 announcement. The company just announced terrific earnings last week, which is what got me interested in the name.

On Day 1 following earnings, INTC faded its gap up. We are seeing lots of these types of setups so far this earnings season (gap up fades, followed by potential U-turns).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jGJ7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fae22c344-1cd1-4e82-8096-89c901e8836f_1266x837.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jGJ7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fae22c344-1cd1-4e82-8096-89c901e8836f_1266x837.png)

After that Day 1 earnings gap up fade, I am ideally looking for the fade/dip to be bought up quickly and for the stock to start make a U-turn pattern. This is what we saw with INTC yesterday. The stock bounced right back on Day 2 (yesterday) and formed an inside day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Vl34!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F56300cb0-3087-40bf-9a3f-690e825ed387_1137x752.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Vl34!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F56300cb0-3087-40bf-9a3f-690e825ed387_1137x752.png)

Traditionally, the inside day strategy is to buy through the previous day’s (the inside day) highs. For INTC, this would have been at $40.68. But, in bullish markets and in leading names, I will sometimes look to anticipate the inside day breakout ahead of time. This allows you to get a better entry with tighter risk (allowing you to get heavier in the position).

Speaking of position sizing, I recommend all new subscribers read [“Volatility Melt”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/volatility-melt?utm_source=publication-search), where I cover my Active Trade position sizing in detail. This is the exact equation that I use to determine the size on all my positions.

You start with how much of your account you are willing to lose on the trade. From there, that tells you how many shares to enter on your trade.

Now back to INTC. As I mentioned, I will sometimes anticipate an inside day breakout ahead of time to get a better entry with tighter risk. If I have sufficient evidence/confidence to believe that the stock will end up breaking the inside day highs (leading stock, bullish market, earnings catalyst, etc.), then I will not hesitate to enter in anticipation of that breakout.

With INTC, you could tell right off the open that the stock was super strong out of the gate and was likely going to break inside day highs at $40.68. The first 5 minute candle topped at $40.55 (so it nearly did right away).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Oc95!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff70ffa5f-d99c-4062-aafa-85ef9fa0af57_1266x837.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Oc95!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff70ffa5f-d99c-4062-aafa-85ef9fa0af57_1266x837.png)

Then, INTC pulled back on the next 5 minute candle, where I bought at $39.96.

We are off to a good start here for INTC with the stock finishing +5% today. This a potential leading name, so I want to let this trade play out. My first target is $45 here.

**GLW**

So far this earnings season, we are seeing lots of leading names gap down, only to see that dip bought right up. This is another way I like to buy earnings names.

[Back in Q1](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-4-trade-ideas-from-q1-earnings?utm_source=publication-search), I said that it’s sometimes even more bullish to see an earnings gap down/dip that gets bought right up because that shows there is strong demand to buy at lower prices (aka support). This was the setup on our HOOD $49.48 buy.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nxV4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb5c03762-75c2-445d-b5a5-fa6ace8b2cf1_1213x747.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!nxV4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb5c03762-75c2-445d-b5a5-fa6ace8b2cf1_1213x747.jpeg)

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Xj2J!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F704d550e-d50b-4f5e-8eb3-3f3e3fa1ee22_1208x754.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Xj2J!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F704d550e-d50b-4f5e-8eb3-3f3e3fa1ee22_1208x754.jpeg)

This is the setup here for GLW. GLW is a name that has been leading since last earnings season. I called it out as a potential leading name in [“New Ideas”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/new-ideas?utm_source=publication-search), but the stock never gave a good entry and we didn’t end up trading it (note: I will not trade every setup/stock I talk about, but that doesn’t mean I’m not bullish. Sometimes, I am not at my desk at the exact right time/there could various reasons I might not enter).

GLW announced strong earnings yesterday, but the stock gapped down this morning. This is not a surprising result for a leading name that has rallied a lot (gapping down on strong results).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!G77W!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6633eea5-0473-41d9-bf2a-a95c1584c7ae_1266x837.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!G77W!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6633eea5-0473-41d9-bf2a-a95c1584c7ae_1266x837.png)

The gap down is where I like to start watching closely. If that gap down is bought right up, this is where I become interested in trading the stock. Because to me, this is the key evidence that Bulls are still winning the battle.

You will notice that the absolute strongest stocks do not give many dips during their ascent. When they do pull back, that dip does NOT last very long (as buyers who have been waiting for a dip pile in). It’s bought up right away. This is a signal that there is still huge buying demand for the stock.

**When looking at leading stocks, you should be thinking to yourself “this is crazy, it never pulls back, it just goes straight up”.**  When you are looking at a stock and have those thoughts in your head, just know that you are looking at a leading stock.

For GLW, today was a strong Day 1 reaction to the earnings gap down/dip. Remember, a pullback/dip in a leading stock is neither bullish nor bearish. It is what happens from there that determines whether it’s bullish or bearish. This is why I like to wait for the strength after the dip/evidence of dip buying. Because THAT is the bullish signal, not the dip (**the fact that there is high demand to buy the dip**).

For GLW, I think bulls are still in control of this battle and see the stock continuing higher from here. I will be looking for GLW to tighten up for a possible entry in the coming days.

It’s always crazy for me to look at CLS. This is a stock [I first highlighted back at $21 in July 2023](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/the-week-ahead-934), because the stock was seeing MASSIVE volume following a HUGE earnings beat. We picked out CLS right away, as the volume and fundamentals were impossible to ignore. We had a couple decent trades in CLS, but it’s obviously nothing compared to the >1200% the stock has been up had we just held.

And this, my friends, is a reminder of why you must always be holding great stocks in Bull Markets. This is why 3 of our 4 portfolios here at Marlin Capital are “buy and hold” (in a Bull Market). You simply can NOT time every move in a stock. If you are a skilled stock picker, your biggest outperformance will probably come from “buying and holding” multi-bagger names. This has been true for us here at Marlin Capital in the 2024 Small Cap 6 (gained +99% in ~7 months) and the SMID Cap 6 (up +82% in <5 months).
