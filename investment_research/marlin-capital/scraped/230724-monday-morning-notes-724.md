# Monday Morning Notes 7.24

Technical Update

We have a huge week ahead with a number of big earnings announcements to come as well as the FOMC decision on interest rates on Wednesday.

As for earnings, the weekly schedule is highlighted by MSFT and GOOGL tomorrow and META on Wednesday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pMkf!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F247a0930-112b-4839-a88d-dd485009eb17_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pMkf!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F247a0930-112b-4839-a88d-dd485009eb17_1920x1080.jpeg)

The FOMC meeting concludes on Wednesday. There is currently a 96% probability that the Fed will hike another 25bps on Wednesday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jqsG!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F402a2d87-d38f-42c8-8fe5-fd2a5dcb9824_2048x1176.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jqsG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F402a2d87-d38f-42c8-8fe5-fd2a5dcb9824_2048x1176.jpeg)

More important than this meeting’s rate hike will be the Fed’s commentary about the future. Is this the last hike of the cycle, or will they leave the door open for another in September?

Technically, the market ran out of steam a bit towards the end of last week. The QQQ broke and closed below its 8 EMA on Thursday and failed to rally back above the level ($378) on Friday.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6UHC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F18c910c4-5cd7-47d6-91c0-a914e1c3b84d_965x723.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6UHC!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F18c910c4-5cd7-47d6-91c0-a914e1c3b84d_965x723.jpeg)

The S&P 500, on the other hand, was the stronger of the 2 indices to close out last week as we saw some rotation into lagging YTD sectors.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2ePp!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9261bbb1-39b7-4465-b8cb-0b7b8090b3e9_961x724.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2ePp!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9261bbb1-39b7-4465-b8cb-0b7b8090b3e9_961x724.jpeg)

Big tech earnings this week should provide a key catalyst for whether or not this rotation continues.

Thus far in earnings season (only ~50 $SPX names have reported so far), vol appears to have been priced too low coming in.

The avg realized move this earnings season has been +/-4.6% vs a +/-3.6% implied move 5 days prior.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sPG_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff9225e9a-eed1-49ed-9464-c4736c96217c_1284x679.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sPG_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff9225e9a-eed1-49ed-9464-c4736c96217c_1284x679.jpeg)

The last time we saw stocks meaningfully outperform their implied moves by this much was Q3 2008.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xCfP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F799f83d6-bbd6-4deb-bc84-ee2c3a2dc292_831x553.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xCfP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F799f83d6-bbd6-4deb-bc84-ee2c3a2dc292_831x553.jpeg)

This will be a theme to continue to watch this week. Big tech earnings are not off to a strong start with TSLA and NFLX last week, but the tide can certainly change this week.

For me, there is nothing to do but watch and observe how the early part of this week unfolds. Tuesday and Wednesday, specifically, are likely to be more volatile sessions than we have recently observed. I’ll be watching to see how the market reacts to these key earnings and Fed decision, as we have some major potential price catalysts right ahead.
