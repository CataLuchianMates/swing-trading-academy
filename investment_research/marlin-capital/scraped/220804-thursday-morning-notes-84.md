# Thursday Morning Notes 8.4

Technical Analysis

The market made a decisive move higher on Wednesday, breaking through and closing above the key $320 resistance level. Now, there is little resistance ahead to stop the QQQ from testing the 200 day moving average ($343).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yVJa!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F09a5de98-0285-4313-8626-5d94e586097d_1218x901.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yVJa!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F09a5de98-0285-4313-8626-5d94e586097d_1218x901.jpeg)

Throughout this Bear market, we have had 1 counter trend rally up to the 200 day moving average (in March). This is where the rally ultimately failed before resuming the downtrend. It appears we are likely headed for our second test of the long term trend line.

With such a big move behind us, any new buys/trades should be kept on a short leash in case of a reversal. At this point in the rally, intraday setups and daytrades offer some of the best potential risk/reward trades. These short term trades will be my point of focus for the rest of the week, but I am in no rush to force anything without the proper setup.
