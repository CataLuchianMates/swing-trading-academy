# 2025 Year-End Update

And then there were 2.

2 trading days remain in 2025.

With New Year’s Eve coming up, it’s a good time to review our current holdings heading into 2026 and what’s coming up next here at Marlin Capital.

#### **2026 Top Picks Portfolio**

We just initiated our [2026 Top Picks Portfolio](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-picks-for-2026) this morning.

35 longs and 2 shorts at equal-weight. As I talked about in [yesterday’s report](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/my-top-picks-for-2026), my plan is to hold these positions for 12+ months (just like the 2025 portfolio). But, being that I believe we are in the later innings of this Bull Market and it is possible we see the cycle top in 2026, this could very well be subject to change at any time.

During market cycle tops, we typically see individual stocks going their own way and peaking/topping before the indices. We saw this type of action during the Dot Com Bust and during the Covid Bust. During the Dot Com Bust, over 40% of the SPX was already at 52 week lows by the time the S&P 500 topped in March 2000. The same thing happened during the 2021-22 market top (the Covid Bust). By the time the S&P 500 topped in January 2022, 66% of the Nasdaq was already in a Bear Market (and 35% of the NDX was down over -50%). Historically, this is how markets top. Not all at once, but one by one.

For this reason, it will require me to manage each position individually in 2026. This is also why it will be especially critical for everyone to follow along throughout the year with this portfolio. This is NOT just a “set it and forget it” portfolio like 2025.

I have received some questions from new subscribers on this portfolio (former portfolio). We [closed out the SMID Cap 6 in full in November](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/portfolio-change-and-performance?utm_source=publication-search) for +64% gains overall.

We have had great success the last 2 years with these portfolios with +99% gains in the ‘24 Small Cap 6 and +64% gains in the ‘25 SMID Cap 6. So, why not run it back again in 2026? The answer is that it’s market willing. There is very much an element of market timing to releasing these types of thematic portfolios. When we initiated both of these portfolios, a big part of my thesis (both times) was that we were setting up for a period of outperformance for Small and Mid Caps. As of now, I do not see the same type of clear skies ahead. I will be covering my views in detail in my 2026 preview (which will be released at the end of this week), but I foresee a much more challenging year ahead.

**Active Trading Portfolio -** We are 100% cash in the Active Trading Portfolio right now. I do not see any reason to press the gas on new trades during this choppy/low volume holiday trading week. It’s the last week of the year and there’s not much action to see. The SPX has been flat for the last 2 months. The QQQ is down -3% over the last 2 months. It has not been a conducive environment for new swing trades. When this is the case, I have no problem simply waiting it out. As Jesse Livermore once said: “There's a time to go long, a time to go short and a time to go fishing”.

For new subscribers, I recommend reading [“September Lull”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/september-lull?utm_source=publication-search) where I give an example of how winning stocks typically move. They do not go straight up every day and usually have long consolidation patterns, which are challenging periods for swing traders. My goal is to be very active during the meat of the trends (like April-October), and passive during the choppy/pullback phases (like October-now).
