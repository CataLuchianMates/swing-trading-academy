# Thursday Morning Notes 3.16

Technical Update

The S&P 500 continued its choppy week yesterday, finishing the day -0.7%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uv5D!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcf38f80f-1d60-47b7-a457-9bf3c012199a_990x726.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uv5D!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcf38f80f-1d60-47b7-a457-9bf3c012199a_990x726.jpeg)

On a technical basis, I remain bearish on the index below the 200 SMA resistance level (and 21 and 60 EMAs).

With put/call ratio spiking to YTD highs late last week and triple quad witching tomorrow, it would be no surprise to see more chop/premium burn for both sides.

We are set for a tiny down this morning. Key support levels below include $3870-$3875, $3850-$3855 and $3800-$3810.

Key resistance levels to watch above include $3900-$3905, $3935-$3940, $3970-$3975, and $3990-$4000.
