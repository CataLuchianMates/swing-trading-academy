# Retracement Day 

After yesterday’s monster rally, we saw some retracement today with the market giving back a chunk of those gains. The S&P 500 finished -3.5% while the QQQ finished -4.3%.

I didn’t think it was possible to witness a “quiet” -4% tape, but after the last few days of action, today certainly felt that way. Volumes normalized a bit after setting a new record in total shares traded in 3 of the last 4 trading sessions (today finished ~23B vs 30B ATH yest).

So what’s next? Let’s dive into the 2 charts I’m really focused on right now:

(1) Total Positioning; and

(2) The VIX

Again, the Total Positioning chart below is the name of the game for me right now. We are currently sitting at -2.5 standard deviations below the 15-year norm. -2.5z total positioning is in the region where every other Bear Market/Market Correction since 2010 has bottomed. Positioning has never gone much lower than this in the last decade and a half (the absolute lowest we’ve gone was -3z in Oct ‘11, which marked that correction bottom).

With the S&P 500 down ~15% from highs and total positioning near 15 year lows, it is tough for me to get overly bearish here. Yes, positioning could still drift lower, (and/or stay extremely light for a prolonged period, like in the 2022 Bear). But let’s not forget, 2022 was a slow motion Bear Market compared to this one. In 2022, the SPX declined -25% over 10 months without a VIX spike above 45. Compare that to today, where we got slammed with a -21% drop in ~2 months (a much more crashy vibe, much like March 2020 or December 2018), and positioning went from ATHs to -2.5z in the blink of an eye.

With positioning so washed out/bearish, it is no surprise that yesterday’s 90 day tariff pause news led to a massive forced buying/repositioning and short covering rally. Yesterday’s short cover bid among Hedge Funds was the largest since August 2015, according to data from Goldman Prime:

> From GS Prime - “Yesterday’s cover bid was really intense. Our PB data showed yesterday as the largest notional net buying in US equities in 10+ years (+4.4 SDs), driven almost entirely by short covers. Yesterday’s notional US short covering was the largest since August 2015 and the 4th largest in the past 15 years (+6.3 Z score). US listed ETF shorts were net covered -4.7% (now +0.5% week/week) - the largest % covering since September 2024 and the 5th largest covering day in the past 5 years.”

In addition to the mechanical/repositioning rally and short squeeze, volatility reset lower. As I [highlighted yesterday](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/bottomed-out), the VIX had its largest single day % drop ever at -36.4%.

The VIX ticked back up +21% today, but remains firmly below Monday’s peak. Tomorrow will be a critical day as we look for volatility to stabilize a bit.

If (and a big if) my thesis that positioning has potentially bottomed and the VIX has (hopefully) peaked is correct, I still lean towards the idea that the market low is probably in. As traders, we work in terms of probabilities and in a market like this, a trader should keep an open mind and not get married to one viewpoint.

With that said, we are certainly not out of the woods yet from a fundamental/economic perspective. While yesterday’s news removed the immediate risk of a catastrophic economic collapse (the recessionary & $220 earnings scenario [I highlighted](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/recession-roulette)), the broader economic picture is still full of risk.

Even with the 90 day tariff delay, the effective tariff rates are currently hovering above ~15% (or higher), which will still be a sizable drag on GDP and Earnings. Tariffs are also potentially spooking CapEx planning, and the damage there may already be done. Heading into Q1 Earnings Season, I expect more companies to pull guidance this quarter. Higher policy uncertainty typically correlates with fewer companies issuing guidance, throughout history.

Yesterday’s face ripping rally and tariff pause also took some heat off the Fed to step in and cut rates, which is a double edged sword. On the one hand, yesterday’s massive bounce reset volatility (and sentiment, to a degree). On the other, the underlying risks of stagflation/sluggish growth with a generally unsupportive Fed remain.

At $5268 SPX, (with fwd earnings around $250 - the current estimate of BofA and Goldman), the market is currently trading at a ~21x fwd P/E on flattish growth (+3% YoY). That’s not exactly a screaming buy for me, and it’s why I certainly don’t see any V shaped market recovery like March 2020 (when the Fed cut rates to 0 and started printing money like candy).

So, what’s my main takeaway? The global trade war induced economic collapse and [$220 EPS recession scenario](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/recession-roulette) is now off the table, which is a huge relief for the market. But the outlook is still muddy and plenty of risks remain. And at a ~21x Fwd P/E, the market is not exactly cheap.

For these reasons, I continue to expect a choppier and continuously volatile market ahead (certainly through Q1 earnings season). With that said, I remain cautiously optimistic that the VIX topped on Monday while positioning has potentially bottomed this week (after yesterday’s forced repositioning rally). If that proves to be the case, I think the worst of this Bear Market is behind us and this week’s lows can hold.
