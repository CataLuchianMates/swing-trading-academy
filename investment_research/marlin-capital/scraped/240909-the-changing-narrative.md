# The Changing Narrative

The S&P 500 recorded its worst week since March 2023 last week as worries over Economic growth weighed on the major indices.  Tech led the decline, with NVDA shedding $300B in market cap on the week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pj0h!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdf13fe2a-cfca-4112-b76c-b105c1500ad4_1284x661.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pj0h!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdf13fe2a-cfca-4112-b76c-b105c1500ad4_1284x661.jpeg)

Conversely, Defensives (Utilities, Consumer Staples, Real Estate) held up better and outperformed on the week.  This has turned into a global theme in Q3 (and even beyond), as Defensive sectors are the top performers in both the US and Europe over the past 1, 3, and 6 months.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wP-9!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd3db3f5d-54dc-4e8a-bde4-8c83a1ac635b_1035x766.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!wP-9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd3db3f5d-54dc-4e8a-bde4-8c83a1ac635b_1035x766.jpeg)

This Defensive outperformance has become even more pronounced in recent days/weeks, as US economic data has generally surprised to the downside.  On Tuesday, the ISM US Manufacturing Index remained firmly in correction territory in August, contracting for the 5th consecutive month (to 47.2).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vJUc!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faaa86cdf-f242-468f-9f42-5778cfcd35ca_863x474.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vJUc!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Faaa86cdf-f242-468f-9f42-5778cfcd35ca_863x474.png)

Historically, a sub-50 ISM number has led to Defensives outperforming, which is exactly what we are seeing now.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3ls0!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2d3376f5-7774-4d66-818c-2d6eaf126e97_1158x420.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3ls0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2d3376f5-7774-4d66-818c-2d6eaf126e97_1158x420.jpeg)

The Soft Landing consensus that has been priced in for much of the last 1-2 years is now being challenged with this recent slew of Economic data.  Some Cyclical sectors (like Semis and Homebuilders) are late to the economic worry party, and remain the most vulnerable to a Hard Landing (as they are pricing in a much stronger ISM number).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HZtF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbf7013e9-8644-43ab-a72f-36ccdf2a1129_568x543.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!HZtF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fbf7013e9-8644-43ab-a72f-36ccdf2a1129_568x543.jpeg)

Relative to its own history, no index is particularly cheap at these levels.  There is little question, in my mind, that a Soft Landing is still priced in at these valuations (despite the recent anxiety and Defensive outperformance).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6ywd!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0deab0b4-80ee-49c4-b917-622e3cdccebd_871x431.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!6ywd!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0deab0b4-80ee-49c4-b917-622e3cdccebd_871x431.jpeg)

Indeed, Recession probabilities are greatly diverging across asset classes, with the Fixed Income market pricing in a much greater chance of a Recession.  There is an arbitrage opportunity here somewhere depending on where you stand in the Hard vs Soft Landing debate.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!P8tK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc8021679-671d-4e5a-a8f9-42b7fae64b37_1284x809.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!P8tK!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc8021679-671d-4e5a-a8f9-42b7fae64b37_1284x809.jpeg)

Friday’s NFP Jobs report added fuel to the Hard Landing fire, with the US economy adding 142k jobs in August (below consensus for 160k).  Moreover (as I pointed out Friday), July’s gains were revised down to 89k, the lowest number since the December 2020 Covid pandemic.

Digging deeper into the August jobs report, the numbers look even worse.  Full time employment dropped by 1M workers YoY in August, marking the 7th consecutive monthly decline.  Permanent job losses are also on the rise, reaching the highest number since November 2021.

This latest bad data led to a “Hard Landing” / Recessionary trade, with huge outperformance in Defensive “Bond Proxy” Equities versus Cyclicals / High Beta stuff getting rinsed.

Adding fuel to the Hard Landing fire, after 565 consecutive sessions inverted, the US 2/10 Yield Curve finally un-inverted last week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pkEH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F85df82fc-81fc-4e3b-a7bd-cfa0118b187a_974x545.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!pkEH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F85df82fc-81fc-4e3b-a7bd-cfa0118b187a_974x545.jpeg)

This was the longest number of consecutive sessions inverted dating back to 1980.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2BLz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fab8bc1a3-7c7f-458c-a3de-ed7064991186_406x241.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2BLz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fab8bc1a3-7c7f-458c-a3de-ed7064991186_406x241.jpeg)

Following 15 historical Yield Curve un-inversions since 1962, the median SPX performance is mixed:

In recent memory, the SPX (red) has underperformed when the Yield Curve steepened.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5XkB!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcdb2dd0a-20bf-4db4-887d-724c862c0c1a_1214x637.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5XkB!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fcdb2dd0a-20bf-4db4-887d-724c862c0c1a_1214x637.jpeg)

The median path of the S&P 500 following un-inversion of the Yield Curve has more often than not been negative, but growth and economic data have been the key factors that have driven these results.  In other words, it’s all about the Economy (Soft Landing vs Hard Landing) from here.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!r-Hk!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc17f60b6-3278-4123-bf27-b6ad0a9d5d2f_571x350.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!r-Hk!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc17f60b6-3278-4123-bf27-b6ad0a9d5d2f_571x350.jpeg)

On a sector level, when the Bull Curve steepens, it is good for Defensives (Health Care, High Margins, Low Volatility).

The bad news for the market is, this was supposed to be the strong(er) part of September (with some systematic tailwinds that I pointed out last week).  We are about to enter the Corporate Buyback Blackout period on September 13th, removing the biggest mechanical buying tailwind in the market.  Moreover, we are nearing the worst 2 week seasonal period on the calendar for the SPX.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Bc-v!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F88ecdc85-6500-43b0-b3ff-e28eb8522d1c_501x330.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Bc-v!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F88ecdc85-6500-43b0-b3ff-e28eb8522d1c_501x330.jpeg)

The Bull Case is that this late September weakness is being frontrun by the market, and so too is the September Fed rate cut.  This could certainly be a risk clearing event if stocks continue to sell off into the FOMC.

With that said, the recent price action in stocks is pretty clear to me, and it’s spelling out the market’s growing concerns for a Hard Landing.  As I pointed out above, I believe a Soft Landing is still being priced in as the consensus view.  This makes the market very vulnerable to any incoming Economic data, as we saw with Friday’s jobs report (leading to a -2.6% drop in the Nasdaq on Fri).  If data continues surprising to the downside, stocks (particularly High Beta) remain very vulnerable here.  The Economic data calendar remains full over the next couple weeks, so I do not believe it is time to push your luck as a trader. At this point, any/all incoming economic data points are potential market moving events depending on where they push the Hard vs Soft Landing scale.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!afd3!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7259388d-ca3f-4c08-8404-c3548dd8bb32_1141x1395.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!afd3!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7259388d-ca3f-4c08-8404-c3548dd8bb32_1141x1395.jpeg)

I expect the market to remain volatile into the Sept. 17th/18th FOMC meeting, which (depending on this week’s price action) could be a temporary risk clearing event for the market.  With that said, I believe now is the time for patience.  Now is not the time to be aggressive as a trader, in my opinion.

I head into this week holding just 1 position (long SN) with a stop at $93.  No planned trades for me in the early part of this week.
