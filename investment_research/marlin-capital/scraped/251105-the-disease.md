# The Disease

US stocks plunged on Tuesday with the SPX -1.2%, NDX -2.1%, and RTY -1.6%.

We continue to see risk off action across the board. Names like PLTR, UBER, SPOT, SHOP, etc. all failed to trade higher despite big earnings beats. Crypto continues to melt down, with Bitcoin having its worst day in the last 6 months (a clear “risk off” signal).

**The Anatomy of a Market Top**

Market weakness spreads like a disease.

At first, it might only be contained to certain groups or sectors. If you’re not watching closely, it’s easy to miss the bearishness creeping in. But make no mistake, the environment has flipped.

One by one, individual stocks and groups begin breaking down. It does not happen all at once. Topping is a process (not an event), and not all stocks peak at the same time.

Throughout history, market tops all begin the same way.

During the topping of the Dot Com Bubble, the most speculative stocks who had nothing more than a business plan (i.e. Pets.com) peaked first. From that point, the weakness continued to spread. Individual names got taken out one by one. **By the time the S&P 500 topped in March 2000, over 40% of the SPX was already at 52 week lows.**

The same thing happened in 2021-22 with SPACs, ZM, ARKK, etc. peaking many months before the S&P 500.Pre-revenueSPACs (most now bankrupt) began peaking 14+ months before the SPX did. ZM peaked 14 months before the SPX. ARKK peaked 10 months before the SPX.

**By the time the S&P 500 topped in January 2022, 66% of the Nasdaq was already in a Bear Market (and 35% of the NDX was down over -50%).** I wrote about this rapidly deteriorating breadth way back in December 2021 in [“Wild Times in the Market”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/is-this-market-crazy-or-is-it-just?utm_source=publication-search). This report was written about 1 week before the SPX topped and we entered the 2022 Bear Market. Most every stock had already topped for the cycle at that point.

Once again, the same thing played out during the Q1 2025 tariff tank (prior to the SPX’s -20% drawdown). In “Red Flags Emerge”, written in late February, I talked about how Semis and Small Caps were beginning to break down while the market was still just a stone’s throw away from ATHs (along with many retail favorites like HIMS, RKLB, OKLO, etc. which would go on to drop -50% +).

As I often say, it’s a market of stocks. To assess the overall health of the market, you must look at the entire stock universe. You must look underneath the surface to get the full picture.

This is precisely why we have been so cautious here at Marlin Capital for the last month +. When breadth starts to deteriorate and we see stocks (especially former leaders) begin to break down, it is no time to play around. For me, deteriorating breadth is a major concern and must be treated as such.

**My Big Picture View**

It has been 6 straight green months for the SPX. A 5-10% mini correction here is my base case. Underneath the surface, most stocks are already WELL beyond that point.

After that mini-correction, I am expecting the SPX to recover back to highs and have at least 1 more leg up to my $7300 price target. I do not think this Bull Market is over, especially in the indices.

With that said, as I talked about in “Weakness”, I believe many individual stocks have already topped for the cycle (OKLO, IONQ, etc, etc). If we do get the 5-10% SPX correction I am currently anticipating, these names will likely continue to get hit hard.

Then, when the SPX recovers back to highs, these stocks will bounce big and retrace some of their drawdowns. This will lead many to say that the bottom is in for these names and that they are on their way back to highs (the classic Bull trap scenario). Instead, I anticipate they will end up forming a lower high. This is all part of the topping process.

Again, I believe many stocks have already peaked for this cycle. They are currently in their Bear cycles, and a big bounce/short squeeze (often >50% + is all part of that Bear cycle). This is what makes shorting so difficult (but, we will be shorting when the time is right).

One thing is for certain.

The easy dollar environment we enjoyed from May-September is over.
