# Heading into June

May was a big month for US stocks with the Nasdaq (+9%) leading the charge higher, its best month since November 2023.

In my view, the stock market rally in May was largely driven by:

(1) Better than expected Q1 earnings

(2) Positioning tailwinds (discretionary & systematic funds were very underweight and have been forced to chase)

(3) Tariff news relief (no major tape bombs)

The positioning dynamic is one that I have pounded the table on here at Marlin Capital throughout 2025. The velocity of moves in total positioning have been nothing short of historic this year.

So, where do we stand now positioning-wise?

Per Goldman Prime Book, US equities were net bought for a 4th consecutive week last week (+1.5 SDs), driven primarily by long buys. Last week’s notional net buying in US stocks was the largest since last November (97th percentile vs history), suggesting Hedge Funds are fully chasing this rally higher now.

Tech was by far the most bought global sector last week. Hedge Funds net bought US Tech stocks for a 3rd straight week and every trading session last week, driven by risk-on flows. Last week’s notional long buying in Tech was the largest in more than 10+ years (+1.6 z score).

Again, it is very much worth noting that the recent streak of buying in Tech came after the sector was net sold in 10 of the previous 12 weeks. Even after these “re-leveraging” flows, Goldman’s Total Positioning measure still sits at -1.0 (in light territory) as we head into June.

My key takeaway - light Hedge Fund/Mutual Fund positioning should remain a real tailwind for the market going forward (though not quite as big of a tailwind as it was in May). Still, this is a welcome dynamic for bulls heading into June.

Another bullish catalyst positioning-wise will be corporate buybacks. 2025 has already seen a record high $ number of share repurchase programs authorized.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dqiz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F37c5b3bf-37be-4029-9613-b6cea6ca143e_574x425.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!dqiz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F37c5b3bf-37be-4029-9613-b6cea6ca143e_574x425.png)

With the corporate blackout window now ending following Q1 earnings season, buybacks will be a key bullish catalyst for the market this summer (primarily in June & July).

As mentioned, Q1 earnings were largely better than expected. The Mag 7 just posted its best quarterly EPS surprise since Q3 of 2023.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NgJr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffb700902-34c8-4968-8d07-68608f089a32_790x643.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NgJr!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffb700902-34c8-4968-8d07-68608f089a32_790x643.jpeg)

With all this said, it is worth noting that May’s stellar returns were front-loaded with the NDX now flat over the last 13 trading sessions. It has shifted to a rangebound tape for the past couple weeks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!664m!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1ccc9629-7e02-4def-898c-03580779aa7e_1172x658.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!664m!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1ccc9629-7e02-4def-898c-03580779aa7e_1172x658.jpeg)

This undoubtedly raises some questions about the path forward from here after a big rally off April lows.

In my view, I view this recent index consolidation as bullish for the market. Following a big rally, it is healthy to get some backfilling/consolidation. This is exactly what we have seen over the past couple weeks.

As I talked about on Friday, leading stocks are ultimately the best gauge of market health. We have already seen a number of leading stocks break out to ATHs before the market (4 of which I hold in APH, SPOT, HOOD, and DUOL). I remain bullish here, and believe that we are likely setting up for a break higher from this trading range. I am positioned for a break higher in the market here.
