**Market Feedback Tells All**

As I have talked about before, success does not hinge on predicting the market’s next move. It comes from understanding probabilities and responding to the real-time feedback the market gives you.

The feedback on my trades tells me all I need to know. **When my trades are making money and setups are plentiful, I place more trades. When my trades are faltering/not gaining any traction, or losing, I pull back on entering new trades.**

The feedback on my trades tells me how aggressive I should be. No predictions necessary.

Recent feedback on my trades have all been negative. So, I have responded to that critical market feedback. How? I have traded much lighter in the past 3+ weeks with only a 3 new tester positions along the way. Because of this, our drawdown has been very controlled in this portfolio.

#### General Market Analysis

One reason this feels like Bear Market price behavior to me, is because it is.

Over the last 8 trading sessions, 115+ stocks in the S&P 500 have declined by -7% or more in a single day. These kinds of extreme short-term “damage” days have **\*only\*** occurred during major Bear Markets since 2000.

In fact, the average SPX drawdown when this happens is -34%. We are currently just ~2% from ATHs right now.

Look at the chart above closely. Every cluster of red dots you see corresponds with a major Bear Market since 2000.

These clustered, sharp declines in individual names signal serious deterioration in the market’s health. As mentioned, it is historically Bear Market behavior. We have 0 examples (in the last 26+ years) of these “red dot” clusters triggering during Bull Market dips/routine market corrections.

Bear Market price behavior tends to be very specific, so this is a **big warning sign** for me.

Another big warning sign is a cluster of Hindenburg Omen’s, which we now have (4+ in the last month).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K4JO!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feb963020-f61b-449f-a7c8-1a932e2ff4d2_2048x1310.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!K4JO!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feb963020-f61b-449f-a7c8-1a932e2ff4d2_2048x1310.jpeg)

I talked about Hindenburg Omen’s in [“Mixed Market”.](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/mixed-market-a68?utm_source=publication-search) It is a technical indicator that signals major market weakness underneath the surface. It is triggered when a significant number of stocks hit both 52-week highs and 52-week lows simultaneously (indicating weak market breadth).

Historically, forward returns are extremely weak following these signals. The past 30 times this happened, the SPX fell 63% of the time over the next 6 months for an average return of -1.43%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-S4r!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5e197af-92a5-4772-aed3-a43142ea7f82_1496x1778.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-S4r!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc5e197af-92a5-4772-aed3-a43142ea7f82_1496x1778.jpeg)
