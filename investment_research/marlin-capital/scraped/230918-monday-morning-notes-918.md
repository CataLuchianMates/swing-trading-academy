# Monday Morning Notes 9.18

Technical Update

The S&P 500 sold off on high volume to conclude last week (-1.22%).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JCgx!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F96aeb4f9-2b66-4fde-974e-124f2e8cbdfd_962x721.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JCgx!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F96aeb4f9-2b66-4fde-974e-124f2e8cbdfd_962x721.jpeg)

August/September has been characterized by lots of choppy price action in the indices. With that said, the current technical structure leans bearish as the SPX has been rejected twice at lower highs. It still has not decisively broken below the 60 EMA ($4430), however, so we have not seen the selling accelerate into any type of waterfall.

Key support levels to watch below include $4420-$4430, $4400, and $4330-$4350.

Key resistance levels to watch above include $4470-$4480, $4500, and $4540-$4550.

I remain holding COUR and CLS into this week with no changes.

COUR - stop on final 2/3rds at $17.32

CLS - stop placed below $22.4

Nothing else to do for me until we exit this chop/consolidation zone.

Looking ahead, all eyes turn to the September FOMC meeting on Wednesday, which will be the main focus of this week. The Fed is widely expected to hit the pause button for the 2nd time this year. This is the main catalyst to watch for this week.

---

Trading Flows: Overall US equities were net sold for a 2nd straight week (-0.3 SDs vs. the past year), driven by short sales outpacing long buys 1.5 to 1. US short flow has ticked up in 4 of the past 5 weeks on the Prime book, and cumulative short flow ended the week at a new YTD high (+22%) driven by single stock shorts
