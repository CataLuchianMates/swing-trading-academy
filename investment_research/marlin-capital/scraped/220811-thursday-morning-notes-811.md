# Thursday Morning Notes 8.11

Technical Analysis:

It was a huge day for the market yesterday after July CPI came in lower than expected. The QQQ gained 2.8% on the day, and closed right at the recent highs of $326.

Financial conditions were supportive of this rally - most notably, the dollar weakened considerably in one of its biggest single day declines in 2 decades. Future rate hikes were priced out of the rate futures market - now pricing in a peak of around 3.4.%. We had 2 Fed speakers today post CPI release - Evans and Kashkari. Kashkari reiterated his rate hike forecast for 3.9% and 4.4% for next year. Evans anticipates a rate of 3.25-3.5% at the end of this year, and 3.75% to 4% at the end of next year. The market does not see it that way - now pricing in an implied rate of 2.5% at the end of 2023, with rate cuts beginning in early 2023. Big picture, the re-pricing lower will be quick if the market learns that the Federal Reserve will not be cutting rates at the beginning of 2023.

On a technical basis, yesterday’s rally negated the recent pullback and we closed near recent highs. **For the QQQ, I believe we are likely headed for a test of the 200 day moving average ($342), like we saw during March’s rally. This is my target range for the TQQQ long trade ($40-$42).**
