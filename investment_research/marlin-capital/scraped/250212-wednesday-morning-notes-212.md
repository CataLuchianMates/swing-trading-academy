# Wednesday Morning Notes 2.12

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Flattish tape yesterday (SPX +0.03%, NDX -0.29%) with all eyes on this morning’s CPI report.

CPI 0.5% MoM, Exp. 0.3%

CPI Core 0.4% MoM, Exp. 0.3%

CPI 3.0% YoY, Exp. 2.9%

CPI Core 3.3% YoY, Exp. 3.1%

January CPI inflation RISES to 3.0%, above expectations of 2.9%.

Core CPI inflation RISES to 3.3%, above expectations of 3.1%.

This is obviously a worrisome inflation report for bulls. January was the largest monthly increase in CPI since August 2023.

After this morning’s report, the market is pricing in just 1 Fed rate cut in 2025 (in October) and just 1 in 2026 (December ‘26).

The indices gapped down over -1% following this morning’s inflation report, but those losses have already been (more than) cut in half an hour into Wednesday’s trading session.

Despite today’s hotter than expected CPI report, the playbook very much remains the same to me here (and the action in the 1st hour is proving it again): Buy weakness, sell strength, take profits quick. We are still very much in “[Chop City](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/chop-city)”.

Active Portfolio -

As I talked about on Monday, it has been difficult to gain any traction on swing trades in this choppy market environment. I sold the rest of my VIAV long yesterday at the close for a +2.6% gain and closed out AFRM this morning for a +1.4% gain. I am now just holding my DOCS dip buy from yesterday (at $75.94).

In a choppy market environment like this, I will continue to look to size smaller (<10% position sizes), take gains (and losses) quicker, and stay nimble.
