# Thursday Morning Notes 11.3

Technical Analysis & Quick Thoughts

The Fed raised interest rates by 75 BPs for November, as anticipated. While the statement originally was interpreted as dovish, Powell poured water on the fire during the press conference. The Fed Chair emphasized how far a pivot/pause in policy remains.

Perhaps the most interesting moment of the press conference occurred when a journalist wrongly mislead Powell into thinking the market was still rallying (it was not). The Fed Chair appeared frustrated and listed only reasons why this is the wrong interpretation.

Investors expecting some type of pivot in Fed policy at yesterday’s meeting must now face the music. Jerome Powell was clear and concise with his message, putting the made up idea of a pivot to rest.

So, where does that leave the market? Although there was plenty of optimism over a pivot, the futures markets weren’t really buying it. Following yesterday’s FOMC meeting, Fed funds futures are about 15 BPs higher at the peak.

Technically, yesterday’s move was very bearish. We broke all major key support levels on high volume.

The next key support to watch is 52 week lows ($255-$260). It is still possible to see an end of year rally if this re-test holds (or if we make a higher low). If support breaks, look out below.

Put/Call ratio is significantly elevated after yesterday’s action, and now at its highest level since 9/23. This adds to the complications of trading this market and should add to the volatility.

US Economics - FOMC – Slower pace, higher destination @ CITI -

The overall takeaway from the FOMC is hawkish with Chair Powell explicitly stating he would prefer to overtighten, and have to subsequently back off, than to under-tighten and potentially let inflation get out of control. Changes to statement language indicate the Fed has a strong preference to slow the pace of rate hikes (to 50bp in December in our base case), but balanced any potential dovishness by reasserting that policy rates can continue to rise to levels sufficient to quell too-high inflation – and likely need to rise to above levels contemplated in September. We are revising up our expectations for Fed policy rates and project a 50bp hike in December, another 50bp in February, 25bp in March, and a final 25bp May resulting in a terminal rate of 5.25-5.50%
