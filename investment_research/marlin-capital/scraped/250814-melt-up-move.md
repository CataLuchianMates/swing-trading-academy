# Melt Up Move 

The market is starting to show some serious momentum post-CPI.

The S&P 500 just posted back-to-back 80% breadth days for the first time since 4/11-4/14.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Urv9!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F76388e9d-9cea-4193-a951-db6100a351b8_603x374.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Urv9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F76388e9d-9cea-4193-a951-db6100a351b8_603x374.png)

This kind of broad market strength often points to big moves ahead. Remember what I said in [“Day 3”](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/day-3) on April 24th about strong breadth?

> Over the past 10 days, we have witnessed 2 days of exceptional market breadth, with over 96% of S&P 500 stocks advancing. Historically, this has been a very bullish signal for future returns.
>
> Remember, it’s a market of stocks. When 96% of stocks are being bought, this is no doubt extremely bullish. The historical data confirms this as well, as strong breadth (especially the Zweig Breadth Thrust) has been an incredibly accurate indicator for future returns.

We saw another day of Small and Mid Cap outperformance post-CPI, with the IWM +1.9% and the MDY +1.5%.

This strength across the board has me excited about the potential for market gains ahead. Tons of stocks are setting up for big runs post-Q2 earnings, in my view. Could we start to get that late ‘99 Dot Com Bubble style melt up I talked about in [Re-Rating Season](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/re-rating-season)?
