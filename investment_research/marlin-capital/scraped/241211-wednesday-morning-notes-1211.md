# Wednesday Morning Notes 12.11

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Quiet/choppy action in the indices yesterday ahead of this morning’s CPI report, with the SPX closing -0.3% and NDX -0.34%.

The price action more or less felt like some follow through from Monday’s momentum drawdown with the large majority of the index strength coming from TSLA, GOOGL, and META. GOOGL was 1 of the best performing stocks in the S&P after the company discussed progress made in quantum computing using its new chip.

**CPI Report**

US CPI YoY Actual 2.7% (Forecast 2.7%, Previous 2.6%)

US Core CPI YoY Actual 3.3% (Forecast 3.3%, Previous 3.3%)

US CPI MoM Actual 0.3% (Forecast 0.3%, Previous 0.2%)

US Core CPI MoM Actual 0.3% (Forecast 0.3%, Previous 0.3%)

Headline CPI came in at 2.7% YoY, in line with expectations. Core CPI also met expectations.

The market was perhaps feeling some anxiety that the number would come in higher than expected, but that risk was alleviated with this morning’s report.

Similar to how Friday’s NFP report hit the “sweet spot” for stocks, the same appears to be true with this morning’s CPI report. There was some anxiety that CPI would come in hotter than expectations, but also some worry that a much cooler number than anticipated would point to a significantly slowing economy. With today’s in-line number, both those fears went out the window (at least, for now) and the December Fed rate cut has now been all but confirmed (98% probability now).
