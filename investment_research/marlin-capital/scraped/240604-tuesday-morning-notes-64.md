# Tuesday Morning Notes 6.4

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Choppy tape to kick off the week yesterday with the S&P 500 closing +0.11% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KV9F!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F277602ca-70c4-41da-8241-42313a02e1f7_1209x887.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KV9F!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F277602ca-70c4-41da-8241-42313a02e1f7_1209x887.jpeg)

NDX & SPX > Equal Weights thanks to NVDA +5% on unveiling its next-gen Rubin AI platform during the Computex Conference in Taiwan yesterday.  Lighter ISM Manufacturing caused the US 10 YR to ease 10bps to 4.39%.  Questions around economic growth are starting to rise with cyclicals weighing on the indices.  Goldman lowered their Q2 GDP tracking estimate by 0.1pp to +2.7%.

Overall, 328/500 (66%) S&P names finished lower.  We saw a continuation of last Thursday/Friday’s action with pain visible on both long and short sides.  Momentum -3% in the last 3 sessions with little to no support in oversold pockets (think Software, Payments, etc.).  Low quality shorts +113bps (GME +80% intraday at 1 point after Roaring Kitty disclose his position) and out Biotech +265bps (beneficiary of lower bond yields).

NVO triggered me long yesterday on the break of $136.  I will be looking to add to my position on a daily close above $136.
