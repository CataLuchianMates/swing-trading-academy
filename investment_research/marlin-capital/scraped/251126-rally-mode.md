For me, the market showed its hand today.

And so did big money. More on that below…

As Marlin Capital readers know, I am a big fan of monthly charts. Monthly charts [helped us get bearish in October](https://marlincapital.substack.com/p/risk-off-a70) when names like OKLO, IONQ, etc were forming huge shooting stars/topping candles.

When looking at monthly charts, the close of the month is critical. In fact, you can expand this to any time frame for technical analysis. How the candle closes is the most important tell for me. It doesn’t matter if its 5m, 1 HR, 1D, 1W, or 1M. The close of the candle is the #1 thing that I look at.

This is why I believe the real action comes at the close. Again, you can expand this to any time frame. On the 1 HR chart, the real action comes in the last 10-15 minutes of the candle. On the daily time frame, the real action comes in the last hour. And on the monthly chart, the real action comes in the final few days of the month.

As I have talked about before, I like to think of price action as a battle between buyers and sellers. Each candle is its own unique battle. How the battle ends is the most important factor. Which side is finishing stronger at the end? Which side is finishing with momentum?

For the S&P 500, with this current rally off Friday’s lows, we are heading for a strong close to November.

This tells me that buyers are regaining control right when they need to (at the end of the month). In my view, this end of November rally bodes well for December. As long as we close out this month strong (only 1.5 trading days left in Nov), I now see December as more likely to be an up month. And that would mean that the lows are in for this mini-correction on the SPX.

But most importantly, in recent days, the Hedge Fund buying finally expanded beyond healthcare. On Friday and Monday, it was NOT just Healthcare. Hedge Funds were BIG buyers, and they were buying EVERYTHING.

Hedge Funds significantly bought US equities on Friday and Monday (+2.2 SDs Friday and +1.6 SDs Monday). In cumulative $ terms, the 2-day pace of net buying combined was the largest over any 2-day period in more than 6 months and one of the largest in the past 2 years.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_7IJ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F449affab-9247-4b18-9964-13a4eacc1f30_1284x889.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!_7IJ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F449affab-9247-4b18-9964-13a4eacc1f30_1284x889.jpeg)

Tech was by far the most $ net bought US sector over these 2 sessions, driven by long buys and to a lesser extent short covers (4 to 1). Most subsectors were net bought, led by Semis and Hardware.

Hedge Funds are now getting back in the water across the board (from underexposed levels), which is a major tailwind for stocks.

**My Strategy**

At this point in the cycle (I will be talking more about this in my December preview report), I believe it is crucial to focus on the strongest names. These are the names that will have the potential for a real blowoff top if the SPX hits my big picture target ($7300+).

With the month ending, it should be easier to spot these leaders. The strongest names are all setting up to close well off their November lows. The strongest names are forming bullish handle candles on the monthly chart. If you are looking at a name and it’s NOT closing the month well off November lows, then it is NOT a leader.

The good thing about this mini-market correction is that it came during Q3 earnings season. This gave us a good chance to see which earnings gaps the market liked most originally. And now, we are able to see which of those earnings names held up the best during this recent market weakness.

Along those lines, 2 names are really standing out to me here.

**VIAV**

VIAV was a top earnings name that held up like a champ during the recent market weakness. The stock barely broke its day 1 earnings low and never filled its gap (or really came close).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-wf9!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb5a813c6-7a8f-42a7-99ac-726ad638a9ad_1266x844.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!-wf9!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb5a813c6-7a8f-42a7-99ac-726ad638a9ad_1266x844.png)

Earnings gappers like VIAV had ample opportunity to fill those gaps during this recent market weakness. For ones that didn’t, I view this as a display of strong demand.

Now, VIAV is already back above its Day 1 earnings VWAP.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KPll!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6fed2646-709f-4a34-9601-a684cee3a8e4_1566x846.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KPll!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6fed2646-709f-4a34-9601-a684cee3a8e4_1566x846.png)

As I have said before, when the dip in a leading stock gets immediately bought up, it means demand for the stock is extremely high. This is a common theme in leading stocks throughout history: dips don’t last long.

Bulls are clearly in control in VIAV. This stock is emerging as a clear leader here for me.

1 HR VWAP support at $17.0-$17.15 would be the first potential buy area for me.

Semis are another group that are showing relative strength right now. TER was a leading Q3 earnings name that held up well during the recent market weakness.

Similar to VIAV, TER never filled its earnings gap during the market selloff. This is a display of strong demand in a leading name.
