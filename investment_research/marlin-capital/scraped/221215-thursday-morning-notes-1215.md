# Thursday Morning Notes 12.15

Quick FOMC Recap & Technical Analysis

The FOMC meeting went as expected yesterday, with the Fed raising rates 50 BPs and hinting at a 25 BP hike next. In the press conference, Powell was indeed more hawkish than many anticipated. The disconnect between the Fed’s dot plot and market pricing continues, with the market still pricing in rate cuts in the 2nd half of 2023.

In my view, rate cuts are not realistic in the spring of 2023 (as the market implies) unless there is severe economic damage. Such economic damage is not priced into stocks with EPS expected to grow in 2023.

The market reaction was muted, with the indices closing near unchanged on the day. There is often a delayed reaction to Fed meetings, and we are set for a sizable gap down this morning.

Technically, the false range breakout and higher low setup remains a possibility.

The key level to watch on this downside for the SPX is $3965-$3970 (yesterday’s low). A break below could send us towards the low of our month long range ($3900-$3930). If this crucial support level breaks, my target is the unfilled November CPI gap to $3749.

The key levels I will be watching on the downside include $4030 (high of range) along with $4055-$4060, which is the current higher low pivot that was made yesterday.

I will be releasing a detailed analysis on the FOMC, CPI, and what it all means for the market by the end of this week.
