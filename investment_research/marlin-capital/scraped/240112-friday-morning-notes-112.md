# Friday Morning Notes 1.12

**Daily Market Commentary:**

The S&P 500 finished flat on Thursday following the CPI report.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yCIh!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3b17e4d9-c831-44b7-b975-3ca54dcd24bb_1192x891.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yCIh!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3b17e4d9-c831-44b7-b975-3ca54dcd24bb_1192x891.jpeg)

Investors and traders alike were sitting on their hands all week coming into yesterday’s CPI report.  The numbers were right down the middle with a very slight firm tilt, though not enough to significantly change anything.  Core CPI rose 0.31%, 1 BP above consensus expectations and compared to +0.28% in November.

The market reversed off the lows mid session (closed just below unchanged after being down -90bps), though it was quiet despite the move / very little offense played with traders not feeling pressed to do much as Q4 earnings season officially begins.

TNA finished -2.31%, but well off the lows of the day and closed just below the key $35 support level I discussed in yesterday’s morning notes.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VWcd!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fae847c4a-a110-4d43-aa15-747a05e8e34c_1057x783.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!VWcd!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fae847c4a-a110-4d43-aa15-747a05e8e34c_1057x783.jpeg)

TNA did not break the previous days highs of $36.10, so there was no fill on yesterday’s long trade idea.
