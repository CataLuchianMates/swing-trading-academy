# The Main Event

Last week was a volatile one for US stocks, with an early week sell off giving way to a big snapback rally to end the week. Markets continue to power through some clear headwinds (govt shutdown, China/Tariffs, weak jobs, etc), with the indices rallying back to all-time highs.

So, what’s driving this market resilience, and what’s next?

Detailed analysis below…

I just mentioned some of the clear market headwinds, but there are a few major market tailwinds here as well.

3 key bullish tailwinds stick out to me right now:

**1.) Of course, it all starts with Artificial Intelligence. The driver of this entire Bull Market.**

This week is a HUGE one for the AI theme with 5 of the Mag 7 (GOOGL, META, MSFT, AAPL, and AMZN) set to report (+ GLW and others).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uXfG!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5016bd7d-a44a-41a9-8fc0-364891cbc228_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uXfG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5016bd7d-a44a-41a9-8fc0-364891cbc228_1920x1080.jpeg)

I will be most interested in CapEx trends from the heavyweights like GOOGL, MSFT, and META. Their AI spending guidance/updates will set the tone for the whole AI trade. A strong CapEx outlook could ignite the next leg higher for AI names, while any hint of moderation could spark a pullback. Personally, I am not expecting any hint of moderation here. Either way, we have a MASSIVE week ahead of us.

**2.) Second is the Fed (and most all global Central Banks). Don’t Fight the Central Banks**

We are currently in the midst of the largest global easing cycle since the post-GFC recovery in 2010.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TlkM!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F961ae635-12ef-44f9-a6d0-7da819fd4cdd_549x372.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!TlkM!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F961ae635-12ef-44f9-a6d0-7da819fd4cdd_549x372.jpeg)

History is very clear as to what comes next. As long as there is no recession, stocks typically always climb higher in this environment.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WrEs!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F880722e4-6715-42e5-b3a7-a1657250658d_1215x847.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!WrEs!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F880722e4-6715-42e5-b3a7-a1657250658d_1215x847.jpeg)

With monetary policy easing globally, the path of least resistance for equities remains up.

**3.) Third is total market positioning, which remains a bullish tailwind here.**

Total market positioning (-0.2z) is still slightly underweight here.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1dGq!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1c1df4d4-4e2a-4ace-a8fd-ce835982437a_1284x1305.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1dGq!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1c1df4d4-4e2a-4ace-a8fd-ce835982437a_1284x1305.jpeg)

This is nowhere near the frothy levels (>2z) we’ve seen at prior bull market tops since 2015.

This tells me there is still plenty more room for buying before we hit the kind of bullish overcrowding that signals major market peaks.

#### **Active Trading Portfolio**

I’m satisfied with how we’ve navigated the Active Trading portfolio through the recent market volatility. We took a couple small losses on 2 feeler trades (ARM, GLXY) that didn’t pan out, but we didn’t force new trades or chase.

My goal is not to time the exact bottom or be the first to dive back in the pool. For me, it’s about staying disciplined, sidestepping drawdowns, and staying ready for when the market turns. I want the charts to force me back in, not the other way around.

As I talked about last week, there are times where it’s easy to make money and there are times where it’s equally easy to lose money. If the market has really turned here, we could be entering another “easy dollar” environment. If not, than I will have missed out on nothing but a false breakout. As Livermore says, “wait for the trend to develop, then hop on”.
