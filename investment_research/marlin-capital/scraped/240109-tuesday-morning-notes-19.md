# Tuesday Morning Notes 1.9

**Daily Market Commentary:**

US stocks bounced back to kick off the week yesterday with the S&P, Nasdaq, and R2K closing +1.4%, +2.1%, and 1.9% respectively.

After many scratched their heads on the move lower last week, it did feel as if we were due for a snapback.  Strength was fueled by (1) renewed optimism in Semis (+3.5% yesterday, best 1D move since early November) as the Consumer Electronic Show (CES) takes place in Las Vegas throughout this week (AMD, NVDA, INTC, QCOM all slated to speak + sell side meetings with large portion of the semi complex).  A technical breakout in NVDA fueled the fire with the stock breaking $500 and hitting a new ATH after failing at that level 5+ times last year.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!f3-J!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F524289e3-6b1b-4aa1-985b-3900e9fb259a_1212x894.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!f3-J!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F524289e3-6b1b-4aa1-985b-3900e9fb259a_1212x894.jpeg)

(2) Lorie Logan QT remarks over the weekend. (3) Consumer Discretionary caught a bid with ONON bouncing +6% on speculation that Tiger Woods could leave Nike for ON Holdings (ONON CEO later said no plans to partner with Tiger); and (4) Bitcoin +6% added to the hype as the market awaits SEC approval and the ETF launch.

We enter Tuesday’s session with the SPX now 69bps from its ATH ($4796, hit on 1/3/22 – 2+ years ago).  Yields retreated as much as 7bps intraday yesterday (US 10YR finished the day around 4%).  The unofficial start of the Q4 ’23 earnings season will kick off Friday (BLK, BK, BAC, WFC, JPM, C).  Consensus forecasts for S&P 500 Q4 profits will rise by +3% YoY, driven by +3% sales growth and 14bp of margin contraction.

We are set for an immediate gap down this morning after yesterday’s strong action and close, highlighting the choppiness we have seen early in 2024.  For the SPX, key support is at $4720-$4730, $4700, and $4680.  These levels should hold if yesterday’s move is to be any indication of a larger rally ahead.  If today’s action is constructive following this morning’s gap down, we will find ourselves with some nice potential long setups heading into the end of the week.  First, I will want to see how the indices react to today’s gap down and key support levels.
