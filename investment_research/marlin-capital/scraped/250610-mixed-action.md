# Mixed Action

Rangebound session to kick off the week yesterday with the SPX +0.09%, NDX +0.17%, and RTY leading the way +0.7%. Underneath the hood, the action remains somewhat mixed with highly shorted/lower quality names leading in recent sessions.

Goldman’s Megacap Tech (the Mag 7) vs. Non-Profitable Tech pair is now down 5 days in a row and is having its worst 5-day stretch of performance in >3 months (down -4.5% over 5 days).

Goldman’s Most Short Basket is now up +16% over the last month (+11% in last 5 sessions), the best performing basket on both time frames. The 2nd best performing basket over the last week is TMT 12M Laggards (+7%) as traders look for possible catch up trades. Some of the best performers so far in June include names like MU, ETSY, PINS, MCHP, etc. (former laggards), whereas names that were previously leading are down/underperforming on the month (PLTR, CEG, DUOL, MELI, etc.).

As I have highlighted, this move has felt driven by a combination of more resilient Macro data (ISM + NFP last week) and institutional re-positioning (HF net leverage +1.6pts in June; CTAs have net bought ~$30B in US equities over the last month). Despite this recent squeeze, we are not yet at aggressive short squeeze territory just yet.

To reiterate, arguably the biggest factor of all driving this rally off post-Liberation Day lows has been sharply declining recession odds. Polymarket recession odds have fallen off a cliff, from ~68% after Liberation Day to now ~26%. The upside move in stocks is consistent with a recession being priced out/a non-recessionary Bear Market rebound. Historically, market rebounds off major, non-recessionary pullbacks have been powerful.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QH9q!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd6bebe71-256f-4273-ac68-ce4cb21e90eb_1081x670.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QH9q!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd6bebe71-256f-4273-ac68-ce4cb21e90eb_1081x670.jpeg)

Alongside the sharply declining recession odds we have seen, earnings revision breadth has turned positive for the first time all year. With no imminent recession on the horizon, analysts are realizing their future estimates may have been revised too low during the Q1 Tariff Tank.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OJc8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F528f7787-63aa-4922-9abe-490956c69484_706x572.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OJc8!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F528f7787-63aa-4922-9abe-490956c69484_706x572.jpeg)

Seasonality - We are in a historically strong calendar period for stocks. 4 of the last 5 June’s have finished positive for an average of +1.12%. Each of the last 5 July’s have finished positive (and 17 of the last 18 for the NDX), for an average gain of +4.23% (the 2nd best avg month of the year behind Nov.).
