# The Final Stretch

The S&P 500 rallied 1% last week, its 4th consecutive weekly gain and the market’s first such winning streak since NVDA kicked off the AI boom in late May.

Month-to-date, the SPX is +8.7%, NDX +10.9%, and RTY +8.7%.  From a factor/thematic perspective, Tech has led the way in November (as it has all year).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7A5z!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F33cf4bbc-513d-4af1-a883-4d01d172c2d5_1104x372.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7A5z!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F33cf4bbc-513d-4af1-a883-4d01d172c2d5_1104x372.jpeg)

We did not learn a whole lot last week.  Economic data was mixed.  Unemployment claims were lower (a sign of economic resiliency), durable goods were weak, and Global PMIs were just above 50 (goldilocks economy).  According to Mastercard Spendingpulse, US Black Friday sales rose +2.5% this year.

The 10-YR Treasury inched higher, finishing the week at 4.47% (up from 4.44%, and it was as low as 4.39%).  Since it was illiquid/holiday oriented trading, we can probably ignore most of last week’s moves as just noise.

We also had NVDA earnings, the leader in AI technology.  NVDA itself is down slightly since last week’s earnings, but the market as a whole has not suffered.  That, along with the ongoing outperformance of the equal weighted indices and Russell 2000, is worth noting.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OD6S!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff2441116-d132-4452-a9a5-69ee3f272259_1070x593.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OD6S!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff2441116-d132-4452-a9a5-69ee3f272259_1070x593.jpeg)

Since November 9th, the Russell 2000 index of small caps has led the way, followed by the equal weighted indices.  Again, it is worth noting that the R2K and equal weighted indices have been laggards/underperforming throughout 2023 (but that has changed over the last 3 weeks).

The VIX, Wall Street’s Fear Gauge, has plummeted to 2 year lows amid the recent market rally.  This is an indication that there is no fear from market participants currently, which bodes well for the rest of 2023.  We will likely need to see some type of catalyst (economic, credit, etc.) to see fear re-introduced to the market and the VIX spike significantly.

Valuations – the rally in equities has not only brought us back to multi-month highs in terms of prices, but also int terms of valuation as well.   Across 8 different measures, the median valuation metric trades in its 89th percentile relative to history.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!W3AN!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fac27919e-0800-42d2-bf1f-b482b77f145f_752x650.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!W3AN!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fac27919e-0800-42d2-bf1f-b482b77f145f_752x650.jpeg)

Earnings – the high starting point for valuations combined with minimal EPS growth projected for 2024 leave an uninspiring overall outlook on a risk-adjusted basis.

Hedge fund positioning -

Info tech and communication services were the most net sold sectors on the US prime book last week, driven almost entirely by long sales.  Last week’s net selling US TMT was the largest since July and executed long supply was the largest in nearly 8 months.

Software, Semis, and Interactive Media were by far the most net sold subsectors, which outweighed buying in IT services and media.

Aggregate US TMT long/short ratio ended the week at 2.04, which is in the 3rd percentile vs the past year and in the 1st percentile vs the past 5 years.

TMT stocks now make up 30.2% of total US single stock net exposure (vs a YTD high in late Oct. of 35.9%), which is in the 36th percentile vs the past year and in the 16th percentile vs the past 5 years.

With the indices stabilized above all key moving averages, we are seeing some strong long setups in individual names emerge. Overall, I would prefer to see some consolidation/chop in the early part of this week for even better entry points in my potential long setups. Some pockets of the market are short-term overbought and we could use a small pullback/some rest.

My trading watchlist will be focused on Q3 earnings gappers that are setting up for a continuation move higher into year end.
