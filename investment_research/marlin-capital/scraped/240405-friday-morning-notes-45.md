# Friday Morning Notes 4.5

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Huge reversal yesterday in the major indices with the S&P 500 finishing -1.23% and closing at lows.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cwgl!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2bf51196-0f13-465e-ab46-c3abdb3a0074_1204x889.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!cwgl!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2bf51196-0f13-465e-ab46-c3abdb3a0074_1204x889.jpeg)

For the SPX, this is the first decisive close below the 21 EMA (red line in chart above) since this uptrend/rally began last November (~5 months ago).

The QQQ finished -1.53% and also closed decisively below its 21 EMA.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GMKw!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa2c89309-a988-4be7-a621-6a9638d42e46_1201x887.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GMKw!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa2c89309-a988-4be7-a621-6a9638d42e46_1201x887.jpeg)

The 1-Hour chart tells the story of yesterday’s reversal.  We have not seen selling pressure like this hit the market in quite some time (notice the giant red candles on high volume).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OCpv!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7e507334-5600-49aa-995d-ee6dbfc8c685_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OCpv!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7e507334-5600-49aa-995d-ee6dbfc8c685_1396x653.png)

$433-$434 remains the key support level to watch in the QQQ.  A break below would open the door for a test of the 60 EMA on the daily ($430).

The indices have now relinquished all of the gains from/following March’s FOMC meeting (which was initially followed by a big surge higher in the market).

Meanwhile, the VIX closed +14.3% and is now at its highest level this year.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9WMH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd49e728f-5360-4fd5-a6b7-f63bef20df91_1091x712.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!9WMH!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd49e728f-5360-4fd5-a6b7-f63bef20df91_1091x712.jpeg)

The SPX intraday band of 2.15% is the highest of 2024 and the 2nd largest in the last 52 weeks.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uDq0!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4ced1429-ef41-4643-afe3-aa2a45477a55_1148x682.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uDq0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4ced1429-ef41-4643-afe3-aa2a45477a55_1148x682.jpeg)

Technically, the uptrends that began in the indices 5+ months ago are now seeing their biggest test.  As mentioned, yesterday was the first decisive close below the 21 EMA for both the SPX and QQQ in 5+ months.  For the QQQ, previous closes below the 21 EMA during this rally bounced back above the trendline within 2-3 days.

While I believe it’s too early to get super bearish based off the technical pictures, yesterday’s reversal should not be taken lightly.  This is a potential character change in the market (unlike anything we have seen during this 5+ month rally).  The action in the coming days (today and next week) will ultimately determine if yesterday was just a shakeout or the start of a larger market correction/technical downtrend.  In any case, caution is very warranted here.

COIN – With the market breaking its uptrend, I will be closing out this position as a risk reducing trade.  I’d rather keep this loss small and look to re-enter when the market pressure eases.

MU – Hit my first target of $130 yesterday morning before reversing along with the general market.  I took 1/3rd profits at my target and am now holding the final 2/3rd of this position with a stop below $119.
