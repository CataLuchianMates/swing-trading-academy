# Post Fed Meeting Notes 12.19

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Major sell off yesterday with the S&P 500 -2.95%. This was the SPX’s worst performance on a post FOMC announcement since March 2020 and the index’s 2nd worst trading day of the year (behind 8/5). So much for a ~0.6% implied move. The actual result was much much different.

The Fed delivered on its well telegraphed 25bps cut as expected. However, what surprised markets was how **hawkish** the committee leaned on the decision. The biggest takeaway was the median dot plot showing only 2 cuts for 2025 vs. consensus of 3. 4 members had no cut for today. An overwhelming 14 people had 2 cuts or less for next year. Only 5 people had 3 cuts or more for next year. So, the more hawkish message in the median dots was not a particularly close call.

Powell did not do anything to walk back the hawkish statement during his presser, making it clear that the Fed is back to caring more about inflation than economic growth. As Powell put it “the central bank’s year-end inflation projection has kind of fallen apart”.

The last line of the presser was a pretty good summary of Powell’s tone: “We’re at 4.3%. That’s meaningfully restrictive. And I think it’s a well-calibrated rate for us to continue making progress on inflation while keeping a strong labor market.” Message being - we still think we are restrictive now (Powell did reiterate that policy was restrictive about 10 times), and that’s where we want to be for awhile (bias is on hold). I think this is a firm message from the Fed/Powell that the committee is going to be on hold and the bar for further cuts is high.

Following yesterday’s FOMC, the Fed Funds Futures market is now pricing in around 35bps of cuts for 2025. The odds of a January rate cut (which I said would be the most important datapoint to watch yesterday) fell from 19% to near 0%.

I said at the beginning of the week that yesterday’s Fed meeting would be the last real hurdle for the market in 2024. The market did not clear this hurdle. You never know what will happen on a Fed day, and this is why you should not take on a bunch of new risk into a potential market moving event, no matter how bullish the market looks going in.

Portfolio Updates -

Trump Trades, Small Cap 6 - Long Term Holds.

SEZL - From the Small Cap 6.

Hit with a double whammy yesterday as the Hindenberg Short report cut it down in the morning and the RTY’s worst day in 2+ years did nothing to help matters.

Even after yesterday’s big decline, we are still up +200% on SEZL. Not a bad gain for ~5 months and not a bad time to take more profits. I would suggest being out more than half up here.

This also goes for WGS and INOD, which we are up >100%+ on. We know how volatile Small Caps can be, and we benefitted from this volatility to the upside since my July report. But yesterday was an ugly candle for the Russell 2000 and it’s time to prioritize risk management. So far, the Russell 2000 post-election breakout of $230 has been a false breakout.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3PSA!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa8f300c3-081c-4679-93ba-63a64939f2a8_1198x885.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3PSA!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa8f300c3-081c-4679-93ba-63a64939f2a8_1198x885.jpeg)

If this leads to a bigger decline for the RTY, our huge momentum winners would likely be hit hard. The biggest winners are often the hardest hit during market corrections because people ring the register and don’t want to lose their profits.

Only time will tell what happens from here, but 1 general rule should hold true (especially with Small Caps, where a large portion of R2K companies end up failing): You should never lose money on a stock (or trade) you had a >100%+ gain on. Where and how much profits you take is ultimately up to you, but just make sure you follow that general risk management rule.
