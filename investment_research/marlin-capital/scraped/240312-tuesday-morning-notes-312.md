# Tuesday Morning Notes 3.12

**Daily Market Commentary:**

It was a muted start to the week yesterday with trading volumes down -7% vs 20dma as investors sat on hands ahead of this morning’s CPI report.

The QQQ finished the day -0.37% after hitting its 21 EMA support level.  This is the 8th test of the 21 EMA (green line in chart below) for the QQQ since this uptrend began in late October.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aLnJ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb6d8765b-c1b0-4fcd-8cf7-306627192665_1218x887.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aLnJ!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb6d8765b-c1b0-4fcd-8cf7-306627192665_1218x887.png)

Each previous dip to the 21 EMA has been bought up with a couple shakeouts below the trend line.  In my view, it is too early to get bearish technically until we start to close below the 21 EMA and continue to the downside.

The 1 Hour chart shows a similar pattern with key support at $433-$434 (previous pivot lows).  Until we break below these previous lows and the 21 EMA support level, the trend remains up.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OKtl!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F76ffe214-38ae-456f-896d-01ab8812a560_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!OKtl!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F76ffe214-38ae-456f-896d-01ab8812a560_1396x653.png)

February CPI – February CPI Inflation rate rose to 3.2%, above expectations of 3.1%.

Core CPI Inflation fell to 3.8%, above expectations of 3.7%.

This is the 35th consecutive month with inflation above 3% and the 2nd straight increase.

Price changes over last year (Feb CPI report)...

Transportation: +9.9%

Shelter: +5.7%

Food away from home: +4.5%

Electricity: +3.6%

Overall CPI: +3.2%

Medical Care: +1.1%

Food at home: +1.0%

New Cars: +0.4%

Used Cars: -1.8%

Gasoline: -3.9%

Fuel Oil: -5.4%

Gas Utilities: -8.8%

Despite inflation coming in a bit hotter than expected for February, the market is set for a gap up this morning (as of this writing).  It will be important to see if this gap up fades or we close strong today.  If we do fade, key support levels to watch in the QQQ include the 21 EMA at $436 and previous lows at $433-$434.
