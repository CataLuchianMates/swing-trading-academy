# The Week Ahead

From a technical perspective, we are in the midst of the first real test for the indices since this rally began off the SVB lows.  Since the March YTD lows, the S&P 500 has not spent more than 2 days consecutively under the 21 EMA support line (until now).  We have now closed under the 21 EMA 4 days in a row and 6 of the last 7.

Moreover, this is the first bearish 8 and 21 EMA crossover in the SPX we have observed since the March lows.

The QQQ has been the weaker index so far, as it has closed below the 21 EMA 7 days in a row.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NPmL!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5ccc1a5b-774b-41d6-aee8-27301cffcf79_1187x887.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NPmL!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5ccc1a5b-774b-41d6-aee8-27301cffcf79_1187x887.jpeg)

Zooming out on the weekly SPX chart, there is nothing to be alarmed about yet. This is an orderly 2 week correction that has not broken any key weekly support lines.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!I2Dy!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F89b8767c-91fc-487e-a57f-7411d8350f2a_956x724.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!I2Dy!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F89b8767c-91fc-487e-a57f-7411d8350f2a_956x724.jpeg)

This has been a normal and healthy pullback so far following a >15% rally in the S&P 500.  Pullbacks after strong rallies like the one we experienced YTD (>15%) are common and have averaged about -8% since 1950.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JtL_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc2990474-8a21-4c7f-a5ff-dcb3b1c3cf09_1284x799.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JtL_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc2990474-8a21-4c7f-a5ff-dcb3b1c3cf09_1284x799.jpeg)

Mega Cap Tech stocks, specifically the Magnificent 7, have corrected the most since the start of August.  These are the stocks that advanced the most during the rally, so some give back is to be expected.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!w49I!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F11a69121-eb61-46c3-bd15-70a5ccf62efd_1284x798.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!w49I!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F11a69121-eb61-46c3-bd15-70a5ccf62efd_1284x798.jpeg)

Interestingly, the S&P 500 is following historical seasonality trends for pre-election years very closely.  The historical path suggests more market consolidation and chop into November before a year end rally.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kKGc!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc8ae272b-857c-417b-9f7f-5d0f5e767f82_933x564.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kKGc!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc8ae272b-857c-417b-9f7f-5d0f5e767f82_933x564.jpeg)

Earnings are tracking better than expected, but the overall market is down slightly so far through earnings season.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yjIn!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb82f7be7-496e-4e15-acaf-1ef12c163206_1184x845.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yjIn!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb82f7be7-496e-4e15-acaf-1ef12c163206_1184x845.jpeg)

Stocks that have beaten earnings estimates have seen muted outperformance, indicating much of this was priced in ahead of time with the YTD rally.  Many stocks got ahead of themselves, and their beats were not impressive enough for the rallies to continue.

We have a quieter lineup of earnings ahead this week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aEq4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3e6328a5-5f39-488c-b330-98b2582c329a_1920x1080.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aEq4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3e6328a5-5f39-488c-b330-98b2582c329a_1920x1080.jpeg)

With the market struggling its way through earnings season, earnings gapper setups have become rarer this quarter and we have seen plenty of post-day 1 fades.  In my experience, it is best not to fight the general tide in trading.  General market weakness eventually hits all stocks.

With that said, and as I have mentioned, this pullback has been orderly so far.  There has been no major spike in fear or panic from market participants.  There are plenty of stocks on my watchlist showing relative strength during this pullback.  I believe these stocks will be best set up for large advances once the general market pressure eases.

**Relative Strength Watchlist:**

-          CLS (holding small long)

-          COUR

-          FRSH

-          UPWK

-          VRT

-          RELY

-          APP

-          IONQ

-          ROVR

With the indices currently below my key short/medium term EMAs, I want to continue to be patient to start off this week.  Ideally, we can see a bottom start to build in the indices around the 60 EMAs.  However, it is best to be prepared for all scenarios, including an acceleration of selling and break of the 60 EMAs, in which case I would not want to be caught with more than 1 small long.  I am in observation mode to kick off this week.
