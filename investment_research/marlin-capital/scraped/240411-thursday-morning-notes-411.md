# Thursday Morning Notes 4.11

**Daily Market Commentary:**

US stocks endured broad selling pressure yesterday as yields spiked (10 YR +18bps to 4.55%) on CPI coming in hotter than expected.  The US 10 YR had its biggest daily move in over a year.

The SPX only closed down -0.95%, showing more resiliency as the low of the day was made in the pre-market.

As expected, rate sensitive sectors were among the hardest hit: Regional Banks -4.8%, REITS -4.1%, Housing -4.1%, Utilities -1.7%, Non-Profitable -3.5%, RTY -2.9% (worst day since Feb 13th).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qqPE!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F920e2695-58bf-45df-8c55-9de13ae66a4c_1177x1019.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qqPE!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F920e2695-58bf-45df-8c55-9de13ae66a4c_1177x1019.jpeg)

Following yesterday’s CPI report, the market is now pricing in just 2 rate cuts by year end.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PQt4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb383f619-4582-4340-b76c-90392cdd825f_1226x364.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PQt4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb383f619-4582-4340-b76c-90392cdd825f_1226x364.jpeg)

The choppy/rangebound action in the QQQ continued after yesterday’s gap down.  Most importantly, support held and bears were unable to take the market down following yesterday’s catalyst and gap down.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uBj-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fee26aa64-75cf-44ce-b4a8-d7484d22b3ec_1396x653.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!uBj-!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fee26aa64-75cf-44ce-b4a8-d7484d22b3ec_1396x653.png)

Overall, yesterday’s action was not overly bearish (i.e. no character change) when considering the catalyst for a potential larger drop.  The market continues to show resiliency in the face of bad news, and key support levels have not been breached.

MU – still holding the last 2/3rds of my position into Thursday.  My stop loss at $119 did not hit yesterday.  My stop will remain there and I will let this trade play out.
