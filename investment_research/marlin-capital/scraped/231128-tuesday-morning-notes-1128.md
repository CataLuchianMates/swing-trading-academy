# Tuesday Morning Notes 11.28

**Morning Market Commentary**

Yesterday was a quiet day coming out of the holiday weekend with volumes tracking -18% vs 20dma with the majority of investors sitting on their hands into a busy back half of the week (US PCE, EU, CPI, OPEC, China PMIs all on Thursday).  The S&P 500 finished -0.2% on the day.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!npv5!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F78efea88-68b3-4089-80ba-068a5fe1c015_970x715.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!npv5!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F78efea88-68b3-4089-80ba-068a5fe1c015_970x715.jpeg)

E-commerce strength was the big standout yesterday on the back of Adobe Analytics reporting that Black Friday Shoppers spent a record $9.8B online in the US on Friday.  Web sales were up +7.5% YoY, and as a result, SHOP +5%, ROKU +8.5%, ETSY +3%, AMZN +1% all led the way.  AMZN is now near 18-month highs.  Shopify (+5% yesterday) hit a record $4.1B in Black Friday sales, up 22% YoY.

The other major theme of the largest shopping weekend of 2023 was the growth of “Buy Now, Pay Later” (BNPL), which saw sales grow a staggering +47% YoY on Black Friday.  As a result, AFRM finished the day +12%.  For those who share the same long-term bullish thesis on AFRM (and BNPL in the US) as I do, I still see these prices (<$30) as an attractive buy point for long term investors.

Including in-person shopping, Black Friday sales rose +2.5% YoY.  We are seeing household savings dropping along with household debt rising, but as long as the labor market remains tight, I do not see the negative catalyst to disrupt the market and trigger the recession trade.

Cyber Monday – we will see these numbers start to come through, but according to Adobe, consumers spent $8.3B through 6PM yesterday.  Adobe expects as much as $12.4B in total Cyber Monday sales, which would be the largest online shopping day of all time.

CTA positioning – a lot of the heavy lifting is now behind us.  CTA flows are more in the neutral column now after their recent buying spree.  CTA positioning is currently close to the average of the past 5 years (and is no longer expected to be a bullish catalyst for the market this week/the near future).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Xvq8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa829ba7c-958e-4695-92b8-6e02445c9fdc_1027x760.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Xvq8!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa829ba7c-958e-4695-92b8-6e02445c9fdc_1027x760.jpeg)

The IPO market appears to be heating up with Reddit, Skims, Rubrik, and Shien tentative IPO headlines hitting the news flow yesterday.  Last time the market took on a large bevy of new issues, it marked a temporary top in the indices.  This is something to watch as some signs of froth appears to be returning to the equity markets.
