# Tuesday Morning Notes 10.10

Daily Market Commentary

US stocks recovered their early losses yesterday with the S&P 500 closing the day +0.63%.  The index closed right at key 21 EMA resistance at $4339.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vu0f!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F903a3296-ff7e-49c0-8c2d-74f70ab8b850_1269x946.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vu0f!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F903a3296-ff7e-49c0-8c2d-74f70ab8b850_1269x946.jpeg)

Equity trading volumes were extremely light yesterday with 8.6B shares traded across all exchanges, making it the 2nd lightest full session of the year.  Bonds have been leading equities recently in terms of risk sentiment so it is not surprising to see the equity market quiet yesterday with the Bond Market closed.

Despite the geopolitical developments over the weekend, stocks reversed off their morning lows and grinded higher throughout the day.  Price action was positive as the market liked Fed speaker Logan’s comments that higher yield may mean less need to raise rates.  Daly made similar comments last week.

In addition, CTA positioning no longer remains a headwind for equities.  This cohort sold $135B in equities over the last month but flipped better to buy yesterday with a further medium term momentum threshold at $4353 (which would trigger an uptick in buying demand).

The stronger tech led QQQ finished Monday +0.51%.  The index cleared its 21 EMA resistance level and has now closed above both its 8 EMA and 21 EMA.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZSg1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8dcace53-52db-45e2-9aad-8511952aa5b6_1209x889.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ZSg1!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8dcace53-52db-45e2-9aad-8511952aa5b6_1209x889.jpeg)

On a shorter time frame (1 HR), it is clear how yesterday’s action was bullish as the QQQ made its low of the day in the first hour of trading, successfully tested that low in the next hour, and then grinded higher the rest of the day (breaking Friday’s highs and closing near daily highs).

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!29P4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5cfc08f3-077b-4c79-ab06-57fd57ca8b94_1396x663.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!29P4!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F5cfc08f3-077b-4c79-ab06-57fd57ca8b94_1396x663.png)

Key resistance levels to watch above include $369-$370 and $372.
