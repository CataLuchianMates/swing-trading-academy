# Tuesday Morning Notes 2.6

**Daily Market Commentary**

Some expected giveback yesterday in the market to kick off the week with the S&P 500 finishing the day -0.32% and well of the lows of the session.

The theme of the day was reversion from nosebleed/overbought levels in the face of yields pushing back up to 4.16% on another strong economic data point in the ISM Svc read + remarks from Powell suggesting that rate cuts may not come until the middle of the year (CBS 60 Mins interview).

Long duration and rate sensitive pockets of the market sold off while popular pairs continued to work: MegaCap Tech vs Non-Profitable Tech +2.6%, HF Long vs Popular Shorts +1.9%, AI Winners vs Losers +2.3%, and Strong vs Weak Balance Sheets +1.5%.

For the Mag 7, there was continued strength in NVDA (+4.8%) on Goldman’s price target increase to $800 on robust AI server demand and improving GPU supply.  There was some give back in MSFT (-1.4%), META (-3.3%), and  AMZN (-80bps), which was reasonable following last week’s chase (reminder – META closed with a 14d RSI of 86 after adding $200B in market cap on Friday).

After the bell, PLTR beat for the quarter on Revs/EBITDA, with the company citing big AI demand driving upside, however Q1 rev guide was below at the midpoint.

The stock is set to gap up +20% this morning and is on my radar for an earnings gapper trade setup.  However, with that said, PLTR has not been my favorite stock to trade in the past.  It has a very large float (1.9B) and often gives many fake technical moves, so for that reason, I will only be entering light in PLTR today if the setup presents itself.
