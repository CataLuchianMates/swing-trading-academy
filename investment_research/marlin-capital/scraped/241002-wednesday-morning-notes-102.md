# Wednesday Morning Notes 10.2

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Stocks sold off yesterday on flaring tensions in the Middle East (\*ISRAEL’S IDF SAYS ROCKETS FROM IRAN HAVE BEEN FIREDAT ISRAEL).  The S&P 500 closed -0.93% on the day while the Nasdaq sold off -1.43%.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PCqg!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F50030004-c448-43a9-b46a-29bebc364eda_1200x881.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!PCqg!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F50030004-c448-43a9-b46a-29bebc364eda_1200x881.jpeg)

Lower quality pockets of the market lagged the most with Goldman’s Most Short, Non-Profitable Tech, Low Profit R2K, High Beta all down over -2% on the day.  On the data front, job openings came in slightly above expectations while ISM Manufacturing and Construction spending were slightly below.

The April Israel/Iran tensions led to a ~6% decline in US stocks.  In any case, volatility has returned and I am not looking to add any new positions just yet.  I will stay managing my current positions for now.

Active Portfolio –

SN – No changes here.  Holding my final 1/4th of this long with a stop loss at $104, ensuring a +14% gain on the final portion.

NMM – No changes here.  Holding my final 1/3rd of this long with a stop at $60.26, ensuring a +9.5% gain on the final portion.

AVO – Letting this 1 play out.  My stop is set at $12.47 for -4.5% risk on this trade.
