# Thursday Morning Notes 8.8

**Daily Market Commentary:**

Market is in rally mode once again this morning with the SPX up > 1.8% and QQQ up >2%.

A hallmark of a bearish market is a weak close, which we have seen the last 2 days with the indices fading into the bell.  With that said, I am on watch for a higher low forming in the indices, which may have been made yesterday.  The VIX is down another -11% this morning and under 25.

This morning, initial jobless claims came in lower than expected at 233k versus estimates of 240k (a decline of -17k from the week before).  As I stated last week, we are now in a “bad news is bad” and “good news is good” market regime, different than what we saw throughout the year.  After last week’s concerning economic prints, this is a welcome “good news” data point for bulls.  It shows things aren’t quite as bad as they looked last week, and the recessionary trade was more of a knee-jerk reaction.

Pulling it all together, positioning is much cleaner than it was a few weeks ago (after Monday’s washout).  Economic growth is at least more durable than was being priced at the end of last week (with defensives having their best 2-day performance over cyclicals in 17+ yrs).

With that said, I suspect the path from here will not be an easy one for traders, particularly as we are in the worst liquidity window of the year (and August is historically the worst month for equity flows).  By multiple measures, ES and NQ liquidity is currently at one of its lowest levels in years.
