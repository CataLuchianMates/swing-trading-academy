## Main Thesis

We are at the early stages of a major multi-year asset rotation: out of US growth stocks and into emerging markets, commodities, gold, and value. The catalyst is a weakening US economy + Fed rate cuts + dollar weakness. EM/S&P 500 ratio has bottomed and is rolling up. This mirrors the 2001–2007 rotation playbook. The rotation is already underway but still early relative to history.

## The Asset Rotation Framework

Historically, asset cycles rotate together:
- **Strong dollar era** → US growth/tech dominates; EM/commodities/gold underperform (1980–2001, 2011–2026)
- **Weak dollar era** → EM/commodities/gold outperform; US growth stagnates (1971–1980, 2001–2011)

Dollar weakening hurts EM less (they carry dollar-denominated debt), EM economies recover, commodities boom, global capital rotates in.

Historical rotation triggers: 1971 (gold standard default), 1985 (Plaza Accord), 2001 (recession + dot-com bust at peak US capital concentration).

**Now (2026)**: US capital concentration is at its most extreme ever. Fed is cutting rates. Dollar already softening. The setup is better than 2019 (when it didn't follow through due to COVID).

## Memory Stocks Deep Dive

Three companies make almost all the world's RAM: **MU** (US), **SK Hynix** (Korea), **Samsung** (Korea). All have broken to fresh all-time highs as of May 2026.

- Lyn's trade (Apr 12, 2026 report): Selling **MU June 18 cash-secured puts at $380 strike** (stock was at $420) — entering at ~$350 cost basis if assigned
- **Bear case**: Memory is cyclical; oversupply kills margins historically
- **Bull case**: AI agents need persistent memory; this isn't a normal cycle; supply constraints limit new capacity
- **Lyn's position**: Bull. Recommends conservative position sizing.
