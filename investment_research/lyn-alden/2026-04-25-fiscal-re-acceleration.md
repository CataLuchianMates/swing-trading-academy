## 3. Fiscal Re-Acceleration Thesis

### The Setup
- **"One Big Beautiful Bill Act"** (passed ~July 2025): tax cuts + spending — providing mild fiscal boost in 2026
- Partially offset by higher fuel/energy costs hitting consumers
- Net effect: fiscal impulse remains **positive but modest** in 2026

### The Core Thesis (Fiscal Dominance)
- US deficit is structural, not cyclical — no political path to meaningful reduction
- Both parties committed to spending; the constraint is monetary, not fiscal
- Result: **asset prices benefit** as new money flows through the system — fiscal spending lands in pockets that buy assets
- This is the "K-shaped" outcome: asset owners and fiscal recipients win; middle-income earners hurt by inflation/costs

### K-Shaped Economy
| Winners | Losers |
|---------|--------|
| Asset owners (stocks, real estate) | Fixed-wage workers |
| Defense/government contractors | Small business (high rates + costs) |
| Fiscal recipients (transfer payments) | Middle class (inflation erodes purchasing power) |
| Energy sector | European manufacturers |

| **PNC** | PNC Financial | 12.83x | ~7.8% | Large regional; diversified fee income |

### Why Banks Now
- Unrealized losses peaked at **~$700B** (2023 SVB era) → now down to **$306B**
- Losses shrinking as short-duration bonds mature and reprice
- Net interest margins recovering as deposit repricing normalizes
- If rates stay high = banks earn more on assets. If rates cut = unrealized losses continue shrinking. **Both scenarios constructive.**
- Cheap relative to history: 10-13x P/E vs 15-20x in normal times

### Risk
- Commercial real estate exposure remains a tail risk
- Recession = credit losses spike and overwhelm margin improvement

## 5. DeFi — Structural Failure on Display

### KelpDAO Hack → Arbitrum Freeze
- KelpDAO was hacked; Arbitrum's **multi-sig council voted to freeze** the hacker's funds
- This proves: **DeFi is not decentralized in practice**
- A council of ~11 addresses can veto/freeze any transaction on Arbitrum
- This is the same trust model as a bank — just with worse UX and more volatility

### DeFi Overall
- **Total Value Locked (TVL) still below 2021 peak** — no recovery after the collapse
- DeFi's promise (permissionless, unstoppable money) is undermined by multi-sig governance
- Lyn's view: DeFi as a sector likely continues to stagnate — capital flows to Bitcoin (actual decentralization) instead
